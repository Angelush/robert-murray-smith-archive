## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a "Wind Wall" — a vertical-axis wind turbine array inspired by an online design he later discovered was only a render and had never actually been built. After his initial flat-blade version fails to turn in the wind, he iterates to a Savonius-type rotor design using uPVC guttering, which works successfully and generates electricity via repurposed NEMA stepper motors acting as generators.

## Key Topics
- Discovery of the "Wind Wall" concept (attributed to "Joe") via friend Dennis Kremer
- Construction of flat-blade wind wall rotors using foam board
- Testing and failure of the flat-blade design
- Redesign using Savonius-type curved blades cut from uPVC guttering
- Three-blade Savonius configuration (based on a research tip from friend Wez Goodman)
- Frame construction from plywood and builder's board
- Attaching NEMA stepper motors as generators
- Wiring stepper motor coils through full-wave bridge rectifiers for DC output
- Cost breakdown and replicability discussion
- Aesthetic and practical appeal of the wind wall concept (balconies, high-rises, architectural integration)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Foam board (A4 board / "builder's board" / Celotex board)** — used for blade squares, frame construction, and foam plugs
- **9mm wooden dowel** — used as the blade axle rod
- **Gorilla Glue** — used to bond blades to dowel
- **Expanding foam** — injected into blade joints for stiffening and waterproofing
- **Broom handle wood** — used for wooden end plugs on the dowel
- **8mm steel bar** — used as the bearing axle through the wooden plugs
- **uPVC guttering (semicircular profile)** — main material for Savonius-type curved blades
- **uPVC pipe adhesive (Bostik and others)** — used to bond uPVC blade sections
- **Super glue** — alternative adhesive for uPVC
- **18mm exterior-grade plywood (4×8 ft sheet)** — used for the revised support frame (~£43)
- **Flexible couplings** — used to connect axle to motor shaft (8mm to 5mm)
- **Plastic alignment strips** — used to align motors with axles
- **NEMA stepper motors** — used as generators (6 units, ~£40 total)
- **Full-wave bridge rectifiers (1000V, 2A)** — used to convert AC stepper output to DC

## Techniques and Methods
- **Jig-making** — simple nail-and-baseboard jig for marking centerlines on 200 blade squares at 7cm
- **Guillotine cutting** — for rapid production of foam board squares
- **Alternating blade attachment** — every second blade glued at 90° offset on the dowel
- **Expanding foam injection** — for structural reinforcement and weatherproofing of blade joints
- **Hole-saw foam plug cutting** — for centering the steel axle inside the uPVC rotor assembly
- **60° rotational offset stacking** — Savonius blade units stacked with 60° phase offset along the axle
- **Three-blade Savonius configuration** — three curved half-shells per rotor unit for optimum efficiency
- **Coil identification via resistance testing** — multimeter used to identify stepper motor coil pairs
- **Full-wave rectification** — bridge rectifiers wired to each coil for DC output (12 coils total across 6 motors)
- **Timber cutting service** — local DIY store used to pre-cut plywood to spec
