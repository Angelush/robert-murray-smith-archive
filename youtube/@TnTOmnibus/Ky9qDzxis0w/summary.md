## Overview
This episode of "Man In A Shed" is a wide-ranging survey of alternative vehicle propulsion technologies, covering the rise and fall of electric cars, hydrogen generation from aluminium and water, ammonia as a fuel, zinc-chlorine flow batteries, wind-powered cars, and the long history of "water car" claims. Robert argues that the death and rebirth of the electric car was driven purely by consumer interest rather than conspiracy, and he highlights several underexplored technologies — particularly the aluminium-water hydrogen system and Graneau's cavitation research — as genuinely promising directions that deserve more attention.

## Key Topics
- History of electric vehicles from 1828 (Robert Anderson) through the Edison-Ford project to the modern EV revival
- Why EVs died (battery limitations, cheap oil, cheaper petrol cars) and why they returned (consumer demand, government policy)
- Current EV shortcomings: cost, charging infrastructure, insurance, energy source still largely fossil fuels
- Hydrogen generation via aluminium + potassium hydroxide (the Yeno method), including live demonstration
- The Francois Cornish patent (US 4,702,894) for an aluminium-wire/water hydrogen generator, and BMW's trials
- Ammonia as a combustion fuel: history (X-15 rocket plane, Belgian WWII buses), GAC/Toyota production ammonia car, infrastructure advantages
- Zinc-chlorine flow batteries: Gulf Western's 1970s system, Stanford's 2021 microporous carbon advance, 2023 manganese dioxide cathode paper, and the NanoFlowcell production car
- Wind-powered vehicles: Greenbird, Blackbird, Savonius rotor model build, Dutch inventor Oscar DEA's turbine-charged EV
- The "water car" — clarifying what it is and is not, history of fraud cases, and Graneau's cavitation-based approach as the only legitimate candidate

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Potassium hydroxide (KOH), 5 M concentrated solution** — electrolyte/reactant in the Yeno method hydrogen generation demo
- **Aluminium (foil and wire)** — reacts with KOH to produce hydrogen; also used as the metal in Cornish's patented system
- **Potassium aluminate** — reaction product of aluminium + KOH
- **Hydrogen (H₂)** — gas produced by Yeno method; fuel for hydrogen cars and the Cornish BMW trial
- **Nickel-iron battery (Edison's "nickeline" battery)** — intended for Ford-Edison EV project; had high internal resistance issues
- **Lead-acid battery** — the limiting battery technology of early EVs
- **Ammonia (NH₃)** — zero-CO₂ combustion fuel (burns to N₂ + H₂O); used in X-15 aircraft, Belgian WWII buses, University of Michigan cross-country drive, and GAC/Toyota 2 L saloon car
- **Liquid oxygen / hydrogen (promoters)** — added at ~3% to make ammonia burn as fast as conventional fuel
- **Zinc chloride (ZnCl₂)** — electrolyte in zinc-chlorine flow battery
- **Chlorine (Cl₂)** — cathode reactant in zinc-chlorine battery; stored as chlorine hydrate
- **Chlorine hydrate (Cl₂·8H₂O)** — ice-like frozen storage form of chlorine in Gulf Western battery
- **Microporous / activated carbon** — used by Stanford (2021) to trap chlorine (and other halogens) in battery cathode
- **Manganese dioxide (MnO₂)** — cathode coating in 2023 paper that improved zinc-chlorine battery reaction kinetics
- **Sodium / lithium** — metals substituted for zinc in Stanford's updated chlorine battery work
- **Sodium chloride (saltwater)** — claimed working fluid of NanoFlowcell's "Quantino" flow-battery car
- **HHO / Brown's gas** — partial electrolysis product, clarified as *not* the basis of a true "water car"
- **Metal hydride** — mentioned in context of the 2008 Japanese Genipax claim

## Techniques and Methods
- **Yeno method** — reacting aluminium with concentrated KOH solution to chemically generate pure hydrogen (also produces electrical current and heat); demonstrated live in the video
- **Aluminium-spark hydrogen generation (Cornish patent)** — high-voltage spark along an aluminium wire reel submerged in water strips oxide and exposes fresh aluminium, producing hydrogen on demand
- **Flow battery chemistry** — separating reactants (zinc and chlorine) into distinct storage chambers and pumping them into a reaction cell; basis of both Gulf Western's 1970s design and NanoFlowcell's modern car
- **Chlorine storage as hydrate** — chilling chlorine gas with water to form a frozen, safer solid (Gulf Western approach)
- **Microporous carbon cathode coating** — trapping halogen gases inside tiny pores to eliminate external chlorine storage (Stanford 2021)
- **MnO₂ cathode coating** — catalytic layer that speeds up and completes the chlorine reaction in zinc-chlorine cells (2023 paper)
- **Electrolysis of water** — splitting water into H₂ and O₂; noted as only 4% of current hydrogen production due to low efficiency
- **Cavitation via high-voltage arc** — applying a high-voltage arc to water to create explosive cavitation bubbles (Graneau 1998); Robert's candidate for a genuine "water as fuel" mechanism
- **Savonius rotor wind turbine** — omnidirectional wind rotor 3D-printed and assembled into a model wind-powered car, demonstrated with a hairdryer
- **3D printing (Tinkercad / Thingiverse)** — used to fabricate parts for the wind-car model (frame, wheels, bevel gears, axles, bearings)
- **Ammonia combustion with promoter** — adding ~3% hydrogen to ammonia to achieve combustion speed comparable to petrol in a standard internal combustion engine
