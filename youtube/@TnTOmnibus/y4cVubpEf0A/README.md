## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith argues that mathematics is simply a language — specifically, the language of shape, size, and order — and demystifies the common perception that it is magical or uniquely difficult. He draws parallels between learning maths and learning a foreign language, explaining that the real barrier is social embarrassment rather than inherent complexity. He introduces this as the first in a planned series of mathematics videos.
