## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith traces the centuries-long history of solar thermal energy, arguing it has repeatedly been adopted during fuel crises only to be abandoned when easier options returned. Using this historical context as motivation, he demonstrates how to build practical solar thermal devices — a solar kettle and a solar barbecue — from cheap evacuated tubes and 3D-printed parts, and explores concentrated solar power techniques including parabolic reflectors and a DIY Fresnel lens made from water-filled plastic tubing.
