## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith designs and builds a DIY micro hydroelectric generator based on the Archimedes screw principle, inspired by a Japanese inventor's low-head water turbine. Using recycled CDs as screw flights and expanding foam for the housing, he constructs a working prototype that successfully generates 5.1 volts in a shallow stream. The video also includes a theoretical discussion on torque, generator matching, and energy scavenging philosophy.
