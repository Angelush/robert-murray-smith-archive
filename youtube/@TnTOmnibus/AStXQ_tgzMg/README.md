## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build the "Thunderbolt" (wooden) and "Mighty Mite" (metal) axial-flux generators entirely from hardware store components — bolts, plywood or mild steel discs, neodymium magnets, and enamel wire. He explains the physics behind why the design works (Lorentz law, 90° field angle) and integrates the final generator into a Darrieus/Grinus-type wind turbine made from drain pipe, achieving roughly 50–120 V AC output. The key takeaway is that this generator is not more efficient than any other — its real value is that anyone with a basic drill can build it cheaply from scavenged parts.
