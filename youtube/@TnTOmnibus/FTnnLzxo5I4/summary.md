## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to convert a common rooftop whirlybird ventilator turbine into a small wind/thermal generator. By exploiting the large diameter of the turbine (giving useful edge speed despite low torque) and building a custom axial generator with coils around the rim and a north-south magnet rotor, he achieves 6–9 watts of output even in light breezes.

## Key Topics
- How whirlybird/roof turbine vents work (wind and thermal convection driven)
- Why low shaft torque prevents simply bolting a motor to the center
- Exploiting rim speed via an axial generator design
- Flywheel effect of the turbine's mass and why coreless coils were chosen (no cogging)
- Australian modification: adding a fan blade for ~40% efficiency increase
- Construction of stator (coil ring) and rotor (magnet ring) from uPVC trim board
- Wiring coils in parallel for higher current output
- Safety note on handling sharp/heavy components

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **uPVC trim board / Sintra / builders board** — used to form the stator and rotor rings
- **ABS strip** — used as inner lining for the rotor hoop
- **MDF** — used as a jig base
- **Four-inch nails** — jig pins for bending plastic into circles
- **Super glue / cyanoacrylate** — primary adhesive for uPVC and ABS bonds; also mentioned for closing a cut
- **Hot glue** — used to secure magnets on the rotor
- **Brass pins** — used to anchor wiring on the stator
- **Neodymium magnets (×50)** — arranged in alternating north-south pattern on the rotor
- **12 mm extension bolt and bar** — used to extend the central axle

## Techniques and Methods
- **Axial flux generator construction** — coils fixed on a stationary ring, magnets on a rotating ring
- **Coreless coil winding** — deliberate omission of iron cores to avoid cogging and preserve flywheel spin
- **Jig-based ring forming** — nails in MDF used to bend uPVC strip into a precise circle
- **Mitre-joining and laminating board** — for constructing the lower stiffening ring
- **Heat welding** — bonding fan blade edges to ABS strip
- **Soldering** — connecting coil wires to brass-pin terminals
- **Parallel wiring of coils** — to increase current (amps) output at the expense of voltage
- **Coil polarity tracking** — marking start/end of each winding to ensure consistent phase orientation
