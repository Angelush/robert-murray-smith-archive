## Overview
This pilot/omnibus episode of "Man In A Shed" is a wide-ranging tour through Robert's current obsessions, connecting ideas across nature, engineering, sustainability, and alternative energy. He draws a thread from pliosaur swimming biomechanics through to dual-rotor wind turbines, then pivots through shopping-trolley wheelchair hacks, 3D scanning cultural heritage, a carbon "forever battery" replication, and finally a gravity-powered generator. The throughline is that nature and overlooked junk are both packed with engineering solutions waiting to be noticed.

## Key Topics
- Pliosaur anatomy and locomotion — the four-flipper figure-of-eight swimming motion and its 50% efficiency/thrust gains (Dr Luke Muscutt, University of Southampton)
- Biomimicry: how pliosaur flipper vortex mechanics parallel goose-formation aerodynamics and inform dual-rotor wind turbine design
- OWI Wind Turbine — James Dyson Award 2018 winner, spherical/tetrahedral design, Venturi-effect wind capture from any direction; Robert builds and tests a 3D-printed version from the US patent
- Repurposing waste: abandoned shopping trolleys converted into wheelchairs (Thailand case study); China's surplus bicycle-share fleet as mobility resource
- 3D scanning with the Creality Ferret Pro: scanning the Gandhi statue in Parliament Square and other public sculptures
- Scan the World — MyMiniFactory's open-access 3D scan archive of global cultural artifacts
- Art UK — UK's digital catalog of publicly owned sculptures and paintings
- Carpon pile — a "forever battery" running since 1950 in Romania; Robert replicates a Type 3 cell from French patent FR 57787, tests temperature dependence of output voltage
- Gravity energy storage: Archimedes screw worm gear, spiral-drop ratchet generator, humming-top ratchet mechanism, and a student-built portable gravity-powered emergency generator from the Technological University of the Philippines

## Techniques and Methods
- **Fluid dynamics analysis** — reviewing Dr Muscutt's computational research on pliosaur four-flipper vortex interaction
- **Patent analysis** — dissecting US Patent 2021/034950 A1 (OWI turbine) and French patent FR 57787 (Carpon cell) to understand design principles
- **TinkerCAD 3D modelling** — designing OWI turbine, Archimedes screw worm gear, spiral gravity drop mechanism, and humming-top cage from scratch and from remixed Thingiverse parts
- **FDM 3D printing** — printing all modelled parts
- **Wind capture testing** — using a hair dryer to verify OWI turbine unidirectional spin from multiple approach angles
- **Electrochemical cell construction** — building graphite-foil/sodium-carbonate and graphite/copper-chloride cells; measuring voltage across a 10 Ω resistor
- **Temperature-controlled voltage measurement** — heating electrolyte on a hot plate to demonstrate thermal EMF increase (1.5 V → 2.3 V)
- **3D scanning** — using Creality Ferret Pro to capture the Gandhi statue in 10 stitched scans; mesh repair in 3D Build; export as STL for printing
- **Metal cutting and welding** — cutting shopping trolley frame to 50 cm seat depth; cutting and welding tricycle rear axle assembly onto trolley to create a functional wheelchair
- **Ratchet mechanism assembly** — extracting and adapting a ratchet from an OTVinta spinning top design; integrating thrust bearings, clutch plate, and flywheel weight
- **Serpentine coil winding** — winding a pickup coil around the spinning-top generator (links to a separate coil-making video)
