## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the challenge of converting oscillating or reciprocating motion (like footsteps or buffeting wind) into unidirectional rotation for energy generation. He builds on the 1956 SEO "magic lever" — a clever ratchet mechanism that always outputs rotation in one direction regardless of input direction — combining it with a 3D-printed axial flux serpentine coil generator and an oscillating wind arm to demonstrate a working proof-of-concept wind energy harvester.

## Key Topics
- Human footstep energy potential (~5–10W per step, ~14Wh per 10,000 steps)
- The problem of converting reciprocating/oscillating motion to unidirectional rotation
- The SEO "magic lever" (1956) and how it achieves unidirectional output
- Edison's phonomotor flywheel ratchet concept as historical inspiration
- Building a 3D-printed flywheel with integrated ratchet
- Design and winding of a modular axial flux serpentine coil generator
- Assembly of a spring-loaded "magic button" generator
- Building a wind-buffet oscillating arm to drive the button mechanism
- Proof-of-concept demonstration of the combined oscillating wind turbine system

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 10mm steel ball bearings (press-fit into flywheel rim as outer weights)
- Skate bearings: 22mm × 8mm × 7mm (flywheel axle), 42mm × 20mm × 12mm (coil former center)
- 0.1mm enamel copper wire (serpentine coil, ~2000 turns, recommend ~1500)
- 18 × 2 neodymium magnets per generator disc, arranged north–south alternating
- Compression springs: 25.5mm long × 18.5mm diameter (two used in series for button mechanism)
- Rubber bands (acting as return springs for wind arm pivot)
- 4mm and 8mm rod stock
- 8mm steel washer (magnet disc spacer)
- Super glue (cap bonding)
- Black electrical tape (coil bundle retention)
- 3D-printed PLA parts (TinkerCAD designed, shared on Thingiverse)

## Techniques and Methods
- TinkerCAD 3D modelling and Thingiverse file sharing
- Ratchet mechanism assembly using the SEO magic lever (hooked fork / crank design)
- Press-fit installation of ball bearings into 3D-printed holes using a hammer
- Serpentine coil winding using a sliding adjustable coil former (designed by "grindle1960")
- Hand-winding ~2000 turns of 0.1mm enamel wire; measuring slot length with a wire template then adding 10%
- Folding wound coil into a star shape for insertion into coil former, then gluing cap
- Axial flux generator assembly: two magnet discs either side of a stationary serpentine coil
- Spring-loaded push-button mechanism construction
- 3D-printed oscillating wind arm with pivot, sliding joint, and rubber-band springs
- Integration of wind arm rod into button generator slot to convert wind oscillation to rotation
- Voltage measurement with multimeter during hand-spin and button-press tests
