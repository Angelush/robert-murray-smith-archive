## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a series of 3D-printed mechanisms he calls "impossible gears" — gear arrangements that produce a non-1:1 gear reduction ratio even when all gears involved are exactly the same size, contradicting a long-held assumption in mechanical engineering. He builds and tests five distinct mechanisms across the video (a week-long series), achieving ratios of 2:1 and 3:1 from same-sized gears, and closes with a mathematical explanation rooted in epicycloids and epicyclic geometry.
