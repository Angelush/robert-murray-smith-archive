## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates the Hofler tube — a thermoacoustic engine developed in the 1980s and used by NASA in refrigeration — showing it is far simpler and more forgiving to build than earlier thermoacoustic designs. He traces the history from Rijke and Sondhauss tubes through to the Hofler tube, builds progressively more refined versions, and successfully generates a small voltage by coupling one to a speaker coil, working toward mounting such an engine on a rocket stove.
