## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores sand batteries as a practical and cheap method of thermal energy storage, demonstrating a DIY version using a kettle heating element buried in sand, and building a rocket stove with a sand-filled gas cylinder as a mass heater. He argues that storing energy as heat — rather than in lithium batteries — is underutilised despite being one of the oldest and most cost-effective solutions, noting the concept predates the Finnish commercialisation by at least 40 years.
