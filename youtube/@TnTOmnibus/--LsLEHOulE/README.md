## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores biomimicry in wind turbine design, focusing on the Samara seed (sycamore/maple winged seed) as a blade shape for a small wind turbine. He scans a real sycamore seed, 3D prints it as a three-blade turbine, then refines the blade profile using the free sculpting tool SculptGL. He also investigates whether adding whale-fin-inspired tubercles (nodules along the leading edge) to the blade improves efficiency, drawing on research by marine biologist Frank Fish.
