## Overview

This video explores moisture-enabled electricity generation (MEG), where damp cellulose-based paper or graphene oxide films generate electric current through the migration of hydrogen ions across a moisture gradient. Robert demonstrates a basic working experiment using aluminium sheets and paper moistened by breath, then discusses a more advanced graphene oxide-based self-recharging battery developed by Australian company Strategic Elements with CSIRO and the University of New South Wales. The main takeaway is that these devices are surprisingly simple and cheap to fabricate, with significant potential for powering small electronics and wearable skin patches.
