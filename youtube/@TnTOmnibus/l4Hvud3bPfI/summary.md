## Overview
"Man In A Shed – Episode 6" is a wide-ranging episode in which Robert covers five distinct science and engineering topics: a 3D-printed dual-input planetary gearbox that acts as an electronically controlled continuously variable transmission (CVT); a critical look at the Betaray spherical solar concentrator alongside hands-on optics experiments; a smart adaptive roofing tile that uses wax motors to switch between super-black and super-white surfaces; the surprising resurgence of vertical axis wind turbines (VAWTs); the conversion of HDPE plastic into spider silk via bacterial fermentation; and a humorous but sobering history of animals used as weapons of war. The overarching theme is that clever mechanical and biological simplicity often outperforms expensive high-tech solutions.

## Key Topics
- Explanation of planetary gear systems: components, operating modes, and ratio calculations
- 3D-printed dual-input planetary gearbox with external ring-gear teeth, enabling a CVT
- Self-regulating wind turbine speed control using an idler gear as a passive feedback mechanism
- Betaray spherical solar concentrator (André Broessel / Rawlemon) — critical review of its claimed 57% efficiency
- Hands-on experiments: bullseye car-headlight lens and fluorescent-acrylic waveguide applied to a small solar cell
- Adaptive roofing tile from UC Santa Barbara: wax motor + super-black + super-white paint, claimed 2.5× reduction in heating/cooling energy
- History and economics of horizontal axis wind turbines (HAWTs) — materials limits, cost per MWh, degradation rates
- Revival of vertical axis wind turbines (VAWTs): SeaTwirl prototype, 15% efficiency gain from clustering multiple VAWTs
- Destructive distillation of HDPE plastic into wax/fuel
- Bacterial fermentation of HDPE-derived wax into spider-silk protein (Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute research)
- Historical use of animals in warfare: Soviet tank dogs, American bat bombs (Project X-Ray), Project Pigeon (B.F. Skinner)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **HDPE (high-density polyethylene)** — milk bottles and plastic bags used as feedstock for destructive distillation into wax and spider silk precursor
- **Strontium titanium trioxide** — catalyst used to lower the temperature required for HDPE pyrolysis
- **Bacterial culture ("pamone orosa" / likely *Pseudomonas* sp.)** — ferments HDPE-derived wax into silk-like protein over 72 hours
- **Vantablack** — ultra-black material absorbing 99.96% of light; used conceptually as the "super black" surface in the roofing tile
- **Radiative cooling white paint** (Purdue University recipe, also replicated by Nighthawk-in-light and Tech Ingredients) — reflects heat back into space; the "super white" component
- **Wax (in wax motors)** — expands 5–20% on melting to drive a linear actuator; sourced from washing machine/dishwasher components
- **Acrylic filled with fluorescent material** — acts as a luminescent solar concentrator / light waveguide
- **Optical glass bullseye lens** — hemispherical lens salvaged from a car headlight, used to concentrate ambient light onto a solar cell
- **Copper pipe** — condenser coil in the plastic distillation setup
- **Lithium battery** — noted inside cheap £2 solar fence lights used as the solar cell source

## Techniques and Methods
- **3D printing in Tinkercad** — all planetary gear components, idler gear, cradle, and drive gears designed and printed
- **Dual-input planetary gear assembly** — ring gear given external teeth so it can be independently driven, creating a CVT with two inputs and one output
- **Passive idler-gear feedback loop** — same-tooth-count idler reverses ring-gear rotation so faster blade spin automatically slows the output, no electronics needed
- **Destructive distillation (pyrolysis) of HDPE** — plastic heated in a sealed paint can; vapour condensed through a copper coil to yield wax
- **Catalytic pyrolysis** — strontium titanium trioxide catalyst reduces required temperature for HDPE breakdown
- **Bacterial fermentation** — HDPE-derived wax incubated with bacteria for 72 hours; freeze-dried product spun into silk thread
- **Concentrated photovoltaics (CPV) experiment** — bullseye lens placed over solar cell; voltage measured with multimeter before and after
- **Luminescent solar concentrator experiment** — fluorescent acrylic panel held over solar cell in darkened room to demonstrate wavelength down-conversion
- **Wax motor actuation** — thermal expansion of wax drives a pivot-and-linkage to flip a tile between black and white faces
