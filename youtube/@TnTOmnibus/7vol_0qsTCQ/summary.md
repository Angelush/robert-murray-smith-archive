## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds and iterates on a flat wind turbine design inspired by Finnish artist Simon Grippenberg, incorporating "feather" flaps that passively adjust drag as the turbine rotates. He combines this with rim-mounted magnets and a serpentine coil to create a simple generator, and adds magnetic levitation bearings to reduce friction. The video demonstrates the turbine generating enough voltage to light an LED and produce measurable open-circuit voltage (~2.5–5V).

## Key Topics
- Discovery of Simon Grippenberg's YouTube channel as inspiration
- Construction of a prototype flat/vertical-axis wind turbine using feather flaps
- Salvaging brushless DC motors from old DVD/CD drives to use as generators
- 3D-printed turbine design ("feather generator") available on Tinkercad
- Serpentine coil winding for a flat generator
- Rim-mounted magnet generation vs. axle-mounted generation (speed vs. torque trade-off)
- Magnetic levitation (maglev) bearings to reduce friction and extend spin-down time
- Community-contributed redesign of the feather blade (by "k-rex" / Dursan) with ~3× larger surface area
- Using open-circuit voltage as a relative performance metric during development

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Fiberglass rod** — used as the pivoting blade axle in the initial prototype
- **Aluminium sheet** — bent to form the blades of the first prototype
- **Tyvek (stiff cloth alternative)** — used for feather flaps in the CD-motor version
- **Super glue** — adhering Tyvek/cloth to the CD wheel
- **Hair-thin magnet wire** — wound into the serpentine generator coil
- **Neodymium magnets (1 cm × 2 mm)** — 20 magnets, alternating N-S, mounted on the turbine rim
- **Ceramic ring magnets** — sourced from speakers or Amazon, used for magnetic levitation bearings
- **CDs / CD spindle motors (brushless DC)** — salvaged from old DVD/CD players for use as generators
- **Three-phase rectifier** — used to convert three-phase AC output of brushless motor to DC

## Techniques and Methods
- Salvaging brushless DC motors from discarded DVD/CD players and converting them to generators by soldering wires to the three motor terminals and adding a three-phase rectifier
- Winding a serpentine coil around a wooden board with bolts as a former, then transferring it to a 3D-printed coil former
- 3D printing turbine components (wheel, hub, feather blades) in Tinkercad; STL files shared publicly
- Using rim-mounted alternating-polarity magnets passing over a flat serpentine coil for power generation
- Magnetic levitation bearing using two ceramic ring magnets in repulsion to float the rotor axle
- Using open-circuit voltage (EMF ∝ rotational velocity) as a quick comparative metric during iterative development
- Fan-based indoor wind speed testing (~1.5 m/s and ~3.4 m/s) and outdoor natural wind testing
