## Overview
Robert builds a large human-powered hamster wheel generator inspired by medieval treadmill cranes and modern "active desk" exercising trends. Using cold molding to construct two large wooden hoops, he assembles the wheel on a rolling sled frame and couples it to a stepper motor via longboard wheels to generate electricity. The finished device produces roughly 50–120 watts — enough to power a games console — demonstrating that human-powered generation is surprisingly practical.

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Plywood strips** — 5 mm thick, ~2.4 m long, cut as laminate layers for cold molding
- **Cascamite (Caskamite)** — waterproof, gap-filling urea-formaldehyde boat builder's glue; primary adhesive choice
- **Gorilla Glue (wood glue)** — mentioned as an easier-to-apply but less tough alternative
- **A stronger unnamed adhesive** — described as "spectacular but difficult to apply"; likely a resorcinol or epoxy type
- **Oil** — applied to the mold as a release agent
- **Acetone** — used to clean oil residue from the wood before finishing
- **Rubber tape** — wrapped around the flexible coupling for grip
- **8 mm steel bar** — axle rod for the sled bearings
- **Longboard wheels** — used as chunky, load-bearing rolling bearings in the sled and as the friction-drive interface to the motor
- **Stepper motor ("Tommy" stepper motor)** — used as the generator
- **LEDs (~35 V load)** and **filament light bulb** — test loads for the generator

## Techniques and Methods
- **Cold molding** — layering thin plywood strips over a rough semicircular former, gluing each layer, and clamping until set to create permanently curved wooden sections
- **Mold preparation** — oiling the former to prevent adhesion; wiping with acetone post-cure
- **Clamping methods** — luggage strap wrapped around the layered strips as a flexible clamp; supplementary screws and G-clamps to start the process
- **Joining semicircles** — two semicircular cold-molded sections joined to form each complete hoop; two hoops form the wheel
- **Friction/contact drive** — stepper motor shaft presses against a longboard wheel which in turn contacts the large wooden wheel, creating a ~100:1 gear ratio (25:1 from big wheel to longboard wheel × 4:1 from longboard wheel to motor shaft)
- **Generator testing** — multimeter measurements of open-circuit voltage and current under resistive and LED loads
