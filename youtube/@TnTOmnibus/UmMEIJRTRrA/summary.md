## Overview
Robert surveys a range of innovative vertical axis and alternative wind turbine designs, covering the O-Wind tetrahedron turbine, the Harmony furling Savonius turbine, crossflow turbines (including the Ridge Blade), Fibonacci spiral turbines, kite-based airborne wind generation, and triboelectric nanogenerators (TENG) for wind energy. He 3D-prints and bench-tests several of these designs himself. His central conclusion is that no single wind energy technology suits all environments or users — diversity of approach, matched to local conditions and resources, is the real path forward.

## Key Topics
- O-Wind turbine: James Dyson Award-winning tetrahedral VAWT designed to capture wind from any direction
- O-Wind patent analysis (US Patent 2021/0349530 A1) and 3D-printed model demonstration
- Savonius turbine basics: differential drag, efficiency (~15% historically, ~30% per recent research)
- Harmony wind turbine: furling Savonius design by Christopher Mo, allowing variable blade pitch to operate across a wider wind speed range
- Crossflow wind turbines: history (Paul Mortier 1891, Banki-Mitchell, Ossberger), design parameters, advantages and limitations
- Ridge Blade: a commercial rooftop crossflow turbine
- Fibonacci/conical spiral turbines: research results showing up to 73.1% power coefficient; inspiration for the Liam F1 turbine
- Types of VAWT: Darrieus and Savonius subtypes
- Types of spirals: Fibonacci, logarithmic, golden ratio, Archimedes
- Kite-based airborne wind energy: history of kites, two generation modes (drum/rope lift-cycle and rotating tether), scalability, Sky Sails Power example
- Triboelectric nanogenerators (TENG) for wind energy: flutter/flapping and rotating-disc approaches
- FLAG project (Flex Linear Aerostatic Generator): Canadian triboelectric flag generator
- Philosophical argument for energy diversity over single-solution optimisation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Steel bar (8 mm)** — used as the axis of rotation in the 3D-printed O-Wind model
- **608 skate bearings (22 mm × 8 mm × 7 mm)** — fitted in the O-Wind model foot for rotation
- **PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene)** — used as one of the two materials in flutter-type triboelectric wind generators
- **Kapton** — paired with PTFE in flexible triboelectric generators to generate high charge and current
- **Aluminium** — deposited as a thin layer on plastic in triboelectric generator prototypes
- **Fiberglass** — noted in the context of conventional wind tower construction and its long-term waste problem
- **Concrete** — noted as a large material input for conventional wind tower foundations
- **Plastic (3D-printed PLA/filament implied)** — used for all printed turbine components

## Techniques and Methods
- **3D printing with TinkerCAD** — used to design and fabricate the O-Wind model, Harmony turbine components, and conical spiral turbine
- **Patent analysis** — O-Wind US patent examined to understand the two-layer fin and Venturi-effect channel structure
- **Hair dryer wind testing** — benchtop test to verify unidirectional rotation of O-Wind and Savonius models under airflow from any direction
- **Gear-driven furling mechanism** — motor-controlled cog system driving pinion arms to open/close Harmony turbine blades with wind speed
- **Fibonacci spiral blade construction** — flat sheet cut to spiral profile then rolled into a cone; three blades placed at 120° to form conical spiral turbine
- **Capacitor-based energy storage** — recommended method for accumulating triboelectric charge output for practical use
- **Rectification circuit** — used in both the FLAG generator and rotating-disc TENG designs to convert AC triboelectric output to usable DC
- **Flutter/flapping triboelectric generation** — two dissimilar plastic sheets rubbing as they vibrate in wind
- **Rotating-disc triboelectric generation** — two electrode discs with low-friction plastic contact rotating in wind, no electromagnetic component
