## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the global fresh water scarcity problem and demonstrates that the atmosphere contains six times more water than all the world's rivers combined. He surveys both historical and modern methods of atmospheric water generation — from ancient Incan fog catchers to cutting-edge absorbent gels — and builds a simple working proof-of-concept device using a salvaged Peltier mini-fridge to condense water directly from the air.
