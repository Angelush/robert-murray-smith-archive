## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores earth batteries — devices using buried electrodes to generate electricity — covering their history from Galvani and Volta through to modern research. He investigates the central debate over their mechanism (galvanic corrosion, telluric currents, or exoelectrogenic bacteria) through a live outdoor experiment testing various electrode material combinations. A key practical finding is that isolating each cell in a short section of plastic pipe allows cells to be connected in series to increase voltage, something normally prevented by the earth acting as a shared electrolyte.
