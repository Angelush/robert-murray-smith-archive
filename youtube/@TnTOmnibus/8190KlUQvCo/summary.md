## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a flywheel generator from scratch, combining a concrete-filled bicycle wheel flywheel with a novel single-coil alternator design derived from a salvaged microwave turntable motor. The video demonstrates that a single large toroidal coil wound around a slotted steel bicycle rim, paired with neodymium magnets on the spinning flywheel, can generate usable voltage (11V, ~4mA), validating the concept as a simpler alternative to winding many individual coils.

## Key Topics
- What flywheels are and how they function as mechanical batteries (energy storage analogy)
- Building a small flywheel from wooden discs, iron powder, and resin
- Building a large flywheel from a bicycle wheel filled with postcrete (concrete)
- Concrete curing science: crystal growth, strength vs. hardness, and sealing
- Balancing a flywheel after casting
- Disassembly and analysis of a microwave oven synchronous motor to understand flux-path geometry
- Scaling the microwave motor coil concept to a bicycle wheel rim
- Interdigitating finger (claw-pole) stator design using slotted and bent steel rim teeth
- Magnet arrangement experiments: alternating N-S vs. half-north/half-south configurations and their effects on voltage vs. current output
- Wire gauge selection (18 SWG vs. 28 SWG) and its effect on voltage/amperage
- Proof-of-concept generator test: lighting an LED panel

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Plywood (thin and thick)** — used for the small flywheel disc construction
- **Iron powder** — mixed with resin to add mass at the rim of the small flywheel
- **Resin** — binder for iron powder fill in drilled holes
- **8mm steel bar** — used as the flywheel axle
- **Speaker magnet (central metal part)** — repurposed as the axle bearing/hub
- **Postcrete / post mix concrete** — used to fill the bicycle wheel rim to form the large flywheel
- **PVA (white glue)** — mixed 50/50 with water as a concrete sealant
- **Silicon bedine rubber (SBRR)** — mentioned as an alternative concrete sealant
- **Concrete sealant** — commercial product option for sealing the flywheel
- **Car body filler** — mentioned as a premium finishing option for the concrete flywheel
- **Fiberglass** — used to glue the slotted rim stator assembly together
- **18 SWG enamel copper wire** — first winding attempt (too thick)
- **28 SWG enamel copper wire** — final winding (~2 kg on a 26-inch wheel), gave better voltage
- **Neodymium magnets (20mm × 2mm)** — attached to the flywheel rim as the rotor poles
- **Ceramic magnets** — used in initial bench test of the stator ring
- **Kapton tape** — used as a camera filter to improve meter readability on film

## Techniques and Methods
- Salvaging a speaker magnet assembly as a precision axle hub
- Cutting and laminating plywood discs; drilling 30mm holes to fill with weighted material
- Casting iron powder/resin composite into disc holes for edge weighting
- Removing tire, gear cluster, and axle from a bicycle wheel to prepare a mold
- Using gaffer tape to seal one side of the bicycle rim as a concrete casting mold
- Casting postcrete into the bicycle wheel rim and curing (10–15 min hardening; full cure over days)
- Balancing a flywheel by finding the heavy point and removing material with a wire brush or chisel
- Sealing concrete with PVA/water mix, SBRR, or commercial concrete sealant
- Disassembling a microwave synchronous motor to study claw-pole flux-path geometry
- Cutting slots in a steel bicycle rim and bending alternating teeth in opposite directions to create interdigitating claw-pole stator fingers
- Bonding the slotted rim with fiberglass
- Winding a single large coil around the full circumference of the stator ring
- Bench-testing the stator with hand-pushed ceramic magnets before flywheel integration
- Mounting the stator ring around the spinning flywheel in a wooden cradle
- Measuring output with a multimeter (voltage and milliamp modes)
- Testing alternating N-S vs. half-north/half-south magnet arrangements to tune output characteristics
- Connecting output to an LED panel as a functional load test
