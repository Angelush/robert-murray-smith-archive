## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a flywheel generator from scratch, combining a concrete-filled bicycle wheel flywheel with a novel single-coil alternator design derived from a salvaged microwave turntable motor. The video demonstrates that a single large toroidal coil wound around a slotted steel bicycle rim, paired with neodymium magnets on the spinning flywheel, can generate usable voltage (11V, ~4mA), validating the concept as a simpler alternative to winding many individual coils.
