## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) generator using ceramic magnets, copper wire, and a blowtorch flame as a conductive plasma. He achieves measurable voltage output (~0.6V) by seeding the flame with potassium nitrate solution to increase ion density, then extends the experiment by replacing the magnetic field with a high-voltage electrostatic field, producing even more impressive results (~2V under load).
