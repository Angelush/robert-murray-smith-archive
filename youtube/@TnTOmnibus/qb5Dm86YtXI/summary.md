## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) generator using ceramic magnets, copper wire, and a blowtorch flame as a conductive plasma. He achieves measurable voltage output (~0.6V) by seeding the flame with potassium nitrate solution to increase ion density, then extends the experiment by replacing the magnetic field with a high-voltage electrostatic field, producing even more impressive results (~2V under load).

## Key Topics
- Faraday's principle: conductors moving through magnetic fields generate current
- MHD (magnetohydrodynamic) generation — motors and generators with no moving parts
- Historical MHD experiments: Faraday measuring current in the Thames (1851), the Japanese Yamato-1 boat, Yugoslav and Japanese industrial MHD generators (50–100 MW)
- MHD efficiency (~60%) vs. conventional turbines (~30–35%)
- Open vs. closed MHD systems; MHD as a "topping cycle" for existing power plants
- Ion seeding to lower required operating temperature
- Thermionic effect in flames (baseline, without magnetic field)
- Electrostatic field as an alternative to magnetic field for flame-based generation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Ceramic/ferrite magnets** — salvaged from hospital pipe demineralising units; ~2 cm × 5 cm × 1 cm
- **Steel plates** — 125 mm square, 1 mm thick; used as magnet carriers and field plates
- **Copper wire** — 18 SWG / 2.5 mm² cross-section, stripped from housing cable; used as electrodes
- **Super glue** — for bonding magnets to steel plates
- **Potassium nitrate (KNO₃)** — dissolved as a saturated solution; used as an ion seed sprayed into the flame
- **Tungsten / Nichrome (NiCr)** — mentioned as superior electrode materials for high-temperature use; nichrome sourced from "killing wire"
- **Salty water** — mentioned as an alternative conductive fluid for MHD
- **Inert gases** — mentioned for use in closed-loop MHD systems to improve efficiency
- **Wood** — noted to naturally contain significant potassium (relevant for rocket stove integration)

## Techniques and Methods
- **MHD generator construction**: stacking ceramic magnets on opposing-polarity steel plates, with copper wire electrodes arranged in series triangles at 90° to the magnetic field
- **Flame plasma generation**: blowtorch used to create a low-grade plasma as the working fluid
- **Ion seeding via wick atomiser**: saturated KNO₃ solution drawn up a zipper wick in a glass tube and sprayed into the flame to increase ion density and lower effective operating temperature
- **Electrostatic field generation**: high-voltage DC generator (12V in → ~20 kV out) connected to the steel plates instead of magnets, creating an electrostatic field across the flame channel
- **Under-load measurement**: 220 Ω resistor placed in series to measure real power output (current × voltage)
- **Picture-in-picture camera setup**: phone camera pointed at multimeter overlaid on main video feed for live readings
