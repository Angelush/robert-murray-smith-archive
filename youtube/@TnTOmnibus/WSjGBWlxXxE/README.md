## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a cheap and simple vertical-axis wind turbine (VAWT) using an LP record and a strip of tyvek house wrap, tracing its conceptual lineage from thousand-year-old Persian windmills through Finnish artist Simon Gripenberg to Dr. Robert D. Hunt's patented flat-plate drag turbine. He also demonstrates a DIY hydrostatic thrust bearing made from a plastic bottle and a pipe of water, offering a near-frictionless, near-free solution to one of the key mechanical challenges in VAWT construction. The video touches on the "free energy" controversy that dogged Dr. Hunt's gravity plane concept, using it as a springboard to discuss scope-of-system thinking.
