## Overview
This video explores the aerodynamic benefits of whale tubercles (the bumpy nodules on humpback whale flippers) and applies them to 3D-printed wind turbine and fan blades. Robert builds and compares blades with and without tubercles across several configurations — a sycamore-seed wind turbine, a toroidal fan, and a vertical-axis wind turbine (VAWT) — demonstrating measurable improvements in airflow, efficiency, and noise. He also introduces SculptGL as an accessible 3D sculpting tool for cleaning up scanned blade models.

## Key Topics
- History and science of whale tubercles: marine biologist Frank Fish and engineer Philip Watts' research showing 15–20% improvement in lift and drag reduction
- Patent implications: patents cover commercial use only, leaving private/educational use open
- 3D printing and testing tubercle-covered blades vs. smooth blades side-by-side
- Introduction to **SculptGL** as an intuitive, browser-based 3D sculpting tool for cleaning up scanned STL files
- Fan blade testing: toroidal (petal) fan blade with and without tubercles, measuring airspeed and amp draw
- Water turbine demonstration using a garden hose on a tubercle blade mounted to a PC fan generator
- Vertical-axis wind turbines (VAWTs): design types (Darrieus, Savonius, helical), advantages/disadvantages, and tubercle integration
- VAWT RPM comparison: 100 RPM without tubercles vs. 149 RPM with tubercles
- Biomimicry as a design philosophy

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA/3D-printed filament** — used to print all blade prototypes
- **Steel rod** — axle for bearing mounts
- **Skateboard bearings** (22mm × 8mm × 7mm) — used in VAWT cradle assembly
- **Cyanoacrylate (superglue)** — for assembling VAWT components
- **Plaster of Paris** — mentioned as an option for mold-making from clay models
- **Clay** (natural, dug from a hill) — used to sculpt tubercle models for scanning
- **Plasticine / Milliput** — mentioned as alternative modeling materials
- **Reflective aluminium strip** — used as tachometer target on VAWT rotor
- **3V battery pack** — power source for fan blade motor tests

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing blade prototypes from Thingiverse files (STL format)
- Scanning physical clay models with a 3D scanner to produce digital STL files
- Using **SculptGL** (browser-based sculpting tool) to smooth, inflate, pinch, flatten, and clean up scanned blade geometry
- Side-by-side wind testing on a hillside (qualitative)
- Hand-drill anemometer test: measuring airspeed (m/s) and amp draw (mA) for fan blade comparison
- Digital tachometer (RPM counter using reflective strip) for VAWT speed comparison
- Converting a PC fan into a generator (removing blades/electronics, soldering to coils)
- Physical clay sculpting of tubercles, scanning, then printing
- Mold-making with plaster of Paris (described as alternative to scanning)
- VAWT assembly: 3-blade rotor with coupler rods, washer spacers, and skateboard bearings in a printed cradle
