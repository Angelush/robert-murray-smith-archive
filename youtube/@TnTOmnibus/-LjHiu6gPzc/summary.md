## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores electrostatic generation as an alternative to traditional coil-and-magnet generators, replicating a Hackaday project that uses a variable capacitor to harvest energy. He builds a Bennett doubler-style electrostatic generator using acrylic discs and aluminium foil, then replaces the metal plates with his homemade graphite-based conductive ink — achieving comparable or better results, and demonstrating that triboelectric pre-charging significantly boosts output.

## Key Topics
- Electrostatic induction as an alternative to electromagnetic generation
- Replication of a Canadian Hackaday project using a flapping thin-film variable capacitor
- Construction of a Bennett doubler electrostatic generator from acrylic and aluminium foil
- Homemade graphite conductive ink as a replacement for metal plates
- Diode rectification circuits for harvesting electrostatic energy
- Triboelectric (tribo) electricity and its role in boosting generator output
- Pre-charging strategies using rubbing materials and small batteries
- Future directions: printed/painted electrostatic generators and fractal capacitors

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Sheet acrylic** — used for the main rotor disc and upright supports
- **Aluminium foil (HVAC-grade, adhesive-backed)** — used as conductive capacitor plates
- **Ball bearings** — used in the axle uprights
- **Graphite (5-micron flake)** — active conductive material in the homemade ink; sourced as lock lubricant powder
- **Gum arabic** — binder for the conductive ink (100 g per litre of water); also used in watercolours
- **Water** — carrier for the ink
- **Glycerin** — plasticiser added to the ink (3 ml)
- **Oil of cloves** — preservative for the gum arabic binder (1 ml)
- **Listerine** — alternative preservative for gum arabic (~5 ml)
- **Microwave oven capacitor (1 µF, 2 kV)** — used as the storage capacitor in the circuit
- **Microwave oven diodes** — used in the rectification circuit
- **Carbon felt** — tested as a triboelectric rubbing material; produced ~6 V pre-charge
- **Cotton wool** — tested as a triboelectric rubbing material; produced ~4 V
- **Wool felt, rubber (EPDM), PVC, HDPE, man-made fibres, waxed paper** — various materials tested for triboelectric contribution
- **AA battery (1.5 V)** — used for electrical pre-charging of the capacitor

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting acrylic discs with a large hole saw
- Building axle bosses by laminating smaller acrylic discs to prevent wobble
- Applying adhesive aluminium foil to acrylic discs to form capacitor plates
- Wiring a diode rectification circuit (microwave oven diodes) to harvest charge
- Monitoring output voltage across a storage capacitor with a multimeter
- Formulating graphite conductive ink (hot-water dissolution of gum arabic, addition of graphite, heating and stirring, optional wet-grinder milling for 1 hour)
- Painting conductive ink onto acrylic plates as a metal-free replacement
- Triboelectric pre-charging by rubbing various materials against the spinning disc
- Electrical pre-charging using a small battery touched briefly to the capacitor
- Hand-spinning and drill-driving the rotor for comparative output measurements
