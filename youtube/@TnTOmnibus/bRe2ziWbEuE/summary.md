## Overview
"Man In A Shed - Episode 4" is a wide-ranging science and maker episode in which Robert covers four largely independent topics: converting coffee grounds into concrete-strengthening biochar, designing a 3D-printed wind flutter energy harvester, explaining and demonstrating nuclear (alpha-voltaic) batteries, and exploring the history of unconventional engine fuels culminating in hand-mixed gunpowder and a 3D-printed compressed air engine. The overarching theme is that inexpensive, low-tech approaches can reproduce cutting-edge or historically interesting science.

## Key Topics
- Coffee ground biochar as a concrete additive (up to 30% strength increase)
- History and chemistry of Roman pozzolanic concrete
- Wind energy harvesting via flutter, vortex shedding, galloping, and wind-induced vibration
- 3D-printed integral-hinge flutter energy harvesting device
- Betavolt BV100 nuclear battery (nickel-63 + diamond semiconductor)
- History of nuclear/radioisotope batteries from 1950s pacemakers to Bristol/Russian carbon-14 diamond batteries
- DIY alpha-voltaic nuclear battery demonstration using americium-241 and a transistor
- Historical unconventional engine fuels: acetylene, ethanol, coal dust, liquid air, ammonia, compressed air, gunpowder
- Gunpowder chemistry, manufacture, and burn-rate tuning
- Charlie Macklin's gunpowder-powered oscillating steam engine
- 3D-printed single-acting compressed air engine (minimal-part design)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Coffee grounds** — feedstock pyrolysed into biochar for concrete additive
- **Biochar** — porous carbon-rich charcoal formed by pyrolysis; replaces 15% of sand by volume in concrete
- **Activated carbon** — used as an oxygen-absorbing layer during the pyrolysis kiln process
- **Pozzolan (pozzolanic ash)** — volcanic alumino-silicate used by Romans; reacts with calcium hydroxide to form insoluble hydrates
- **Calcium hydroxide / lime** — reacts with pozzolan in the presence of water
- **Calcium silicate hydrate / calcium aluminate hydrate** — reaction products that give Roman concrete its durability
- **Nickel-63** — radioactive isotope (beta emitter) used in Betavolt BV100 nuclear battery; decays to harmless copper
- **Single-crystal diamond** — semiconductor layers in the BV100 (10-micron layers sandwiching 2-micron Ni-63)
- **Carbon-14** — radioactive graphite produced in nuclear reactors; used by Bristol University to grow radioactive diamond sheets for a battery
- **Americium-241** — alpha-emitting radioisotope inside ionisation-type smoke alarms; used in the DIY nuclear battery demo; half-life ~432 years
- **2N3055 transistor** — NPN silicon transistor used as the semiconductor junction in the DIY alpha-voltaic demo
- **Potassium nitrate (saltpeter)** — oxidiser in gunpowder (75% by weight); food-grade, also sold as stump remover
- **Sulfur** — fuel and ignition-temperature reducer in gunpowder (10% by weight)
- **Softwood charcoal (pine)** — carbon fuel component of gunpowder (15% by weight); preferred over pure carbon
- **Graphite** — tumbled onto corned gunpowder grains to prevent moisture ingress and static build-up
- **Acetylene** — historically explored engine fuel; dangerous above 20 psi and forms explosive copper acetylide in brass/copper pipes
- **Copper acetylide** — percussive explosive formed when acetylene contacts copper or brass
- **Ethanol** — early external-combustion engine working fluid; expansion-engine variant predates internal combustion use
- **Plutonium** — used in 1970s nuclear pacemaker batteries; ~88-year lifespan

## Techniques and Methods
- **Pyrolysis of coffee grounds** — heating to ~350 °C (660 °F) inside a sealed pot filled with activated carbon to exclude oxygen; kiln runs ~4 hours
- **Biochar-concrete mixing** — replacing 15% of sand by volume with biochar yields ~30% stronger concrete and permits use of non-spherical/desert sand
- **3D printing with integral living hinge** — single-print flutter device; no post-print assembly required; ~15p cost in plastic and electricity
- **Flutter energy harvesting** — flexible 3D-printed structure captures bursty wind, foot traffic vibration, or bridge/roadside flutter; energy extraction options: piezoelectric, electrostatic (variable capacitor), magnetic induction
- **Alpha-voltaic battery construction** — americium-241 source from disassembled smoke alarm pointed at the exposed NP junction of a decapped 2N3055 transistor; demonstrates current generation without light
- **Beta-voltaic / diamond battery fabrication (described)** — sublimation of radioactive graphite, CVD (chemical vapour deposition) to grow radioactive diamond sheets, then sandwiching with a semiconductor
- **Gunpowder manufacture** — grinding each of the three components separately (blender for charcoal, mortar-and-pestle/grinder for KNO₃), drying hygroscopic KNO₃ in oven at ~120 °C, then gently tumble-mixing in sealed plastic cups (75 KNO₃ : 15 charcoal : 10 sulfur by weight)
- **Wet corning (described)** — wetting mixed powder with alcohol, milling with non-sparking Limestone or lead media, pressing into cake, sieving into grains of controlled size
- **Graphite tumbling** — coating corned grains in graphite to prevent moisture absorption and static
- **3D-printed compressed air engine assembly** — piston-barrel, piston rod with hook, rotary valve spigot, flywheel with M8 nut inserts for added mass; minimal post-print fitting/sanding
- **IC engine to air-engine conversion (described)** — replacing combustion valve with a slide valve to allow compressed air operation
