## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reflects on the phenomenon of "users" — people who shamelessly try to take advantage of others' generosity, particularly in the context of open-source work and collaboration. Using historical parallels (the Tragedy of the Commons, the Enclosure Acts) and everyday examples (motorway behaviour), he argues that selfishness at the individual level degrades outcomes for everyone. His main takeaway is that users are not hard to identify and should be firmly dealt with.
