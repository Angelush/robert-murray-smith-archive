## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith responds to viewer comments questioning why he spends time on projects like low-power air conditioning instead of focusing solely on batteries and supercapacitors. He explains that his view of "the energy problem" has evolved from storage-only, to generation-plus-storage, to a three-part framework encompassing energy generation, storage, and *use*. The video's key takeaway is that his diverse projects share a consistent underlying mission: addressing energy consumption as seriously as energy production.

## Key Topics
- Evolution of Robert's personal thinking on the energy problem
- Energy generation as a core challenge (moving away from burning fossil fuels)
- Energy storage (batteries, supercapacitors) as one piece of a larger puzzle
- Energy *use* and efficiency as an underappreciated third dimension
- Air conditioning as a case study in wasteful energy consumption (~20% of building electrical use, ~10% of global electrical use)
- His low-power fan/air conditioner project (~0.5 W vs. ~10 W equivalent) as a practical example of energy-use reduction
- The role of fun and approachability in science communication and creative work
