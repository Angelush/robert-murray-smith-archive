## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of control in science, using the evolution of pig farming as a central metaphor. He argues that traditional science — like intensive farming — has been defined by a desire to forcibly control nature, which is both brutal and inefficient. The video's key takeaway is that a shift is underway toward *working with* nature rather than against it, yielding better outcomes with far less energy.

## Key Topics
- The history and critique of control as the defining impulse of modern science
- Intensive pig farming vs. free-range farming as a metaphor for control vs. collaboration with nature
- The Jurassic Park / Ian Malcolm quote on science as "rape of the natural world"
- Tesla's concept of "working with the wheelwork of nature"
- Low-temperature differential energy systems (combined heat and power) as an example of working with nature
- Robert's desalination video series as a practical application of the same philosophy
