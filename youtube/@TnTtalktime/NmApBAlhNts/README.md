## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reflects on his philosophy of experimentation and learning, emphasising that you don't need to be an expert before trying something new. He describes himself as a curious, self-taught tinkerer who reads research papers, gives ideas a go with basic materials, and relies on community feedback to improve his work. The core takeaway is that doing and learning happen together — action matters more than prior knowledge.
