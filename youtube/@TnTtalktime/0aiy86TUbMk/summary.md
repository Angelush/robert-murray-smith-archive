## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explains how tank circuits (LC circuits) work by exploring the energy exchange between capacitors and inductors. He draws an analogy between LC oscillation and a pendulum's simple harmonic motion, highlighting that the oscillation frequency depends only on component values, not energy stored. The video concludes with practical applications including AC signal stabilization, filtering, and radio receivers.

## Key Topics
- What a tank circuit is (inductor + capacitor, i.e. LC circuit)
- How capacitors store energy as an electric field (potential/voltage) and inductors as a magnetic field (kinetic current)
- The energy exchange cycle between capacitor and inductor producing self-sustaining AC oscillation
- Damping of oscillation due to stray resistance losses
- Analogy between LC oscillation and a pendulum (simple harmonic motion / SHM)
- Frequency independence from stored energy — rate depends only on L and C values
- Applications: AC oscillator stabilization, impedance filtering, radio receiver tuning
- Tesla's use of tank circuits in bifilar coils and high-voltage generators

## Techniques and Methods
- Connecting a capacitor and inductor directly to observe natural resonant oscillation
- Applying a sudden voltage (e.g. from a battery) to initiate the charge/discharge cycle
- Using LC circuits as frequency-stable oscillators
- Using resonant circuits as frequency-selective filters (high or low impedance at target frequencies)
- Radio receiver tuning via resonance
