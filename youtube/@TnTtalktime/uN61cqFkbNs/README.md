## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith presents a wide-ranging philosophical discussion on genetics, intelligence, learning, and the philosophy of science, prompted by his wife's suggestion after his earlier creativity video. He argues that genes play a far smaller role in development than commonly assumed — it is the environment that primarily shapes both body and mind — and extends this into a broader thesis that intelligence is not a property of individuals but an emergent quality of the entire world system, of which we are all expressions.
