## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith reflects on his experience with negative comments and trolls on YouTube, sharing practical mental frameworks for dealing with online criticism. His central message is that negative commenters are statistically rare, that their remarks reflect more about themselves than about the creator, and that understanding this should breed empathy rather than anger.

## Key Topics
- The statistical distribution of YouTube comments (bell curve analogy)
- Keeping negative feedback in proportion relative to overall positive engagement
- The psychology of negative commenters — projecting their own insecurities
- The "gatekeeping" view of science vs. science as open methodology
- Creativity as building on others' work ("standing on the shoulders of giants")
- Developing resilience as a content creator

## Techniques and Methods
- Statistical framing (binomial/bell curve distribution) applied to audience comment analysis
- Reframing negative comments by modelling the commenter's motivations
- Using the ratio of positive to negative feedback as a gauge of perspective
