## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith draws a crucial distinction between "experts" (people) and "expertise" (accumulated knowledge), arguing that society's crisis of trust in experts stems from conflating the two. He contends that real expertise deserves deep respect, but experts themselves often undermine their own credibility through condescension and jargon, while a separate problem — "empty suit" experts — further erodes public trust in genuinely knowledgeable people.
