## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith debunks the "self-charging car" marketing term used by manufacturers like Lexus, exposing it as simply a rebranded hybrid (equivalent to a 20-year-old Toyota Prius). He argues that neither clever battery branding nor improved battery technology alone can solve the energy transition challenge — the energy must come from somewhere — and that the real solution requires addressing all three sides of the problem: batteries, generation, and usage.
