## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reflects on the nature of creativity, arguing that it is not a rare gift but a skill that can be developed like a muscle. He critiques the "sledgehammer" approach to idea generation popularised by Edison and Linus Pauling, favouring instead a mindset of active observation and noticing the world around you. His central takeaway is that creativity flourishes through self-belief, a supportive community, and the habit of truly paying attention.
