## Overview
Robert shares that his wife recently suggested he has adult ADHD, after watching a TV programme about it. He reflects on the diagnosis with characteristic good humour, noting that rather than seeing it as a problem, he views the traits — hyper-focus, unconventional thinking, and the ability to make unexpected connections — as advantages that have driven his prolific creative output.

## Key Topics
- Robert's introduction to the concept of adult ADHD via his wife
- His initial (limited) understanding of ADHD as a childhood condition
- Specific traits he recognises in himself: hyper-focus, social disengagement when concentrating, discomfort with disruption to routine
- His ambivalence about being "labelled"
- Reframing ADHD as an advantage rather than a disorder
- The ability to link apparently unrelated ideas as a defining ADHD trait
- Suspicion that his collaborator Luke shares similar traits
