## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith addresses a recurring question from his audience: how much electricity does a wind turbine generate? He explains that generation output is not the correct metric for evaluating a rotor's performance — torque on the shaft is. The video clarifies the physics of wind power, including the Betz limit, and why claiming output beyond ~30% of available wind energy is likely misleading.

## Key Topics
- Why wind turbine output is bounded by the energy available in the wind
- How wind power relates to wind speed and swept area (not density changes)
- Torque as the correct measure of rotor performance
- Generator efficiency (65–80%) and its role in the power chain
- The Betz limit: why you cannot extract 100% of wind energy
- Using a fixed resistive load (filament lamp) as a practical output indicator
- Cost context: his mini generator cost £2

## Techniques and Methods
- Using a hairdryer to simulate wind on a small turbine rotor
- Using a filament lamp (fixed resistor) as a resistive load to better characterize output
- Prony brake measurement (mentioned as the correct method for measuring shaft torque, though not performed)
- Comparative rotor testing using a standardised generator as a fixed yardstick
