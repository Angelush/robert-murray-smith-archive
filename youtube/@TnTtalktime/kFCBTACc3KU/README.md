## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores whether cryptocurrency has genuine value by framing it within the three historical types of money: commodity, representative, and fiat. He concludes that cryptocurrency is essentially fiat money — its value rests entirely on collective belief and trust — and that while the core concept is sound, rampant lack of regulation and widespread scams currently make it untrustworthy and risky. He takes a wait-and-see stance, hoping the market will eventually self-correct.
