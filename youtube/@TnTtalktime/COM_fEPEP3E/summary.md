## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith gives his honest opinion on the "Quantum Magnetics PMMG4 Hybrid Silicon Carbide Nano Battery," a $4,000 device he has never personally tested or handled. He is deeply skeptical of its value and marketing, citing the high price, lack of independent test results, and heavy use of scientific buzzwords as major red flags. His core conclusion is simple: he would not buy it.

## Key Topics
- Overview of the PMMG4 Hybrid Silicon Carbide Nano Battery and its $4,000 price point
- Robert's lack of direct experience with the device and his limitations in evaluating it
- Cost comparison: buying conventional batteries vs. this product
- Comparison to a "glorified Bini SSG" — a device he estimates could be replicated for ~$200
- Absence of independent test data or white papers on the product
- Skepticism toward buzzword-heavy marketing ("Nano," "free energy," "quantum")
- Historical analogy to radium being marketed as a wonder ingredient in the 1950s
- Offer to test the device if the manufacturer sends one
- Mention of his own magnesium battery research as a credible alternative
