## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of "pathological science," a term introduced by Irving Langmuir in 1953 to describe genuine scientists who are deceived by subjective effects, wishful thinking, and barely-detectable phenomena into building elaborate but unfounded theories. Unlike fraud or pseudoscience, pathological scientists sincerely believe they are doing real science. Robert argues this phenomenon is not a relic of the past — he sees its markers in modern research areas like dark energy and cold fusion.
