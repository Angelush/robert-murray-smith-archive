## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reflects on the cyclical nature of societal responses to energy crises, drawing parallels between the 1970s/80s self-sufficiency movement and today. He argues that rather than waiting for a technological "wonder pill" (fusion, thorium, etc.), the real solution lies in changing how we *use* energy — drawing on low-tech, historically-proven approaches. He introduces two key resources he plans to base future projects on: the *Low Tech Magazine* website and the 1977 book *Pedal Power*.
