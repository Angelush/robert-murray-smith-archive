## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith reflects on the cyclical nature of societal responses to energy crises, drawing parallels between the 1970s/80s self-sufficiency movement and today. He argues that rather than waiting for a technological "wonder pill" (fusion, thorium, etc.), the real solution lies in changing how we *use* energy — drawing on low-tech, historically-proven approaches. He introduces two key resources he plans to base future projects on: the *Low Tech Magazine* website and the 1977 book *Pedal Power*.

## Key Topics
- Historical parallels between the 1970s–80s energy/political crisis and current problems
- The self-sufficiency and "make it yourself" movement, then and now
- Critique of waiting for a single technological solution ("wonder pill") to energy problems
- The overlooked side of the energy equation: consumption and usage, not just production
- Pedal power and direct mechanical energy as practical alternatives to electric motors
- Low Tech Magazine (lowtechmagazine.com) as a resource for rediscovered solutions
- The 1977 book *Pedal Power in Work, Leisure and Transportation* by James McCullagh
- Human power output: the "human power unit" (hup) = 75 watts sustained output
- Net-energy-positive housing projects as an example of implementable solutions now
