## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the nature of thinking, arguing that thought is an action that occurs *through* the brain rather than *in* it. Drawing an analogy with walking — which happens in space outside the body, enabled by the legs — he proposes that the brain is the organ of thought, but thinking itself emerges from the interaction between the organism and its environment. This short philosophical video is part of a broader personal framework connecting thought, creativity, and intelligence.

## Key Topics
- The common assumption that thinking occurs inside the brain
- Thinking as an *action* rather than an internal state
- The walking analogy: legs as the organ of walking, brain as the organ of thought
- Thought as an interaction between organism and environment
- The relationship between thinking, intelligence, and creativity
- Reference to related videos on genes and creativity
