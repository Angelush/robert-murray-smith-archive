## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith shares a brief philosophical reflection on experimental methodology, arguing that simplicity is the cornerstone of good science and engineering. Using his ongoing work on graphene-enhanced concrete and graphene-coated sand as concrete examples, he explains why stripping an experiment down to its bare essentials gives it the best chance of working and reaching real-world application. The video is a direct response to viewers who frequently suggest adding complexity to his experiments.
