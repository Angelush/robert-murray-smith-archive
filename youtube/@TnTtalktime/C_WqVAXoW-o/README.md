## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith makes the case that Stirling engines are a seriously underrated technology with a long history of development. Contrary to their reputation as low-power, inefficient, and outdated, modern Stirling engines have demonstrated 45% greater efficiency than conventional petrol engines, quiet operation, and cost-effectiveness up to 100 kW — making them a compelling choice for combined heat and power (CHP) applications. The video concludes by explaining why Robert has chosen the Stirling engine route over alternatives like Peltier devices for his own CHP project.
