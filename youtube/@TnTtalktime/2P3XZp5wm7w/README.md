## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith argues that liquid hydrocarbon fuels remain the most energy-dense portable energy source by a wide margin, and then makes the case that these fuels can be synthesised directly from CO₂ and water vapour captured from the air using a two-step catalytic process. He highlights a recent Swiss pilot-scale solar reactor that successfully produced kerosene-grade jet fuel from air, and argues this pathway could allow the world to continue using existing hydrocarbon infrastructure while achieving net carbon neutrality.
