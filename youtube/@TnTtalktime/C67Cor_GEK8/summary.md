## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the history and future of human waste management, arguing that sewage should be reframed as a "resource flow" rather than something to be flushed and forgotten. He traces attitudes from ancient Rome through Victorian London to the present day, highlighting innovative approaches — particularly from Africa — that treat waste as a valuable source of fuel, fertilizer, and energy. His central conclusion is that with 8 billion people on Earth, closing the loop on waste is no longer optional.

## Key Topics
- History of human waste disposal: from tribal cesspits to Roman sewage systems
- The 1858 Great Stink in London and Joseph Bazalgette's sewer network
- Thomas Crapper's flush toilet and the "flush and forget" mindset
- Reframing sewage as "resource flow" in the modern treatment industry
- Decentralized waste solutions: composting toilets, biodigesters, incinerating toilets
- Centralized solutions: branded fertilizer products (e.g., Milorganite)
- African innovation in waste-to-fuel (Kenya's Sanivation, Rwanda prison biodigester)
- Agricultural waste: ammonia from chickens, cows, and sheep
- Water use statistics: 26.7% of household water goes down the toilet

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Urine** — used historically for bleaching linen and teeth whitening; subject to a Roman urine tax; also collected in the Middle Ages for gunpowder production
- **Methane** — produced by anaerobic biodigesters from fecal waste; used as cooking/heating fuel
- **Fixed nitrogen** — present in human sewage; valuable as fertilizer
- **Phosphates** — present in human sewage; fertilizer component
- **Sawdust / straw** — used as cover material in composting toilets to create aerobic conditions
- **Perlite** — used in the "mulite" (perlite) toilet variant to aid decomposition alongside earthworms
- **Ash** — end product of incinerating toilets; nutrient-rich, approximately one cup per family of four every couple of days
- **Ammonia** — produced in large quantities by agricultural livestock (chickens, cows, sheep)
- **Milorganite** — branded sewage sludge fertilizer product sold commercially in the US

## Techniques and Methods
- **Anaerobic biodigestion** — waste sealed in a tank, bacteria digest it and produce methane gas for fuel (used in Rwanda prison, and at home scale)
- **Solar drying and briquetting** — fecal material collected, sun-dried, and compressed into cooking fuel briquettes (Sanivation, Kenya)
- **Composting toilet** — aerobic decomposition using sawdust or straw as cover; pathogens killed by fungi and bacteria
- **Perlite/vermiculite toilet** — composting toilet variant assisted by earthworms
- **Incinerating toilet** — burns waste to a teaspoon of ash; filtered to prevent odour; suited to boats or space-constrained settings
- **Sewage sludge treatment** — centralised processing of municipal waste into fertiliser products
- **Septic tanks** — time-based pathogen die-off with chemical assistance
