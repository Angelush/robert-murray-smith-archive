## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reflects on the process of turning ideas into practical reality, emphasising that the gap between having an idea and executing it is vast and often underestimated. He argues that success requires breaking ideas down into sub-problems, doing genuine research, acquiring new skills through practice and failure, and doing the work yourself — there are no shortcuts.
