## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith exposes the "energy saver plug" as a scam, explaining that the device is simply a capacitor wired across the live and neutral mains — functionally useless in a domestic setting where inductive loads are minimal. He argues the scam succeeds not because people are foolish, but because it exploits genuine fears about rising energy costs, offering the illusion of an easy fix that requires no lifestyle change.
