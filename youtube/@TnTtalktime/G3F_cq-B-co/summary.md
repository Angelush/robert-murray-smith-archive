## Overview
Robert shares the "Parable of the Chimpanzees" — a story about how learned behaviours persist long after the original reasons for them have vanished. He uses it to argue that conventional wisdom around testing generators (using a resistive load) is not the only valid approach, and that charging a capacitor can reveal the same information.

## Key Topics
- The chimpanzee parable: how groups internalize rules without experiencing the original consequence
- Institutional and collective decision-making based on inherited assumptions
- Testing generator output: resistive load vs. capacitor charging as equivalent methods
- Encouraging critical re-examination of accepted practices

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Capacitors (used to store and measure energy from a generator)
- Resistive load (conventional method for testing generator output)

## Techniques and Methods
- Resistive load testing of generators (conventional approach)
- Capacitor charging from a generator, followed by voltage measurement to determine total energy generated
