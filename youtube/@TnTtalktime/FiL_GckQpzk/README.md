## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reflects on the importance of context when interpreting statistics and scientific claims. Using examples from taxation figures and secondhand smoking cancer rates (drawn from Bill Bryson), he argues that numbers presented without context can be deeply misleading. He applies this lesson directly to graphene, cautioning viewers to look beyond headline claims about its properties.
