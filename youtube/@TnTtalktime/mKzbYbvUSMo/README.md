## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reflects on the philosophy behind his channel, concluding that it has always fundamentally been about learning and inspiration through doing. He argues that kinesthetic learning — learning by physically doing and experimenting — is essential to what makes us human, and that modern education's drift toward purely audio-visual methods has disenfranchised many learners. His channel's experiments and hands-on approach are his way of countering that trend.
