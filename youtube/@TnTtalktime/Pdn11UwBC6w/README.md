## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith addresses common fears around handling lead, arguing that the metal itself is largely harmless and that toxicity risk is narrow and easily managed. He explains that lead only becomes dangerous in two specific forms: as a fine dust (inhalable) or as a soluble salt (absorbable/ingestible). With simple, low-cost precautions, working with lead is straightforward and safe.
