## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the potential of water-based systems as a practical home energy storage solution, combining historical hydraulic technology (accumulators) with water motors. He argues that pressurized water accumulators — which can last over a century — offer a compelling alternative to short-lived battery banks, and that even partially efficient DIY implementations are worthwhile. The main takeaway is that water can function simultaneously as a battery, store, generator, and renewable medium accessible to anyone, even in urban environments without natural water sources.
