## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents nine unconventional renewable energy sources that go well beyond the usual wind, solar, and biofuels conversation. Ranging from the heat of London Underground passengers to space-based solar wind collection, the video blends the plausible, the whimsical, and the futuristic. His core takeaway is that local, low-cost, out-of-the-box solutions using existing technology are more valuable right now than grandiose multi-billion-dollar schemes.

## Key Topics
- Heat recovery from London Underground passengers and trains
- Space-based solar wind collection via satellite sail
- Crematorium heat recovery for electricity and heating
- Piezoelectric dance floors and pedestrian footfall energy
- Jellyfish protein-based fluorescent solar cells
- "Duck" — a solar-panelled floating energy storage sculpture
- Wind turbine trees (VAWTs styled as urban trees in Paris)
- Luminescent rock bike paths (glow-in-the-dark roads in the Netherlands)
- Exploding lakes (Lake Kivu, Rwanda) as a methane energy source

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Methane and carbon dioxide** — gases trapped beneath exploding lakes (e.g. Lake Kivu), captured and used for electricity generation
- **Copper wire** — proposed conductor in the solar wind satellite collection system
- **Piezoelectric transducers** — embedded in dance floors and airport corridors to convert kinetic energy to electricity
- **Luminescent/phosphorescent rocks** — used in Dutch smart bike paths to absorb sunlight and glow at night; "lit" cited as one of the strongest glow-in-the-dark materials
- **Green fluorescent protein (GFP)** — extracted from jellyfish, applied to solar cells as a light-harvesting sensitiser
- **Chlorophyll** — mentioned briefly as a prior analogous biologically-sourced solar material

## Techniques and Methods
- Heat pump / ventilation shaft recovery from underground rail systems
- Magnetic field electron collection via satellite copper wire and sail (proposed)
- Laser transmission of collected energy from orbit to Earth (proposed)
- Piezoelectric transduction of mechanical footfall/dance energy into electricity
- Dye-sensitised (bio-sensitised) solar cell fabrication using jellyfish fluorescent protein
- Pumped-storage energy using a buoyant floating structure ("Duck") as a reversible battery
- Vertical axis wind turbines (VAWTs) integrated into tree-shaped urban structures
- Passive solar charging of photoluminescent road/path materials
- Methane capture from stratified volcanic lakes for grid electricity generation
