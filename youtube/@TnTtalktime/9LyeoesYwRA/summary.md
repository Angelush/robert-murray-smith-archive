## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith visits the Cabaret of Mechanical Movement museum in Glasgow to explore automata — kinetic mechanical sculptures — and makes the case that building automata is a valuable pursuit for anyone interested in engineering. He argues that hands-on experience with mechanical movement informs broader engineering work (such as building wind turbines) and that these objects make excellent gifts while teaching a great deal about mechanics.

## Key Topics
- Robert's lifelong fascination with automata since childhood
- History and travels of the Cabaret of Mechanical Movement museum (Covent Garden → Devon → Glasgow)
- A tour of the Glasgow Cabaret of Mechanical Movement museum
- The value of building your own automata — for learning, for gifts, and for the joy of creating things that move
- The cross-disciplinary connection between automata mechanics and larger engineering projects like wind turbines
