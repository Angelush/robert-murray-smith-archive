## Overview

In this reflective video, Robert Murray-Smith responds to audience requests to explain where his open-mindedness comes from. Drawing on his difficult working-class upbringing — poverty, petty crime, an abusive mother, and an eventual educational awakening inspired by his father — he identifies the life experiences that shaped his worldview. He concludes that true open-mindedness means neither believing nor disbelieving something outright, but instead asking what is actually happening and whether it is interesting.
