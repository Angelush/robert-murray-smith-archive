## Overview
In this reflective video, Robert Murray-Smith responds to audience requests to explain where his open-mindedness comes from. Drawing on his difficult working-class upbringing — poverty, petty crime, an abusive mother, and an eventual educational awakening inspired by his father — he identifies the life experiences that shaped his worldview. He concludes that true open-mindedness means neither believing nor disbelieving something outright, but instead asking what is actually happening and whether it is interesting.

## Key Topics
- Robert's background: poverty, crime, and turning to education in his mid-teens
- The influence of his father getting a degree as a model for self-improvement
- The concept of personal responsibility: your life's *view* is within your control
- The "trauma" of realising you are not the centre of the world
- Three principles of open-mindedness: (1) you are not the centre of the world, (2) you are your own responsibility, (3) you can only control yourself
- Using his perpetual motion video as a case study in open vs. closed thinking
- Civility in disagreement as a prerequisite for open-minded discourse
- Praising commenter Kimberly Peacock as an example of open-minded enquiry
