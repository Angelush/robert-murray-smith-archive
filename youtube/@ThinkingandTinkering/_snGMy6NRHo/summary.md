## Overview
This video is a follow-up to Robert's original SWAG Tesla Radiant Energy Collector video, testing the device outdoors in direct sunlight in response to viewer requests. Using a single SWAG plate connected to a voltmeter and grounded identically to his previous setup, he finds a dramatic increase in output — from roughly 20–24 millivolts indoors to approximately 129–130 millivolts in direct sunlight — demonstrating that light (and possibly heat) from the sun significantly boosts the collector's output.

## Key Topics
- Follow-up testing of the SWAG Tesla Radiant Energy Collector
- Effect of direct sunlight on collected voltage
- Comparison of single-plate vs. multi-plate configuration
- Insulation of the plate from ground using a cardboard box
- Plans to test at height (rooftop) as a future experiment

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- SWAG plate — a graphene-coated BoPET (biaxially oriented polyethylene terephthalate) sheet, used as the energy-collecting antenna/plate

## Techniques and Methods
- Single-plate configuration connected to a voltmeter and grounded to earth
- Cardboard box used as an insulating base to prevent unintended grounding
- Comparative measurement: indoor baseline vs. outdoor direct-sunlight reading
- Millivolt-range DC voltage measurement
