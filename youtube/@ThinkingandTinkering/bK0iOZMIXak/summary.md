## Overview
This is a viewer competition entry submitted to Robert Murray-Smith and Luke, in which a maker from Nanaimo, British Columbia demonstrates two simple hand-cranked LED generators built from salvaged electronics found in the trash. The core insight is that the small DC motors inside DVD and VCR tray mechanisms can act as generators when turned manually, requiring almost no modification to produce usable electricity.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging useful components from discarded electronics ("trash to generator" concept)
- Using a DVD player tray motor as a hand-cranked generator
- Using a VCR mechanism in the same way
- Soldering an LED directly to the back of an existing circuit board
- Teasing a future wind-powered generator project

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Old portable DVD player (salvaged from garbage)
- Old VCR (salvaged)
- LED (light-emitting diode)
- Solder

## Techniques and Methods
- Extracting the tray drive mechanism from a DVD/VCR unit intact
- Attaching a hand crank handle to an existing gear in the drive mechanism
- Soldering an LED onto the back of the original circuit board to tap generator output
