## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a water motor inspired by the Japanese *shishi odoshi* (deer scarer), in which water fills a bamboo tube causing it to rock back and forth. He then converts that oscillating motion into continuous unidirectional rotation using a cog and ratchet mechanism adapted from a previous "SEO winding device." The resulting motor is presented as a practical concept for scavenging small amounts of energy from low-flow, low-drop water sources.
