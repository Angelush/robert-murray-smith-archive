## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a water motor inspired by the Japanese *shishi odoshi* (deer scarer), in which water fills a bamboo tube causing it to rock back and forth. He then converts that oscillating motion into continuous unidirectional rotation using a cog and ratchet mechanism adapted from a previous "SEO winding device." The resulting motor is presented as a practical concept for scavenging small amounts of energy from low-flow, low-drop water sources.

## Key Topics
- The *shishi odoshi* (Japanese deer scarer) as mechanical inspiration
- Converting oscillating/pendulum motion into continuous rotational output
- Ratchet-based one-way motion conversion (adapted from his earlier "SEO winding device")
- Gearing strategies to increase rotational speed
- Energy scavenging from minimal water flow and low head pressure
- 3D-printed parts shared on Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Bamboo (tube used as the water-filling rocker)
- Bearings and pivot points (for the rocker assembly)
- Cogs/gears (driving cog on the swivel, output cog)
- Brackets (to limit swing arc)

No chemicals mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing of structural components (files uploaded to Thingiverse)
- Ratchet mechanism to convert bidirectional rocking into unidirectional rotation
- Gearing up output speed from a slow rocking input
- Limiting swing arc with fixed bracket stops
- Water feed horn to direct flow into the bamboo tube
