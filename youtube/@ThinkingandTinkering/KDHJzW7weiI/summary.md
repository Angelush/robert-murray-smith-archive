## Overview
This is Part 4 of Robert Murray-Smith's DIY gasification and generator series, providing an update on the completed gasifier unit built from a beer barrel and a central fire tube. Robert explains the three-stage syngas cleaning process — cyclone filter, particle filter, and cooler — and demonstrates how he plans to build a cyclone filter from a repurposed fire extinguisher.

## Key Topics
- Status update on the completed gasifier unit construction
- Design of the gasifier: beer barrel body, central fire tube, single syngas exhaust port, and bottom grate
- The three stages of syngas cleaning: cyclone filter → particle filter → cooler
- How a cyclone filter works (angled inlet, inner exit tube, spiral particle separation)
- Building a cyclone filter from a recycled fire extinguisher

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 3mm box section steel (50×25mm / ~1"×2"), salvaged from a skip
- Beer barrel (used as the gasifier body)
- Steel plate (used for the door and exhaust flange, sourced from an old mold)
- Fire extinguisher (repurposed as the cyclone filter body)
- Syngas / producer gas (the output being filtered)
- Sealant (used with bolts to attach the exhaust plate without welding directly to the finished unit)

## Techniques and Methods
- MIG/arc welding (used to seal holes in the beer barrel and attach components)
- Bolting with sealant as an alternative to direct welding on finished sections (to avoid ruining completed work)
- Cyclone separation: tangential gas inlet forces particles to spiral outward, collect on the wall, and drop into a bottom trap
- Grate shaking for ash/char cleaning at the base of the fire tube
