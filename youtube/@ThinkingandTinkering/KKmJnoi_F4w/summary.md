## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to copper plate a nylon scouring sponge by first making it electrically conductive with a water-resistant conductive ink, then electroplating it in a copper sulfate solution. The video also covers the optional step of dissolving the nylon substrate in concentrated sulfuric acid to leave a pure copper sponge, and briefly explains why a copper intermediate layer is needed before applying his "Lacey solution" for plating other metals.

## Key Topics
- Making a non-conductive sponge electrically conductive using conductive ink
- Electroplating the sponge with copper using a copper sulfate/sulfuric acid bath
- Removing the nylon substrate with concentrated sulfuric acid to yield a pure copper sponge
- Applications of copper sponges (heat exchangers, battery plates)
- Why copper must be plated first before using the Lacey safe electroplating solution

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Nylon scouring sponge** — the substrate being plated
- **Water-resistant conductive ink** — used to coat the sponge and make it conductive prior to plating
- **Copper sulfate (100 g)** — dissolved in 500 mL water as the copper ion source in the plating bath
- **Concentrated sulfuric acid, 98% (26 mL)** — added to the plating bath as electrolyte; also used neat to dissolve the nylon after plating
- **Battery acid** — mentioned as a lower-concentration alternative (use 3× the volume)
- **Lacey solution** — a safe electroplating solution for copper and other metals; noted that it will not plate directly onto carbon, only onto copper

## Techniques and Methods
- **Conductive ink impregnation** — squeezing sponge in conductive ink then letting it dry to create a conductive substrate
- **Electroplating setup** — copper anode connected to positive terminal, sponge (cathode) connected to negative; clip held just above the solution surface to prevent plating the clip
- **Voltage ramping** — starting at 5 V for ~5 minutes, then reducing to 1.5 V for the remainder of the plating session
- **Periodic rotation** — turning the sponge during plating to ensure even coverage
- **Nylon dissolution** — immersing the plated sponge in 98% sulfuric acid for several hours (overnight) to dissolve the nylon, leaving only the copper structure
- **Sequential plating** — applying a copper base layer first to enable subsequent plating with the Lacey solution
