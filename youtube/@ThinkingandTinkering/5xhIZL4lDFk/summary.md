## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith reviews two thermal battery technologies — Antora Energy and Brenmiller Energy (B-Gen) — both recognised by Time magazine as among the best inventions of 2023. The video explains how each system stores and retrieves heat energy at industrial scale, and argues that thermal storage is cost-competitive with lithium-ion and pumped hydro solutions, making it a compelling option for both industry and grid storage.

## Key Topics
- Time magazine's Best Inventions of 2023 list, specifically the green energy category
- Antora Energy's carbon/graphite brick thermal battery system
- Brenmiller Energy's crushed rock thermal battery (B-Gen)
- Comparison with Polar Night Energy's sand battery (Finland)
- Cost comparison: thermal storage vs. lithium-iron batteries vs. pumped hydro
- Industrial process heat as a major energy use case (30% of industrial energy)
- Historical DIY thermal storage (popular in the 1970s–80s)
- Potential for grid-scale adoption

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Carbon/graphite bricks** — heated to white-hot temperatures in the Antora system
- **Molten metal** — pumped through the Antora system to extract heat and boil water
- **Ceramics and graphite** — used in MIT-developed pumps capable of handling metals at up to 2,000°C
- **Crushed rock** — the thermal storage medium in the Brenmiller B-Gen system
- **Sand** — used by Polar Night Energy's Finnish sand battery (referenced for comparison)
- **Water/steam** — working fluid in the Brenmiller system; raised to 750°C steam
- **Lithium-iron (LiFePO₄) batteries** — cited for cost comparison ($50–$150/kWh)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Electrical resistance heating** — used to heat carbon/graphite blocks to white-hot temperatures (Antora)
- **Thermophotovoltaic (TPV) cells** — convert infrared/visible light from glowing bricks directly into electricity; mirrors used internally to maximise light capture
- **Molten metal pumping** — MIT-developed ceramic/graphite gear pumps circulate liquid metal at up to 2,000°C to extract heat as steam
- **Crushed rock thermal storage with gas/electric heating** — rocks heated by electricity, exhaust gases, or flue gases (Brenmiller)
- **Steam extraction** — water pumped through heated rock bed, raised to 750°C steam for process heat or turbine drive
- **DIY ground thermal storage** — historical technique: rocks buried in a pit, heated with summer air, used to warm buildings in winter
