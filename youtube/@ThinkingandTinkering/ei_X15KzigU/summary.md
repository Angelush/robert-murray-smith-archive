## Overview
Robert introduces a magnetic ink he has developed as part of his broader Indiegogo campaign to create affordable functional inks. He demonstrates the ink's ability to hold a magnet and paper, then proposes the concept of painting an entire wall with magnetic ink (then covering it with standard emulsion) to create a seamless, hole-free display surface for pictures, notices, and magnetic lettering.

## Key Topics
- Development of magnetic ink as part of a functional inks project
- The Indiegogo campaign goal of producing affordable conductive, magnetic, piezo, and electrochromic inks
- Concept of a magnetic paint display wall
- Practical applications: gallery walls, notice boards, children's room decoration
- Compatibility with standard emulsion paint and wallpaper as a top coat

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Magnetic ink** — the newly developed functional ink being demonstrated
- **Conductive ink** — the primary ink from the Indiegogo campaign, referenced as a comparison
- **Emulsion paint** — proposed as a top coat over the magnetic ink to give a normal wall appearance
- **Piezo inks** — mentioned as another ink type under development
- **Electrochromic inks** — mentioned as another ink type under development

## Techniques and Methods
- Printing magnetic ink onto a substrate (a square patch shown on camera)
- Layering technique: apply magnetic ink to wall first, then cover with emulsion or wallpaper for a seamless finish
- Using small magnets to attach paper/pictures directly to the treated wall surface
