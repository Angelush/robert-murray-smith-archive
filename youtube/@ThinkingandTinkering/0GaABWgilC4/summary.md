## Overview
Robert reviews the Revopoint Miraco, an all-in-one handheld 3D scanner/camera sent to him by Revopoint. He uses it to scan an outdoor sculpture from a local art walk and then scans himself to create a printable bust, demonstrating the full workflow from capture to 3D print. His conclusion is that the device's integration of tablet, battery, and scanner into a single unit makes it exceptionally easy to pick up and use with minimal learning curve.

## Key Topics
- Introduction and Star Trek / holodeck comparison as inspiration
- Hands-on scanning of an outdoor wooden sculpture
- Device features: near/far modes, quad camera, pause-and-resume scanning, single-shot mode
- Transferring scans to PC via Wi-Fi or USB-C using Revo Scan 5.3.1 software
- Exporting scan data as STL, PLY, OBJ formats
- Mesh simplification to meet TinkerCAD's 25 MB / 300,000 triangle limit
- Editing and preparing the model in TinkerCAD (adding a plinth)
- Slicing and printing in Ultimaker Cura on an Ultimaker S5 (Eligo Max)
- Kickstarter campaign availability and accessory bundle overview
- Uploading models to Thingiverse for community use

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Wood (the scanned outdoor sculpture, noted to have visible wood grain and moss)
- Moss (visible in scan detail)
- Black plastic marker mat ("Magic Mat") with dot pattern for reference tracking

## Techniques and Methods
- Handheld 3D scanning with real-time color feedback (red/green indicator)
- Pause-and-resume scanning with automatic position re-acquisition
- Single-shot mode with ~60% overlap for stable captures
- Near mode (small objects: rings, fossils) vs. far mode (people, large objects)
- Point cloud capture and in-device editing (hole filling, floating point isolation)
- One-tap automated edit vs. manual step-by-step editing on device
- Export to STL (meshed mode) or PLY/OBJ (textured mode)
- Online mesh simplification (reducing triangle count below 300,000 for TinkerCAD)
- TinkerCAD model editing (adding plinth, cleaning edges)
- Slicing in Ultimaker Cura with raft adhesion, supports, at 250 mm/s
- FDM 3D printing (scaled down from full size)

## Robert's Own Replies
- Clarified to one commenter that a technique they described is **photogrammetry**, distinguishing it from what the Miraco does.
- Confirmed the video was **made at Revopoint's request** ("we were asked to by revopoint").
- Noted **TinkerCAD's hard limits**: 25 MB file size and 300,000 triangles maximum — no workaround within TinkerCAD itself.
- Mentioned he has only tried the **Revopoint Inspire** in addition to the Miraco; no experience with other Revopoint models.
- Suggested **photogrammetry as an alternative** for users who can't access the Miraco.
- Confirmed the Miraco design was **based on the PADD** from Star Trek.
- Defended his close-up shots of the scanner and pointed viewers to the provided STL files to judge scan quality directly, in response to a critical comment.
- Explained the value of the device's integrated storage: useful **"when you are doing lots of shooting at one time or can't be bothered to swap files over"**.
