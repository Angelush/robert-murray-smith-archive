## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith compares his custom Pelton wheel wind turbine design against a standard Archimedes spiral turbine, demonstrating that the Pelton wheel spins effectively in very light winds. He tests it by powering a 6-LED emergency light at roughly 3 m/s wind speed, successfully lighting it both indoors and outdoors, showcasing wind power as a viable nighttime alternative to solar.

## Key Topics
- Comparison between an Archimedes spiral wind turbine and a Pelton wheel-inspired turbine design
- Performance of the Archimedes spiral (~200 mW at 3.4 m/s wind, 170mm × 200mm)
- Pelton wheel design principles: central cone to spread incoming air, forcing it through blades
- Low wind-speed responsiveness of the Pelton wheel design
- Practical demonstration: powering a 6-LED RAC emergency light
- Wind power vs. solar for nighttime emergency lighting
- Mention of future "Darwin wind turbine" concept with a funnel/duct

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- PLA or similar plastic (3D-printed turbine blades — noted as having imprecise tolerances)
- LEDs (6-LED RAC emergency light, drawing 10–30 mA each)

## Techniques and Methods
- Anemometer measurement of wind speed at test point (~3 m/s)
- Bench fan used as controlled wind source for indoor testing
- Direct electrical connection of generator output to LED load (no batteries)
- Comparative sizing/output estimation (diameter × depth → expected milliwatts)
- Outdoor real-world validation following lab testing
