## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a DIY "sound bug" — a device that turns an ordinary window pane into a speaker — for approximately £1, as a low-cost alternative to commercial units selling for around £25. Commercial versions use terfenol-D, a giant magnetostrictive material, but his homemade electromagnetic coil design achieves a similar effect. The finished device, stuck to a window with magnets and rubber, makes the entire glass surface radiate audible sound both inside and outside the room.
