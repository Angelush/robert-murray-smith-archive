## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith investigates whether coating zeolite with graphene fluid improves its thermal conductivity and heat distribution. He runs a simple comparative experiment using two beakers — one with zeolite in water, one with zeolite in graphene fluid — heated by a 70W tungsten lamp for 30 minutes each. The graphene-zeolite mix shows markedly more even temperature distribution across the mass and achieves a slightly higher overall temperature, suggesting graphene encapsulation could improve the efficiency of zeolite-based absorption chillers.
