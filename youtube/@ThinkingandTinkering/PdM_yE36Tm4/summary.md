## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a DIY light box from a spare 600×600mm LED lighting panel, showing it is far cheaper and simpler than buying a commercial equivalent. Using builders board (Cintra board) for the housing and salvaged hardware, he produces a finished, gift-ready light box for around £14–20, compared to £80–100 for a store-bought version. The project is presented as an ideal handmade gift for artists, designers, or anyone who does tracing work.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing spare LED ceiling lighting panels as a light box light source
- Building a housing box from builders board / Cintra board (uPVC fascia/soffit material)
- Cost comparison: DIY (~£14–20) vs. commercial light boxes (£80–100)
- Optional use of broken LCD screens as an alternative light source (shown by "Luke")
- Wiring: connecting the transformer, adding an on/off switch on the live (brown) wire
- Cable management and finishing details
- Suitability as a homemade gift for artists and designers

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **LED lighting panel** — 600×600mm, 36W, 24V, plugs into mains via transformer; the primary light source
- **Builders board / Cintra board / uPVC fascia and soffit** — used to construct the box housing; ~50p per metre
- **PVC window trim** — used as an internal shelf to seat the lighting panel
- **Chrome pipe** — salvaged material used to fabricate handles
- **Super glue / crazy glue** — used to join the builders board sections; glue joint reportedly stronger than the plastic itself
- **Self-adhesive cable tie mounts** — small sticky squares used for cable management
- **Cable ties** — used with the mounts to secure wiring
- **Sticky pads** — used to mount the transformer inside the box
- **IP-rated cable connector** — fitted through a drilled hole in the rear of the box for the mains cable entry
- **Salvaged flex (mains cable)** — used for the power lead

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting builders board to length with 45-degree mitre cuts (or straight cuts with corner caps) to form a box frame
- Reinforcing corners with 8 small 45-degree triangular offcuts of the same board, super-glued in place
- Drilling a hole in the rear panel with a hole cutter for the IP-rated cable gland
- Fabricating handles by drilling tight-fitting holes in chrome pipe offcuts, gluing two pieces together and cutting in half
- Wiring: joining neutral (blue) wires together, switching the live (brown) through an inline on/off switch
- Press-fit installation of the LED panel into the box (optional gluing or screw-fixing via rear fixing points)
- Cable winding around a rear peg for tidy storage

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Material sourcing:** Identified the builders board more precisely as **uPVC fascia and soffit**; recommends searching for "building plastics" suppliers — most UK towns have a local one. Provided an example link to a fascia/soffit supplier.
- **Voltage:** Confirmed the panel runs at **24V**; noted battery power conversion would also be "super simple."
- **Panel purchasing:** Advises buying the complete LED panel (transformer included) rather than ordering separate LEDs — the goal is a quick, easy project. Notes that 600×600mm panels of all kinds are widely available online.
- **Wood alternative:** Agrees the box could be built in timber for a higher-end result.
- **Daylight simulation:** Suggests using a **daylight-simulating LED panel** for colour-accurate art work.
- **Gift intent:** Confirms the light box is being made for his artist sister; enthusiasm for it as a homemade gift at a fraction of retail cost.
- **Project philosophy:** Emphasises this is intentionally a "super simple" project — an hour or two in the workshop producing something comparable to a store-bought gift but much cheaper, with no need to over-engineer it.
