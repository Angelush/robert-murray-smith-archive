## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith converts an electric tricycle into a pedal-powered emergency generator ahead of Storm Eunice hitting the UK in February 2022. By attaching a hand-crank generator (built in a previous video) to the trike's gear cluster via a sprocket and chain, he demonstrates a reversible setup that produces usable power for charging phones and batteries — without permanently modifying the bicycle.

## Key Topics
- Using a pedal-powered tricycle as a DIY emergency generator during storm power outages
- Repurposing a hand-crank generator (from video 1436) by replacing the handle with a bike sprocket
- Building a stabilising frame from a salvaged broken pipe bender
- The physics of human power output: legs vs. arms, and how gear ratios affect generator speed
- Reversibility — the bicycle remains fully functional after the generator is removed

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Old/broken pipe bender (repurposed as a stand frame)
- Scaffold tube (used as horizontal support pipes)
- Bicycle/tricycle sprocket and chain
- 120W motor with internal gearbox (from video 1436)
- LED lighting bank (used as test load)
- Voltage meter

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting and welding scrap metal (pipe bender frame) into a stabilising stand
- Welding a bicycle sprocket onto the shaft of an existing hand-crank generator
- Looping a chain between the bicycle's gear cluster and the generator sprocket for power transfer
- Using the bicycle's existing gear shifter to vary output (5 gears available)
- Dynamo-on-tyre approach mentioned and rejected in favour of the chain-drive method
