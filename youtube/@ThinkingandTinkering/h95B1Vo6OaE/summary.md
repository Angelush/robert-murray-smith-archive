## Overview
In this second part of his DIY gasification series, Robert Murray-Smith explains the design and construction of the "fire pipe" — the gas-generation core of a downdraft stratified gasifier. He demonstrates that the system is remarkably simple: the reactor is just a steel pipe sized to the engine, dropped into a stainless steel beer keg that acts as a collection jacket, with almost no critical tolerances to worry about.

## Key Topics
- Recap of the four main subsystems of a gasifier: gas generation, filtering/cleaning, cooling, and utilization
- Why the engine size dictates the dimensions of the fire pipe
- How a downdraft stratified gasifier works and its distinct reaction layers (drying, biomass, pyrolysis, combustion, reduction, ash)
- Using a stainless steel beer keg as the outer jacket to collect produced gas
- Grate options for the bottom of the pipe (colander, welded rebar ring)
- Flange fabrication and Robert's plan to learn welding for assembly
- Cost breakdown of components (~£50–60 total for main parts)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Steel pipe** — 4.5 inch (125 mm) diameter, 400 mm long, 6 mm wall thickness; the fire/reactor tube, sized per FEMA guidelines for a 6.5 hp engine
- **Stainless steel beer keg** — used as the outer gas-collection jacket (~£20 on eBay)
- **Steel flange** — fabricated by a friend in the steel industry, used to mount the pipe into the keg lid
- **Stainless steel colander** — proposed grate to hold biomass and let ash fall through
- **Rebar** — alternative grate material (welded ring option)
- **Wood/biomass** — the feedstock loaded into the fire pipe

## Techniques and Methods
- **Downdraft stratified gasification** — air drawn downward through layered biomass; no modifications to the pipe required
- **Jacket design** — enclosing the fire pipe inside a larger vessel to collect producer gas from the bottom
- **Grate shaking** — chains attached to a colander grate to periodically clear ash
- **Arc/MIG welding** — Robert plans to weld the flange to the keg lid himself, using an auto-darkening welding mask (first serious attempt at welding)
- **FEMA gasifier sizing tables** — used to determine pipe dimensions from engine horsepower
