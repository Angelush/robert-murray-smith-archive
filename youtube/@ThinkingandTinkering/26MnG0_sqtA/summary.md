## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith tests the Elegoo Centauri Carbon 3D printer by running a series of practical prints, comparing it head-to-head against his Bambu printer. The video covers a single-layer "transfer" printing technique developed with a collaborator, a carbon fiber PETG print of a custom latch hook needle, and a general assessment of the machine's speed, noise, and print quality. His main conclusion is that the Elegoo Centauri is a compelling alternative to the Bambu, consistently faster and producing indistinguishable results.

## Key Topics
- Head-to-head speed and noise comparison between the Elegoo Centauri Carbon and a Bambu printer
- A collaborative "transfer" technique for creating 2D images with a 3D printer (developed with Ross of the "Intelligent Tinkering" channel)
- Single-layer printing as a pseudo-lithophane method
- Printing a custom-designed latch hook needle in carbon fiber PETG
- Overview of the Centauri Carbon's specifications and design features (capacitive touchscreen placement, high-speed cooling fans, powerful heaters)
- General workflow and slicer settings (Orca Slicer with generic PETG CF profile)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA** — used for an initial prototype latch hook needle; noted as not durable enough for functional use
- **PETG Carbon Fiber (PETG CF)** — used to reprint the latch hook needle; expected to be significantly tougher than PLA
- **PEI sheet** — the print bed surface used; Robert notes two sides optimised for different materials (one side for PLA/TPU/ABS/PETG CF, one optimised for PLA)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Single-layer printing** — printing one layer thick to create semi-transparent panels, compared to lithophanes but simpler
- **Transfer printing technique** — a collaborative method (with Ross/Intelligent Tinkering) for producing two-tone 2D images on a 3D printer; described as still experimental
- **Concentric infill layering** — noted as contributing to strong inter-bead adhesion in the single-layer print
- **Manual filament loading via settings screen** — raising nozzle temperature to 260°C for PETG CF before feeding
- **Orca Slicer with generic PETG CF profile** — used without custom tuning for the carbon fiber print

## Robert's Own Replies
Most of Robert's comment replies in this thread are brief acknowledgements unrelated to this specific video. One relevant clarification stands out:

- **On print speed**: Robert confirms the Elegoo Centauri is **consistently faster** than the Bambu throughout a print — not just due to skipping setup steps. He notes the video was filmed some weeks prior and he kept it secret, but has been running both machines side by side since.
