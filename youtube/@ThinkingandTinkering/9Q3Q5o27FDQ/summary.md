## Overview
Luke builds a ride-on scooter frame from the salvaged parts of a 16-inch children's bike, fabricating a custom steel footplate and welding the bike's existing front forks, rear wheel assembly, and handlebars together. The build covers the mechanical frame construction only, with electronics and a motor planned for a follow-up video to convert it into an e-scooter.

## Key Topics
- Disassembling a 16-inch children's bike for usable components
- Designing and welding a steel H-frame footplate
- Attaching the front and rear wheel assemblies to the footplate
- Extending and bending the handlebars for ergonomic fit
- Painting and finishing the frame
- Preview of a planned electric conversion in a follow-up video

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 16-inch children's bike (donor parts: handlebars, brakes, front forks, rear wheel assembly, tires)
- Steel angle iron (two lengths of 800 mm, three lengths of 160 mm)
- Steel pipe/tube (for handlebar stem extension)
- Wood (for the standing footplate surface)
- Hammerite-style paint ("hammer right") for the frame
- Black paint for handlebars
- Lacquer for the wooden footplate

## Techniques and Methods
- Angle grinding / cutting steel to length
- MIG or stick welding (first-time experience noted) to assemble the H-frame footplate
- Spot welding for handlebar stem extensions
- Heat bending the handlebar stem for ergonomic angle
- Sanding and lacquering the wooden footplate
- Test riding before final paint to verify structural integrity
