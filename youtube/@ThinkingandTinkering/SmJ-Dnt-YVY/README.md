## Overview

In this second installment of his rotating ventilator wind generator series, Robert Murray-Smith explains how he plans to turn a salvaged roof ventilator into a low-speed axial generator by winding his own coils from recycled materials. He demonstrates that a single homemade coreless coil — built from scrap ABS bobbins wound on a lathe — can produce roughly 2 watts in a test rig, leading him to project approximately 64 watts from a full set of 32 coils arranged around the ventilator's circumference.
