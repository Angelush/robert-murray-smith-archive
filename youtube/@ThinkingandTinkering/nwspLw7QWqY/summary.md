## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents an upgraded version of his Archimedes screw wind turbine, now integrated with an axial flux coreless generator he designed in TinkerCAD. The build uses only five 3D-printed parts and is tested both as a bare generator (spinning by hand) and outdoors in low wind conditions (~1–2 m/s), demonstrating that it successfully self-starts and generates voltage in real-world conditions.

## Key Topics
- Design and assembly of a 5-part 3D-printed Archimedes screw wind turbine with integrated generator
- Axial flux coreless generator design using two opposing magnetic discs
- Serpentine coil winding for the generator stator
- Magnetic bearing system using neodymium magnets
- Outdoor real-world wind speed testing (~0.9–2.0 m/s)
- Versatility of the generator design (applicable to water wheels, hand generators, horizontal wind turbines)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Neodymium magnets** — 15 mm circular, 4.5 mm deep, arranged north-south-north-south on both magnetic discs
- **8 mm steel bar** — central shaft/axle for the assembly
- **0.2 mm magnet wire (enamelled copper wire)** — used to wind the serpentine coil (400 turns, enamel-coated)
- **Skate bearing** — 22 mm × 8 mm × 7 mm, used in the magnetic disc assembly

## Techniques and Methods
- TinkerCAD CAD design (design built from 3 merged primitives)
- 3D printing of all structural components
- Axial flux coreless generator construction (two magnetic rotor discs with a stationary coil between them)
- Serpentine coil winding (400 turns of 0.2 mm wire)
- North-south alternating magnet pole arrangement on rotor discs
- Voltmeter testing of generator output by hand-spinning
- Outdoor anemometer wind speed measurement during turbine testing

## Robert's Own Replies
- **On adding more coils:** Clarifies that adding more coils will not generate more power — the limit is always the mechanical energy input (wind turning the rotor), not the number of coils. A generator converts mechanical to electrical energy; the wind is the bottleneck.
- **On the wire:** Confirms the coil uses magnet wire, which is enamelled copper wire; provides an RS Components example link (noting it is an example, not a specific recommendation).
- **On the CAD design:** States the TinkerCAD model is built from just 3 merged primitives.
- **On purchasing/obtaining:** Does not sell the turbine; the design is free on Thingiverse (link in description). Suggests sending the file to a 3D printing company if you cannot print it yourself.
- **On a specific 3D printing supplier in Trinidad and Tobago:** Points a commenter to The 3D Printing Specialist as a local option.
- **On the coil carrier attachment:** Notes there are two projections on the base that the coil carrier fixes to.
