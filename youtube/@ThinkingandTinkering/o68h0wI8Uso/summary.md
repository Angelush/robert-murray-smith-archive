## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a DIY hydraulic ram pump from standard plumbing parts, walking through its history and operating principles. He successfully tests it and then pivots to his real interest: using the pressurised accumulator chamber as a form of energy storage rather than for water pumping. The video ends with plans to upgrade the flapper valves to spring valves and explore the pump as a pressure-based energy store.

## Key Topics
- History of the hydraulic ram pump (1298 Alhambra reference through to modern day)
- How a ram pump works: water hammer effect, check valves, air chamber/accumulator
- DIY construction from off-the-shelf 1-inch threaded plumbing fittings
- Valve types: flapper valves vs. spring valves and tuning via weight or spring tension
- Repurposing the accumulator as a pressure/energy storage vessel
- Historical context: Joshua Whitehurst (1772), Montgolfier (1796/1816), Joshua Easton (1820), Hans Günther wave power proposal (1931), Selwyn Selman's Venturi-based "pepper ram pump" (1996)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 1-inch threaded steel/plastic pipe sections (from local plumbing store)
- Flapper (flat) check valves
- Spring check valves (planned replacement)
- PTFE plumber's tape
- Central heating system accumulator (pressure vessel with ¾-inch pipe connection flange)
- Pressure gauge
- Improvised accumulator alternatives mentioned: fire extinguishers, propane tanks, glued pipe sections

## Techniques and Methods
- Threaded pipe assembly with PTFE tape sealing
- Weight-loading a flapper valve to simulate spring tension for oscillation tuning
- Water hammer cycling to drive pressure build-up in the accumulator
- Using an existing central heating accumulator as the pressure chamber
- Proposed: connecting the pressurised air/water output to an airbrush or other load as an energy store
