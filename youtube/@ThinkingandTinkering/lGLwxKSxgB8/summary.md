## Overview
Robert demonstrates the Lumberjack BS228 bandsaw by using it to fabricate a false radiator grille for "Bessie," his Doctor Who electric car build. He cuts and assembles pieces of builders board (foam board with PVC coating) into a decorative front radiator frame, showcasing how easy the bandsaw makes straight cuts in this material.

## Key Topics
- First practical use of the Lumberjack BS228 bandsaw after its unboxing video
- Fabricating a false/decorative radiator for the Doctor Who electric car project ("Bessie")
- Properties and workability of builders board as a modelling material
- Assembly and finishing of the radiator mock-up

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Builders board** (known as Celtec board in the US) — foam board with a PVC coating, used as the primary structural material
- **Super glue** — used to bond builders board pieces; bond stated to be stronger than the material itself
- **Storm light** — flexible PVC strip material used to cap the arch gap
- **Aluminium mesh** — mentioned as an alternative grill material (not used)
- **Paper tray** — repurposed as a black grill insert for the radiator hole
- **Paint** — a coat applied to finish the assembled piece

## Techniques and Methods
- Band saw cutting of foam/PVC board (Lumberjack BS228) for straight, clean edges
- Dry-fit and super glue assembly of flat pieces into a box/frame structure
- Adding decorative arch cutouts to the frame
- Flexible strip application to cover gaps
- Repurposing found objects (paper tray) as functional grill elements
