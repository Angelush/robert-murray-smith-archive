## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates an experiment where his "SWAG ink" (a conductive nanoparticle-based ink) is applied to a surface while in a magnetic field, causing the particles to self-align along field lines. He tests three configurations — no field (control), field lines parallel to electrodes, and field lines running between electrodes — measuring the resulting electrical output in salt water. The key finding is a dramatic improvement of approximately three orders of magnitude in generator output when the ink is dried while aligned in a magnetic field with field lines running between the current-collecting electrodes.
