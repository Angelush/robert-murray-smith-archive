## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a Hero's engine (aeolipile) — the world's earliest known steam engine, dating to around 50 AD — using a large bean can and salvaged hat-off speaker magnet components. He mounts it on a bearing, drills opposing steam-jet holes, and connects it to a small motor to generate electricity. While voltage output was modest, the prototype successfully demonstrated the concept and highlighted how steam pressure and nozzle size critically affect performance.

## Key Topics
- History of Hero's engine (~50 AD, with earlier description by Vitruvius ~85 BC)
- Construction of a Hero/aeolipile steam engine from a bean can
- Using salvaged hat-off speaker magnets as structural/rotational components
- Connecting the steam engine to a DC motor to generate electricity
- Effect of nozzle hole size on steam pressure and rotational speed
- Directions for further experimentation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Large bean can (boiler body)
- Water (working fluid/steam source)
- Hat-off speaker magnet (salvaged from a speaker, used as the rotating assembly hub)
- Eight-build bar (fits inside the magnet, used as a mounting/shaft component)
- Stop caps (end caps fitted to the steam nozzle arms)
- Bearing (allows the can assembly to spin freely)
- LED (used as an electrical load indicator)
- Small DC motor (used as a generator)

## Techniques and Methods
- Hat-off technique for removing speaker magnets (referenced from subsidiary channel TNT)
- Drilling opposing 1 mm holes in the can for directional steam jets
- Metal spinning (referenced for fabricating end components — detailed in TNT channel video)
- Mounting a rotating steam vessel on a bearing for free rotation
- Connecting a spinning steam-driven assembly to a motor shaft for electricity generation
- Iterative nozzle sizing — reducing hole diameter to increase steam pressure and achieve rotation

- **Safety:** Dismisses concerns about the can exploding, noting it already has two holes in it.
- **Heron's Fountain:** Mentions he has made previous videos on Heron's fountain (both a 3-cell and 4-cell version).
- **Turbine idea:** A viewer suggestion about a turbine-style configuration gets him thinking.
- **Jet nozzle:** Notes he has been considering the steam jet approach and finds it useful.
- **General enthusiasm:** Repeatedly expresses wonder at how simple ancient engines can be, and that it is "astounding what was around 2,000 years ago."
