## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a Hero's engine (aeolipile) — the world's earliest known steam engine, dating to around 50 AD — using a large bean can and salvaged hat-off speaker magnet components. He mounts it on a bearing, drills opposing steam-jet holes, and connects it to a small motor to generate electricity. While voltage output was modest, the prototype successfully demonstrated the concept and highlighted how steam pressure and nozzle size critically affect performance.
