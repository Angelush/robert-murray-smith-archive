## Overview
A guest presenter named Luke builds a simple DIY battery level indicator circuit designed to test 12V vehicle batteries. The device uses a voltage divider principle with five LEDs and a potentiometer to visually show remaining charge. The project is motivated by the frustration of a dead car battery and serves as a low-cost alternative to dedicated battery testers.

## Key Topics
- Motivation for building a 12V battery level indicator (vehicle battery testing)
- Circuit schematic overview and component selection
- Soldering LEDs and resistors in series
- Calibrating the circuit to a 12V reference using a potentiometer
- Testing voltage response across a range (12V down to ~3V)
- Explanation of voltage divider circuits
- Enclosing the finished circuit in a 1mm acrylic case

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Five 1kΩ resistors
- Five LEDs (3 red, 2 green)
- 10kΩ potentiometer
- 1mm thick acrylic sheet (for enclosure)

## Techniques and Methods
- Soldering (hand soldering of through-hole components)
- Connecting LEDs in series (positive-to-negative chaining)
- Voltage calibration using a bench power supply
- Voltage divider circuit construction
- Laser cutting or fabrication of acrylic enclosure (implied)
