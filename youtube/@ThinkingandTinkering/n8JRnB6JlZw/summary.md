## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith reviews the Anycubic Kobra S1 Combo 3D printer, sent to him as part of Anycubic's 10th anniversary celebration. He unboxes and sets up the machine, highlighting its standout feature: a combined automatic filament feeder and dryer (the ACE unit) that he considers a genuinely novel innovation. He concludes it looks very promising out of the box but reserves final judgment for a follow-up review after 3–6 months of heavy use.

## Key Topics
- Anycubic's 10th anniversary and the evolution of consumer 3D printing over the past decade
- Unboxing and initial assembly of the Kobra S1 Combo
- The ACE (Automatic Combined Enclosure) unit: integrated multi-filament feeder and filament dryer/heater
- Active carbon air filtration system and its non-proprietary filter design
- Build specifications: 250×250×250 mm build volume, 600 mm/s speed, CoreXY architecture
- Slicer software (Orca Slicer port) and Wi-Fi / remote access features
- RFID filament detection (similar to Bambu Lab)
- Price competitiveness versus rivals such as Bambu Lab
- Anycubic anniversary promotions (giveaways, coupons, flash sales, memory tree)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA filament** — four spools provided with the machine; noted to absorb moisture over time if stored
- **ABS filament** — mentioned as a material requiring the enclosed chamber and active carbon filter due to fumes
- **TPU filament** — noted as *not* compatible with the ACE multi-filament feeder unit
- **Activated carbon (granules)** — used in the replaceable air filtration bag/filter at the rear of the enclosure
- **PTFE tubes** — used to connect the ACE feeder unit to the printer

## Techniques and Methods
- Unboxing and hardware assembly (removing retaining screws, attaching ACE feeder unit, inserting PTFE tubes, removing blue locking clips)
- Filament loading via the ACE auto-feed system (cutting kinked ends before feeding)
- Filament drying using the integrated heater in the ACE unit (temperature and drying time configurable via on-screen menu)
- RFID-based automatic filament profile detection
- Slicing with Orca Slicer (via the Anycubic-branded port); multi-colour slicing workflow
- Wi-Fi network connection and remote monitoring via the Workbench feature in the slicer
- Sneakernet (USB stick) as an alternative transfer method
