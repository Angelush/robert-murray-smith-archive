## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to extend the capabilities of a small hobby lathe by using a fan belt to drive oversized workpieces that wouldn't normally fit the machine. He shows this technique while helping his brother build a photography press, turning down large steel rollers to fit their bearings using angle grinders as the cutting tool instead of a lathe tool.

## Key Topics
- Adapting a mini/hobby lathe to handle workpieces far larger than its normal capacity
- Using a fan belt as a drive mechanism to spin large cylindrical workpieces
- Supporting heavy workpieces on casters mounted to a bench
- Using angle grinders (grinding disc and flap wheel) as a substitute for a lathe cutting tool
- Importance of patience and incremental test-fitting when reducing a diameter to a precise sliding fit

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Solid steel rollers (large cylindrical workpieces)
- Fan belt (used as drive belt between lathe headstock and workpiece)
- Bearings (target fit for the reduced axle diameter)

## Techniques and Methods
- Wrapping a fan belt around an oversized cylinder and connecting it to the lathe headstock to spin the workpiece externally
- Mounting heavy workpieces on castor wheels fixed to a bench for support and alignment
- Using an angle grinder with a grinding disc for material removal
- Using an angle grinder with a flap wheel for finishing/truing
- Incremental test-fitting: removing a small amount, testing bearing fit, repeating until a sliding fit is achieved
