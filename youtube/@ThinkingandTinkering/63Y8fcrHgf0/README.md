## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a zinc-iodine battery that he considers among the simplest batteries anyone can build at home, using only water, iodine, zinc foil, and a copper mesh electrode coated in conductive plastic. The battery self-forms as iodine reacts with zinc, turns purple when charged, and is rechargeable at 1.2V. The video is framed as a direct contrast to fraudulent "supercapacitor" DIY videos — this one actually works.
