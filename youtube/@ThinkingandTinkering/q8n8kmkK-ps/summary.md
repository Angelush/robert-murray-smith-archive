## Overview
A maker submits an entry to Robert Murray-Smith's competition, demonstrating a hand-powered "Pink Elephant" coin-operated kitty ride repurposed as a human-powered electricity generator. By mounting a child's bicycle onto the ride's crankshaft and inverting it, pedaling or cranking drives both the bouncing elephant mechanism and a potential generator. The project is intentionally simple and playful, with the creator sketching future directions like an electric cargo tricycle or pedal-powered food/drink appliances.

## Key Topics
- Coin-operated kiddie ride (pink elephant) as a creative reuse platform
- Human-powered electricity generation via a repurposed bicycle drivetrain
- Replacing an AC electric motor with a generator
- Competition entry for Robert Murray-Smith's challenge
- Future project concepts: electric-assist cargo tricycle, pedal-powered blender/ice grinder

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- AC electric motor (built into the kitty ride, candidate for replacement with a generator)
- Child's bicycle (mounted to the ride's crankshaft as the drive mechanism)
- LEDs (proposed decoration/load for generated electricity)
- LCD screens and audio equipment (used in a prior artistic installation of the same ride)

## Techniques and Methods
- Inverting a bicycle and mounting it directly to an existing crankshaft to avoid building a separate base
- Using a wheel-to-wheel friction/belt transmission to transfer mechanical power to the ride mechanism and (proposed) generator
- Motor-to-generator substitution concept for human-powered electricity production
- Hand-cranking as an alternative to foot pedals
