## Overview
Robert briefly tests the water resistance and adhesion properties of his custom conductive ink formulation by attempting to scrub it off a plastic disc under running water. Despite vigorous scrubbing with a scouring pad, the ink proves extremely durable — it only came off by removing the top layer of the plastic itself. The video serves as an unplanned but informative mechanical durability demonstration before his next build (a Dirod electrostatic generator/motor).

## Key Topics
- Water resistance testing of Robert's conductive ink
- Adhesion strength of the ink to plastic surfaces

- Alternative approach using aluminium tape sectors instead of conductive ink for the Dirod disc

- Plastic disc (substrate used in the adhesion/water resistance test)

## Techniques and Methods
- Wet scrubbing test: running the ink-coated plastic disc under tap water while scrubbing with a scouring pad to assess water resistance and adhesion
- Visual/manual assessment of ink removal and substrate damage
