## Overview
Robert demonstrates how to build a "safety heater" — an alcohol burner that automatically extinguishes if knocked over, by applying the principle of the Davy lamp. The design uses a stainless steel mesh as a flame arrestor to separate the fuel reservoir from the open flame, meaning the flame cannot travel back through the mesh to the fuel source. The result is described as one of the safest possible DIY burners.

## Key Topics
- The Davy lamp principle as the scientific basis for the design
- Flame arrestor mechanics (mesh prevents flame from passing back to the fuel)
- Construction of a low-cost, safe alcohol burner from household/scavenged materials
- Fuel vaporisation via heat conducted through the wire mesh
- Knock-over safety: burner self-extinguishes when displaced
- Re-priming and re-lighting the burner
- Potential applications (chimney stoves, vortex burners, rocket stoves)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Stainless steel mesh** — 1 mm hole separation; acts as the flame arrestor (available on Amazon, typically sold as bird/pest exclusion mesh)
- **Sugar jar lid / bean tin / P tin** — used as the fuel canister body
- **Oasis (floral foam)** — used as the wicking material inside the fuel canister
- **Cotton wool / carbon felt** — mentioned as alternative wicking materials
- **Metal strapping** — used in house construction (from builders' supplies); bent into a ring to support the upper mesh
- **Hose clip** — used to fasten the mesh to the ring
- **Isopropanol (IPA)** — primary fuel demonstrated
- **Ethanol** — confirmed as a working fuel
- **Methanol** — confirmed as a working fuel; Robert notes he has a video on making methanol from plastic
- **Hand sanitiser / methylated spirits (meths)** — mentioned in comments as viable fuels

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting stainless steel mesh to shape with scissors
- Pressing a tin lid into floral foam (Oasis) to fill the canister compartment with wicking material
- Bending metal strapping into a support ring and fastening mesh with a hose clip
- Pre-priming the burner with a paper soaked in alcohol before lighting
- Knock-over extinguishing test to demonstrate safety

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Oasis is not required** — any wicking material works: cotton wool, carbon felt, or anything that wicks. He used Oasis purely because he had it to hand. He explicitly warns that mentioning specific materials risks people thinking they are mandatory.
- **Mesh hole size matters** — 1 mm is correct; going larger prevents the flame-arrestor effect from working. Smaller sizes are worth experimenting with.
- **Fuel options** — confirmed methanol, ethanol, and isopropanol. Also mentions hand sanitiser and meths as viable. Notes that most alcohols already contain some water absorbed from the air.
- **Ring construction** — many options work: napkin ring, halved tin can, freehand bent and pop-riveted strip, a slice of pipe. The only structural requirement is that it supports the upper mesh at the right height.
- **Oasis does not overheat** — the cooling effect of alcohol vaporisation keeps it cool. People worried about this should simply use a different wicking material.
- **Replication video** — links to a replication by "caryex2": https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gTR6yiPW3OA
- **Related videos** — refers viewers to his own video *"1728 – Playing with Fire"* for background on alcohol burners.
- **Broader applications** — confirms the burner head can be integrated into rocket stoves; says he has ordered parts to adapt it further.
- **Credit** — Robert explicitly says he considers this idea clever in its original form and sees himself as applying it, not inventing it from scratch.
