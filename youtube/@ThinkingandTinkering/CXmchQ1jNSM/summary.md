## Overview
This video explores the EMF/EMI shielding and energy scavenging potential of electrostatically bonded graphene on a Bowet plastic substrate. Robert and a colleague (Mandy) compare Wi-Fi signal attenuation across several conductive materials using an EM radiation meter, then demonstrate a radiant energy harvesting circuit that converts ambient AC electromagnetic waves into usable DC voltage — briefly spinning a small motor via a joule thief circuit.

## Key Topics
- EMF/EMI shielding performance of graphene-coated Bowet vs. plain plastic and aluminium foil
- Measuring Wi-Fi signal attenuation using an electromagnetic radiation meter (V/m and µT readings)
- Comparison of standard ink vs. water-resistant ink printed on Bowet
- Energy scavenging from ambient radio/Wi-Fi signals using a rectifying circuit
- Voltage build-up and power extraction using a capacitor and joule thief
- Plans for a radiant energy phone charger/torch and an upcoming workshop combining radiant energy and microbial fuel cell projects

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene** — electrostatically bonded onto Bowet substrate
- **Bowet** — ~100 micron thick plastic substrate used as the base sheet
- **Water-resistant ink** — printed onto Bowet as one test sample
- **Standard ink** — printed onto Bowet as a second test sample
- **Aluminium foil** — used as a reference shielding material for comparison

## Techniques and Methods
- Electrostatic bonding of graphene onto a plastic (Bowet) substrate
- EM field measurement using a handheld radiation detector (dual-channel: V/m electrical field and µT magnetic field)
- Wi-Fi signal attenuation testing by interposing materials between a Wi-Fi hub and the meter
- Wrapping graphene-Bowet sheet around a stanion (post) to form a quasi-cubic antenna/shield
- Rectification of ambient AC electromagnetic radiation to DC using a cheap rectifying circuit (~$1–2)
- Energy storage via capacitor
- Power boosting via joule thief circuit to drive a small DC motor
