## Overview
Robert demonstrates that his previously built air/wind motor (from video 1584) can also function as a water motor, showing how a "prime mover" converts any input energy source into usable motion. He then extends the device into a simple generator by attaching a magnet and coil, explaining the components of a complete generation system. The key takeaway is that a motor and generator are distinct components, and the motor can be driven by multiple energy sources.

## Key Topics
- Definition and role of a "prime mover" in energy conversion
- The difference between a motor and a generator
- Electromagnetic induction as the basis of electricity generation
- Demonstrating the device running on both wind (hairdryer) and water
- Why specialised designs (e.g. Pelton wheels, wind blades) improve efficiency for specific energy sources

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- A 3 mm bar (used as the motor shaft)
- A magnet (attached to the top of the bar, described as a weak magnet)
- A coil (placed next to the spinning magnet to form the generator section)

## Techniques and Methods
- Attaching a magnet to the motor shaft to convert rotational motion into electricity via electromagnetic induction
- Using a hairdryer as a wind source to spin the motor
- Using flowing water as an alternative energy input to the same motor
- Measuring output voltage with a multimeter
