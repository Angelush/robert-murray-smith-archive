## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores earth batteries — electrodes (metal or carbon) inserted into soil to generate small electrical currents — framing them as a fascinating crossover between energy storage and energy generation. He debates whether their output stems from galvanic (corrosion-based) reactions or from tapping natural telluric currents, suggesting that if the latter, they qualify as a form of "free energy" generator. Using scrap materials and a digital multimeter, he conducts a live outdoor experiment measuring voltages across multiple electrode combinations and encourages viewers to investigate further at home.
