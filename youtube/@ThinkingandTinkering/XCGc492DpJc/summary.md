## Overview
Robert demonstrates a simple, tool-free trick for removing the cotterless crankshaft from a bicycle or exercise bicycle without a specialist crank puller. After struggling for up to an hour on a heavily rusted bike, he discovered that sharply striking the metal causes it to expand just enough to break the interference fit, allowing the crankshaft to pop free in a couple of taps. The method works in about 2–5 minutes and requires no specialist tools.

## Key Topics
- The difficulty of removing cotterless crankshafts without a dedicated crank puller
- Robert's previous (less effective) approach: clamping in a vice with a 10mm bolt and hammering
- The thermal/impact expansion trick that finally worked on a severely rusted bike
- Comparing the new method (~5 minutes) to the old struggle (~45–60 minutes)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Vice + 10mm bolt + hammer**: Robert's prior standard method — bolt inserted into the crank thread, struck repeatedly; effective on lightly corroded cranks but failed on heavily rusted ones
- **Impact-expansion trick**: Striking the crank body directly (rather than the bolt) to cause the metal to expand enough to break the press fit; described as working in a couple of sharp taps
- **Angle grinder** (considered but avoided): Would have been used as a last resort to cut the crank off
