## Overview
In this video, Robert Murray-Smith adapts a tea towel-based vertical axis wind turbine (built in video 1353) to more closely match an original folding wind turbine design submitted by a viewer named Ron Ratting. The adaptation involves adding stays and extra arms to create a three-bladed rotor with fabric sails, resulting in a collapsible, portable wind turbine. Robert tests it outdoors and begins planning a further evolution toward an H-Darrieus configuration with NACA airfoil blades.

## Key Topics
- Adapting a previous tea towel-based Savonius wind turbine toward Ron Ratting's original folding design
- Constructing three triangular fabric sails from an old bed sheet
- Using Wonder Web (iron-on hem tape) as an alternative to sewing
- Treating fabric with diluted PVA glue for waterproofing and stiffening
- Assembling a three-armed rotor at 120-degree spacing using wooden discs
- Outdoor testing of the completed turbine
- Discussion of the next step: an H-Darrieus design with NACA 0015 airfoil blades
- Design goal of portability and collapsibility for off-grid or field use

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Bed sheet (light cotton)** — used as sail material
- **Wonder Web (iron-on fusible web / Elmer's-style fabric glue)** — used to hem the sail triangles without sewing
- **PVA glue (50/50 mix with water)** — painted onto the fabric to waterproof and stiffen the sails (equivalent to Elmer's White Glue)
- **Metal stay / fiberglass stays** — used as structural rods through the central bars
- **Plastic pipe** — mentioned as an alternative sail substitute for a simpler collapsible turbine
- **Ripstop nylon / sail canvas** — mentioned as higher-quality sail material alternatives

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling 3 mm holes (for metal stays) or 4 mm holes (for fiberglass stays) at the midpoint of 60 cm central bars
- Spacing three rotor arms at 120-degree intervals using drilled wooden discs
- Cutting triangular sail panels (60 cm long, 34 cm from point to center) with hem allowance
- Hemming fabric using Wonder Web iron-on tape as a no-sew seaming method
- Painting sails with a 50/50 PVA glue and water solution to stiffen and waterproof the fabric
- Gluing and stapling the finished sails onto the rotor bars
- Collapsing and folding the assembled turbine into a compact package
