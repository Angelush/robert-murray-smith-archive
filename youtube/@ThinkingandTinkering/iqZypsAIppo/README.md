## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains how to build a three-phase serpentine coil generator by simply placing three identical serpentine coils side by side, offset so that each occupies one-third of the time a magnet spends crossing them — producing outputs 120 electrical degrees apart. He then argues that for most home generation setups, three-phase offers no practical advantage over single-phase, since the real benefits of three-phase are in power transmission and running motors, not in generation itself.
