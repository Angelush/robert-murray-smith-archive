## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the history of propeller-driven trains, framing them within the broader context of the revolutionary impact of railways and the early aviation craze of the early 20th century. He profiles three key experimental propeller trains — the Russian Aerowagon, the German Rail Zeppelin, and the Scottish Benny Rail Plane — and concludes that while such inventions may seem absurd in hindsight, out-of-the-box thinking like this is precisely what drives genuine innovation.
