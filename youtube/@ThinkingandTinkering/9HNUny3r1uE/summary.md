## Overview
Robert and a friend take two Eskute e-bikes — the Voyager and the Wayfarer — out for a ride to shoot promotional photos for the brand. Beyond the promo content, Robert shares his genuine surprise at how enjoyable the pedal-assist (PAS) mode is compared to the throttle, and reflects on recent changes to English highway law that have made cycling a more comfortable experience on public roads.

## Robert's Own Replies
- The Eskute bikes do have **front suspension**, but Robert dismisses complaints about ride comfort with humour — noting that when he was a kid, your backside was the only suspension a bike had.
- On the Highway Code changes: cyclists now have **right of way priority**, and Robert believes it is effectively the driver's fault by default if a cyclist is hit.
- He acknowledges he would get significant **viewer criticism** if he didn't wear a helmet, implying he wore one partly out of viewer expectation.
- A commenter apparently worried his friend might actually crash into the puddle — Robert confirms he was genuinely worried too.
- He enthusiastically encourages a commenter who mentions they're considering getting an e-bike, saying they had great fun.
