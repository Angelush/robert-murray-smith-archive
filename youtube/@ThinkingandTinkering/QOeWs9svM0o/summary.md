## Overview
Robert takes the orrery mechanism he built in video 2306 and repurposes it as a 3D-printed Moon Nightlight Mobile for children, featuring a lithophane moon globe lit from within by an LED and Apollo mission spacecraft orbiting it via a hand-cranked worm-gear drive. The key insight is that the mechanism is modular and holiday-agnostic — the central globe and orbiting figures can be swapped for Halloween (pumpkin, ghost, jack-o'-lantern) or Christmas theming, making it a versatile, interactive gift.

## Key Topics
- Reuse of the orrery mechanism from video 2306 as a nightlight/mobile platform
- 3D-printed design created in TinkerCAD
- Lithophane moon globe as the central illuminated element
- Apollo mission Command Module and Lunar Lander as orbiting decorative elements
- Modular, swappable central globe and orbiting figures for seasonal themes (Halloween, Christmas)
- Deliberate choice of hand-crank over motor to encourage hands-on interaction
- Use of Creative Commons models from Thingiverse for the lithophane moon, Apollo spacecraft, and feet
- STL files shared on Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **COB LED** — salvaged from a defunct solar garden light; used to illuminate the lithophane globe from within
- **Thrust bearing** (42 mm × 25 mm × 11 mm deep) — optional but improves rotation smoothness
- **Battery box** — powers the LED, includes an on/off switch
- **Zip ties** — used for wire management
- **Glue** — used to fix gears, the worm gear, arm, and moon globe in place
- **Wire/solder** — to connect the LED to the battery pack

## Techniques and Methods
- **TinkerCAD CAD design** — modelling the entire mechanism and custom parts for 3D printing
- **FDM 3D printing** — printing all structural and decorative components from STL files
- **Worm-gear drive mechanism** — 90° torque redirection from hand crank to ring gear/central gear
- **Lithophane printing** — moon surface rendered as a translucent lithophane globe (sourced from Thingiverse)
- **LED wiring and soldering** — attaching wires to the COB LED, routing them through the central light stand
- **Creative Commons asset reuse** — sourcing and combining Thingiverse models (moon, Apollo craft, feet) with original mechanism design
- **Modular assembly** — central globe and orbiting figures left unglued for easy seasonal swapping

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly brief acknowledgements ("cheers mate"), but a few contain useful clarifications:
- He **specifically chose not to add a motor** because he values engagement — he wants users to physically handle and interact with the device: *"I want engagement"* and *"I like the idea that you have to handle it — get close to it — nothing like having something in your hands."*
- He confirms a **small motor would work** for hands-free operation if desired.
- He confirms the **battery box just lifts out** — it is not fixed permanently.
- He records **in his own house**, so acoustic treatment (e.g. foam) is not feasible.
- He credits **Venice Inventors** in comments for originally suggesting the seasonal theming idea, consistent with his on-video acknowledgement.
- He encouraged community members to attempt building their own versions: *"do it! make a run at it — the hardest step is the first mate."*
