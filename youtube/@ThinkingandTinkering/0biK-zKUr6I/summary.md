## Overview
A short practical build video in which Luke (presenting on Robert Murray-Smith's channel) repurposes a collection of old lithium mobile phone batteries to construct a custom battery pack intended to power a wireless speaker for 2–3 hours. The build centres on wiring four cells in series and integrating a Battery Management System (BMS) to regulate output voltage.

## Key Topics
- Motivation: a dead wireless speaker battery prompting a DIY replacement build
- Selecting and testing salvaged lithium phone batteries before use
- Wiring four cells in series to achieve ~15 V nominal
- Role of the BMS as both a protection circuit and voltage regulator
- Verifying output voltage with a multimeter (~13 V measured)
- Safety considerations when working with lithium cells

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Lithium mobile phone batteries (rated 3,500 mAh each at 3.85 V)
- Battery Management System (BMS) — acts as protection circuit and voltage regulator

## Techniques and Methods
- Series wiring of four lithium cells to sum voltages (~15 V raw)
- Soldering connections to the BMS (B+/B− and P+/P− terminals)
- Multimeter voltage testing at both raw cell level and regulated BMS output
