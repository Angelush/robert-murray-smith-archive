## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a pair of steampunk-style speakers inspired by a friend's steampunk computer project. Starting with cheap £5 USB speakers, he fabricates gramophone-style flared cones from PVC board using a petal/trapezoid construction method, mounts them on bent copper towel-holder brackets, and produces a functional and visually striking result. The video demonstrates that creative DIY aesthetics can be achieved with minimal cost and simple materials.

## Key Topics
- Motivation: envy of a friend's steampunk computer build
- Design approach: steampunk gramophone cone aesthetic
- Geometry of cone construction using 8 trapezoid "petals" forming an octagon approximating a circle
- Template-making technique using flexible dowel as a curve guide
- Material selection for the cone panels
- Repurposing copper towel holders as speaker stands with S-bend shaping
- Assembly, spray painting, and wiring the finished speakers

## Techniques and Methods
- Trapezoid/petal cone construction: 8 curved trapezoid panels glued edge-to-edge to form a flared cone
- Flexible-dowel curve templating: bending a dowel between two weights to define and draw a consistent curve
- Mirror-flip templating: cutting one curved side, flipping the template on its centre line to get a symmetrical mirror curve
- S-bend shaping of copper pipe/towel holders by hand
- Spray painting for finish
- Soldering speaker wires back onto the driver units
