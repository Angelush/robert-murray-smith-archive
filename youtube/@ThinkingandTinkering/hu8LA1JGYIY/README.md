## Overview

Robert examines the "Beta.ey" spherical solar concentrator invented by André Brøssel, which uses a large glass ball as a focusing lens for a small photovoltaic cell and claims 57% efficiency. He tests the principle hands-on using a salvaged car headlight bullseye lens and fluorescent acrylic panels on a cheap solar cell, demonstrating modest but real concentration effects. His conclusion is that while the design is visually striking, it faces the same fundamental problems as all concentrated photovoltaic systems and the efficiency claims are likely overstated.
