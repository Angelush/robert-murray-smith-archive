## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates an improved design for a large-scale copper coil alcohol burner that overcomes the key limitation of traditional coil burners — their inability to handle large fuel reservoirs. By using two concentric copper pipes with an activated carbon felt wick between them, he creates a self-heating vaporizer that burns ethanol cleanly and at high heat output. The device is then adapted into a practical space heater using a stainless steel mantle and a terracotta plant pot.

## Key Topics
- Limitations of traditional coiled copper alcohol burners with large fuel volumes
- Concentric copper pipe design as a solution for scaling up fuel capacity
- Activated carbon felt as a wicking material between the pipes
- Flame height control via adjustable inner pipe height
- Drilled holes in the inner pipe to produce a taller, full-length blue flame
- Converting the burner into a mantle heater and a plant pot radiant/convection heater

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Ethanol (alcohol)** — primary fuel for the burner
- **Copper pipe (22mm / ¾ inch)** — outer pipe of the concentric assembly
- **Copper pipe (15mm / ½ inch)** — inner pipe; also used with 1mm holes drilled along four sides for enhanced flame distribution
- **Activated carbon felt** — used as the wick wrapped around the inner pipe; noted as an industrial product also available on Amazon marketed as a plumbing filter material
- **JB Weld** — used to seal/finish the pipe assembly
- **Stainless steel mesh roll** — repurposed vermin-proofing strip, folded into a mantle around the burner
- **Terracotta plant pot** — used as a radiant heat body placed over the flame

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling a 20mm hole in a lid to accept the outer copper pipe
- Wrapping the inner pipe with activated carbon felt before sliding on the outer pipe to create a wicked annular gap
- Assembling the concentric pipe unit and sealing with JB Weld
- Adjusting inner pipe height to control flame intensity
- Drilling 1mm holes along four sides of the inner pipe to distribute the flame along its full length
- Placing a stainless steel mantle and plant pot over the assembly to capture and radiate heat via both convection and radiation

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirmed ethanol is the same as drinking alcohol, joking that "some would argue it was a poison."
- When asked about burn duration, estimated the large reservoir would last **hours**, not years.
- When asked about corrosion/degradation of materials, speculated it would take **years** and noted it likely doesn't matter given the low mechanical stress on the assembly.
- Acknowledged the design is exploratory ("really just an idea — might be worth investigating") and encouraged commenters to try it themselves.
