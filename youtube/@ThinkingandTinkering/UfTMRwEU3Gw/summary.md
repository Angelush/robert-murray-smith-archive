## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple desktop catapult as a Christmas gift project, using cheap cutting boards as a hardwood source. The build requires only four pieces of wood, two dowels, and a couple of elastic bands, with most of the effort going into sanding and finishing rather than complex construction.

## Key Topics
- "Breadboard projects" series — using cheap cutting boards as a source of hardwood for gift-making
- Step-by-step construction of a small desktop catapult
- Sanding and finishing techniques to make the piece look attractive as a desk ornament
- Using worn sandpaper as an improvised cabinet scraper for a silky wood finish

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Hardwood cutting boards (beech or similar, ~£2 each) — used as the primary wood source
- 9 mm wooden dowel — used for the pivot pin and elastic band anchor
- Elastic bands (x2) — power source for the catapult's throwing arm
- Wood oil — applied after finishing for a final sheen

## Techniques and Methods
- Rough cutting wood into base, uprights, and throwing arm with a saw
- Taping uprights together before drilling to ensure matching hole registration
- Drilling with a **forstner bit** (called a "frosting bit") to create a smooth dish-shaped indentation on the throwing arm, avoiding the puncture hole left by a spade bit
- Sanding through 80-grit then 120-grit paper
- Using a worn piece of sandpaper held in a vice as an improvised **cabinet scraper** for a final silky finish
- Driving a 9 mm dowel into a 9 mm hole (tight fit) and a 10 mm hole (loose fit) to create the pivot mechanism
- Mounting uprights to the base by drilling through from both sides after pencil-marking
