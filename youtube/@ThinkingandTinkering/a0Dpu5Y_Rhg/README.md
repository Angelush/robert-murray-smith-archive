## Overview

In this reflective talk, Robert Murray-Smith responds to a viewer's question about how he inspires people, distilling his approach into a practical list of interpersonal principles. Rather than presenting a personal formula, he frames inspiration as a set of learnable behaviours rooted in genuine care, honesty, and respect for others. His central takeaway is that meaningful inspiration comes from consistent small actions, and that changing one behaviour at a time — practised over ten days to two weeks until it becomes habitual — is the most realistic path forward.
