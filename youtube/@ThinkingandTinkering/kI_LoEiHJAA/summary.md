## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith gives a hands-on walkthrough of how to level the print bed on the Elegoo Neptune Max 3D printer, explaining the physical mechanism in detail before demonstrating the manual leveling procedure step by step. He clarifies the counter-intuitive relationship between tightening/loosening the adjustment knobs and bed movement, and explains how manual leveling must precede the printer's auto-leveling routine. His main takeaway is that bed leveling is simpler than it seems once you understand the mechanics and just get hands-on with it.

## Key Topics
- Overview of the Elegoo Neptune Max 3D printer and its design philosophy
- Physical construction of the print bed: carriage plate, spring-loaded bolts, central fixed bolts, and outer adjustment knobs
- Explanation of how the bed tilts and how the three outer adjustment knobs control that tilt
- Step-by-step manual leveling procedure using a piece of paper to gauge nozzle gap
- The printer's on-screen leveling menu and point selection (positions 1–6)
- Auto-leveling routine that runs after manual leveling, performed with a heated bed
- Standalone vs. USB/SD card operation modes
- Design details Robert appreciates: dual-sided print surface, directional arrows on knobs, built-in tool drawer

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Flexible print bed surface (rough side and smooth side) — for print adhesion
- Paper — used as a feeler gauge during the manual leveling procedure

## Techniques and Methods
- Manual bed leveling using a paper drag test at each adjustment point
- Spring-loaded bolt adjustment: tightening pulls the bed down; loosening allows the spring to push it back up
- Sequential corner/center leveling pattern (center first, then opposite corners, cycling between points)
- Auto-leveling routine via the printer's onboard touchscreen menu (runs after manual leveling, with heated bed)
