## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a self-heating cup made by applying his conductive ink/paint to a ceramic mug with copper strip contacts at the base. Running at 24V (current-limited to ~48W), the cup heats cold water from 12°C to 82°C over roughly an hour — enough to brew a real cup of tea. The video serves as both a fun demonstration and a proof-of-concept for the ink's broader heating applications.

## Key Topics
- Building a self-heating ceramic cup using conductive paint
- Electrical characterisation: resistance (~9 ohms), voltage (24V), current (3A), power (~48W)
- Temperature progression over time, monitored with a sugar thermometer
- Government research on preferred hot-drink serving temperatures (~60°C ±8°C)
- Product design critique: safety (exposed contacts), efficiency (heat radiation losses), insulation options
- Broader commercial and industrial applications of the conductive ink

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Conductive paint/ink** — applied to the outside of the ceramic cup as the heating element
- **Copper strips** — used as electrical contact points on the cup base
- **Nickel / nickel-coated spring contacts** — used in the base plate to connect power to the cup
- **Ceramic cup** — substrate for the conductive ink
- **Tea bag** — used to brew tea and validate the temperature achieved

## Techniques and Methods
- Painting conductive ink conformally onto a cylindrical ceramic surface
- Using spring contacts on a base plate for tool-free electrical connection
- Voltage- and current-limiting the power supply to cap wattage (~48W) and avoid runaway heating
- Temperature measurement with a sugar thermometer (12°C–200°C range)
- Controlling steady-state temperature by restricting wattage (~40W targets ~70°C)

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly brief acknowledgements, but several add useful context:
- **Kit and sleeve:** He was considering turning the heated cup into a purchasable kit, and liked the suggestion of a neoprene sleeve for insulation and safety.
- **Cost reality:** He noted that for a commercial mug heater, the cost budget is only "a dollar or two to play with," making the ink's price a key constraint.
- **Boiling water irony:** He wryly pointed out the no-win situation — if he had boiled the water, commenters would have called it dangerous; since he didn't, he was told he should have. He acknowledged the irony without complaint.
- **Debate on boiling and tea:** He noted there is genuine debate about whether boiling water "scalds" the tea leaves.
- **Furnace idea:** He mentioned he owns a furnace and was interested in trying a suggestion involving one.
- **Home load-levelling:** He responded positively to a suggestion about using the ink heating system for home-based energy load levelling, calling it "very do-able."
- **"Stubby holders":** He was unfamiliar with the Australian term for insulating can/cup sleeves and said he'd look into UK equivalents.
