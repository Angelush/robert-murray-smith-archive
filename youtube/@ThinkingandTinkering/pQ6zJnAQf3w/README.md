## Overview

Robert demonstrates an upgrade to a previously built Stirling engine — the addition of a water jacket around the cylinder. The modification significantly improves thermal efficiency, allowing the engine to run continuously from a very low-grade heat source: just three tea light candles. He concludes by noting the obvious next step would be to attach a generator coil (bobbin) to measure electrical output.
