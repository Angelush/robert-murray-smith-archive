## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a quick and simple method for fabricating NACA 0015 airfoil blades for a Darrieus-type (Gyromill) vertical-axis wind turbine. By folding a thin sheet of aluminium in half, he exploits the metal's natural spring to approximate the correct aerodynamic profile without complex tooling. The resulting blades are filled with expanding foam and fiberglass filler, making them lightweight and fast to produce.

## Key Topics
- Background on the NACA airfoil naming system (4-, 5-, and 6-digit codes)
- The NACA 0015 profile and its use in Darrieus/Gyromill wind turbines
- Folded aluminium sheet as a shortcut for forming an airfoil shape
- Filling and reinforcing the hollow airfoil with expanding foam and fiberglass filler
- Attaching the blades to a DIY music-stand wind turbine frame
- Plans to add a generation (electrical output) section in a future video

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **0.3 mm aluminium sheet** — reclaimed from school notice boards; bent to form the airfoil skin
- **Gap-filling expanding foam** — used to fill the hollow core of each airfoil
- **Isopon PE 40** (fiberglass-filled body filler) — used to fill and harden the ends of the airfoil
- **Isopon P38** — mentioned as an alternative fine body filler
- **uPVC fascia/soffit board ("builders board")** — cut into pads to act as blade attachment points

## Techniques and Methods
- Folding aluminium sheet in half over a wooden bending jig to form the NACA 0015 profile
- Verifying the bent profile against a printed/drawn NACA 0015 template
- Taping the folded aluminium, then injecting expanding foam to fill the core
- Applying fiberglass filler (PE 40) to the open ends for a strong, workable surface
- Stacking and drilling uPVC board pads, then gluing them to the trailing edge for blade-to-strut attachment
- Slotting finished blades onto the struts of a repurposed music-stand turbine frame

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Blade material clarification:** The "builders board" is uPVC fascia/soffit board; he linked a specific product example.
- **NACA 0015 for Gyromills:** He confirms the NACA 0015 is an established choice for Gyromill (H-form Darrieus) blades, noting there is published research on NACA profiles for gyromill design.
- **Correct turbine terminology:** He clarifies the build is strictly a Gyromill (H-form Darrieus), not the more familiar egg-whisk Darrieus shape, and emphasises there is nothing special about his version beyond hand-forming the profile.
- **Wells turbine speculation:** He muses that the symmetric NACA 0015 profile might also suit a Wells turbine, and invites others to explore that idea.
- **Profile selection advice:** For those wanting to adapt the idea, he suggests checking the full NACA database to find the optimal profile for their specific application.
