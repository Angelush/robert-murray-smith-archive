## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a quick and simple method for fabricating NACA 0015 airfoil blades for a Darrieus-type (Gyromill) vertical-axis wind turbine. By folding a thin sheet of aluminium in half, he exploits the metal's natural spring to approximate the correct aerodynamic profile without complex tooling. The resulting blades are filled with expanding foam and fiberglass filler, making them lightweight and fast to produce.
