## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simple, low-cost method for cleaning rusty, oily, and greasy small metal parts using a homemade vibratory cleaner combined with a "miracle cleaning solution" made from common household ingredients. After just 30 minutes, parts go from dirty and rusty to remarkably clean. The key insight is combining a vibrating orbital sander (with a Tupperware container attached) with an acidic cleaning solution and appropriate abrasive media.

## Key Topics
- The problem of cleaning large quantities of rusty, oily small metal parts
- Overview of cleaning approaches (wire brushing, evaporative, tumble/vibratory cleaning)
- Building a DIY vibratory cleaner from an orbital sander and Tupperware
- Choosing the right cleaning media for different outcomes (cleaning vs. polishing)
- The "miracle cleaning solution" recipe using household chemicals
- 30-minute demonstration showing before/after results

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **White vinegar** (5% acetic acid) — primary acidic cleaning agent, 400 ml
- **Table salt** — added to vinegar to enhance rust removal, 2 tablespoons
- **Washing-up liquid (dish soap)** — surfactant/degreaser, 20 ml
- **Isopropanol (IPA / rubbing alcohol)** — anti-foaming agent, 100 ml
- **Zirconium oxide cooking beads** — primary cleaning media used in the demo
- **Glass frit** — alternative cleaning/polishing media
- **Carborundum (silicon carbide)** — mentioned as a hard media option for paint removal
- **Gravel chips** — mentioned as a low-cost cleaning media alternative
- **Cat litter** — mentioned as an alternative media
- **Crushed walnut shells** — mentioned as an alternative media
- **Sodium carbonate** — mentioned as a softer polishing media option
- **Ruby sand / agate sand** — mentioned as hard media for paint stripping

## Techniques and Methods
- **Vibratory cleaning** — placing parts and abrasive media in a container attached to a vibrating motor (orbital sander) to mechanically scrub surfaces
- **DIY vibratory cleaner construction** — attaching a Tupperware box to a flatbed orbital sander with double-sided tape
- **Wet vibratory cleaning** — running the cleaner with liquid cleaning solution rather than dry media
- **Media selection by hardness** — using coarse/hard media for rust and paint removal; soft media for polishing
- **Acidic rust removal** — acetic acid from vinegar dissolving iron oxide
- **Degreasing** — washing-up liquid removing oils and grease
