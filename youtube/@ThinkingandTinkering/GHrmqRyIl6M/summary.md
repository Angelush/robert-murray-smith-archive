## Overview
Robert visits Cologne (Köln), Germany as part of a trip through Vienna, Munich, and Cologne, offering a whistle-stop tour of the city's history and highlights. He also provides detailed practical guidance on the COVID-era travel requirements for British citizens travelling to Europe post-Brexit, covering vaccinations, passenger locator forms, and testing. The overall takeaway is that European travel in late 2021 was manageable but required careful preparation with paperwork.

## Key Topics
- Cologne Cathedral (Dom) — described as the third-tallest church in the world and tallest cathedral, undergoing ongoing restoration
- Cologne's claim to having the most pubs per capita of any German city, surpassing even Munich
- WWII bombing history — most bombed city in Germany; population reduced to 5% of pre-war levels; post-war reconstruction focused on 12 churches, the city gates, and the Dom
- History of Eau de Cologne, originating around 1709
- The love lock bridge tradition (noted as originating in Prague)
- The Rhine River — surprisingly clean, with salmon present, and pleasant riverside promenades
- Roman history of Cologne; Franks expelled Romans around 462 AD
- Flood defences along the Rhine, including a lock gate near the Chocolate Museum
- Proposed use of Archimedes screw generators at underutilised flood infrastructure (100–150 kWh generation potential noted)
- Cologne's extensive cycling path network and city-registered e-scooter schemes
- Cologne's 30–33 museums, including the Chocolate Museum
- Post-Brexit COVID travel requirements: double vaccination, passenger locator forms for each country transited, negative antigen test before departure, and a pre-booked two-day post-return test for re-entry to the UK

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Eau de Cologne** — men's fragrance/perfume, historically associated with Cologne, reportedly first produced around 1709; discussed in a cultural/historical context, not a scientific one

## Techniques and Methods
- **Antigen (lateral flow) testing** — 15-minute COVID tests used for pre-departure certification; walk-in test centres noted as well-organised in Cologne, Munich, and Vienna (free in Vienna)
- **Archimedes screw generators** — proposed (not implemented at this site) as a means of generating electricity from the flow through existing flood-control lock gates; Robert references a similar installation in London as a future video subject

## Robert's Own Replies
Most of Robert's replies are brief social exchanges, but two stand out as substantive:

- **On paper vs. digital**: He explains his preference for printing all travel documents — "I just carry paper copies because I don't trust digital to work when it is most needed" — reinforcing the practical advice given in the video.
- **On vaccine conspiracy theories**: Responding to a commenter apparently raising graphene oxide claims, Robert clearly dismisses them: *"I am sorry mate — I don't believe they put graphene oxide in the vaccine — even if they do — I don't really care, I also don't really care if the vaccine works or not — I need to travel and I also need that certificate to travel — end of story for me."* This reflects his pragmatic, no-nonsense attitude toward the travel requirements.
