## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a variable-speed ball mill using a household drill, recycled desk legs as rollers, inline skate bearings, and a wooden cradle. He explains the two fundamental ball mill designs (central-axis and dual-roller), makes the case for the dual-roller design as the more practical homebuild, and walks through the complete construction from cutting timber to a working final assembly. The key takeaway is that a functional ball mill can be built in roughly two hours from scrap materials at zero cost.
