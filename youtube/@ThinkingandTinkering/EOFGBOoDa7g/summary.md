## Overview
This video is a community contest entry responding to Robert Murray-Smith's challenge to build something from paper with at least one moving part. The creator, working alongside their grandchildren and a friend, built an imaginative interactive paper sculpture — a tetrahedron on wheels featuring a sailboat, fish, and various moving elements — born from spontaneous creativity rather than a fixed plan.

## Key Topics
- Robert's "Made From Paper" competition challenge (minimum one moving part)
- Intergenerational building: grandparent creating with grandchildren
- Evolution of the project from a remote-controlled robot concept to a nautical sculpture
- Encouraging spontaneous, child-led creativity in toy-making
- Presenting the finished interactive paper sculpture

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Paper (primary construction material)
- Cork (used for the base)
- Wood shavings (decorative, sourced from a craft store)
- Sand (used as ballast weight inside the boat to prevent it falling off the turret)
- Pipe cleaner (used to make a small figure)
- Casters from an old bed frame (used for mobility)

## Techniques and Methods
- Tetrahedron construction from paper
- Paper suspension system built to elevate the structure above RC truck wheels
- Partial enclosure/cladding of the tetrahedron frame
- Sand-weighted boat bottom to allow repositioning on a turret without falling
- Attaching a small pinwheel to move in the wind
- Decorating with drawn waves on the enclosure panels
