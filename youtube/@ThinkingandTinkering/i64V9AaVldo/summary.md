## Overview
Luke demonstrates a simple DIY method for removing a large dent from a plastic car bumper using nothing more than boiling water and hand pressure. After removing the rear bumper, he pours hot water over the dented plastic to soften it, then pushes the dent out from the inside. The result is near-perfect restoration with no visible trace of the original dent.

## Key Topics
- Removing a plastic rear bumper from a car
- Using heat to soften plastic for dent repair
- Pushing dents out from the inside of a bumper
- Low-cost car exterior repair

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Hot water (from a kettle, used to soften plastic)
- Heat-resistant gloves (safety equipment)

## Techniques and Methods
- Removing tail light clusters to access bumper screws
- Detaching the full rear bumper by unscrewing wheel arch and boot-area fixings
- Pouring boiling water over both sides of the dented bumper to soften the plastic
- Manually pushing the dent outward from the inside while the plastic is hot
- Reattaching the bumper and light clusters
