## Overview

Robert demonstrates how to repurpose the eject motor and gear train from an old CD/DVD drive into a small hand-crank generator. Using a CD disc as a flywheel and a nail as a crank handle, he shows that the tiny DC motor can produce around 10 milliwatts (roughly 20 milliamps at half a volt) — enough to power a small LED or trickle-charge a tiny device. The video's core lesson is about gearing trade-offs: higher speed ratios yield more voltage but require greater turning effort.
