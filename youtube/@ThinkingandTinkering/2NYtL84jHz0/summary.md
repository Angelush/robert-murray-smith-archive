## Overview
This video documents the physical disassembly of a Renault Twizy electric vehicle as part of Robert's "FWG powered by paper" project, which aims to drive the Twizy from Land's End to John O'Groats using a self-developed paper-based battery. Robert walks through the full stripdown procedure step by step, culminating in the removal of the Twizy's original lithium-ion battery pack from beneath the vehicle.

## Key Topics
- Brief update on the paper-based battery fabrication process (coating active material onto paper, cutting into plates)
- Overview of the Twizy's battery architecture (lead-acid auxiliary + main lithium-ion pack)
- Complete interior disassembly sequence: seat, trim panels, floor panels
- Underside access using car ramps and a trolley jack
- Disconnecting battery connectors and retaining hardware
- Lowering and extracting the battery pack from beneath the chassis

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Paper** — used as the substrate for the active electrode material in Robert's custom battery cells
- **Lead-acid battery** — auxiliary battery located in the front of the Twizy
- **Lithium-ion battery pack** — the Twizy's main traction battery (~6.1 kWh, confirmed in comments)
- **Aluminium** — material of the battery base plate
- **Steel** — retention bars that bolt the battery pack to the chassis

## Techniques and Methods
- Sequential panel removal (clips, lugs, and interlocking/interleaved plastic panels)
- Torx fastener removal (T30 for carriage/trim, T10 for side panels)
- 14–15 mm socket work for seat and battery retaining bolts
- Organisational tip: storing fasteners in labelled bags taped to the part they came from
- Raising the vehicle on four car ramps (8-tonne rated) for underbody access
- Lowering the battery pack onto a trolley jack using ratchet straps, incrementally removing wedges
- Earthing strap disconnection before final battery drop

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Battery capacity confirmed:** "yeah - we read the label - 6.1kWh - awesome - we'll do better lol" — the original Twizy pack is 6.1 kWh; the paper battery is expected to exceed this.
- **Paper is not a gimmick:** "cheers mate and no - paper has been used as a battery separator for a 100 years or more" — clarifying that paper in batteries is well-established technology.
- **BMS rewrite required:** Multiple replies confirm that rewriting the Battery Management System is a major upcoming challenge: "you are right mate - the BMS is going to be a major task" and "no thermal control required and we are going to have to rewrite the BMS mate."
- **Paper battery weight:** "no mate - our battery will still weigh a fair bit" — the replacement pack won't be significantly lighter.
- **Centre of gravity concern:** "it also plays a part in the centre of gravity - the twixy is quite high it might make it a bit hard on cornering if you reduce the weight of the bars too much."
- **Twizy will be resold:** "we do need it mate - we are going to put it back in and resell the twizy when we have done" — the original battery will be reinstalled after the project concludes.
- **Blade battery specs withheld:** "as for the specs on the blade i am reluctant to release specs mate - just in case i make an idiot out of myself lol."
- **On the printing process:** "the printer mod is easy mate - take one printer - throw it in the bin and grab a paint brush lol" — they apply the active material by brush, not a modified printer.
