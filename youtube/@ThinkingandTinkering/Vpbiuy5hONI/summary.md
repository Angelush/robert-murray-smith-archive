## Overview
This video is a competition entry submitted by Gary Slate (from Australia) for Robert Murray-Smith's "light up an LED" contest. Gary presents his homemade backyard shed toilet — nicknamed the "Turtis" (a Tardis-shaped outhouse) — which is solar-powered, LED-lit, odour-controlled via a pressure ventilation system, and equipped with a custom water pressure system.

## Key Topics
- Robert Murray-Smith's LED lighting competition and its entry criteria
- The "Turtis": a Tardis-themed outdoor toilet built a few years prior
- Solar power system powering the entire structure
- LED lighting inside the toilet, triggered by a motion/presence sensor
- Positive pressure ventilation system to eliminate odours
- Homemade water pressure system using a salvaged party keg and caravan pump
- Automatic door mechanism triggered by the sensor

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Zinc bromide (mentioned in context of a separate previous entry — a Coca-Cola can wind generator with a zinc bromide buffer)
- Old car battery (for energy storage)
- Salvaged party keg (used as a pressure vessel for the water system)

## Techniques and Methods
- Homemade solar panel fabrication (panel mounted on roof of the Turtis)
- Solar charge controller integration with a car battery
- Positive pressure ventilation: two computer fans push air in; an exhaust fan in the wall draws air out via a gap in the toilet lid, creating low pressure that pulls air down through the toilet bowl
- Pressure-controlled water supply: caravan pump pulls from a tank, with a magnetic tube + micro switch acting as a pressure cut-off
- Sensor-triggered relay controlling lights and the pressure/door systems
