## Overview
This short tutorial demonstrates the correct technique for applying silicone sealant, presented by a collaborator named Luke. The presenter shares a tip he learned after struggling with silicone application in a previous project: using washing-up liquid on a gloved finger to smooth the bead into a clean, professional seal. The key takeaway is that a small amount of dish soap prevents the silicone from sticking to your fingers and allows it to be pressed firmly into gaps.

## Key Topics
- How to cut the tip of a silicone tube (cutting at a notch/angle)
- Loading the tube into a caulking gun
- Laying a bead of silicone along a gap
- Using washing-up liquid and a glove to smooth and seat the silicone
- Releasing pressure on the gun after application

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Silicone sealant** — the primary material being applied as a sealant/gap filler
- **Washing-up liquid (dish soap)** — applied to a gloved finger to prevent sticking and aid smoothing
- **Disposable glove** — used to protect the finger during smoothing

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting the silicone tube tip at a notch to control bead width and direction
- Orienting the nozzle notch parallel to the gap for consistent application
- Releasing pressure on the caulking gun after the bead is laid (to prevent oozing)
- Smoothing the bead with a soapy gloved finger to press silicone into the gap and create a flush seal
- Allowing to cure fully before use
