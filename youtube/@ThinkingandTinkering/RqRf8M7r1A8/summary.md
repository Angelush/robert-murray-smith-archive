## Overview
A competition entry responding to Robert Murray-Smith's challenge to build a generator from scrap and light up at least one LED. The creator converts an induction fan motor (salvaged from a microwave) into an AC generator by modifying the rotor with additional magnets and extended coils, successfully lighting a diode. The video is split into two parts: a short competition submission and a detailed teardown/build explanation.

## Key Topics
- Converting a microwave induction fan motor into a hand-spun AC generator
- Using salvaged washing machine pump motor parts as the rotor
- Modifying the stator coil by extending it with windings from an identical motor
- Challenges of non-standard shaft sizing and improvised bearing solutions
- AC vs DC output considerations and potential for rectification

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Ceramic magnets (original rotor magnets from washing machine pump motor)
- Additional neodymium/permanent magnets (stuck to rotor by the builder)
- Brass tubing (used as improvised bearing sleeve for the 3.5 mm shaft)
- Solid tape (suggested as a shim to hold loose coil bobbins)
- Thicker copper wire (planned for improved DC rectification performance)

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly of microwave induction fan motor for reuse as a generator
- Pressing out the shaft using a vise, screwdriver, and small hammer (alternating sides)
- Sanding the shaft sides to allow easy swapping of coil bobbins
- Extending stator coils by adding windings harvested from an identical donor motor
- Improvised bearing fabrication using brass tubing to accommodate a non-standard 3.5 mm shaft
- Hand-spinning the rotor to generate ~50 V AC output
