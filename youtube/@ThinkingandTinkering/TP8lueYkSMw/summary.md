## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a super-sensitive relay from scratch, using a hand-wound coil, a hacksaw blade as the switching arm, and neodymium magnets to create a balanced magnetic field. Unlike a standard relay, this design allows the trip voltage to be precisely tuned by adjusting the position of the magnets, and can even be extended to act as a multi-switch that triggers different contacts at different applied voltages.

## Key Topics
- Basic relay construction (coil, bobbin, switching contact)
- How magnetic field strength relates to coil turns and wire gauge
- Using a permanent magnetic field to bias the relay arm
- Adjustable trip voltage by varying magnet distance
- Multi-position switching: different contacts closing at different voltages
- Comparison with conventional relays

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- High Impact Polystyrene (HIPS) sheet — used to fabricate the bobbin
- Acrylic (blue baseboard) — used as the mounting base
- Brass pins — used as coil connection points
- Hacksaw blade — used as the flexible switching arm
- 4 mm threaded bar and cap nuts — used to mount the hacksaw blade block
- Microwave oven transformer iron core — large metal mass used to concentrate the magnetic field
- Neodymium (neo) magnets — used in pairs (N-S N-S orientation) to create the permanent bias field
- Dome nuts and bolts — used as contact points (normally open / normally closed)
- Approximately 20-gauge copper wire — used to wind the coil
- Super glue — used to assemble the bobbin

## Techniques and Methods
- Fabricating a custom letterbox-shaped bobbin from HIPS sheet
- Hand-winding a coil onto the bobbin
- Cutting and de-toothing a hacksaw blade section for use as a spring arm
- Crimping connectors onto threaded bar for electrical connection
- Slotting a block to mount and glue the hacksaw blade at a fixed angle
- Positioning neodymium magnets in matched N-S N-S orientation to create a balanced bias field
- Setting trip voltage by adjusting magnet-to-arm distance
- Configuring multiple contact bolts for multi-voltage switching
