## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith completes and tests his electric trike built almost entirely from rolled-up newspaper tubes held together with glue and fiber. After assembling the final structural reinforcements, attaching wheels, motor, handlebars, and a saddle (opting against a recumbent seat), he takes the paper trike for its first run in a car park. The trike holds together under his weight plus the battery load, moves at a decent speed, and the homemade rear differential functions as intended.
