## Overview
This video is the fourth in Robert Murray-Smith's graphene series, providing troubleshooting updates on the graphene oxide synthesis process and demonstrating how to reduce graphene oxide for practical use. Robert explains that temperature sensitivity (keeping the reaction between ~0°C and 55°C) is the most common cause of failure, clarifies the washing procedure step by step, and then shows three methods of reducing graphene oxide — using a LightScribe drive, a standard home oven at 200°C, or thermal annealing — applied to paper, aluminium foil, and glass substrates.

## Key Topics
- Troubleshooting the graphene oxide synthesis (temperature sensitivity, failed attempts)
- Detailed explanation of the washing/purification process
- Sonication to disperse graphene oxide into solution
- Reduction of graphene oxide via oven annealing at 200°C vs. LightScribe method
- Coating substrates: paper, aluminium, glass, and plastic (via transfer)
- Target applications: supercapacitors, touchscreens, solar cells, batteries
- Film deposition techniques: pour coating, dip coating, spin coating

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene oxide (GO)** — the primary product being processed and reduced
- **Potassium permanganate** — oxidising agent in the synthesis; warned to keep reaction below 55°C to avoid formation of explosive manganese pentoxide
- **Manganese pentoxide** — explosive by-product formed above 55°C, used as a safety warning
- **Hydrogen peroxide** — added at the end of the synthesis reaction (solution turns yellow)
- **Hydrochloric acid (1 molar HCl)** — used once in the washing process to remove ionic contaminants
- **Water (DI/warm)** — used for repeated washing steps; pH washed down to ~4–5
- **Aluminium (foil/sheet)** — substrate for GO coating and transfer to plastic
- **Glass** — substrate for GO coating (proof-of-concept touchscreen/solar cell application)
- **Xerox/copy paper** — substrate for GO coating
- **Acetone** — mentioned by Robert in comments for cleaning glass before coating
- **Ethanol** — mentioned in comments as fuel for alcohol lamp in a nanotube growth method
- **Iron-doped carbon nitride** — mentioned in comments as a material Robert had made, considered for glass coating
- **Vor ink** — noted in comments as a graphene-based ink product

## Techniques and Methods
- **Temperature-controlled oxidation** — reaction held between ~40°C and below 55°C (on a radiator) to avoid explosive by-products
- **Washing/settling purification** — repeated dilution in large water volumes, allowing GO to settle, decanting supernatant, then one HCl wash, then multiple water washes until pH ~4–5
- **Ultrasonic bath sonication** — jeweller's sonicator (~700 W, ~20 kHz), run 5 × 8-minute cycles to disperse GO in water
- **Oven thermal reduction (annealing)** — GO coated on substrate, dried, then heated at 200°C for 1 hour to produce reduced graphene oxide (rGO)
- **LightScribe laser reduction** — GO painted on plastic disc, run through a LightScribe drive 6–7 times; produces a "fluffy" porous rGO suited to supercapacitors
- **Pour coating** — GO solution poured/swirled onto glass substrate
- **Dip coating** — proposed improvement: substrate dipped and lifted out to drip-dry
- **Spin coating** — proposed improvement: centrifugal force used to produce uniform thin film
- **Transfer coating to plastic** — rGO formed on aluminium foil, plastic pressed/ironed on top, then peeled off
- **Pre-wetting paper** — paper soaked and smoothed onto a board before GO application to prevent cockling

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Washing details**: The HCl wash is done only once; the water washes are done three times. For stubborn dispersions, let the mixture sit for three days with occasional stirring every 1–2 hours.
- **Sonicator specs**: Desktop ultrasonic bath, ~100 W, frequency likely ~20 kHz. Recommends experimenting if using a different unit. Strongly advocates buying a sonicator (~£30): "A sonicator is about the only way I have found to properly disperse the graphite oxide into water."
- **GO solution concentration**: 4–10 mg/mL in DI water; sonicate until dispersed.
- **Glass coating method**: Dripped GO solution on glass, swirled, dried in sun, then oven at 200°C for 1 hour. Plans to build a spin coater and clean glass with acetone first; also considering iron-doped carbon nitride for the glass coating.
- **Graphene structure note**: True single-atom graphene has too many practical problems; the practical target is 2–3 layer thick platelets, ideally bridged by carbon nanotubes.
- **Battery application idea**: Coating both sides of aluminium foil with rGO — the foil acts as current collector, the rGO as electrode material.
- **Nanotube growth suggestion**: Coat fine woven fibreglass in graphene, then pass it above an ethanol alcohol-lamp flame; soot deposits oriented nanotubes perpendicular to the cloth — proposed as a good battery material.
- **Industrial scale-up**: Mentions large rollers as the equivalent industrial process for coating.
