## Overview
Robert continues developing his CD4 longboard prototype (first seen in video #1181), correcting the truck orientation based on viewer feedback, removing the bottom skids to reduce ride height, and laminating the underside with carbon fiber reinforced Kevlar. The result is a functional "bulletproof" longboard that receives positive test rides from both Robert and a friend named Luke/Matt.

## Key Topics
- Iterative prototype development using community feedback from previous videos
- Correcting truck orientation (both trucks must face the same relative direction — both inward or both outward)
- Lowering the board profile by removing the skid strips
- Applying Kevlar reinforcement to the underside for structural stiffness
- Fitting trucks to a curved board surface using fiberglass repair paste
- Test riding and user impressions of the wave-shaped longboard

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Carbon fiber reinforced Kevlar** — used as a laminate sheet applied to the underside of the board for stiffening and the "bulletproof" branding
- **Fiberglass repair paste (Upol product)** — a two-part paste with hardener used to fill the gap between the curved board and the flat truck mounting surface

## Techniques and Methods
- Sanding the board after skid removal to smooth the surface
- Laminating/coating the board underside with a Kevlar sheet
- Mixing and applying fiberglass repair paste with hardener to fill curved-surface gaps before bolting on trucks
- Drilling holes through the Kevlar-coated board to remount trucks
- Packing curved mounting points with washers to compensate for the board's curve

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Primary motivation for Kevlar was stiffness, not ballistics:** Robert repeatedly clarifies that he only used Kevlar because it was the material he had on hand after removing the skids — he simply wanted to stiffen the board back up, and the "bulletproof" angle was just a joke that amused him.
- **Truck orientation research:** He confirms he tried both truck orientations himself and also researched it; boards are legitimately mounted both ways and the choice depends on intended riding style.
- **Partial vs. full Kevlar coverage:** He acknowledges he had enough Kevlar to cover the whole board but preferred the wood aesthetic and is considering adding extra ply layers instead.
- **Bioplastics suggestion noted:** He agreed the board would be a good candidate for bioplastic materials (raised by a commenter).
- **Age context:** Confirms he is 60 years old, adding self-deprecating humour about his skating ability.
- **Board maturity:** Pushed back on impatient commenters — the board had only existed for two days at the time of replies.
