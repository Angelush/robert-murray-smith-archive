## Overview
A short Christmas advent calendar video (Day 13) in which Robert demonstrates how to build a simple balloon hovercraft from a CD and a balloon. The balloon is glued to the CD so that air escaping from it creates a cushion, lifting the CD off a flat surface and allowing it to glide freely.

## Key Topics
- DIY balloon hovercraft as a Christmas science activity
- Advent Science Magic series (Day 13 of 25)
- Basic fluid dynamics / air-cushion lift principle

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- CD (compact disc) — base of the hovercraft
- Balloon — inflated and attached to the CD to supply air
- Super glue — used to seal the balloon neck to the centre hole of the CD
- Q-tip (cotton swab) — for applying super glue carefully around the edge
- Rubber ring — used as an intermediary seal/adapter to feed the balloon through the CD hole

## Techniques and Methods
- Threading the balloon through the CD's centre hole
- Applying super glue with a Q-tip around the join and letting it cure in a cup
- Pre-inflating the balloon slightly to smooth it out before full inflation
- Placing the assembled hovercraft on a flat surface to demonstrate air-cushion levitation
