## Overview
A short philosophical video in which Robert uses the "Parable of the Chimpanzees" — a behavioural experiment where chimps learn to avoid a ladder due to cold water spraying, and pass this avoidance on to generations who never experienced the spray — to illustrate how inherited assumptions can persist long after the original reason has disappeared. He applies this to a specific electronics debate: whether a resistive load is the only valid way to test a generator's output, arguing that charging a capacitor and measuring its voltage is equally informative.

## Key Topics
- The chimpanzee parable as a metaphor for inherited, unquestioned behaviour
- How organisations and individuals make decisions based on outdated or transmitted assumptions
- Testing generator performance: resistive load vs. capacitor charging methods
- Encouraging critical re-examination of accepted practices

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Capacitor (used to store charge from a generator for voltage measurement)

## Techniques and Methods
- Resistive load testing of generators (conventional approach)
- Capacitor charging as an alternative generator output measurement: charge the capacitor, switch off, then measure voltage across it
