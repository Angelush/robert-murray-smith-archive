## Overview
Robert unboxes and reviews the Creality CR Scan Ferret Pro 3D scanner, walks through its setup with a wireless bridge connected to a Surface Pro laptop, and uses it to scan the life-size bronze statue of Amy Johnson at Herne Bay. The resulting 3D model was cleaned up, exported as an STL, scaled down, and printed on a Mingda Magician Pro 2 — demonstrating a complete scan-to-print workflow with high praise for the scanner's ease of use.

## Key Topics
- Unboxing and hardware overview of the Creality CR Scan Ferret Pro
- Wireless bridge setup for connecting the scanner to a PC via Wi-Fi
- Cross-platform compatibility (Windows, Mac, Android, iOS)
- Software workflow: scanning, optimizing, merging, exporting STL
- Scanning the Amy Johnson bronze statue at Herne Bay
- 3D printing the resulting model on the Mingda Magician Pro 2
- UK copyright law regarding public works of art

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Bronze (the Amy Johnson statue is cast in bronze)

## Techniques and Methods
- Handheld 3D scanning using structured light/depth sensing (Creality CR Scan Ferret Pro)
- Multi-scan merging: taking six separate scans of the statue and merging them in software
- Point cloud optimization and lasso-delete cleanup before merging
- STL export from Creality scan software
- Mesh simplification using Fast Quadric Mesh Simplification (myminifactory.github.io tool) to reduce polygon count for import into Tinkercad
- Model scaling and base addition in Tinkercad
- FDM 3D printing on the Mingda Magician Pro 2 (print volume up to 400 mm; model scaled to 280 mm tall)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Photogrammetry comparison:** Robert tried photogrammetry before and was unimpressed, but acknowledged it may have improved.
- **Post-processing workflow:** He clarified his full pipeline — scan, merge, then use the Fast Quadric Mesh Simplification tool (myminifactory.github.io) to reduce the mesh enough to import into Tinkercad, where he added a base and resized it.
- **Processing power vs. software:** In response to a comment, he explained that processing power is like "muscle" and software is like "technique" — both contribute to scan quality, but all else being equal, more processing power helps with the high data-point demands of 3D scanning.
- **STL resources:** Pointed users to Yeggi and Hum3D for echidna STLs when asked.
