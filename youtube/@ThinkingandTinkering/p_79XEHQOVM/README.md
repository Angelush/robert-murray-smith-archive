## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the possibility of converting coal mining spoil heap waste — abundant across the UK, US, Poland, Australia, and elsewhere — into graphite suitable for use in lithium-ion batteries. He collects a sample from a former Kent coalmine, processes it in the lab, and demonstrates a dramatic increase in electrical conductivity, strongly suggesting successful graphitization. The video presents this as a potential economic lifeline for dying coal mining communities, turning a near-worthless waste product into a high-value battery material.
