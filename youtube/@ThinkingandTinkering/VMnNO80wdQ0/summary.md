## Overview
Robert converts a discarded microwave oven into a UV curing station for resin 3D prints, inspired by a suggestion from community member "Ron." The build strips out all microwave internals while retaining the turntable motor and power board, then adds UV LED strip lights and basic inline switches — total cost around £10 and about an hour of work. The finished station provides a cheap, enclosed alternative to commercial curing stations that can cost £100 or more.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing/upcycling discarded electronics
- UV curing of resin 3D printer parts
- Microwave oven disassembly and internal components
- Basic mains wiring using inline switches and a back box
- USB-powered UV LED strip lights as a low-cost light source

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- IPA (isopropyl alcohol) — used to wash resin prints before curing
- UV LED strip lights (385–405 nm wavelength) — the curing light source, USB-powered, ~£5

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling a microwave oven (removing magnetron, transformer, capacitor, fan, heating elements)
- Retaining the original turntable motor for rotating prints during curing
- Wiring mains switches inline to independently control the motor and LED lights
- Using a Patras back box to safely enclose mains wiring
- Mounting a USB plug socket (with built-in USB port) to power the LED strip
