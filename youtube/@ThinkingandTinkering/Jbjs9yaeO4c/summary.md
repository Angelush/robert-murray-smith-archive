## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a DIY sound lens made from two plastic water lens shells (one concave, one convex) filled with CO₂ gas. The idea was suggested by his physicist brother, who noted that CO₂ — being denser than air — should refract sound in the same way a glass lens refracts light. The device costs around 20 pence and takes roughly 10 minutes to build, yet demonstrably focuses sound, offering a cheap alternative to commercial parametric speakers that cost ~£500.

## Key Topics
- The physics of sound focusing using a gas lens (CO₂ denser than air refracts sound)
- Comparison to commercial parametric/directional speaker technology (~£500)
- Practical applications of directional sound: targeted advertising, zone-specific audio warnings, shared-space personal audio without headphones
- DIY construction of a sound lens from water lens moulds and CO₂
- Future investigation directions: flat vs. convex surfaces, optimal curvature, adaptive lenses

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **CO₂ (carbon dioxide)** — the filling gas; heavier than air, used to refract/focus sound
- **Dry ice** — used as a convenient CO₂ source; evaporates inside the lens to displace air
- **uPVC fascia/soffit board ("central board")** — used as a rigid backing/protective frame for the lens
- **Acrylic gel tape** — used to seal the lens edges; described as waterproof and flexible (normally used for mobile phone screen repair)
- **Double-sided sticky tape** — used to attach the plastic lens membranes to the board
- **CO₂ cylinder** — used with a pillowcase to collect dry ice
- **Clear resin** — mentioned in comments as a possible alternative material

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting a hole in uPVC board with a hole saw to match lens diameter
- Attaching flexible plastic lens membranes to the board using double-sided tape and acrylic gel tape to create an airtight seal
- Filling the sealed lens with CO₂ by inserting a small piece of dry ice, allowing it to sublimate and purge the air through a straw vent
- Creating matched concave/convex lens pairs (one of each shape) for sound focusing
- Live demonstration by speaking through the lens and comparing audible focus

- Clarifies the acrylic gel tape: the gel itself is clear, the cover tape is red plastic that peels off after application.
- For UK viewers asking about the board material, explains it is **uPVC fascia and soffit board** — searchable as "uPVC fascia and soffit" — available locally in 3–5m lengths, 19–25mm thick, 100–300mm wide.
