## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith shares a surprising experimental finding: carbon felt — a material used in air filters — outperforms zinc as an antenna in his RF energy harvesting circuit, charging a capacitor bank to 1 volt in half the time of an equivalent zinc piece, representing a roughly 50% improvement in energy collection. The result is counterintuitive because carbon felt has lower conductivity than zinc, which had led Robert to dismiss it initially. He presents this as a preliminary but compelling observation worth further investigation.
