## Overview
Luke retrieves a discarded Dyson Cool bladeless fan from a bin and attempts to repair it by disassembling, cleaning, and reassembling the unit. After attaching a new plug, the fan powers on successfully, and the total repair cost of £25 (for a replacement base plate and remote) is compared favourably against the £300–400 retail price of the device.

## Key Topics
- Finding a discarded Dyson Cool bladeless fan and deciding to repair it
- Full disassembly of the fan's base unit, including circlip removal and motor extraction
- Cleaning the circuit board, motor, and plastic components
- Reassembly and first power-on test
- Cost analysis: £25 in replacement parts vs. £300–400 new retail price

None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Circlip removal using circlip pliers and a flathead screwdriver
- Motor extraction by gripping and twisting the housing
- Dust removal from small crevices using a paintbrush
- Washing plastic mesh/sponge components in a sink
- Reassembly with torx screws and clip refastening
- Fitting a new mains plug and performing a live power test
