## Overview
This video is a contest entry by "Sanjitos" from Venezuela, submitted to Robert Murray-Smith's channel, showcasing a miniature hydroelectric generator built entirely from scrap and paper materials. The creator constructs an origami water wheel, mounts it on a paper-box structure, and connects it to a salvaged stepper motor to light an LED. The project demonstrates that functional electricity generation is achievable with minimal resources and origami techniques.

## Key Topics
- Origami water wheel design using interlocking paper pieces
- Waterproofing paper structures for wet environments
- Salvaging a stepper motor from an old printer for power generation
- Electrical circuit design (bridge rectifier, capacitor, LED)
- Using a pulley system to increase rotational speed and voltage output
- Building structural support from 80 origami "paper lego" boxes

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Paper / flyers** — used to fold the origami water wheel segments
- **Cardboard** — stuffed inside the wheel and support boxes for rigidity
- **PVA glue** — painted onto paper surfaces to waterproof the wheel
- **PET (polyethylene terephthalate) film** — airport luggage-wrap film used to waterproof the support structure boxes
- **Stepper motor** — salvaged from an old printer; used as the generator
- **Bridge rectifier** — used to convert AC output from the motor to DC
- **Capacitor** — used in the circuit between motor and LED
- **LED** — load device used to demonstrate successful power generation

## Techniques and Methods
- Origami wheel construction by interlocking identical folded paper pieces into a circle
- Cardboard core reinforcement of paper structures
- PVA glue coating for waterproofing paper components
- PET film wrapping as an alternative waterproofing layer for the modular box structure
- Bridge rectifier + capacitor circuit for rectifying stepper motor output
- Pulley system to step up rotational speed from wheel to motor shaft
