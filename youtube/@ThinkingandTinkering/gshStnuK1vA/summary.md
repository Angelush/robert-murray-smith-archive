## Overview
Robert demonstrates using a previously built Tesla turbine with an involute aluminium housing as an air pump for his wood gasifier project. He tests it at both low (60V) and full mains voltage (240V DC), successfully drawing air as shown by cigarette smoke. He also presents a disassembled handheld cordless vacuum cleaner as a cheaper backup alternative, concluding that both are viable options for supplying air to the gasifier during startup.

## Key Topics
- Using a Tesla turbine as an air/gas pump for a wood gasifier
- Involute vs. circular housing for Tesla turbines used as pumps
- Construction details of Robert's spindle-free disc stack turbine design
- Motor selection: a DC motor salvaged from a hand blender, rated for 240V DC
- Testing at 60V and full 240V mains voltage with smoke visualisation
- Handheld cordless vacuum cleaner as an alternative air pump
- Bearing limitations at high RPM

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 3mm aluminium disc (base plate for turbine stack)
- Aluminium box section (scrap, used as motor support bracket)
- Aluminium brazing/welding rods (used to weld bracket to housing)
- Stainless steel roofing washers (spacers between turbine discs)
- Bearings (two, housed in bearing blocks; noted as insufficient for full-speed operation)
- Super glue (temporary assembly for testing)
- Lithium batteries, 3.7V (inside the cordless vacuum)
- Stainless steel impeller (inside the vacuum cleaner)

## Techniques and Methods
- Ally (aluminium) welding/brazing using low-temperature aluminium rods and a blowtorch
- Wire brushing aluminium with a stainless steel brush before welding
- Smoke visualisation (lit cigarette) to confirm airflow direction and suction
- Drill-and-tap fastening of bracket to housing
- Running a DC motor below rated voltage to test at reduced RPM before full-power run
- Disassembly of a commercial handheld vacuum cleaner to extract motor/impeller assembly

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly brief acknowledgements, jokes, or short reactions to viewer suggestions. A few noteworthy points emerge:

- **Cigarette smoke as a visualisation tool:** He acknowledged viewer complaints and jokingly suggested a unit of fan power called "CPM" (cigarettes per minute), crediting a commenter called "The Creative Chemist."
- **Bearing imbalance:** He confirmed the rattling was likely due to the turbine being out of balance, not just insufficient bearings.
- **Voltage step-up options:** He mentioned a buck converter as one option to run the turbine from a 12V battery at higher voltage, noting "always more than one solution."
- **Gasifier startup plan:** He clarified he only intends to run the fan during startup — once the gasifier is self-sustaining he will turn it off.
- **Tesla turbine efficiency:** He agreed with commenters that efficiency increases significantly at higher RPM.
- **Building his own components:** His default preference is to build rather than buy off-the-shelf solutions.
- **Bellows idea:** He expressed genuine fondness for a viewer's suggestion of using bellows as an alternative pump.
