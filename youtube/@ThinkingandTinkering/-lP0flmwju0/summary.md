## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith takes an ordinary aging desktop PC and rebuilds it inside a custom steampunk-styled enclosure. Motivated by a friend's steampunk computer build and his own previous steampunk speaker project, he walks through the complete process of stripping a standard tower PC, fabricating a new case from PVC foam board, and reassembling the components with decorative elements. The result is a fully functional steampunk computer with matching speakers, intended as an accessible DIY project for anyone with a basic home PC.

## Key Topics
- Motivation: matching steampunk speakers (video 972) and inspiration from a friend's build
- Stripping a standard desktop tower down to its essential components
- Fabricating a new enclosure from PVC foam board (builders board / Sintra board)
- Achieving a cast-iron aesthetic through polishing and copper pipe framing
- Reassembling motherboard, hard drive, and power supply into the new case
- Modifying a Logitech trackball mouse to fit the steampunk aesthetic
- Plans for future steampunk keyboard and monitor builds

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **uPVC foam core roofline board** (UK) / **Sintra board** (US) — used to fabricate the enclosure frame and panels
- **Super glue** — used to bond the PVC foam board pieces
- **Copper pipe** — used as structural/decorative framework for the cast-iron-style plinth
- **Flexible vacuum cleaner hose** — used to route power supply cables decoratively
- **Logitech trackball mouse** — disassembled and modified; copper and gold switch contacts noted internally

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling a standard ATX desktop tower; identifying and retaining essential components (motherboard, PSU, HDD, front I/O panel)
- Static electricity precautions when handling motherboard (use of cardboard, avoiding nylon/bare socks on carpet)
- Cutting PVC foam board with carpet knife, hand saw, or machine tools; mitre (45°) corner joints
- Building a frame with triangular internal corner bracing for squareness
- Using a hole saw to cut decorative circles from PVC foam board
- Dry-buffing the painted PVC surface with a cloth to produce a silvery cast-iron appearance
- Mounting the motherboard inverted on a removable lid for easy access
- Disassembling and reassembling a trackball mouse (5 screws, press-out switches)
- Routing cables through flexible hose for a clean decorative finish
- Photographing connector positions before removal to aid reassembly
