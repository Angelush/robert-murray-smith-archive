## Overview
Luke completes a tabletop arcade cabinet build, walking through the final assembly steps including button and joystick installation, RetroPie firmware setup on a Raspberry Pi 2, wiring to USB encoders, custom printed side graphics, and a protective acrylic screen panel. The finished machine runs classic arcade games like Street Fighter and Mortal Kombat and is presented as a fully functional retro arcade cabinet built from scratch.

## Key Topics
- Final assembly of a DIY tabletop arcade cabinet
- Installing arcade buttons and joysticks to a control board
- Flashing RetroPie firmware using Raspberry Pi Imager
- Wiring buttons and joysticks to USB encoder boards
- Configuring button bindings in EmulationStation
- Designing and applying custom printed side panel graphics
- Adding a rocker power switch and fan for the cabinet
- Blacking out an acrylic panel to frame the screen

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Black paint (applied to the cabinet body in a prior session)
- Acrylic sheet (used for the screen surround/bezel)
- Sticky-back plastic film (used to black out the acrylic)
- M4 bolts (used to mount the joysticks)

## Techniques and Methods
- Installing arcade buttons by connecting positive and negative pins
- Mounting joysticks with M4 bolts
- Writing RetroPie OS image to SD card using Raspberry Pi Imager
- Connecting buttons and joystick 5-pin connector to USB encoder (k1–k12 wiring layout)
- Configuring gamepad button bindings interactively in EmulationStation
- Custom graphic design and professional printing for side panels
- Cutting and applying printed graphics to cabinet sides
- Applying sticky-back plastic film to acrylic for screen masking
