## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a hand-cranked mechanical power generator using salvaged bicycle parts, timber, and electrical components. By connecting a repurposed 12V tape recorder motor to a gear system driven by a hand crank, he demonstrates that mechanical motion can generate enough electricity to light LEDs. The project shows how common scrap materials can be combined into a functional generator.

## Key Topics
- Hand-cranked mechanical electricity generation
- Repurposing salvaged bicycle parts and a tape recorder motor
- Gear-driven power transmission from crank to generator motor
- Powering LEDs with mechanically generated voltage

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 12V DC motor (salvaged from a tape recorder)
- LEDs (two)
- Bicycle parts (gears/chain, obtained for free)
- Timber (wooden frame/base)
- Electrical wiring

## Techniques and Methods
- Mounting a motor to a timber frame to act as a generator
- Using bicycle gearing to transfer rotational force from a hand crank to the motor
- Routing wiring through the frame to connect the motor to LEDs
- Testing open-circuit voltage output of a back-driven motor
