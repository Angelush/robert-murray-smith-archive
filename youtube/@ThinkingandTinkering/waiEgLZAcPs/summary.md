## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith walks through how to design and 3D print a ratchet and pawl (pole) mechanism using Tinkercad. He demonstrates the step-by-step construction of a 12-tooth, 40mm ratchet and its corresponding pawl, then slices and prints both parts using Cura. The video fills a gap from earlier videos where he showed ratchets in use but had not yet explained how to model them from scratch.

## Key Topics
- What ratchets are and where they are used (catapults, winding mechanisms, general machinery)
- Step-by-step ratchet design in Tinkercad using cylinders, cubes, and boolean operations
- Using a guide circle and rotation/repeat technique to evenly space 12 teeth at 30° intervals
- Designing the pawl (pole) from a crescent shape with a pivot circle
- Exporting from Tinkercad, slicing in Cura with a raft, and printing on an FDM printer
- Tips for scaling the design (fewer/larger teeth vs. more/smaller teeth using triangles)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Boolean operations in Tinkercad (cylinder + cube hole to form a tooth profile)
- Guide circle alignment technique to maintain geometry during rotation
- Copy-and-rotate repeat workflow to distribute 12 teeth evenly around a circle
- Crescent shape construction (two offset circles merged) to form the pawl body
- Using a cube hole as a "razor" to clean up unwanted geometry artifacts
- FDM slicing in Cura with raft support; scraping and snapping the raft off after printing
