## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores why the standard bicycle crank is mechanically inefficient and how the treadle mechanism from Singer sewing machines solved this problem by converting reciprocating motion into rotation with up to five times the torque. He traces the treadle's influence on the penny farthing bicycle and railway pump trolleys, then demonstrates building a simple hand-powered electromagnetic generator using a 3D-printed crank mechanism, a magnet disc, and a coil — successfully producing over 1.5V from a single coil.
