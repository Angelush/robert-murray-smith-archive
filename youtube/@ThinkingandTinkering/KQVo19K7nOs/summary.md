## Overview
A short, lighthearted video in which Robert discovers he needs a single wall plug to rehang a curtain rail, but is 40 minutes from the nearest store. Rather than making the drive, he downloads a model from Thingiverse and 3D prints a replacement wall plug in about 10 minutes, demonstrating how having the right mindset about your tools can save both time and money.

## Key Topics
- Using a 3D printer to solve an everyday household problem
- The value of rethinking what your tools are capable of
- Cost comparison between printed and store-bought wall plugs
- Brief discussion of alternative DIY fixes (toothpicks, matchsticks, twigs, wooden dowels) and their limitations

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- PLA+ filament (used in the Neptune 4 Pro printer; approximately 1 gram per plug, costing ~1.5p)
- Store-bought 6mm wall plugs (cited for cost comparison: ~2.56p each from Amazon)

## Techniques and Methods
- FDM 3D printing of a wall plug using a model downloaded from Thingiverse
- Snap-off removal of a printed plug from a printed strip
- Hammer-fit installation of the printed plug into a wall

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Print time and effort:** About 10 minutes to print (he made a coffee/tea while waiting); roughly 2 minutes of hands-on setup time. The model was downloaded from Thingiverse (link shown in the video).
- **Printer and filament:** Used a Neptune 4 Pro with PLA+.
- **Cost breakdown:** 1 gram of filament at £15/kg = ~1.5p per plug, versus ~2.56p for a store-bought plug — cheaper to print, even accounting for electricity.
- **On alternative fixes (toothpicks, matchsticks, twigs, wooden dowels):** Robert acknowledges these can work in a pinch and says he has used them himself, but consistently pushes back — they are a "poor job," hammering often leaves marks or splays the material, and he would not use them in a freshly decorated room. A twig is mostly sapwood and has little strength when dry.
- **On asking neighbours or borrowing:** Admits he "hates asking for anything."
- **Liability caveat:** Notes his hesitation about recommending it for shelves is partly about not wanting to be blamed if someone overloads one.
- **General tone:** Relaxed and good-humoured throughout, happy to debate wall-plug philosophy but eventually calls time: *"I think this is enough talk about wall plugs — let's agree to disagree."*
