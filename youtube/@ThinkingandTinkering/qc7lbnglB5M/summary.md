## Overview
Robert builds a collapsible Savonius-style wind turbine using tea towels as sails, inspired by a design shared by a viewer named Ron Rattick. The turbine is constructed from a repurposed music stand, a large salvaged bearing, wooden discs, and fibreglass rods, with the key innovation being that flexible sail-based blades allow the whole structure to fold down for portability. Surprisingly, the prototype works — spinning reliably in the wind without toppling over — and Robert plans to iterate toward Ron's original three-sail, 120°-spaced design.

## Key Topics
- Community-sourced idea from viewer Ron Rattick (three-sail wind turbine concept)
- Savonius rotor design using flexible fabric sails instead of rigid blades
- Portability and packability as a core design goal (for hiking/off-grid phone charging)
- Repurposing everyday and salvaged materials (music stand, old bearing, tea towels)
- Prototype demonstration and validation — the turbine spins without falling over
- Plans to iterate to a three-blade design at 120° spacing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Tea towels** — used as the turbine sail/blade material
- **Fibreglass rods** — threaded through the tea towels to create spar-stiffened sail shapes (also available as tent poles, fishing rods, or electrician's pull-throughs)
- **Wooden discs** (~200 mm diameter) — serve as the top and bottom rotor hubs
- **Large salvaged bearing** — salvaged from a large motor, enables smooth rotation
- **Music stand** — repurposed as the collapsible tripod base
- **PVA** — mentioned in comments as a waterproofing treatment for the fabric sails
- **Ripstop nylon** — suggested in comments as a more weather-resistant alternative to tea towels

## Techniques and Methods
- Repurposing a folding music stand as a collapsible structural tripod
- Turning down a metal attachment on a lathe to fit the bearing
- Sinking bearings into wooden discs to create spinning hubs
- Threading fibreglass rods through fabric hems to form battened sails (analogous to sailmaking)
- Bending rods around spars to tension the sail into a curved aerofoil-like shape
- Prototype-first approach: build a simple two-blade version first, then plan a three-blade iteration at 120° intervals
