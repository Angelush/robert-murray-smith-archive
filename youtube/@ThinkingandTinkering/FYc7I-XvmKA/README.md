## Overview

In this brief dusk-lit follow-up to his "Exploding Water" series, Robert tests a suggestion from a viewer named "Illusion" that the spark gap in his electrolysis setup is unnecessary — that the reaction occurs directly between the bare metal plates. He confirms the hypothesis works, observing a bright combustion reaction with just two connected bars and a thin film of water, and theorises that the large surface area of the thin water layer rapidly produces and burns hydrogen/gas, making the spark gap redundant.
