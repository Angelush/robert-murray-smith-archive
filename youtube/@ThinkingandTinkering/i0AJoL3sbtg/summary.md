## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith takes his cuboctahedron wind turbine outside to test it in real outdoor conditions. The device spins readily in even a light breeze and successfully charges capacitors through a modified stepper motor with bridge rectifiers. He concludes that the device works well but that the motor could be improved by replacing it with a purpose-built brushless DC generator.

## Key Topics
- Outdoor wind testing of the cuboctahedron wind turbine
- Energy generation and capacitor charging from wind
- Stepper motor used as a generator with bridge rectifier modification
- Capacitor smoothing of voltage output
- Potential future improvements to the generator component

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 1000 µF / 50 V electrolytic capacitors (used as smoothing/storage)
- Stepper motor (repurposed as a generator)
- Bridge rectifiers (one per coil)

## Techniques and Methods
- Using a stepper motor's two coils independently, each feeding its own bridge rectifier
- Rectifying AC output from each coil into DC
- Capacitor smoothing to stabilise voltage output under intermittent wind
- Voltmeter measurement of output during spinning
