## Overview
In this short advent calendar video (Day 16), Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple robotic hand using drinking straws, string, and sticky tape. The hand mimics the tendon-driven mechanism of a real human hand, where pulling strings causes the "fingers" to curl and close. It serves as a fun, accessible science activity for children or families.

## Key Topics
- Biomimicry of the human hand (finger lengths, knuckle joints)
- Tendon-driven mechanisms as a model for robotic grippers
- Simple DIY robotics/engineering for kids

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Drinking straws (cut to mimic finger segments with knuckle joints)
- String (cut into five pieces, 32 cm each)
- Sticky tape

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting straws to different lengths with notched "diamond" joints to replicate knuckle articulation
- Threading string through each straw and taping it at the far end to create a pull-actuated tendon
- Arranging four straws as fingers and one as a thumb, then tying them together into a hand form
- Pulling the free ends of the strings to close the fingers and grip objects
