## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores three practical ways to improve lead-acid batteries, with a focus on replacing the heavy lead negative plate with a lightweight carbon-based electrode to achieve a 30–40% increase in energy density. He demonstrates how to build a modified lead-acid cell using either copper sulfate or zinc sulfate as the electrolyte, referencing two academic papers that validate the approach.

## Key Topics
- The dominance of lead-acid batteries in the global market (~55% share)
- The UltraBattery concept (carbon-wrapped lead negative plate, Australian research)
- Replacing the lead negative plate entirely with a lightweight stainless steel/carbon composite electrode
- Two novel battery chemistries: copper-lead oxide and zinc-lead dioxide cells
- Comparison of copper sulfate vs. zinc sulfate electrolytes
- Energy density improvement from ~30 Wh/kg to ~45 Wh/kg (comparable to NiCd)
- DIY construction walkthrough of a modified lead-acid cell

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Lead acid battery (AGM type)** — 6V 1.2Ah Macklin battery used as demonstration unit
- **Lead oxide plates** — positive electrode in standard lead-acid cells
- **Lead plates** — negative electrode being replaced
- **Absorbent glass mat (AGM)** — separator material soaked in sulfuric acid
- **Sulfuric acid** — original electrolyte present in the AGM separator
- **Stainless steel mesh (2000 mesh and insect screen grade)** — current collector substrate for new electrode
- **High-density polyethylene (HDPE) filled with carbon black** — conductive, acid-resistant laminate for electrode
- **Carbon felt (100% carbon)** — high-surface-area active material for the new electrode
- **Copper sulfate (1 mol solution)** — electrolyte for copper-lead oxide variant; operates at ~1.2V
- **Zinc sulfate (1 mol solution)** — electrolyte for zinc-lead dioxide variant; operates at ~2.4V

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly of AGM lead-acid batteries to separate plates and separator material
- Removing lead negative plates to reduce battery weight by 30–40%
- Fabricating a composite electrode: wrapping stainless steel mesh in HDPE/carbon black sheet, sealed with a household iron (using greaseproof paper to prevent sticking)
- Attaching carbon felt to the composite plate as the active surface layer
- Re-wrapping lead oxide plates in fresh AGM separator material
- Assembling a modified cell: carbon electrode + separator + lead oxide plate, filled with copper sulfate or zinc sulfate electrolyte
- Visual charge-state monitoring via electrolyte colour change (copper sulfate turns from blue to clear when fully charged, indicating copper deposition into carbon)
- Demonstrating cell output by running a small motor

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Scaling to larger batteries**: Confirmed the same technique works for larger flooded cells — plates are simply bigger (e.g., 15 cm × 15 cm instead of 3 cm × 3 cm).
- **Role of HDPE/carbon black sheet**: Clarified it prevents acid from attacking the steel current collector while maintaining electrical conductivity between collector and active material.
- **Carbon felt advantage**: Noted the felt has no binder, which helps it perform reliably (avoiding issues some users reported with other carbon materials or binders).
- **Zinc dendrites / hydrogen evolution**: Explained that dendrites are mainly a concern in alkaline solutions; the real issue with zinc in acid is hydrogen evolution, but mitigation strategies exist.
- **Future cell format**: Indicated he would likely build prismatic cells as they are easier to fabricate.
- **Crystal/mineral batteries**: Acknowledged interest but said he focuses on carbon-based devices where there is still much unexplored work.
- **Shop links**: Provided direct links to activated carbon felt and Celgard separator material on his shop for viewers who asked.
