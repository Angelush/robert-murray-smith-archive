## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents a novel 3D-printed wind turbine concept designed specifically for boats, camper vans, and roof ridges, where conventional open-blade turbines are impractical or unsafe. The design uses a stacked spiral wind-capture shroud to funnel air through an enclosed impeller connected to a magnet disc generator. A self-directing rear shield allows the device to passively orient into the wind, making it multi-directional and safe for confined or crowded environments.

## Key Topics
- Design rationale for enclosed turbines suited to boats, camper vans, and roof ridges
- Spiral wind-capture shroud that funnels and concentrates airflow
- Stacking multiple units to scale power output
- Impeller and magnet-disc generator assembly
- Self-directing rear shield for passive wind orientation
- Multi-directional wind capture capability
- Energy output scaling proportional to the wind-capture inlet area (not swept rotor area)
- Availability of design files on Tinkercad (link in video description)
- Prior related work: Darwin wind turbine and Tesla turbines

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA filament** — used for 3D printing the turbine components (noted in comments as potentially needing replacement with a more suitable filament)
- **Permanent magnets** — mounted on the rotating disc to form the generator

## Techniques and Methods
- **3D printing** — all turbine components printed using FDM (filament: PLA)
- **CAD design in Tinkercad** — design files publicly shared via the video description
- **Impeller integration** — an externally contributed impeller design (credited to "sun K racks") was adapted and scaled to fit the shroud assembly
- **Magnet-disc generator construction** — a disc with magnets rotates past coils to generate electricity
- **Stacking/modular assembly** — six shroud units printed and stacked to increase air capture area and output torque

- **Measurement advice**: Clarified that measuring volts alone is insufficient; shaft torque is the correct metric for evaluating performance.
- **Guide vanes**: Confirmed the design includes guide vanes and stated this was mentioned in the video.
- **Public domain**: Confirmed the design is public domain — anyone can make one.
