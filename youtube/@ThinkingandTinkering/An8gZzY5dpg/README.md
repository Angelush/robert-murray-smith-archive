## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the limitations of electric vehicles and presents an alternative hydrogen generation system using aluminium and water (or potassium hydroxide). He demonstrates the reaction chemistry live, then traces the history of a promising 1987 patent by François Cornish — validated by BMW — that produced hydrogen on demand from aluminium and water, and asks why this elegant system never went anywhere.
