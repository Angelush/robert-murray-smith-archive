## Overview
Robert repurposes two discarded vacuum cleaners, demonstrating how their motors, cyclone filters, and sealed pump systems can be salvaged for practical workshop use. He identifies two main applications: a dust collector for his circular saw, and a gas pump for his wood gasifier project. The key insight is that vacuum cleaner motors are universal motors, capable of running on both AC and DC power.

## Key Topics
- Identifying and salvaging useful components from broken vacuum cleaners
- Universal motor operation on both AC (mains) and DC (12V battery)
- Cyclone filter systems and sealed exhaust/suction designs
- Repurposing a vacuum as a dust extractor for a circular saw
- Adapting a vacuum with an isolated exhaust for use as a gasifier pump

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly of vacuum cleaner to isolate motor, impeller, cyclone filter, and exhaust sections
- Running a universal motor on 12V DC by clipping battery leads directly to the motor wires (after cutting the plug)
- Resealing the pump housing to create a single inlet/outlet airflow channel
- Connecting vacuum exhaust port directly to a circular saw's dust extraction point
- Adapting a vacuum with an isolated exhaust housing to serve as a gasifier pump
