## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith critically analyzes MIT's viral concrete supercapacitor research, which claimed that adding carbon black and potassium chloride to concrete could create an energy storage material. He systematically debunks the practical viability of the technology by comparing its energy density to conventional batteries and supercapacitors, concluding that the research makes for a good headline but is unlikely to lead to any real-world application.
