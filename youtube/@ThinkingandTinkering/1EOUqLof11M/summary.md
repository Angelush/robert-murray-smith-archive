## Overview
This video is a competition entry submission for a challenge hosted by Robert Murray-Smith, created by "Ross from Intelligent Tinkering." Ross demonstrates a DIY wind turbine system built from a repurposed fan motor, custom-made blades, and a homemade three-phase bridge rectifier, all wired to a charge controller and battery. The system keeps an LED lit at all times — either powered by live wind generation or by the stored battery when wind stops.

## Key Topics
- DIY wind turbine construction using a salvaged fan motor
- Homemade three-phase bridge rectifier
- Charge controller circuit for battery management
- LED indicator circuit with automatic switchover between generation and battery power
- Outdoor installation of the wind turbine on a pole

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Repurposed fan motor (used as generator)
- Drain pipe (used to fabricate custom blades)
- Capacitors (for energy storage/smoothing)
- 300-ohm resistors (two, in the circuit)
- LED diodes (two — one power indicator, one battery-powered backup)
- 1.2V rechargeable battery
- Waterproof enclosure box
- Tapping screw and nut (for mounting)

## Techniques and Methods
- Repurposing a DC fan motor as a three-phase wind generator
- Hand-fabricating turbine blades from drain pipe
- Building a three-phase bridge rectifier from discrete components
- Wiring a charge controller to manage battery charging and LED switchover
- Outdoor pole-mounting of the turbine assembly
