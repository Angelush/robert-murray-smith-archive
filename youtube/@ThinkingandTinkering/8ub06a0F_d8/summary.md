## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a 3D-printed water motor based on 19th-century designs, showing how mains water pressure can be scavenged to generate small amounts of electricity. He explains the historical context of water motors, why direct mains connection became illegal, and how the device can legally be used in-line with hoses, sprinkler systems, or other water uses. With UK mains pressure (~30 PSI), the device is expected to yield around 10 watts of electrical output when paired with a PC fan generator.

## Key Topics
- History of water motors (origins ~1660s, peak popularity around the turn of the 19th century)
- How municipal water supplies maintain positive pressure (~30 PSI) to prevent contamination
- Why direct mains connection of water motors became illegal (pressure reduction)
- Legal use cases: in-line with garden hoses, sprinkler systems, pond filling, toilet cisterns, etc.
- Design of a 3D-printed water motor with a simple rotor and narrowing nozzle
- Power generation potential (~10 watts from UK mains pressure)
- Using a PC fan as the generator stage
- Possible rotor improvements: Tesla turbine, Pelton wheel designs

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- PLA (referenced in author comments — Robert notes he has seen a steam engine run on steam using 3D-printed PLA parts)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing (FDM) — motor body and rotor fabricated in Tinkercad and printed
- Nozzle narrowing to accelerate water flow onto the rotor via mains pressure
- PC fan repurposed as a small generator, attached to the rotor shaft
- In-line hose connection for integrating the motor into existing water flow

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comments are mostly brief acknowledgements, but a few notable points:
- **Tinkercad files**: The design files are available in his Tinkercad area, not scattered — "they are in one place."
- **PLA and steam**: He confirms he has seen a steam engine run on steam using 3D-printed PLA parts, suggesting the motor could potentially handle steam as a working fluid.
- **Audio echo issue**: He was aware of an echo in the audio and was considering a fix (possibly hanging cloth for acoustic dampening), with an improved version coming in a few days.
- **Accumulator idea**: When asked about energy storage, he hints that an accumulator might be a suitable solution.
