## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains why pure graphene cannot be used directly in a bulletproof vest — the edges and grain boundaries of graphene flakes (~45 microns) are inherently weak and will fail under ballistic impact. He presents his solution: a graphene-reinforced plastic composite he developed, which is 10 times stronger than steel by weight and twice as strong by volume, and demonstrates its resistance to penetration by a sharpened steel point.
