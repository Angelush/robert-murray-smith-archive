## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates building a DIY Fresnel lens by coiling 7 metres of clear polypropylene tube, filling it with water, and testing whether it can focus light. The experiment successfully focuses sunlight enough to burn paper, confirming the coiled water-filled tube functions as a Fresnel lens. He credits viewer "Iz pilot land" for suggesting the idea.

## Key Topics
- History of Fresnel lenses: Thomas Rogers (1788), Buffon, and Augustin-Jean Fresnel
- How Fresnel lenses reduce material while maintaining optical properties
- DIY construction of a Fresnel lens from coiled polypropylene tube
- Practical uses of Fresnel lenses in solar thermal concentration
- Caution around using Fresnel/concentrating lenses with solar PV (heat degrades efficiency)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 5 mm clear polypropylene tube (7 metres)
- Water (used to fill the tube)
- 1 cm double-sided tape (used to hold the coil together)
- Scrap paper (used as a burn target to demonstrate focus)

## Techniques and Methods
- Coiling tube into a flat spiral using double-sided tape
- Injecting water into the tube via syringe to fill it
- Plugging the centre gap with filler to complete the lens shape
- Testing focus using an indoor torch/point light source before sun testing
- Adjusting lens height with rulers to find the focal point outdoors
