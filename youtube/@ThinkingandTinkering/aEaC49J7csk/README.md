## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores small-scale hydroelectric generation by replicating a miniature version of the Turbulent Hydro vortex turbine — a Belgian design claimed to be environmentally friendly, fish-safe, and capable of powering ~300 homes from a low water fall. He builds and tests two prototypes combining an involute water channel with his previously designed "Rose" turbine blade, measuring output voltage as a proxy for performance, and concludes the approach is promising and worthy of further community experimentation.
