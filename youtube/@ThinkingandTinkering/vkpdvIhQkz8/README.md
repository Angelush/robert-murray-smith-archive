## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith presents a proof-of-concept CVT (Continuously Variable Transmission) designed for bicycles, built around a sliding gear and mechanical rectifier mechanism introduced in his previous video (2382). The design philosophy centres on keeping costs competitive with standard bicycle gearing (~£40), prioritising affordability over engineering elegance. The prototype demonstrates working variable-ratio transmission by combining a rack-and-pinion rectifier with a variable-pivot crank (Whitworth mechanism), with 3D-printed parts available on Thingiverse.
