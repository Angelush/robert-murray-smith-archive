## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a conductive ink made from an unusually high-conductivity graphite sourced from a mine in Sri Lanka, where he was working as a consultant. The ink achieves approximately 2 ohms per centimetre resistance — dramatically better than competing products claiming 40–60 ohms per centimetre. He teases using this ink as a basis for future projects including a printed transformer.
