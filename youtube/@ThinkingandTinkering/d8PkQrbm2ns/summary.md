## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a conductive ink made from an unusually high-conductivity graphite sourced from a mine in Sri Lanka, where he was working as a consultant. The ink achieves approximately 2 ohms per centimetre resistance — dramatically better than competing products claiming 40–60 ohms per centimetre. He teases using this ink as a basis for future projects including a printed transformer.

## Key Topics
- Unusual high-conductivity graphite from a Sri Lankan mine
- Formulation of a graphite-based conductive ink
- Resistance measurement of the dried ink (~2 Ω/cm)
- Comparison with commercial conductive inks (~40–60 Ω/cm)
- Potential future applications (printed transformer, motor)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphite** — an unusually conductive, silvery variety sourced from a Sri Lankan mine; the primary active ingredient in the ink
- **Conductive ink** — formulated from the graphite, bottled in 25 mL quantities
- **Gum arabic** — mentioned in comments as a water-based binder used in the ink formulation
- **Oil-based microporous varnish** — suggested in comments as an alternative binder for wax-compatible formulations

## Techniques and Methods
- Brush application of ink onto a substrate (paper/card)
- Blow-drying the applied ink layer to cure it
- Two-probe resistance measurement with a multimeter (~1 cm probe spacing)
- Bottling/packaging the ink in small (25 mL) quantities

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Graphite composition:** Robert believes the extraordinary conductivity is due to mineral inclusions in this specific graphite; elemental analysis was being conducted and he promised to share results once available.
- **Toxicity:** He was unconcerned, noting the graphite has been mined for centuries and is even used to coat cheese; he suggested waiting for the analysis or wearing gloves if worried.
- **Diamagnetic properties:** Confirmed the ink retains some diamagnetic character (all graphite is diamagnetic to a degree), though less so than the raw diamagnetic graphite since the ink isn't made from that specific form.
- **Binder advice:** Recommended an oil-based microporous varnish as an alternative to gum arabic for wax-compatible substrates.
- **Flexibility:** Had not yet tested the dried ink under bending; wondered aloud whether adding a rubber-based adhesive might help.
- **Screen printing:** Open to formulating a dedicated screen-printing version to maximise conductivity for interested makers.
- **Where to buy:** Promised to add a purchase link (£10 for 25 mL) to the video description.
- **Patents:** Acknowledged a commenter's point about changes in US patent law with some reluctance ("I hate patents").
- **Microwave resistance:** Doubted the ink would withstand microwave irradiation because graphite is a microwave susceptor, but asked for context before commenting further.
