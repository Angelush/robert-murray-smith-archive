## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make a simple homemade cuprous oxide solar cell using copper mesh rather than the traditional copper plate method. He argues that using fine woven copper mesh and oven-oxidizing it produces a more uniform, controlled cuprous oxide (Cu₂O) coating — including nanocrystal "hairs" at higher temperatures — resulting in a better-performing cell than the conventional hotplate approach. While the output remains modest (around 0.2V), he frames this as an introductory step toward developing genuinely useful homemade solar cells.
