## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith recounts the story of Starlite, a remarkable heat-resistant material invented by hairdresser Maurice Ward in 1986 that attracted serious interest from the UK Ministry of Defence, NASA, and others, but never reached commercialisation. The video argues that the material's failure was not a technical one — it was Maurice Ward himself, whose extreme paranoia about industrial espionage and unrealistic financial demands (£10 billion upfront plus 51% ownership) blocked every deal. Robert draws broader lessons about invention, ideas, and the importance of collaboration over secrecy.
