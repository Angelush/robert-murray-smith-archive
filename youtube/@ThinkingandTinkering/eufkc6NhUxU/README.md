## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the eccentric cycloidal drive — a compact, back-drivable gearing mechanism originally patented by a Russian inventor (US patent 2009, lapsed 2020) — and repurposes it as a power generator. By mounting neodymium magnets on a spinning disc and surrounding it with a serpentine coil, he demonstrates that the drive's 10:1 speed multiplication can produce useful electrical output, achieving 16 volts in a live demonstration.
