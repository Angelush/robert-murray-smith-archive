## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith argues that gravity (and energy) are not intrinsic properties of particles or matter, but rather emergent behaviors of complex systems in relation — analogous to how "walking" is not found inside the legs themselves. Using the analogy of walking, emergent AI systems (boids, Game of Life), and systems thinking, he concludes that gravity is movement/transition of things through space and time, and criticizes the search for a gravity particle (Higgs boson) as fundamentally misguided. He connects these ideas to practical work on more efficient energy storage using oscillating capacitor systems.

## Key Topics
- Gravity and energy as relational, systemic properties rather than things particles "possess"
- The walking analogy: emergence cannot be found by dissecting its organ
- Critique of the Higgs boson search at CERN as conceptually misguided
- Matter as the "organ" of gravity (analogous to legs for walking, brain for thought)
- Energy defined as a system transitioning from one state to another through space-time
- Entropy reconsidered: the "disorder" framing as anthropomorphic projection
- Emergent behavior in complex systems (boids, Conway's Game of Life)
- Gravity as emergent behavior — paralleled with thought and life
- Practical application: oscillating capacitor/battery systems for more efficient energy use
- The jewel thief circuit as a planned demonstration of these ideas

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Capacitors (supercapacitors) — discussed as energy storage systems to be reconceived under the systems framework
- Batteries (D cells) — used as an example of conventional energy-as-thing thinking
- No specific chemicals or compounds mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Conceptual/philosophical systems analysis — reframing physical phenomena as emergent relational properties
- Oscillating circuit design — proposed approach to cycling a capacitor/battery system at a resonant frequency to improve efficiency rather than simply draining charge
- Jewel thief circuit — referenced as a prior/planned practical demonstration of oscillation-based energy use
