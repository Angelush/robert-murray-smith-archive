## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates an interesting property of his teaspoon-based wind turbine: it continues to generate power across a wide range of wind angles, not just when the wind hits it head-on. Testing both outdoors and with a lab fan, he shows the turbine maintains consistent output (~10 volts) when rotated up to 60 degrees off-axis in either direction — a 120-degree effective sweep — making it well-suited for urban environments where wind direction is variable or gusty.
