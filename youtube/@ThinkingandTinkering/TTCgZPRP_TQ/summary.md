## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a self-charging graphene air battery built around Prussian blue — an iron-based compound that bleaches to white when exposed to oxygen and sunlight, then recharges itself. The battery uses a graphene ink-coated cloth cathode wrapped around a plastic pipe separator, with Prussian blue formed by reacting potassium hexacyanoferrate with an iron salt. The key takeaway is that this photochemical self-recharging mechanism (Prussian blue ↔ Prussian white) makes for a theoretically indefinite-lifetime battery, though current power output is modest.

## Key Topics
- Construction of a graphene ink air cathode battery (referencing his earlier "improved magnesium air battery" video)
- Introduction of Prussian blue as an electrochemically and photochemically active material
- The Prussian blue → Prussian white photoreaction driven by oxygen and sunlight
- Self-recharging mechanism: discharge turns Prussian blue white; sunlight and air restore it to blue
- Limitations: low power output (~0.5V), slow recharge time
- The broader potential of air cathode structures for investigation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene ink** — used to coat the cloth cathode
- **Potassium hexacyanoferrate (potassium ferricyanide)** — orange-yellow solution used to form Prussian blue
- **Iron salt (iron sulfate or iron chloride)** — reacted with potassium hexacyanoferrate to precipitate Prussian blue
- **Prussian blue (iron(III) hexacyanoferrate)** — the active electrochemical/photochemical material; bleaches to Prussian white on discharge/sunlight exposure
- **Prussian white** — the discharged/bleached state of Prussian blue
- **Copper wire** — used as a coil conductor in the cell
- **Plastic pipe** — structural/separator component
- **Dishcloth** — substrate for the graphene ink cathode

## Techniques and Methods
- Dipping dishcloth in graphene ink and drying to form the cathode
- Wrapping the coated cloth around a plastic pipe separator
- Preparing Prussian blue by dipping copper wire coil in potassium hexacyanoferrate solution, then into an iron salt solution (wet chemical precipitation)
- Using sunlight and air exposure to recharge (oxidise) the Prussian white back to Prussian blue
- Discharging the cell and observing the colour change as a visual indicator of state of charge
