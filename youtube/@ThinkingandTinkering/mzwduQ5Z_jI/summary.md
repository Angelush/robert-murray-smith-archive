## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the S866 controller fitted to the ADO A20 e-bike, walking through all 20 "P settings" and demonstrating — for educational/research purposes only — how to remove the speed limiter and switch the bike from pedal-assist mode to throttle control. The video concludes with a practical tip from hard-won personal experience: always write down your factory settings before making any changes.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the S866 controller on the ADO A20 e-bike
- Navigating the P settings menu (P1–P20)
- Removing the 25 km/h speed cap (P8)
- Configuring non-zero start sensitivity (P9)
- Switching between pedal-assist and throttle control modes via a button-press hack
- Overview of all 20 P settings and their functions
- Practical advice on recording factory settings before modifying

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- ADO A20 e-bike with a 36V battery and 350W motor
- S866 controller (display/control unit with USB charging port)

## Techniques and Methods
- Accessing hidden P settings menu by holding the `+` and `−` buttons simultaneously
- Removing the speed limiter by raising P8 from 25 km/h to 100 km/h
- Setting P9 (non-zero start) to 0 for maximum sensitivity
- **Pedal-assist mode hack:** hold both brakes, power on, count to 20, release, power off
- **Throttle mode hack:** twist throttle fully, hold both brakes, power on, count to 20, release — throttle then operates at assist level 3
- Reverting to pedal-assist by repeating the dual-brake startup sequence
