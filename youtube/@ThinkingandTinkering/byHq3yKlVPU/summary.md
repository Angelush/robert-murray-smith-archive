## Overview
In this pilot episode of "Three Men In Shed," Robert Murray-Smith and two colleagues build a low-cost DIY freeze dryer from scavenged parts (a chest freezer, two pressure cookers, and a repurposed vacuum pump), successfully achieving a working vacuum. Between builds, the group also forages oak galls to brew a batch of traditional iron gall ink using a medieval recipe.

## Key Topics
- History of freeze drying (Nestle 1938, WWII blood serum and penicillin, NASA space food)
- DIY freeze dryer construction from budget components
- Vacuum system principles: vacuum chamber, cold trap, and vacuum pump
- Vacuum testing and leak checking
- Foraging oak apples (galls) from roadside trees
- Making iron gall ink using a medieval recipe
- Brief: Steve's woodworking tip for holding small pins; rum tasting; bike wheelie demonstration; roadside foraging discussion

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Chest freezer (Kelvinator, ~£30)** — housing for the cold trap
- **Pressure cookers (×2)** — one as the vacuum chamber, one as the cold trap internal vessel
- **Copper pipe** — cold trap coil, routed to the bottom of the pressure cooker
- **Tank fittings** — pipe-to-vessel connections; lathe-modified to remove internal ledge
- **Jubilee clips** — hose clamps on all connections
- **Silicone sealant** — sealing drilled holes in the freezer
- **Aluminium tape** — patching the freezer interior
- **Ceramic/pamic fibre** — re-packed insulation in the freezer
- **Vacuum pump** — old bench unit repurposed for the system
- **Vacuum gauge** — monitoring system vacuum (target: 28 in Hg)
- **Hot plate with PID controller (~£20)** — gentle heat source for the vacuum chamber
- **Oak apples / galls (~37 g)** — tannin-rich galls caused by wasps laying eggs in oak trees; contain galotannic acid
- **Water (250 ml)** — solvent for ink extraction
- **Gum arabic (7 g)** — binder and adhesion agent for the ink
- **Ferrous sulfate / iron sulfate (14 g, sold as garden fertilizer)** — iron salt that reacts with tannins to produce black ink
- **Oil of cloves or linseed oil** — optional preservative to prevent the ink going off
- **Spiced rum (from Grenada)** — tasted and unanimously condemned by the group

## Techniques and Methods
- **Freeze dryer assembly** — connecting vacuum chamber → cold trap → vacuum pump in series to prevent moisture entering the pump
- **Cold trap modification** — routing copper pipe to the bottom of a pressure cooker submerged in a chest freezer so vapour condenses before reaching the pump
- **Pressure vessel modification** — drilling and fitting tank fittings to pressure cooker lids; removing internal ledge with a lathe-mounted round file
- **Vacuum leak testing** — drawing vacuum then switching off the pump and monitoring gauge stability
- **Iron gall ink production** — drying and blending galls, soaking 24 hours, filtering through a coffee/lab filter, dissolving iron sulfate under stirring, adding gum arabic
- **Freeze drying a strawberry** — freezing first, then placing in vacuum chamber to sublimate the water out
- **DIY PID temperature control** — rewiring a cheap hotplate to accept a PID controller for precise low-level heating
- **Woodworking tip (Steve)** — pushing a small pin through a scrap of paper to hold it safely while starting it into wood
