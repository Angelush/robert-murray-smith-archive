## Overview
Robert visits Reclamet, described as the biggest and best scrapyard in Kent, to source materials for his ongoing rocket stove build. He reflects on the kindness of people — particularly Stuart, the MD, and the yard staff — who gave him free rein to explore and take useful scrap. In return, Robert plans to make instructional videos for the yard on how to safely separate metals for better scrap prices.

## Key Topics
- How to approach scrapyards to source project materials cheaply
- The importance of simply asking and being kind when seeking help
- Materials found at Reclamet useful for maker projects
- Metal separation at scrapyards and how it improves the price you receive
- Robert's planned quid pro quo: tutorial videos for the yard

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Steel** — main body material of most fire extinguishers
- **Aluminium** — thick-walled body of CO2 fire extinguishers
- **Brass** — fittings on fire extinguishers
- **CO2 cylinders / fire extinguishers** — sourced for project use
- **Large magnets** (surplus NHS pipe magnets) — offered for use in building alternators and generators
- **Transformers** — including two 4-ton units Stuart offered but Robert couldn't move
- **Motors and impellers** — seen in skips at the yard

## Techniques and Methods
- Separating mixed metals before scrapping to obtain individual metal prices rather than a bulk mixed-metal rate
- Disassembling CO2/fire extinguisher cylinders to separate aluminium bodies from brass fittings
- Using scrapyards as a sourcing strategy for maker/fabrication projects

## Robert's Own Replies
- **On approaching scrapyards:** Look up local yards and phone ahead. Explain who you are and what you want. You shouldn't have to pay to enter; you'll pay scrap price or slightly above as a favour. Be nice, open, and kind — it's very much a people thing.
- **On health and safety restrictions:** H&S is often used as an excuse, but the real issue is insurance — some people have sued yards after injuring themselves, driving insurance costs up. Robert feels personal responsibility is the key issue.
- **On what's available:** Stuart actually offered him two 4-ton transformers; Robert couldn't move them but would have taken them. It never ceases to amaze him what gets thrown away.
- **On the location:** Reclamet is on Wood Church Road, Birchington, Kent.
- **On the US:** Noted that scrapyard access may be easier in the US, per a commenter from Detroit.
- **On kindness:** Agrees that kindness is its own reward, and that Stuart's generosity made Robert want to do better — even without Stuart realising it.
