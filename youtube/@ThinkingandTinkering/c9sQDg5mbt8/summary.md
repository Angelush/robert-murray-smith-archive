## Overview
This video demonstrates how to build a vertical-axis wind turbine from a 2-litre plastic bottle, using heat-shaping to form fins and mounting it on bearings via a metal bar. The presenter (Luke) shows that even on a nearly windless day the turbine spins freely and quickly. The video ends as a part one, with a follow-up planned to wire a stepper motor to the turbine to generate electricity.

## Key Topics
- Marking and cutting a 2-litre bottle to create vertical fins
- Heat-bending the fins outward to catch wind
- Mounting the bottle on an 8 mm bar through drilled holes
- Using builder's board (Cintra board) and press-fit bearings as the frame
- Demonstrating spin performance in low-wind conditions
- Teaser for next video: connecting a stepper motor to generate current

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 2-litre plastic bottle (used as the turbine rotor)
- 8 mm metal bar (axle)
- Builder's board / Cintra board (frame material)
- 22 mm bearings (to allow free rotation)

## Techniques and Methods
- Marking equal-spaced rings and lines on the bottle to guide cuts
- Cutting fins, leaving a third of each side uncut as a fold point
- Heat-forming (using a heat source to soften and twist the plastic fins outward)
- Drilling 8 mm holes through the bottle top and bottom for the axle
- Drilling 22 mm holes in builder's board to press-fit bearings
