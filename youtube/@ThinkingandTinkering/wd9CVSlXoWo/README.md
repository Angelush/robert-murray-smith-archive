## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith 3D prints and assembles a demonstration model of the Di Pietro air engine, invented by Angelo Di Pietro in 1999, to explore how it overcomes the key limitations of conventional vane motors. He dissects both engine types side by side, explaining the Di Pietro's clever central rolling-ring mechanism and valve system. While praising the design's elegance, he notes his printed model is a learning tool rather than a precision working engine, and flags the valve arrangement and vane sealing as areas he wants to investigate further.
