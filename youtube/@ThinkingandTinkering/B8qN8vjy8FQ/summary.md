## Overview
This video is the second part of Robert Murray-Smith's series on producing graphene in quantity using a relatively safe, acid-free method. He demonstrates how to combine micronized graphite powder with an aqueous bromine solution, allow bromine intercalation to occur over 48 hours, and then use a bath sonicator to exfoliate the graphite into a concentrated graphene suspension. The result forms the primary constituent of his graphene-based conductive ink, part of an Indiegogo campaign to make graphene ink widely accessible.

## Key Topics
- Safety precautions when working with bromine solution (working outdoors or in a fume cupboard, keeping neutralizers on hand)
- Mixing ratios: 2 g of micronized graphite per 200 mL of saturated bromine solution
- Bromine intercalation mechanism — bromine enters between graphite layers and forces them apart
- Bath sonication to complete exfoliation of graphene sheets
- Settling/sedimentation to separate graphene suspension from unexfoliated particles
- Use of a dispersant (sodium citrate) to stabilise the graphene suspension
- Application of the product as graphene ink

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Aqueous bromine solution** — 1 molar concentration; the intercalating medium prepared in Part 1
- **Graphite powder** — micronized, ~5 µm particle size (D90); added at 2 g per 200 mL bromine solution
- **Sodium thiosulfate / sodium bisulfate** — safety neutralisers kept on hand to quench bromine spills
- **Sodium citrate** — added at ~0.5 g as a dispersant to keep graphene in suspension
- **Bromine gas / chlorine gas** — noted as an off-gassing hazard when the vessel is open

## Techniques and Methods
- **Bromine intercalation** — graphite is added to aqueous bromine solution and left undisturbed for 48 hours; bromine diffuses between graphite layers and expands the interlayer spacing
- **Bath sonication** — 100 W jeweler's/bath sonicator used for 10–20 minutes to shear exfoliated layers apart; explicitly capped at under 45 minutes to avoid sheet fragmentation
- **Sedimentation** — after sonication, the mixture is left to settle; larger unexfoliated particles fall to the bottom while the graphene-rich supernatant is collected
- **Dispersant addition** — sodium citrate is stirred in to stabilise the colloidal graphene suspension for use as ink
