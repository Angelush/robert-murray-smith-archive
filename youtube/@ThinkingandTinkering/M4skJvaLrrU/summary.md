## Overview
Part 4 of Robert's rotating ventilator wind power series focuses on constructing the rotor to pair with the previously built stator. He repurposes a desktop fan as the rotor's central hub, builds the rotor body from Sintra board and ABS strip, and attaches 50 neodymium magnets in a north-south alternating pattern. A quick hand-spin test produces around 3 volts and 60 milliamps from a single coil, suggesting a rough total output of ~5 watts across all 29 coils.

## Key Topics
- Adapting the stator jig to serve as a rotor-building jig
- Using a desktop fan as the rotor's central component
- Constructing the rotor hoop from Sintra board (PVC) and ABS strip
- Placing 50 magnets in a north-south alternating pattern
- Initial voltage and current testing of the generator
- Plan to couple the rotor/stator assembly into the ventilator cowl

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Sintra board (PVC builders board)** — used to form the main rotor body
- **ABS strip** — used as the inner hoop lining, heat welded to the blades
- **Hot glue** — used to fix magnets onto the rotor
- **Neodymium magnets (×50)** — arranged in north-south alternating pattern around the rotor
- **Four-inch nails** — used as jig pins

## Techniques and Methods
- Jig adaptation: redrilling hole positions on the existing stator jig to new rotor dimensions, with a ~2 mm clearance gap accounted for
- Heat welding ABS strip to Sintra board to form the rotor hoop
- Hot-glue bonding of magnets to the rotor rim
- Single-coil bench test using a multimeter (voltage and milliamp measurement) to validate generator output before full assembly
- Flexible coupling planned to connect rotor axle to the ventilator shaft

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Prototype mindset**: Robert emphasises the whole build is a prototype and he's learning by doing — he's already identified things he'd change but is committed to seeing it through rather than endlessly iterating.
- **Fan uncertainty**: He's not sure whether including the central fan is the right decision, but plans to test it and remove it if it doesn't work.
- **Coil winding direction**: He notes he paid close attention to winding direction and polarity when mounting the coils, and plans to explain this in the next video.
- **Chimney use warning**: He advises against mounting this on an active chimney due to soot and heat, suggesting external ring mounting if someone insists on doing so.
- **Commercial direction**: His current focus is on transitioning the concept toward mass production rather than more prototypes, with news expected soon.
- **Series format**: He acknowledges splitting the build into parts gets more views than a single long video — a deliberate choice.
- **Timeline**: Completion just needs two things — finish it, then wait for wind.
