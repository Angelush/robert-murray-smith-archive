## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a Frenette (friction) heater — a device where an inner drum rotates inside an outer drum with a narrow oil-filled gap, generating heat through fluid shear. He walks through his construction process using available pipe, perspex/acrylic discs, skate bearings, and standard hardware, then tests the finished device. The build is acknowledged as non-optimal but serves as a working proof-of-concept, achieving a temperature rise from ~13°C to ~33°C in roughly six minutes at 2,800 RPM.
