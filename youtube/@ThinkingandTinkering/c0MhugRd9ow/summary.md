## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith completes and test-rides a homemade electric quad bike built primarily from bamboo, demonstrating that bamboo is robust enough to serve as a structural material in a vehicle chassis. The final version incorporates an improved gear ratio using a bicycle cassette, solving earlier overheating and performance issues. The project proves bamboo's viability for demanding mechanical applications.

## Key Topics
- Completion and final build details of the bamboo electric quad bike
- Gear ratio optimization for electric motor efficiency
- Test ride demonstration in a car park
- Bamboo as a structural/chassis material for vehicles
- Low-cost, scrap-based building philosophy

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Bamboo** — primary structural/chassis material for the quad bike frame
- **Bicycle cassette (gear cassette)** — used to add an intermediate gear stage between the motor and chain drive
- **Chain drive** — final drive to the wheels
- **Electric motor** — 500W motor sourced from eBay for approximately £20

## Techniques and Methods
- Modifying gear ratios using a salvaged bicycle cassette to achieve a 6:1 to 8:1 reduction (up from ~2–3:1)
- Scrap-based fabrication — building from available materials rather than purpose-bought components
- Chaining a cassette between motor output and final chain drive to reduce current draw and heat generation

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Motor power:** Confirms he used a 500W motor and suggests 1kW would be sufficient for those wanting more performance.
- **Total build cost:** Emphasises the scrap/low-cost ethos — the entire build cost approximately **£46**, with the motor costing just £20 from eBay.
- **Purpose:** Describes it plainly as "little more than a toy" and jokes it's "for shopping," not a serious e-bike replacement.
- **Legality/compliance:** Suggests adding pedals and limiting the motor as a path to making it road-legal, acknowledging some riders will likely bypass those restrictions.
- **Bamboo vs. other materials:** Acknowledges alternatives would work but prefers bamboo for its "green" credentials.
- **Simplicity as a design goal:** Opted for a simpler design over a more sophisticated one deliberately.
- **General tone:** Enthusiastic and encouraging — repeatedly tells viewers it's "an easy build" and urges them to get building.
