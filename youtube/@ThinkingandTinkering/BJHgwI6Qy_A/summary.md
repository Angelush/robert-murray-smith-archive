## Overview
This is Part 2 of Robert's build series for a large-wheeled electric kick scooter with a steampunk aesthetic. Robert shows the scooter painted racing green with wheels fitted, explains his design decisions and component sourcing (primarily from a fire-damaged donor moped), and outlines the planned steampunk-style decorative and functional additions. The key takeaway is that form is as important as function to Robert — the chunky green frame naturally suggested a steampunk/diesel punk direction, and he's leaning into that fully.

## Key Topics
- Progress update from Part 1: wheels fitted, painted racing green, steering column reinforced
- Steampunk/diesel punk design theme inspired by the 1960s *The Time Machine* film
- Parts sourced from a burnt-out donor moped (handlebars, kickstand)
- Planned decorative elements: gold pinstripe, print drum repurposed as instrument panel, Munson rings
- Motor specification and expected top speed (~18–20 km/h)
- Disc brake system with red calipers
- Plans for electrics walkthrough (battery, controller, display) in a future video
- Consideration of mudguards, potentially in copper

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Steel** — frame, box section support, structural components
- **Racing green paint** — applied to the frame
- **Gold pinstripe** — planned decorative detail
- **Copper** — considered for mudguards (Robert noted he'd need to lacquer it to prevent constant cleaning)
- **Brass Munson rings** — for mounting the instrument panel to the frame
- **Wood (cutting board)** — to be cleaned, oiled, and used as the standing deck
- **Disc brake calipers** — red-coloured, sourced separately

## Techniques and Methods
- Welding box section supports to reinforce the steering column fork
- Welding a kickstand onto the frame
- Spot-welding the fork suspension to lock it out (suspension removed; tyre flex used instead)
- Repurposing a printer drum as an instrument/display panel housing
- Mounting components with Munson rings fastened to frame lugs
- Drilling or welding extra lugs/brackets as needed for component attachment

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Suspension removed intentionally:** Robert confirmed he took out the fork suspension, spot-welding the forks rigid; he relies on tyre flex alone. He still needs to trim the fork tops to clear the frame.
- **Handlebars adjustable:** If the rake or height proves wrong once the scooter is on the ground, he can pipe-bend the bars to a better angle — nothing is final yet.
- **Battery placement undecided:** At time of filming he hadn't settled on where to put the battery, but expected to decide within a day or two.
- **Copper mudguards would need lacquering** to avoid constant polishing — he acknowledged this trade-off.
- **Legal reality in Europe:** Robert repeatedly noted that given European e-scooter laws, this will realistically only ever be ridden in a car park.
- **Possibly giving the scooter away:** He mentioned wanting to give the finished build away, which he's factoring into design choices.
- **Light show planned:** He confirmed he intends to go for a light show on the build.
- **Mini gasifiers:** Humorously noted he'd been looking at mini gasifiers — suggesting a fantasy of making it steam-powered in appearance if not reality.
- **Frame alignment:** Admitted he had to hit the frame with a sledgehammer to correct a misalignment during construction.
