## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith takes a walking tour of Faversham, Kent, to explore the real historical murder that inspired "Arden of Faversham" — a Elizabethan play whose authorship is contested, with Shakespeare among the leading candidates. The video weaves together the town's history (gunpowder manufacturing, its role as a Cinque Port) with the dramatic story of Thomas Arden's murder by his wife Alice and her lover Mosby, concluding that textual analysis supports Shakespeare writing at least the central section of the play.

## Key Topics
- The conservation of matter and Bill Bryson's claim that we each contain roughly a million atoms of Shakespeare
- "Lost" Shakespeare plays — works of uncertain or disputed authorship
- *Arden of Faversham*: the play's plot, historical basis, and the Shakespeare attribution debate (alternative candidates: Thomas Kyd, Christopher Marlowe)
- Thomas Arden (born 1508) — the real murder victim, who profited from the Dissolution of the Monasteries
- The murder conspiracy: Alice Arden, her lover Mosby, and hired mercenaries "Black Will" and "Shake-bag"
- Faversham's history as a Cinque Port (a limb of Dover) and former capital of England
- The Faversham Gunpowder Works and its explosions
- The proposed Cleve Hill solar farm and battery storage facility and local opposition to it

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Gunpowder** — three-ingredient mixture; described as an astounding yet simple invention
- **Saltpeter (potassium nitrate, KNO₃)** — the hardest ingredient to source; historically extracted by collecting urine and depositing it on dung heaps, allowing crystals to rise to the surface
- **Charcoal** — one of the three gunpowder ingredients
- **Sulfur** — one of the three gunpowder ingredients
- **Battery storage systems** — referenced in the context of the Cleve Hill project; Robert cites 23 fires in South Korea and a three-day fire in Illinois as safety concerns; notes the facility would store the TNT equivalent of 1/20th of the Hiroshima bomb

## Techniques and Methods
- **Saltpeter extraction from dung heaps** — urine collected and added to dung heaps periodically; potassium nitrate crystals rise to the surface and are scraped off
- **Spatial separation of gunpowder production stages** — individual process buildings kept far apart to limit blast propagation in the event of an explosion
