## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith surveys the mechanical (top-down) methods for producing graphene, contrasting them with the chemical methods covered in the previous video (1083). He walks through the full progression from the original Nobel Prize-winning sticky tape method through blending, three-roll milling, ball milling, bead stirring, and sonication, explaining how each balances shear force and impact to exfoliate graphene layers from graphite.

## Key Topics
- Distinction between top-down (mechanical) and bottom-up graphene production methods
- The original Novoselov & Geim sticky tape exfoliation technique
- Pencil-on-paper as a simple shear method
- Sandpaper/abrasive rubbing method (Japanese research, 4000-grit sandpaper on plastic)
- Electrostatic attraction causing graphene to adhere to plastic substrates
- Blender-based liquid exfoliation (Trinity College Dublin method)
- Three-roll mill method (American contribution) — high shear, low impact
- Ball mill method — medium shear, medium impact
- Bead stirrer method — low shear, no impact, using glycerin as a carrier
- Sonication (bath and horn sonicators) as a shear/impact method
- Use of exfoliated graphite as a superior starting material for all mechanical methods
- Preview of upcoming electrochemical methods video

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Sri Lankan graphite** — high-quality natural graphite used as the primary starting material
- **Sticky tape / sellotape / packing tape** — used in the original Novoselov & Geim exfoliation
- **Graphite powder** — used in sandpaper and blender methods
- **Sandpaper (4000-grit)** — abrasive used in the Japanese rubbing method
- **Plastic substrate** — graphene adheres to it via electrostatic attraction during the sandpaper method
- **Wool, cotton, paper** — alternative abrasive/rubbing materials tested
- **Alcohol, water, and washing-up liquid** — solvent mixture used in Trinity College Dublin blender method
- **Glue / epoxy-type binder** — used in three-roll mill method to hold graphene during shearing
- **Glycerin** — used as carrier/binder in bead stirrer method; easier to separate than solid media
- **Exfoliated graphite** — pre-expanded graphite (made in video 1083) that makes all mechanical methods more effective
- **Pencil (graphite + clay)** — demonstrated as an everyday shear method

## Techniques and Methods
- **Tape exfoliation** — repeated peeling with sticky tape to progressively thin graphite down to single layers
- **Abrasive rubbing (sandpaper method)** — rubbing graphite powder on plastic with 4000-grit sandpaper; graphene adheres electrostatically
- **Blender/liquid shear exfoliation** — household blender with alcohol/water/surfactant mixture (Trinity College Dublin)
- **Three-roll milling** — rollers at different speeds create high shear, low impact; graphene dispersed in glue
- **Ball milling** — tumbling balls create combined shear and impact; tunable by changing media, speed, and direction
- **Bead stirring** — glass beads stirred in glycerin/graphite mixture; pure low-shear, no-impact approach
- **Sonication** — ultrasonic bath or horn sonicator vibrates and shears graphene flakes off graphite
- **Exfoliated graphite as feedstock** — pre-expanding graphite layers before applying any mechanical method to improve yield
