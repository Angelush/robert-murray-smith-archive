## Overview
A short demonstration video showing how to bend copper microbore pipe into a coil for use in distillation setups. Robert uses a pipe as a former to shape the copper tubing into consistent loops. He notes that for tighter bends, filling the tube with sand prevents kinking.

## Key Topics
- Constructing a distillation coil from copper microbore pipe
- Using a pipe as a bending former
- Preventing kinks when bending copper tubing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Copper microbore pipe (8mm diameter) — used as the coil material
- Sand — mentioned as a fill material to prevent kinking on tighter radius bends

## Techniques and Methods
- Hand-bending copper microbore pipe around a cylindrical former (piece of pipe) to form a coil
- Filling tube with sand before bending to achieve tighter bends without deformation
