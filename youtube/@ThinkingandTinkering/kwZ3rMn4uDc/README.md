## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates his graphene-based conductive ink, recently repackaged into recyclable metal tins, by applying it to polyethylene — one of the most notoriously difficult plastics to coat. He walks through the full process of cleaning, priming, and painting the plastic with the ink, then electroplating the coated surface with copper using a universal electroplating solution. The key takeaway is that this low-cost, highly conductive graphene/graphite ink can render otherwise uncoatable plastics suitable for electroplating and EMF shielding.
