## Overview
In this short video, Robert demonstrates how he fabricates decorative plastic panels for his large-wheel electric kick scooter build. Using a PVC foam board called Stormlight as the base material, he walks through his process of templating, cutting, shaping, filling, and finishing panels to achieve smooth, organic curves. The key takeaway is that this combination of preformed PVC board, body filler, and optional fibreglass reinforcement is a fast and cost-effective alternative to metal panel beating.

## Key Topics
- Fabricating decorative (non-structural) plastic body panels for the scooter
- Using Stormlight PVC board as a base material
- Card templating to get the right shape before cutting
- Using body filler and sanding to achieve smooth, curved forms
- Deciding when to add fibreglass reinforcement vs. leaving the panel as-is
- Painting and finishing the completed panels

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Stormlight PVC board** — the primary panel material; large sheets (~4m × 1.2m) available for ~£28/sheet
- **Super glue (cyanoacrylate)** — used to bond PVC pieces; described as creating joints stronger than the base material
- **Body filler** — applied over the PVC substrate to smooth out boxy corners and create organic curves
- **Fibreglass tissue** — used as a thin reinforcing layer over panels expected to take impact
- **Fibreglass resin** — used with the tissue; noted to not adhere particularly well to the PVC, which can be exploited to create a removable shell

## Techniques and Methods
- **Card templating** — cutting rough card shapes to test fit before committing to the PVC material
- **Super glue bonding** — joining PVC board pieces together
- **Body filler application and sanding** — building up and smoothing curves over the base PVC form
- **Fibreglass tissue layup** — laminating a thin tissue layer over higher-impact panels for durability
- **Edge wrapping with fibreglass** — wrapping fibreglass around panel edges to lock the laminate in place
- **Painting and finishing** — final surface treatment after sanding
