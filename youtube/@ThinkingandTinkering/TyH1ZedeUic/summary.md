## Overview
Robert 3D prints both a toroidal turbine blade and a standard three-bladed wind turbine blade, then tests them under identical conditions (same swept diameter, same fan, same distance) to see which generates more voltage. The result is that the toroidal blade performs no better than the standard blade, which Robert explains is unsurprising given that both are governed by the same physical limit (Betz's Law).

## Key Topics
- Toroidal propeller/turbine blade design vs. standard three-bladed wind turbine blade
- Comparative voltage output testing of the two blade designs
- Betz's Law as the governing limit on wind energy extraction
- 3D printing blades from Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing both blade designs (toroidal and standard) to the same diameter for a fair comparison
- Mounting each blade on a small NEMA stepper/generator motor
- Measuring open-circuit voltage output from each blade under identical fan-driven airflow conditions
