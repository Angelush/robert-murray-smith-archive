## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a 3D-printed differential inspired by Leonardo da Vinci's 15th-century design, which da Vinci used in his spring-driven cart. The video explores the history of the differential — from ancient China and the Antikythera mechanism through to da Vinci — and demonstrates how lantern-and-pinion gears (the pre-bevel-gear solution for transmitting power at 90°) can be assembled into a functional differential. Robert's takeaway is that starting with a crude working model is often more productive than striving for complexity from the outset.
