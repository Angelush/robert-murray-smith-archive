## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores how metallic wax products (metal flakes suspended in wax) can be rubbed onto 3D-printed plastic models to achieve a convincing steampunk-style metallic finish. Using a 3D-printed pendulum and escapement mechanism as a demonstration piece, he shows how a simple application technique transforms plain black PLA into something resembling aged steel, gold, or bronze. His main takeaway is that this approach is far more accessible than traditional model painting for hobbyists who aren't confident artists.

## Key Topics
- Evolution of his 3D-printed mechanical model aesthetic — from plain gray PLA for clarity, to multicolor for part identification, to decorative finishing
- Introduction to steampunk as a design philosophy (Victorian-inspired, forward-looking "what if" aesthetic)
- Demonstration of metallic wax applied to a combined pendulum and escapement mechanism
- Practical notes on cost, color variety, and technique
- Brief discussion of the pendulum mechanism itself and weight-driving it

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Black PLA** — base filament used for the printed models; noted as working especially well with metallic waxes
- **Gray PLA** — earlier functional models printed in this for camera clarity
- **White PLA** — used for a hypocycloid drive model
- **Metallic wax (silver)** — metal flakes in a wax carrier, rubbed onto the surface to create a steel-like finish
- **Metallic wax (gold)** — same product in gold tone, used on the backing plate
- **Metallic wax (various colors)** — noted as available in a range of colors; purchased from Amazon for £5–7 each

## Techniques and Methods
- **Dry rubbing / burnishing** — applying metallic wax with a cloth, dabbing a small amount and rubbing it over the surface to build up a metallic sheen
- **Variable coverage** — heavier application produces a more uniform metallic look; lighter application gives a worn, distressed surface effect
- **Base color selection** — using black PLA as the substrate to complement the metallic wax tones
- **Weight-driven pendulum testing** — attaching a hanging weight to demonstrate the escapement mechanism in motion
