## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores whether replacing the flat discs of a classic Tesla turbine with 3D-printed hyperbolic cones — the same funnel shape found in wishing-well coin spirals — produces a more effective turbine. He builds a stack of 10 hyperbolic cones on an axle, prints a custom housing in Tinkercad, and tests the assembly with a hairdryer, achieving ~2,500 RPM from a non-pressurised air source. His conclusion is that the hyperbolic shape naturally induces the inward spiral without the tedious angle-tuning required by flat discs, and appears to outperform the conventional Tesla turbine in the low-RPM range.
