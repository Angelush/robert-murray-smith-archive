## Overview
Luke (a collaborator on Robert Murray-Smith's channel) repurposes a bamboo quad bike — originally built by Rob in video #1155 — by converting it into a reverse-configuration electric trike with two wheels at the front and one at the back. The first part of the build focuses on fabricating and attaching the rear wheel assembly using modified bicycle back stays, welding, and structural support brackets.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing an existing bamboo quad bike frame rather than dismantling it
- Design rationale for a reverse trike configuration (two front wheels, one rear)
- Rear wheel attachment using modified bicycle back stays
- Reference to Rob's earlier large-cog/gear-ratio video (#1084) for context on the drive wheel
- Preview of upcoming electronics and drive system installation in Part 2

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Bamboo (frame of the original quad bike)
- Steel bicycle back stays (modified for the rear fork)
- U-bolts (for axle attachment)
- Large sprocket/cog wheel (from prior build, ~8:1 gear ratio)
- Steel tube/pole (for extending the back stay arms)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting and sectioning bicycle back stays to refit around a large-cog rear wheel
- Welding extended steel sections and structural support brackets to the bamboo frame
- U-bolt mounting of the rear axle
- Structural reinforcement with four support brackets around the rear wheel mount
