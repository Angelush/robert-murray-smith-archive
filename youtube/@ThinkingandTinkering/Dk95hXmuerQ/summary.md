## Overview
Luke demonstrates how to build a DIY resistive cup warmer using conductive ink and copper strips. The cup is painted with conductive ink between two copper contact strips, and placed on a custom "warming plate" with spring contacts salvaged from a battery box. The completed device successfully heats liquid in the cup to around 50°C.

## Key Topics
- Explanation of resistive heating as a concept
- Designing a painted conductive ink heater for a ceramic cup
- Creating a registration-based warming plate with spring contacts
- Safety considerations for cup contact placement (keeping copper away from lips)
- Live demonstration heating water from 11°C ambient to ~50°C

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Conductive ink — painted onto the cup surface to form the resistive heating element
- Copper strips — applied at top and bottom of the cup as electrical contacts
- AA battery box springs — salvaged and repurposed as spring contacts on the warming plate

## Techniques and Methods
- Painting conductive ink onto a ceramic cup surface
- Attaching copper strip electrodes to the cup
- Designing a registration/alignment system for repeatable cup placement
- Using salvaged spring contacts to make a self-connecting warming plate
- Measuring resistance with a multimeter to verify circuit continuity
- Adjusting voltage to drive resistive heating
