## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith discusses the fragility of the global economy and the UK's potential energy crisis in winter 2022, driven by supply chain disruptions from China's COVID policies and Russia's gas leverage over Europe. He outlines the UK government's three-stage contingency plan for energy shortages — including potential rolling blackouts — and shares practical, low-tech solutions he personally uses for heating and cooking to maintain self-reliance.

## Key Topics
- Fragility of the global economy and supply chain disruptions
- China's zero-COVID policy and its impact on electronics/manufacturing supply
- Russia-Ukraine war's effect on European gas and UK electricity supply
- UK government's three-stage energy shortage contingency plan
- Critical home energy needs: heating, cooking, lighting, refrigeration
- DIY alternatives for heating (methanol heater) and cooking (rocket stove)
- The importance of personal self-reliance and skills sharing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Methanol** — used as fuel in a DIY heater Robert built and uses at home, reportedly reducing his heating bill by 75%

## Techniques and Methods
- **Rocket stove construction** — referenced as a previously demonstrated method for off-grid cooking
- **DIY methanol heater** — a home-built heater Robert uses as a primary heat source, with optional safety cap for households needing it
- **Load-shifting** — reducing electricity use during peak demand hours (4–7 PM) as suggested by the government plan
