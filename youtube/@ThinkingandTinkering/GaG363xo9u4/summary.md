## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a DIY solar kettle using an evacuated solar tube — a remarkably simple build that heats water to boiling in 20–30 minutes using sunlight alone. He notes that the same concept can be used as a portable solar shower by mixing the boiled water with cold water. He expresses puzzlement that a commercial UK version (which sold for £35) is no longer available.

## Key Topics
- Evacuated solar tubes: what they are and where to source them (eBay)
- Prior use of evacuated tubes in heat pipe and steam engine experiments
- Commercial solar kettles and why Robert decided to build his own
- Construction of a DIY solar kettle from plastic pipe and foam
- Optimal tilt angle (30°) for maximum solar collection efficiency
- Alternative applications: solar ovens, solar barbecues, solar showers

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Evacuated solar tube (half-metre long, 60 mm diameter; available up to 113 mm diameter)
- 10 PSI plastic pipe (leftover from a previous ram pump project)
- Reflective aluminium foam (used as an internal reflector lining)
- Expanding foam (used to seal and fix the tube in the pipe housing)
- Wire (bent for the support legs)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting a window and a 30° angle into a section of plastic pipe to create the kettle housing
- Lining the interior of the housing with reflective aluminium foam to improve heat concentration
- Setting the device at a 30° angle to the sun (cited from research papers as the optimal angle)
- Optional enhancement: hinging the cut-out pipe sections and covering with foil to act as parabolic reflectors pointing at the tube

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirmed he sourced his evacuated tubes from **eBay**.
- Agreed that a **both-ends-open** tube design is significantly more useful.
- On the 30° angle: stated it came from **research papers** — he has no deeper explanation beyond that.
- Expressed general support for evacuated solar tube technology, calling it **better than photovoltaics** for heating purposes ("the poor cousin of photovoltaic and to my mind better").
- Noted that wider adoption of solar heating will likely only happen once **energy prices are high enough** to motivate people.
- Expressed interest in trying a **kiln application** for evacuated tubes, as suggested by a commenter.
