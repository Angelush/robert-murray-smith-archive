## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a hubless wheel using salvaged aluminium bicycle rims, explaining that a "hubless" wheel is essentially one where the axle is enlarged to encompass the entire wheel. He builds a working prototype using roller bearings mounted on a simple iron frame, with only three points of contact, and outlines his plan to develop it into a collapsible, electric hubless bicycle driven by a toothed belt.
