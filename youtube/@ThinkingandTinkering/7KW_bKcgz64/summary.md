## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a hubless wheel using salvaged aluminium bicycle rims, explaining that a "hubless" wheel is essentially one where the axle is enlarged to encompass the entire wheel. He builds a working prototype using roller bearings mounted on a simple iron frame, with only three points of contact, and outlines his plan to develop it into a collapsible, electric hubless bicycle driven by a toothed belt.

## Key Topics
- The true mechanical definition of a hubless wheel (an axle scaled up to the wheel's full diameter)
- Preparing bicycle rims by removing spoke inserts and smoothing the inner surface
- The load distribution principle: weight bearing at the bottom roller, top contact only for lateral stability
- Roller bearing carriage design using nylon-tyred roller bearings, nylon washers, and an iron frame
- Comparison between motorcycle-grade hubless wheels (full bearing ring) and bicycle-grade (3-point contact)
- Plans for a collapsible frame inspired by existing folding bicycle designs
- Adding a toothed belt to the rim to enable electric motor drive
- Tire valve relocation to avoid obstruction
- Accessibility of the design for home builders with minimal tools

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Aluminium bicycle rims (salvaged from a scrapped bike)
- Steel spoke inserts (removed during preparation)
- Nylon-tyred roller bearings (pre-made, purchased online)
- Nylon washers (used as spacers between bearings)
- Iron/steel frame stock (used to fabricate the bearing carriage)
- Toothed belt (planned addition for motor drive)

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling out steel spoke inserts flush with the rim
- Pushing out drilled inserts with a screwdriver from the reverse side
- Sanding the inner rim surface smooth for roller contact
- Fabricating a simple iron bearing carriage to hold roller bearings at two bottom contact points and one top contact point
- Screwing roller bearings onto the carriage spaced apart with nylon washers
- Planned: gluing a toothed belt onto the outer rim to create a large drive gear
- Planned: relocating the tyre valve to the side to clear the bearing assembly

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Project status / COVID delay:** The battery/electric project has not been abandoned — it was postponed due to the pandemic, giving him time to work on other things.
- **Design provenance:** He is not an original designer; the roller-bearing hubless wheel concept is already used in production bicycles, and he is intentionally replicating it. He links to an existing production bike for reference.
- **Philosophy on complexity:** He strongly prefers simplicity and minimum-tool builds so that others could replicate his work at home. This guides most of his design decisions (e.g., rejecting full bearing rings, rejecting maglev).
- **Maglev bearings:** He looked into magnetic levitation but considers it impractical due to power consumption, cost, and stability concerns.
- **Braking:** He intends to use standard cantilever rim brakes — simple and proven.
- **Frame direction:** He was considering a quad recumbent design, having four hubless wheels.
- **On "just use an axle" criticism:** He deliberately keeps the design visually hubless because a hidden internal axle would invite comments questioning the point of the exercise.
- **Ethos of the channel:** He describes the channel as a journal of ongoing experimentation rather than a showcase of finished products.
- **Home-builder focus:** Repeatedly emphasises that accessibility for builders without specialist tools is his primary design constraint.
