## Overview

This is the second video in Robert Murray-Smith's kiln-free pottery series, focused entirely on the specific recipe and method for making a geopolymer modelling material from cat litter, sodium silicate, and magnesium oxide. He demonstrates mixing and sculpting a small face mask, then curing it in a household oven at 180°C, with the finished piece coming out rock hard. He also shares an insight that this material may be useful to traditional potters as a pre-drying stabiliser, potentially eliminating pot explosions during kiln firing.
