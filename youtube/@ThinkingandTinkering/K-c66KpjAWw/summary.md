## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith takes the alternator-style generator from a cheap ceiling fan (explored in video 1717) and pairs it with a DIY Savonius vertical-axis wind turbine rotor built from leftover materials. The experiment demonstrates that the ceiling fan's generator section is actually quite efficient — the problem in the previous video was the poor wind-capture blades, not the generator. His main takeaway is that this low-cost alternator design could be a viable path to cheap, accessible wind power generation.

## Key Topics
- Critique of the original ceiling fan wind turbine build (video 1717): good generator, bad rotor
- Construction of a Savonius VAWT rotor from scrap materials
- Power output measurements of the new rotor on the same generator
- Comparison of expected vs. actual power output
- Argument for the alternator-style generator (ring magnet + single coil) as a cheap wind generation solution
- Horizontal vs. vertical axis wind turbine debate — no universal winner, depends on situation
- Potential for scaling this generator type in a wind wall configuration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PVC-clad polyurethane foam board** (sold as "Central Board" in the US, "Fascia and Soffit / Builders Board" in the UK, product by Eurozone) — used for the Savonius rotor discs
- **Rainwater gutter sections** (curved plastic) — used as the Savonius rotor blades
- **Super glue** — used to bond rotor components
- **Pressed steel** — noted as the material of the cheap ceiling fan generator housing

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly of a cheap ceiling fan to extract the alternator/generator core
- Fabrication of a Savonius VAWT rotor by gluing foam discs and curved gutter pieces to the fan's generator hub
- Electrical output measurement under load: current (milliamps) and voltage (volts) recorded separately using an LED load
- Comparison of measured output against theoretical expected power for given wind speed and rotor swept area

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Harmony turbines**: Robert acknowledges being aware of the Harmony wind turbine brand (which uses furling); notes his design does not furl, distinguishing it from that product.
- **HAWT in built-up areas**: Bluntly states a horizontal-axis wind turbine won't work well in an urban/built-up environment, implying the VAWT approach is more suited to such settings.
- **Sourcing the cheap ceiling fan generators**: The Bang Good stock has sold out; he found them on Alibaba for £4–5 (499 rupees) but UK resellers are charging £25–29, which he calls a rip-off. He is planning a follow-up video on **how to build this style of generator yourself** specifically to avoid being overcharged.
