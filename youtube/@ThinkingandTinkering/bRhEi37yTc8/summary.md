## Overview
This video features Luke (a guest creator on Robert Murray-Smith's channel) customizing a budget PC case with an apocalyptic/steampunk aesthetic. He disassembles the case, spray paints it copper, and adds decorative elements including a wood-grained front panel, decorative bulbs, a gold amp meter, and gold handles to achieve the final look.

## Key Topics
- Steampunk/apocalyptic PC case customization
- Spray painting a computer case copper
- Wood graining technique for the front panel
- Adding decorative hardware elements (bulbs, amp meter, handles)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Copper spray paint
- Wood stain/varnish (used for graining effect)
- Gold spray paint (for amp meter and handles)
- Clear acrylic side panel (pre-existing on case)

## Techniques and Methods
- Case disassembly before painting
- Spray booth painting
- Wood graining technique: base coat in sandy color, then wood stain/varnish applied over top to simulate burl walnut
- Drilling out holes to fit components (amp meter)
- Spray painting decorative hardware gold
