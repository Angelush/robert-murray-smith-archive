## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith debunks a popular "free energy device" hoax circulating online, in which two coils and a magnet are claimed to generate electricity to light a bulb. He demonstrates that the device is simply a magic trick prop with a hidden battery, and explains the various techniques used to conceal the power source and misdirect viewers.

## Key Topics
- Explanation of the "free energy device" construction (straight coil + conical coil + magnet)
- Reveal that the device is a prop — the coil arrangement is irrelevant misdirection
- Hidden battery mechanism: contacts on the prop close a circuit when touched
- CR2032 watch batteries as a concealable power source for LEDs
- Alternative methods to light bulbs deceptively (hidden LED in hand, laser on phosphor coating, Tesla coil / high voltage)
- Distinction between genuine free energy researchers and clickbait fraudsters

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Straight coil (wound on a former)
- Conical / pancake coil
- Permanent magnet (hot-glued to inner coil)
- LED light bulb
- CR2032 coin cell batteries
- Salt water (used to make fingers conductive)
- Phosphor (coating on incandescent/fluorescent bulbs)

## Techniques and Methods
- Winding and stretching a conical coil to match a straight coil
- Hot gluing a magnet to the centre of the inner coil
- Hiding a battery inside a light bulb or motor prop with two exposed contacts
- Closing a circuit via skin conductivity (fingers, wedding ring, salt-wetted skin)
- Illuminating phosphor-coated bulbs via laser or high-voltage (Tesla coil) as alternative tricks
