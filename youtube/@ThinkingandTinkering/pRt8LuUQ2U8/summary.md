## Overview
This video, presented by Franz as part of a challenge to build a useful machine from a bicycle, demonstrates how a stationary exercise bike can be converted into an electricity generator. Three different generator setups are tested — a bicycle dynamo, a car power window motor, and a windshield wiper motor — each capable of powering or charging various household devices. The main takeaway is that with minimal components (a DC motor, a diode, and a battery), anyone can build a practical energy harvester from a bicycle.

## Key Topics
- Converting a stationary exercise bike into an electricity generator
- Comparison of three generator types by output wattage
- Charging batteries (lead-acid, cordless tool batteries) via pedal power
- Powering small appliances directly from the bicycle generator
- Basic circuit components needed to build an energy harvester

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Bicycle dynamo (AC, 2.5W output)
- Car power window motor (DC, 6W output)
- Car windshield wiper motor (DC, up to 48W output)
- Lead-acid battery (small, used for charging demo)
- Cordless drill/screwdriver battery pack
- Electrolytic capacitor (for voltage smoothing)
- Diode (to prevent back-feeding from battery)
- 75W car inverter (plugged into cigarette lighter outlet)
- CC (constant current) transformer/step-down converter
- Spring (for applying friction tension to wheel)

## Techniques and Methods
- Friction-drive coupling of generator motors to the bicycle wheel
- Stepping down AC dynamo voltage using a CC transformer
- Using a diode to block reverse current from battery to generator
- Smoothing DC output with an electrolytic capacitor
- Direct DC motor-to-battery charging circuit
- Converting DC to AC via a small car inverter to power mains appliances
