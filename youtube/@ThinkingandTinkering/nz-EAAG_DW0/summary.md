## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith addresses viewer questions about his unfinished projects, explaining the practical realities of running a YouTube channel as a small operation. He outlines how decisions about continuing or abandoning a project series are driven by a balance of viewer interest, income (advertising and memberships), and his own enthusiasm. The core takeaway is that projects are not abandoned arbitrarily — they are discontinued when viewership drops too low to justify the cost and effort relative to other content.

## Key Topics
- Why some video series go unfinished or are discontinued
- The three main drivers of the channel: Robert's interests, viewer interests, and income
- The role of advertising revenue and channel memberships in sustaining the channel
- How viewership drop-off determines when a project series ends
- The specific case of the wind turbine (VAWT) project being concluded
- The Mini electric conversion project and the cost barrier of sourcing a suitable motor
- The channel's size (~195,000 subscribers at time of recording) and its effect on sponsorship opportunities
