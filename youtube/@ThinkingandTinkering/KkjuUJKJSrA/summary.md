## Overview
Luke, presenting on Robert Murray-Smith's channel, builds a functional "gaming" PC at zero cost by sourcing all components through scavenging and asking friends. Despite the age and low spec of the parts, the finished machine successfully runs Windows 10 and several games at playable framerates. The video demonstrates that a usable gaming PC can be assembled without spending any money.

## Key Topics
- Sourcing free PC components through personal networks, IT contacts, and bin-salvaging
- Assembling a budget/zero-cost PC from mismatched second-hand parts
- Installing Windows 10 on salvaged hardware
- Gaming performance benchmarks on low-end, older components

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Graphene-based thermal paste — used on the CPU; noted as homemade, high-performing, and free

## Techniques and Methods
- CPU installation — aligning corner markers on CPU and motherboard socket, seating without bending pins
- RAM installation — matching notch alignment and pressing until dual-click confirmation
- Thermal paste application — pea-sized amount applied before mounting CPU cooler
- GPU sag mitigation — improvised physical support to prevent graphics card droop (no IO shield)
- POST testing — booting assembled system to verify hardware initialisation
- Windows 10 installation — installed from scratch onto salvaged 80GB HDD
