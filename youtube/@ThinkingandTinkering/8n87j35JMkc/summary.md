## Overview
In this short follow-up video (part of an ongoing series), Robert demonstrates his 3D-printed "twisted serpent" radial generator configured as a flywheel generator. Responding to viewer requests from video 1869, he adds a weighted plug to the top of the device, loads it with free weights (~7.5 kg), and uses a pull cord to spin it, successfully lighting four LEDs in series. He closes by suggesting a more practical real-world implementation using gearing and a supercapacitor for energy storage.

## Key Topics
- Follow-up to the twisted serpent generator series (videos 1869 and 1870)
- Converting the radial serpent generator into a flywheel configuration
- Adding free weights as a flywheel mass
- Pull-cord actuation to drive the generator
- Powering LEDs from the flywheel generator
- Practical improvement: gearing + supercapacitor storage for sustained energy delivery
- 3D-printable files available on Tinkercad

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Free weights (~7.5 kg, three weights used)
- A bearing (glued to the device hub)
- A pull cord
- Supercapacitor (mentioned as recommended storage solution)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing (parts designed in Tinkercad)
- Flywheel energy storage via added mass (free weights)
- Pull-cord actuation of the generator
- LED load testing (four LEDs in series)
- Gearing system integration (referenced from video 1860 pull-cord generator)
- Supercapacitor buffering for intermittent generation use cases
