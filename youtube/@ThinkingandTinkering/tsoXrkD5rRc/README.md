## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith describes his accidental discovery that his original SWAG ink formulation is optimal for electricity generation — any modification makes it worse — and decides to stop tweaking the ink and instead build a prototype generator. He outlines a passive, scalable design: a stack of SWAG-ink-coated sheets arranged like fish gills, placed in a seawater pipe or tidal zone, requiring no motor input to generate current.
