## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith designs and builds a DIY folding portable wind turbine, motivated by the high cost and surprisingly low performance of commercial options like the Shine ($360) and Rutland 504 (£425), which produce only 3–5 watts in typical UK wind conditions. The turbine uses 3D-printed hinged hubs combined with blades cut from standard soil pipe, and incorporates a magnetic gearbox from a previous video. After a successful outdoor test at just 1.8 m/s wind speed with helpers Ginger Jack and Jacob, Robert concludes the folding blade and gearbox sections are working well and announces a custom generator will be built in a follow-up video.
