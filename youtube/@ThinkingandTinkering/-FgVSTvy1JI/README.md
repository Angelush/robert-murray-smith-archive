## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith traces the surprisingly underappreciated history of the bicycle, arguing that it shaped not only modern industrial practices (assembly lines, advertising, lobbying) but also drove major social change — including the emancipation of working-class Europeans and women. He closes by celebrating the bicycle's ongoing relevance as both a global product and a rich source of parts for upcycling projects.
