## Overview
This video is an update to Robert's earlier methanol room heater (video 1702), addressing the main complaint viewers had: difficulty sourcing the carbon felt wick used in the original design. He demonstrates a simpler replacement wick built from an old sock, stainless steel mesh, and standard food tins, concluding that it works just as well and is far easier to make from common household items.

## Key Topics
- Limitations of the original carbon felt wick and why a replacement was needed
- Step-by-step construction of a new burner/wick unit using recycled tins and stainless steel mesh
- Assembly of the sock-based wick inside the tin burner housing
- Filling the burner with methanol and lighting it safely
- Safety guidance for first-time users of open-flame burners

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Carbon felt (100 carbon felt)** — original wick material from video 1702, noted as difficult to source
- **Stainless steel mesh (1mm)** — used as structural wick support; typically sold for blocking insects/rodents from basement eaves
- **Standard bean cans (large)** — outer housing for the burner
- **Mushy peas tin (300g, smaller)** — inner tube; chosen because it fits neatly inside the bean can
- **Old sock (cotton)** — the replacement wick material; folded double thickness inside the tin tube
- **Methanol** — fuel for the burner; burns with a near-invisible blue flame

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting stainless steel mesh with scissors into 27 × 150 mm strips
- Folding and interlocking two mesh strips into an L-shape to form a flat stainless steel "sock"
- Cutting and removing the bottom of the smaller tin to create an open tube
- Cutting four equidistant nicks at the base of the tube and bending them inward to secure it inside the mesh sock
- Threading a real sock into the tube at the lip, trimming to ~8 cm, and folding back on itself to create a double-thickness wick
- Cutting a ring from a second bean can (to the first rib) to serve as a lid/top collar for the burner
- Filling the assembled burner with methanol via a funnel
- Lighting by applying methanol to the exterior surface and holding a match or lighter to it
