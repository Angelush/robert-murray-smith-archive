## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to convert a BMW brushless DC fan motor into a hand-cranked generator using a modified exercise bicycle as the mechanical base. He fabricates improvised timing-belt cogs to connect the bicycle flywheel to the motor shaft, achieving roughly 5V at a couple of amps under load. The video emphasizes that the basic principle works well and encourages viewers to experiment with improved gear ratios and heavier flywheels for more output.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing a BMW brushless DC fan motor as a three-phase AC generator (rectified to DC)
- Adapting an exercise bicycle into a hand-cranked generator platform
- Using timing belts as DIY cogs/gears to couple the flywheel to the motor
- Truing a metal flywheel to achieve the correct diameter for belt fit
- Flywheel energy storage and its effect on generator output
- Gear ratio considerations for increasing output voltage/current

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- BMW brushless DC fan motor (three-phase AC output)
- Exercise bicycle (modified — seat removed, one pedal removed to form a crank handle)
- Timing belt (used as improvised cog teeth, glued onto wheel surfaces)
- Rubber tubing/liner (used to build up a surface to correct cog diameter)
- Sandpaper (high-grit, for truing the flywheel circumference)
- Sharpening stone and oil (alternative method for truing the wheel)
- M6 threaded bolts (for mounting the cog assembly)
- Half-watt LED lighting/display panel (connected as load)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting and gluing timing belt onto circular surfaces to create large improvised cogs
- Truing a metal flywheel by holding sandpaper or an angle grinder against it while spinning
- Using rubber tubing as a liner/shim to fine-tune cog diameter
- Single-stage gear reduction (large flywheel cog driving small motor cog) to increase motor RPM
- Hand-cranking with a single pedal repurposed as a crank handle
