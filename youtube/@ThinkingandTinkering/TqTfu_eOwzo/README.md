## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith visits Kew Gardens to introduce the field of biophotovoltaics — the conversion of light energy into electricity using photosynthesis and microbial electrochemistry. He explains how microorganisms at plant roots act like microbial fuel cells, harvesting electrons released during cellular respiration, and notes that the CO₂ produced is reabsorbed by the plants, making the process carbon-neutral. The video concludes with a hopeful outlook on this early-stage technology as a living, self-repairing energy source.
