## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make a wooden chessboard from inexpensive chopping boards, cutting them into precise squares and gluing them together in strips. The project costs just a few pounds in materials and results in a functional, attractive board finished with wax, shown off with a Doctor Who chess set.

## Key Topics
- Sourcing cheap materials (chopping boards from a local store)
- Cutting boards into strips and squares with precise dimensions
- Gluing squares together in strips using an alignment jig
- Sanding the assembled board flat
- Applying a wax finish
- Pairing the finished board with a previously made Doctor Who chess set

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Brown chopping board (used as dark squares)
- Beech chopping board (used as light squares)
- Gorilla Glue (wood adhesive)
- Masking tape (clamping aid during glue-up)
- Wax (final surface finish)
- Aluminium angle (used as a straightening jig)

## Techniques and Methods
- Ripping boards into strips, then cross-cutting into individual squares
- Cutting inner squares to 30×30 mm, edge pieces to 30×36 mm, and corner pieces to 36×36 mm (so rounding produces uniform 30×30 mm visual squares)
- Gluing squares into strips using masking tape as a clamp
- Using an aluminium angle section as an alignment jig to keep strips straight
- Weighting the assembly flat while glue sets
- Sanding the glued-up board smooth
- Wax finishing (preferred over varnish or oil to avoid scratching or yellowing)
