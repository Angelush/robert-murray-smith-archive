## Overview

Robert demonstrates how to make a conductive plastic filament suitable for 3D printing electronic components. Using a solvent casting method drawn from an academic paper, he dissolves a low-melting-point plastic in a solvent, mixes in graphite powder, evaporates the solvent, and rolls the resulting composite into filament. The video was prompted by a friend's project to 3D-print a conductive glove, and Robert confirms the concept works with a measured resistivity of ~9 ohms/cm.
