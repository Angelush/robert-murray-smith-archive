## Overview
Robert explores whether fluorescent 3D printing filament can act as a light guide to improve solar cell output, building on his earlier work with fluorescent acrylic panels (video 2198). He prints a right-angle prism using a fluorescent yellow filament and demonstrates over a 10% improvement in solar cell voltage output. The experiment is presented as an exciting early result, with Robert encouraging others to refine it further by making the printed plastic transparent.

## Key Topics
- Fluorescent acrylic as a waveguide/light concentrator for solar cells
- Fluorescent 3D printing filament (123-3D brand, yellow) as an optical fiber analogue
- 3D printing a right-angle prism (triangular prism) to act as a light guide over a solar cell
- Total internal reflection in the printed prism
- Over 10% improvement in solar cell output demonstrated live
- Potential to improve results further by making printed filament transparent

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Fluorescent acrylic panels** — used in prior video (2198) as waveguide/light concentrator reference
- **Fluorescent yellow 3D printing filament** — sourced from 123-3D; found to be transparent and to act as a light guide
- **Silicon solar cell** — used as the test photovoltaic device

## Techniques and Methods
- Holding fluorescent acrylic panels over a solar cell to demonstrate light concentration
- 3D printing a right-angle triangular prism at 100% infill with linear infill lines (using TinkerCAD primitive)
- Placing the printed prism over a solar cell with the short face toward the light source to exploit total internal reflection
- Live voltage measurement with a meter to compare readings with and without the prism in place
