## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith introduces Oogoo, a DIY alternative to the commercial moldable rubber product Sugru, made from silicone sealant and cornflour. He explains the chemistry behind both products and demonstrates how to make Oogoo at home for a fraction of the cost. The main takeaway is that a near-identical substitute to Sugru can be made for roughly £15–20/kg versus Sugru's ~£550/kg, with the only trade-off being a vinegar smell during curing.

## Key Topics
- History and background of Sugru, invented by Jen Ní Ghailín and launched in 2010
- Why Sugru is expensive and what it's made of (silicone sealant + talcum powder + crosslinking agent)
- Introduction to Oogoo as a community-developed DIY substitute
- The chemistry of silicone sealant curing (moisture crosslinking, acetic acid release)
- Step-by-step demonstration of making Oogoo
- Practical application: adding rubber damping strips to a folding wind turbine
- Compatibility of Oogoo/Sugru with 3D-printed plastic parts

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Silicone sealant (100% silicone / Type 1)** — the base material for both Sugru and Oogoo
- **Talcum powder** — filler used in the original Sugru formulation
- **Cornflour / cornstarch** — filler used in the Oogoo DIY substitute
- **Acetic acid** — byproduct of moisture-cure silicone (responsible for vinegar smell)
- **Food colouring** — can be added to transparent silicone to tint the final product
- **Xylene** — mentioned as a reported thinning agent (Robert avoids it)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Hand-kneading on a paper plate**: Depositing a dollop of silicone onto cornflour, dusting with more cornflour, then kneading until a non-sticky putty forms
- **Tin mixing**: Weighing out 50/50 silicone and cornflour by mass and mixing in a container — preferred for larger batches
- **Ratio adjustment**: Reducing cornflour proportion extends working/setting time at the cost of increased stickiness
- **Glove use**: Worn not for safety (material is non-toxic) but to prevent sticking
- **Moisture-cure curing**: Finished Oogoo cures in ~15 minutes exposed to air (vs. 24 hours for Sugru)
