## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith tests a motorised quad vehicle he built, driven by a 12-volt motor connected through a gearbox (adapted from a car winch). The test run is chaotic and hilarious, but the main purpose was to learn what would be needed for future electric vehicle conversions — specifically projects called "Bessie" and "the Mini."

## Key Topics
- Design challenges of the motorised quad's drive system
- Use of a 12V motor and the need for torque management via gearbox
- Why flexible drives were rejected (shear forces too high)
- Practical test run of the vehicle
- Lessons learned for upcoming EV conversion projects (Bessie and the Mini)

## Techniques and Methods
- Gearbox reduction to increase torque from a low-voltage motor
- Adaptation of a car winch as a drive/generator component
- Practical field testing of a DIY motorised vehicle
