## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates the first stage of building a homemade ukulele neck, sourcing oak from cheap DIY store flooring as an alternative to specialist hardwood suppliers. He outlines the key dimensions for a soprano ukulele, then shows how he laminated two strips of oak flooring together — reinforced with wooden dowels — to create a neck blank ready for shaping and fretting.

## Key Topics
- Sourcing affordable hardwood for instrument building (DIY store oak flooring)
- Soprano ukulele dimensions (scale length, neck width, fret count)
- Laminating thin wood strips to achieve the required neck thickness
- Using machine-finished surfaces to minimise warping
- Overview of the headless tuner design and string path

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Oak flooring strips (£5 for five strips from a local DIY store) — used as the neck material
- 6mm wooden dowels — used to reinforce the laminated neck layers

## Techniques and Methods
- Marking out neck profile on a plank and cutting with a jigsaw
- Shaping with a wood rasp to near-final dimensions
- Laminating two oak strips together for thickness, with sanding allowance built in
- Drilling through both layers and inserting wooden dowels for alignment and strength
- Clamping and leaving overnight for the glue to set
- Fretting planned as the next step before final shaping
