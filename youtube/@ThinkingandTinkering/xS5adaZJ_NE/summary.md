## Overview
In this video, Luke builds a DIY wireless Bluetooth speaker using salvaged components, including a battery pack made from old mobile phone batteries (covered in video #1542). The build uses an acrylic enclosure, a pre-built control board with Bluetooth/aux input, and a voltage regulator to step down the battery voltage to 5V USB. The final result is a compact, functional portable speaker he plans to keep in his car.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing old mobile phone batteries as a power source for a DIY project
- Building a wireless speaker from salvaged and low-cost components
- Constructing an acrylic enclosure for electronics
- Voltage regulation from a 12–14V battery pack down to 5V USB

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Old mobile phone batteries (repurposed into a battery pack from video #1542)
- Acrylic sheet (used for the speaker enclosure/case)
- Kitchen cloth (cut to size and glued over plastic to create a speaker grille aesthetic)

## Techniques and Methods
- Salvaging speakers from old amplifiers and other equipment
- Cutting acrylic sheet to form a box enclosure
- Cutting holes in acrylic panels for speaker drivers and input/output ports
- Gluing kitchen cloth over plastic panel to simulate a speaker grille
- Using a voltage regulator circuit to step 12–14V battery output down to 5V
- Wiring battery pack → voltage regulator → USB-powered control board → speakers
