## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to dramatically increase the cargo capacity of his tricycle by repurposing the basket from a salvaged supermarket shopping trolley. He first explains the legal and practical ways to source a trolley without stealing one, then shows the simple fabrication process — cutting, welding, and bolting — to adapt the basket as a rear carrier on his trike.

## Key Topics
- Legal and ethical ways to obtain a shopping trolley (UK law on "theft by finding", asking supermarkets for broken ones, buying secondhand or new)
- Disassembling the trolley to extract the wheeled dolly frame and basket
- Modifying the basket by cutting and welding to clear the rider's back
- Mounting the finished cargo basket onto a tricycle
- The load-carrying rating of shopping trolleys (~150 kg)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Supermarket shopping trolley (steel frame and wire basket) — used as the raw material; purchased secondhand for ~£20 on eBay (new ~£120)
- Tricycle / trike — the recipient vehicle for the cargo conversion

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting metal spring clips to detach the basket from the trolley frame
- Angle grinding — trimming the basket sides and frame to the desired height and shape
- Welding — removing unwanted sections and welding a new crossbar across the top of the frame at the correct height to clear the rider's back
- Bolting — securing the finished basket carrier onto the trike
