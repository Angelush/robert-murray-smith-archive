## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a proof-of-concept electrostatic speaker built from a sheet of graphene-coated glass he fabricated in a previous video. The graphene acts as a conductive base plate charged to high voltage via an induction coil and amplifier, inducing a charge on an unconnected foil diaphragm placed on top, which vibrates to produce sound. While admittedly not hi-fi, the demo establishes the working principle of graphene-based electrostatic sound reproduction.
