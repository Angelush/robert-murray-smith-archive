## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith investigates the long-standing debate over whether wet cold (as experienced in England) feels worse than dry cold (as in the American Midwest), prompted by his wife's experience moving from Ohio to England. He reviews military experiments dating back to the 1950s and concludes that objectively, wet and dry cold make no meaningful difference to core body temperature — but acknowledges that perceived warmth is real and valid, and suggests calcium chloride dehumidification as a cheap way to improve comfort.
