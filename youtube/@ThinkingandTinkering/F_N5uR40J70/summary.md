## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Steel / ferrous metals** — primary material for MMA and MAG welding
- **Aluminium** — requires MIG with inert gas; not possible with basic stick welder
- **Argon** — inert shielding gas used in MIG welding (e.g. for aluminium); noted as more expensive
- **Carbon dioxide (CO₂)** — active shielding gas used in MAG welding (e.g. for steel); cheaper than argon
- **Welding wire** — consumable for MIG/MAG; material must match the workpiece (steel wire vs aluminium wire)
- **Flux** — coating on MMA rods that provides shielding protection during stick welding

## Techniques and Methods
- **MMA / Stick welding** — striking an arc with a flux-coated rod; shielding provided by flux
- **MIG welding** — continuous wire feed with inert gas shielding; voltage-controlled arc
- **MAG welding** — same as MIG but with active gas (CO₂) for steel
- Polarity setup (clamp in negative, torch in positive) for stick welding
- Amperage adjustment based on metal thickness (stick welding)
- Voltage control for arc management in MIG/MAG welding
