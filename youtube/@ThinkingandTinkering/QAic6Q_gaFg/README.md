## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith visits Deal Castle (built 1539, Kent Coast) to argue that engineering is far more than the textbook definition — it is the application of science, mathematics, logic, politics, economics, and psychology to solve constrained real-world problems. Using Henry VIII's fortress as a case study, he demonstrates how cost, speed, available materials, and social context drove every design decision. He concludes that medieval castles are not only historical artefacts but a source of practical ideas directly applicable to modern engineering challenges.
