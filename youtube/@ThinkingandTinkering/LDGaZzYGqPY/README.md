## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how simple commercial heaters and toasters are by disassembling them, then uses that knowledge to encourage DIY heater construction. He shows that most low-voltage heating projects (car seat heaters, clothing heaters, pad heaters) require little more than a heating element and a power source. His main takeaway is that over-engineering and internet safety alarmism are unnecessary for basic resistive heating at low voltages.
