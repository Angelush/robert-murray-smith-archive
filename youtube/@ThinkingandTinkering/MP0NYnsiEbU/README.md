## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores whether the blade design from NASA's Ingenuity Mars helicopter — engineered to fly in an atmosphere just 1% the density of Earth's — could be repurposed as an efficient wind turbine blade. He downloads NASA's freely available 3D model, extracts and smooths the blade geometry, 3D prints a set, and conducts a quick outdoor comparison test against a standard wind turbine blade design. The initial test is inconclusive due to low wind speeds but demonstrates the blade does spin, validating the principle that a propeller blade and a wind turbine blade are aerodynamically interchangeable.
