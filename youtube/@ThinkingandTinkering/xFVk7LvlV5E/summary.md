## Overview
Part 3 of a series on CartaPesta (Italian papier mache) sculpture, demonstrating how to apply wet newspaper strips over a foam armature to create a figure with flowing fabric details. Robert shows the full finishing sequence — from paper application through gap-filling with polyfiller, to a final black paint and metallic wax finish — arguing that this accessible technique can produce impressive results even with artistically inexperienced students.

## Key Topics
- Applying wet newspaper strips to a foam sculpted armature
- Allowing paper to fall and fold naturally to preserve vitality in the finished piece
- Filling gaps between paper and foam with polyfiller to achieve a carved-stone appearance
- Finishing with black paint (black ground/primer) followed by metallic wax
- Using a paper-and-PVA pulp mixture for sculptural detail work (sanding and carving)
- Suitability of the technique for classroom use with teenagers

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Newspaper** — torn/cut strips soaked in PVA mix for applying to the armature
- **PVA glue** (50/50 mix with water) — adhesive binder for the paper layers
- **Wallpaper paste** — mentioned as a possible alternative to PVA (untested by Robert)
- **Flexible gap polyfiller** — used to fill recessed gaps between paper and foam so the surface reads as carved stone
- **Black paint / black ground** — base coat applied after filling
- **Treasure Silver** — a coloured metallic wax applied over highlights; available in arts and crafts shops
- **Treasure Gold** — a gold variant of the same metallic wax, used on a more elaborate example piece
- **Foam** — the underlying armature material

## Techniques and Methods
- Wet newspaper draping — soaking strips and letting them fall naturally rather than smoothing flat, to retain texture and movement
- Gap assessment and filling — evaluating every recess by asking "could a carving tool reach this?"; filling only inaccessible gaps with polyfiller
- Overnight drying between stages
- Black-paint reveal — applying a coat of paint to expose any missed gaps as dark spots for a second fill pass
- Metallic wax highlighting — wiping Treasure Silver/Gold over raised surfaces after the black base coat
- Paper-PVA pulp sculpting — working the mixture until pulpy, then squeezing into shape and allowing to dry for a woody, sandable/carvable material
- Casting technique (referenced for clothing on an advanced example, not demonstrated in this video)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Finishing tip:** Apply silver wax, then mahogany varnish over the top as an alternative/additional finish layer.
- **Source of technique:** Robert learned about CartaPesta while living in Lecce in southern Italy. He notes that practitioners there keep the technique "a huge secret" and use coloured papers so the pieces don't even need painting. He strongly recommends visiting Lecce to learn directly from local craftspeople, describing it as a transformative experience.
