## Overview
Robert demonstrates how to adapt a cheap £5 circlip tool into a miniature circlip pliers capable of removing tiny metal circlips from small electronic components. Rather than paying £66 for a specialist tool, he files down the pins of a standard circlip tool to fit the smaller clips. The broader takeaway is a general philosophy for DIY toolmaking: identify the job, find the nearest existing tool, and adapt it.

## Key Topics
- The problem of removing tiny metal circlips from small components
- Cost-saving motivation for making your own specialist tools
- Adapting a standard £5 circlip tool by filing it down
- General philosophy and repeatable process for DIY toolmaking

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Metal circlips (small, from the components being disassembled)
- Standard circlip pliers (cheap, £5 drop-forged tool)

## Techniques and Methods
- Filing down the pins of a standard circlip tool until they close tightly together
- Reducing the pin tips to fit smaller circlip openings
