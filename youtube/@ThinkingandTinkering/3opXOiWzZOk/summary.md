## Overview
Robert reviews the Elegoo Mercury 3.0 Plus Wash and Cure Station, a combined unit sent to him by Elegoo. He walks through its key design features — magnetic impeller, adjustable basket, airtight lid, UV-transparent turntable, and angled mirrors — and concludes it's a well-engineered, time-saving device for resin 3D printer users, priced at around £110.

## Key Topics
- The post-processing burden of resin printing vs. filament printing (washing and curing)
- Overview of the Elegoo Mercury 3+ Wash and Cure Station and its product history
- Design features of the wash tub: magnetic impeller, airtight seal, polypropylene basket with adjustable opening
- Wash solvents: isopropanol (IPA), ethanol, or water (for water-washable resins)
- Cure station design: UV-transparent turntable, dual UV light stacks, angled mirrors for underside coverage
- Operation and controls: wash/cure modes, timer (up to 30 min), lid safety switch
- Compatibility with any resin-based 3D printer (not Elegoo-specific)
- Cost/value assessment at £110

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Isopropanol alcohol (IPA)** — primary wash solvent for standard photo resins; ~7.5 litres required to fill the tub
- **Ethanol** — noted as an equally effective alternative to IPA
- **Water** — usable as wash liquid when using water-washable resins
- **UV resin (photo resin)** — the type of resin used in the printers being post-processed
- **Polypropylene** — material used for the wash basket; noted for chemical/corrosion resistance

## Techniques and Methods
- **Magnetic coupling drive** — impeller driven by external magnets, eliminating shaft seals and leak risk
- **Agitation washing** — motorised impeller creates turbulent flow to remove uncured resin from prints
- **Pre-washing tip** — recommended to pre-wash in old IPA before a final clean IPA wash, to reduce solvent costs
- **UV curing on turntable** — slow rotation under dual UV light strips for even exposure
- **Mirror-assisted under-curing** — angled mirrors beneath the transparent turntable direct UV light upward for full 360° coverage
- **Solar curing of water** — for water-washable resins, placing the used water in sunlight to cure and filter out the resin solids before disposal
- **DIY cure station reference** — Robert previously built his own cure station from a microwave oven with UV lights
