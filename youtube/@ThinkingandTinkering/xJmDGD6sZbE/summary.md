## Overview
Robert demonstrates a simple but effective discovery: by placing a mini gasifier stove on top of an alcohol burner running on diesel, the smoky flame is eliminated. The gasifier acts as an afterburner, transferring combustion to small drilled holes and producing a clean, smokeless, intensely hot flame. This is presented as a proof-of-concept that could be developed into a practical smokeless diesel or paraffin heater.

## Key Topics
- Fuels compatible with alcohol burners (with focus on heavier fuels like diesel and paraffin)
- Problem of smoky combustion with diesel/paraffin in simple burners
- Using a mini gasifier stove as an afterburner to eliminate smoke
- Proof-of-concept demonstration of a smokeless diesel heater
- Suggestions for further development by the community

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Diesel** — used as fuel in the alcohol burner; produces a very smoky flame without the gasifier
- **Paraffin** — mentioned alongside diesel as another heavy fuel with the same smoky combustion problem
- **Alcohol** — the burner's intended fuel, referenced as the baseline

## Techniques and Methods
- Placing a mini gasifier stove on top of an alcohol burner as an improvised afterburner
- Drilling small holes in the gasifier to create jets of flame
- Smothering to extinguish the flame (no water/blowing)
