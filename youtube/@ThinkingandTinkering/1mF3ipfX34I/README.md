## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains the operating principles of Stirling engines, starting from first principles using a balloon in hot and cold water to demonstrate thermal expansion and contraction. He walks through the key components — the displacer, rubber membrane, and regenerator — showing how they work together to convert a temperature differential into mechanical motion. The video also covers the specific design choices in his own previously-built engine, including how phase displacement is achieved without a rigid mechanical linkage.
