## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith, collaborating with Ross from "Intelligent Tinkering," explores a method for adding printed designs to 3D prints using laser jet toner transfers. The technique exploits the fact that laser toner fuses at ~130°C while PLA prints at ~220°C, causing the toner to bond preferentially to the hot plastic. The result is a simple, accessible way to add decorative imagery to 3D-printed objects without changing filament or post-painting.

## Key Topics
- The tension between functional engineering design and visual aesthetics
- Concept origin: Ross's idea to transfer an image onto a 3D print surface
- How laser jet toner works (colored resin/co-polymer particles fused at ~130°C)
- The transfer process: printing an image, placing it on the print bed, and letting the hot plastic fuse the toner
- Soaking in water to remove the paper backing
- Application example: a single-layer sun-and-moon Renaissance design used as a decorative lamp shade
- Sources for artwork (Pexels, PictureBay) for those who don't draw their own designs
- Files shared on Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA filament** — used for the 3D print substrate
- **Laser jet toner** — colored particles coated in resins and co-polymers; fuses at ~130°C
- **Prit Stick (glue stick)** — used to temporarily affix the printed paper to the print bed
- **Water** — used to soak and remove the paper backing after the transfer
- **LED + 3V battery** — used in the lamp holder demonstration project

## Techniques and Methods
- **Toner transfer onto 3D prints** — placing a laser-printed image face-down on the print bed so the extruded hot plastic (~220°C) fuses the toner (~130°C) to the print surface
- **Wet paper removal** — soaking the finished print in water to dissolve/loosen and rub away the paper, leaving only the toner image
- **Single-layer print test** — printing one layer to both test the transfer method and evaluate single-layer structural strength
- **Print bed auto-detect** — relying on the printer's bed levelling/detection routine to accommodate the paper sheet thickness without manual Z-offset adjustment
