## Overview
A short build-and-demo video in which the creator constructs a small water-powered generator using a custom rotor, neodymium magnets, and coils. The key design innovation is a revised turbine housing that keeps the rotor spinning in the same direction regardless of which way the unit is oriented, allowing a fixed reservoir of water (stored in repurposed Coca-Cola bottles) to be reused simply by flipping the entire system upside down.

## Key Topics
- Revised turbine blade and housing design to ensure consistent rotor spin direction
- Construction of a self-contained water generator with no external water supply
- Using two bottle reservoirs (top and bottom) with water that cycles by flipping the unit
- Live demonstration lighting white LEDs from the generated electricity
- First-version limitations and plans for future improvement

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Neodymium magnets (four, attached to the rotor)
- Copper wire coils (three, attached to the housing)
- Coca-Cola bottles (repurposed as water storage tanks)
- White LEDs (17, used as the electrical load in the demo)

## Techniques and Methods
- Custom turbine housing design to enforce single-direction rotor rotation
- Winding/attaching coils to a generator housing for electromagnetic induction
- Gravity-fed water flow through a turbine to generate electricity
- Flip-to-reuse reservoir system: water drains through the turbine into the lower bottle, then the whole unit is inverted to repeat the cycle
