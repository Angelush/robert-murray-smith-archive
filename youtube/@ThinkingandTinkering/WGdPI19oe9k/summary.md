## Overview
This short video by Robert Murray-Smith follows up on video 1358 (which focused on making an NACA0015 airfoil from aluminium) by explaining the broader context of Darrieus-type vertical axis wind turbines. Robert covers the various sub-types of the Darrieus design and gives a balanced assessment of their practical pros and cons for small to mid-scale power generation.

## Key Topics
- History of the Darrieus turbine (patented 1926 by Georges Darrieus, French aeronautical engineer)
- Sub-types: egg-beater/troposkein, H-type/gyromill/H-bar, cyclo-turbine (variable-pitch blades), helical-twist variant
- Real-world installations: Magdalen Islands, Quebec, Taiwan, Hobart (Australia)
- Advantages: lower cost, no large concrete tower required, can be anchored with guy ropes, high torque, spins faster than wind speed
- Disadvantages: size limitations due to blade stress, not self-starting, vulnerability to extreme wind conditions
- Self-starting workaround: briefly powering the generator as a motor to initiate rotation, then switching back to generation mode
- Suitability for small and mid-scale deployments

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Aluminium (used in the companion video 1358 to fabricate the NACA0015 airfoil)

## Techniques and Methods
- NACA0015 airfoil fabrication from aluminium (referenced from prior video)
- Using a generator as a motor to provide initial spin-up torque, then switching back to generator mode for self-starting workaround
- Guy-rope anchoring as a low-cost installation method (vs. concrete tower)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Cyclo-turbine complexity**: Confirmed that making blades independently rotate (cyclo-turbine approach) does improve starting but significantly increases build complexity and maintenance.
- **Taiwanese installations**: Noted that the Taiwanese solved a related problem by the same method discussed by a commenter ("that's exactly what the Taiwanese did mate").
- **Hobart turbines**: Mentioned he had actually visited Hobart and found it reminded him of his home town.
- **Some installations stopped working**: Acknowledged reports that certain Darrieus installations had ceased to operate, noting he initially mistook them for cell phone towers.
- **Location and cost are real-world barriers**: Reiterated that practical deployment of wind turbines is heavily constrained by location and cost, and referenced a separate video he made on that topic.
- **UK washing machine motors**: Pointed out that standard UK washing machine motors are universal motors and can be adapted into generators relatively easily — relevant to the motor-assist self-starting method.
- **Intentional focus of previous video**: Clarified that video 1358 was deliberately kept focused on the airfoil fabrication, not the turbine design.
