## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple DIY air engine from scratch, explaining each major component — piston/cylinder, slide valve, scotch yolk crankshaft, and flywheel. He walks through both the construction and the underlying mechanical principles, emphasizing that the same concepts apply to converting a conventional engine to run on compressed air. The engine is ultimately powered using a vacuum cleaner as an air source.

## Key Topics
- Components of an air engine: piston, cylinder, slide valve, crankshaft, flywheel
- How a slide valve controls air admission and exhaust
- The scotch yolk mechanism and its advantages over a conventional crank-and-connecting-rod
- Valve timing: the valve must lead the piston by 90° in the direction of rotation
- How reversing the 90° offset (clockwise vs. anticlockwise) reverses the engine
- The role of the flywheel in carrying the engine through dead-center positions
- Relevance to converting normal combustion engines to run on compressed air

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Builder board** (UK) / **Centra board** (US equivalent) — used for piston and flywheel
- **Aluminium rod** — piston rod and spacers (4 mm and 6 mm stock mentioned)
- **PTFE tape** — wrapped around the piston to create a snug fit in the cylinder
- **Acrylic tube** — used as the cylinder body
- **Acrylic rod/sheet** — used for the connecting rod
- **22 mm (¾ in) plumbing fittings (Mason rings)** — used as bearing holders
- **Inline skate bearings (8 mm)** — standard skateboard/skate bearings for the crankshaft
- **Superglue** — used to fix the con-rod length at top dead center
- **Rubber balloon** — used to seal the vacuum cleaner hose to the engine inlet

## Techniques and Methods
- Turning/shaping builder board on a lathe (implied by "turned that down")
- Wrapping PTFE tape to achieve a snug piston-to-cylinder fit
- Using Mason (plumbing) rings as bearing holders for shaft alignment
- Scotch yolk construction: drilling offset pin holes to set the "throw" (stroke)
- Setting valve timing by positioning the valve crank 90° ahead of the piston crank
- Setting connecting-rod length by sliding two pieces until top-dead-center alignment, then fixing with superglue
- Using a vacuum cleaner (suction) as a substitute air source for running the engine
