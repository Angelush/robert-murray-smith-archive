## Overview
Luke demonstrates a heat-forming technique to shape flat HDPE sheet plastic into curved halves matching an oil drum's profile. Using a heat gun and the original oil drum as a mold, he heats the plastic until pliable, uses bungee cords and screws to hold it against the barrel's shape, and allows it to cool to permanently retain the curved form. The result is a plastic oil drum half intended for use in a future project.

## Key Topics
- Heat forming / heat bending of plastic sheet
- Using an existing object as a mold/former
- Retaining curved shapes in thermoplastic after cooling
- Problem-solving springback at the edges

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) flat sheet — the thermoplastic being formed
- Steel oil drum — used as the mold/former to define the curved shape

## Techniques and Methods
- Heat gun application to soften thermoplastic sheet
- Bungee cord clamping to hold plastic against the mold during heating
- Oversizing the sheet to allow folding over the mold edges to prevent springback
- Drilling holes and driving screws to mechanically fix the softened plastic to the mold edges
- Passive cooling to set the final shape
