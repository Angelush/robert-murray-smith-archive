## Overview
This video provides a comprehensive theoretical introduction to gasification — the process of converting biomass, plastics, or coal into combustible gas under a reduced-oxygen atmosphere. Robert traces the history of gasification from 19th-century town gas lighting through WWII vehicle fuel applications, explains the four core stages of the process, and frames the topic as groundwork for a future project: producing syngas for catalytic methanol synthesis.

## Key Topics
- History of gasification: town gas (1807), WWI/WWII vehicle use (~9 million gasifier-powered cars), Soviet gasified motorcycle
- Gasification vs. incineration debate — "six and two threes" for industrial use, but attractive at the home/individual level
- The four sequential stages of gasification: dehydration, pyrolysis, combustion, and gasification proper
- The water-gas shift reaction as a fifth balancing element
- Updraft vs. downdraft gasifier designs for home builders; industrial variants (fluidized bed, entrained flow)
- Practical considerations: tar fouling, cyclone particle separation, heat exchangers, material-specific tweaks (wet feedstocks, grass clippings)
- Separating pyrolysis and gasification stages for clarity and experimentation
- Syngas as the target output, as a precursor to catalytic methanol production

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Biomass** — primary feedstock for gasification
- **Plastics / coal / tires** — alternative feedstocks mentioned
- **Methanol** — produced by plants in living tissue; also the downstream synthesis target
- **Ethanol** — volatile driven off during pyrolysis
- **Sugars** — component of raw biomass feedstock
- **Tars / creosote / creosols** — heavy volatiles released during pyrolysis, a known fouling problem
- **Methane** — combustible gas produced during pyrolysis stage
- **Carbon monoxide** — product of the gasification (char) stage; key component of syngas
- **Hydrogen** — co-produced with CO in the gasification stage; key component of syngas
- **Carbon dioxide** — reacts with char in stage 4; involved in water-gas shift equilibrium
- **Steam (water)** — reacts with char; part of the water-gas shift reaction
- **Charcoal / char** — solid residue after pyrolysis, rich in carbon; gasified in stage 4

## Techniques and Methods
- **Gasification** — heating feedstock under restricted oxygen to produce combustible syngas
- **Dehydration** — first stage: driving off moisture from wet biomass before processing
- **Pyrolysis** — second stage: thermal decomposition (~300–400 °C, reduced oxygen) to release volatiles
- **Combustion stage** — third stage: partial oxidation of char/volatiles to generate process heat
- **Char gasification** — fourth stage: char reacts with steam and CO₂ to yield CO and H₂
- **Water-gas shift reaction** — equilibrium adjustment between CO/H₂ and CO₂/H₂O
- **Cyclone separation** — removing particulate matter and heavy tars from the raw gas stream
- **Heat exchanger preheating** — using hot outgoing gas to preheat incoming air/feedstock
- **Separated-stage experimentation** — running pyrolysis and gasification as independent steps to study each process individually
- **Catalytic methanol synthesis** — stated next step: passing cleaned syngas over a catalyst to produce methanol
