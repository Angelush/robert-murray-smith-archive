## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents a process shared by his collaborator Simon for synthesizing a graphene oxide / manganese dioxide composite cathode material suitable for batteries and supercapacitors. The video walks through the wet chemistry synthesis step by step, producing a deep brown-black powder that is then formulated into a conductive ink. The takeaway is a practical, reproducible recipe for a high-performance cathode material paired with a zinc anode to form a complete cell.

## Key Topics
- Collaborative, open-sharing ethos between Robert and his network of independent researchers
- Synthesis of a graphene oxide / manganese dioxide composite via potassium permanganate reduction
- Role of zinc sulfate as a dopant in the reaction
- Formulation of the dried product into an electrode ink using graphite
- Assembly of a complete zinc-anode / MnO₂-cathode cell with a glass fiber separator

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene oxide** — 160 ml at 1 mg/ml concentration; forms the carbon matrix of the composite
- **Potassium permanganate (KMnO₄)** — 1.5 g in 40 ml water; oxidising agent and source of manganese for MnO₂
- **Manganese sulfate (MnSO₄)** — 4 g in the secondary solution; additional manganese source
- **Zinc sulfate (ZnSO₄)** — 0.3 g in the secondary solution; minor dopant
- **Graphite powder** — added at 3% by weight to improve conductivity of the dried cathode material
- **Powdered zinc (400 mesh)** — used as the anode material
- **PVA (polyvinyl alcohol), 5% solution** — binder for gluing electrode material to current collector
- **Graphite foil** — current collector substrate
- **Fine glass fiber** — used as the cell separator

## Techniques and Methods
- **Magnetic stir plate mixing** with a conical flask (to contain splashing from the vigorous permanganate reaction)
- **Dropwise addition** of secondary salt solution into the stirring graphene oxide / permanganate mixture
- **Gravity sedimentation** — leaving the reaction mixture to settle for ~1 hour to separate product from supernatant
- **Vacuum filtration** — to isolate and wash the brown-black solid product
- **Drying** of the filtered solid
- **Ink formulation** — blending dried composite with 3 wt% graphite to produce a conductive electrode ink
- **Electrode fabrication** — coating/gluing electrode ink onto graphite foil current collector with 5% PVA binder
