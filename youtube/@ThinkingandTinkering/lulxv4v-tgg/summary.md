## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a contra-rotating (counter-rotating) gear mechanism using simple peg gears cut from cutting boards. The video explains that turning a central gear causes two flanking gears to spin simultaneously in opposite directions, a principle with real-world applications in helicopters and drivetrains. The build is designed to be quick, robust, and accessible using basic workshop materials.

## Key Topics
- The concept of counter rotation: three gears arranged so that one drive gear causes two output gears to spin in opposite directions
- Applications of counter rotation (helicopters, drivetrains)
- Peg gears as a simple, robust, and quick-to-build gearing system
- Gear tooth spacing and engagement rules
- Constructing a cradle/frame to hold the gears in correct relation
- Use of bearings for smooth axle movement

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Cutting boards** — used as stock material for the gear discs
- **9mm wooden dowel** — cut and sanded to form gear pegs (teeth)
- **Glue** — used to fix pegs into drilled holes
- **Bearings** — pressed into 22mm stepped holes to support the 12mm axles

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting circles from cutting board stock to form gear blanks
- Marking out and centre-locating peg positions using paper templates
- Drilling peg holes measured in from the edge of each disc
- Cutting and sanding dowel segments to form uniform pegs
- Gluing pegs into holes around each gear
- Drilling stepped holes (22mm outer, 12mm through) in cradle uprights to seat bearings
- Assembling six upright cradle pieces into a base-and-top frame structure
