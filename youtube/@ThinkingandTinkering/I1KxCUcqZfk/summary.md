## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a prototype magnetic levitation (MagLev) vertical-axis wind turbine (VAWT) generator built from a children's ankle-spin toy and salvaged speaker magnets. By gluing the twisted toy rotor onto a magnetically levitated magnet assembly, he shows that a rotating north-north (like-pole) field can generate electricity — confirmed by lighting an LED at ~2.2V. The key insight is that maglev normally used for lift can simultaneously serve as the rotating magnetic field for generation, with minimal bearing friction.

## Key Topics
- Vertical-axis wind turbines (VAWTs) and their rotor geometry
- Magnetic levitation using like-pole (north-north) ring magnets
- Using a maglev arrangement as a low-friction generator
- Proof-of-concept: generating electricity from a rotating north-only field
- Low-cost, tabletop prototyping philosophy (under £3)
- Scaling potential: replacing toy parts with neodymium magnet discs

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Ring magnets salvaged from a speaker (used for magnetic levitation)
- A children's ankle-spin toy (twisted hemisphere rotor, used as the turbine blade structure)
- Super glue (to bond the toy rotor to the magnet)
- A small bearing (center-pole constraint to prevent lateral drift — carries no load)
- A pickup coil (for electricity generation)
- An LED (used as a voltage indicator)
- Neodymium magnets (mentioned as the upgrade path for a larger disc version)

## Techniques and Methods
- Magnetic levitation via opposing like poles (north-north repulsion) to carry the full weight of the rotor
- Using a center bearing purely for lateral restraint, not load-bearing
- Attaching a shaped rotor (repurposed toy) to a levitated magnet with super glue
- Using a hair dryer as a wind source to spin the prototype
- Inductive generation from a rotating single-polarity (north) field with a stationary coil
- LED testing as a quick, no-meter voltage verification method
