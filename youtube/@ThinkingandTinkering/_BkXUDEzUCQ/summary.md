## Overview
Robert announces a competition — inspired by a suggestion from Canadian friend Alain Young — to win a Printed Bot 3D printer (worth ~£600) plus a spool of ABS filament. Entrants must build a working device (not just a concept or drawing) that generates enough electricity to light at least one LED. The competition runs from 15 September to 31 October 2021.

## Key Topics
- Competition rules and prize details (3D printer + ABS filament spool)
- Entry requirements: working physical device that genuinely powers an LED
- Allowed energy sources and materials (wind, pedal, hand, water, wave, walking, chemical, etc.)
- Submission format: video showing the device working, no hidden batteries or external power supplies
- Judging criteria: public likes/dislikes for shortlisted entries, with Robert judging on creativity, entertainment, practicality, and inventiveness
- Submission deadline: 31 October 2021
- Open to entrants worldwide

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- ABS filament (prize material for 3D printing)
- Copper(I) oxide and steel (suggested by Robert in comments as a thermoelectric/flame-based generator approach, referencing Nyle Steiner's work)
- Potatoes (jokingly suggested as a valid chemical cell if linked in series)

## Techniques and Methods
- Hand-built electricity generators (wind turbine, pedal power, hand-cranked, water/wave-powered, walking-powered)
- Chemical electricity generation (e.g., potato battery, flame-based thermoelectric using copper oxide and steel)
- Video documentation of working device (phone, camera, stills montage, voiceover all acceptable)
- Homemade battery charging from a self-built generator (allowed; commercial chargers not allowed)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Submission email:** `robert@fwgltd.co.uk` (also mentions `luke@fwgltd.co.uk`)
- **Open to all:** Confirmed the competition is open worldwide with no restrictions on nationality or number of entries per person.
- **Battery/external power rule clarified:** Bought panels, bought batteries, or batteries charged from a commercial charger are not allowed. A homemade battery charged by a homemade generator (e.g., a water wheel) is acceptable.
- **Minimum output:** Single LED is the floor; there is no maximum — more power is welcome.
- **Chemical entries allowed:** A chemical cell (e.g., potato battery) is valid, but Robert gently advises thinking about what will capture the public's imagination for the voting stage.
- **Inventiveness over craft:** Robert explicitly states he values unusual and inventive approaches over beautifully crafted but conventional machines.
- **Practical tips offered:** Suggested sticking a motor on a child's windmill, or using copper(I) oxide and steel in a flame (16 in series) as per Nyle Steiner to light an LED.
- **Upload timing:** Shortlisted videos will only be uploaded after 31 October so all entries get equal exposure time in the public vote.
- **Credit:** Robert repeatedly defers credit for the competition idea to Alain Young.
