## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to create conductive plastic coatings by dissolving polycaprolactone (PCL) in dichloromethane (DCM) and mixing in carbon/graphite powder, as an alternative to carbon-black-filled HDPE which had become hard to source. The coating is applied to metal electrodes (such as stainless steel mesh) via dip-coating, building up layers until the desired thickness is achieved. The key principle is the percolation threshold — finding the minimum amount of conductive additive needed to form a continuous conductive network within the dried plastic matrix.
