## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a prototype Venturi-based wind turbine system inspired by the commercial Invelox design, where a shaped horn captures wind and directs it through a pipe to a turbine mounted at ground level rather than atop a tall tower. The experiment successfully generates enough power to light an LED, demonstrating the concept's viability. The key appeal is that all machinery stays at ground level, dramatically reducing tower costs and simplifying maintenance.
