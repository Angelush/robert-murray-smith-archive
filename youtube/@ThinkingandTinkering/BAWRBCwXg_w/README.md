## Overview

Robert demonstrates the successful operation of a diamagnetic graphite solar engine, the follow-up to a previous experiment. A chip of unusually diamagnetic graphite — sourced from a mine in Sri Lanka — is levitated on a custom neodymium magnet array, and focused sunlight from a Fresnel lens causes it to spin. The engine works because heat from the light spot alters the graphite's diamagnetic properties, disturbing equilibrium; the chip's high thermal conductivity then dissipates the heat as it moves away, creating a repeating cycle that sustains rotation.
