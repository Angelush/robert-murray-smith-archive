## Overview
Robert demonstrates the successful operation of a diamagnetic graphite solar engine, the follow-up to a previous experiment. A chip of unusually diamagnetic graphite — sourced from a mine in Sri Lanka — is levitated on a custom neodymium magnet array, and focused sunlight from a Fresnel lens causes it to spin. The engine works because heat from the light spot alters the graphite's diamagnetic properties, disturbing equilibrium; the chip's high thermal conductivity then dissipates the heat as it moves away, creating a repeating cycle that sustains rotation.

## Key Topics
- Diamagnetic levitation of graphite over a permanent magnet array
- Using focused sunlight as the sole driving energy source
- The thermodynamic mechanism behind the rotation (heat-induced change in diamagnetic properties)
- Magnet configuration required to create a stable levitation "hill"
- Properties of the Sri Lankan graphite sample (unusual diamagnetic strength, metal-like inclusions)
- Future directions: making a foil from the graphite, scaling up with larger magnets

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Diamagnetic graphite** — an unusually strongly diamagnetic chip sourced from a mine in Sri Lanka; painted black on top to improve light absorption
- **Neodymium (NdFeB) magnets, grade N35** — ring magnets (24 mm diameter, 8 mm hole, 2 mm thick) and a small disc magnet (8 mm × 2 mm); stacked in a specific north/south pole arrangement to form a levitation hill
- **Black paint** — applied to the graphite surface to increase heat absorption from sunlight

## Techniques and Methods
- **Diamagnetic levitation** — graphite chip floated passively above the magnet stack by repulsion of the induced magnetic field
- **Magnet array engineering** — four ring magnets stacked on the outside, three on the centre, with opposing pole orientations (north up on ring, south up on centre disc) to shape the magnetic potential well/hill
- **Fresnel lens focusing** — small square Fresnel lens used to concentrate sunlight onto the levitated chip
- **Surface blackening** — top face of graphite painted black to maximise solar absorption and speed up heating

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Mechanism explanation:** Heat from the sunlight alters the diamagnetic properties of the graphite, disturbing the levitation equilibrium. Because graphite is highly thermally conductive, as soon as the chip moves out of the heat spot the heat dissipates rapidly across the surface — this cycle repeats and sustains the spin.
- **Scaling:** Larger magnets should work fine for a bigger version, though he notes time constraints prevent him from pursuing it further in the near term.
- **Alternative drive methods:** Agrees a laser would work well as a substitute for sunlight; notes a rounder disc would perform better than the irregular chip used.
- **Graphite foil:** Wants to make a foil from the diamagnetic graphite to test whether the properties are preserved in that form.
- **Source of graphite:** Describes the material as unusual — more like it has metal inclusions; notes the supplier has listed it on eBay at a reasonable price.
- **Graphene/charcoal aside:** Clarifies that extracting graphene from charcoal is very difficult and expensive at home, but charcoal *can* be used to make supercapacitors with accessible home chemistry.
- **Hybrid motor concept:** Entertains the idea of combining this with a Mendocino motor design, jokingly suggesting calling it the "Earle Smith motor."
- **Nanoparticles:** Corrects a comment — particles in a related experiment are 4 nanometres (not micrometres); also mentions copper nanoparticles (he has 4 µm copper particles on hand) as an alternative approach being explored by Rice University.
