## Overview

In this short reflective video, Robert Murray-Smith discusses the importance of simplicity and clarity of objectives in experimental methodology. Prompted by viewer suggestions to add complexity to his concrete and graphene experiments, he argues that stripping an experiment to its bare essentials is the most reliable path to a working, manufacturable result. Complexity should only be introduced after the simple version has been proven to work.
