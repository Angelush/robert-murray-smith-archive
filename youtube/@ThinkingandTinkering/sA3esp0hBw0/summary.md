## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith teardowns an HHO (Brown's gas) torch that a friend gave him three years prior, examining its internal components to understand how it works. He measures the operating voltage and current, reassembles it, and tests the flame, finding it surprisingly limited in power but useful for specific tasks like bending HDPE and — as he later notes in comments — soldering.

## Key Topics
- Identification of an unknown device as an HHO/Brown's gas electrolyzer torch
- Internal teardown: electrolyzer tank, bubbler, pressure valve, and electronics board
- Operating parameters: voltage, current, and electrolyte composition
- Practical flame testing on various materials
- Use of the bubbler as a flame arrestor
- Potential modifications and future experiments

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Potassium hydroxide (KOH)** — used as electrolyte at ~15% by weight in water; alkaline electrolyzer solution
- **Sodium hydroxide (NaOH)** — mentioned as an alternative electrolyte
- **Water** — primary bubbler/electrolyte fluid
- **Alcohol** — mentioned as a bubbler additive to alter flame temperature
- **Acetone** — mentioned as a bubbler additive to alter flame temperature
- **Stainless steel** — inferred material for the electrolyzer electrodes
- **Aluminium** — tested as a cutting target (thin sheet, unsuccessful)
- **Steel** — tested as a cutting target (unsuccessful at this power level)
- **Polycarbonate** — briefly tested, melted
- **HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene)** — successfully bent using the flame as a heat line
- **Urea** — mentioned in comments as a potential electrolyte to try in a dry-cell replacement

## Techniques and Methods
- Mechanical teardown (removing screws, panels, and handles to expose internals)
- Earth continuity checking for electrical safety
- DC voltage measurement across electrolyzer terminals with a voltmeter
- Visual inspection of electronics board and IC identification
- Flame testing on multiple materials
- Using the bubbler as a passive flame arrestor
- Heat-line bending of HDPE sheet

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Soldering use:** Robert states he now uses the torch for soldering, finding it better than an electric torch for that purpose.
- **Power context:** He clarifies it is a 144 W device designed for jewellers — adequate for that niche but not a serious shop torch.
- **Flame temperature and bubbler mix:** He speculates that adding volatile liquids (alcohol, acetone) to the bubbler produces a hotter flame because you are effectively burning a mixed gas, though he acknowledges uncertainty. He notes he watched someone else report a different flame temperature with this approach.
- **Steel corrosion:** He confirms the alkaline electrolyte does corrode/rot the stainless steel electrodes over time.
- **Planned modifications:** He mentions wanting to replace the internal wet-cell generator with a dry cell, and to try urea as an electrolyte additive.
- **Backflash/flashback arrester:** He confirms the unit does not have a dedicated flashback arrestor; the bubbler is the only safety measure, consistent with similar models he researched.
- **Gas output:** He notes this model is rated at 75 L/h, which is at the low end of the range; similar eBay units claim 95 L/h.
- **General outlook:** Despite its current limitations, he believes the device could be useful with experimentation and that it "sparked off a couple of ideas."
