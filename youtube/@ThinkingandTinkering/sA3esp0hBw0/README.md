## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith teardowns an HHO (Brown's gas) torch that a friend gave him three years prior, examining its internal components to understand how it works. He measures the operating voltage and current, reassembles it, and tests the flame, finding it surprisingly limited in power but useful for specific tasks like bending HDPE and — as he later notes in comments — soldering.
