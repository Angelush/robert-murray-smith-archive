## Overview

Robert demonstrates how to build an extremely simple aluminium-air battery using a conductive-ink-soaked sponge as the carbon electrode, aluminium foil, kitchen towel as a separator, and salt water as the electrolyte — producing a measured voltage immediately upon wetting. The video is part of his "101 Things to Do with Conductive Ink" Indiegogo campaign, and uses the demo as a springboard to explain the underlying principles of all metal-air batteries.
