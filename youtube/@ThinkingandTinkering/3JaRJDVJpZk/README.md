## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith presents iron powder as a compelling zero-carbon fuel: it burns cleanly (producing only iron oxide/rust), can be fully recycled with no material loss, and is abundant and already mass-produced. He covers recent progress from Dutch researchers at Eindhoven Technical University, who have scaled from a 20 kW prototype furnace to a 1 MW demonstration plant, with interest now from NASA, ESA, and Canadian partners for use in lunar habitation.
