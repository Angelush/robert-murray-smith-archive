## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith gives a concise, skeptical overview of the LK-99 superconductor hype that swept the internet in mid-2023. He explains the long-standing promise and limitations of superconductors, contextualizes the premature viral spread of the LK-99 paper, and argues that the excitement says more about society's hunger for a "quick fix" than it does about the science. His conclusion is that the episode will likely do more reputational damage than good to the researchers involved.
