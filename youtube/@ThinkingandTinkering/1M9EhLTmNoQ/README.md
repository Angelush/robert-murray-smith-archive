## Overview

Robert provides an update on his rubberised conductive graphene ink, which he first developed in a previous video as a casein-and-sulfur-based rubber replacement. By adding 5% graphene and processing through a three-roll mill, he reduced the ink's resistance from ~2 kilohms to 55 ohms (normalised to 6.6 ohms/mil/square), bringing it in line with — or better than — commercially available conductive paints. The ink is flexible, waterproof, sticks strongly to plastic, and is made entirely from water, milk, carbon, and sulfur with no volatile organic compounds.
