## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith visits Oxford Street in London — where up to half a million people walk daily — to explore the concept of footfall energy harvesting. He reviews existing commercial implementations (notably Pavegen's electromechanical pavement tiles) and then presents his own 3D-printed electromechanical energy harvester design, which uses an oscillating spring-and-gear mechanism to generate voltage from impacts. The video argues that while footfall harvesting is still early-stage, dismissing it for low output mirrors short-sighted criticism of early electric motors.
