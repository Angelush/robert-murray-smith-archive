## Overview
This video (MK2 follow-up to video 1150) demonstrates a working gravity battery/generator built around a flywheel. Robert addresses the core failure of his previous attempt — intermittent power delivery — by coupling a falling weight to a flywheel that also drives a small generator, achieving smoother and sustained energy output. The experiment successfully produces measurable voltage (~0.8–1 V) and current (~0.5–0.7 A), validating the flywheel-assisted gravity generator concept.

## Key Topics
- Gravity batteries and gravity generators as energy storage/generation concepts
- Critique and lessons learned from the MK1 gravity generator (video 1150)
- Role of a flywheel in smoothing power output and extending generation after the weight lands
- Practical assembly: flywheel + generator + rope + hanging weight over a stairwell
- Scalability principle: adding sequential weights on hooks to maintain continuous generation
- Connection to real-world large-scale gravity energy storage facilities

## Techniques and Methods
- Hanging a weighted rope over a stairwell to drive a flywheel via friction/mechanical coupling
- Bolting a DC generator directly onto the flywheel axle
- Measuring output with a multimeter (voltage and current modes)
- Using rope-and-hook system to add successive weights and sustain flywheel momentum
