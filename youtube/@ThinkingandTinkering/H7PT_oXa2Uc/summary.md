## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith sets up his SWAG plates (conductive-ink-on-plastic capacitor assemblies) wired identically to Tesla's radiant energy collection circuit — one plate elevated as an aerial, the other side grounded to a copper water pipe via a capacitor. He measures a surprising ~8.756 V AC across the system and observes that touching the plate (even through rubber gloves) or raising it higher dramatically increases the reading, while placing it on the floor drops it. He shares the results openly, admitting he doesn't fully understand what's happening and inviting the community to theorise.

## Key Topics
- Tesla's radiant energy collection circuit and its replication
- SWAG plates repurposed as a radiant energy aerial collector
- Role of the earth/ground connection via copper water pipe
- Capacitive behaviour of the SWAG plate structure (two conductors separated by a dielectric)
- Effect of human body capacitance on readings
- Effect of plate height on measured voltage
- AC vs. DC measurement behaviour

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **SWAG plates** — two copper strips with conductive ink painted onto OHP (overhead projector) plastic sheet, acting as a capacitor
- **Conductive ink** — painted across and over the copper strips
- **Copper water pipe** — used as the earth/ground connection
- **Wire** — connecting the circuit elements
- **Rubber insulating gloves** — worn during touching experiments

## Techniques and Methods
- Wiring the SWAG plate array to mirror Tesla's radiant energy schematic (aerial plate → one side of capacitor → earth on the other side)
- AC voltage measurement with a voltmeter across the capacitor terminals
- Qualitative body-capacitance test: touching the plate with gloved vs. bare hand to observe reading changes
- Height variation test: raising and lowering the plate to observe voltage response

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Height is the key variable**: Robert repeatedly emphasises that raising the plate higher gives larger readings; he mentions a ladder, rooftop, and even painting a helium balloon with conductive ink to carry a plate aloft, potentially mapping voltage vs. altitude.
- **Earth/ground as electron source**: He states explicitly that "the pipe is a ground and the ground is the electron source," linking it to Tesla's own thinking on alpha particles and direct energy conversion.
- **Lab is a Faraday cage**: He notes the experiment was conducted at his lab, which is "basically a huge Faraday cage," a significant caveat for interpreting the results.
- **Surface area matters**: He agrees with commenters that surface area of the collector plate is an important factor, and that a separator would be needed for stacked configurations.
- **Plates are all soldered together**: He clarifies that in the full SWAG generator the plates are linked (soldered top and bottom), not individual capacitors.
- **Follow-up done in sunlight**: He mentions having done a follow-up experiment outdoors in the sun using a single plate.
- **Metal plate test planned**: He agrees to test a plain metal plate for comparison.
- **Putting it on the back burner**: He intends to return to the SWAG generator as the primary project and revisit this radiant energy work later, encouraging others to pick it up in the meantime.
