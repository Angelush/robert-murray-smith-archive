## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith provides a complete step-by-step walkthrough for building a simple symmetric supercapacitor using activated carbon felt, stainless steel mesh current collectors, a separator, and saltwater electrolyte — all sealed inside a vacuum-packed plastic bag. The resulting device achieves around 7 watt-hours per kilogram (his homemade version) compared to roughly 3 watt-hours per kilogram for commercial supercapacitors, demonstrating that DIY energy storage can be surprisingly competitive. The video emphasises accessibility: the materials are inexpensive, the construction requires no specialist skills, and the finished device is capable of powering a small motor.
