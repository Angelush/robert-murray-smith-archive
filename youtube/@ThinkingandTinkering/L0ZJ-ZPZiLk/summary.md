## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates his DIY copper plating kit, available from his Working Ink shop, designed to let fans create a Harry Potter-style copper-plated magic wand. The kit includes nearly everything needed — from wand wood and string to conductive ink and copper sulfate — making it accessible for beginners and science enthusiasts alike. The full plating process takes around 30 hours to achieve a complete copper coating.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the copper wand-making kit sold via the Working Ink shop
- Step-by-step wand construction: wrapping string, applying glue, painting conductive ink
- Setting up the copper electroplating circuit (copper strip as anode, wand as cathode)
- Preparing the copper sulfate electrolyte solution
- Plating duration, troubleshooting, and finishing (polishing and varnishing)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Wand wood** — the base material for the wand
- **String** — wound around the wand handle for texture
- **Glue (two pots)** — used to fix and coat the string
- **Conductive ink** — painted onto the handle to allow copper deposition
- **Copper wire** — wound around the base of the handle to make electrical contact with the ink
- **Copper strip** — used as the anode in the electroplating setup
- **Copper sulfate** — the electrolyte salt dissolved in water for the plating bath
- **Saccharin tablets (x2)** — added to the copper sulfate solution (likely as a grain refiner for smoother plating)
- **Deionised water** — recommended but noted as not strictly necessary
- **Abrasive paper** — included in the kit for polishing the finished wand
- **Varnish** — applied as a final protective coat

## Techniques and Methods
- **String winding and gluing** — wrapping and bonding string to the wand handle, then sealing with a full glue coat left overnight
- **Conductive ink painting** — applying two coats of conductive ink, each dried with a hairdryer (~15 min) and buffed with a tissue
- **Electroplating setup** — connecting copper strip (positive/anode) and wand (negative/cathode) via wires to a 1.5V battery in a copper sulfate solution
- **Solution preparation** — dissolving copper sulfate and saccharin in 250 ml water, microwaved 30–60 seconds
- **Occasional agitation** — gently moving the wand in solution to encourage even ion distribution
- **Polishing** — buffing the dried, plated wand with abrasive paper for a shiny finish
- **Varnishing** — sealing the finished wand to protect the copper surface
