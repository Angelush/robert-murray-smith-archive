## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith covers the promise of deep geothermal energy and the startup Quaise Energy, which is using gyrotron technology originally developed for nuclear fusion at MIT to drill 20 km into the Earth's crust. The video explains the four types of geothermal energy, the historical challenges of deep drilling, and why Quaise's approach — melting rock rather than mechanically cutting through it — could be a breakthrough. The key takeaway is that Quaise's $21 million funding round, backed by Mitsubishi, marks a significant step toward making deep geothermal a viable part of the 21st-century energy mix.
