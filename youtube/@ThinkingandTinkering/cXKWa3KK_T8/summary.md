## Overview
Robert presents a hastily assembled water wheel generator as a competition entry, acknowledging it is incomplete and rough. The concept involves attaching a washing machine motor assembly to a fabricated angle-iron water wheel and placing it in a stream to generate electricity. He demonstrates it can power an LED and suggests it could realistically provide emergency LED lighting.

## Key Topics
- DIY water wheel construction from salvaged angle iron
- Repurposing a washing machine motor/assembly as a generator
- Low-power electricity generation from flowing water
- Practical application for emergency LED lighting

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Angle iron (salvaged scrap pieces, cut at 60-degree inner angles)
- Twine (proposed as a drive belt/connection)
- LED (used to demonstrate power output)
- Washing machine motor/assembly (proposed but not yet fitted)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting inner angles on angle iron segments to allow them to butt together and form a wheel frame
- Using a washing machine's universal motor as a generator (belt-free, direct coupling concept)
- Using twine as a low-tension drive linkage between wheel and generator
