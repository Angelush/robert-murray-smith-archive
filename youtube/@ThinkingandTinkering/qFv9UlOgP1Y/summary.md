## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents a FEMM 4.2 magnetic simulation of the Flynn device, demonstrating that pulsing a single side coil instead of the two center coils produces the same flux effect at a fraction of the power — approximately 2 watts versus 16 watts. He concludes the video by announcing his intention to build a physical prototype to test the simulation findings.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the FEMM 4.2 magnetic modeling software (by David Meeker, GNU licensed for private use)
- Simulation of the Flynn device using an H-frame structure
- Comparison of different coil pulsing configurations and their effect on magnetic flux
- Significant power reduction discovery: same Flynn effect achievable at ~87% less power
- Plans to build a physical prototype

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- M Steel M45 (H-frame material)
- Neodymium N50 magnets (two, placed between the coils)

## Techniques and Methods
- FEMM 4.2 finite element magnetic modeling simulation
- Pulsing center coils at 1A / 8V per coil (16W total) — original configuration
- Pulsing a single side coil at 1A / 8V (8W) — first alternative
- Pulsing a single side coil at 0.5A / 4V (2W) — optimized alternative
- Reversing current direction (clockwise vs. counter-clockwise) to observe flux shifting behavior
- Flux density visualization using a color scale (blue/green = low → purple = high)
