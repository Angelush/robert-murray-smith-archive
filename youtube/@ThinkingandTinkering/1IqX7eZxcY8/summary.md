## Overview
This short tips-and-tricks video, presented by Luke on the Robert Murray-Smith channel, demonstrates how to use WD-40 to remove super glue from timing belts and other surfaces. The method — spraying WD-40, allowing it to soak, then scraping — proves far more effective than acetone alone. The takeaway is that WD-40's degreasing properties loosen cyanoacrylate adhesive quickly and cleanly.

## Key Topics
- Using WD-40 as a super glue remover
- Practical context: removing misaligned super-glued timing belts used for DIY gears
- Comparison with acetone as an alternative solvent
- Additional applications: removing sticker residue from fridges, walls, and surfaces

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **WD-40** — used as the primary degreaser/solvent to loosen cured super glue
- **Super glue / instant bond adhesive** — the adhesive being removed
- **Acetone** — mentioned as a previously attempted alternative, found slower and less effective
- **Timing belt** — the substrate from which glue is being removed

## Techniques and Methods
- Spray WD-40 generously over the glue-covered surface
- Allow to soak for approximately 5 minutes
- Scrape off loosened glue with a flat cutting/craft blade (scraping away from the body for safety)
- Alternative: use a steel scouring pad instead of a blade
- Apply a second coat of WD-40 if residue remains, soak again briefly, then repeat scraping
