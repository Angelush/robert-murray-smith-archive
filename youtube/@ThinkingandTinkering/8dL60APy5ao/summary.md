## Overview
This video is an announcement and preview for a live workshop event focused on making graphene safely at home using the electrochemical exfoliation method. Rob's wife Falconeri introduces the event while Rob explains the technique, the kit contents, and the equipment participants will need. The workshop was scheduled for Saturday 9th February 2019, 4–7pm UK time, with kits available for purchase on their webshop.

## Key Topics
- Announcement of a live graphene-making workshop event
- Why most graphene synthesis methods are dangerous and why this one is safe
- Electrochemical exfoliation technique overview
- The "concertina" voltage-cycling method for intercalating ions into graphite
- Kit contents and what participants need to source themselves
- Two options for mechanical finishing: sonication bath vs. kitchen blender
- Using vitamin B2 to keep graphene in suspension

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphoil (graphite foil)** — used as the electrode/graphite source for exfoliation
- **Sodium chloride (NaCl, table salt)** — electrolyte solution; drives electrochemical intercalation
- **Vitamin B2 (riboflavin)** — added to keep exfoliated graphene flakes in suspension (clarified in comments: not for neutralising chlorine)
- **Lead-acid batteries (6V x2 in series)** — alternative 12V power source

## Techniques and Methods
- **Electrochemical exfoliation** — passing current through graphite electrodes in a salt solution to intercalate ions
- **Voltage cycling ("concertina" method)** — alternating +12V polarity using a timer relay circuit to push and pull ions in/out of graphite, separating graphene flakes
- **Mechanical exfoliation via kitchen blender** — quick (~10 minutes) finishing step to break down flakes further
- **Sonication bath** — alternative finishing method (slower, ~6 hours)
- **Timer relay circuit assembly** — no-solder circuit using two timer relays and wiring to automate voltage reversal

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Vitamin B2 clarification:** B2 does not neutralise chlorine — its role is as a stabiliser to keep graphene flakes in suspension.
- **Voltage switching intervals:** The switching should be done at 2–10 minute intervals, not too fast, to allow proper intercalation. Switching too fast doesn't give time for the ions to intercalate.
- **Blender vs. sonicator:** The blender is used because it is much quicker; a 6-hour seminar using only sonication would be too long.
- **Graphite burning (for other methods):** Should be done in an argon atmosphere, which produces no ash.
- **Venue:** The workshop may be held in the factory, as the ceiling height is more suitable.
