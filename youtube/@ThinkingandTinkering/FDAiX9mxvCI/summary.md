## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make an organic, biodegradable gelatin foam, inspired by prosthetics maker Michael Davey who uses gelatin instead of latex for theater prosthetics. The foam is plastic, flexible, castable, and reheatable, making it a surprisingly versatile material. This is the first in a two-part series; the second video uses the foam to construct a "jelly capacitor."

## Key Topics
- Introduction to Michael Davey's gelatin-based prosthetic foam technique
- Recipe and proportions for making gelatin foam by volume
- Role of plasticizers in modifying foam properties
- Foaming process using a kitchen mixer
- Setting, casting, and reheating the foam
- Preserving the foam to prevent bacterial/mold growth

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **300 bloom pig skin gelatin (acid refined)** — the base structural material; available at grocers or eBay
- **Water** — used to hydrate the gelatin (1 part water to 2 parts gelatin)
- **Honey** — one of two plasticizers; 1 part (50 ml)
- **Glycerin (glycerol)** — second plasticizer; 2 parts (100 ml); requires ~40–45°C to dissolve
- **Washing-up liquid (dish soap)** — foaming agent; ~5 ml
- **Oil of cloves** — antibacterial/preservative agent
- **Listerine mouthwash** — contains thymol; used as preservative at ~10 ml per litre (~5 ml for 500 ml of foam)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Hydration**: Gelatin crystals soaked in water and left to swell before mixing
- **Heating**: Mix heated in microwave (or on hob) to ~40–45°C to dissolve gelatin and glycerin; kept below boiling to avoid burning
- **Whipping**: Hot mix transferred to bowl, washing-up liquid added, then whipped with a standard kitchen mixer for ~10 minutes to increase volume (~1/3 increase)
- **Setting**: Foam placed in fridge quickly to set while retaining foam structure
- **Casting**: Foam can be reheated in microwave and poured into moulds — reheating does not destroy the foam structure
- **Preservation**: Antibacterial agent (oil of cloves or Listerine) whipped in before setting
