## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith opens with a brief history of the 17th–18th century longitude problem — the difficulty of knowing one's east-west position at sea — and explains how the search for accurate timekeeping led to an obsession with pendulums. He then demonstrates a simple proof-of-concept pendulum generator built from a gym free weight, a 3D-printed bracket, bearings, and a salvaged coil from an old hamster-cage generator, showing that swinging a pendulum over a coil is enough to light an LED. The main takeaway is that the oscillating energy stored in a pendulum system can be harvested as electricity, and that multiple coils arranged along the arc of swing would increase output significantly.
