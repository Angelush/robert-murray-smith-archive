## Overview
Luke (presenting on the channel) builds a steampunk/dieselpunk-themed lamp as a Christmas Eve gift for his father-in-law. The lamp repurposes what appears to be a mechanical engine component as its decorative base, fitted with spark plugs, a brass lamp holder, and LED accent lighting underneath. The finished piece creates a striking floating effect with glowing underglow.

## Key Topics
- Steampunk/dieselpunk aesthetic design principles
- Repurposing an engine component as a lamp base
- Adding decorative spark plugs as visual accents
- Installing and securing LED strip lighting
- Selecting a brass lamp holder over a chrome-painted plastic one for period-appropriate styling
- Wire management and finishing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Dark wood — used as the lamp base
- Spark plugs — decorative elements drilled into the side of the base
- LED strip (24V) — accent underglow lighting
- Gorilla super glue — used to secure LED strips and components
- Brass lamp connector/holder — chosen to better match the steampunk theme
- Bearing — installed to hold the lamp holder straight
- Rubber tubing — used as wiring conduit, spray-painted chrome
- Chrome spray paint — applied to rubber tubing
- LED transformer — steps down 230V mains to 24V for the LED strip
- Rubber matting — cut to size and glued over wiring to hide and protect connections
- Spray adhesive — used to fix rubber matting

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling holes to fit spark plugs into the engine block base
- Threading lamp wiring through the existing spark plug/piston channel
- Sanding wood foot to square edges and rounded corners
- Improvised clamping: hanging a 2.5 kg weight on a string to apply downward pressure while glue sets
- Hand-bending LED strips around corners with glue, held under a weight
- Installing a bearing to seat and align the lamp holder
- Mains wiring with a 230V–24V LED transformer
- Wire concealment using adhesive-backed rubber matting on the base underside
