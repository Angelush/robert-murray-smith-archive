## Overview
Robert demonstrates "hot ice" — a supersaturated sodium acetate solution that crystallises instantly when seeded with a tiny crystal, releasing heat in the process. The video shows how to prepare and trigger this reaction as a Christmas science trick, highlighting that the same material is used in reusable hand warmers.

## Key Topics
- Supersaturated sodium acetate solutions and supercooling
- Instant crystallisation triggered by a seed crystal
- Hot ice as a festive science demonstration
- Practical application in reusable hot packs

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Sodium acetate** — the main material; melted, dissolved in water, and allowed to cool into a supersaturated liquid
- **Water** — approximately 10–12 ml added to aid dissolving

## Techniques and Methods
- Melting sodium acetate and adding a small amount of water to create a supersaturated solution
- Cooling the solution to room temperature without disturbing it (maintaining the supersaturated state)
- Seeding crystallisation by touching the surface with a solid sodium acetate crystal on a stick
