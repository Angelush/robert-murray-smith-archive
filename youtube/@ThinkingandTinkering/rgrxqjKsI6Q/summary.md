## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how he "cloned" himself multiple times in a single video frame using the free video editor OpenShot, appearing as three simultaneous versions of himself. He explains that the key to convincing visual trickery lies not in complex editing but in careful scene setup — keeping the camera fixed and using simple crop/layer techniques. The video serves as a playful lesson in how easily video can deceive, echoing the craft behind professional film illusions.

## Key Topics
- Using OpenShot (free, open-source video editor) to create a "clone" effect
- The importance of scene setup over editing complexity in visual trickery
- Layer-based video editing and the crop effect
- How magicians and filmmakers use setup and effort to make illusions believable
- A secondary demonstration of a "levitating ball" using the same crop technique
- Critical media literacy: seeing is not always believing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Fixed-camera multi-take filming (three separate takes from the same position)
- Crop effect in OpenShot to isolate and overlay scene regions
- Layer compositing in OpenShot (three independent video layers)
- Brightness/contrast adjustment (mentioned as a fallback for uneven lighting between takes)
- Audio augmentation to sell a visual effect (levitating ball with added sound)
