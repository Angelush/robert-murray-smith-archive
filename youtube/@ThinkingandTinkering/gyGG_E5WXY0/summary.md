## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to combine weatherproof glass bearings (introduced in video 2001) with a 3D-printed hypoid gear to create a rust-proof gear train suited for outdoor use. The assembly is designed with wind turbines and water wheels in mind, offering both a built-in gear ratio and weather resistance without needing precise alignment.

## Key Topics
- Recap of weatherproof glass bearings from the previous video (2001)
- Introduction to hypoid gears: their geometry, offset axis, and use in car differentials
- Remixing an existing hypoid gear model to incorporate bearing races
- Assembly of the glass bearing (inner race, outer race, cage, balls, superglue)
- Integration of the bearing into the hypoid gear train
- Practical application: vertical wind turbines and water wheels
- Power transmission concepts: gear ratio, torque vs. speed trade-off

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Glass ball bearings** — used instead of steel for rust-proof outdoor applications
- **Plastic** — used for the printed bearing races and gear components
- **Super glue (cyanoacrylate)** — a small spot used to fix the bearing cage in place
- **Ceramic** — mentioned as an alternative to glass for the bearing balls

## Techniques and Methods
- **3D printing** — all parts designed in TinkerCAD and published on Thingiverse
- **Remixing an existing model** — modifying OT Vintner's hypoid gear design to add bearing race housings
- **Glass bearing assembly** — inserting inner race into outer race, seating six ball bearings, fitting cage, and securing with a spot of superglue
- **Gear train integration** — press-fitting bearing assemblies into gear hubs before attaching gear bodies
