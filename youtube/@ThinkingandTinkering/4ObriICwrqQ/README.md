## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a quick DIY low-friction bearing made from graphite-coated HDPE (high-density polyethylene), motivated by excessive friction in a small wind turbine generator he had been building. By rubbing graphite into both sides of a cut HDPE disc and placing it between rotating surfaces, he achieves surprisingly smooth, sustained rotation. The technique is presented as an accessible solution for prototyping and model-making.
