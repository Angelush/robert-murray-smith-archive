## Overview
This video demonstrates how to build a simple DIY homopolar motor using just three common items: a battery, a magnet, and copper wire. The presenter (Luke) explains the basic working principle of a homopolar motor and shows two designs, with the preferred version using a copper wire coil wound around a broom handle for shape. The result is a continuously spinning wire assembly driven purely by electromagnetic interaction.

## Key Topics
- What a homopolar motor is: a DC electric motor with two magnetic poles producing constant circular motion
- How the circuit works: magnet on one battery terminal, wire connecting the other terminal back to the magnet
- Two build variations: a simple bent wire and a more visually impressive spiral coil
- Practical tips for winding the coil using a broom handle as a former

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- AA or AAA battery (DC power supply)
- Small neodymium/permanent magnet (attached to the negative terminal)
- Copper wire (used for both the simple and coil designs)

## Techniques and Methods
- Hand-winding copper wire into a spiral coil using a broom handle as a mandrel
- Balancing the wire so the hook contacts the positive terminal and the base touches the magnet
- Manual starting push to initiate rotation
