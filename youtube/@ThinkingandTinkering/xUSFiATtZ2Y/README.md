## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to repurpose an old hard disk drive as a functional speaker by extracting the voice coil and magnet assembly from the read/write head mechanism. He builds a simple frame from foamboard and acrylic, constructs a cellophane diaphragm, and couples it to the HDD armature to produce sound. The result is a working (if tinny) speaker, with the suggestion that a salvaged speaker cone would yield better audio reproduction.
