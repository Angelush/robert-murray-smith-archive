## Overview

Robert demonstrates a copper nanoparticle gel he developed as a more convenient, solid-form alternative to a liquid coppering solution. By connecting a copper wire brush to a steel surface and applying the gel as an electrolyte bridge, he shows that the system acts as a self-contained battery — enabling autocatalytic copper deposition that continues building thickness beyond what simple displacement reactions (like copper sulfate on mild steel) can achieve. The key takeaway is that the gel completes a galvanic circuit, and once an initial copper layer forms, it acts as a nucleation site for continued electroless-style plating.
