## Overview
Luke builds a battery pack for an e-bike/scooter using nickel-metal hydride cells scavenged from an old lighting system. He wires eight cells into two series packs and connects them in parallel to achieve approximately 14.4V at 8Ah, then tests the pack on a motor driver with a speed controller. The pack performs well, reading nearly 17V on a multimeter, and successfully spins the motor.

## Key Topics
- Sourcing and repurposing scavenged NiMH cells from old lighting equipment
- Basic battery concepts: voltage (push) and amperage (energy capacity) explained via analogy
- Series vs. parallel wiring configurations and their effects on voltage and capacity
- Testing the assembled pack with a multimeter and motor driver
- Charging the completed pack and plans for future installation on a DIY scooter

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Nickel-metal hydride (NiMH) rechargeable cells (scavenged from an old lighting system)

## Techniques and Methods
- Spot welding cells together to form series packs
- Wiring two 4-cell series packs in parallel
- Multimeter voltage measurement to verify pack output
- Bench-testing the pack against a motor driver and throttle/speed controller
