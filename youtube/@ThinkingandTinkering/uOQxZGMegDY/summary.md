## Overview
Robert demonstrates how to build a simple, low-cost distillation apparatus from copper microbore pipe and a pressure cooker. He explains the principles of distillation — separating liquids by their different boiling points — and shows the equipment producing both essential oils and high-proof alcohol, which he frames as a homemade biofuel suitable for burning as fuel.

## Key Topics
- Principles of distillation and how liquids separate by boiling point
- DIY construction of a copper-coil condenser still
- Using a pressure cooker as a sealed boiling vessel
- Distillation of alcohol from a fermented mixture to produce high-proof biofuel
- Heat control as the primary operating parameter
- Brief mention of vacuum distillation to lower operating temperatures
- Legal note: distilling spirits for consumption is illegal in the UK, US, and Canada

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Copper microbore pipe (8mm)** — bent into a coil to act as the condenser
- **15mm copper fittings and reducers (15mm to 8mm)** — used to connect the coil to the pressure cooker lid
- **Tank connector** — used to seal the drilled lid fitting
- **Aluminium pressure cooker** — boiling vessel (stainless steel recommended as a higher-quality alternative)
- **Alcohol (ethanol)** — the target distillate; boiling point ~73°C, separated from water (boiling point ~100°C)
- **Water** — the residual liquid left behind after alcohol boils off
- **Essential oils** — briefly distilled as a demonstration before the alcohol run

## Techniques and Methods
- **Distillation** — heating a liquid mixture so components evaporate in order of boiling point, then condensing the vapor back to liquid
- **Air-cooled copper coil condensation** — using a long exposed copper coil (rather than a water-jacketed condenser) to cool and condense vapor
- **Heat-rate control** — adjusting hob temperature to achieve drip-rate condensation rather than vapor breakthrough
- **Angle adjustment of the coil** — tilting the coil to allow condensed liquid to flow freely with gravity
- **Vacuum/reduced-pressure distillation** — mentioned as a method to lower the boiling point and reduce energy input
- **Drilling and fitting a pressure cooker lid** — 25mm hole saw used to mount the tank connector
