## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith revisits the classic childhood cotton reel tank toy, identifying its core flaw: the rubber band powering it creates axial clamping force that makes the mechanism harder to turn the more it is wound. He designs a 3D-printed replacement using TinkerCAD, incorporating a thrust bearing to absorb those axial forces, resulting in a smooth, free-spinning rubber band motor module he intends to use as a foundation for future projects.

## Key Topics
- The cotton reel tank/tractor toy and its mechanical limitations
- Axial clamping force as a fundamental problem in rubber band-powered mechanisms
- Thrust bearings as a solution to axial load issues
- Designing a replacement barrel and gear assembly in TinkerCAD
- 3D printing and assembling the rubber band motor
- Plans to release the files on Thingiverse and build further projects using the motor

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Rubber band** (~0.5 cm wide) — the energy storage/power source, threaded and knotted through the barrel
- **Thrust bearing** (11 mm deep, 42 mm OD, 25 mm ID) — two metal rings and a ring of ball bearings; absorbs axial clamping force
- **Super glue** — used to secure the rubber band knot and glue frame components
- **Candle wax / grease** — mentioned as the traditional lubricating fix for the original cotton reel toy (insufficient)
- **Latex** (from comments) — Robert recommends latex rubber bands for better performance and longevity

## Techniques and Methods
- **CAD design in TinkerCAD** — designing the barrel, gear, pegs, frame, and clip pieces
- **FDM 3D printing** — printing all structural components (black filament used to deplete an old reel)
- **Thrust bearing assembly** — pressing metal rings into gear slots, stacking ball race and rings
- **Rubber band threading and knotting** — feeding band through the barrel bar, tying a knot, and securing with super glue
- **Press-fit assembly** — cap and turn handle pressed onto locating lugs without glue for easy band replacement

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Use latex rubber bands and avoid overwinding** — the real durability issue is not the band itself but that the mechanism is so easy to wind that people overwind it; latex handles stress better.
- **Three upcoming projects** planned that use this motor module; he is also hoping the community will build their own ideas on top of it.
- **Gearing advice** — gear the output to whatever speed is needed; he discussed shear-thickening fluids for braking and clutches in reply to a suggestion, noting that containing liquid between moving plates is a significant engineering challenge.
- **Axial flux motor** — teased as the subject of that Friday's video in response to a related question.
- **Filament colour** — the black filament is being used up from an old reel and will revert to grey in subsequent videos.
- **Encouragement to builders** — repeatedly urged commenters with ideas to just go ahead and build, and offered to watch and share links to community builds.
