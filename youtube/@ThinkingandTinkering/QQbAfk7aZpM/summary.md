## Overview
This video (episode 2100) builds on the previous Samara wind turbine project by adding tubercles — the bumpy nodules found on humpback whale pectoral fins — to the blade's leading edge. Robert investigates whether this biomimetic modification improves turbine performance, inspired by the research of marine biologist Frank Fish and engineer Philip Watts, who demonstrated a 15–20% improvement in lift and drag reduction. He 3D-prints a modified blade and conducts a side-by-side field comparison on a windy hill, concluding the results are promising but somewhat equivocal given real-world conditions.

- Research by Frank Fish and Philip Watts linking tubercles to improved hydrodynamic/aerodynamic performance
- 15–20% improvement in lift and reduction in drag reported in their findings

- Side-by-side field test of original vs. tubercle-modified blade
- Potential future applications of tubercle technology

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Fairness of the test:** Repeatedly defended the side-by-side methodology — because both turbines are in identical condition and tested simultaneously, it is a valid comparison of one design against the other, even if it wouldn't generalise to the broader world.
- **Research backing:** Directed viewers to Google Scholar for the substantial body of existing studies; framed his video as "more of a demo than anything" given how well-supported the tubercle effect already is.
- **Wind tunnel suggestion:** Acknowledged he lacks the space and funds for a wind tunnel currently, but called it a reasonable idea.
- **Quantitative testing:** Expressed interest in a small generator, load, and RPM readout for more rigorous measurement, but noted that for now the visual comparison is practical.
- **Swept area:** Briefly noted "swept area" as a relevant metric (likely in response to a viewer question about turbine efficiency).
- **Patents:** Reinforced that patents are only relevant to the patent holder and commercial use — country of origin of the maker doesn't matter.
- **Further applications:** Called several viewer suggestions "good ideas" and indicated he would ponder how to implement them.
