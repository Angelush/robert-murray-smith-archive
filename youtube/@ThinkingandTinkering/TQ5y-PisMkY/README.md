## Overview

A one-year follow-up video testing graphene-based conductive ink used as thermal paste in a PC build. The presenter (Luke) runs a series of CPU stress and performance benchmarks to compare temperatures against results from six months prior, finding notable improvements across most tests — with peak CPU temperatures dropping by up to 19°C depending on the workload. The video concludes that the conductive ink has continued to improve over time, and the decision is made to leave it in the system rather than remove it.
