## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates that improving wind turbine performance doesn't require exotic or cutting-edge designs. Using the Waters turbine as a starting point, he shows how simply placing a cone in front of a small turbine dramatically increases its power output by concentrating airflow. The key takeaway is that elegant, simple solutions are often the most effective.

## Key Topics
- The Waters turbine as a compact, efficient baseline design
- Using a hyperbolic (or plain) cone as a wind concentrator in front of a HAWT
- The Ventum wind turbine as a commercial example of stacked cones over a conventional HAWT
- The Envelop wind collector as another cone-based improvement
- Covering one side of a Savonius (VAWT) to reduce drag — as practised in the Nashtifan windmill in Iran (over 1,000 years old)
- The broader principle: simple interventions yield dramatic real-world improvements

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Mounting a hyperbolic or straight cone in front of a small horizontal-axis wind turbine (HAWT) to concentrate airflow
- Partially shielding one side of a vertical-axis wind turbine (VAWT/Savonius) to eliminate parasitic drag
- Live demonstration of the Waters turbine with and without cone attachment
