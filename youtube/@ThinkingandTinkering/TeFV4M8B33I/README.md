## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates that improving wind turbine performance doesn't require exotic or cutting-edge designs. Using the Waters turbine as a starting point, he shows how simply placing a cone in front of a small turbine dramatically increases its power output by concentrating airflow. The key takeaway is that elegant, simple solutions are often the most effective.
