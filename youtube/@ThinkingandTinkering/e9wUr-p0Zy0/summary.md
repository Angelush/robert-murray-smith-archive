## Overview
Part 3 of Robert's project to convert a vintage lawn mower into "Bessie," the iconic yellow car from Doctor Who. This episode covers the reassembly phase after all the cleaning, sanding, and repainting shown in earlier videos. The vehicle is being built as a slow-moving electric vehicle targeting 15–20 mph, with the front steering subframe and rear belt transmission reassembled by the end of the video.

## Key Topics
- Reassembly of the front steering subframe
- Use of rubber door stops as improvised suspension buffers
- Reinstallation of the rear belt transmission system
- Belt guide arms and tensioner spring reassembly
- Drive pulley fitting and belt routing
- Plans for bodywork as the next step

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Rubber door stops (used as suspension buffers under the front steering arm)
- Banjo nuts (used to mount steering rod pivot points)
- Drive belt and pulleys (part of the rear transmission)
- Tensioner spring (for belt tension in the transmission)

## Techniques and Methods
- Subframe reassembly with front steering linkage
- Two-pivot steering rod connection (Ackermann-style linkage)
- Belt drive transmission reinstallation with guide arms
- Spring tensioning of drive belt
- Flipping the chassis to access underside for transmission work, then flipping back

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert confirmed the project is finished (in a later comment). He mentioned seriously considering making it road legal as a **light quad**, which in the UK requires no driving licence. He noted wanting to retain as much of the original transmission as possible rather than replacing components, and was open to suggestions but preferred to keep the character of the original machine intact.
