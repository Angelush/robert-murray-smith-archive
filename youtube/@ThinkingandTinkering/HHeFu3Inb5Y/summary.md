## Overview
This video is Part 4 of Robert Murray-Smith's series on building a Van Der Graaf generator, focusing specifically on constructing the toroid component. He walks through the full fabrication process: forming the toroid shape from vacuum cleaner hose, filling its ridged surface with polyester resin, and metallizing it with aluminium ducting tape. The finished toroid is shown fitted to the generator's tower beneath the gazing ball (sphere electrode).

## Key Topics
- Sourcing and sizing vacuum cleaner hose for the toroid form
- Bending and gluing the hose into a toroid shape around the support pipe
- Filling surface ridges with polyester builder's resin (Seifix) for a smooth finish
- Applying aluminium ducting tape in two layers (inner and outer strips) to metallize the surface
- Burnishing and polishing the tape for a clean finish
- Brief digression on adapting the technique for a Tesla coil toroid
- Purpose of the toroid: preventing high-voltage creepage down the support column

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Vacuum cleaner hose (~45 mm diameter)** — sourced from an old Dyson, used as the toroid body
- **Seifix polyester resin** — a builder's bolt-fixing compound used to fill the ridges of the corrugated hose; described as containing sand, being sticky and flexible
- **Super glue** — used to join the ends of the hose
- **Aluminium ducting tape** — used in 10 cm rolls (also available in 6 cm); applied in inner and outer strips to metallize the surface
- **Car body filler** — mentioned as an alternative to Seifix, with the caveat that it must be sealed before taping

## Techniques and Methods
- Measuring and cutting hose to fit the circumference of the 110 mm support pipe
- Bending hose around a former pipe to achieve the toroid geometry
- Applying super glue to join hose ends; leaving to set
- Filling corrugated ridges with polyester resin via a caulking gun with mixing nozzle
- Sanding the filled surface flat once cured
- Applying aluminium tape in two separate bands (outer strips ~1.5–2 cm wide, inner strips ~1 cm or less), working in opposing quarters/eighths/sixteenths to avoid drift and kinking
- Burnishing tape with an 8 mm steel rod
- Polishing with a Dremel tool fitted with a polishing pad
- Sealing car body filler with varnish or emulsion paint before taping (tip for alternative build)

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert added an important top-level comment explaining the **functional purpose of the toroid**: it prevents high-voltage creepage — on a high-voltage machine like this, the charge would travel down the support column rather than staying on the sphere, and the toroid at the base of the sphere redirects it back onto the sphere. This is a key clarification not covered in the video itself.
