## Overview

This video explores moisture-enabled generation (MEG), a technology that generates electricity from the moisture gradient across cellulose (paper) or graphene oxide thin films. Robert explains the underlying proton-migration mechanism, demonstrates a simple breath-powered paper generator, and highlights a self-recharging graphene oxide battery developed by Australian company Strategic Elements with CSIRO and UNSW. The key takeaway is that these devices are remarkably simple and cheap to fabricate, with significant potential for powering wearable sensors and electronic skin patches.
