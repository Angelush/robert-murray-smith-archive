## Overview
A short maker tutorial showing how to convert a standard kitchen rolling pin into a rubber-surfaced DIY roller. By removing the handles, threading the central bar, wrapping the barrel in rubber matting (held with spray adhesive), and fitting a custom wooden handle, the result is a versatile workshop roller suitable for the many applications demonstrated in the companion video #1360.

## Key Topics
- Motivation: addressing viewer feedback that video #1360 assumed you already had a roller
- Sourcing a rolling pin from a hardware/big-box store as the base component
- Threading the internal metal bar to accept bolts and a handle assembly
- Applying rubber matting to the rolling pin barrel
- Constructing a custom wooden handle with a sleeve fitting
- Securing hardware permanently with super glue or spot welding

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Rolling pin (off-the-shelf, with 6 mm internal steel bar)
- 6 mm steel bar (internal axle of the rolling pin)
- 8 mm steel bar (handle shaft)
- 12 mm sleeve (handle collar)
- Rubber matting (wrapped around barrel)
- Spray adhesive / spray glue (to bond rubber to roller)
- Super glue / Loctite (to lock nuts permanently)
- Block of wood and wooden dowel (for handle construction)
- Bolts and washers (axle fasteners)

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling a rolling pin by removing its handles
- Threading a metal bar (to accept bolts)
- Cutting rubber matting to size and butt-joining with overlap-and-slice technique for a clean seam
- Bonding rubber to a cylindrical surface with spray adhesive
- Drilling an 8 mm bore through a wooden block and dowelling a grip piece to form a handle
- Sliding a sleeve over a bar to connect handle to axle
- Permanently locking nuts with super glue or spot welding to prevent wobble
