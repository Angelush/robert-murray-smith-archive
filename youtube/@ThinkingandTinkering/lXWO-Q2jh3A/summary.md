## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simple hack for making gears by cutting up toothed timing belts (the kind used in car engines and 3D printers) and gluing them onto circular objects. He presents this as a practical solution to a longstanding frustration with gear-making in DIY motor and machine projects, showing how a strip of belt glued to a flywheel creates a functional, durable cog with very little effort.

## Key Topics
- The difficulty of making custom gears for DIY motors and machines
- Using toothed timing belts as a shortcut for gear fabrication
- Sourcing timing belts cheaply (new or free from auto shops)
- Demonstrating the technique on an exercise bike flywheel
- Durability and sizing range of timing belts

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Toothed timing belt from 3D printers (small profile)
- Toothed timing belt from car engines — specifically a **Conta 1025** cambelt (approx. £20–£30 new)
- Adhesive/glue (implied, used to attach belt strips to wheel surfaces)
- Exercise bike flywheel (used as the substrate for demonstration)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting a toothed timing belt into strips
- Gluing belt strips onto the circumference of a circular object (e.g., a caster wheel, flywheel) to create a functional gear/cog
- Sourcing used cambelts for free from local auto garages (service-replacement parts)
