## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores a simple solution for using the heat from a rocket stove indoors, where smoke and chimney requirements normally prevent indoor use. A community member named DS suggested heating a thermal mass object (stone, brick, or sand) on the outdoor rocket stove and then bringing it inside to radiate heat. Robert praises the idea for its elegant simplicity and accessibility.

## Key Topics
- The main limitation of rocket stoves: they require a chimney and are difficult to use indoors
- Using thermal mass objects as heat transfer intermediaries
- Community-sourced solutions (credit to viewer "DS")
- Brief mention of more complex alternatives (copper coil water heating loop)
- Reference to a previously built "Dutch stove" from video 1760

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Stone (used as the thermal mass object in the demonstration)
- Brick (mentioned as an alternative thermal mass)
- Sand (mentioned as an alternative thermal mass)
- Copper coil (mentioned briefly as an alternative heat-transfer approach)

## Techniques and Methods
- Heating a dense thermal mass object on an outdoor rocket stove
- Transferring the heated object indoors to radiate stored heat
- Using a Dutch stove enclosure to contain/place the thermal mass
