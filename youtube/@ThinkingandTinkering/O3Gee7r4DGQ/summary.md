## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a method for copper plating aluminium — a process normally considered impossible due to aluminium's high reactivity. The key is first applying a sodium molybdate-based passivation (conversion) coating that turns the aluminium surface golden, after which standard electroplating can proceed. He is motivated by supercapacitor applications, where a corrosion-resistant, conductive aluminium current collector is needed.

## Key Topics
- Why copper plating aluminium is normally considered impossible (high surface reactivity)
- Why traditional chromate passivation is unsuitable (carcinogens)
- Sodium molybdate as a safe alternative passivation/conversion coating
- Step-by-step surface preparation and passivation procedure
- Copper electroplating onto the passivated aluminium surface
- Application to supercapacitor current collectors

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Aluminium (kitchen foil)** — the substrate being plated
- **Isopropyl alcohol (IPA)** — initial degreasing/cleaning step
- **Sodium hydroxide (0.1 M solution)** — alkaline etch to clean the aluminium surface
- **Sodium molybdate** — key passivation agent; forms the golden conversion coating (exact recipe from comments: 2.4 g sodium molybdate in 100 mL DI water)
- **Acetic acid (5% / vinegar)** — co-solvent in the passivation bath (100 mL of 5% acetic acid)
- **Lithium or sodium fluoride (1 g)** — part of the full passivation recipe (from comments)
- **Phosphoric acid (2 mL concentrated)** — part of the full passivation recipe (from comments)
- **Universal plating solution** — copper electroplating electrolyte
- **Copper anode** — electrode used in the plating bath
- **Chromates / chromic acid** — mentioned as the traditional (but carcinogenic) passivation method, explicitly rejected

## Techniques and Methods
- **Degreasing** with isopropyl alcohol to remove surface contaminants
- **Alkaline etching** in 0.1 M NaOH for 15–30 seconds to clean the aluminium surface
- **Conversion coating / passivation** via immersion in sodium molybdate + acetic acid bath for 20–30 minutes, producing a visible golden surface layer
- **Electroplating** using a universal copper plating solution with a copper anode, applied directly to the passivated surface

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Full passivation recipe clarified:** The exact formulation is 2.4 g sodium molybdate, 100 mL DI water, 100 mL of 5% acetic acid, 1 g lithium or sodium fluoride, and 2 mL concentrated phosphoric acid.
- **Academic references:** He directs commenters to Google Scholar, suggesting searches for "sodium molybdate passivation" or "conversion coating" to find supporting literature.
- **Electrolyte compatibility:** He notes the passivation layer itself is electrolyte-independent; compatibility questions arise only when copper plating is added on top.
- **Pain plating distinction:** He clarifies that another method suggested in comments is "more of a pain plate" and "strictly not electroplating."
