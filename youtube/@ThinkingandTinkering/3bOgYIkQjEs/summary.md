## Overview
This video is Part 2 of Robert Murray-Smith's series on building a large Van der Graaf generator, focusing specifically on constructing the tower section. He walks through cutting and assembling the main PVC pipe column, attaching decorative/functional fins, and fabricating the base plate, explaining material choices and tool techniques along the way. The key takeaway is that uPVC foam board ("builders board") is an ideal material for high-voltage projects due to its insulating properties and ease of fabrication.

## Key Topics
- Cutting the main tower pipe (drainpipe/conduit) to length
- Design and spacing of stabilising fins along the tower
- Why timber is unsuitable for high-voltage builds (it conducts at high voltage)
- Using uPVC foam board ("builders board") as the primary structural material
- Cutting circular holes in the fins using a variable hole cutter or DIY scoring tool
- Gluing fins to the pipe with cyanoacrylate (superglue)
- Base plate construction and lateral stability

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PVC drainpipe/conduit** — 110 mm diameter, 60 cm length; forms the main tower column
- **Builders board (uPVC coated foam board)** — used for fins and base plate; preferred over timber for its insulating properties at high voltage
- **Cyanoacrylate (superglue)** — used to bond fins and structural components; creates an extremely strong permanent joint

## Techniques and Methods
- **Pipe cutting**: Marking circumference at multiple points using a flange as a guide, then hacksawing in stages while rotating the pipe to prevent drift
- **Edge cleanup**: Scraping cut edges clean with a trimming knife blade
- **Circle cutting in board**: Using a commercial variable hole cutter ("panning tool") or a DIY timber-and-nail scoring compass
- **Fin fitting**: Dry-fitting fins first, marking position, applying superglue ring, then sliding fin back into place
- **Surface finishing**: Using a flap wheel on a bench-mounted drill to remove melted plastic nodules after cutting
