## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith disassembles a broken strimmer (string trimmer) salvaged from a friend and repurposes its parts into a prop gun inspired by the Mandalorian blaster from Star Wars. Rather than aiming for an exact replica, he takes a Lego-inspired interpretive approach — suggesting the gun's silhouette with readily available scrap materials. The finished prop is wall-mounted and built entirely from salvaged and hardware-store components.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging and repurposing a broken strimmer for prop-making
- Lego-inspired interpretation vs. direct reproduction as a design philosophy
- Building a Mandalorian blaster prop from scrap materials
- Assembly and finishing of the prop gun

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Strimmer (string trimmer)** — donor object; handle used as gun body, shaft section as gun nozzle
- **Shelf brackets** — cut and shaped into the forked "horn" prongs at the gun's muzzle
- **Speedfit (push-fit) plumbing fittings** — repurposed as the gun sight
- **15mm copper/plastic pipe** — inserted into fittings to form the sight
- **Pipe clip** — used to mount the barrel/sight assembly
- **Stainless steel canister (thermos flask)** — used as the ammunition cartridge
- **Fibral (fiberglass paste/resin product by Upol)** — used to bond and fill the horn prongs
- **Black spray paint** — finishing coat on all components
- **Filler (general purpose)** — used to fill holes after cutting

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly and part-salvage from a broken power tool (strimmer)
- Cutting shelf brackets to shape for the muzzle prongs
- Bending metal brackets to achieve parallel alignment
- Bonding with fiberglass paste resin (Fibral/Upol)
- Sawing and trimming the strimmer handle to accommodate the canister insert
- Fitting a screw cap to accept the thermos flask cartridge
- Spray painting for uniform black finish
- Gluing down the original strimmer trigger to serve as the prop trigger
