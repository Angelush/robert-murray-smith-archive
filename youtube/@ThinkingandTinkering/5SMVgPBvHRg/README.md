## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a homemade Heron's Fountain built from stacked food containers, framing it as a "mechanical water battery" — a gravitational pressure store of energy rather than a perpetual motion machine. He explores the concept of separating the fountain's two lower containers vertically (e.g., one upstairs, one downstairs) to increase head pressure, with the goal of using that pressure to spin a small inline impeller generator to produce electricity.
