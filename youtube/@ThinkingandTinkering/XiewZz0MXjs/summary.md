## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith covers the announcement of Betavolt's BV100 nuclear battery from China — a device combining nickel-63 and diamond semiconductors capable of powering devices for 50 years. He places it in historical context alongside Bristol University's radioactive diamond battery and Russian research, then demonstrates how to build a rudimentary nuclear battery at home using a smoke alarm (americium-241 source) and a 2N3055 transistor.

## Key Topics
- Betavolt's BV100 battery: specs, operating principle, and production timeline
- History of nuclear batteries from the 1950s to present (RTGs, pacemakers, Bristol diamond battery, Russian research)
- Beta voltaic vs. alpha voltaic principles, and their similarity to photovoltaic cells
- How nuclear waste graphite from reactors is converted into radioactive diamond via sublimation and CVD
- DIY nuclear battery demonstration using consumer components

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Nickel-63** — radioactive isotope used in Betavolt's BV100 as the beta particle source
- **Diamond (single-crystal, CVD)** — semiconductor material sandwiching the nickel-63 layer; 10-micron layers
- **Carbon-14** — produced in nuclear reactor graphite rods when carbon-12/13 absorbs fast neutrons; used in Bristol's diamond battery
- **Graphite (reactor-grade)** — decommissioned nuclear reactor moderator rods, source material for radioactive diamond
- **Americium-241** — alpha-emitting isotope found inside ionization-type smoke alarms; half-life ~432 years
- **Plutonium** — mentioned as the isotope used in 1970s nuclear pacemakers
- **Copper** — the harmless decay product of nickel-63

## Techniques and Methods
- **Beta voltaic conversion** — beta particles from a radioactive source strike an NP semiconductor junction, driving a current (used in BV100)
- **Alpha voltaic conversion** — same principle using alpha particles; demonstrated in the DIY experiment with americium-241
- **Sublimation + chemical vapor deposition (CVD)** — Bristol University's method to convert radioactive graphite into radioactive diamond sheets
- **Stacking/series-parallel module assembly** — multiple sandwich cells linked to boost voltage or current, analogous to conventional batteries
- **Transistor modification as a radiation detector/cell** — metal cap removed from a 2N3055 NPN transistor to expose the semiconductor die; collector and base leads used as output; placed adjacent to americium-241 source to generate measurable voltage
