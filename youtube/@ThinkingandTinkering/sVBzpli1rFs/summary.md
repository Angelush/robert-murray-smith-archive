## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of contra-rotation — two rotors spinning in opposite directions on the same axle — and tests it in a 3D-printed wind turbine build. He compares axial flux generation (both rotors spinning the same direction, magnetically locked) against contra-rotation, finding that the contra-rotating setup produces over 6V where the single and axial configurations could only manage ~2V. The video also draws a compelling parallel to Dyson's CR1 contra-rotating washing machine as motivation for the concept.

## Key Topics
- Distinction between counter-rotation (separate axles) and contra-rotation (same axle)
- Efficiency advantages of contra-rotation: 6–16% improvement, used in aircraft and torpedoes
- Dyson CR1 washing machine: contra-rotation mimics hand-washing, 15 min vs. 67 min cycle
- Potential for simpler contra-rotating washing machines using rim-mounted motors
- Reference to prior videos (1867, 1868) where rim-based Savonius turbine generation was explored
- 3D printing a mirrored second turbine rotor to create a contra-rotating pair
- Magnetic locking between rotors and how wind direction controls same-direction vs. contra-rotation
- Comparative test: axial flux (fan aimed at one side) vs. contra-rotation (fan aimed at center)
- Results: contra-rotation successfully lit a 6V LED strip; axial flux configuration did not

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 3D-printed turbine rotor (Savonius-style, printed twice as mirror images)
- Permanent magnets (embedded in rotors for magnetic locking between the two discs)
- LED lighting strip (rated ~6V threshold for illumination)
- Fan (used at 2.4 m/s wind speed for testing)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing of mirrored rotor pairs for contra-rotating assembly
- Rim-based (axial flux) electrical generation rather than axle-based generation
- Magnetic locking between co-axial rotors to switch between axial and contra-rotation modes by changing wind direction
- Controlled comparative testing: same fan power, same distance, fan position varied to induce different rotation modes
