## Overview
This short video demonstrates a simple two-ingredient hand-cleaning method using sugar and hand soap as a DIY exfoliant. The presenter, Luke, explains that the sugar acts as a grit to scrub away dead skin and grime, leaving hands clean and soft. The technique is particularly recommended for people who work in dirty environments such as garages or mechanics workshops.

## Key Topics
- DIY hand exfoliation using household ingredients
- Removing grease, oil, and grime from heavily soiled hands
- The concept of exfoliation and removal of dead skin
- Inspiration from mechanics' workshop hand soap products

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Sugar** — used as an abrasive/exfoliating grit
- **Hand soap** — combined with sugar as the cleaning agent
- **Hot water** — used to rinse after scrubbing

## Techniques and Methods
- Mixing a portion of sugar with squirts of hand soap in the palm
- Rubbing the mixture vigorously over both hands for approximately one minute
- Adding more sugar mid-scrub if additional abrasion is needed
- Rinsing with hot water
