## Overview
A competition entry responding to a challenge (from Robert and Luke) to generate enough electricity to light an LED. The presenter built a pendulum-based energy harvester using permanent magnets, inductors salvaged from a photocopier, and a voltage multiplier circuit. The LED was ultimately lit — both via the pendulum system and a backup three-cell potato battery.

## Key Topics
- Pendulum-driven electromagnetic energy harvesting
- Use of potential energy recovery as a pendulum swings upward on the return stroke
- Electromagnetic induction using salvaged photocopier inductors
- Voltage multiplication to boost low AC output
- Flyback transformer as an attempted voltage booster
- Potato battery as a fallback to meet contest requirements

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Neodymium magnets (on a piston, and as magnetic amplifier with electromagnet)
- Inductors salvaged from an old photocopier
- Green tape (to hold coil around a plastic tube)
- Flyback transformer
- Voltage multiplier circuit (capacitor-diode ladder implied)
- Red LED (requires ~1500 mV DC to fire)
- Potatoes (three-cell potato battery)

## Techniques and Methods
- Pendulum mechanics to drive a magnet-piston in and out of a coil
- Electromagnetic induction via a loosely wound recovery coil
- Voltage multiplication (Cockcroft-Walton or similar ladder circuit) to boost ~200 mV AC output
- Flyback transformer connection (abandoned — too much load on pendulum)
- Electrochemical energy generation via potato battery
