## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a DIY oil burner heater based on the principles of the 18th-century Argand lamp, using only standard copper and brass plumbing fittings — no welding required. He traces the history of oil lamps from 10,000 BC through to the Argand design of 1780 to explain *why* the design works, then demonstrates that a chimney and central airflow dramatically improve combustion. The key takeaway is that the Argand-style burner is best suited to heavier oils (vegetable, used mineral), while the kerosene-stove style (covered in video 1677) suits lighter fuels.
