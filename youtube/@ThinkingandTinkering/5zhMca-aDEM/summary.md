## Overview
In this short community address, Robert Murray-Smith responds to encountering a particularly rude commenter and uses it as an opportunity to remind his growing subscriber base of the channel's unwritten rule: all comments are welcome, but must be polite, respectful, and considerate of others. He frames the channel as a special community worth protecting, and makes clear that rudeness will not be tolerated — up to and including banning.

## Key Topics
- Robert's experience banning a rude commenter after attempts to address the behaviour directly
- The channel's core community standard: free speech paired with responsibility and respect
- A call to existing community members to help moderate by calling out rudeness
- Acknowledgement that the channel's rapid subscriber growth has brought in people unfamiliar with the community ethos
- A direct message to those who don't share these values: unsubscribe

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies reinforce the video's themes with a few notable clarifications:
- He emphasises that what upset him was not what the rude commenter said *to him* (he has a thick skin), but what was said *to other people* in the community.
- He engages thoughtfully with a commenter who apparently argued for a more permissive approach, acknowledging their view while disagreeing: *"I get what you are saying I just disagree is all… You have a nice view I just consider it a little unworkable in practice."*
- He clarifies to at least one regular commenter that his remarks were not aimed at them personally, noting he can recognise genuine troublemakers easily after long experience.
- He reaffirms the core principle: *"if someone can't respect others — well they made their own bed."*
- The majority of his replies are warm, brief acknowledgements (*"bless your heart"*, *"cheers mate"*), consistent with the welcoming tone he advocates for.
