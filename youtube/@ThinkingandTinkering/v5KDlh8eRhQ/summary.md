## Overview
A short, lighthearted video in which Robert revisits his earlier human-powered generator concept and pivots to exploring the long history of animals — dogs, goats, pigs, donkeys, oxen, and horses — being used as motive power "engines." He surveys historical examples from the turnspIt dog to Victorian "pooch mobiles," concluding with a tongue-in-cheek suggestion that animal (or child) power could serve as an unconventional answer to the energy crisis.

## Key Topics
- Recap of Robert's earlier human-powered walking generator (~100–150 W output)
- Historical use of animals as motive/mechanical power
- The turnspIt dog breed and its extinction
- Victorian "pooch mobile" dog-powered vehicle (last design ~1935, USA)
- Modern dog exercise wheels as improvised generators
- Donkey wheels (e.g., Dover well engine, ~1910)
- Goat sleds, goat mobiles, and goat engines
- Pigs used as power sources
- Working dogs (sled dogs) as continuing example of animal motive power
- Humorous framing: animal/child power as answer to the energy crisis

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Walking-wheel / treadwheel mechanism (hamster-wheel style) geared down to drive a load
- Attaching a generator to an animal exercise wheel
- Clockwork "jack spit" as mechanical replacement for the turnspIt dog
- Steam- and turbine-driven spit jacks (historical)
- Donkey wheel for raising water from wells

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly playful and brief ("lol"), but several add genuine substance:

- He clarifies the video is **primarily a bit of fun**, not a serious engineering proposal, though he adds: *"in all seriousness I think that's a good idea — I know I did this video as a bit of fun — but I still think there is value in the idea."*
- He references his **earlier human-powered desk treadmill video** as a more serious treatment of the same concept, noting exercise bikes and rowing machines are excellent candidates for generator conversion.
- He confirms there were **serious Victorian plans** to use human treadwheels in the 1800s.
- On gasifying animal waste: *"there we go — much better economically and we can gasify their poop!"* — suggesting a dual-use angle (power + biogas).
- He notes the turnspIt dog breed is genuinely gone and expresses mild puzzlement at why.
- He jokes that attaching a PlayStation to a dog wheel would keep dogs running all day.
- He corrects a commenter, noting the small animal in question is **guinea pigs**, not another species.
- On cats as power sources: *"effective cat utilisation — I like it mate"* (though earlier in the video he advises against trying to use cats).
