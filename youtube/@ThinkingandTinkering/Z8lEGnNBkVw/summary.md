## Overview
Luke (appearing on Robert Murray-Smith's channel) demonstrates how to convert comfortable car seats salvaged from a scrap Nissan into functional office chairs. The project involves fabricating a wooden adaptation plate to join the car seat to the base and legs of an existing office chair. The result is a highly comfortable, throne-like office chair that Robert himself tries out and enthusiastically endorses at the end.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging car seats from a scrap Nissan vehicle
- Designing and fabricating a wooden adaptation plate to interface a car seat with an office chair base
- Repurposing an old office chair (keeping only the legs and swivel mechanism)
- Assembly and load-spreading techniques using washers and bolts

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Wood (for the adaptation plate and gap-filling piece)
- Bolts and washers (for load spreading and mounting)
- Screws and dowels (for securing the wooden filler piece)

## Techniques and Methods
- Removing the sliding mechanism and adjustment lever from the car seat base to allow it to sit flat
- Marking and cutting a wooden adaptation plate to match the seat mounting points
- Drilling and counter-boring holes for bolted assembly
- Gluing and screwing a secondary filler piece of wood to eliminate the gap between the seat and adaptation plate
- Locating the centre of the adaptation plate to align the office chair swivel column
