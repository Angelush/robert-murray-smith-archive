## Overview
A practical demonstration of using conductive ink to electromagnetically shield the pickup cavities of an electric bass guitar. The presenter removes the pickups, paints the inside of the cavities with conductive ink, and reinstalls everything, resulting in a dramatic reduction in white noise and feedback.

## Key Topics
- Electromagnetic shielding concept for electric guitars/basses
- Why pickups pick up EMF interference and produce white noise
- Step-by-step process of removing and reinstalling guitar pickups
- Before-and-after audio demonstration of noise reduction

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Conductive ink** — applied by paintbrush to the inside of pickup cavities to act as an electromagnetic shield
- **Pickup screws** — also dipped in the conductive ink to potentially improve shielding continuity

## Techniques and Methods
- De-stringing the guitar to access the pickup cavities
- Removing pickups by unscrewing four screws per pickup
- Painting the interior of pickup cavities with conductive ink using both large and small brushes to ensure full coverage and edge detail
- Dipping the mounting screws in conductive ink
- Reinstalling pickups, restringing, and retuning the guitar
