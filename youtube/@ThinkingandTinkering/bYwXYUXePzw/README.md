## Overview

This video is a follow-up "addendum" to Robert's previous video demonstrating graphene-based conductive ink used as a printable antenna (which he calls the "Graftenna"). He explores the broader implications of being able to paint or print an antenna onto virtually any surface — walls, phone cases, PCBs — and notes that the ink, being 100% carbon with no metal particles, is cheaper and more stable than metal-based conductive inks. The main takeaway is that printable graphene antennas have significant untapped potential across RFID, TV reception, Wi-Fi, and mobile devices.
