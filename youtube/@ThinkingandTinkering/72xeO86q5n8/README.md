## Overview

Robert follows up on a previous video about the nature of mathematical universes, using Euclidean geometry as a concrete example to argue that mathematical principles are not universally true in an absolute sense — they are only true within the type of universe to which they are applied. His central takeaway is that on curved surfaces, foundational Euclidean axioms break down, and this should prompt us to always question the underlying assumptions of any mathematical or logical statement.
