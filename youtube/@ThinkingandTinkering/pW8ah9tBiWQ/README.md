## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a DIY magnetic stirrer, a lab tool used to stir solutions for extended periods without manual effort. He opts for a more powerful drill motor over the commonly seen PC-fan approach, building a resin disc to hold magnets and enclosing everything in a builder's board box. The finished device includes a PWM speed controller and a homemade plastic-coated stir bar.
