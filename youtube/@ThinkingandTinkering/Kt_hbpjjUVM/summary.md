## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a working escapement mechanism — the core timekeeping component of a clock — that he designed to function correctly straight off a 3D printer without manual adjustment. He explains the physics of harmonic oscillators (balance wheel and spring) and how an escapement regulates energy input, then walks through the assembly and successful test of his printed design. The video is a precursor to building a spring motor mechanism, presented as a simpler alternative to the tourbillon he built in video 2384.

## Key Topics
- What an escapement mechanism is and how it works as the "heart of a clock"
- The physics of harmonic oscillators: pendulums, balance wheels, and springs
- Why oscillator frequency is independent of amplitude (a fundamental property)
- Energy storage and regulated release in mechanical timekeeping
- The tourbillon (from video 2384) and the debate over its practical benefit
- 3D printing escapements and the challenge of getting them to work without fettling
- Assembly walkthrough of Robert's custom-designed escapement
- Tips for achieving a print-ready working escapement (lubrication and bearing choice)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Vaseline (petroleum jelly)** — applied to the pallet wheel to reduce friction and help the escapement run straight from the printer
- **Skate bearings** — used in the frame; metal-sealed bearings recommended over rubber-sealed
- **Ceramic bearings** — recommended over standard bearings to minimise friction
- **PLA or similar filament** — implied by use of an Elegoo Saturn Carbon resin/FDM printer (printer name mentioned)

## Techniques and Methods
- **3D printing** — full mechanism printed on an Elegoo Saturn Carbon printer
- **Press-fit assembly** — bearings and pillars pressed into frame holes without fasteners
- **Spot gluing** — spring key glued onto the balance wheel
- **String-and-weight test rig** — temporary weight drive used to verify the escapement runs before installing a spring motor
- **Lubrication** — light Vaseline application on the pallet wheel to reduce friction at the escapement interface
- **Bearing selection** — choosing metal-sealed or ceramic bearings over rubber-sealed to minimise drag
