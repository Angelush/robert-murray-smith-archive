## Overview
This short tutorial demonstrates how to create a stained glass effect on clear acrylic using common household materials. The presenter (Luke) traces a printed template using a black acrylic/PVA glue mixture to simulate lead lines, then colors the sections with dry erase markers and Sharpies for a translucent look, sealed with clear lacquer. The finished panel is applied decoratively to the side of a computer case with convincing results.

## Key Topics
- Creating a stained glass effect on clear acrylic sheet
- Simulating lead lines using a PVA glue and black acrylic paint mixture
- Choosing transparent coloring media (markers) over opaque acrylic paint to preserve light transmission
- Sealing the finished piece with clear lacquer spray
- Applying the finished panel as a decorative computer case accent

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Clear acrylic sheet** — the base/substrate material
- **Black acrylic paint** — mixed with PVA glue to simulate lead lines
- **PVA glue** — mixed with black acrylic to create the lead-line medium
- **Dry erase board markers** — used for transparent color fill
- **Sharpies (permanent markers)** — used alongside dry erase markers for coloring
- **Clear lacquer spray** — applied as a topcoat to seal and fix the marker coloring
- **Acrylic paint (colored)** — considered for coloring but rejected due to opacity
- **Masking tape** — mentioned as optional aid for neater line tracing

## Techniques and Methods
- Placing a printed paper template underneath the clear acrylic and tracing through it
- Mixing PVA glue with black acrylic paint to produce a lead-line simulation medium
- Freehand line tracing (preferred by presenter over masking tape for aesthetic reasons)
- Using transparent marker media (dry erase and Sharpie) to color sections while retaining translucency
- Spraying a coat of clear lacquer over the completed coloring to fix it in place
