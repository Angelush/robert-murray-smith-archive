## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith introduces the Agora flow battery, which claims 90 Wh/kg energy density and the ability to capture CO2 from the air as part of its charging process. He first explains how batteries work fundamentally, then demonstrates building a DIY zinc-bromine battery from a terracotta plant pot and glass, before explaining how true flow batteries differ and where Agora's chemistry fits in.

## Key Topics
- Fundamentals of how batteries work (electron transfer, anodes, cathodes, electrolytes, separators)
- Difference between traditional plate-based batteries and flow batteries
- DIY zinc-bromine battery construction and charging
- Red Flow's commercial zinc-bromide battery as a stepping stone to true flow batteries
- Agora's CO2-sequestering flow battery chemistry and energy density claims
- Economic scale threshold for flow batteries (~50 kWh+)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Zinc bromide (ZnBr₂)** — electrolyte for the DIY and Red Flow batteries
- **Sodium bromide + zinc sulfate** — precursors for making zinc bromide at home
- **Sodium hydroxide / potassium hydroxide** — used by Agora to create high-pH conditions for CO2 dissolution
- **Potassium formate** — intermediate compound formed when CO2 is captured and converted
- **Potassium bromide** — discharge product in Agora's chemistry
- **Potassium carbonate / sodium carbonate** — discharge by-products that can be recovered and sold
- **Graphite** — used as electrodes (naturally mined, can be cut into plates)
- **Zinc foil / zinc metal** — anode material in the DIY battery
- **Bromine (Br₂)** — forms visibly during charging at the carbon electrode
- **Palladium and lanthanum** — expensive catalysts used by Agora to convert CO2 into formate
- **Unglazed terracotta** — used as a porous separator/membrane in the DIY cell

## Techniques and Methods
- DIY flow cell construction using a terracotta plant pot (porous membrane) inside a glass jar
- Constant voltage charging (1.8 V) — described as the safer but slower method
- Constant current charging (30–50 mA/cm² of electrode area) — described as the correct/faster method
- Plating/stripping mechanism for zinc deposition and dissolution
- Home synthesis of zinc bromide by mixing sodium bromide and zinc sulfate in water, then evaporating off sodium sulfate
- CO2 dissolution at high pH with a catalyst to form formate ions, integrated into the Agora battery's charge cycle
- External tank + cell stack architecture for true flow battery operation
