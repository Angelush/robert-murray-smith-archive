## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the history and principles of magnetic gears, tracing magnetism from ancient lodestone through to modern second-order magnetic gear designs. He demonstrates a 3D-printed second-order magnetic gear with a 3:1 gear ratio, highlighting that these devices are remarkably easy to fabricate at home without precision machining. The key takeaway is that magnetic gears offer significant practical advantages — no physical contact between components — and can achieve high gear ratios in a compact package.
