## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the history and principles of magnetic gears, tracing magnetism from ancient lodestone through to modern second-order magnetic gear designs. He demonstrates a 3D-printed second-order magnetic gear with a 3:1 gear ratio, highlighting that these devices are remarkably easy to fabricate at home without precision machining. The key takeaway is that magnetic gears offer significant practical advantages — no physical contact between components — and can achieve high gear ratios in a compact package.

## Key Topics
- The mystery and basic physics of magnetism
- Types of magnetism: ferromagnetism, paramagnetism, and diamagnetism
- History of magnetic gears from early couplings (1940s) to second-order designs
- First-order vs. second-order magnetic gears
- Analogy between second-order magnetic gears and strain wave (harmonic drive) gears
- Gear ratio calculation using pole pairs
- Hands-on demonstration of a 3D-printed second-order magnetic gear
- Home workshop fabricability of magnetic gears

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Lodestone (iron(III) oxide / Fe₂O₃)** — naturally magnetic mineral known to the ancients, used for compasses
- **Nickel** — ferromagnetic material
- **Samarium-cobalt / neodymium (implied)** — referenced as ferromagnetic materials ("Samaria near dimia monoxide" — likely samarium, neodymium)
- **Carbon** — diamagnetic material; can be levitated over strong magnets
- **Bismuth** — diamagnetic material; demonstrated floating on strong magnets
- **Copper** — example of a diamagnetic material
- **Iron / soft iron (M8 nuts)** — used as the ferromagnetic flux modulator segments in the middle ring of the gear
- **Steel** — central bearing component in the gear assembly

## Techniques and Methods
- **3D printing** — used to fabricate the three concentric ring gear housing; files made publicly available
- **Flux modulation** — using a ring of soft iron segments between inner and outer magnet rings to create the second-order magnetic gear effect
- **Pole pair configuration** — arranging magnets in alternating north-south orientation; gear ratio determined by ratio of inner to outer pole pairs, with modulator slot count equal to their sum
- **Variable gear ratio selection** — holding different rings stationary to obtain different gear ratios from the same assembly
