## Overview
Robert demonstrates a 3D-printed mechanical device that behaves as the physical analogue of an LC tank circuit: a flywheel (representing an inductor) and a spring (representing a capacitor) exchange energy back and forth, producing a sustained oscillation. The device was inspired by a YouTube creator he credits as "Barley Week One" and is presented as an interesting mechanical curiosity, not a free-energy or perpetual motion machine. It will eventually stop due to friction.

## Key Topics
- LC tank circuits (inductor + capacitor oscillating) as the conceptual basis
- The mechanical analogue: flywheel stores kinetic energy, spring stores potential energy, the two exchange energy just as current oscillates in a tank circuit
- Credit to the original design by "Barley Week One" (spring-powered motor / dominant flywheel)
- 3D-printed construction using TinkerCAD
- Assembly walkthrough of the flywheel cradle
- Tuning the oscillation using magnets as adjustable weights
- Demonstration of the device running freely after a hand-spin

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Barbell weight** — used as the flywheel mass
- **3D-printed parts** — cradle, plug for barbell centre, bearing caps (designed in TinkerCAD)
- **Shelf support pin** — 6 mm × 25 mm, used as axle pin
- **8 mm steel bar** — 50 mm long, axle shaft
- **Skateboard/flanged bearings** — 22 mm × 8 mm (×2), for smooth axle rotation
- **8 mm washers** — used as spacers on either side of the flywheel
- **3 mm bearing caps** — press-fit to retain bearings
- **Super glue** — to fix bearing caps and spring holder
- **Hacksaw blade** — 120 mm length used as the spring element
- **Magnets** — used as sliding tuning weights on the flywheel arm

## Techniques and Methods
- **LC tank circuit analogy** — conceptual mapping of electrical oscillation to mechanical oscillation
- **3D design in TinkerCAD** — parametric part design for cradle, plug, caps, and spring holder
- **FDM 3D printing** — fabrication of all custom structural parts
- **Press-fit assembly** — barbell plug fitted tightly into the weight's centre hole
- **Bearing mounting with washers** — spacers to correctly position flywheel between bearings
- **Spring positioning and gluing** — hacksaw blade slot-fitted and adhesive-fixed so it just catches the flywheel peg
- **Magnet-based tuning** — sliding magnets along the arm to adjust the effective moment of inertia and match the resonant frequency of the spring
