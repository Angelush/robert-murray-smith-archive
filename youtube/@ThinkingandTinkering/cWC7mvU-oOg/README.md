## Overview

Scientists at the University of Nottingham have developed a photocatalytic method to convert atmospheric CO₂ and water into methanol using single copper atoms dispersed in a graphitic carbon nitride matrix, activated by sunlight. The catalyst is 44 times more effective than conventional carbon nitride and, critically, produces methanol rather than the greenhouse gas methane. Robert highlights this as a significant step toward a circular carbon economy using Earth-abundant, scalable materials.
