## Overview
Robert provides a 12-month follow-up review of the Elegoo Neptune 3 Pro 3D printer, reflecting on its real-world performance through daily use. He shares honest observations about minor issues encountered and praises the machine's reliability and customer support, before introducing the upcoming Elegoo Neptune Giga Orangestorm as an exciting development in large-format printing.

## Key Topics
- 12-month real-world performance review of the Elegoo Neptune 3 Pro
- Daily printing habits and material choices (primarily PLA)
- Common issues encountered: nozzle clogs and print bed adhesion problems
- Tips for improving print success (washers for small footprints, warming the bed before large prints)
- Overview of the 3D printing market growth and industry trajectory
- Introduction to the upcoming Elegoo Neptune Giga Orangestorm (large-format printer, Kickstarter launch)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA** — primary filament used; chosen for its lack of smell, de facto standard status, and partial biodegradability
- **PETG** — being trialled for printing transparent lenses
- **ABS** — briefly mentioned and rejected due to off-gassing smell

## Techniques and Methods
- Adding a washer/larger footplate in the design to improve bed adhesion for prints with small bases
- Warming the heated bed before printing large prints that extend to the edges
- Clearing nozzle clogs by unscrewing the hotend, removing plastic manually, and reassembling
- Using a camera and phone app to monitor prints remotely
