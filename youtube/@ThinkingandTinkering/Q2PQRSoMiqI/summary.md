## Overview
This video is a competition entry submitted by Gerald Dupuis from Michigan, USA, for Robert Murray-Smith's water wheel challenge. Gerald demonstrates a small water wheel generator built almost entirely from recycled and reclaimed components, inspired by Robert's earlier videos on water turbines and stepper motor generators. The build successfully generates enough electricity to light an LED.

## Key Topics
- Water wheel / water turbine construction inspired by Robert's video #1447 (Pelton/Poncilla-style)
- Sourcing and repurposing salvaged components for a generator build
- Converting a stepper motor into a generator
- Bridge rectifier wiring to increase output voltage
- Capacitor reclamation from CFL bulbs
- Demonstrating the completed build running outdoors

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Blower/fan motor (squirrel cage type, reclaimed from a car)
- Stepper motors (reclaimed from a $10 colour laser jet printer, ~8 motors total)
- 3D-printed rod used as an axle
- JB Weld (epoxy adhesive used to fix shaft into axle)
- Diodes (new, purchased as a pack)
- Bridge rectifiers (built from diodes, wired in series)
- Capacitors (reclaimed from burned-out CFL light bulbs)
- LED (reclaimed from a $1 solar yard stake light)
- Plexiglass (used as a splash guard/motor cover)
- Wiring (reclaimed from the printer)
- Wood (used for structural framing/bracket)

## Techniques and Methods
- Reclaiming stepper motors from discarded printers for use as generators
- Cutting a squirrel cage fan spindle with a hacksaw and bonding it into a 3D-printed axle with JB Weld
- Wiring multiple bridge rectifiers in series to increase output voltage
- Salvaging capacitors from CFL bulb circuit boards
- Using a notched wooden bracket to mount the stepper motor
- Protecting the motor from water spray with a slotted plexiglass shield
