## Overview
Robert unboxes and reviews the Tilswall 160W rotary multi-tool (Model S1J-FE7-10), sent to him by the company for an honest review. He compares it directly against his Dremel, highlighting two standout features — an attachable pistol grip handle and user-replaceable motor brushes — and concludes it compares favourably to the Dremel at a lower price point.

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Mystery batteries solved:** Robert confirms he eventually worked out that the tiny batteries power an LED worklight built into the attachable handle.
- **Dremel brush cover criticism:** Agrees with commenters that Dremel's sealed brush cover is "a really poor decision."
- **Universal bits confirmed:** Confirms the bits are standard/universal, fitting the same chuck as Dremel accessories.
- **Repairability quip:** If the tool breaks, he'll "cannibalise it" for parts — and notes it is a German-designed product made in China, drawing a comparison to Apple.
- **Price perspective:** Reacts with surprise when a commenter mentions a similar tool at £18, calling it "half the price of this tool."
- **General enthusiasm:** Repeatedly agrees with commenters that rotary multi-tools are invaluable for small fiddly jobs that would be awkward without them.
