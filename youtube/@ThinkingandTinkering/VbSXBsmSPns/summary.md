## Overview
Robert tests the self-discharge rate of his FWG supercapacitor, comparing results against a commercial GreenCap 2.7V 500F unit and Maxwell's published datasheet specifications. His prototype loses 10% charge in the first 24 hours and approximately 13% over three days — performance comparable to Maxwell's spec of 80% retention over 30 days — while the commercial GreenCap unit performed poorly, losing 50% of its energy in the first 24 hours. The video also serves as an update on FWG's move toward commercial production and early distribution plans.

## Key Topics
- Self-discharge rate testing methodology (charge, measure, wait, repeat over 3 days)
- Comparison of FWG supercapacitor vs. GreenCap 500F commercial unit
- Comparison against Maxwell supercapacitor datasheet specifications
- Weight advantage of FWG device (15g vs. 60g for the GreenCap)
- Plans to hire manufacturing staff and begin commercial production
- Early distribution/sales process for a "selected few" testers and buyers
- Mention of YouTuber Julian Ilett as a planned independent reviewer

## Techniques and Methods
- **Constant-voltage charge then open-circuit voltage monitoring** — device charged to full voltage, then left disconnected and voltage measured at 24-hour intervals over 3 days
- **Comparison against manufacturer datasheets** — FWG actual performance benchmarked against Maxwell's published theoretical specifications
- **Laser cutting** — mentioned in comments as the method used to cut connection plates for the devices
- **Functional testing philosophy** — Robert explicitly prefers real-world functional testing over fixed laboratory metrics

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Electrolyte choice**: FWG uses water-based electrolytes deliberately, for safety and cost reasons, though this limits performance ("hamstrings" them). He is aware that higher-performance options like tetraalkylammonium fluoride salts in ether solvents exist.
- **Production/QC approach**: His plan is for plates or inks to come from FWG directly, which helps ensure quality control.
- **Distribution policy**: He only sends devices to people who specifically ask, as unsolicited samples tend to sit unused and production capacity is limited.
- **Functional testing philosophy**: He prefers testing devices as they would actually be used rather than against fixed laboratory metrics.
- **Maxwell comparison clarification**: He acknowledges that Maxwell's 80% retention in 30 days is their spec, and FWG appears to track similarly. He notes 1000F at 2.7V stores more energy than 1000F at 1.8V, and prefers expressing energy in coulombs/joules though uses mWh for accessibility.
- **Thin form factor feasibility**: Thinner devices are possible in principle, but manual handling during current production makes thin materials impractical; more optimisation is still needed.
- **Timeline for commercial availability**: He is deliberately cautious about giving timelines, noting other factors always intervene.
- **Starlite material**: He mentions FWG previously made a carbon foam with similar insulating properties (links a prior video), confirming he believes Starlite is real.
- **Laser cutter repair**: A PC running the laser cutter broke and had to be fixed before he could cut connection plates — a practical production bottleneck mentioned to explain delays.
