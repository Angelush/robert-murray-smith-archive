## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a zinc-iodine "hairy battery" using cheap, readily available materials as an alternative to zinc-bromide batteries. The battery uses Lugol solution (iodine dissolved in potassium iodide) as the electrolyte, with a graphite or carbon fiber positive electrode and a zinc negative electrode, separated by a terracotta pot. The key takeaway is that this chemistry offers 3–4× higher energy density than lithium-ion (~422 Wh/kg) and can be constructed at home from low-hazard materials.
