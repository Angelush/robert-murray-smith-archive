## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a zinc-iodine "hairy battery" using cheap, readily available materials as an alternative to zinc-bromide batteries. The battery uses Lugol solution (iodine dissolved in potassium iodide) as the electrolyte, with a graphite or carbon fiber positive electrode and a zinc negative electrode, separated by a terracotta pot. The key takeaway is that this chemistry offers 3–4× higher energy density than lithium-ion (~422 Wh/kg) and can be constructed at home from low-hazard materials.

## Key Topics
- Why iodine was chosen over bromine (availability issues with zinc bromide)
- Chemistry of Lugol solution (triiodide formation) and its role as electrolyte
- Cell construction using carbon fiber cloth, zinc strip, and a terracotta pot separator
- Self-discharge problem and the mechanical solution (removing the zinc electrode)
- Dendrite formation on recharge and how the terracotta pot mitigates it
- Energy density comparison: ~422 Wh/kg vs. ~100–150 Wh/kg for lithium-ion
- Limitations: low cell voltage (1.2 V), lower power density due to large geometry
- Connection to current academic research on flow batteries and Mg/Al battery disconnection strategies

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Iodine (I₂)** — solid, used as the active positive material; purchased in 500 g jars
- **Potassium iodide (KI)** — salt dissolved in water to create the electrolyte; purchased on Amazon
- **Lugol solution** — mixture of iodine + potassium iodide in water, forming triiodide (I₃⁻); acts as the electrolyte/catholyte; WHO-listed essential medicine
- **Zinc iodide (ZnI₂)** — the clear discharge product formed when the battery is fully discharged
- **Zinc (Zn)** — negative electrode material, used as a strip
- **Graphite foil** — positive electrode material (alternative)
- **Carbon fiber cloth (woven)** — positive electrode material, used as a practical alternative to graphite foil
- **Terracotta (unglazed clay pot)** — used as an ion-permeable membrane/separator between electrodes

## Techniques and Methods
- Dissolving iodine in potassium iodide solution to produce Lugol solution (triiodide complex)
- Using woven carbon fiber cloth stuffed into a container as the positive electrode
- Inserting a zinc strip inside a terracotta pot placed within the electrolyte as the negative electrode
- Using an unglazed terracotta pot as a ceramic membrane separator to prevent electrode contact and suppress dendrite growth
- Mechanical disconnection (physically removing the zinc electrode) to eliminate self-discharge when not in use
- Recharging by applying external current (zinc deposits back onto negative electrode, iodine regenerated at positive)
