## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith examines the INN Engine by eRex Motors, a novel internal combustion engine design from Granada, Spain, which uses opposing pistons connected to rollers that drive a hyperbolic paraboloid cam rather than a traditional crankshaft. He builds a 3D-printed model of the mechanism in Tinkercad to demonstrate how it works, and contextualises it within the broader debate about the future of petrol engines and the practicality of full electrification.
