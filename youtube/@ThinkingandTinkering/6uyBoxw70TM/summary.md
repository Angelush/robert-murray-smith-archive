## Overview
Robert explains how to decode and use salvaged windscreen wiper motors for machine automation projects. He demystifies the wiring by breaking it down into just two core functions — the park mechanism and speed control — showing that despite the variety of wire counts across different motors, the underlying logic is straightforward.

## Key Topics
- Why wiper motors are appealing for DIY automation (strong, cheap, widely available)
- The two core functions: park (home position) and variable speed
- OR gate logic used to implement the park/home function
- How the park switch works (cam or plate contacting two prongs)
- Speed control via multiple motor coil taps
- Identifying wires by measuring resistance with a multimeter
- Motor direction reversal by swapping polarity

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Windscreen wiper motor (salvaged/scrap)
- Battery (12V, implied)
- Normally open switches (for OR gate demo)

## Techniques and Methods
- Using a multimeter to measure resistance between wires and motor case to identify ground, speed, and park wires
- Wiring two switches in parallel to demonstrate OR gate logic
- Testing motor speed by connecting different wire combinations
- Reversing motor direction by swapping polarity
