## Overview
This video demonstrates how to build a DIY heated coaster using conductive ink painted onto a ceramic tile, designed to keep a cup of coffee at a stable warm temperature. The coaster is powered via USB at 5V/1A and incorporates a thermal cutout switch to regulate temperature around 40–41°C. The finished device successfully maintained coffee temperature without letting it go cold.

## Key Topics
- Motivation: coffee going cold during project work sessions
- Designing a heated coaster using conductive ink on a ceramic tile
- Applying copper tape strips as electrodes
- Painting conductive ink as the resistive heating element
- Protecting the surface with outdoor varnish
- Powering the coaster via USB (5V, 1A)
- Using a thermal cutout switch for temperature regulation
- Testing and verifying temperature performance with a thermometer

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Blank art tile — used as the base (heat-tolerant substrate)
- Copper tape — used as electrical contact strips along the edges
- Conductive ink — painted onto the tile as the resistive heating element
- Baby blue paint — applied to the border for aesthetics
- Outdoor varnish — protective topcoat over the ink
- Thermal cutout switch — wired in series to cut power at 40°C

## Techniques and Methods
- Masking off a border (1.5 cm) around the tile perimeter before painting
- Brushing conductive ink onto the tile surface
- Wiring copper tape strips as electrodes to the conductive ink layer
- Series wiring a thermal cutout switch into the live circuit for over-temperature protection
- USB power supply connection (5V, 1A)
- Infrared/contact thermometer testing of ambient, coffee, and plate temperatures
