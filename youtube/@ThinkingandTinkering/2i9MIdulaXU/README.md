## Overview

This video is part two of Robert's conductive thin films project, in which he needs aluminium chloride to dope zinc oxide. Rather than simply reacting aluminium with hydrochloric acid, he chooses the more interesting route of reacting aluminium with warm sodium hydroxide, which vigorously produces hydrogen gas and sodium aluminate as intermediates. He walks through the full chemistry and proposes the reaction as a practical method for hydrogen generation, outlining a simple aluminium-foil-based hydrogen fuel cell design.
