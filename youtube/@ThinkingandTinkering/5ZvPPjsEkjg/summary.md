## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explains the fundamental operating principles of electrical generators, demonstrating that motors and generators are exactly the same device. Using a copper pipe and magnets to illustrate Lenz's Law, and a 12V DC motor and washing machine pump motor as practical examples, he shows how voltage, current, back-EMF, and counter-torque are all interconnected — and how to improve generator output by modifying wire length, magnet strength, or rotation speed.

## Key Topics
- Lenz's Law demonstrated with magnets falling through a copper pipe
- Motors and generators as identical devices
- Back-EMF and counter-torque: why turning a generator requires increasing effort as load increases
- Ohm's Law (V = IR) applied to generator output and resistive loads
- Minimum voltage threshold required to drive a circuit
- Why starter motors make poor generators (short, thick wire, low RPM)
- Rewinding motors with thinner, longer wire to increase voltage output
- Three ways to improve generator output: stronger magnets, more wire turns, higher RPM
- Real power vs. apparent/reactive power (briefly noted, not covered in depth)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Permanent magnets (neodymium-type implied, used in demonstrations)
- Copper pipe (used for Lenz's Law demonstration)
- 12V DC motor (disassembled to show commutator, rotor coils, and permanent magnets)
- Washing machine pump motor (brushless DC, used as generator demo)
- Filament lamp / incandescent light bulb (used as resistive load)
- Thin enamelled copper wire (fine-wound coils in washing machine motor)

## Techniques and Methods
- Demonstrating Lenz's Law using magnets dropped through a copper pipe
- Disassembling a 12V DC motor to identify generator components
- Hand-spinning a washing machine pump motor to generate enough voltage to light a filament lamp
- Using a filament bulb as a simple resistive load to visualise generator output
- Rewinding motor coils with thinner, longer wire to increase output voltage
- Qualitative comparison of generator performance at varying RPM
