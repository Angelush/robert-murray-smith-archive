## Overview
Robert revisits his Elegoo Mars 2 Pro resin printer to share an honest update on his ongoing attempts to create conductive 3D-printing resin by adding graphene — experiments that have so far failed. As a pivot, he demonstrates successfully printing a functional Fresnel lens using a plant-based translucent resin, proving that resin printers can produce working optical components.

## Key Topics
- Progress update on conductive resin research using graphene additives
- Comparison of four resin types: ABS-like photopolymer, standard photopolymer, plant-based translucent, and water-washable photopolymer
- Smell and usability characteristics of different resins
- 3D printing a Fresnel lens from a Thingiverse STL file
- The PTFE "buttering" trick for preventing print delamination
- Post-processing with IPA wash and UV curing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **ABS-like photopolymer resin** — initial resin used for conductive experiments; described as a failure
- **Standard photopolymer resin** — tested with graphene additive
- **Plant-based translucent photopolymer resin** — low odour; successfully used to print the Fresnel lens
- **Water-washable photopolymer resin** — smells similar to PVA/Elmer's glue; also tested with graphene
- **Graphene** — added at 0.5–1% concentrations (and higher) to resins in an attempt to impart conductivity; blocks UV curing when present in sufficient quantity
- **IPA (isopropyl alcohol)** — used for washing prints post-cure
- **PTFE** — applied to the build plate ("buttering") to prevent delamination
- **AZO (aluminium-doped zinc oxide)** — mentioned in author replies as a potential transparent conductive material to disperse in water-washable resin
- **MCF (multi-walled carbon fibre?)** — referenced in author replies as something Robert has tried with the same unsuccessful results

## Techniques and Methods
- Resin 3D printing using an Elegoo Mars 2 Pro MSLA printer
- Slicing with Chitubox software
- PTFE plate "buttering" technique to prevent print delamination
- IPA wash followed by UV curing for post-processing
- Graphene doping of photopolymer resins to attempt conductivity

## Robert's Own Replies
- **On conductivity approaches:** Robert clarifies that applying a conductive coating externally is not the real challenge — the goal is a *single-step, low-cost* process that produces bulk conductivity in the printed part.
- **On ionic conductivity suggestion:** He notes that ionic conductivity (as in electrolysis) would work but wouldn't be durable long-term.
- **On AZO:** Robert himself floated the idea of dispersing AZO (aluminium-doped zinc oxide) in the water-washable resin as a possible path to transparent conductivity — suggesting this as his own next avenue to investigate.
- **On MCF:** He confirmed he has tried multi-walled carbon fibre and got the same unsuccessful results others reported, but found the comments thread interesting for revisiting the problem.
- **On delamination:** He credits the PTFE buttering technique for his clean print record — no delamination so far — and points to an earlier video for the full method.
- **On other lens types:** He believes the translucent resin could print convex lenses as well, given the Fresnel result.
- **On metal-doped resins:** He questioned whether 20% metal loading would simply settle out in the resin bath rather than remaining dispersed.
