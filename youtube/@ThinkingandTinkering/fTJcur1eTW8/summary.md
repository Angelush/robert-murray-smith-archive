## Overview
This video demonstrates how to make a simple slow-burning fuse using only three readily available ingredients: potassium nitrate, sugar, and absorbent string. The presenter makes the fuse to ignite a model rocket and tests two different string types, finding both burn approximately 5 cm per minute.

## Key Topics
- Motivation: rebuilding and finally launching a model rocket
- Selecting appropriate absorbent string materials
- Preparing a potassium nitrate/sugar solution
- Soaking and drying the fuse string
- Burn rate testing and comparison of string types

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Potassium nitrate (KNO₃)** — oxidizer, used at 60% of the dry mixture
- **Sugar** — fuel component, used at 40% of the dry mixture
- **Water** — solvent; 250 mL used to dissolve 30 g of the KNO₃/sugar mixture
- **Natural twine** — absorbent string used as primary fuse material
- **Cotton string** — second string type tested
- **J cloth** — mentioned as a thin alternative to string (burns faster due to lower thickness)

## Techniques and Methods
- Grinding KNO₃ and sugar to fine powder using a mortar and pestle
- Dissolving the powder mixture in water to form a concentrated solution
- Soaking string lengths in the solution for 1–2 hours (longer soak = faster burn)
- Air-drying the soaked string outdoors
- Timed burn testing of 5 cm fuse lengths to characterise burn rate
