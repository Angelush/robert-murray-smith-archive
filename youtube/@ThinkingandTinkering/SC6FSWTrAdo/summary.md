## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates his homemade "Ecofoam" dry-powder carpet cleaner, part of a line of eco-friendly cleaning products he developed. He shows it cleaning a heavily soiled carpet using just two tablespoons of powder dissolved in hot water, applied with a cloth — no scrubbing brush required. The product is notable for being gentle on hands while still cutting through heavy grime.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the Ecofoam carpet cleaner as part of a broader product line
- Motivation: cleaning graphite, grease, and grime from his work environment
- Eco-friendly, dry powder format to reduce plastic and liquid waste
- Dosage instructions for heavy vs. light soiling
- Demonstration on a heavily used, visibly dirty carpet
- Skin-safe formulation with a hand-softening additive
- Alternative application method: air foamer bottle with the related hard surface cleaner

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Ecofoam carpet cleaner (dry powder formulation, unspecified composition)
- Unspecified hand-softening additive incorporated into the cleaner
- Hot water (used to dissolve the powder)

## Techniques and Methods
- Dissolving powder in a bowl of hot water (2 tablespoons per bowl for heavy soiling; 1 tablespoon for light soiling)
- Applying solution with a cloth and rubbing directly onto carpet
- Scrubbing brush as an optional alternative to cloth application
- Air foamer bottle method: 1 teaspoon of powder added to bottle with hot water, foamed onto surface
