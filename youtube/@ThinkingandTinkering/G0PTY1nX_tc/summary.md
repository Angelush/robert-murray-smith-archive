## Overview
In this short Christmas advent calendar video (Day 15), Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simple but striking science trick: bent toothpicks slowly straighten themselves out when a drop of water is placed at the bend. He explains that wood fibres absorb the water and the material tries to return to its original straight shape, calling it "science magic."

## Key Topics
- Surface tension and capillary absorption in wood
- The mechanics of why bent (but unbroken) wood straightens when wetted
- Brief note that pasta exhibits the same behaviour

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Toothpicks (bent but not broken)
- Water (applied as drops at the bend points)
- Pasta (mentioned as an analogy — absorbs water and expands/straightens similarly)

## Techniques and Methods
- Bending toothpicks in half without fully breaking them
- Applying small spots of water precisely at the bent/stressed points
- Observing passive material response over time (no active manipulation after wetting)
