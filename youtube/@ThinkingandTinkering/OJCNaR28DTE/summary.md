## Overview
Robert demonstrates a proof-of-concept magnetically levitated, near-frictionless wind turbine bearing inspired by the ballpoint pen bearing from a 1994 video. Using 3D-printed parts, a 12mm steel ball bearing, and neodymium magnets, he constructs a low-friction pivot that keeps the rotating element stable without wobbling. The build culminates in a small wind turbine that successfully lights an LED when blown upon, validating the concept.

## Key Topics
- Limitations of simple magnetic floating pen bearings (wobble/bounce under rotation)
- Ballpoint pen ball bearing as a low-friction pivot concept
- Scaling the pen-tip bearing concept to a larger 3D-printed structure
- Combining magnetic levitation with ball bearing contact to eliminate wobble
- Using the assembly as the hub of a wind turbine with a generating disk
- Proof-of-concept electricity generation from wind (lighting an LED)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **12mm steel ball bearing** — used as the pivot/friction bearing at the core of the mount
- **Neodymium magnets (5mm × 10mm)** — primary levitation/repulsion magnets
- **Neodymium magnets (15mm × 3mm)** — used inside the printed housing structure
- **Neodymium magnets (10mm × 2mm, north-south alternating × 8)** — arranged on the generating disk for electricity generation
- **3D-printed PLA parts** — housing, clip, and turbine disk (designed in Tinkercad)
- **Copper coil** — used to capture the changing magnetic field and generate current
- **LED** — used to demonstrate electrical output

## Techniques and Methods
- Designing parts in **Tinkercad** and 3D printing them
- Press-fitting a ball bearing into a printed clip to replicate a ballpoint pen tip at larger scale
- Embedding neodymium magnets in opposing orientations to create vertical magnetic lift while constraining lateral movement via ball contact
- Arranging alternating north-south magnets on a rotating disk for AC generation
- Hand-held coil induction test — holding a coil against the spinning magnet disk to verify LED illumination
