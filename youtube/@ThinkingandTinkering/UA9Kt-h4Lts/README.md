## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates diode-like behavior in a graphene sheet made with a blender and soap, dried on a plastic substrate with copper strips. By applying a DC bias and swapping polarity, he shows a dramatic asymmetry in resistance (~20+ MΩ in one direction, ~8 MΩ in the other), consistent with rectifying behavior. He concludes this suggests a simple, printable graphene diode is achievable, and notes the effect is stronger when graphene lines run parallel to the conductive strips.
