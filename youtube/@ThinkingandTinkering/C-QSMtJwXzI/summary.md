## Overview
Robert completes his steampunk PC build by constructing a fake cathode ray tube (CRT) display using a flat panel LCD monitor dressed up with salvaged CRT components. He builds a decorative CRT shell from cardboard, vacuum cleaner hose, and resin, then finishes it with a conductive metallic ink for a polished steampunk aesthetic. The completed all-in-one "Victorian PC" is functional and intended to become his video editing machine.

## Key Topics
- Designing a fake CRT display for a steampunk PC build
- Salvaging deflection coils and electron gun housing from an old Apple iMac CRT
- Building a CRT shell from low-cost materials
- Applying a decorative metallic finish using conductive ink
- Mounting the finished display into the steampunk PC frame

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Flat panel LCD monitor — used as the actual display, disguised as a CRT
- Old Apple iMac CRT — donor unit; deflection coils and electron gun connector harvested from it
- Vacuum cleaner hose — used as the structural tube forming the CRT "neck"
- Cardboard — used to form curved infill panels on the CRT body
- Resin — applied over cardboard to harden and stiffen the shell
- Conductive/decorative ink — painted over the shell to produce a shiny metallic finish; same ink used on the computer base
- Cintra board (builders board) — used to construct the PC enclosure, cut with a carpet knife and saw

## Techniques and Methods
- Salvaging and repurposing CRT deflection coils as decorative elements
- Forming curved structures using cardboard templates coated with resin
- Gluing vacuum cleaner hose sections to create a CRT neck/gun assembly
- Painting with conductive ink and polishing to achieve a glass-like metallic appearance
- Mounting LCD panel into a custom frame using clips
