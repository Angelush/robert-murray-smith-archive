## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a DIY solar thermal kettle using an evacuated solar thermal tube, 3D-printed parts, and curved reflectors made from household guttering. He explains how evacuated tubes work — including their getter, vacuum insulation, and magnetron-sputtered black absorber coating — and demonstrates the finished kettle successfully heating water to make coffee, though it takes around 80 minutes. The video argues that solar thermal is a highly efficient, underutilised technology that makes a practical alternative to a 3 kW electric kettle.
