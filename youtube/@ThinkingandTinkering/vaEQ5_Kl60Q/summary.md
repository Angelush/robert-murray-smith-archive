## Overview
Robert demonstrates how to build a simple, low-cost multi-function generator using a high-voltage, high-speed motor salvaged from a hand blender or detail sander. He attaches a blower fan to the motor and shows it generating electricity from three energy sources: wind (hairdryer), friction, and water. The core takeaway is that high RPM-per-volt motors make excellent generators and this build costs around £7 and takes about 10 minutes.

## Key Topics
- High-voltage, high-speed motors from hand blenders and detail sanders as generator candidates
- The concept of RPM-per-volt as a key metric for selecting generator motors
- Attaching a blower fan to a motor shaft using a terminal block connector
- Demonstrating generation from wind, friction, and water sources
- Powering an LED strip as a load demonstration
- Energy storage options (supercapacitors, batteries)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- High-voltage, high-speed motor (~240V, 10,000–18,000 RPM) from a hand blender or detail sander — used as the generator
- Drone motor (~3.7V, ~12,000 RPM) — mentioned as a comparison (lower RPM-per-volt)
- Blower fan (conic section type) — attached to the motor as the turbine
- Terminal block connector — used to couple the fan to the motor rotor
- LED strip (~12V, ~15 LEDs) — used as the electrical load
- Tube/pipe section — used as a coupling adapter
- Supercapacitor — mentioned as an option for energy storage
- Battery — mentioned as an option for energy storage

## Techniques and Methods
- Salvaging motors from consumer appliances (hand blenders, detail sanders)
- Coupling a blower fan to a motor shaft using a tube adapter and terminal block connector
- Using a hairdryer as a wind source to spin the fan/generator
- Testing generation via friction (direct contact driving)
- Testing generation via water stream
- Connecting generator output directly to an LED strip for live demonstration
