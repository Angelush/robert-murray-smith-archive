## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents the completed homemade ukulele he has been building, walking through the final assembly details and fret spacings. He demonstrates how to correctly measure fret positions (always from the nut, not fret-to-fret) and gives a brief playthrough to show how it sounds. The video serves as a practical conclusion to his ukulele-building project.

## Key Topics
- Correct fret spacing measurement technique
- Exact fret position measurements for a 33 cm scale ukulele
- Securing components with superglue and small bolts
- A short demonstration of the finished instrument being played

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Superglue — used to hold components in place before mechanical fastening

## Techniques and Methods
- Using an online scale calculator to determine fret spacings
- Measuring fret positions from the nut (not fret-to-fret) to avoid compounding errors
- Securing the bridge/nut with superglue, then reinforcing with small bolts through the underside
- Trimming bolt ends flush after fastening
