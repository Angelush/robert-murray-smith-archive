## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith combines two previously built machines — a hand-cranked flywheel device (from video 700) and a blender attachment unit (from video 1100) — to create a mechanical, hand-powered smoothie maker. He demonstrates the build and a live smoothie test, arguing the device has genuine commercial potential as both a kitchen product and a street-vending tool.

## Key Topics
- Recap of prior builds: the exercise bike/flywheel generator (video 700) and the dinopod blender conversion (video 1100)
- Combining the two machines into a single hand-cranked blender
- Mechanical coupling via drive belt and pulley system
- Commercial viability as a kitchen product or portable smoothie business
- Live smoothie demonstration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Strawberries, banana, and milk (smoothie ingredients)

## Techniques and Methods
- Removing and replacing a central axle on the flywheel unit
- Adding a ring/groove to the flywheel to act as a pulley and retain the drive belt
- Removing circlips to disassemble and reassemble the flywheel assembly
- Fitting a drive belt between the flywheel and blender pulley
- Hand-cranking the flywheel to drive the blender at high RPM
