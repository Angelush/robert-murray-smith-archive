## Overview
Robert introduces his plan to build an electric trike with a paper frame — a follow-up to his earlier paper bike project. He shares free trike plans (based on the Catrike Expedition's geometry), explains his design choice of two rear wheels and one front wheel, and previews the upcoming build series. The key takeaway is that redesigned paper "sticks," a cheap rear axle, and a 500W motor will combine into a trike he intends to actually keep rather than recycle for parts.

## Key Topics
- Lessons learned from the original paper bike build
- Design choice: delta configuration (two wheels rear, one front) vs. tadpole configuration (two wheels front, one rear)
- Free trike plans based on Catrike Expedition measurements, scaled for a 5'8" rider
- Redesigned paper construction sticks incorporating community suggestions
- Component overview: rear axle, drive cog, and 500W electric motor
- Preview of upcoming trike build video series and a race between Robert and Luke

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Kraft paper** — primary structural material for the frame (confirmed in author comments)
- **Rear axle** (purchased from Funbikes, £20) — with two flanges: one for a cog, one for a brake disc
- **68-tooth cog** (6mm pitch) — driven sprocket on the rear axle
- **Drive cog** (small, on motor) — gives a 6:1 gear ratio

## Techniques and Methods
- Paper "stick" fabrication — rolling/forming paper into structural members (redesigned version with community input)
- Frame geometry adaptation — copying Catrike Expedition angles and dimensions, then modifying for delta (two-rear-wheel) layout
- Gear ratio calculation — matching motor cog to rear axle cog for a 6:1 drive ratio
- Plan drawing and sharing — hand-drawn dimensional plans shown on screen for viewers to copy
