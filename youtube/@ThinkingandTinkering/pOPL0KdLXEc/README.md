## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a 3D-printed diaphragm piston air engine featuring a custom rotary valve, following up on his earlier standalone diaphragm piston video (2213). The engine uses compressed air or vacuum to drive a reciprocating piston, with the rotary valve controlling the air flow direction. After assembly, the engine is successfully run using a household vacuum cleaner, demonstrating the concept works — though Robert notes the rotary valve introduced pressure loss and required significant fitting and lubrication work.
