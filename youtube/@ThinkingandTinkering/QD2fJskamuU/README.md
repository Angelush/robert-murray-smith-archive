## Overview

Robert builds a working 3D-printed demonstration model of the Constantinescu torque converter, invented by George Constantinescu roughly 100 years ago, to explain how it achieves continuously variable transmission through a pendulum-based mechanical mechanism. The core insight is that the pendulum acts as a temporary energy store, allowing the engine to maintain constant speed against variable resistance loads. Robert argues this largely forgotten design is genuinely ingenious and more elegant than modern fluid-based or stepped-gear solutions.
