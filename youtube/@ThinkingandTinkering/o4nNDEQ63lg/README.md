## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the phenomenon of creating a fire vortex using angled blades or slots around a small flame, investigating the critical geometry needed to sustain it. He demonstrates that a specific number of blades at a specific angle is required, and successfully creates a powerful vortex using pieces cut from a fire extinguisher cylinder. The video serves as a proof-of-concept investigation ahead of building a full vortex stove.
