## Overview
Robert demonstrates a variable hypocycloid mechanism — an evolution of his earlier hypocycloid engine (video 2327) — where a worm gear allows the stroke length to be adjusted independently of rotation speed. By exploiting the Tan's couple property (inner gear diameter exactly half the outer), and adding a rotatable inner ring, the effective stroke can be varied continuously from zero to maximum, giving the mechanism the essential properties of a CVT (continuously variable transmission).

## Key Topics
- The hypocycloid and the Tan's couple (inner circle diameter = half outer diameter produces straight-line motion)
- How rotating the inner ring via a worm gear changes the direction and length of the output stroke
- Constraining the output pin with a sliding bar to extract variable linear motion
- The mechanism as the basis for a continuously variable transmission (CVT)
- Credit to YouTube channel "Steamboat Ed" (Edward Har) for suggesting the variation
- 3D-printed files available on Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 10 mm acrylic bar (used as the constraining rod for the slide)
- 3D-printed parts (reprinted back, front, base, flywheel, cap, center, drive gear, outer ring, worm gear, square slide)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing all mechanical components (parts uploaded to Thingiverse, including a printable 10 mm rod as an alternative to the acrylic bar)
- Worm gear adjustment to rotate the inner ring and vary the stroke direction/length
- Degree markings (0–360) on the outer ring for precise angular positioning
- Constraining a drive pin with a sliding square guide and rod to convert rotary motion into variable-length linear motion
