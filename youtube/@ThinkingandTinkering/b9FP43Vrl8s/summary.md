## Overview
Robert takes his exercise bike wind turbine — built in a previous video — outside on a genuinely windy day to test whether it can produce useful power. He connects a 24V, 90-LED strip directly to the modified microwave oven transformer coil with no intermediate circuitry, successfully lighting the LEDs in the dark. The video is a brief, real-world proof-of-concept rather than a polished build, demonstrating that the setup can generate meaningful voltage from wind alone.

## Key Topics
- Follow-up test to the "Make a Wind Turbine from an Exercise Bike" video
- Real-world outdoor wind power generation test
- AC output directly driving LED strips (no rectifier or smoothing capacitor)
- Voltage output: achieving 24V to drive the LED strip
- One neodymium magnet coming loose during the test

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Exercise bike** — repurposed as the turbine rotor/frame
- **Microwave oven transformer (MOT)** — modified to act as the generator coil (primary coil removed, secondary coil used for output)
- **2 cm neodymium N35 magnets** — glued around the exercise bike wheel to induce current as they pass the coil
- **24V LED strip** — 90 LEDs used as the electrical load for the test

## Techniques and Methods
- **MOT modification**: cutting the top off the transformer, removing the primary coil, and using the secondary coil as a pickup coil positioned near the rotating magnets
- **Direct connection**: clipping the LED strip straight to the transformer output with no rectifier or smoothing capacitor ("bare bones" test)
- **Implicit half-wave rectification**: LEDs inherently only conduct in one direction, so the AC output is passively half-wave rectified by the load itself
- **Outdoor field test**: running the turbine in natural wind conditions at night to make LED output clearly visible

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly brief and social, but a few add genuine technical and philosophical context:

- **On the electrical setup**: He clarifies that no external rectifier was needed — *"it already has half wave rectification - they are LEDs"* — since LEDs only conduct in one direction, the AC output is inherently half-wave rectified by the load.
- **On the bare-bones approach**: He repeatedly emphasises this was intentionally minimal — *"I wanted a 'bare bones' test"* and *"you could add a cap for smoothing - but I wanted bare bones."* He confirms it is *"just a test piece."*
- **On economics**: *"To me it's all about cost per kWh - I only paid for the magnets here - so the generation cost will certainly be low."*
- **On single-coil design**: When asked why only one coil, he replied simply: *"No real reason mate - I just wanted a single coil is all."*
- **On further development**: He points viewers toward his charge controller playlist for deeper follow-up on practical energy storage and regulation.
- **On Sturmey-Archer dynamos**: He mentions the UK brand in the context of hub dynamos but admits he doesn't know much about their specific output figures.
