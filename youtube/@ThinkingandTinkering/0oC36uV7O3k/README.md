## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simple adsorption chiller built from two paint cans connected by a tube, filled with water and zeolite respectively. By pulling a vacuum, water evaporates at ambient temperature in one can (cooling it) while the vapor is absorbed by the zeolite in the other (heating it). He explains how this same principle underpins desalination, solid-state Stirling engines, and thermal batteries.
