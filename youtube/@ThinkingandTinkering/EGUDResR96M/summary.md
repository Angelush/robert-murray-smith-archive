## Overview
This video demonstrates converting a TENG (Triboelectric Nanogenerator) — previously featured in episode 1485 — into a wind-powered energy harvester. The presenter builds a simple enclosure using a food storage container and rubber band suspension, tests it outdoors, and achieves a peak of ~1.8 volts in real wind conditions. The experiment shows the concept is viable, with room for further optimisation.

## Key Topics
- Follow-up to a previous TENG generator video (episode 1485), responding to viewer requests
- Indoor simulation of wind using a hairdryer to baseline performance
- Designing a wind-capture housing from a repurposed food storage container
- Suspending the TENG on rubber bands to allow vibration/flapping from airflow
- Adding an angled cardboard deflector to direct wind onto the TENG plates
- Outdoor real-world wind testing and voltage measurement

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- TENG (Triboelectric Nanogenerator) — two flexible plates that generate charge when rubbed together
- Rectifier — used to convert AC output from the TENG to DC
- Multimeter — for measuring output voltage
- Hairdryer — used indoors to simulate wind
- Square food storage container — repurposed as the wind-capture enclosure
- Rubber bands — used to suspend the TENG inside the container to allow movement
- Cardboard — cut and angled to deflect wind downward onto the TENG plates

## Techniques and Methods
- Triboelectric nanogeneration — harvesting energy from mechanical contact/friction between two surfaces
- AC-to-DC rectification of TENG output
- Hairdryer wind simulation for controlled indoor baseline testing
- Elastic suspension mounting (rubber bands) to maximise TENG plate vibration from airflow
- Passive wind deflection using an angled cardboard baffle
- Outdoor open-air voltage measurement with a multimeter
