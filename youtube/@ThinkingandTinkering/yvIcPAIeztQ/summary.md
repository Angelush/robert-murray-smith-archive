## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith introduces mechanochemical synthesis — a solvent-minimal, eco-friendly chemistry technique that uses mechanical force (grinding, milling, shearing) to drive chemical reactions. He demonstrates the synthesis of copper nanoparticles and copper formate by grinding copper(I) oxide with formic acid in an Indian spice grinder. The resulting material has a wide range of practical applications including copper plating, conductive inks, and nanoparticle production, and is accessible to home chemists.

## Key Topics
- What mechanochemical synthesis is and why it matters (green chemistry, solvent-free, low temperature)
- How mechanical force acts as a catalyst by breaking kinetic barriers and promoting molecular rearrangement
- Applications across pharmaceuticals, nanomaterials, polymers, ceramics, MOFs, and composites
- Practical synthesis of copper formate and copper nanoparticles using a consumer spice grinder
- Uses of the resulting material: copper plating on steel, conductive organometallic inks for inkjet printing, copper nanoparticle/nanorod formation in graphite/carbon matrices
- Safety considerations for handling 85% formic acid

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Copper(I) oxide (Cu₂O)** — red powder; the copper source, reduced by formic acid under mechanochemical conditions
- **Copper(II) oxide (CuO)** — black powder; mentioned for comparison but not used in this experiment
- **Formic acid (85%)** — powerful lachrymator; reacts with copper oxide to form copper formate and copper particles
- **Copper formate** — blue product of the reaction; thermally decomposes at ~200°C to yield copper, CO₂, and water
- **Copper nanoparticles** — black; produced by thermal decomposition of copper formate
- **Copper particles (sub-micron, salmon pink)** — larger than true nanoparticles; useful for painted/brushed copper traces
- **Monoethanolamine / triethanolamine** — added to copper formate to form a sticky blue copper complex used for coating
- **Graphite / carbon** — mixed with copper formate and heated to form copper nanoparticles or nanorods within the carbon matrix
- **Granite** — material of the spice grinder stones; inert grinding medium

## Techniques and Methods
- **Mechanochemical synthesis** — sustained mechanical grinding/shearing of reagents without solvent or elevated heat to drive chemical reactions
- **Spice grinder milling** — using a consumer wet-spice grinder (granite stones, stainless steel body) run for 24–48 hours
- **Dilution/decantation** — adding water to the product to separate copper particles (settle as salmon-pink sediment) from the blue copper formate solution
- **Thermal decomposition** — heating copper formate to ~200°C to produce copper metal, CO₂, and water
- **Organometallic ink formulation** — mixing the copper complex into an ink suitable for standard inkjet printers
- **Two-pass printing** — printing copper ink then applying a developer (the formate solution) to reduce it back to metallic copper lines on paper
- **Contact copper plating** — rubbing the copper complex onto stainless steel or steel surfaces with a cloth to deposit an adherent copper coating
