## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith introduces mechanochemical synthesis — a solvent-minimal, eco-friendly chemistry technique that uses mechanical force (grinding, milling, shearing) to drive chemical reactions. He demonstrates the synthesis of copper nanoparticles and copper formate by grinding copper(I) oxide with formic acid in an Indian spice grinder. The resulting material has a wide range of practical applications including copper plating, conductive inks, and nanoparticle production, and is accessible to home chemists.
