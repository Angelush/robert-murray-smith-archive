## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith visits Machynlleth, a picturesque Welsh town near the Snowdonia National Park, as a prelude to visiting the Centre for Alternative Technology (CAT). He briefly explores the town's history, its role as a hub for alternative lifestyles, and notes its significance as a gateway to both Snowdonia and the CAT facility.

## Key Topics
- The Centre for Alternative Technology (CAT) and its ethos of using existing solutions to address environmental challenges
- Brief history of Machynlleth, including Owain Glyndŵr calling the first Welsh Parliament there in 1404
- The role of Glyndŵr in unifying Wales and creating a Welsh national identity
- The town as a hub for alternative lifestyles (vegetarian cafes, boutiques)
- Notable local architecture: the 1873 clock tower and the railway station
- A wind turbine at the railway station
