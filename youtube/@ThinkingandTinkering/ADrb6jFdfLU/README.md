## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates homemade flexible graphene heating elements built by applying graphite coatings between copper tape contacts on various substrates. He showcases three substrate types — PET plastic, Kapton tape, and granite — each enabling different maximum operating temperatures. The video concludes with a preview of a graphene ink formulation and the next steps of adding temperature control circuitry for mains voltage operation.
