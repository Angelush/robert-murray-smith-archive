## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains how heat pumps work by first reviewing the four fundamental gas laws (Boyle's, Charles's, Gay-Lussac's, and Avogadro's), then showing how the relationship between pressure, volume, and temperature underlies all heat pump devices. He demonstrates that refrigerators, air conditioners, ground-source heat pumps, and air-source heat pumps are all the same machine, requiring only five basic components. The key takeaway is that heat pumps are elegantly simple: compressing gas heats it, expanding gas cools it, and that's all there is to it.
