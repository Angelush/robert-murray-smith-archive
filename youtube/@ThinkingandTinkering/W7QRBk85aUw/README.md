## Overview

A short follow-up to video 105.1, where Robert hooks his mechanical inverter (built from two PC fans) up to an oscilloscope and observes the output waveform. The device produces a genuine pure sine wave at ~66 Hz — something that commercial digital inverters, even expensive "pure sine wave" models, only approximate. He suggests the device could serve as a low-cost sine wave function generator for audio amplification experiments.
