## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith traces the history of epicyclic (planetary) gear systems from the ancient Antikythera mechanism through to the modern Wolfrom Drive, a compounded planetary gear invented by Urs Wolfram in 1912. The video explains how the Wolfrom Drive achieves extremely high gear reductions in a compact space by exploiting a lever effect created by stacking two slightly different-sized planet gears on a shared axle. Robert demonstrates a 3D-printed model and discusses the two practical challenges — gear indexing and tooth-size limits — that have kept it from widespread adoption.
