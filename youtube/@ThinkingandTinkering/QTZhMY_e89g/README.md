## Overview

This video explores two related mechanical coupling mechanisms: the Hobson's coupler (which connects two axles at 90 degrees so they rotate in the same direction) and the Cardan joint (named after 16th-century mathematician Cardano). Robert demonstrates how combining these two mechanisms creates a versatile variable-angle power transmission joint capable of handling significant torque even while the angle between shafts is actively changing, and argues that old mechanisms deserve re-evaluation in light of modern materials and applications.
