## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a quick experiment using three ordinary umbrellas attached to a rotor to form a makeshift wind turbine. The umbrellas, acting as a combination of airfoil and bucket-style blades, spin effectively even in the lightest breeze. The takeaway is a playful proof-of-concept: umbrellas can serve as functional wind turbine blades.
