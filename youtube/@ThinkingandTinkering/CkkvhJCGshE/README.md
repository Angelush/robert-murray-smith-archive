## Overview

Robert investigates whether a magnetic field can improve the conductivity of a transparent, flexible graphene-based solar cell by aligning the graphene flakes during deposition. He tests two samples dried on a printer's magnet at different orientations (parallel and perpendicular to the field lines) and finds a striking difference in output — 0.1 µA vs 5.6–7 µA — confirming that the diamagnetic nature of graphene causes it to orient under the field, significantly affecting charge transport between the electrodes.
