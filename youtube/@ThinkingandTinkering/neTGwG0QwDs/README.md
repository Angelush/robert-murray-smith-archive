## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the challenge of insulating a J-type rocket stove to maintain the heat needed for efficient combustion and to run a Stirling engine. He identifies carbon foam as the best available insulation material and demonstrates how to make it at home using sugar, water, and nitric acid — based on a research paper on "sugar blobbing."
