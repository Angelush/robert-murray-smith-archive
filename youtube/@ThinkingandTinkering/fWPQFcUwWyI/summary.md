## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents a simplified home method for producing graphene oxide (GO) in high yield, developed as an improvement over the complex multi-step "West Lothian" process shown in part 1. The method requires only about five minutes of preparation — combining sulfuric acid, phosphoric acid, graphite, and potassium permanganate — followed by three days of passive stirring at room temperature, with a claimed yield approaching 100%. The video captures the reaction in progress and ends just before the washing stage, with Robert noting he'll follow up once processing is complete.

## Key Topics
- Motivation for simplifying the earlier graphene oxide synthesis method
- Critique of yield terminology in academic graphene literature
- Step-by-step preparation of the acid-graphite oxidation mixture
- Visual indicators of reaction progress (color changes: green → brown → yellow)
- Safety precautions when working with concentrated acids and potassium permanganate
- Washing and pH adjustment as the final processing step
- Comparison with modified Hummer's method and the Huang et al. approach

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Concentrated sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄, 98%)** — 320 mL; primary intercalation and oxidation agent
- **Phosphoric acid (H₃PO₄, 75%)** — 80 mL; co-solvent/oxidation moderator
- **Graphite** — 3.2 g; graphite-to-sulfuric acid ratio approximately 1:100
- **Potassium permanganate (KMnO₄)** — ~18 g; oxidising agent added slowly; causes characteristic green colour and purple fumes
- **Hydrochloric acid (HCl, 1 molar)** — used in washing step to remove ionic impurities
- **Deionised/distilled water** — used for washing to bring pH to 4–5
- **Hot water** — used to quench the reaction, producing a bright yellow precipitate

## Techniques and Methods
- **Room-temperature oxidative intercalation**: graphite mixed into sulfuric/phosphoric acid blend with slow addition of KMnO₄; left to react for 3–4 days
- **Magnetic stir bar stirring**: continuous stirring over 3 days to drive reaction to completion (also tested without stirring)
- **Reaction quenching with hot water**: rapidly changes colour from brown to bright yellow, indicating GO formation
- **Acid washing**: precipitate washed with 1M HCl to remove ionic contaminants
- **Water washing / pH adjustment**: repeated washing (noted in comments as ~7 litres warm water, decanted 3×) until pH reaches 4–5
- **Centrifugation**: mentioned as slow but used for pelleting the GO product
- **Safety measures**: heavy-duty gloves, eye protection, loose lid to prevent pressure build-up, cloth over cap when opening, well-ventilated area

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Better method available**: Robert repeatedly directs viewers to a later video, *"How To Make Graphene Oxide - The Tour Method"*, describing it as "much better" with higher yield.
- **Acid concentrations confirmed**: 98% sulfuric acid and 75% phosphoric acid; corrects a slip of the tongue — the phosphoric acid volume is 80 mL.
- **Temperature tip**: The reaction can be gently heated to 40 °C to speed completion, but he warns emphatically **do not exceed 50 °C**.
- **Washing clarification**: Rather than centrifugation, simply adding the product to ~7 litres of warm water, letting it settle, decanting, and repeating 2–3 times brings the pH to 4–5. Details also covered in *Graphene Part 4*.
- **Yield estimate**: Even without stirring, a 1-day reaction would likely give ~80% yield; 3 days approaches full completion.
- **Source paper**: Method is based on Huang et al., *International Journal of Nanomedicine* — "Simple room temperature preparation of high yield large area graphene oxide", combined with a note from *Carbon* journal about quenching in a large volume of hot water.
- **Graphite quantity**: The 3.2 g figure is not critical; what matters more is temperature control.
- **Downstream uses**: The GO made by this method was used in his spin-coating and electrostatic speaker videos.
- **Dispersal challenge**: After washing, dispersing the GO properly requires a sonicator; he was struggling with this at the time of posting.
- **Cost comparison**: He estimated making ~15 g for ~£10, versus ~£100/g on eBay at the time.
