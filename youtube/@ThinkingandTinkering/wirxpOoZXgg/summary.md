## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith traces the history of the spirit burner from its origins in alchemical laboratories of ancient Egypt and China through medieval Europe and into modern chemistry, arguing it symbolises the transition from mystical to empirical science. He then demonstrates how to build one yourself using simple materials like a glass bottle, a wick, and a brass plumbing cap. The video concludes with a working alcohol burner filled with methylated spirits.

## Key Topics
- The philosophical role of heat as a driving force in chemistry and engineering
- Historical evolution of the spirit burner from alchemy to modern laboratory use
- The alchemical use of spirit burners for distillation and sublimation
- Refinements to burner design during the 18th and 19th centuries
- The spirit burner as an educational tool in modern schools
- Step-by-step construction of a DIY spirit burner

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Glass bottles** (pill bottle, clove oil bottle) — used as the burner body
- **Methanol / wood spirit** — preferred fuel mentioned by Robert
- **Methylated spirits (meths / denatured alcohol)** — used to fill the completed burner
- **Ethanol / ethyl alcohol ("spiritus")** — historically used fuel in alchemical burners
- **Cotton wool** — packed inside the bottle as a fuel reservoir and spill absorber
- **Zippo lighter wick** — used as the burner wick
- **Copper wire** — used to hold the wick in place
- **PTFE tape** — optionally applied to seal the cap fitting
- **Brass compression fitting (15 mm / 22 mm)** — used as the burner cap/top
- **Brass blanking disc** — drilled through the centre to pass the wick
- **Washer** — simpler alternative cap option

## Techniques and Methods
- Selecting and repurposing glass bottles as burner vessels
- Press-fitting or screw-fitting a plumbing compression cap onto the bottle neck
- Soldering the blanking disc into the compression fitting for a neater finish
- Lathe-turning the brass cap assembly
- Drilling a central 4 mm hole in the cap for the wick
- Packing cotton wool around the wick inside the bottle
- Filling with alcohol fuel and lighting the assembled burner
