## Overview
Robert demonstrates using a commercially available low-temperature Stirling engine (a "gamma type" thermal difference engine, purchased for ~£30 on Amazon) to test whether an evaporative cooler — made from water-soaked floral foam and black cloth — can generate a temperature differential sufficient to run the engine. The experiment succeeds with a 5°C difference between the hot (sun-heated) and cold (evaporating) sides, proving that solar evaporative cooling can drive a Stirling engine and thus generate electricity. Robert positions this as a practical, low-tool-barrier alternative to Peltier devices for small-scale off-grid power.

## Key Topics
- Low-temperature (LTD) Stirling engines and where to buy them
- How gamma-type Stirling engines work (hot/cold side air shuttling, piston cycle)
- Building a simple evaporative cooler from floral foam and black cloth
- Using sunlight + evaporative cooling as a heat source/sink pair to drive the engine
- Comparing Stirling engines to Peltier devices and internal combustion engines in terms of efficiency
- Potential for attaching a generator to produce electricity
- Discussion of inverting the engine and design limitations of the crank

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Floral foam (Oasis)** — used as a water-absorbing reservoir for the evaporative cooler
- **Black cloth** — placed on the foam to increase solar absorption and accelerate evaporation
- **Water** — soaked into the Oasis foam as the evaporating medium
- **Low-temperature Stirling engine (gamma type)** — purchased from Amazon (~£30), the primary experimental device

## Techniques and Methods
- **Evaporative cooling** — soaking floral foam in water, covering with black cloth, and exposing to sunlight to produce a cool surface via evaporation
- **Solar thermal differential driving** — positioning the Stirling engine so one plate faces the sun (hot side) and the other rests on the evaporative cooler (cold side)
- **Temperature differential measurement** — confirming a ~5°C difference between the two plates before running the engine
- **Manual spin-start** — flipping/spinning the flywheel to initiate engine rotation

## Robert's Own Replies

- **Low boiling point fluids** as working media for the cooler side — he thinks this would work well.
- **Zeolite cooling** (from a previous video, possibly #1269) is mentioned as another approach that could work here for either heating or cooling.
