## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith rushes back from a US trip to demonstrate that distilled/deionized water combined with magnetically aligned carbon ink can generate measurable electrical current and voltage. He connects this phenomenon to Gerald Pollack's "EZ water" (exclusion zone water) theory, proposing that macroscale structures using hydrophilic aligned ink surfaces could harness this effect for both power generation and water purification.
