## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith designs a 3D-printed sand wheel generator inspired by a child's sand toy, exploring the concept of sand as a gravity battery. By letting sand flow through a spoon-wheel (like a waterwheel) that spins a flywheel embedded with N52 magnets past a hand-held coil, he demonstrates that the falling sand can generate approximately 1 volt of open-circuit electrical output. The video concludes that sand—being denser than water—is a promising, reusable, and scalable medium for gravity-based energy storage.
