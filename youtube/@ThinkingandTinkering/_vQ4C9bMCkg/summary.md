## Overview
Luke builds a mount for his first wind turbine, repurposing a salvaged TV antenna arm as the main structure. He fabricates a swivel joint using a caster wheel to allow the turbine to rotate freely and track wind direction, then tests the completed assembly outdoors on a camera tripod. The result successfully keeps the turbine aligned with the wind at all times.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing a salvaged TV antenna as a turbine mount
- Designing and fabricating a wind-tracking swivel joint
- Creating a tail fin to orient the turbine into the wind
- Making an adapter plate to attach the turbine to the mount
- Field testing the completed assembly in a car park

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Steel bar and steel plates (used for swivel assembly and adapter plate)
- M6 bolts (for attaching turbine adapter plate)
- Caster wheel (used as the pivot/swivel joint)
- Recycled TV antenna (donor part for the mounting arm)
- Old camera tripod (used as temporary base for demonstration)

## Techniques and Methods
- Welding (caster to pipe, plates to bar, tail fin, T-shaped adapter plate)
- Drilling (holes in adapter plate for M6 bolt mounting)
- Cutting and painting (tail fin fabrication)
- Slot-and-tighten assembly (tripod base attachment)
