## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to convert a PC fan motor into a small water-powered generator by 3D printing and attaching turbine wheels. He tests both a Pelton wheel and a Turgo turbine (sourced from Thingiverse) on the same PC fan motor, comparing their output. The Turgo turbine proves more capable, producing an estimated 3–4 watts from a small water jet.

## Key Topics
- Converting a PC fan motor into a water generator
- Pelton wheel vs. Turgo turbine design and performance comparison
- 3D printing turbine wheels from Thingiverse
- Magnetic bearing Pelton wheel as the original inspiration
- Waterproofing the motor coil for water exposure

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Magnet wire (coated)** — the internal coil winding found inside the PC fan motor after removing the circuit board
- **Varnish** — recommended for waterproofing the motor windings
- **Plasti-Dip (rubber coating)** — alternative waterproofing material for the motor

## Techniques and Methods
- Removing the PC fan circuit board to expose the raw motor coil (motor-to-generator conversion)
- Snapping off existing fan blades and sanding the hub
- Gluing a 3D-printed turbine wheel (Pelton or Turgo) onto the fan hub
- Waterproofing motor windings by dipping in varnish or Plasti-Dip
- Aiming the water jet at 20–30° angle for the Turgo turbine
- Using a small LED light as a load to estimate wattage output
