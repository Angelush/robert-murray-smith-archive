## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith unboxes and sets up the Elegoo Neptune 4 Plus 3D printer, positioning it as a strong mid-range option compared to the Bambu Lab A1. He walks through assembly, software configuration, bed leveling, and filament loading, concluding that the Neptune 4 Plus offers superior build volume and proven reliability at a competitive price point.

## Key Topics
- Comparison of the Elegoo Neptune 4 Plus vs. Bambu Lab A1 (price, build volume, features)
- Unboxing and physical assembly of the Neptune 4 Plus
- Software setup using a Cura-based slicer with a Neptune 4 Plus profile
- Bed leveling process: manual auxiliary leveling followed by 121-point auto-leveling
- Filament loading procedure
- Commentary on "unnecessary" features: built-in camera and Wi-Fi
- Robert's longer-term experience with the Elegoo Neptune product line

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- PLA filament
- ABS filament
- Carbon fiber filament
- Nylon filament
- TPU (thermoplastic polyurethane) filament

## Techniques and Methods
- Manual auxiliary bed leveling using adjustment wheels and a paper drag test
- 121-point automatic bed leveling to generate a surface map
- Z-offset calibration
- Filament loading via dual-gear extruder head
- Slicing with Cura (importing STL, selecting printer profile, exporting G-code to USB)
- Standalone/sneakernet printing via USB memory card
