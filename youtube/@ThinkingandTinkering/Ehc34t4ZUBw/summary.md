## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith completes a multi-part project by assembling a working DIY concertina (bellows-based) pump from readily available plastic plumbing parts and gaffer tape. The pump uses two one-way valves to ensure unidirectional water flow, keeping the bellows completely dry. The successful proof-of-concept demonstrates that a functional water pump can be built cheaply and without power tools, making it viable in emergency or survival situations.

## Key Topics
- Assembling the concertina pump from previously built bellows and one-way valves
- Using a Tupperware container as an airtight pump chamber
- Connecting inlet and outlet fittings to the chamber
- Live demonstration of the pump moving water from a bucket
- Practical considerations: the need for a lever, adding a filter, and priming
- Design philosophy: simplicity, no precision parts, emergency buildability

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Tupperware container (airtight plastic box used as the pump chamber)
- Plastic tank connectors / tank fittings (for inlet and outlet holes)
- 22.5 mm overflow pipe (to connect bellows to chamber)
- Pipe reducer fittings (solvent-weld type, bought from a plumbing store)
- Gaffer tape (used in bellows construction)
- Venetian blind slabs (cut to size for bellows construction, referenced from earlier video)

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling holes in a Tupperware container and fitting plastic tank connectors
- Solvent-welding (gluing) plastic plumbing fittings together
- Assembling two one-way valves in series with arrows aligned for unidirectional flow
- Priming a suction pump by accounting for water-level and valve placement
- Proof-of-concept bench testing by pumping water from a bucket

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Lever strongly recommended:** Robert repeatedly emphasises that a lever should be added, as lifting the bellows handle moves a considerable weight of water and would tire the user quickly.
- **Bellows acts as its own spring:** He clarifies that the bellows design is self-returning due to its spring properties, unlike a piston arrangement.
- **Piston alternative is inferior without precision:** He notes that a piston pump can work but requires a good fit, which is harder to achieve than it sounds — making the bellows approach preferable for DIY/emergency use.
- **Tesla valve rejected:** In response to a suggestion, he explains a Tesla valve was ruled out because it is more complicated to make, conflicting with the simplicity design brief.
- **Cage/piston hybrid idea:** He floated the interesting notion of a cage that fixes the piston relative to the bellows while keeping the pump mobile.
- **Gear set reference:** Mentioned that a gear set from video 2009 could potentially be relevant for a future mechanism.
- **Follow-up video planned:** He confirmed an update/new video would go up, and that the earlier bellows video would be reviewed/re-covered for those having trouble finding it.
