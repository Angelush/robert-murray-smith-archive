## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates the concept of a magnetically assisted solenoid, showing how combining a permanent magnet with an electromagnet dramatically increases lifting force beyond what either can achieve alone. By reversing current direction via an H-bridge, the electromagnetic coil can either reinforce or cancel the permanent magnet's field, allowing the device to be switched fully on or fully off. The key takeaway is that magnetic assistance is a practical and significant force multiplier for solenoid designs.

## Key Topics
- Limitations of a standalone electromagnet (insufficient force on its own)
- Limitations of ceramic permanent magnets alone
- Combining permanent magnets with an electromagnet for dramatically increased attraction force
- Polarity alignment: how south-north-south-north stacking amplifies flux vs. cancelling it
- H-bridge control circuit to reverse current direction and switch the field on/off

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Steel bolt (sawed to length, used as the magnetic core)
- Steel washers (fitted on either end of the bolt)
- Magnet wire / coil wire (wound around the bolt to form the electromagnet)
- Ceramic magnets (20 cm × 0.5 cm, used as the permanent magnet assist)
- Block of steel (used as the load/target to be attracted)

## Techniques and Methods
- Winding an electromagnetic coil around a bolt core
- Attaching ceramic permanent magnets to the coil assembly to create a hybrid magnet
- Polarity alignment to reinforce permanent-magnet flux through the core
- H-bridge wiring to reverse current polarity and switch magnetic force fully on or off
