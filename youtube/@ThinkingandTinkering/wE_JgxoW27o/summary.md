## Overview
In this follow-up video, Robert Murray-Smith iterates on his cube-octahedron O Wind Generator prototype by addressing community feedback: adding holes to reduce drag and, more critically, attaching curved airfoil sections to the flat faces to prevent stalling. By the end of the video, the modified generator is successfully spinning in a single consistent direction even in a light breeze, demonstrating meaningful progress toward a functional multi-directional wind turbine.

## Key Topics
- Critique and iteration based on viewer comments (holes for drag reduction, flat-face stalling problem)
- Conceptual framing of the O Wind design as two interlocked three-bladed wind turbines with bent blades
- Fabrication of curved airfoil blades from ordinary drainage pipe using a heat gun
- Mounting mechanism using aluminium tube, resin, and an acrylic equilateral triangle plate
- Initial outdoor testing showing consistent unidirectional rotation in light wind
- Discussion of potential further modifications (extending blade length for more swept area)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Drainage pipe (PVC)** — cut in half and heat-formed into curved airfoil sections
- **Aluminium tube** — used as the central spindle/mounting bar, resined into the base of the cube-octahedron frame
- **Acrylic plate (equilateral triangle)** — used as the top mounting plate, drilled centrally to accept the spindle
- **Resin** — used to fix the aluminium tube into the frame

## Techniques and Methods
- **Hole-punching** — adding holes to the flat faces of the cube-octahedron to reduce aerodynamic resistance
- **Pipe splitting and heat-forming** — cutting drainage pipe in half lengthwise, then heating with a heat gun until soft, then pressing flat under a steel weight to produce a curved airfoil profile
- **Bolted assembly** — attaching the formed airfoil sections to the frame edges in consistent orientation (leading/trailing edge aligned) around top and bottom
- **Spin-testing in ambient wind** — outdoor functional test to verify unidirectional rotation and assess performance in light breeze
