## Overview
Luke dismantles a broken hard drive and repurposes its aluminium casing and spinning disc as the face of a functional wall clock. The motor housing is removed and replaced with a standard clock mechanism, producing a clean, industrial-looking clock. The finished piece is compact and visually striking, making clever use of the hard drive's original disc as the clock face.

## Key Topics
- Disassembling a broken hard drive for parts reuse
- Removing the spindle motor to create space for a clock mechanism
- Cutting the aluminium casing with a hole saw to seat the clock module
- Reassembling the components into a functional wall clock
- Upcycling/repurposing electronics into decorative items

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Hard drive aluminium casing (repurposed as clock body)
- Hard drive magnetic disc (repurposed as clock face)
- Neodymium (neo) magnet from the read/write arm assembly
- Clock mechanism (off-the-shelf quartz movement)
- Clock hands
- Glue (unspecified adhesive used to fix clock mechanism in place)

## Techniques and Methods
- Hard drive disassembly using Torx screws
- Motor extraction via vice and percussive force (mallet/hammer)
- Coil removal using a lever tool
- Hole-saw cutting of aluminium casing to fit clock mechanism shaft
- Centering and gluing clock mechanism into casing
- Reassembly using original screw holes for back panel alignment
