## Overview
A short introductory video in which Robert Murray-Smith explains the difference between deductive and inductive reasoning. He uses everyday examples — including a deliberately provocative logical syllogism and the act of sitting on a chair — to illustrate how inductive reasoning works and why, despite being foundational to science, it remains inherently flawed.

## Key Topics
- Deductive reasoning: top-down approach, drawing conclusions from universal premises
- Inductive reasoning: bottom-up approach, generalising from repeated experimental observation
- The symmetry and contrast between the two reasoning methods
- Limitations of deductive reasoning (garbage-in, garbage-out premises)
- Limitations of inductive reasoning (conclusions only valid for experiments performed so far)
- Everyday examples of inductive reasoning in action

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Logical syllogism (used to illustrate deductive fallacy)
- Repeated experimental observation as the basis of inductive generalisation
