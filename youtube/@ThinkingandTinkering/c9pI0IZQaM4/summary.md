## Overview
Robert introduces a conceptual solution to a fundamental problem with Savonius-type wind turbines built from oil drums: they perform well in low winds when open but risk overspeeding in high winds. He proposes a centrifugal, pulley-based sliding mechanism that automatically closes the turbine blades as rotation speed increases, and opens them again as it drops — self-regulating without electronics.

## Key Topics
- Savonius wind turbine design using repurposed oil drums
- The trade-off between low-wind capture area and high-wind overspeed risk
- Centrifugal force as a self-regulating mechanism
- Opposed sliding mechanism linked by a pulley to translate outward centrifugal movement into inward blade closure
- Plans to prototype using drawer runners

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Oil drums (halved for Savonius blades)
- Drawer runners (linear slides for the blade adjustment mechanism)
- A weight (acting as the centrifugal actuator)
- String/pulley (for demonstrating and linking the mechanism)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting oil drums in half to form Savonius rotor blades
- Harnessing centrifugal force for passive, speed-dependent actuation
- Opposing sliding mechanisms connected through a pulley so outward weight movement drives inward blade movement
- Demonstration with a string and weight to illustrate the centrifugal principle
