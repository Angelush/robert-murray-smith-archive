## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains hydrothermal carbonization (HTC), a process that converts organic waste — including sewage, biomass, and plastics — into usable fuel by applying heat and pressure in an autoclave with a small amount of dilute acid. The process mimics natural coal formation but compresses millions of years into a few hours, producing both a burnable carbon solid and a liquid precursor for biodiesel. Robert frames this as a genuinely accessible technology that he has been experimenting with at home for nine years.
