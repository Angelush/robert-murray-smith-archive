## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a low-cost method for making homemade copper conductive paint using copper sulfate and ascorbic acid (vitamin C). The ascorbic acid reduces the copper sulfate to nanoscale copper particles, which are then mixed with gum arabic as a binder and burnished to produce a highly conductive paint. The resulting ink achieves approximately 0.33 ohms/cm resistance — dramatically better than commercial alternatives like Bare Conductive's carbon-based ink at ~430 ohms/cm.
