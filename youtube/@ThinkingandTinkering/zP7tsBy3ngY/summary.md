## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of a hypocycloid differential — a type of differential gear based on hypocycloid geometry rather than the familiar ring-and-pinion found in cars. Building on his previous video (2282) about epicyclic gears, he adapts a hypocycloid gear design (sourced from Otvinta) by adding external cogs to its ring and output gears, demonstrating that the result functions as a true differential that can split or combine rotational input at variable speeds.

## Key Topics
- The standard automotive differential (ring and pinion type) as context
- Other differential types: epicyclic (sun and planet), spheria, and hypocycloid
- Historical use of differentials — traced back to the Antikythera mechanism (~80 BC)
- Gear tooth profiles: involute (most common) vs. cycloid
- How cycloid gears work and their niche uses (e.g., watches)
- The hypocycloid gear mechanism sourced from Otvinta
- Modification of a hypocycloid gear into a differential (adding cogs to ring and output gears, mounting in a cradle)
- Demonstration of differential behaviour: same-direction input = fast output; opposing-direction input = slow output
- Applications of hypocycloid gears in robotics (thin profile, high gear ratio, high torque, low friction)
- General definition of a differential: any gear arrangement that splits or combines one shaft's power into two (or vice versa) with independent, variable ratios

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 3D-printed plastic gears (implied by the fabrication context and Otvinta design source)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing gear components (ring gear, output gear, cam, cradle)
- Adapting an existing hypocycloid gear design (from Otvinta) by adding external cogs to the ring and output gears
- Mounting components on an axle with a cam, enclosed in a cradle
- Using pencil marks on pegs and teeth to visually track differential motion during demonstration
- Manual demonstration of differential action by holding one gear while rotating the other, and by rotating both in the same or opposing directions
