## Overview
This short DIY tutorial demonstrates how to convert a standard soldering iron into a hot knife capable of cutting plastics. The presenter (Luke) shows that by removing the soldering tip, sawing a slit into it, and clamping an X-Acto or Stanley blade in its place, a cheap and effective plastic-cutting tool can be made. Testing confirms it cuts through both thin packaging plastic and 1.5 mm acrylic with ease.

## Key Topics
- Motivation: frequent acrylic work requiring a better cutting method
- Selecting a suitable blade (Stanley blade vs. X-Acto blade)
- Modifying the soldering iron tip attachment to accept a blade
- Assembly and securing the blade using the iron's existing clamping screws
- Live cutting tests on plastic packaging and acrylic sheet

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Soldering iron** — base tool repurposed as a heat source
- **Stanley blade** — one blade option (requires trimming to fit)
- **X-Acto knife blade** — preferred blade option; fits without modification
- **Plastic packaging** — first test material (thin, unspecified polymer)
- **1.5 mm acrylic sheet** — second test material; cut successfully though more slowly than thin packaging

## Techniques and Methods
- Removing the soldering iron's tip attachment
- Cutting a longitudinal slit down the tip attachment with a saw
- Using a vice to hold the assembly steady during blade insertion
- Clamping the blade in place by tightening the iron's existing set screws
- Slow-pass hot cutting: guiding the heated blade along a marked line with steady pressure
