## Overview
Robert demonstrates a "floating rack" mechanism — a device that converts variable, bidirectional linear motion into consistent one-directional rotary motion — and shows how he designed and 3D-printed one using FreeCAD's Gears Workbench. The video highlights the floating rack as a practical tool for applications like wave and wind power generation, while also serving as a showcase for how accessible FreeCAD's gear design tools have become.

## Key Topics
- The floating rack mechanism and how it rectifies bidirectional linear motion into one-way rotation
- Comparison to a related mechanism from video 2399 and the CVT built in video 2400
- Potential applications: wave power, reciprocating wind power, driving generators
- FreeCAD's Gears Workbench: installation via Add-on Manager and overview of gear types
- Gear parameters: module, number of teeth, pitch angle, and how module must match for meshing gears
- Step-by-step assembly of the 3D-printed floating rack prototype
- Files available on Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 3D-printed parts: rack bodies (with bevels), pin plates, lever arms, U-shaped joining bars, gear, axle, backing case, cover plate
- Spring (sourced from a ballpoint pen; 3D-printed spring suggested as an alternative)
- Adhesive (glue used to fix the U-shaped bars)

## Techniques and Methods
- FreeCAD Gears Workbench for parametric gear and rack design (involute rack, spur gears, cycloidal/hypocycloidal gears, bevel gears, worm gears, lantern gears, timing gears, ring gears)
- 3D printing of all mechanism components
- Press-fit assembly of pin plates into rack bodies
- Glue bonding of U-shaped structural bars
- Spring-loaded gear engagement within the rack housing
