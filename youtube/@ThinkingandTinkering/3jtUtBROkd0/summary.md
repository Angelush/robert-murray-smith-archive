## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores gravity as a cheap, clean, and simple energy storage medium, demonstrating several prototype mechanisms that convert falling weights into rotational electrical generation. He walks through his 3D-printed designs — including a worm gear derived from an Archimedes screw and a spiral ball-bearing drop mechanism — before spotlighting an impressive emergency gravity generator built by students at the Technological University of the Philippines, which he considers a near-perfect realisation of the concept.

## Key Topics
- Gravity as a conservative energy storage medium (analogous to hydro and tidal power)
- Worm gear design derived from duplicating and rotating an Archimedes screw
- Ratchet mechanism reverse-engineered from a humming top toy
- 3D printing workflow using TinkerCad and Thingiverse community parts
- Spiral twist drop mechanism with ball bearings as a gravity-to-rotation converter
- Flywheel effect using barbell weights
- Serpentine coil and magnet array for electrical generation
- Student-designed portable gravity power backup from the Technological University of the Philippines

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Steel** — used in the humming top's slotted washer and twisted bar mechanism
- **Thrust bearings (47 mm × 25 mm)** — main rotating element in the generator assembly
- **20 mm × 4.5 mm neodymium magnets** — arranged N-S-N-S-N-S in the carrier rotor
- **12 mm ball bearings** — fitted into the ring rider on the spiral twist mechanism
- **Barbell weights (2.5 kg and 1.25 kg)** — used as flywheel masses and drop weights
- **Superglue** — used to assemble prototype spiral sections and secure the flywheel weight

## Techniques and Methods
- **Worm gear fabrication** — derived by duplicating an Archimedes screw profile and rotating it in 90° increments
- **3D printing (FDM)** — designed in TinkerCad; community STLs sourced from Thingiverse (pillars, feet, ratchet mechanism) and modified to fit
- **Ratchet mechanism extraction** — adapted from an existing spinning-top design; ball/cage top removed and replaced with a custom carrier cage
- **Serpentine coil winding** — copper coil wound around the magnet carrier for induction (separate video linked by Robert)
- **Spiral drop mechanism** — 60 mm printed twist sections glued end-to-end; ball bearings in a ring rider spin as the rider falls along the helix
- **Voltage metering** — multimeter used to verify electrical output during spin-up test
- **Weight-driven worm gear generator (Philippines student design)** — falling weight drives a worm gear coupled to a generator; cage rotated by crank handle to reset the weight to the top
