## Overview
Luke (presenting on Robert Murray-Smith's channel) demonstrates printing a replacement tripod quick-release shoe on a resin 3D printer, sourced from Thingiverse, to solve the problem of two cameras sharing a single shoe. The print cost only 31 cents and took 30 minutes, saving approximately £19 compared to buying an official replacement part.

## Key Topics
- Finding replacement parts on Thingiverse as an alternative to buying them
- Cost comparison of 3D printing vs. purchasing OEM parts
- Practical, functional use cases for home resin 3D printing
- Quick-release tripod shoe mechanism and attachment hardware

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Resin (for the 3D printer, type unspecified)

## Techniques and Methods
- Searching Thingiverse for model files by tripod manufacturer name
- Slicing the model in Chitubox to estimate print time and resin cost
- Resin 3D printing (~30-minute print)
- Post-print washing and cleaning of the resin print
- Attaching the shoe to a camera body using a universal screw secured with a circlip
