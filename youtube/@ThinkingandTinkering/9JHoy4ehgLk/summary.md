## Overview
Robert receives a generous gift of heavy-duty DC brushless PC fans from viewer Timothy Box and his son-in-law Mario, who drove from North London specifically to deliver them. He demonstrates the fans' impressive thrust at 12V and shows how one has been converted into a small generator, producing ~2.6V at ~90mA when driven by a hairdryer. The video is a combination of heartfelt thanks and a practical exploration of what these fans can do as motors and generators.

## Key Topics
- Receiving a gift of Delta 12V DC brushless PC fans from Tim and Mario
- Demonstrating the fans' powerful airflow/thrust at 12V
- Disassembly of a fan (removing circlip, separating fan blade from motor section)
- Converting a brushless fan motor into a generator
- Brainstorming future projects using these fans

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Delta DC brushless fans (12V, heavy-duty)
- LED (used as generator load indicator)
- Hairdryer (used as wind source for generator test)

## Techniques and Methods
- Direct 12V wiring (black and red power wires only, ignoring sensor/control wires)
- Disassembly via circlip removal to separate fan blade from motor housing
- Circuit board removal and rewiring to repurpose brushless motor as a generator
- Open-circuit voltage and current measurement (~2.6V, ~90mA output)
