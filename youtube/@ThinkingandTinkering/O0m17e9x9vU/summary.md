## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a fully printed Joule Thief circuit made using graphene ink, building on a previous video about a printed induction coil. The circuit uses inductive coupling through a paper substrate to power an LED, with the two coils remaining electrically isolated from each other. He presents this as a proof-of-concept and teases upcoming step-by-step build videos.

## Key Topics
- Completion of a printed Joule Thief circuit using graphene conductive ink
- Inductive coupling through paper to transfer power wirelessly between two isolated coils
- DC-to-oscillating current conversion enabling the inductive effect
- Potential for silkscreen printing of fine conductive lines
- Alternative coil configurations (single-sided bifilar coil)
- Plans for a detailed how-to build video

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene ink** — used to paint/print the conductive traces of the circuit
- **Paper** — used as the substrate and electrical isolator between the two coils

## Techniques and Methods
- Painting/printing a Joule Thief circuit with graphene conductive ink
- Inductive (non-contact) power coupling between two coils separated by a paper layer
- Silkscreen printing of conductive ink for fine-line circuit traces
