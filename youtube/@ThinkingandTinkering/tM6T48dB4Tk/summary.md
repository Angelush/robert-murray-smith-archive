## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to convert a previously built free-piston Stirling engine into a functional electricity generator by attaching a strong neodymium magnet to the engine's membrane and building a wire coil spool around it. The build is deliberately low-engineering and uses inexpensive, accessible parts. In testing, the generator produces approximately 3.3 volts and 1.09 amps of AC output from a hot plate heat source.

## Key Topics
- Converting a free-piston Stirling engine into a linear generator
- Electromagnetic induction via a moving magnet inside a coil
- Fabricating a magnet carrier rod attached to the engine membrane
- Winding a wire coil spool and positioning it around the magnet
- Measuring AC voltage and current output
- Suggestions for improving efficiency (cold water jacket, solar concentrating lens)
- Possibility of selling the project as a kit

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **N52 neodymium magnet** (26 kg pull, 6 mm centre hole, ~£10) — the moving element of the generator
- **24 SWG copper wire** — wound into the generating coil
- **Cyanoacrylate (Crazy Glue / superglue)** — used to fix tap washers to the membrane and to lock nuts in place
- **Tap washers** — used to spread load across the rubber membrane
- **6 mm coach bolt and nuts** — forms the magnet carrier rod
- **Plastic pipe** — forms the body of the coil spool
- **Two plastic discs** — flanges for the spool
- **Brown tape** — used to seal the engine lid/membrane assembly

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling and re-sealing the free-piston Stirling engine lid using tape
- Gluing tap washers to the rubber membrane as bolt anchor points, then puncturing with a needle
- Threading a coach bolt through the membrane to create a rigid magnet carrier
- Locking nuts with cyanoacrylate to prevent unwinding under vibration
- Winding wire onto a hand-made spool and gluing legs to position the coil centrally over the magnet
- Direct measurement of AC output with a multimeter (voltage and amperage modes)
- Suggested enhancement: adding a cold-water jacket to increase temperature differential
- Suggested enhancement: using a Fresnel/concentrating lens to run the engine on solar energy
