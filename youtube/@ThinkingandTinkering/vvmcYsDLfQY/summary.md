## Overview
Robert demonstrates a simple energy scavenging experiment using infrared diodes connected in series, placed near a flame to generate enough voltage to light an LED. He frames this as the reverse of the Stokes shift in spectroscopy — converting low-energy infrared light into higher-energy visible (blue) light, and notes the concept could theoretically be extended toward ultraviolet.

## Key Topics
- Energy scavenging from a heat source using infrared diodes
- Series connection of IR diodes to accumulate voltage
- Reverse Stokes shift concept: upconverting low-energy IR light to higher-energy visible light
- Theoretical extension toward ultraviolet light generation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Infrared (IR) diodes (three connected in series)
- LED (used as output indicator)
- Flame (heat/IR source)

## Techniques and Methods
- Series connection of IR photodiodes to sum photovoltaic output
- Proximity of diode array to an open flame as IR source
- Visual confirmation of LED illumination as proof of energy conversion
