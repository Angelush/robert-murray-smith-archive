## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith examines a commercially available "floating pen" science toy — a pen that levitates on a magnetic field — and builds his own version from scratch. His primary motivation is to explore its potential as a low-friction pivot bearing for solar/induction motors, while also making it adjustable and affordable. He walks through the three key construction principles and assembles a kit he sells in his store.
