## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith examines a commercially available "floating pen" science toy — a pen that levitates on a magnetic field — and builds his own version from scratch. His primary motivation is to explore its potential as a low-friction pivot bearing for solar/induction motors, while also making it adjustable and affordable. He walks through the three key construction principles and assembles a kit he sells in his store.

## Key Topics
- How the floating pen levitation mechanism works (magnetic valley effect)
- The three key principles for constructing a floating pen
- Step-by-step assembly of a DIY floating pen from a kit
- Adjustability of magnet spacing to compensate for added weight
- Potential use as a frictionless bearing for electrostatic and induction motors
- Attaching a small propeller to demonstrate rotation via blown air

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Neodymium disc magnets (4 mm diameter × 2 mm thick) — used as hidden barrel magnets and support magnets
- Plastic drinking straw (cut to 7 cm) — replaces the pen ink refill section to house the hidden magnets
- Ballpoint pen (barrel, cap, refill, tip) — the levitating object
- Acrylic base — structural base for the kit
- L-shaped angle brackets (support angles) — hold the support magnets
- Small blocking bracket — positions one support angle
- Small propeller — demonstration add-on
- Superglue — fixing magnets into the straw
- Double-sided tape — attaching angles to the base
- Ordinary (craft) glue — alternative adhesive
- Blue tack / plasticine — plugging the ink refill to prevent leakage when cut

## Techniques and Methods
- Marking magnet poles with a black dot to track orientation during assembly
- Cutting a slot in the straw at the 4 mm mark to prevent magnets from sliding together
- Using a 4 mm drill bit as a handling tool to apply superglue and insert magnets without getting adhesive on fingers
- Measuring centre-to-centre magnet spacing (6.6 cm) accounting for magnet thickness
- Plugging the ink refill with blue tack before cutting to contain ink
- Sliding (rather than pulling) neodymium magnets apart to separate them safely
- Adjusting support magnet spacing inward/outward to re-level the pen after adding weight
