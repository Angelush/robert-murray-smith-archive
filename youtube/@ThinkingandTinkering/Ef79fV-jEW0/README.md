## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores aluminium as a practical on-demand hydrogen generation source, demonstrating that aluminium reacts with water (once its oxide coating is removed) to produce hydrogen, electricity, and heat. He compares the cost of an aluminium-fuelled hydrogen car journey against conventional petrol and outlines two key technological approaches to making this viable. The video concludes that hydrogen-on-demand systems based on aluminium could solve the major storage and transportation problems currently holding back hydrogen vehicles.
