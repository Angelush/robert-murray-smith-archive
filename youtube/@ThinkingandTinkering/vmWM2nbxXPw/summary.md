## Overview
Robert builds a large-scale vibration cleaner using a floor sander (a "Tomcat" orbital sander) as the base, welding a steel cradle frame to hold interchangeable Tupperware containers. The device is designed to clean heavy metal parts using his "wonder solution" combined with gravel as an abrasive medium, scaling up a technique he had previously demonstrated with a small hand sander.

## Key Topics
- Scaling up a previous small vibration cleaner experiment to industrial size
- Building a welded steel cradle frame for the floor sander
- Using interchangeable containers to accommodate parts of different sizes
- Wiring a fused on/off switch for 230V UK mains supply
- Using gravel (10mm concrete aggregate) as an abrasive cleaning medium

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Vinegar (5% acetic acid)** — 400ml, primary cleaning agent in the "wonder solution"
- **Salt** — 2 tablespoons, part of the wonder solution
- **Washing up liquid** — 20ml, surfactant in the wonder solution
- **Isopropanol (IPA)** — 100ml, part of the wonder solution
- **10mm gravel** (concrete aggregate) — used as abrasive tumbling media
- **Steel** — for welding the cradle frame
- **Double-sided tape** — used in earlier small-scale version

## Techniques and Methods
- **Vibration cleaning** — parts tumbled in solution and abrasive media on a vibrating plate
- **Welding** — fabricating the steel U-frame cradle to mount the motor
- **Mains wiring** — fitting a fused flip switch to a 230V floor sander motor
- **Modular container approach** — interchangeable Tupperware boxes for different part sizes
