## Overview
This video is presented by Luke, a younger collaborator (likely Robert Murray-Smith's son or assistant), who builds a simple wind turbine based on Robert's earlier "easiest wind generator" video. Using a donated car radiator fan with a brushed DC motor as the generator and 3D-printed blades made by Robert, Luke constructs and tests the turbine outdoors, achieving performance comparable to a commercial wind turbine of the same blade length at the same wind speed.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing a car radiator fan motor as a wind generator
- Fabricating a custom blade attachment/adaption plate
- Angling blades to catch wind effectively (sail effect)
- Outdoor testing with a multimeter (voltage and current measurement)
- Comparing homemade turbine output to commercial turbines using an online calculator (Omni Calculator)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Car radiator fan (donated, contains a brushed DC motor) — used as the generator
- 3D-printed blades (made by Robert Murray-Smith) — used as the turbine blades
- Plastic adaptive case — keyed onto the motor shaft
- M6 nut — used to secure the adaption plate to the motor spindle
- Metal bars cut at 120-degree intervals — used as the blade mounting arms

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling holes in an adaption plate to mount blades and pass the motor spindle through
- Cutting metal bars into three triangles spaced at 120 degrees for even blade distribution
- Angling blades to optimise wind capture
- Testing generator output with a multimeter (voltage ~0.8V, current ~0.6A by hand; ~2W outdoors in wind)
- Using Omni Calculator to compare actual output against theoretical commercial turbine output (blade length 52 cm, wind speed 2.5 m/s)
