## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith designs and 3D prints a blisk (blade + disk) turbine inspired by the V2 rocket engine, using TinkerCAD and a NASA turbine blade image as a reference. The video covers the historical context of the V2's engineering, a step-by-step CAD walkthrough, and a live spin test, with plans to add neodymium magnets and a coil to turn the device into a small generator.

## Key Topics
- Wernher von Braun and the Nazi V-weapons program (V1–V4)
- Operation Paperclip and von Braun's role in the Saturn V rocket
- The V2 rocket's turbine design as engineering inspiration
- The term "blisk" (blade + disk fusion)
- Converting a JPEG turbine blade image to SVG for CAD import
- Step-by-step blisk design in TinkerCAD (blade shaping, array rotation, magnet pockets)
- 3D printing the blisk and assembling it with skateboard bearings
- Plans to build an edge-generation (rim-drive) generator using the blisk

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Neodymium magnets** — 15 mm diameter, 3 mm deep; to be press-fit into pockets on the outer ring for electricity generation
- **PLA** — implied as the 3D printing material
- **Skateboard bearings** — 22 mm, pressed into the blisk hub
- **Super glue** — mentioned for bonding magnets to PLA (Robert notes this bond is extremely strong)

## Techniques and Methods
- JPEG-to-SVG conversion using an online converter, then imported into TinkerCAD
- TinkerCAD **Extrusion shape generator** used to trace and isolate the turbine blade profile
- Blade rotation: blades set at **22° off vertical**, matching V2 turbine geometry
- **Copy-and-rotate array** at 10° increments to produce 36 blades around the disc
- Magnet pocket creation: cylindrical holes (15.1 mm × 4 mm deep) arrayed at 10° intervals around the outer ring
- 3D printing of the assembled blisk
- Air-blast spin test using a metal tube

## Robert's Own Replies
- **TinkerCAD as a stepping stone:** Robert sees TinkerCAD as a useful introductory CAD tool but expects users to migrate to more capable software over time. He recommends **OnShape** as a popular current alternative.
- **Inkscape:** Robert confirms he uses Linux (alongside Windows and Mac) and endorses Inkscape as "awesome," particularly for SVG work.
- **Magnet retention concern:** He dismisses worries about magnets flying off, noting that super-gluing neodymium magnets to PLA creates an extremely strong bond — "give it a go, you will soon stop worrying."
- **STL paywall:** Confirms that Teso's STL files are now behind a paywall, which is why he designed his own version.
- **Phoenix turbine:** Notes that a hybrid of his blisk concept may already exist, called the "phoenix turbine."
- **Steam as the intended power source:** Clarifies he specifically wants to drive the turbine with steam, not just compressed air.
- **Metal 3D printing:** Considering sending the blisk design out for metal 3D printing to handle higher stresses.
- **Blade force distribution:** Explains that each individual blade only experiences a small fraction of the total aerodynamic force, not the full load.
- **Generator progress:** States he is already working on completing the generator (adding coils and magnets).
