## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of replacing mechanical springs in a Da Vinci-inspired catapult with magnets acting as a contactless spring. He demonstrates a 3D-printed "magnetic battery" — a holder that keeps two like-pole magnets in registration so their repulsive force stores energy, which is then released to fire the catapult. The core insight is that magnet repulsion behaves identically to a spring but without the fatigue and failure associated with physical contact.
