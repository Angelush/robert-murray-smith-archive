## Overview
This short competition-entry video demonstrates a simple experiment combining two phenomena: an inductive flyback effect (a voltage spike produced when an inductor is disconnected) and a basic homemade motor built from a battery, magnets, and a bearing. The setup uses strips of tape to intermittently break the circuit, triggering the flyback spike to drive the motor. Robert closes with a caution that the battery is being run in a near-short-circuit condition, which is not recommended.

## Key Topics
- Inductive flyback effect — voltage spikes generated when an inductor is suddenly disconnected
- Simple battery-and-magnet motor construction
- Circuit interruption using tape strips to trigger the flyback pulse
- Using a transistor and a second winding to produce a flyback-driven oscillation
- Combining the flyback coil with the spinning motor as a competition demonstration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- AA or similar battery (used as both power source and rotor hub)
- Permanent magnets (at least two — one for the motor, one as a shaft attachment)
- Bearing
- Strips of tape (used to break the circuit intermittently)
- Inductor/coil winding
- Diode
- Transistor
- Ferrite or similar inductor core (implied by "second winding on the core")

## Techniques and Methods
- Building a flyback oscillator using a transistor and dual-wound inductor core
- Constructing a battery-magnet homopolar-style motor with a bearing
- Using tape strips on a rotating assembly to mechanically interrupt current and trigger flyback pulses
- Coupling a flyback coil to the motor shaft via a small magnet
