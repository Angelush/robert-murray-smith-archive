## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a large DIY tumble cleaner from a 30-litre food storage container, designed to clean rust, oil, paint, and grease from chunky steel parts. Motivated by the complexity of his earlier shaker-based cleaner (which required welding and a large motor), he devised this simpler friction-drive rotary drum approach using salvaged and inexpensive components. The build requires no welding and uses a repurposed drill motor as the power source.

## Key Topics
- Limitations of the previous shaker cleaner design (large motor, welding required)
- Using a small commercial stone tumbler as inspiration
- Scaling up the tumble cleaner concept to handle larger steel parts
- Friction drive mechanism using a modified drill motor
- Roller cradle construction for supporting and spinning the drum
- Gear ratio analogy applied to friction drive (small wheel driving large drum = high torque, low speed)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **30-litre food storage container** — repurposed as the tumbling drum (sourced from eBay, ~£10)
- **Neoprene foam tape** (door insulation tape) — applied in two-strip-thick layers to create rubber drive rings on the drum
- **Rubber tubing/strips** — wrapped over the neoprene tape to form the drive rings
- **Printer rollers** — used as the cradle rollers the drum rests and spins on
- **Caster wheels** — used both as the friction drive wheel (with foam/rubber applied) and as support casters for the lid-end of the drum
- **Angle metal and wooden board** — cradle frame construction
- **Super glue** — fixing support casters to the cradle
- **Gravel** — tumbling media to be added with parts
- **"Miracle cleaning solution"** — referenced but not specified in this video

## Techniques and Methods
- **Friction drive** — a rubber-coated caster wheel (chucked into a repurposed drill motor) presses against the drum's rubber rings to spin it
- **Drum cradle construction** — two free-spinning printer rollers mounted on an angle-metal/wood frame support and allow the drum to rotate
- **Anti-progression blocking** — wooden stop blocks and support casters prevent the drum from drifting axially off the rollers
- **Drill motor repurposing** — handle sawn off a drill; motor and chuck retained and mounted to drive the friction wheel
- **Variable speed control** — voltage regulation used to adjust drum rotation speed
- **Tumble/barrel cleaning** — slow rotation of drum with gravel and cleaning solution to abrasively clean metal parts
