## Overview

An introductory lecture from Robert Murray-Smith's practical graphene course, aimed at hobbyists and kitchen-table experimenters rather than lab scientists. Robert explains what graphene is, situates it among the carbon allotropes, and surveys the two main production philosophies (top-down and bottom-up), concluding that choice of method should always be driven by the intended end application — and that accepting material limitations is more productive than chasing perfection.
