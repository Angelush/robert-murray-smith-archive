## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of a self-charging solar battery using Prussian Blue, a photochromic/electrochromic material. He demonstrates how Prussian Blue on filter paper can store energy when bleached white by sunlight, then release it as electrical current while reverting to blue. The video combines a brief survey of solar cell efficiency history with a hands-on construction of a simple Prussian Blue cell that charges a supercapacitor.

## Key Topics
- History and efficiency of solar cells (copper oxide, silicon, perovskites, tandem cells)
- The Shockley-Queisser efficiency limit (~33.7%)
- The concept of a solar cell that is also its own battery
- Prussian Blue as a photoelectrochemical energy storage material
- Hands-on synthesis of Prussian Blue on filter paper
- Construction and demonstration of a simple Prussian Blue solar battery cell
- Electrochromic windows as a related technology

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Copper oxide** — early, low-efficiency solar cell material (~4%)
- **Silicon** — modern solar cells with 18–22% efficiency
- **Perovskites** — emerging solar material at ~25% efficiency, stability issues
- **Prussian Blue (iron(III) hexacyanoferrate)** — the core electrochromic energy storage material
- **Potassium ferrocyanide (K4)** — one synthesis route for Prussian Blue, paired with an iron(III) salt
- **Iron(III) chloride** — iron source for the K4 synthesis route
- **Potassium ferricyanide (K3)** — the synthesis route used in the video
- **Iron(II) sulfate** — iron source for the K3 route; noted as a fertilizer and cheaper option
- **Potassium chloride (3M solution)** — used as the electrolyte
- **Aluminium** — used as one electrode, assists oxidation of Prussian Blue to Prussian White
- **Stainless steel mesh** — used as the semi-transparent counter electrode
- **Coffee filter paper** — substrate for depositing Prussian Blue

## Techniques and Methods
- **Prussian Blue synthesis via dip-coating** — filter paper strips soaked in potassium ferricyanide solution, dried, then dipped in excess iron(II) sulfate solution to produce insoluble Prussian Blue
- **Cell assembly** — layering aluminium foil, separator paper soaked in KCl electrolyte, Prussian Blue paper, and stainless steel mesh
- **Supercapacitor charging test** — voltage across a supercapacitor monitored to confirm the cell's discharge output (~0.8 V initial)
- **Solar recharging** — bleached (Prussian White) cell exposed to sunlight to regenerate Prussian Blue and restore charge capacity
