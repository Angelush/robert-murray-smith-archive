## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith repurposes a resin 3D printer (Elegoo Saturn 16K Ultra) as a UV exposure unit to create cyanotype prints — a 19th-century photographic process invented by John Herschel in 1842. He walks through the full workflow from coating paper with the cyanotype chemistry to developing and then toning the prints in tea to produce permanent, fade-resistant black iron-tannate images.
