## Overview
Robert demonstrates a quick and affordable method for copper-coating steel using a homemade copper plating gel made from copper particles suspended in copper formate. The technique allows him to give steel parts a convincing copper appearance for a steampunk project, avoiding the expense and softness of solid copper. The process is simple: clean the steel, apply the gel, brush with water, and buff to a shine.

## Key Topics
- Motivation for copper-coating steel instead of using solid copper (cost and strength)
- Steampunk aesthetic project using repurposed steel tabletop parts
- Demonstration of the copper formate/copper particle plating gel
- Application process: surface prep, gel application, brushing, and buffing
- Reference to an earlier video on making the copper nanoparticle gel

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Steel** — base material being coated (repurposed steel tabletops)
- **Copper formate** — component of the plating gel acting as the copper-ion carrier
- **Copper particles/nanoparticles** — suspended in the gel to deposit copper onto the steel surface
- **Acetone** — used to clean the steel surface before plating
- **Sandpaper** — used to remove the zinc/mill scale coating from the steel prior to plating

## Techniques and Methods
- Surface preparation: sanding to remove coating, then wiping with acetone to degrease
- Application of copper formate gel with a brush and water to initiate copper deposition
- Buffing the deposited copper layer to achieve a shiny finish
- Electroless (chemical displacement) copper plating — no electrical current required
