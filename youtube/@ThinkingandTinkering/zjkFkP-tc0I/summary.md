## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates that it is possible to solder standard electrical wire directly onto FWG graphene ink, completing a simple standalone circuit. While the experiment succeeds, Robert ultimately concludes that mechanical fixing (crimping or bolting) is his preferred and recommended method for attaching to the ink.

## Key Topics
- Whether conventional solder can adhere to graphene ink
- Demonstrating soldering technique on a graphene ink circuit board
- Alternative methods for attaching wires to graphene ink (clamping, crimping, riveting, using ink as cold solder)
- Comparison of soldering vs. mechanical fixing for graphene ink connections

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **FWG graphene ink** — the conductive ink being soldered to
- **C-solder** — the specific solder used in the experiment, noted to flow well and adhere to the ink
- **Zinc-specific solder** — mentioned in comments as relevant for certain applications
- **9V battery** — used to test the completed circuit

## Techniques and Methods
- **Pre-tinning** — Robert notes it is better to pre-tin the solder before applying
- **Soldering** — applying C-solder directly to graphene ink traces
- **Mechanical fixing (crimping/bolting)** — discussed as the preferred alternative connection method
- **Using graphene ink as cold solder** — Robert's own favourite bonding approach

## Robert's Own Replies
- Robert confirms the solder used is called **C-solder** and recommends it for this application.
- He clarifies that the bond is **not chemical — it is a wetting phenomenon and a mechanical bond**.
- He acknowledges the experiment worked but personally recommends **mechanical fixing (crimping or bolting) every time** as the more reliable and simpler approach.
- He tested the joint minimally (picked the board up by the wire and dangled it), so the durability was not rigorously assessed.
- He notes the graphene ink is "very fluid" and won't behave like a rigid substrate.
- He mentions a zinc-specific solder that flows well, as potentially relevant.
- He is open to making a follow-up video exploring bonding methods in more depth.
