## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a 3D-printed nutation drive — a compact reduction gear based on the phenomenon of nutation (simultaneous wobbling and rotation). He explains the mechanism's history, traces it to Peter Davidson's 1973 patent, walks through the assembly of a four-bevel-gear system he designed in Tinkercad, and reflects on why a demonstrably superior mechanism never displaced the planetary gear systems found in servo motors.
