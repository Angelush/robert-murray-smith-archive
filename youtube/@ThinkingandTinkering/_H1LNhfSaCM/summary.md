## Overview
This video documents the construction of a DIY coffee table shaped and painted to resemble a retro floppy disk, complete with a functional aluminium sliding mechanism that mimics the floppy disk's metal shutter. The table's standout feature is a hidden secret compartment revealed when the sliding panel is pushed to one side. The build proceeds from raw MDF and wood through joinery, fabrication, and finishing, resulting in a green-painted, varnished piece with a toner-transfer label sticker.

## Key Topics
- Concept and design inspiration for a floppy disk coffee table with a secret compartment
- Constructing the table base with mitered joints and dowel joinery
- Fabricating a sliding aluminium mechanism to replicate a floppy disk shutter
- Building a hidden storage compartment beneath the sliding panel
- Leg construction, levelling, and attachment with a lower shelf
- Surface preparation using poly filler to suppress wood grain
- Finishing with primer, green paint, and varnish
- Water-resistance testing of the varnished sticker

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **MDF** — used for the main tabletop surface
- **Timber/wood** — used for the frame, mitered border joints, and legs
- **Aluminium sheet** — fabricated into the sliding shutter mechanism
- **Dowel** — used to reinforce mitered joints without screws
- **Poly filler** — applied to surface to prevent wood grain showing through paint
- **Homemade fiberglass** — used to install and secure the secret compartment
- **Primer** (white) — base coat before colour paint
- **Green paint** — final colour chosen to evoke retro floppy disk aesthetics
- **Varnish** — applied as a water-resistant topcoat over the paint and sticker
- **Wood glue** — used with dowels and for clamping the MDF panel
- **Countersunk screws** — used to reinforce the tabletop panel, heads recessed below surface

## Techniques and Methods
- Mitered joint construction for the table border frame
- Dowel joinery to reinforce joints without visible fixings
- Countersinking screws so heads sit flush below the surface (filled and painted over)
- Sheet aluminium fabrication for the sliding shutter panel
- Homemade fiberglass lay-up to secure the hidden compartment insert
- Toner transfer method (referred to as "todd printed") for applying the floppy disk label sticker
- Sanding between stages for surface preparation
- Poly filler application to create a smooth, grain-free painting surface
- Priming, painting, and varnishing sequence
- Water-drop test to verify varnish water resistance
