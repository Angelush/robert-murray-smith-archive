## Overview
A concise explainer on the five functions of gears — transferring motion, reversing direction, changing angle, and trading off speed against torque. Robert debunks the common misconception that adding gears increases power output, clarifying that gears always introduce losses and their real purpose is matching the speed and torque requirements of different parts of a machine.

## Key Topics
- The five jobs gears can do: transfer motion, reverse direction, change angle, change speed, change torque
- The speed/torque trade-off: increasing one always decreases the other
- Analogy between gears and levers (torque as rotational force)
- Why adding gears to a wind turbine does NOT increase power
- When gears are genuinely needed (e.g., matching low turbine RPM to high generator RPM)
- Coreless wind turbine designs and lower torque requirements
- Alternatives to gears: pulleys/belts and chains (simpler but lossier)
- Encouraging out-of-the-box thinking in machine design

## Techniques and Methods
- Gear reduction: using a small driving gear meshed with a large driven gear to amplify torque at the cost of speed
- Gear step-up: large driving gear to small driven gear to increase shaft speed (e.g., for generators)
- Pulley-and-rope transmission as a lower-cost alternative to gears
- Chain drive as another mechanical power transmission option
- Coreless/cogging-free generator designs to reduce torque requirements
