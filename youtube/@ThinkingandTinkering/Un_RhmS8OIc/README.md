## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains what amperes (amps) actually are — a flow of electrons past a point — and dismantles the notion that they are abstract or unreal. Using simple analogies (a ball on a table, a wheelbarrow with stones), he walks through how electrons must be "shoved" by a magnetic field, through a completed circuit, against a load, in order for amps to flow. He closes with speculative thoughts on generator design, suggesting that prioritising high voltage at low amperage — then transforming it down — may offer advantages in wind turbine efficiency.
