## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith takes his DIY dual-rotor axial flux wind turbine to a hilltop for a real-world field test, mounting it on a broom handle to demonstrate it in natural wind conditions. At wind speeds of only 2.5–3.4 m/s, the turbine spins at approximately 1,500 RPM and generates enough power (~30V, ~100 mA) to light an LED, validating the dual-rotor design as a genuine high-efficiency solution. He argues that dual-rotor turbines are among the best practical methods for approaching or exceeding the Betz limit in small-scale wind generation.
