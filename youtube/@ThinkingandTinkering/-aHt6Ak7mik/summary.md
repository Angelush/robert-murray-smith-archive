## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith takes his DIY dual-rotor axial flux wind turbine to a hilltop for a real-world field test, mounting it on a broom handle to demonstrate it in natural wind conditions. At wind speeds of only 2.5–3.4 m/s, the turbine spins at approximately 1,500 RPM and generates enough power (~30V, ~100 mA) to light an LED, validating the dual-rotor design as a genuine high-efficiency solution. He argues that dual-rotor turbines are among the best practical methods for approaching or exceeding the Betz limit in small-scale wind generation.

## Key Topics

## Techniques and Methods
- **Field testing** — real-world outdoor test on a hill rather than a lab or wind tunnel
- **Stroboscopic RPM estimation** — using camera frame rate (25 fps) to infer rotor speed (~1,500 RPM) from apparent standstill in footage
- **Tachometer measurement** — confirmed RPM reading with a physical tachometer (silver strip visible in footage)
- **LED as a rough power indicator** — an LED (turn-on voltage ~25V, draw ~100–120 mA) used as a qualitative load to verify generation
- **Wind speed measurement** — handheld anemometer readings (2.5–3.4 m/s)
- **3D printed parts** — redesigned base/foot uploaded to Thingiverse

## Robert's Own Replies
- **RPM confirmation:** Robert clarified he also used a physical tachometer (with a silver strip) in addition to the frame-rate estimation, directly verifying the ~1,500 RPM figure.
- **Rotor spacing rationale:** He didn't use a specific spacing out of habit — the 12 cm gap between rotors was deliberately chosen because it equals roughly 0.25× the rotor diameter, a principled design decision.
- **Anchoring advice:** For mounting, he recommends three anchor points set 120° apart.
- **Overspeed braking:** He would use a friction brake to control runaway rotor speed.
- **Lab meters on the hill:** He humorously clarified he didn't bring his meters (each costing over £1,000, lab-grade) up the hill — hence the LED as a proxy.
- **Local wind industry:** He noted there are companies in his small hometown of Herne Bay that work in this space.
