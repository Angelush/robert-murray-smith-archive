## Overview

Robert demonstrates that LEDs can operate in reverse — shining light on them generates a small voltage and current, just as spinning a motor generates electricity. He pulls apart broken LED lighting units (torch, floodlight, undercabinet strip) to harvest their LED arrays and tests them as improvised solar cells, successfully charging a capacitor. His main takeaway is that COB LEDs salvaged from scrap make a surprisingly capable and essentially free DIY solar panel.
