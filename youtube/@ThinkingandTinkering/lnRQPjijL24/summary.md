## Overview
Luke (presenting on Robert Murray-Smith's channel) demonstrates how to build a contra-rotation pulley mechanism as part of the "mechanical alphabet" series. Using two pairs of MDF pulleys, a leather bootlace belt, and a single twisted belt configuration, the mechanism drives two output shafts spinning in opposite directions simultaneously. The build highlights how this same principle appears in Robert's Wimshurst machine video and in contra-rotating helicopter rotor systems.

## Key Topics
- Contra-rotation pulley mechanism as a foundational mechanical concept
- Construction of MDF pulleys using a hole saw and lathe (or improvised cutting tool)
- Belt fabrication from a leather bootlace, joined with super glue and stitching
- Installing one straight belt and one twisted belt to achieve opposite rotation directions
- Real-world applications: Wimshurst machine and contra-rotating helicopter rotors

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **MDF (medium-density fibreboard)** — used to make the pulley discs
- **Skater bearings** — pressed into the small pulleys to allow free rotation
- **Leather bootlace** — used as the belt material
- **Super glue** — used to join belt ends and secure knots
- **Tongue (wood stain/finish)** — applied to the MDF to darken and improve appearance
- **Axles** — metal rod used to mount both large and small pulleys

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting pulley discs from MDF with a **hole saw**
- Turning the pulley groove using a **lathe** (a sharpened screwdriver as an improvised alternative is noted)
- Sizing the belt by wrapping around pulleys, marking, and cutting **2–3 mm shorter** than measured
- Joining belt ends with **super glue** followed by **two 1 mm drilled holes, 4–5 stitches, and a whipped finish**
- Fitting a **straight belt** on one pair of pulleys for same-direction drive
- Fitting a **twisted (crossed) belt** on the other pair to reverse rotation direction
- Applying wood finish (tongue) to MDF for durability and aesthetics
