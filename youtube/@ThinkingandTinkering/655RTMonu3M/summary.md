## Overview
On Boxing Day 2021, Robert makes an improvised wooden catapult toy entirely from kitchen items "stolen" from around the house — a rolling pin, a breadboard, and a salad spoon. The build is spontaneous and unplanned, loosely inspired by a quick glance at Roman catapult designs online, and results in a functional toy capable of firing chocolates across a room.

## Key Topics
- Improvised Boxing Day woodworking project
- Building a functional catapult from domestic kitchen utensils
- Using a Roman catapult as a loose design reference
- Making wheels from a rolling pin and filling the holes with breadboard plugs
- Fitting an elastic band to complete the catapult mechanism

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Rolling pin** — cut into discs to serve as wheels
- **Breadboard** — used for wheel plugs and the catapult frame
- **Salad spoon** — repurposed as the catapult arm
- **Bolts** — used as axles for the wheels
- **Elastic band** — provides the launch tension

## Techniques and Methods
- Cross-cutting a rolling pin into wheel discs
- Turning/cutting cylindrical plugs from a breadboard to fill the wheel centre holes
- Freehand frame design with no pre-drawn plans ("making it up as I go")
- Assembling a frame with cross beams, uprights, and spreaders (spreaders cut to 7 cm)
- Fitting wheels on bolt axles
- Attaching an elastic band to the spoon arm to complete the catapult action
