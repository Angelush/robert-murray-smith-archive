## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith examines a brand-new Lego worm gear released in April 2023, reverse-engineers its geometry, and 3D prints his own replicas in multiple sizes. Unlike traditional worm gears, this design can be back-driven and arranged in a cross formation where each gear drives the other — a property Robert successfully replicates with his printed versions. He shares the files on Thingiverse and invites the community to explore applications he hasn't yet thought of himself.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to Lego's April 2023 new worm gear and its unusual properties
- Back-drivability: why this worm gear differs from conventional worm gears
- Cross-formation gear arrangement: two gears driving each other perpendicularly
- Geometric analysis: identifying the gear as two turns of an Archimedes screw, rotated 90°
- 3D printing replicas in three sizes (Lego-size, medium, planned large)
- Practical testing of the cross-gear arrangement
- Potential future applications: cycloidal drives, high gear ratios (~100:1)
- References to community creators using the gear (walker/robotic walking machines)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly brief social exchanges, but a few are notable:
- **"it's not lego design it's lego inspired"** — clarifies that his printed gear is an original design inspired by Lego, not a copy of Lego's design.
