## Overview
A father-and-son pair, Alistair and Sam, build and test a simple water turbine as a competition entry. They repurpose an old food processor motor as a generator, spin it with a water-jet-driven wheel, and measure the electrical output. Their final result is approximately 1.5–1.6 watts of generated power.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging a motor from a discarded food processor for use as a generator
- Designing and assembling a basic water turbine from scratch
- Measuring voltage and current output from a hand-built turbine
- Demonstrating small-scale hydroelectric power generation for a competition

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Old food processor motor (repurposed as a generator)
- Turbine wheel (attached to the motor shaft)
- Water nozzle (to direct a jet onto the wheel)
- Voltmeter / multimeter (for voltage and current measurements)
- LED (used as an electrical load)
- Resistor (added in series with the LED to prevent it from blowing)

## Techniques and Methods
- Mounting a salvaged motor on a stand to act as a generator
- Directing a water jet onto a wheel to drive rotation
- Adding a backing/splash guard to protect the motor from water
- Connecting a load (LED + resistor) and measuring watts output (V × I)
