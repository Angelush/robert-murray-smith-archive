## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith offers practical advice on Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs), warning inventors and collaborators to read them carefully before signing. His main takeaway is that NDAs are two-way agreements, and an overly long or one-sided NDA is a red flag — both about the document itself and the character of the party proposing it.

## Key Topics
- What an NDA is and its legitimate purpose
- The inverse relationship between document length and benefit to the signer
- Clauses to watch out for: perpetual work obligations, no payment rights, no time limits
- The two-way nature of NDAs — both parties bring value to the table
- Red flags that suggest a potential collaborator is not worth working with
- Advice for those writing NDAs: keep them fair and reasonable

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Reading and critically evaluating NDA documents before signing
- Inserting a time limit clause if one is absent
- Using pro forma NDAs with caution and reviewing them personally before use
