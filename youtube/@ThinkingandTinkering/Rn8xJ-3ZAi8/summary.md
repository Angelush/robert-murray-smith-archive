## Overview
This is Part 3 of Robert's DIY gasification and generator unit build series, focusing on fabricating and fitting an access door to a stainless steel beer barrel gasifier. Robert is simultaneously learning to weld and building the gasifier, and uses the video to share beginner-friendly welding tricks he discovered along the way — particularly that an angle grinder is an essential recovery tool for imperfect welds.

## Key Topics
- Progress update on the DIY gasifier build using a stainless steel beer barrel
- Design and fabrication of an access port/door for ash removal and cleaning
- Beginner welding techniques and practical workarounds
- Use of high-temperature sealant for sealing the access door
- The dual purpose of the project: building a functional gasifier *and* learning to weld

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Stainless steel beer barrel — main gasifier vessel
- Flat bar steel — used for the door frame
- Angle iron/steel — used in door frame construction
- High-temperature sealant (rated to 1200°C) — for sealing the access door flange
- Welding rod stubs — repurposed as gap filler material (flux knocked off first)
- Fireproof felt, wool, or rope — intended to seal the door against leaks

## Techniques and Methods
- MMA/stick welding — primary welding method used throughout the build
- Angle grinding — used to remove excess weld material and rework imperfect welds
- Rolling flat bar — to fit the door frame flange to the curved barrel
- Using leftover welding rod stubs as filler — a tip shared by community members: knock the flux off short rod offcuts and use them to bridge gaps before welding over
- Bolting the door frame flange to the barrel with sealant — rather than welding the door permanently shut
