## Overview

Robert demonstrates his graphite electrolysis cell running on a urea-based electrolyte (14% urea, 1% potassium hydroxide) instead of the usual potassium hydroxide solution, showing it successfully produces hydrogen gas. The cell runs at 12 volts across 6 cells (averaging 2 V per cell), draws low current, and notably does not heat up during a half-hour run. The key insight is that urea requires significantly less voltage to electrolyze than water (0.47 V vs 1.23 V), making the process net energy-positive since urea's bond energy is lower than water's.
