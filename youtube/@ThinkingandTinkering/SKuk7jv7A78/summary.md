## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a DIY camera lucida — an optical drawing aid — by salvaging a prism from a broken LCD projector. The device superimposes a scene onto paper so the user can trace it accurately. He argues that discarded LCD projectors are a rich source of high-quality optics, and that making a camera lucida from one is a genuinely mind-blowing experience.

## Key Topics
- What a camera lucida is and how it works (prism-based optical superimposition)
- Disassembling a broken LCD projector to recover usable optics
- Extracting the dichroic prism assembly from the projector's optical block
- Setting up the prism as a functional camera lucida
- Other reuses for salvaged optics (magnifier, semi-transparent mirrors as lucida alternatives)
- Historical popularity of the camera lucida for portraiture and landscape drawing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **LCD projector** (broken/discarded) — primary source of salvaged optics
- **Dichroic prism** (from the LCD projector's optical block) — core component of the camera lucida
- **Front-surface (front-facing) mirrors** — recovered from projector optics
- **Optical filters** — recovered from projector, noted as particularly interesting
- **Lenses** — recovered from the projector's focusing section
- **LCD display screens** (×3, RGB) — removed to access the prism housing
- **High-voltage power supply** (lamp driver) — noted as scavengeable electronics

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling LCD projectors using screws and, where necessary, percussive encouragement ("encourage it with a hammer")
- Prising off LCD panels from the optical housing to expose the prism
- Mounting the extracted prism in a stand to function as a camera lucida
- Using semi-transparent mirrors at the same angle as the prism to replicate the lucida effect
- Scavenging reusable electronics and optics from discarded consumer hardware

## Robert's Own Replies
The author replies in the comments are mostly brief social exchanges (acknowledgements, laughter, thanks). The substantive points are:
- He confirms the **quality of the lenses from LCD projectors is excellent**.
- He expresses interest in **taking the prisms apart further**, and wants viewers to report back on their attempts.
- He emphasises that the camera lucida effect is genuinely remarkable: *"make one and it really will knock you dead — it's like magic."*
- He notes that differences in projector model **don't matter — the optics are the same**, encouraging people to use whatever broken projector they can find.
- He was personally inspired by a recent visit to the **Science Museum**.
