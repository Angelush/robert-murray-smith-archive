## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith presents the completed steampunk-styled air conditioning unit he built over the course of a series of videos, showcasing its aesthetic design and measuring its performance. The device uses a bellows driven by a tiny motor to move air through a radiator, achieving 27 cubic metres per hour at only 0.5 watts — giving it a per-watt efficiency of 54 m³/hr/W, more than three times better than a commercial fan he tested. Its key design advantage is the ability to draw air from any chosen location, such as a cool crawl space in summer or a warm loft in winter.
