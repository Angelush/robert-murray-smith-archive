## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents the completed steampunk-styled air conditioning unit he built over the course of a series of videos, showcasing its aesthetic design and measuring its performance. The device uses a bellows driven by a tiny motor to move air through a radiator, achieving 27 cubic metres per hour at only 0.5 watts — giving it a per-watt efficiency of 54 m³/hr/W, more than three times better than a commercial fan he tested. Its key design advantage is the ability to draw air from any chosen location, such as a cool crawl space in summer or a warm loft in winter.

## Key Topics
- Steampunk aesthetic design philosophy and construction
- Performance benchmarking against a commercial fan (Airflow Quiet Air Fan, 10W, 150–170 m³/hr)
- Per-watt air-movement efficiency as the relevant metric
- Bellows-based air displacement vs. conventional rotary fans
- Flexible inlet airflow positioning (drawing from pre-cooled or pre-heated air sources)
- Radiator as a secondary heating or cooling stage

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Water (circulated through the radiator for heating or cooling)
- String (used as a temporary drive linkage, intended to be replaced with chain)

## Techniques and Methods
- Bellows-driven air displacement (8 litres per stroke)
- Small DC motor (12V, 0.05A = 0.5W) driving a crank mechanism
- String/chain linkage from motor to bellows
- Radiator heat exchange with hot or cold water circulation
- Intake ducting to draw air from a chosen remote location (crawl space, loft, etc.)

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirmed he is using the device himself: *"it's now in mine pumping the cool air in."*
- Clarified the motor spec in response to a question: *"watts is volts x amps — it is 12 volts but 0.05 amps."*
- Explained the motivation for the build: his office gets very hot in summer while the lab stays cool, and the reverse is true in winter.
- Noted that the decorative gauge elements *"don't do anything but make it look more steampunk."*
- Described the device simply as *"basically just a lever and a bellows and a radiator."*
- Mentioned he has been collecting cola cans for a future related build.
- Teased an upcoming video related to this concept: *"I do have a plan with this in mind — up and coming video I think!"*
- Dismissed noise concerns, noting the unit likely runs below 32 dB (the threshold for a quiet fan) and that the radiator is doing active work, unlike a plain bellows.
- Expressed enthusiasm for bellows-based approaches generally: *"I think they are much under-utilised with folks heading straight for fans without really thinking about the possibilities."*
- In response to an unrelated question about graphitic carbon nitride, he noted it is a photocatalyst for water splitting but was unsure of its relevance to steam injection without further reading.
