## Overview

This short follow-up video revisits Robert's homemade supercapacitor experiment, presenting energy density calculations and a direct performance comparison against a commercial supercapacitor. Despite using only a single electrode layer versus 12 in the commercial unit, his device stores more energy (2 J vs 1.82 J), and he demonstrates that the active material achieves up to 9 F/g in a compressed version. The main takeaway is that his liquid/printable graphene-based approach is competitive with commercial technology and could scale to a ream-of-paper-sized 2,000 Ah battery.
