## Overview
This is Part 2 of Robert's budget wind turbine build series, focusing on a quick progress update and outdoor rotor spin test. He has reoriented the rotor from horizontal to vertical, reinforced the blade attachments based on viewer feedback, and taken it to a car park to verify it spins freely in light wind — all before adding the magnets and coils.

## Key Topics
- Reorientation of the rotor from horizontal to vertical mounting
- Reinforced blade-to-pipe attachment using bolts and screws (replacing glue-only method)
- Outdoor wind test in a car park at 10–15 mph winds
- Balance quality of the assembled rotor
- Planned next steps: support structure, microwave oven transformer mounting, and magnet installation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PVC/metal pipes** — used as blade-mounting arms; bolted through to the hub
- **Blades** — screwed into the pipes for firm attachment
- **Footplate** — approximately 150 × 150 mm, used as the vertical base
- **Magnets** — not yet attached; planned for next stage
- **Microwave oven transformers** — planned to be mounted on the footplate as part of the generator
- **Coils** — planned addition alongside magnets
- **Wood** — planned for the main support structure arm

## Techniques and Methods
- Blade attachment reinforcement: bolts through each pipe plus screws through each blade into the pipe (moved away from glue-only)
- Vertical mounting on axle with footplate base for freestanding rotation test
- Open-air wind test in a car park to assess rotor response at low wind speeds (10–15 mph)
- Visual balance assessment by observing spin quality

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Wind speed for 1 kW:** Robert acknowledges the turbine will realistically need 25–30 mph winds to approach 1 kW output, but notes that commercial 1 kW fan-blade turbines require the same — he plans to measure properly with a wind speed meter and compare.
- **Weight:** He estimates it is well under 50 lbs, enough that he can lift it easily.
- **Magnets:** He is considering upgrading to Neo (neodymium) magnets if output is insufficient, but wants to keep costs down for now; he glued magnets to the bottom of the assembly.
- **Bearings:** He agrees conical/tapered bearings would be more suitable for this vertical-axle orientation but is working with what he has and believes they'll be adequate.
- **Balance:** He is genuinely surprised at how well balanced the rotor is, attributing it to the chair legs and pipe geometry rather than careful engineering.
- **Blade material:** He is considering Sintra board or uPVC foam-filled board as alternatives; aluminium would work but adds cost, which conflicts with his budget goal.
- **Openness of the build:** He repeatedly encourages viewers to replicate and improve the design and share their own videos.
