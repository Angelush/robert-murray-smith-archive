## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates running a Stirling engine using the heat of hydration from dissolving sodium hydroxide in water, inspired by the historical "soda train" — a real steam locomotive that used the same chemical principle to operate in hazardous environments like mines. He shows first that the engine runs from a hot coffee cup, then successfully powers it from caustic soda dissolved in water, presenting the concept as a rechargeable "heat battery" where the sodium hydroxide can be dried out in the sun and reused.
