## Overview
This video is a viewer competition entry featured on Robert Murray-Smith's channel, demonstrating how to build a simple voltaic pile battery using US pennies and salt-and-vinegar potato chips as the electrolyte separator. The builder stacks alternating copper and zinc penny discs with potato chip spacers to produce over 2 volts, sufficient to light white, green, and red LEDs.

## Key Topics
- Construction of a copper-zinc voltaic pile battery from everyday materials
- Differences between pre-1983 (copper) and post-1983 (zinc-core) US pennies
- Using salt-and-vinegar potato chips as an electrolyte-soaked separator
- Connecting multiple cells in series using aluminum foil tape jumpers
- LED demonstration to verify the battery output

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **US pennies (pre-1983)** — used as the copper electrodes
- **US pennies (post-1983)** — zinc-core pennies, copper plating chemically removed to expose zinc electrodes
- **Salt-and-vinegar potato chips** — soaked in water and punched into discs, serve as the electrolyte-soaked separator between cells
- **Ferric chloride (ferochloride)** — used to strip the copper plating off the zinc pennies
- **Aluminum foil tape** (duct/HVAC type) — cut into strips and used as electrical jumpers between cells
- **Plastic squares** (cut from a vinegar jug) — used as insulating dividers between cell layers
- **Water** — used to soak and soften the potato chips

## Techniques and Methods
- Stripping copper plating from post-1983 zinc pennies using ferric chloride etch
- Punching potato chips into uniform discs with a hole punch
- Assembling a voltaic pile by stacking: zinc penny → potato chip disc → copper penny → plastic divider with foil-tape jumper, repeated 4 times
- Using a folded aluminum foil tape strip to bridge (jumper) successive cells electrically
- Measuring output with a multimeter (noted as partially faulty)
- Testing battery with white, green, and red LEDs to demonstrate output current
