## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a homemade balanced armature speaker, built from a previous electromagnetic reed device by adding a bent brass arm connected to a styrofoam bowl cone on an acrylic frame. He explains the historical significance of the design (popular from the 1920s–1950s, still used in naval sound-powered telephones) and honestly critiques the design flaws in his own implementation while suggesting improvements.
