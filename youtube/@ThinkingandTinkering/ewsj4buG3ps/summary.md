## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith unboxes and tests the Elegoo Saturn 4 Ultra 16K resin printer, comparing output quality and fit tolerances directly against an FDM (filament) printer using the same model. He demonstrates that resin printing — particularly at 16K resolution — produces dramatically smoother surfaces and tighter mechanical fits (a working piston-and-cylinder assembly), while acknowledging that filament printing still has advantages in strength and workflow simplicity.

## Key Topics
- Setting up the Elegoo Saturn 4 Ultra 16K for a first print
- Choosing water-washable photopolymer resin over standard ABS-like resin to avoid strong odours and IPA usage
- Configuring slicer settings in Chitubox Basic for water-washable resin
- Side-by-side comparison of resin vs. filament printed parts (surface texture, layer ridges, strength)
- Fit and tolerance testing using a piston-and-cylinder model
- Washing and curing workflow with the Mercury wash station
- Practical use-case conclusions: resin for precision parts, filament for general/structural parts

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Water-washable photopolymer resin** — chosen as the print medium to reduce odour and avoid IPA washing
- **Standard ABS-like photopolymer resin (AK F polymer)** — supplied by Elegoo, noted as smelly; not used in this video
- **IPA (isopropyl alcohol)** — the typical wash solvent for standard resin; avoided here due to cost and smell
- **WD-40** — used as a lubricant to test piston-cylinder fit

## Techniques and Methods
- Slicer configuration in **Chitubox Basic** — adjusting exposure time (4 s) and bottom exposure time (80 s) for water-washable resin
- File transfer via **USB ("sneaker net")** and Wi-Fi options
- **Washing** printed parts in the Mercury wash station (up to 30 minutes, auto-direction reversal)
- **UV curing** in the Mercury station (30 minutes)
- **Split-screen calibration** — using the Saturn's ability to divide the build plate into four zones to test different exposure settings
- **Tolerance/fit testing** — printing the same piston-and-cylinder model in both technologies and comparing dry and lubricated fit
- **Lung-power pneumatic test** — blowing into the printed cylinder to demonstrate airtight piston movement

## Robert's Own Replies
A few substantive points emerge from the comment replies:

- **Rounded corners in design** — Robert confirms he rounded the bases of supported features intentionally to prevent "elephant foot" artefacts forming during printing.
- **Camera/Wi-Fi safety** — He advises not staying in the same room during a print because the machine has Wi-Fi and a camera, implying remote monitoring is preferable.
- **Parting compound** — Suggests a parting compound as a possible solution (likely for demoulding or releasing stuck parts).
- **Cost tracking** — Notes that the slicer can calculate cost per print if you input the resin price.
- **Fresnel lens with transparent resin** — Mentions he is pondering using a Fresnel lens with transparent resin following a community suggestion.
- **Elegoo Saturn 3 Ultra** — Endorses it as "a good machine" in response to a viewer comment.
- **Possible copyright issue** — Responds to a viewer who flagged someone reposting his content without permission, asking for details so he can have it taken down.
