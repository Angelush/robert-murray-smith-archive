## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make custom-fit latex rubber gloves and reusable molds using a plaster of paris cast (previously made from alginate molding powder in video 1277). The technique relies on dry plaster of paris absorbing moisture from liquid latex, depositing a latex layer on the surface. The longer the cast is submerged, the thicker the resulting latex layer — enabling everything from thin gloves to durable molds capable of hundreds of uses.
