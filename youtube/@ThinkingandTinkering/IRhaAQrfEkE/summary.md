## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make custom-fit latex rubber gloves and reusable molds using a plaster of paris cast (previously made from alginate molding powder in video 1277). The technique relies on dry plaster of paris absorbing moisture from liquid latex, depositing a latex layer on the surface. The longer the cast is submerged, the thicker the resulting latex layer — enabling everything from thin gloves to durable molds capable of hundreds of uses.

## Key Topics
- Continuation of video 1277 (alginate hand casting)
- Motivation: making custom-fit gloves for a friend with difficulty finding gloves that fit
- How plaster of paris absorbs moisture from liquid latex to form a surface layer
- Controlling latex thickness via dip time
- Using baby powder to prevent latex from sticking to itself during peeling
- Broader applications: costume props, replacement parts, reusable molds

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Alginate molding powder** ("alternate molding powder") — used in the prior video to cast the hand
- **Plaster of paris** — used as the positive cast; must be fully dry to work
- **Liquid latex rubber** — water dispersion applied by dipping; available on Amazon
- **Baby powder / talcum powder** — applied to cured latex to prevent self-adhesion before peeling
- **Resin** — mentioned as an alternative to plaster of paris for the positive cast
- **Silicone** — mentioned as an alternative mold material for high-volume production

## Techniques and Methods
- **Alginate casting** — capturing fine surface detail of body parts (covered in video 1277)
- **Plaster of paris casting** — creating a reusable positive from the alginate mold
- **Latex dip coating** — submerging the dry plaster cast in liquid latex; moisture is absorbed by the plaster, leaving a latex film on the surface
- **Latex painting / spraying / pouring** — alternative application methods for wet or non-absorbent forms
- **Air drying** — latex cures in open air; no heat required
- **Baby powder release** — dusting cured latex before peeling to prevent self-sticking
- **Multi-part mold workflow** — alginate → plaster cast → latex or silicone mold → repeated production casting
