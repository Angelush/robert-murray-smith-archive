## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores a recently published (at time of filming) open-access research paper on a water-activated paper battery made from paper, carbon, zinc, and table salt. He demonstrates the construction process by preparing three inks and assembling the battery, then tests it live — first measuring voltage, then powering a digital clock with two cells. The key takeaway is that this biodegradable battery offers comparable or superior energy density to a AA battery gram-for-gram, and represents a compelling, environmentally friendly alternative to the billions of disposable batteries that end up in landfill each year.
