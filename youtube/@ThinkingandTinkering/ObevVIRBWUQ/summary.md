## Overview
This video demonstrates how to convert any pair of gloves into capacitive touch gloves, allowing the wearer to use touchscreen devices without removing their gloves. The technique involves soaking ordinary thread in conductive ink, letting it dry, and then sewing it onto the fingertip areas of the gloves. The method is shown to work effectively even with thick, insulated gardening gloves.

## Key Topics
- Why standard gloves fail on capacitive touchscreens
- Selecting which fingers to modify (thumb and index finger recommended)
- Soaking thread in conductive ink as the core preparation step
- Sewing technique for attaching conductive thread to glove fingertips
- Live demonstration of the finished gloves working on a smartphone

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **3M insulated sight gardening gloves** — the test gloves used in the video (thick, normally non-conductive)
- **Conductive ink** — used to soak ordinary thread and make it electrically conductive
- **Standard sewing thread** — soaked in conductive ink (~2 metres cut per finger)
- **Sewing needle** — for attaching the treated thread to the glove
- **Broom handle / 22mm dowel** — used as a form inside the glove finger to prevent accidentally sewing the fingertip shut

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting a length of thread (~2 metres) and fully submerging it in conductive ink for 5–10 minutes
- Holding thread over the edge of the ink container to wring off excess ink before drying
- Inserting a dowel or broom handle into the glove finger before sewing to keep the fingertip open
- Sewing the dried conductive thread in overlapping passes around the fingertip area (not the very tip, but the pad region)
- Testing conductivity live on a smartphone touchscreen after completion
