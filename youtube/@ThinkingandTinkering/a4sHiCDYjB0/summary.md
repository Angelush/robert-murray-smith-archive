## Overview
This short tutorial video covers the final wiring steps for the motor assembly of a homemade Van de Graaff generator. Robert demonstrates how to correctly connect the power supply wires to a dimmer switch (identifying the common and L1/live-out terminals), close up the housing, and perform a basic test run showing variable speed control. The key takeaway is that while the wiring is straightforward household electrics, Robert cautions that basic electrical confidence is essential given the voltages involved.

## Key Topics
- Closing and securing the motor housing with M3.5 screws
- Wiring the power supply leads to the correct switch terminals (common and L1)
- Proper cable management to ensure double insulation is maintained
- Using a dimmer switch for variable motor speed control
- Safety warning regarding mains electricity

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Connecting mains supply wires to a combined on/off dimmer switch, with correct terminal identification (common vs. L1)
- Cable strain relief: pushing cable in to ensure two layers of insulation are visible at the clamp, with no bare conductor exposed
- Securing housing panels with M3.5 screws
- Live test of motor speed control via dimmer switch
