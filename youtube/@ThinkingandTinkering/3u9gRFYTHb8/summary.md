## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores water as a viable home energy storage medium, revisiting historical hydraulic technology rather than only looking at cutting-edge research. He argues that combining water motors (capable of up to 1/3 horsepower) with an accumulator — a pressurised water tank used widely in Britain until the 1960s–70s — creates a long-lasting, low-maintenance "water battery" that could power a home without relying on mains pressure. The key takeaway is that by scavenging energy from everyday household water use (toilet flushing, bathing), this system is practical even for urban dwellers with no stream or outdoor water source.

## Key Topics
- Historical water motors and their relevance to modern home energy generation
- Legal restrictions on tapping UK mains pressure for power (illegal since 1999)
- Scavenging energy from household water consumption (~150 litres/person/day in the UK)
- Accumulators as long-duration "water batteries" — pressurised tanks with a weighted plunger
- Comparison of accumulators vs. lithium battery banks (longevity, safety, simplicity)
- Off-peak electricity used to recharge an accumulator for daytime use
- Pelton wheel construction as an accessible DIY water motor
- The philosophy of "10% of something beats 0% of everything" — favouring practical action over perfect efficiency

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Water (as working fluid and energy storage medium)
- Teaspoons (repurposed as Pelton wheel buckets for DIY construction)

## Techniques and Methods
- Water motor operation using mains or accumulated water pressure
- Accumulator design: sealed tank, watertight plunger, and weighted top to supply constant pressure (up to 800 psi)
- Energy scavenging from toilet cistern refill (~25 minutes of motor run time per day at 50 litres) and bath/shower fill cycles
- Closed-loop water recycling: waste water collected, repressurised at off-peak rates, reused
- Pelton wheel fabrication with hand tools (handsaw-level construction accessible)
- Magnetic bearings for Pelton wheel (mentioned as a future build)
