## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith revisits his earlier Da Vinci flying pendulum investigation and asks whether the complex upper mechanism is actually necessary. He designs a stripped-down 6-part version in TinkerCAD powered by a rubber band, assembles it, and demonstrates that the simplified concept works — concluding that the original clock mechanism was over-engineered.

## Key Topics
- Re-examination of the Da Vinci flying pendulum clock mechanism
- Simplification of the design to its essential components
- TinkerCAD-based design iteration
- Proof-of-concept build and demonstration
- Future development possibilities (weight-driven mechanism, bevel gear clock drive)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 6 mm steel bar, 250 mm long
- Thrust bearing (40×42×25×11 mm)
- M12 nut
- Rubber band (100 mm long, 5 mm thick)
- String
- 3D-printed parts (6 components)

## Techniques and Methods
- CAD design in TinkerCAD
- FDM 3D printing
- Rubber band as temporary power source (substituting for a weight-driven mechanism)
- String wrapping for pendulum suspension
- Counterweight addition for rotational balance
- Ratchet winding mechanism
