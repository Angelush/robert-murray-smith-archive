## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates building a flat loudspeaker by painting conductive ink onto paper and cellophane, driven by community suggestions for new uses of his conductive ink. The video progresses through increasingly refined designs — from a bare painted piece of paper to a tensioned cellophane diaphragm in an acrylic frame — showing that a functional speaker can be made without a traditional wire coil. His broader motivation is using the same principle in reverse for environmental energy harvesting.
