## Overview
Robert demonstrates a DIY Kinetic Energy Recovery System (KERS) by connecting a concrete flywheel (built in previous videos 1118–1120) to a 500W DC motor via a chain-and-cog drive. The motor acts as a generator, storing recovered kinetic energy in a supercapacitor that then powers an LED panel — illustrating the same principle behind regenerative braking in cars and e-bikes. The video is a proof-of-concept rather than a finished device, with Robert noting clear paths for improvement around gearing and motor winding.

## Key Topics
- Kinetic Energy Recovery System (KERS) principles and real-world analogy to regenerative braking
- Connecting a concrete flywheel to a DC motor for energy generation
- Using a supercapacitor as an intermediate energy store
- Powering LEDs from captured flywheel energy
- Suggested improvements: gear ratio changes and motor rewinding for higher voltage output

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Concrete flywheel (built from cement and a bicycle wheel in prior videos)
- 500W DC motor (purchased from eBay, ~£20 each)
- Chain and cog/freewheel drive
- Supercapacitor
- "Dual thief" boost converter circuit
- LED lighting panel (728 LEDs, 25-second rated)

## Techniques and Methods
- Hand-cranking the flywheel to store kinetic energy
- Chain-and-cog power transmission between flywheel and DC motor
- Using DC motor commutator as a built-in rectifier (no separate rectification needed)
- Charging a supercapacitor from the motor output
- Boosting supercapacitor voltage via a dual-thief circuit to drive LEDs
- Proposed rewinding motor coils with thinner wire to increase output voltage
