## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith surveys the history of light as wave versus particle, explains how conventional silicon photovoltaic cells work, and then introduces the **rectenna** — a device that treats sunlight as an electromagnetic wave, using a nanoscale antenna and a Metal-Insulator-Metal (MIM) diode to convert it directly into electricity. He argues this approach is theoretically capable of near-100% efficiency (versus silicon's ~22.7% limit) and, crucially, is potentially within reach of home experimenters.
