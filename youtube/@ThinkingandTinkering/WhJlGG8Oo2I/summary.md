## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith delivers a sharp, satirical debunking of the viral internet claim that an old CD can be turned into a solar cell using Rodin coils and Schottky diodes. He argues the concept is pseudoscience — no more useful than "glueing a piece of wire onto a household mirror" — and concludes the best thing to do with an old CD is throw it into the ocean. The video ends with a wry lament that honest debunking gets far fewer views than the viral nonsense it critiques.

## Key Topics
- Debunking the "CD solar cell" internet trend
- Critique of viral pseudoscience content and its disproportionate reach
- Rodin coil arrangements and their (lack of) legitimacy as a solar cell component
- The tension between scientific integrity and YouTube virality

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **CD (compact disc)** — the subject of the debunked claim; noted in comments that modern CDs use aluminium rather than silver
- **Schottky diode** — mentioned as part of the debunked circuit
- **Rodin coils** — three coils arranged at 120-degree offsets, central to the viral claim being mocked

## Techniques and Methods
- Arrangement of three Rodin coils offset by 120 degrees (described only to debunk it)
- Placement of a Schottky diode between the coils (described only to debunk it)
