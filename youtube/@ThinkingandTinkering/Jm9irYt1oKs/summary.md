## Overview
This is a competition entry video by a fan demonstrating a PC fan converted into a small AC generator, inspired by techniques shown by Robert Murray-Smith. The creator modifies a standard computer fan by rerouting its coil wires to produce AC output, then conditions the power through a bridge rectifier and capacitor to light LEDs and a small LCD panel. The key takeaway is that a simple, cheap computer fan can be repurposed as a hand-spun or air-powered generator capable of producing 10–12V at ~100mA.

## Key Topics
- Converting a PC case fan into an AC generator by rewiring the internal coils
- Rectifying AC output to DC using a bridge rectifier
- Smoothing DC output with a capacitor and resistor
- Testing output with a voltage indicator circuit (credited to "Luke")
- Human-powered and air compressor-driven generation
- Lighting LEDs and a repurposed LCD backlight panel from the generator output
- Adding a DIY flywheel (drywall disc) for hand/pull-string operation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Standard PC case fans (two units)
- Bridge rectifier (salvaged from a printer)
- 16V, 1000µF electrolytic capacitor
- 1000Ω (1kΩ) resistors
- Small potentiometer (used in Luke's voltage indicator circuit)
- Red LED / bulb (for quick output test)
- 12V LED string
- Half of a computer LCD screen (used as a light panel/diffuser)
- Drywall piece (used as improvised flywheel)
- Hot glue (for mounting)

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling a PC fan to expose coil solder points and re-routing output wires
- Full-wave bridge rectification to convert fan AC output to DC
- Capacitive smoothing with a series bleed resistor to extend LED illumination time after spin-down
- Air compressor used to spin fan to target voltage (achieved ~10–12V)
- Pull-string flywheel mechanism for human-powered operation
- Repurposing an LCD backlight panel as a light output device
