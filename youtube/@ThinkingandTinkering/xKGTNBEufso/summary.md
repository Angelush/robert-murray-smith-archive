## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a quiet, USB-powered desktop fan using a 3D-printed toroidal propeller fitted to a salvaged PC fan motor. He provides historical context for the toroidal propeller design (tracing it back to 1890, well before MIT's widely publicised 2022 patent), and shows that a near-silent fan can be made for the cost of a few pence of filament rather than the £32 commercial equivalents he saw in stores.

## Key Topics
- The toroidal propeller: history from 1890 to MIT's 2017/2022 patent
- Why toroidal propellers are quieter and more efficient (vortices distributed along the blade length rather than concentrated at the tip)
- Application of the design to drones operating in the 1–5 Hz inaudible range
- Step-by-step build of a USB-powered toroidal fan using 3D printing
- Cost comparison: ~£5 USB PC fan + minimal filament vs ~£32 commercial toroidal fans

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 80 mm USB-powered PC fan (£5 from Amazon) — donor motor unit
- 3D-printed toroidal propeller parts (4 parts total, remixed from a Thingiverse design via Tinkercad)
- Super glue — used to fix the motor cone/hub into the printed assembly
- Cable ties — used to manage the USB cable

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly of a standard PC fan: snipping the blade guard and blade assembly to extract the bare motor hub
- 3D printing of a toroidal propeller adapter (4-part print)
- Press-fit and super-glue assembly of motor onto printed hub
- USB plug-and-play testing of the completed fan

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly brief and conversational, but a few add useful context:
- **Why toroidal fans weren't common historically:** carving the complex blade geometry by hand was difficult and expensive — 3D printing is what makes them practical and accessible now.
- **Efficiency is real:** he notes the video includes a graph backing the efficiency claims and emphasises the design is "well researched."
- **You have to make it yourself** — there is no ready-made equivalent at this price point; the DIY build is the point.
