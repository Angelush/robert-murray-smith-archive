## Overview
Luke (the presenter) demonstrates six practical methods for splicing wires together, recommending soldering for a more durable connection wherever possible. The video is a hands-on guide covering techniques ranging from simple twisted joins to tool-based connectors, suited to different situations and skill levels.

## Key Topics
- Why soldering is recommended when splicing wires
- Six distinct wire splicing methods compared
- Use of heat shrink tubing to protect and finish joints
- Tool-based vs. tool-free connection options
- Choosing the right method based on the job at hand

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Flux — applied before soldering to improve the "smush splice" connection
- Solder — used to reinforce twisted wire joints
- Heat shrink tubing — used to insulate and protect finished splices
- Two-way crimp connectors — push-in barrel connectors for tool-based crimping
- Terminal blocks — screw-down connectors cut from a larger strip
- Wire nuts — twist-on connectors described as popular in the US

## Techniques and Methods
- **Parallel twist splice** — strip wires evenly, align in parallel, twist together, solder, and cover with heat shrink
- **90-degree bend splice** — strip further down, form 90° bends in each wire, interlock and twist around each other, then solder and heat-shrink (presenter's personal favourite)
- **"Smush splice"** — push stripped wire ends together and twist with fingers for a clean join, apply flux, then solder and heat-shrink
- **Crimp connector** — strip and lightly twist wires, insert into a two-way crimp connector, and squeeze with a crimping tool
- **Terminal block** — insert stripped wires into a screw-terminal block and tighten with a flat-head screwdriver
- **Wire nut** — strip and align wires, give a light twist, then screw on a wire nut cap
