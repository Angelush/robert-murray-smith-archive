## Overview
Robert builds a DIY magnetiser/demagnetiser using salvaged squirrel cage rotors from induction motors found in a car park, repurposed into a complete unit housed in a found frying pan. The project pairs the coil with a bridge rectifier and a repurposed drill-case momentary safety switch, creating a practical tool for magnetising or demagnetising small items like screwdrivers. The build is intentionally constructed from scrap and leftover parts.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging squirrel cage rotors from induction motors for reuse as electromagnet coils
- Building a safety/momentary switch from a repurposed drill case
- Wiring a bridge rectifier to convert AC mains to DC for the coil
- Housing all components in a found frying pan as a functional stand
- Practical uses: magnetising and demagnetising tools and making small magnets

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Squirrel cage rotors (steel and copper) — salvaged from induction motors, used as the electromagnet core
- Acrylic sheet — cut as a panel to fill the modified drill case housing
- Bridge rectifier — rated ~15 amps, used to rectify AC mains supply to DC
- Compression cable gland — used to secure and strain-relieve the cable entry
- Frying pan — found in the street, repurposed as a housing/stand for the electronics
- Rubber grommets — used when mounting components into the pan

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting and modifying a plastic drill case to create a custom switch housing
- Tracing a card template from the rotor and transferring it to acrylic for a fitted panel
- Drilling a 20 mm hole for a compression gland installation
- Measuring coil resistance to identify the correct winding terminals (highest resistance = main coil, ~60 ohms)
- Wiring a bridge rectifier: AC inputs to the coil supply, DC+ and DC− outputs to the coil terminals
- Mounting electronics inside a pan with drilled holes and grommets for cable routing
