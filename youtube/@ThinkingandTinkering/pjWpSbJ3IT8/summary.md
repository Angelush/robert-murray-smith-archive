## Overview
Robert reviews the Weefun Tina 2, a budget 3D printer costing £12–27 on Amazon, as an entry-level option for people curious about 3D printing who don't want to commit to expensive equipment. He walks through unboxing, software setup, bed levelling, filament loading, and completing a first print, concluding that the machine delivers acceptable quality and lives up to its claim of being usable within minutes of unboxing.

## Key Topics
- Who the Weefun Tina 2 is aimed at (beginners, hobbyists, children, budget-conscious users)
- Unboxing and included accessories
- Printer specifications (build volume, power consumption, print speed)
- Software setup using WeBuilder slicer (with Cura profile as an alternative)
- Slicing an STL file and saving G-code to a micro SD card
- Bed levelling using the probe Z-offset menu and paper method
- Filament loading procedure
- First print result and print quality observations
- Mixed-media project ideas and use of Thingiverse/TinkerCAD for models
- Price comparison discussion (vs. Bambu Lab A1 Mini) in comments

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA** — primary supported filament type; a 200 g spool included in the box
- **PLA+** — supported filament variant
- **TPU** — listed as a supported flexible filament
- **PG (PETG implied)** — mentioned as a supported material
- **Grip tape / frog tape** — adhesive bed surface used instead of a heated bed to help prints adhere

## Techniques and Methods
- **Bed levelling (paper method)** — adjusting probe Z-offset until a sheet of paper has slight friction under the nozzle
- **STL-to-G-code slicing** — using WeBuilder slicer (or Cura with provided profile) to prepare print files
- **Sneakernet file transfer** — saving G-code to micro SD card rather than USB connection
- **Filament loading** — manual feed through the PTFE tube until resistance is felt, then using the on-screen load filament menu to heat and purge
- **Mixed-media fabrication** — combining 3D-printed parts with wood or cut plastic for larger builds that exceed the printer's build volume

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirms he simply **bought the cheapest printer on Amazon** without exhaustive research — acknowledging there are many options available.
- Plans to **make more software-focused videos** in response to viewer requests.
