## Overview
In this video, Luke (presenting on Robert Murray-Smith's channel) attaches a belt drive system and electronics to a bamboo tadpole trike. The build uses a 500W motor with a 16-tooth drive cog paired to a 136-tooth rear wheel cog, achieving roughly an 8:1 gear ratio. The drive system works well in a car park test run, and Luke notes he plans to reuse the same drive setup in a future quad build.

## Key Topics
- Belt drive system assembly on a bamboo tadpole trike
- Gear ratio calculation (16-tooth to 136-tooth cog, ~8:1)
- Pivoting intermediate drive cog mechanism enabling potential pedal/motor switching
- Electronics attachment and integration
- Car park test ride and performance assessment
- Future plans to rebuild as a quad using the same drive system

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Bamboo — structural frame material of the trike
- Carbon wrapping — used on a bar integrated into the frame
- 16-tooth drive belt cog — attached to the 500W motor
- 136-tooth large cog — mounted on the rear wheel
- Intermediate drive cog — mounted on a sleeved pivot bar, connects motor to rear wheel

## Techniques and Methods
- Belt drive transmission assembly (small motor cog to large wheel cog)
- Carbon wrapping of a structural frame bar
- Pivoting sleeve mechanism on an intermediate cog to allow belt tension adjustment
- Gear ratio calculation from tooth counts
- Swapping rear wheel to a smaller size to correct trike balance/tipping
