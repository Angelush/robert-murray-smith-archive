## Overview
A short motivational talk from Robert Murray-Smith reflecting on the nature of learning by doing. He argues that any work done to the best of one's current ability is inherently valuable and worthy of pride, regardless of how it compares to professional standards. The key takeaway is that negative criticism from others misses the point entirely — growth happens through attempting things, not avoiding them for fear of imperfection.

## Key Topics
- The value of doing your best, even when the result is imperfect
- Learning as an inherent part of making and building things
- How to handle negative feedback from others
- The distinction between a "bad" result and a "bad effort"
- Using past work as a benchmark for improvement rather than a source of shame
- Decision-making under uncertainty — good decisions are those made with the best available knowledge at the time
