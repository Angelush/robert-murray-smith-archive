## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates EcoFoam Oven Cleaner, a concentrated powder product in his EcoFoam product line that users dissolve in water at home to create a foaming cleaner, eliminating the need to ship water and reducing plastic waste through reusable bottles. The cleaner effectively removes baked-on grease from oven trays and grill pans without the hazardous safety requirements of conventional oven cleaners. The main takeaway is that it achieves comparable results to harsh commercial cleaners while being safe to handle with bare hands.

## Key Topics
- The EcoFoam product concept: concentrated powder format to reduce shipping weight and plastic waste
- Reusable foamer bottles as an environmental strategy
- Comparison of EcoFoam oven cleaner vs. traditional caustic oven cleaners
- Practical demonstration on a greasy oven tray and grill pan
- Two application methods: foamer bottle for oven interiors, diluted soak for removable pans
- Safety and handling — no gloves, ventilation, or post-use burn-off required

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **EcoFoam oven cleaner powder** — the concentrate; dissolves in water to form a clear cleaning solution that foams on dispensing
- **Water** — added by the user at home to reconstitute the powder
- **Traditional commercial oven cleaner (unbranded, shown for comparison)** — described as corrosive, highly flammable, requiring gloves, ventilation, and a 30-minute high-temperature burn-off after use; also incompatible with rubber, plastic, copper, and sink surfaces
- **Lye (sodium hydroxide)** — mentioned in Robert's comment replies as the likely active ingredient and main component of standard oven cleaners; acknowledged as caustic

## Techniques and Methods
- **Powder reconstitution** — measuring powder into a foamer bottle and adding water to create the working solution
- **Foam application** — using a foamer bottle to apply the product directly as foam, particularly useful inside an oven cavity
- **Dilute soak method** — pouring the solution into water and soaking removable items (e.g., grill pan) before scrubbing
- **Scrubbing** — using a sponge or scrubbing cloth after soaking; Robert notes scrubbing is always required for heavily soiled surfaces regardless of "no-scrub" product claims
- **White towel wipe test** — used to verify cleanliness after washing the oven tray

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert replied to a comment suggesting lye as the active ingredient, acknowledging it would explain the cleaner's effectiveness but noting that many people are reluctant to work with lye due to its caustic nature. He also pointed out that lye is the primary active ingredient in most standard commercial oven cleaners anyway — implying the EcoFoam formulation may use it in a safer or more controlled concentration/form.
