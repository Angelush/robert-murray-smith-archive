## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the "craziest clock in the world" — a historical escapement mechanism known variously as the Ignatz clock, flying foliot clock, or Da Vinci clock — by designing and 3D-printing his own version from scratch. He shares the STL files freely on Thingiverse, walking viewers through assembly and the considerable fine-tuning required to get the mechanism running. He ends with a candid observation: after testing, he suspects one key structural element (the T-section bar) may not actually be necessary, flagging it as a subject for future investigation.
