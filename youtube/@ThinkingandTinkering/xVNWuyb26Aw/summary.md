## Overview
This video demonstrates how to make bioplastic guitar picks using a simple five-ingredient recipe based on casein (milk protein). The presenter walks through the full process from mixing chemicals to pressing a mold, cutting picks, and finally playing guitar with the finished product. The result is a functional, solid pick that the presenter plans to use permanently.

## Key Topics
- Bioplastic synthesis from casein
- pH chemistry involved in precipitating the plastic
- Molding and pressing the bioplastic into a guitar pick shape
- Drying and curing the finished picks
- Practical demonstration of the picks on guitar

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Tannic acid** (0.3 g) — acts as a cross-linking/tanning agent
- **Citric acid** (20 g) — dissolved in 150 mL deionized water; acidic solution that triggers precipitation
- **Potassium hydroxide / KOH** (3 g) — dissolved in 400 mL deionized water; creates alkaline base solution (~pH 13–14)
- **Casein** (20 g) — milk protein; the primary plastic-forming material
- **Deionized water** — used for both solutions; tap water may be a viable substitute

## Techniques and Methods
- Preparing separate alkaline (KOH) and acidic (citric acid) solutions and testing pH with pH paper
- Slow addition of tannic acid to KOH solution to prevent clumping
- Slow addition of casein to the KOH/tannic acid solution with continuous stirring
- Combining the two solutions to precipitate the bioplastic (acid-base reaction)
- Straining the curdy precipitate through fine netting and squeezing out excess water
- Pressing the plastic mass into a mold to form a sheet (~2–3 mm thick)
- Cutting pick shapes from the pressed sheet
- Air drying over ~48 hours (vacuum oven mentioned as a faster alternative)
