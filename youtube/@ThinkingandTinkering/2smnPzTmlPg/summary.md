## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith disassembles a second upright (bagged) vacuum cleaner to repurpose its motor and impeller as an air/gas pump for a gasifier project. He demonstrates that the universal motor inside runs equally well on 12V DC despite being rated for 230V AC, then modifies the impeller housing to isolate gas flow away from the motor internals before reassembling it as a self-contained pump unit.

## Key Topics
- Internal anatomy of an upright bagged vacuum cleaner (motor, impeller, seals, drive belt)
- Universal motors: AC/DC compatibility and voltage flexibility
- Modifying the impeller cover to isolate gas flow from the motor cooling path
- Running the motor at low voltage (12V DC) for a gasifier air pump application
- Using the original dust bag compartment as a sawdust filter

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Neoprene rubber (roll stock, used to fabricate seals)
- Silicone sealant (used to seal gas passages)
- Car body filler (used to block the impeller vanes/centre piece to redirect airflow)
- Pressed steel (impeller construction material)

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly of upright vacuum cleaner (removing shoe, accessory hose clip, cover plate, motor housing)
- Drilling 1 cm holes through the impeller plate to create new gas outlet passages
- Blocking impeller vanes with car body filler to prevent gas ingress into the motor
- Fabricating home-made flexible rubber seals from neoprene roll stock
- Applying silicone sealant to ensure airtight separation of gas and motor chambers
- Direct-wiring the motor to a 12V DC battery (bypassing all original switching/electronics)
- Speed control via voltage adjustment
