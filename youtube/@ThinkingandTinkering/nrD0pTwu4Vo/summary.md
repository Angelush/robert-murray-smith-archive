## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith introduces the "Scan the World" initiative — a free, open-access digital archive of 3D scans of sculptures, artifacts, and historic monuments developed by MyMiniFactory. He argues that the platform democratizes access to cultural heritage by allowing anyone to download and 3D print replicas, and closes with a hands-on example at the King's Hall water fountain in Herne Bay, demonstrating the value of scanning overlooked local architectural details before they are lost.

## Key Topics
- Robert's growing interest in 3D scanning technology
- Overview of the Scan the World platform and its mission
- How Scan the World combines art, technology, and open accessibility
- Contributions from museums, institutions, and individuals to the archive
- Educational and hands-on value of 3D-printed replicas of artifacts
- The importance of scanning local and lesser-known architectural details
- A real-world example: Victorian ironwork water fountain at King's Hall, Herne Bay
