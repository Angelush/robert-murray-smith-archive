## Overview
This video demonstrates how to build a DIY light-up Christmas sign using entirely reclaimed and scavenged materials. Luke (presenting on the channel) walks through constructing a pine frame, mounting salvaged LED strips with a diffuser panel, and finishing with a stencil that conceals the sign's message until illuminated. The result is a decorative lit sign with a satisfying reveal effect.

## Key Topics
- Sourcing and reusing reclaimed materials for a build project
- Constructing a mitered pine frame with glue and clamping
- Mounting salvaged LED strips onto support beams with notched fittings
- Using an acrylic diffuser panel to soften and spread light
- Creating a stencil overlay to produce a readable illuminated sign

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Pine wood** — reclaimed from an old bed frame, used for the frame
- **Acrylic diffuser panel** — reclaimed from an old LCD TV screen, placed over the LEDs
- **LED strip lights** — salvaged from a skip (dumpster) and later from an old light panel
- **LED driver** — salvaged alongside the LED strips from a light panel
- **Wood glue** — used to join the mitered frame corners

## Techniques and Methods
- Mitered corner cuts (45°) for frame construction, glued and clamped overnight
- Notching the frame to seat LED support beams flush
- Reusing structural components (support beams) cut from the original light panel housing
- Screwing the acrylic/diffuser assembly down onto the frame
- Cutting and applying a stencil overlay to create the sign's lettering
