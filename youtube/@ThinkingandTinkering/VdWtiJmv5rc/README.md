## Overview

During a UK heatwave reaching 39°C (102°F), Robert demonstrates that ordinary copier paper laid over insulation board and covered with a sheet of polyethylene can cool a surface by approximately 5°C below ambient temperature. He connects this to the science of radiative sky cooling, referencing a more complex barium sulfate paint method from another creator, and argues that copier paper achieves a surprisingly similar result with almost zero cost or effort.
