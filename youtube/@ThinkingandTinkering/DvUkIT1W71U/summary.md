## Overview
Robert demonstrates a prototype flywheel-based generator in this second part of the series. He mounts a concrete flywheel in a cradle, attaches a coil ring, and places two neodymium magnets in a north-south arrangement to test the concept. The experiment produces approximately 0.6V and 50mA — modest output, but Robert considers it an unqualified success as a proof of concept.

## Key Topics
- Spinning the concrete flywheel and estimating RPM (estimated 300–500 RPM)
- Mounting the stator ring with coil wiring
- Magnet arrangement strategy (starting with a single north-south pole pair)
- Wire gauge selection and its trade-offs (turns vs. voltage vs. current capacity)
- Measuring voltage and current output from the generator

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Concrete flywheel (previously constructed)
- 20mm × 2mm neodymium magnets
- 18 SWG (Standard Wire Gauge) copper wire for the coil
- Steel rim (used as part of the magnetic circuit)
- Wooden blocks (used to hold the ring in place)

## Techniques and Methods
- Flywheel mounting in a cradle assembly
- Hand-cranking to spin the flywheel for electrical generation
- Voltmeter and ammeter measurement of generator output
- Proof-of-concept testing with minimal magnets before optimising arrangement
