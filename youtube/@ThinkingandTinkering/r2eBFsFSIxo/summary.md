## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds the frame for a large electric kick scooter powered by a 16-inch hub motor, fabricated primarily from repurposed steel shelving and salvaged moped parts. This is Part 1 of the build series, covering the design, cutting, and welding of the main frame structure. Being only a few weeks into welding, Robert embraces an overbuilt approach, prioritising strength and practicality over perfection.

## Key Topics
- Inspiration for the build: plans to motorise a future gasifier vehicle, plus friendly rivalry with a collaborator named Luke who was building his own electric kick scooter
- Overview of the 16-inch, 500W hub motor as the centrepiece of the design
- Sourcing and repurposing materials: steel shelving uprights and salvaged moped components
- Frame layout: battery compartment, electronics compartment, standing platform, and front fork column
- Tack welding and full welding of the frame
- Design philosophy: chunky, strong builds using recycled materials

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Steel shelving upright** — 1 inch × 2 inch (25 × 50 mm), 3 mm thick; used as the primary frame material
- **16-inch hub motor** — 500W (half kilowatt); the drive wheel and design anchor for the scooter
- **Moped front fork column** — salvaged from a scrapped/wrecked moped; provides the steering head
- **Moped rear swinging arm** — salvaged from the same moped; used as rear wheel support

## Techniques and Methods
- **Angle grinding** — cutting frame sections from the shelving upright
- **Tack welding** — initial positioning welds to hold frame components before full welding
- **Stick (arc) welding** — using a 3.2 mm 6013 rod at approximately 120 amps to weld the 3 mm steel frame
- **Visual/freehand design** — frame dimensions estimated by eye rather than detailed drawings
- **Parts salvage** — stripping and cleaning components from a scrapped moped for reuse

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Fork rake angle**: Robert confirms he chose 25 degrees off vertical for the front fork angle, taken from the geometry of the donor moped rather than a calculated design choice. He notes choppers typically use a steeper rake.
- **Welding gloves and safety**: Pushes back on a commenter raising electrical safety concerns, clarifying the machine outputs only ~20 volts — harmless to touch bare-handed — and that gloves are worn for heat protection, not electrical insulation.
- **Defending Luke**: Gives a notably firm and heartfelt reply defending his collaborator Luke against a comment suggesting Luke's content be kept separate. Robert argues strongly that it is the responsibility of experienced creators to mentor and give exposure to younger learners.
- **Progress updates**: Several replies confirm he is still collecting parts and continuing work on the build.
- **Philosophy**: Reiterates his love of reusing materials and keeping projects fun, especially around Christmas.
