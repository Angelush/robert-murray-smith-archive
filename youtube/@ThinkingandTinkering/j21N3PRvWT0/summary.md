## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of harvesting energy from buffeting winds — the irregular, oscillating wind common in urban environments — inspired by how plants and trees sway. He designs and 3D prints a simple pivoting arm mechanism that mimics a leaf's motion in the wind, then couples it to a modified button generator to demonstrate energy generation. The proof-of-concept works, showing that a wind-catching "stalk" can drive a small generator through oscillating motion.

## Key Topics
- Buffeting vs. straight-line wind, and why buffeting is dominant in urban environments
- Nature-inspired design: using plant/leaf motion as a model for wind energy harvesting
- "Windstalk" or bladeless oscillating wind turbine concepts
- 3D-printed mechanism design for converting oscillating motion to rotational generation
- Proof-of-concept demonstration and identified areas for improvement (gearing, fit tolerances)
- Sharing design files on Thingiverse for community development

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Rubber bands (used as springs/return mechanism)
- 3D-printed PLA or similar filament (implied by 3D printer use)
- Metal rod (4 mm and approximate 5 mm stock, used as armature/pivot pin)
- Flat plate (wind-catching surface attached to arm tip)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D modelling and printing of multi-part pivot/cradle/arm assembly
- Boolean merge operations in CAD to combine geometry into single printable parts
- Assembly of pivot-pin mechanism with rubber band spring tensioning
- Modification of an existing button generator (spring removed, arm replaced with rod-slot coupling)
- Outdoor wind testing of the oscillating prototype
- Coupling oscillating linear motion to rotational generator input via a sliding-pin/rod joint
