## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith tests "the Daisy," a turbine made from spoons, as a dual-function device capable of generating electricity from both wind and water. The design originated from a viewer suggestion to combine two previous experiments. The test yields 21V and 21mA at just 2 litres per minute flow, leading Robert to tentatively claim this may be a world-first combined wind-and-water turbine.
