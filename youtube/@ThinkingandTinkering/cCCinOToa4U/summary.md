## Overview
Luke demonstrates how to make a DIY heavyweight body filler using only two ingredients: glass resin and zeolite. The mixture is used to fill gaps in a project piece, then sanded smooth after curing. The result is a clean, paintable surface that performs comparably to commercial body filler.

## Key Topics
- Making a two-ingredient DIY body filler from glass resin and zeolite
- Mixing technique and consistency assessment by feel
- Application of the filler to fill gaps in a workpiece
- Adding hardener (supplied with the resin) and application
- Sanding the cured filler to a smooth finish

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Glass resin** — the base binder for the filler
- **Zeolite** — the filler/bulking agent mixed into the resin to achieve a thick, paste-like consistency
- **Hardener** — the standard hardener supplied with the resin kit; slightly more than the recommended amount is suggested

## Techniques and Methods
- Mixing resin and zeolite in a mortar until reaching a thick, paste-like consistency
- Adjusting filler thickness by incrementally adding zeolite until desired body is achieved
- Mixing in hardener before application
- Applying filler to gaps with a tool (spatula/spreader)
- Sanding the cured filler with a mouse/orbital sander for a smooth finish
