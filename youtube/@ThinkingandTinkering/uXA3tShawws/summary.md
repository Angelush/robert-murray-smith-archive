## Overview
This video is a fan competition entry for Robert Murray-Smith's "Made From Paper" challenge, showcasing a marble machine built almost entirely from paper and cardboard. The machine uses a mousetrap-powered governor and spool mechanism to drop a ball, which triggers a sign reading "Sub to TNT." The creator documents the design process and troubleshooting steps, including slowing the governor by adding a rubber band to reduce mousetrap power.

## Key Topics
- Design and construction of a paper/cardboard marble machine
- Governor mechanism for controlling ball-drop speed
- Mousetrap as the power source
- Fan tribute to Robert Murray-Smith and Luke (pictures displayed on levers)
- Iterative troubleshooting (governor initially too fast)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Cardboard box (base structure)
- Paper towel tubes (used as the spool)
- Toothpicks (used as axles for levers)
- Rubber bands (used to reduce mousetrap spring power)
- Mousetrap (power source; wire repurposed as spool support)
- Wire and paper clips (used to pull the TNT paper sleeve)
- Beads (used as bearings for the spool)
- String (connected to spool)
- Thin paper (for the "sub to TNT" sleeve)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting and notching cardboard to create structural slots for L-shaped levers
- Slitting and bending cardboard strips to form a spool housing
- Gluing paper circles onto tube ends to form a spool
- Using a bead as a low-friction bearing point for the spool on a wire
- Repurposing mousetrap wire as a structural and mechanical element
- Adding a rubber band to dampen spring force for speed control
- Slow-motion video capture to analyse mechanism behaviour
