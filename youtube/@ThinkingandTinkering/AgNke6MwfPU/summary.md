## Overview
Robert demonstrates how to build a DIY RFID-blocking wallet using Grafoil as the core shielding material, protecting credit cards from contactless skimming. The build is simple and primarily cosmetic once the Grafoil layer is in place — the functional blocking is achieved with just the Grafoil and tape sandwich. He tests the finished wallet to confirm cards cannot be read by contactless systems.

## Key Topics
- What RFID is and how it works (Radio Frequency Identification via radio waves to an antenna/reader)
- The threat of RFID skimming — unlawfully obtaining card details wirelessly
- Step-by-step construction of a Grafoil-lined card sleeve/wallet
- Cosmetic finishing to make the wallet look like a real product
- Live demonstration that the finished wallet blocks contactless scanning

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Grafoil** — the key RFID-blocking material; sandwiched inside the wallet to block radio signals
- **Denim** — used as the outer structural/heavy-duty material
- **Packing tape** — applied to both sides of the Grafoil sheet to add strength and prevent tearing
- **Electrical tape** — used to line the inside for added strength and a cleaner finish
- **Hot glue gun** — adhesive for bonding layers together
- **Plastidip** — rubberized coating painted around the edges for texture, grip, water resistance, and aesthetics
- **Tab binder / separator tabs** — used to form the card pockets inside the wallet

## Techniques and Methods
- Laminating Grafoil between layers of packing tape to reinforce it before cutting
- Trimming Grafoil to size slightly smaller (~0.5 cm margin) than the outer material
- Hot-gluing the Grafoil layer to the denim base
- Cutting card slots with a centre-measured incision (finding midpoint, cutting ~1–1.5 cm above and below)
- Adding finger-access cutouts to make card insertion/removal easier
- Painting Plastidip around all edges to seal the layers and add a tactile, finished appearance
