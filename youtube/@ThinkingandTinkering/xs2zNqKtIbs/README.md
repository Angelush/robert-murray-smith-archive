## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make a parabolic solar reflector using a spokeless bicycle wheel and a space blanket (Mylar). He walks through the underlying parabola mathematics to determine focal length, surveys several construction methods, and ultimately uses a rubber sheet inflated with a tire pump (inspired by "Nighthawk in Light") to form the parabolic shape — producing a bright, functional mirror he plans to use with a Stirling engine or flash steam boiler.
