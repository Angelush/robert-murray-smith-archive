## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how a condom can be repurposed as a rolling diaphragm piston seal (also called a "top hat seal") for DIY engines, solving the problem of achieving an airtight piston fit without precision machining or o-rings. He assembles a working prototype from pipe, wood, and a condom, then successfully inflates it to prove the seal concept. The video highlights this as a practical, low-cost alternative for hobbyist-built air, water, or steam engines.

## Key Topics
- The challenge of sealing pistons in DIY engine construction
- Traditional piston sealing methods: o-rings and precision-machined piston rings
- The rolling diaphragm seal (top hat seal) as an alternative approach
- Using a condom as a ready-made rolling latex seal
- DIY alternative: casting a custom rolling seal using plaster of paris and liquid latex
- Demonstration of the assembled piston-cylinder seal with a pressure test

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Condoms (latex, extra-safe/thick, lubricated)** — used as the rolling diaphragm seal sleeve over the piston
- **Superglue** — to bond the condom tip to the wooden piston top
- **Butyl rubber** — used to seal the end cap on the cylinder pipe
- **PVC or similar pipe** — forms the cylinder body
- **Wood** — used to form the piston rod/head
- **Electrician's tape** — used to secure/clamp the condom to the outside of the cylinder
- **Plaster of paris** — mentioned as a mold material for casting a custom rolling seal (alternative method)
- **Liquid latex** — mentioned as the casting material for a custom rolling seal

## Techniques and Methods
- **Rolling diaphragm (top hat) seal assembly** — stretching and re-rolling a condom over a piston, then inverting it inside the cylinder so it rolls with piston travel
- **Pressure/blow test** — blowing air into the sealed cylinder to verify the seal holds
- **Latex slip casting from plaster mold** — pouring liquid latex into a plaster of paris mold, allowing it to thicken by cure time, then demolding to produce a custom-sized rolling seal

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comments are mostly jovial and lean into the video's inherent humor, but he adds a few substantive points:
- Confirms the rolling seal is used in **Stirling engines** specifically.
- Notes the seal works for both **air and water** applications.
- Suggests that for actual use, he would use a condom **straight from the packet** (i.e., unlubricated and unused), implying lubrication was a minor issue in this demo.
- Jokingly notes that in England condoms apparently "only come in large," riffing on the size-matching challenge he mentioned in the video.
- Acknowledges **testing is the only way** to get definitive answers about seal performance.
