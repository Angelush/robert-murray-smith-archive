## Overview
Three months after the sudden and unexpected passing of his partner Patty, Robert records a heartfelt thank-you video to his community for the overwhelming support they offered during his grief. He shares that Patty was "the better part of him" and that the kindness shown genuinely helped him through the loss. He announces he is getting back into life through cycling and intends to continue making videos, though perhaps less frequently than before.

## Key Topics
- Grieving the sudden loss of his partner Patty
- Expressing sincere gratitude to the community for condolences and support
- Personal recovery: returning to cycling after ~30 years away from it
- Setting a challenge for himself: cycling the Loch Ness / Cairngorms route with his brothers, and a trip around the Orne and Channel Islands
- Confirming he will continue making YouTube videos

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert responded warmly and personally to a large number of comments. The most substantive replies include:

- **On grief:** "Patti was my life and soul and finding a point to carrying on is hard to do" — he acknowledged how profound and ongoing the loss is, and echoed to one commenter that "you never get over it — you just learn to live with it."
- **On disbelief:** He told one commenter "I still have problems with it being not real — and I know you know what I mean there," suggesting he was still processing the reality of the loss.
- **On community support:** "everybody has been great mate and it really has made a difference."
- **On reconnecting with people:** He told someone named Mandy "I am no longer at the unit — but it would be great to see you," hinting at a prior shared workplace or context.
- The vast majority of replies are brief, warm acknowledgements ("cheers mate," "thank you mate," "bless you"), reflecting Robert's gracious and unpretentious character in engaging with his audience individually.
