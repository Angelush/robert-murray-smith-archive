## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to cut large circular disks from acrylic sheets salvaged from old LCD screens, using a homemade circle cutter built from printer parts. The video is a short, focused technique segment intended to complement a larger Wimshurst machine build series. He shows that a simple custom tool outperforms a chop saw for cutting acrylic without chipping.

## Key Topics
- Building a Wimshurst machine (context for why the disks are needed)
- Salvaging acrylic sheets from disassembled LCD screens
- Challenges of cutting acrylic (chipping, poor behaviour with a chop saw)
- Constructing a circle-cutting jig from reclaimed printer hardware
- Demonstrating the cutting process on a large acrylic sheet

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Acrylic sheet (salvaged from LCD screens)
- 8 mm steel bar (from a printer)
- 6 mm bolt (for clamping the bar in the jig)
- Heavy board (used to secure the acrylic while cutting)

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling LCD screens to recover large acrylic panels
- Fabricating a circle-cutter jig: drilling an 8 mm hole, a 6 mm hole, and a saw-cut slot in a section of printer bar, then fitting a clamping bolt
- Drilling a 7 mm pilot hole in the acrylic as a centre point for the cutter
- Screwing the acrylic to a heavy board to prevent movement during cutting
- Rotating the cutter tool steadily around the centre point, letting the tool do the work
