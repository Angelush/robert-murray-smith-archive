## Overview
Robert demonstrates a simple DIY method for cutting large circular discs from perspex (acrylic) using a modified steel rule as a compass-style scribing tool. The video is part of an ongoing series building an axial flux motor with his collaborator Steve, and the disc-cutting technique serves as a practical, accessible tutorial requiring minimal tools. He also shows a deliberately unsafe finishing method — using a handheld knife blade against a spinning drill-chucked disc — as a playful jab at health and safety culture.

## Key Topics
- Context: returning to work after illness, easing back in with a fun project
- Building an axial flux motor with collaborator Steve (three perspex discs required)
- Cutting large circles from perspex using a pivoting steel rule as a compass

- Finishing/truing the disc edge using a handheld knife blade against a drill-spun disc

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Perspex (acrylic sheet)** — the material being cut into discs for the axial flux motor
- **Steel rule** — modified with drilled holes at various lengths to act as a compass/scribing arm
- **Bolt** — used to mount the disc in a drill chuck for spinning during edge finishing

## Techniques and Methods
- **Compass scribing**: drilling a pivot hole in the perspex, then using a modified steel rule at a fixed radius to scribe a circle
- **Snapping**: breaking the perspex along the scribed line to produce a rough disc
- **Lathe-style edge trimming**: chucking the rough disc on a bolt in a hand drill (held in a vice), spinning it at reduced speed, and paring the edge with a handheld knife blade to produce a smooth, true disc
