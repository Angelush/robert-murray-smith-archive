## Overview
This is a Christmas advent calendar short (Day 18) in which Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a quick, playful craft: making toy "rolling feet" from straw and sticks attached to a roll of tape. He then briefly explains the biomechanics that distinguish walking, running, and jogging in terms of foot-ground contact time.

## Key Topics
- Christmas advent craft activity (Day 18 of 25)
- Construction of simple toy "rolling feet" from household materials
- Biomechanics of human locomotion: walking, running, and jogging
  - Walking: 60% one foot on ground, 40% one foot in air, 20% both feet on ground
  - Running: period where both feet are off the ground
  - Jogging: humorously defined as post-Christmas-dinner guilt exercise

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Straw (cut to approximately 4 cm lengths)
- Sticks (small, used as axles or pegs)
- A roll of tape (used as the rolling base/body)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting straw to uniform ~4 cm lengths
- Inserting sticks into the straw pieces to form feet
- Attaching the straw feet to a roll of tape (top and/or bottom) to create a rolling walking toy
