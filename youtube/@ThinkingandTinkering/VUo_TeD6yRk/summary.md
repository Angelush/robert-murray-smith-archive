## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents the "Wind Tube," a 3D-printable wind turbine designed to be mounted on a stick with an optional tail for self-orienting in the wind. He demonstrates how the same internal mechanism from his earlier "wind brick/wall" design can be adapted into cylindrical, cubic, and hexagonal enclosures using a cookie-cutter approach in 3D modelling software. The video highlights the design's adaptability and weatherproofing options for outdoor use.

## Key Topics
- The Wind Tube concept: a mountable, stick-mounted wind turbine derived from the Wind Brick/Wind Wall design
- Shared internal mechanism: guiding rollers, channel, Archimedean screw (aledi screw), coil, and electronics
- 3D modelling walkthrough to convert the cylindrical design into a cube variant
- Hexagonal variant for honeycomb stacking
- Weatherproofing the electronics compartment
- Plans to share files on Thingiverse
- Encouragement for community adaptation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Potting compound** — used to seal the electronics compartment against weather
- **Paraffin wax** — suggested as an alternative weatherproofing fill for the electronics pan

## Techniques and Methods
- **Cookie-cutter 3D modelling** — using primitive shapes as boolean cutters to create custom enclosure profiles (cube, hexagon)
- **Primitive alignment and merging** — aligning two primitives, converting one to a hole, then merging to produce the final shape
- **Tail fin attachment** — gluing a tail to the rear for passive wind-direction tracking
- **Potting/wax encapsulation** — filling the electronics bay to achieve full waterproofing

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Printer used**: The Wind Tube was printed on a **Flashforge Adventurer 5M Pro** with a 220 × 220 × 220 mm bed — Robert notes this is a small bed and that he uses the full bed area.
- **Learning 3D modelling**: When a commenter complained about not being able to use the software, Robert responded directly: learn the (free) program — it takes about an hour — and pointed out that upgrade, service providers, or self-teaching are the real options, encouraging people to stop complaining and get started.
- **Solar vs wind**: Noted that renewable energy choice always depends on location — no point in solar cells where there isn't much sun.
- **Community designs**: Expressed genuine enthusiasm for seeing how others improve and adapt the basic design, telling one commenter "it certainly looks like you know what you are doing."
