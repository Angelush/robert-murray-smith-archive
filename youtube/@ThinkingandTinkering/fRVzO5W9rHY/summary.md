## Overview
This video demonstrates a variable reciprocating rack mechanism that converts back-and-forth linear motion into continuous one-way rotation, developed as part of Robert's ongoing variable hypocycloid project. The key feature is that the output rotation is proportional to the stroke length, making it fully variable. The video progresses through two iterations — a 3D-printed ratchet design to a cleaner version using industrial one-way clutch bearings — ultimately achieving quiet, accurate, zero-backlash motion conversion.

## Key Topics
- The problem of converting reciprocating motion into continuous one-way rotation with a variable stroke
- 3D-printed ratchet-based reciprocating rack mechanism (designed in Tinkercad)
- Variable stroke control: output rotation scales with push distance
- One-way (clutch) bearings as a quiet, accurate replacement for printed ratchets
- Comparison of three design iterations: full-stroke rack → ratchet version → clutch bearing version
- Reference to Hitchcock's *Mechanical Movements, Devices and Appliances*, device #976 (bevel gear arrangement)
- Connection to the broader variable hypocycloid project

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **One-way clutch bearings** — 22 mm OD, 8 mm ID, 9 mm thick; used as silent, accurate ratchet replacements; available with zero backlash
- **Skate bearings** — 22 mm OD, 8 mm ID, 7 mm thick; referenced for size comparison
- **PLA / 3D-printed parts** — rack body, ratchet pawls, gear, housing, cap, retaining ring
- **Axles** — fitted inside the clutch bearings for the final assembly

## Techniques and Methods
- **CAD design in Tinkercad** — designing the rack and ratchet assembly for 3D printing
- **FDM 3D printing** — printing the rack mechanism, ratchet pawls, housing, and cap
- **Ratchet-and-pawl assembly** — integral printed ratchets with pawl glued to central gear
- **Press/drift fitting** — pressing clutch bearings into printed housing recesses
- **Bearing-on-axle assembly** — one axle rotating inside another, retained with a printed retaining ring
- **Iterative redesign** — reprinting the housing with a bearing pocket to replace the ratchet
