## Overview

In this video, Robert Murray-Smith builds a permanent vortex rocket stove from pre-cut steel components, following up on his earlier investigation (video 1730) into vortex geometry. He fabricates and welds together a vortex chimney (using three segmented fins at 120° intervals) and a rocket stove body, then tests the completed unit on wood fuel. The result works well — roaring with visible vortex airflow — though Robert notes the chimney needs to be longer for better draft, and considers an oil burner adaptation as a future improvement.
