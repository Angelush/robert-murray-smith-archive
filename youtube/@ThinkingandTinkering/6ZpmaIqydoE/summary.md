## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith unboxes and assembles a generic Chinese-made pedal tricycle (purchased for approximately £220) as the first step in a project to convert it into an electric tricycle for under £300. The video responds to viewer feedback that not everyone can build vehicles from scratch using bamboo or rolled newspaper, so this series demonstrates electrifying a production trike instead. Part 1 covers only the mechanical assembly; the electrification is promised for the next video.

## Key Topics
- Unboxing and inspecting a flat-pack Chinese tricycle
- Overview of the trike's frame design: a "step-through" bicycle frame with a bolted-on rear subframe
- Rear axle and drivetrain design: single driven wheel with the other free-spinning (no differential)
- Six-speed gear cluster and derailleur setup
- Strap brake mechanism on the rear axle disc
- Assembly walkthrough: rear subframe, main frame joining, chain fitting, fenders, mudguards, gear and brake cables
- Cost comparison: full trike at ~£220 vs. just the rear axle section priced at ~£150 on US websites

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Steel frame components (main frame and rear subframe)
- Nylon lock nuts (used on rear wheel axle)
- Leather strap brake
- Chain and chain guard
- Six-speed gear cluster / derailleur

## Techniques and Methods
- Part-assembly from flat-pack (trike arrives partially pre-assembled)
- Laying out all parts before assembly (Lego-set approach)
- Bending transit-damaged metal components back into shape using a rubber mallet
- Threading and routing the drive chain through the derailleur
- Connecting gear and brake cables
