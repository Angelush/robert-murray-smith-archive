## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith visits Nuneaton, a Midlands town that suffered heavy WWII bombing and economic decline, to explore its surprisingly rich ghostly heritage. He walks through the town centre, noting its ongoing regeneration efforts, and highlights that Nuneaton is perhaps best known for three things: wartime destruction, being the birthplace of George Eliot, and an exceptional concentration of ghost stories.
