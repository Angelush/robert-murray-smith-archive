## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith visits Nuneaton, a Midlands town that suffered heavy WWII bombing and economic decline, to explore its surprisingly rich ghostly heritage. He walks through the town centre, noting its ongoing regeneration efforts, and highlights that Nuneaton is perhaps best known for three things: wartime destruction, being the birthplace of George Eliot, and an exceptional concentration of ghost stories.

## Key Topics
- Nuneaton's history of WWII bombing and subsequent urban decline
- The town's identity as a commuter settlement between Birmingham and Coventry
- Ongoing £30–34 million regeneration efforts
- George Eliot's connection to Nuneaton and the George Eliot Way walking route
- Canal walks linking Nuneaton to the wider region
- Nuneaton railway station history (built 1847, extended 1904, new section 2004)
- The town's reputation as a ghost hotspot
- Suggestion that a ghost walk could be a viable tourist attraction
