## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith tests his "forever wick" — a carbon felt material — as a direct wick replacement in a brass miner's lamp purchased from eBay. The wick works beautifully in this lamp, which is both exciting and frustrating because it previously failed in a greenhouse heater. The video's central lesson is epistemic humility: thinking you understand something doesn't mean you do.

## Key Topics
- The "forever wick" concept — a reusable, non-consumable wick made from carbon felt
- Successful installation of the forever wick in a brass miner's lamp as a direct replacement
- Contrast with a previous failed attempt in a greenhouse heater (same wick, different result)
- Reflection on scientific uncertainty and the importance of questioning one's assumptions
- Community knowledge-sharing around the forever wick project

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Carbon felt** — the material used to make the forever wick (specifically activated carbon felt, per author comments)
- **Brass** — the material of the miner's lamp
- Lamp fuel (unspecified in transcript; Robert confirms in comments he is using the same fuel as previous tests)
- Note: Robert clarifies in comments that his carbon felt is pure, bought for battery research, and may differ from cooker hood carbon filters which can contain additives

## Techniques and Methods
- Direct wick replacement: cutting/fitting carbon felt to substitute for a standard lamp wick
- Comparative testing across different lamp/holder configurations to diagnose inconsistent results
