## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a step-by-step rocket stove as an early Christmas present for his friend Luke, who is going off-grid. The design uses six steel parts (three core, three decorative) with a sloped fuel feed and secondary air-intake holes, resulting in a highly efficient, fast-lighting stove that achieves near-total combustion through wood gasification and pyrolysis.

## Key Topics
- Design rationale for the rocket stove, including an 800mm chimney for improved draw
- Sloped 45-degree fuel feed hopper for gravity-assisted fuel delivery
- Secondary air-intake holes for boosted combustion ("gasification" principle)
- Steel sourcing and pre-cut ordering (online, ~£80 total)
- Basic welding with a stick/MMA welder and angle grinder
- Future potential uses: water heater, on-demand boiler (copper coil), sand battery/mass heater

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Mild steel square tube** — 100×100mm cross-section; 800mm length (chimney), 400mm length (horizontal burn chamber), 200×200mm junction piece
- **Steel plate** — 300×500mm base/hotplate
- **Bar steel** — used to fabricate legs/feet
- **Wood / wood pellets** — fuel for combustion

## Techniques and Methods
- Pre-ordering steel pre-cut to size from an online metals supplier
- Marking and cutting with an **angle grinder** (100×100mm hole for pipe junction; fuel-feed slot)
- Drilling 12mm air-intake holes (four per side on three faces, positioned 25mm from base)
- **MMA (stick) welding** to assemble all components
- Gravity-fed sloped hopper design for continuous fuel delivery
- Pyrolysis/gasification principle: heating wood to release combustible gases for complete burn

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Sand battery / mass heater**: Confirmed he has built one; directs viewers to the rocket stove playlist.
- **Welding equipment**: Recommends an arc (MMA) welder as affordable and sufficient; notes he used MMA specifically so the build is replicable by the widest audience.
- **Ash/cleaning**: Says there is surprisingly little ash; can be scraped out with a stick.
- **Shutdown**: Clarifies there is no off switch — you simply wait for it to burn out (or damp it).
- **Pipe shape**: Confirms square vs. round cross-section doesn't matter — "any shape will do it."
- **Overloading air holes**: Cautions "not a lot — you can overdo it."
- **Thicker steel for high heat**: If warping occurs, he would use thicker steel.
- **Related video**: Repeatedly directs people to **video 1728 "Playing with Fire"** for related content, and to the **rocket stove playlist** for the full series of builds including water heaters and sand batteries.
- **Hinge/door mechanism**: Explains he used a 100mm butt hinge welded on; suggests viewers check the earlier "picnic version" video for a cross-bar variant.
