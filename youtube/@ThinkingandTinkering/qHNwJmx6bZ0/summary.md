## Overview
Robert demonstrates a simple motor at Think Ink Factory in Sri Lanka, using a chip of rare diamagnetic natural graphite levitated on a bed of stacked ring and disc magnets. A Fresnel lens focuses sunlight onto the floating graphite chip, causing it to spin. The mechanism is thermal: heat from the focused sunlight alters the graphite's diamagnetic properties, disrupting its levitation equilibrium; the chip's high thermal conductivity then rapidly dissipates heat as it moves away from the hot spot, sustaining continuous rotation.

## Key Topics
- Diamagnetism and magnetic levitation of natural graphite
- Construction of a stacked ring-magnet levitation bed
- Using focused sunlight (via Fresnel lens) to drive rotation of a levitated diamagnetic disc
- The thermal mechanism behind the motion
- Potential scaling into a homopolar motor architecture

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Natural diamagnetic graphite** — a rare metallic-like graphite mined in Sri Lanka (Think Ink Factory), sold at £152/gram; bends like metal, peels in sheets, strongly diamagnetic
- **Ring magnets** — 2.4 cm diameter, 8 mm central hole, magnetized north-top/south-bottom; 5 used in the build
- **Disc magnets** — 2 mm thick; 4 used, one pressed into the center of each of four ring magnets

## Techniques and Methods
- Rubbing graphite chip flat before placing on the magnetic bed to ensure stable levitation
- Stacking alternating ring and disc magnets (north-south orientation) to create a levitation bed
- Using a **Fresnel lens** to concentrate sunlight onto the floating graphite chip
- Thermal cycling via focused sunlight to continuously disturb diamagnetic equilibrium and sustain rotation

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Mechanism explained:** "The heat from the sunlight changes the diamagnetic properties of the graphite and so disturbs the equilibrium of the system. But as the diamagnetic graphite is very very thermally conductive, as soon as it moves out of the heat spot the heat is dissipated across the surface and lost rapidly. This continues and the disk spins."
- **Curie temperature:** Suggests the effect may relate to heating the material past its Curie temperature.
- **Scaling up:** Actively working to scale the concept; wonders what would happen with a homopolar motor architecture.
- **Processing limitation:** Tried processing the graphite but found it disorganises the plate structure, reducing the diamagnetic effect.
- **Academic interest:** A team at the University of Michigan had recently obtained samples and was beginning research.
- **Uniqueness of the material:** Believes this graphite is only found in Sri Lanka.
- **Next steps:** Working on a small motor using this principle; plans to post results.
