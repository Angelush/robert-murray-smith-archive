## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explains the Earth's atmospheric electrical field — essentially treating the planet as a giant capacitor — and builds a working Corona motor powered by that static potential difference. He reproduces a design by YouTuber "lasersaber" (also called the "atmo motor"), demonstrating it running from a Van de Graaff generator, and argues that with a high-altitude antenna this motor could be driven by free atmospheric energy alone.

## Key Topics
- The Earth as a natural capacitor: ground (negative), atmosphere (positive), ~100 V/m field
- Origins of the atmospheric field: cosmic ray bombardment and radioactive decay
- Historical context of atmospheric energy research (pre-Franklin, since the 1600s)
- Two approaches to atmospheric motors: electret motors and Corona motors
- The "atmo motor" design by lasersaber / Lind Motors
- Step-by-step build of a 3D-printed Corona motor
- Testing with a Van de Graaff generator
- Possibility of running the motor directly from atmospheric energy via a high-altitude antenna

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Aluminium foil tape** (150 mm, for heating/ventilation ducting) — used to cover the rotor blades and drum
- **Black electrician tape** — used to insulate one end of each blade
- **Multi-stranded wire (red and black)** — electrical connections to blade sets
- **Superglue** — bonding wire braid into blade channels
- **PVC pipe + wool/paper** — mentioned as an alternative static source (polishing to generate charge)
- **Wax** — described as the base material for making an electret

## Techniques and Methods
- **3D printing** (Elegoo Centauri / Flashforge AD5X, mostly PLA) — fabricating side plates, blades, drum, and support pillars to the required precision
- **Corona discharge** — ionising air between high-voltage surfaces to generate ionic wind that spins a drum
- **Electret fabrication** — melting wax, setting it in a strong electrostatic (kilovolt) field to create a permanent electrostatic "magnet"
- **Continuity/resistance testing** — verifying press-fit blade–wire connections with a multimeter (target: ~0.4 Ω)
- **Clean Earth grounding** — using a separate earth rod driven into the ground outside, isolated from the house mains earth
- **Van de Graaff generator excitation** — supplying up to ~500 kV static charge via a needle electrode to drive the motor during the demo
- **High-altitude antenna** — discussed (not demonstrated) as the method to tap the atmospheric potential difference directly

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly brief social exchanges, but a few contain useful information:

- **3D print files**: He acknowledged forgetting to upload the STL files and promised to post them on Thingiverse.
- **Printer setup**: He prints mostly in PLA on an **Elegoo Centauri** or **Flashforge AD5X**.
- **Sourcing**: The difficulty and cost of importing lasersaber's tubes from the USA to the UK was the direct motivation for designing and printing his own version.
- **Working principle (his theory)**: He speculates the motor works similarly to a rotating magnetic field, but using the Earth connection to keep the electric field "moving" rather than rotating permanent magnets — explicitly flagged as a personal guess.
- **Grounding tip**: Suggested using a **large flat copper mat** instead of an earth rod as an alternative ground.
- **Historical note**: Confirmed Corona motors have been known since the 1600s.
- **Related machines**: Recommended looking into the **Wommelsdorf machine** as an improvement over the Wimshurst machine for related high-voltage projects.
- **NASA reference**: Noted that NASA investigated asymmetric capacitors for propulsion ("lifters"), connecting this to the broader ionic/electrostatic thrust concept.
- **Lasersaber's drill**: Directed viewers to lasersaber's channel to see this motor design used to power an actual drill.
