## Overview

Robert demonstrates a high-voltage discharge apparatus built from salvaged microwave oven components, using it to explosively vaporize small quantities of water, a tomato, and thin wire. The key takeaway is that a spark gap acting as a switch dumps capacitor energy near-instantaneously into a small brass chamber, causing anything inside to explode — and when submerged in water, the setup produces plasma electrolysis, a fundamentally different process from conventional battery-driven electrolysis.
