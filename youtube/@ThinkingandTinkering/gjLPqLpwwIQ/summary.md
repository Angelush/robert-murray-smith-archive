## Overview
This video demonstrates how to build a custom DIY tripod mount for a GoPro camera using cut acrylic pieces. The presenter (Luke) walks through the full fabrication process, from prototyping to the finished mount that securely holds the GoPro and attaches to a standard tripod. The result is a snug-fitting, functional mount that retains full tripod movement.

## Key Topics
- Problem: GoPro lacks a native tripod mount
- Prototype design: a square acrylic box frame with a mounting base
- Step-by-step construction of the camera frame and base plate
- Using an M6 bolt/nut system to interface with a standard tripod
- Drilling holes for GoPro button access

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Acrylic sheet (cut to specific dimensions)
- Glue (used for bonding acrylic pieces)
- M6 nut and bolt (for tripod interface)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting acrylic to dimension (frame pieces: 6.5×2.5 cm and 5.2×2.5 cm; base plate: 4.5×5 cm)
- Gluing and clamping acrylic joints (24-hour cure time)
- Drilling an M6 hole through the base with a 12 mm countersink
- Gluing the M6 bolt into the base for the tripod thread
- Drilling access holes for the GoPro record and power buttons
