## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith acquires a secondhand Mini for £100 (trading in a scrapped Nissan Micra) and begins converting it to an electric vehicle. In this first part, he focuses on the initial acquisition and enlists the help of a local garage, Canterbury Automotive, to remove the original 1.6-litre petrol engine for another £100. The video sets up the project with a total outlay of £200 and previews the motor build to come.

## Key Topics
- Motivation for choosing a Mini as the EV conversion platform (style and character vs. the scrapped Nissan Micra)
- Sourcing the vehicle cheaply (£100 swap)
- The value of asking for help and finding local expertise
- Partnering with Canterbury Automotive for engine removal
- Project cost tracking and roadmap for future episodes

## Robert's Own Replies
Several useful clarifications emerged from Robert's comment replies:

- **Drive motor:** He is leaning toward **hub motors**, and acknowledges the trade-offs of additional unsprung weight.
- **Battery type:** Likely **lead acid** initially, at least for the first iteration. He says he is unlikely to build the battery pack himself as it would take too long.
- **BMS:** He mentions being stuck on the BMS (battery management system) at the time of writing.
- **Steering:** Power-assisted steering runs off a 12V motor — it simply plugs back into the battery, requiring no major modification.
- **Brakes:** Needs a vacuum source; he recommends a **fish tank vacuum pump and a vacuum reservoir** (referencing Rich Rebuilds as a prior example of the same approach, in response to a commenter who doubted it was straightforward).
- **Range suitability:** His daily commute is only 8 miles, so even modest range would be sufficient.
- **Project status (at comment time):** The conversion is complete — only the Twizzy remains outstanding.
- **Engine noise:** The exhaust smell/noise in the video was because the previous owner had removed the catalytic converter.
