## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a human-sized hamster wheel (inspired by medieval treadwheel cranes) using a cold laminating technique he developed in a previous video (video 1449). The video covers the construction of the wooden wheel hoops, joining the sections, and building a rolling sled base using plywood boxes, steel rod, and longboard wheels as bearings. This is Part 1 of a series; converting the wheel into an electricity generator is deferred to Part 2.

## Key Topics
- History and mechanics of medieval treadwheel cranes (lifting up to 4–10 tons with human power)
- Motivation for building a human hamster wheel as a power generator
- Cold laminating technique for forming large wooden hoops (referenced from video 1449)
- Assembly of 12 curved sections into two large wooden wheel hoops
- Construction of a rolling sled base to support the wheel
- Initial test ride and inertia characteristics of the finished wheel

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Plywood (boxes for the sled base; old pallets noted as source material, ~12mm thick; Robert suggests 18mm would be better)
- 8mm steel bar (axle/roller rod for the sled)
- Longboard wheels (used as bearings to support the large wheel on the sled)
- Wooden hoops formed via cold lamination (unspecified wood species)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cold laminating — bending thin wood strips over a jig to form large curved hoop sections (detailed in video 1449)
- Joinery — connecting 12 individual curved sections to form two full circular hoops
- Sled fabrication — constructing plywood box frames with drilled holes, threaded steel rod, and longboard wheel bearings to cradle the large wheel

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Build time:** The hoops took about a week to make, but mostly drying time for the lamination.
- **Sizing note:** The wheel was built 400mm wide; Robert suggests 600mm would give a more stable base.
- **Flooring:** Used old pallets (~12mm thick); he thinks 18mm boards would be better.
- **Flywheel effect:** Robert points out the wheel acts as its own flywheel, which is a meaningful design advantage.
- **Generator options:** He notes there are "a ton of options" for the generator stage and is likely to go with the simplest. He mentions considering motors but prefers a magnet-based suggestion from a commenter.
- **Big wheel bike:** Mentions he has been thinking about building a big-wheel bicycle.
- **Work ethic aside:** Notes he only sleeps 4 hours a night and builds primarily from found/salvaged materials, which speeds up the process considerably.
- **Push rod / ball valve idea:** Briefly mentions a commenter suggestion about push rod ball valves seated against a high-tension O-ring.
