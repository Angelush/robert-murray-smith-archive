## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith repurposes brass gears and steel components salvaged from a donated car gearbox to build a dieselpunk-styled freestanding clock, incorporating a Doctor Who pocket watch as the clock face. The project combines automotive salvage, fiberglass resin casting, and welding to create a personalized Christmas gift for his sister. The result is a functional, removable-pocket-watch clock with a striking steampunk/dieselpunk aesthetic.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging brass gears and steel parts from a car gearbox (from Canterbury Automotive)
- Designing a freestanding dieselpunk/steampunk clock around a Doctor Who pocket watch
- Using fiberglass resin to cast a custom holder/insert for the pocket watch
- Welding steel components together to form the clock stand
- Making personalized handmade Christmas gifts

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Brass gears** — salvaged from the gearbox gear train; decorative and structural elements
- **Steel components** — main gear shaft, push rod (repurposed as a clock foot), gear selectors
- **Fibril (Five Roll / "fibril")** — a glass fiber automotive repair product (Upol brand); used to cast a custom pocket watch holder via chemical reaction
- **Vaseline / grease / graphite / washing-up liquid** — mentioned as release agents to prevent the resin from bonding to the pocket watch during casting
- **Super glue** — used to fix brass pieces in place
- **Double-sided tape** — used to temporarily hold components together

## Techniques and Methods
- **Resin casting with a release agent** — pressing the pocket watch into uncured fiberglass resin to create a precise fitted holder, then extracting it cleanly
- **Gel-stage trimming** — cutting excess resin with a knife at the gel stage (before full cure) for a clean finish without sanding
- **Drilling** — adding a push-out hole to the resin holder for battery access and pocket watch removal
- **MIG/arc welding** — joining three steel components to form the clock's base and frame
- **Super glue assembly** — bonding brass gear components to the steel frame
