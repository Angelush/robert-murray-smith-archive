## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a surprisingly simple method for creating "SuperSand" — sand coated with graphene oxide — for use in water filtration. The process involves washing sand with dilute hydrochloric acid, applying a graphene oxide solution, and baking it in an oven under an inert atmosphere created by activated carbon. The resulting material is effective at removing heavy metals from water, and can be further enhanced with silver nanoparticles for antibacterial properties.
