## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a method for producing graphene via liquid-phase exfoliation of graphite using a lab homogenizer and tannic acid (the active component in tea) as a dispersant. The experiment is inspired by a combination of Trinity College's high-shear exfoliation research and Edward Goodridge's 1907 work on graphite flocculation using tea, and Robert presents it as a reproducible, open-source starting point for further research. The video concludes with a candid discussion on open-source science ethics and the importance of crediting prior work.
