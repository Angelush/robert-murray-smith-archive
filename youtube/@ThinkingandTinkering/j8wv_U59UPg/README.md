## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores a "slightly cracked" concept: a wind turbine that works even without wind by swinging it around your head on a string, exploiting the relativity of motion. Inspired by a Kickstarter design shared by a friend (Coach Geo), Robert builds his own modified version using a ball-shaped rotor (to avoid fragile blades), a rectifier bridge, a voltage regulator, and a 100-farad supercapacitor to store harvested energy. The experiment succeeds in charging the capacitor to 2.3V in a few minutes of swinging, though Robert muses that a hand-crank generator would be more practical.
