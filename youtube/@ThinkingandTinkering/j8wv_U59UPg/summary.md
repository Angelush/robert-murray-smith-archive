## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores a "slightly cracked" concept: a wind turbine that works even without wind by swinging it around your head on a string, exploiting the relativity of motion. Inspired by a Kickstarter design shared by a friend (Coach Geo), Robert builds his own modified version using a ball-shaped rotor (to avoid fragile blades), a rectifier bridge, a voltage regulator, and a 100-farad supercapacitor to store harvested energy. The experiment succeeds in charging the capacitor to 2.3V in a few minutes of swinging, though Robert muses that a hand-crank generator would be more practical.

## Key Topics
- Relativity of motion and frames of reference as applied to wind turbines
- The concept of a human-powered "swing turbine" (turbine swung overhead on a string)
- Design critiques of the original Kickstarter concept (fragile blades, swinging via electrical leads)
- Robert's modified design choices: ball shape, string attachment, integrated electronics
- Energy harvesting circuit: rectifier bridge → voltage regulator → supercapacitor
- Setting and calibrating a voltage regulator using a power supply and multimeter
- Practical energy storage options: supercapacitors vs. batteries vs. USB output
- Experimental results and honest assessment of the concept's practicality

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **100-farad supercapacitor (2.7V)** — used as the energy storage device, chosen for fast charging
- **Rectifier bridge** — converts AC generator output to DC
- **Voltage regulator (adjustable)** — stabilizes output voltage; set to 2.7V for the supercapacitor
- **String/rope** — used to swing the turbine overhead
- **LED** — used to verify energy was being stored/output
- **3D-printed enclosure** — housing for the electronics (file updated on Thingiverse)
- **Battery (mentioned as alternative)** — would require a battery control circuit and 3.7V regulation
- **USB output (mentioned as alternative)** — would require 5V regulation

## Techniques and Methods
- Soldering components (rectifier, voltage regulator, supercapacitor) into a compact unit
- Calibrating a voltage regulator using a bench power supply and multimeter (adjusting brass trim-pot screw)
- Rectifier bridge wiring: generator AC in, regulated DC out (plus-to-plus, minus-to-minus)
- Overhead swinging of the turbine on a string to generate relative airflow over the rotor
- Measuring capacitor charge voltage to assess energy harvested
