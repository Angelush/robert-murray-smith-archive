## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith surveys the landscape of solar energy storage technologies — from molten salt and sand batteries to mechanical gravity storage — before introducing the MOST (Molecular Solar Thermal) project, which stores solar energy as a chemical transformation rather than heat or electricity. He concludes that while MOST is scientifically intriguing, its ~3% efficiency and lack of cost data mean it faces a tough road in a highly competitive field where economic and practical factors will ultimately decide the winner.
