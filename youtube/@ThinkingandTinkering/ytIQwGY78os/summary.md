## Overview
Inspired by a viewer's comment about being paralysed by conflicting advice on renewable energy, Robert uses the solar-vs-wind debate as a lens to explore the broader problem of decision paralysis. His central argument is that when equally convincing expert opinions exist on both sides of a question, that is itself strong evidence the options are roughly equivalent — and therefore the most important thing is simply to make a decision and act on it, rather than searching endlessly for the "best" answer.

## Key Topics
- The solar vs. wind energy debate and its competing camps
- Decision paralysis caused by conflicting expert opinions
- Why online forums tend to worsen confusion rather than resolve it
- The philosophical point that evenly-matched competing arguments signal equivalence of options
- The importance of committing to a decision over optimising the decision itself
- Accepting that regrets are inevitable regardless of which path is chosen
- A brief comparison: wind as most accessible/cheapest to implement; solar as more efficient and longer-lasting
