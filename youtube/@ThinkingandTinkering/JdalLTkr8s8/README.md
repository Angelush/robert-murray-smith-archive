## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates the first step in a new method for making graphene ink: producing bromine water from accessible household chemicals. The approach intercalates bromine between graphite layers to weaken the van der Waals forces holding them together, allowing mild sonication to separate the layers into graphene. This video focuses solely on synthesising the bromine water safely and affordably, with the graphite intercalation and sonication steps deferred to a follow-up video.
