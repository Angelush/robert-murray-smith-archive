## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith outlines a low-cost, passive alternative to mechanical solar tracking: placing fluorescent prism-shaped structures on top of solar cells to redirect oblique morning and evening sunlight downward onto the panel. The key insight is that fluorescence in the material can offset the light loss caused by adding a layer on top of the cell, making the concept potentially viable. He proposes using cheap household materials — jello and highlighter pens — as a proof-of-concept prototyping medium before investing in specialist materials.
