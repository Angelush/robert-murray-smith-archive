## Overview

Robert demonstrates a flame test on a graphene-reinforced plastic developed by his colleague Steve, comparing it against standard polypropylene. The 7mm-thick material withstands a butane blow torch tip of approximately 700–800°C, recording only ~260°C on the surface and just 16°C on the reverse side, with minimal charring. The result highlights the material's exceptional thermal insulation properties and hints at potential applications in body armor and automotive industries.
