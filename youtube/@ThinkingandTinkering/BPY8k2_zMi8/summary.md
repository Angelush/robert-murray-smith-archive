## Overview
Robert demonstrates a 3D-printed "impossible gear" mechanism that incorporates the Trammel of Archimedes (also known as the ellipsograph or "do nothing machine") to achieve a 2:1 gear ratio from gears that are all the same size. The build is presented as a fun culmination of a week-long series on impossible gears, and the files are made freely available on Thingiverse.

## Key Topics
- The Trammel of Archimedes (trammel of Archimedes / ellipsograph / "do nothing machine") and how its pivot moves in circular motion
- The concept of "impossible gears" — achieving gear ratio reduction or increase with identically sized gears
- Step-by-step 3D-printed assembly of the mechanism
- Demonstrating a 2:1 gear ratio output

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Silicone oil (used as a lubricant to reduce friction in the printed mechanism)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D modelling in Tinkercad
- FDM 3D printing (implied by the printed parts)
- Force-fitting axles and pegs into printed holes for tight assembly
- Clip-based retention for axles
- Dot-marking technique to count output rotations and verify gear ratio
