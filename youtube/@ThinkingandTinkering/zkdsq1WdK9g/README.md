## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple liquid rheostat (water-based variable resistor) using a plastic tub, metal plates, and saltwater to act as a current limiter for DC arc welding from a battery. The device allows relatively safe welding at low voltages (12–48V) without the intimidation of mains electricity, providing some control over amperage that bare battery connections lack. Robert acknowledges he is not a skilled welder but successfully melts and fuses metal to prove the concept.
