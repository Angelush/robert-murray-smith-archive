## Overview
Robert demonstrates a simplified approach to solar water desalination using carbon felt coated with graphene ink, presented as an accessible alternative to his previous MIT-inspired method using carbonized Oasis foam. By painting graphene ink onto the top surface of carbon felt and floating it on saltwater under a heat lamp, he achieves rapid steam generation that can be condensed into pure water. His key takeaway is that this method eliminates the need for dangerous chemicals or complex fabrication, making it potentially viable for low-cost, large-scale deployment in water-scarce regions.

## Key Topics
- Comparison with his previous desalination video (MIT Oasis foam replication)
- Using carbon felt as a floating solar absorber substrate
- Graphene ink as a light-absorbing, heat-generating coating
- Capillary wicking of water through felt to the graphene surface
- Steam generation and condensation for salt removal
- Goals of robustness and low cost for deployment in developing countries

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Carbon/graphite felt** — porous, water-absorbent substrate that wicks water upward
- **Graphene ink** — painted onto the top surface of the felt; acts as the solar absorber that heats up and generates steam
- **Oasis foam (phenolic foam)** — used in his previous method; mentioned as the more complex alternative
- **Saltwater** — the input water being desalinated
- **Water** — condensed pure output

## Techniques and Methods
- Painting graphene ink onto the top surface of carbon felt and allowing it to dry fully before use
- Floating the coated felt on saltwater so it self-levels as water is consumed
- Illuminating with a heat lamp (as a solar substitute) to drive steam generation
- Condensing steam on a cold glass surface to demonstrate pure water collection
- Capillary wicking — the felt draws saltwater up to the heated graphene layer

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Cleaning/maintenance**: Suggests simply washing the felt in water; references backwashing with brine (as used in reverse osmosis systems) as a likely method for dealing with salt buildup.
- **Webshop**: Directs viewers to his shop at `www.workingink.co.uk` / `https://secure.workingink.co.uk/working-ink-shop/` for the graphene ink and carbon felt.
- **Long-term performance**: Acknowledges uncertainty — this is an early-stage idea and he hasn't yet tested long-term durability or salt saturation behavior.
- **Scaling up**: Notes that large-scale deployment requires significant funding; expresses some frustration at requests for large-scale work from non-supporters.
- **Follow-up testing**: Confirms he plans to test the device outdoors in sunlight when summer arrives.
- **Previous video**: Repeatedly directs commenters with mechanistic questions to watch his companion Oasis foam video for a fuller explanation of the underlying physics.
