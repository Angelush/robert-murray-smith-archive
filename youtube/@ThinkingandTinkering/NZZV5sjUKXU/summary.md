## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates his homemade "Ecofoam" carpet cleaner, a product he developed after years of struggling to clean graphite-contaminated carpets. The cleaner is supplied as a dry powder (reducing plastic and liquid waste), mixed with hot water and scrubbed in, and visibly restores color to heavily soiled carpet in about 20 minutes. Robert presents it as a genuinely effective solution after trying every commercial alternative.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the Ecofoam product line (hand cleaner, hard surface cleaner, carpet cleaner)
- The problem of graphite contamination in his home from his scientific work
- Demonstration of the carpet cleaner on years of embedded graphite grime
- Eco-friendly dry powder format to reduce packaging and liquid waste
- Alternative use with an air foamer device for surface cleaning

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Ecofoam carpet cleaner** — dry powder formulation, Robert's own invention; 2 tablespoons per bowl of hot water
- **Graphite** — the primary contaminant ground into the carpet over years of work
- **Ecofoam hard surface cleaner** — related product in the same line; 1 teaspoon per bottle of hot water, used with an air foamer

## Techniques and Methods
- Dissolving 2 tablespoons of dry powder in a bowl of hot water
- Wetting a scrubbing brush and working the solution into carpet fibres
- Using an air foamer bottle (1 teaspoon powder + hot water, wait until clear, then foam onto surface) as an alternative application method
- Planning to test the cleaner with a Vax carpet cleaning machine (mentioned in comments)
