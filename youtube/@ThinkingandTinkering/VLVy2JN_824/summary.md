## Overview
Robert demonstrates a practical real-world use case for his ADO A20 foldable e-bike: with his wife unwell and unable to give him a lift, he needed a way to drop his car off at the MOT station (UK roadworthiness test) and get back without a taxi. He simply folded the e-bike into the car boot, drove to the station, dropped off the car, assembled the bike, and rode away — saving £20–30 in taxi fare.

## Key Topics
- Solving the "car at the MOT station" logistics problem without a second driver
- Practical utility of a foldable e-bike as a car boot companion
- Pedal-assist vs. traditional folding bikes for older riders
- Cost comparison: e-bike vs. taxi (£20–30 saved)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Folding and unfolding the ADO A20 e-bike for boot storage
- No seat-folding required — bike fits directly into boot without modifying rear seats

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirms he was his own cameraman for most of the video; the follow-shot while riding was filmed by a passing stranger he asked on the spot.
- Clarifies the A20 is a **pedal-assist** e-bike, not a standard folding bike — an important distinction he says he made in the first video of the playlist. Pedal assist is particularly valuable for older riders who can't rely purely on leg power.
- Notes that removing a front wheel (an alternative way to fit a bike in a car) adds inconvenience compared to simply folding the A20.
- Acknowledges public transport as an option but dismisses it as inconvenient, slow, and still requiring a walk.
