## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith addresses criticism of his belief in "free energy" by reframing the concept as a question of perspective: whether something counts as free energy depends entirely on how broadly you define the system you're observing. He argues that we already routinely harness free energy — through solar panels, wind turbines, sailboats, and crystal radios — and that the more esoteric goal of tapping quantum-level energy (quantum foam/zero-point energy) is a natural extension of this same principle.
