## Overview
A short woodworking tutorial demonstrating how to reinforce mitre/butt joint frames using wooden dowel. The presenter (Luke) shows a simple two-step technique: drilling 45-degree angled holes through each corner joint and gluing dowel rods into them, resulting in a dramatically stronger frame. A clever folded-card trick is highlighted as the key tip for reliably gauging the 45-degree drill angle.

## Key Topics
- Reinforcing mitre/butt joint frames with dowel
- Drilling at a 45-degree angle through corner joints
- Using a folded piece of card as a 45-degree angle guide
- Drilling a shallow pilot hole to seat the drill bit before angling
- Gluing and sanding dowel flush after insertion

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Wood dowel (6 mm diameter used in the video; any thickness can work depending on joint size)
- Wood glue (used to coat the dowel before insertion)
- Piece of card/paper (used as an angle guide)

## Techniques and Methods
- Folding a piece of card in half to create a precise 45-degree angle guide for drill alignment
- Drilling a shallow 1 mm starter hole perpendicular to the surface to seat the bit before drilling at the final angle
- Drilling a 2 mm pilot hole at 45 degrees through each corner joint, then widening to 6 mm to match the dowel
- Applying glue to the dowel and tapping it into the hole
- Sanding the protruding dowel flush with the frame surface after drying
