## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith announces planned changes to his YouTube channel after eight years and over 1,100 videos. He explains that the sheer volume of content has made it difficult to navigate, and outlines a reorganization effort: removing low-quality videos, re-editing multi-part series into single concise videos, and organizing content into better playlists. The goal is to make information — particularly on battery-building — easier to find and use.

## Key Topics
- The channel's history: ~8 years old, ~1,100 videos
- The problem of content being hard to discover due to volume
- Plan to remove outdated or poor-quality videos
- Plan to re-edit multi-part video series into single consolidated videos
- Reorganization into better playlists, starting with the batteries playlist

## Robert's Own Replies
In the comments, Robert clarified that older videos will not simply be deleted or made public in reorganized playlists — instead, they will be moved to a **members-only archive playlist**. His reasoning: members are the audience who most care about older content, and the members area functions almost like a separate channel. He noted that non-members are "receivers of a service," while members are effectively customers with access to this archived material.
