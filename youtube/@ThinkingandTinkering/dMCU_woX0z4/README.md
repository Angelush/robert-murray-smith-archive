## Overview

Robert challenges the conventional view of energy as a substance or property that transforms between forms (kinetic, potential, chemical, etc.), arguing instead that energy is a *process* arising from a system — not a thing in itself. Using the analogies of walking and a rock on a hill, he proposes that what we call "energy" is inseparable from the system that produces it, and that understanding this distinction has practical implications for fields like electrochemistry, battery design, and our pursuit of concepts like free energy.
