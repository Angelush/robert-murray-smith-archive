## Overview
Robert challenges the conventional view of energy as a substance or property that transforms between forms (kinetic, potential, chemical, etc.), arguing instead that energy is a *process* arising from a system — not a thing in itself. Using the analogies of walking and a rock on a hill, he proposes that what we call "energy" is inseparable from the system that produces it, and that understanding this distinction has practical implications for fields like electrochemistry, battery design, and our pursuit of concepts like free energy.

## Key Topics
- The conventional model of energy as a transformable substance, and why Robert finds it inadequate
- Energy reframed as a **process** emerging from a **system**, rather than a property of matter
- The walking analogy: walking is not a thing converted from sitting — it is a process performed through space by legs
- The rock-and-hill analogy: carrying a rock uphill does not "store energy" — it changes the system's state
- Equilibrium reframed: systems do not move between "equilibrium" and "disequilibrium" — they move between **different equilibrium positions**
- Electrochemical systems: how changing conditions (e.g. pH, electrolyte) favours different reactions without "adding energy"
- Implications for understanding free energy and Zero Point Energy
- Nature's efficiency explained by the fact that nature *is* a system undergoing processes, not a converter of energy-things
- Entropy and state transitions

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies add several important clarifications:

- **Process vs. change**: He distinguishes between a *change* (which represents one state and then another) and a *process* (the means by which that change occurs) — the latter is what he means by energy.
- **Energy is not a thing, but not everything is energy**: He explicitly clarifies, *"Energy isn't a thing at all — it is the process things undergo — that doesn't mean all things are made of energy. That's like saying the legs are made of walking."*
- **Energy is not the system**: *"I am not saying energy is a system. I am saying things in relation can be seen as a system and energy is the process that a system undergoes to transition from one state to another."*
- **Process vs. work**: He notes that *"the process of transition is very different from 'work being done'"* — an important distinction from classical mechanics.
- **CERN analogy**: Energy as a property of matter drives collider experiments at CERN — to his mind, *"it's a bit like cutting up legs to find that thing called walking — walking doesn't exist inside the legs and energy can't be found inside matter."*
- **Thinking is energy**: In one reply he states simply, *"thinking is energy — they are one and the same,"* extending the framework to cognition.
- **Emergent properties**: He mentions reading about *boids* and emergent properties as something he feels fits into this framework.
- **Follow-up video**: He mentions he is working on a follow-up called *"On Gravity."*
- **Faster-than-light**: He references the Wheatstone experiment as a case where faster-than-light travel is supposedly demonstrated, and says he is considering repeating it for his own peace of mind — questioning Einstein's theory as definitive.
