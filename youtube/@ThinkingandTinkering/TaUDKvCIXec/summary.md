## Overview
A short festive video from Robert Murray-Smith's 2022 Advent Science Magic series (Day 11), demonstrating how to grow rock candy crystals on a stick at home. Using a supersaturated sugar solution with food coloring and a seed-crystal-coated skewer, viewers are shown a simple crystallisation experiment that doubles as an edible Christmas treat, producing results in three to five days.

## Key Topics
- Supersaturated sugar solutions and crystal nucleation
- Growing rock candy (crystal candy) on a seeded skewer

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Sugar** — the primary solute; approximately 3.5 cups used to form the supersaturated solution
- **Water** — approximately 1 cup used as the solvent
- **Food coloring** — added to the cooled solution for visual variety
- **Flavor (unspecified)** — optionally added alongside the coloring

## Techniques and Methods
- Dissolving sugar in heated water to create a supersaturated solution
- Seeding a skewer by dipping it in the solution then rolling it in loose sugar to provide crystal nucleation sites
- Pouring the cooled solution into a jar and suspending the seeded skewer to allow slow crystal growth over 3–5 days
