## Overview
Robert demonstrates a clever, low-cost method for fabricating a large gear/cog by repurposing a bicycle pulley rim (from his earlier power transmission video #1135) as the structural base. He fills the rim with expanding foam, lets it cure, trims and sands it flat, then glues a cut-to-size timing belt around the outer edge to create the gear teeth. The result is a true, square, functional gear that leverages the rim's built-in bearings.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing a bicycle pulley rim as a gear base
- Using expanding foam as a filler material
- Attaching a timing belt to create gear teeth
- Using the rim's existing bearings instead of separate pillow bearings

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Pulley rim** — bicycle rim used as the structural base of the gear (from video #1135)
- **Expanding foam** — used to fill the rim cavity and create a solid surface
- **Timing belt** — cut to size and glued around the rim to form the gear teeth profile
- **Cyanoacrylate (super glue / instant bond glue)** — used to adhere the timing belt to the foam-filled rim

## Techniques and Methods
- Filling a pulley rim with expanding foam and allowing it to cure overnight
- Trimming excess cured foam with a hacksaw blade
- Sanding the foam surface flat to the rim edge
- Dry-fitting a timing belt to verify alignment before gluing
- Cutting a timing belt to length and bonding it to the rim surface with cyanoacrylate adhesive
