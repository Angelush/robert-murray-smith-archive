## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the surprisingly broad world of gear mechanisms, starting from first principles—three types of motion (rotation, translation, screw) and three spatial relationships between axles—before showcasing ten unusual and "bizarre" gear designs from various inventors and researchers. The video argues that gears are far more than the simple circular toothed wheels most people picture, and that the underlying principles open up a vast design space. Many of the examples are novelties or desk toys, but some have genuine practical applications in robotics, flow measurement, and more.
