## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith introduces the concept of plastic pyrolysis — converting waste plastics into liquid fuel — by demonstrating a hands-on destructive distillation experiment using HDPE carrier bags in a simple paint-can reactor. He then pivots to cover a recently published paper describing a groundbreaking low-energy catalytic process that converts polyethylene and polypropylene into gasoline-like compounds at just 70°C, representing a dramatic improvement over conventional high-temperature pyrolysis methods.
