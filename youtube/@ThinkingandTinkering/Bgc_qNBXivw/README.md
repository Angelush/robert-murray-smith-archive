## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reframes the wind turbine not as a device that "produces" electricity, but as a system of three distinct components: input capture, conversion, and output storage. He argues that the generator is strictly a *converter* governed by the laws of thermodynamics, not a producer, and that this conceptual shift is key to diagnosing performance issues and understanding all mechanical energy systems as part of the same family.
