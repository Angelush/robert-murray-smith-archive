## Overview
A detailed build update for Robert's E Quad electric quadricycle project, focusing on two mechanical systems: the front steering linkage and the rear axle/drive assembly. Robert explains the practical design choices made for a low-speed, short-range vehicle, prioritising simplicity over engineering perfection.

## Key Topics
- Front steering linkage design using push rods and turnbuckles
- Z-shaped handlebar extension for knee clearance in the cabin
- Acknowledgement of Ackermann steering geometry and why it was deliberately skipped
- Rear axle construction using pipe sections, bearings, and a 12mm bar
- Chain drive attachment using the original bicycle chainset
- Wheel attachment and bearing arrangement for the rear axle

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 12mm steel bar (threaded at ends, used as rear axle)
- Pipe sections (used as bearing housings for rear axle)
- Bearings (four total, pressed into pipe sections)
- Turnbuckles (8mm, used as adjustable steering tie rods)
- Steel angle and flat bar (welded brackets on fork tops)
- Original bicycle chainset (repurposed for chain drive)
- Wheels with 12mm centre holes

## Techniques and Methods
- Welding angle brackets onto front forks for steering linkage attachment
- Welding a flange onto the axle bar for wheel fixing
- Using turnbuckles as adjustable tie rods to align front wheels
- Pressing/fitting bearings into pipe sections to create axle bearing housings
- Disassembling bicycle rear wheel to extract bare wheel with 12mm bore
- Threading the end of the axle bar for wheel retention
