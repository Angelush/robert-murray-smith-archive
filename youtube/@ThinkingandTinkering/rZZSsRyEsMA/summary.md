## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a 3D-printed mechanism using three identical bevel gears that achieves a 2:1 gear ratio — directly challenging the conventional wisdom that same-sized gears can only produce a 1:1 ratio. This is the second video in a series on "impossible gears," improving on a previous linkage-based design by using only gears. The assembled device is back-drivable and he suggests it hints at something deeper and important hidden in the mechanism.

## Key Topics
- Disproving the adage that identical gears can only give a 1:1 gear ratio
- Design of a three-bevel-gear differential mechanism in Tinkercad
- 3D printing and assembly of the gear mechanism
- Demonstrating a 2:1 gear reduction using only same-sized bevel gears
- Back-drivability of the mechanism
- Plans to share files on Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 3D-printed parts (material unspecified, implied FDM plastic)

## Techniques and Methods
- CAD design in Tinkercad
- FDM 3D printing of all mechanical components
- Press-fit assembly (tight-tolerance pillars and axle)
- Clip retention for rotating bevel gear and axle
- Visual ratio verification using a reference dot on the output
