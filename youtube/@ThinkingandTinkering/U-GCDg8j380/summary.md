## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith compares three different sandblasting media for use with a pressure washer sandblasting attachment: glass frit, Kärcher professional black grit, and ordinary kiln-dried builder's sand. All three performed similarly in cleaning power, but builder's sand won decisively on cost and ease of use. His main takeaway is that cheap kiln-dried sharp builder's sand is the best practical choice for this application.

## Key Topics
- Comparison of sandblasting media: glass frit, Kärcher professional grit, and builder's sand
- Cost analysis of each media type (sand at ~£3/25kg vs Kärcher at £33/25kg)
- Effect of container shape (wide bucket vs. tall narrow container) on media feed consistency
- Practical tips for keeping sand dry to prevent clogging
- Overall cost-effectiveness of the pressure washer sandblasting method vs. sandpaper or angle grinder wire brush

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Glass frit** — sharp glass granules, used in the previous video; relatively expensive (~£10/25kg estimated)
- **Kärcher professional black grit** — dark/black abrasive media, £33 for ~25kg; possibly corundum or similar hard material
- **Kiln-dried builder's sand** — sharp sand from a local hardware store, ~£3–4/25kg; identified as the best value option

## Techniques and Methods
- Pressure washer sandblasting using a dedicated sandblasting attachment
- Testing media in different container types (wide bucket vs. tall narrow bucket vs. cut-down powder fire extinguisher) to improve media uptake
- Shaking the container to break up clumps and restore media flow
- Re-drying and reusing media (noted as impractical for expensive grit due to energy cost)
