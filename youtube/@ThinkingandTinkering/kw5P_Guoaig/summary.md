## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build decorative high-voltage Leyden jar-style capacitors for a Wimshurst electrostatic generator using glass vessels and his own conductive iron-based ink, rather than conventional aluminium foil. He validates the approach by first testing a flat glass sheet painted on both sides, confirming a measured capacitance of 2.7 nanofarads — consistent with the theoretical value for glass with a dielectric constant of 5–10. The resulting capacitors are functional electrical components but also aesthetically striking objects in the steampunk/Victorian tradition.

## Key Topics
- Background on Wimshurst machine capacitors (traditionally Leyden jars) and conventional aluminium-based construction
- Motivation for using conductive ink and glass instead of aluminium tape or foil
- Flat-glass proof-of-concept test to validate the ink-as-electrode approach
- Step-by-step build of two decorative high-voltage capacitors from glass vases, wood offcuts, brass hardware, and conductive ink
- Fabrication of adjustable brass top-electrode assemblies with a dangling brass chain for internal contact
- Applying conductive ink to the inside (by pouring and swirling) and outside (by brush painting) of the glass
- Use of copper slug tape as the external electrode contact strip
- Safety considerations: wood conductivity at high voltage, rubber insulation pads and handle wraps
- Uncertainty about whether the ink will survive arc discharge

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Conductive iron-based ink** — used as the electrode coating on both inner and outer glass surfaces (product from workingink.co.uk)
- **Glass vase / highball glass** — dielectric of the capacitor; 4 mm glass has a dielectric constant of ~5–10
- **Copper tape (slug tape)** — used as the external electrode contact strip around the base of the glass
- **Aluminium tape** (mentioned as conventional alternative) — used in standard Leyden jar / pipe capacitor construction
- **Brass bar (6 mm, various lengths)** — structural and electrical conductor for the top terminal assembly
- **Brass chain (bath plug chain)** — internal conductor hanging from the top terminal to contact the inner ink coating
- **Terminal block (brass insert)** — repurposed to join brass bar sections
- **M10 bolt** (flattened and drilled) — part of the articulated arm joint
- **Resin drawer handle** — decorative component of the top arm assembly
- **Mahogany table leg / coffee table leg offcuts** — wooden structural base and arm components
- **Cutting board** — material for the base discs
- **Rubber** — proposed insulating pads for the base and handles to guard against wood conductivity at high voltage
- **Solder** — for joining brass components
- **Oil** (implied, for wood finishing)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Flat-glass capacitor test**: painting both sides of a 43 × 28 cm sheet of 4 mm glass with conductive ink and copper tape underlayer, then measuring capacitance with a meter to validate the approach
- **Disc-cutting and laminating**: cutting three wooden/cutting-board discs (~100 mm), hollowing one to form a retaining ring, gluing and polishing pairs together to form base and top collar
- **Brass terminal fabrication**: stripping terminal blocks for brass inserts, soldering brass bar through a lug, drilling and flattening an M10 bolt for the pivot joint
- **Wooden knurled adjustment nut**: embedding a nut into a drilled wooden offcut using a vice to create a hand-adjustable fastener
- **Inside coating by pour-and-swirl**: pouring conductive ink into the glass vessel, swirling to coat the interior, then pouring off excess while still wet; using electrician's tape as a fill-level marker
- **Outside coating by brush**: applying three coats of conductive ink to the exterior of the glass over a copper tape contact strip
- **Copper tape electrode strip**: adhering slug tape around the glass base with a folded-back tab to provide an accessible external connection point
- **Dip-coating the brass terminal assembly**: dipping the brass internal contact piece into conductive ink three times for a conductive coating
