## Overview
This video is a technical progress update on Robert's "SWAG generator" prototype — a wave-powered electrochemical energy device using graphite-coated plates as a gill-like electrode structure. Robert explains a key design refinement: isolating the electrode assembly from direct seawater contact using a closed tank and plunger system, which solves the marine fouling problem while allowing fluid experimentation. He also previews a companion video in which the same hardware is tested as a thermal battery using copper chloride solution.

## Key Topics
- Current state of the SWAG generator prototype build
- Gill-like electrode structure: construction, materials, and assembly
- The marine fouling problem and the tank/plunger isolation solution
- Fluid flexibility: using alternative ionic solutions instead of raw seawater
- Teaser for thermal battery experiment using the same hardware
- Invitation for community members to explore the thermal battery direction independently

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Plastic sheet** — substrate for the electrode panels
- **Copper strips** — used as current collectors on each panel
- **Conductive ink** (from FWG) — painted onto panels to form the active electrode surface
- **Bitumen (bitchman)** — applied as a sealant/coating to protect non-active surfaces, leaving only graphite exposed
- **Metal tabs / solder** — used to electrically connect the 10 panels in parallel
- **Plastic blocks (1 cm, kitchen-grade)** — spacers to hold panels apart
- **Saltwater / ionic fluid** — working electrolyte in the tank
- **Copper chloride solution** (~100 g in 15 L water) — used in the companion thermal battery test

## Techniques and Methods
- Screen/brush painting of conductive graphite ink onto plastic substrate panels
- Soldering metal tabs to create a multi-panel parallel electrode assembly
- Bitumen sealing to restrict electrochemical attack to the graphite surface only
- Wave-driven plunger mechanism to circulate fluid over electrodes without direct sea exposure
- Closed-loop tank isolation to prevent marine fouling and allow controlled fluid substitution
- Exploratory testing of the same electrode assembly as a thermal battery
