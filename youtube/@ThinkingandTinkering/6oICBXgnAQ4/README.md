## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the history of the wristwatch from its WWI-era popularization to the revolutionary Bulova Accutron, which replaced traditional balance wheels with a transistor-driven tuning fork vibrating at 360 Hz. He then demonstrates a 3D-printed scale model of the Accutron's indexing mechanism that he designed himself, and highlights a freely available reference book ("Mechanical Movements, Devices and Appliances" by Hiscox) as an invaluable resource for mechanical designers.
