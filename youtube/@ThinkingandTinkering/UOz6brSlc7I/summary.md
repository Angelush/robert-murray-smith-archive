## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make a surface sanitizer (sodium hypochlorite / household bleach) at home by electrolyzing a salt water solution, inspired by a £20 commercial kit being sold on Amazon and eBay. He shows the process is trivially simple and cheap, requiring only common household materials and a basic electrical supply.

## Key Topics
- Critique of a £20 commercial "make your own sanitizer" kit being sold during the COVID-19 second wave
- Electrolysis of salt water to produce sodium hypochlorite
- Safety profile of dilute sodium hypochlorite as a surface sanitizer
- Electrode material selection for home electrolysis
- DIY electrolyzer construction from cheap materials

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Tap water** — 250 ml, the solvent base
- **Table salt (NaCl)** — 10 grams, dissolved in the water to make brine
- **Sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl)** — the sanitizer product formed; equivalent to ~0.8% household bleach
- **Graphite/clay pencil lead** — used as electrodes; specifically HB pencils or carpenter's pencils noted as effective
- **Battery carbon rods** — graphite/clay/wax electrodes salvaged or new from batteries, used as an alternative electrode material
- **Chlorine gas (Cl₂)** — by-product to avoid; generated if voltage is too high or electrolysis runs too long
- **Hydrogen gas (H₂)** — produced at the negative electrode during electrolysis

## Techniques and Methods
- **Electrolysis of brine** — applying 6V DC across two graphite electrodes submerged in salt water for ~5 minutes to produce sodium hypochlorite
- **Electrode preparation** — drilling holes in a plastic furniture fixing block to hold graphite rods ~set distance apart (~half amp draw)
- **Burning off wax from battery rods** — using a blowtorch or flame to remove wax coating before use as electrodes
- **Smell test** — confirming product quality by the characteristic chlorine/bleach odour of the resulting solution
