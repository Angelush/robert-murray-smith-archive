## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith provides a progress update on the FWG supercapacitor/battery project, explaining that the two-person team (Robert and Steve) is working on tight budgets and has settled on a carbon paper electrode approach compatible with existing paper-manufacturing infrastructure. Independent verification is planned with the UK's National Physics Laboratory at a cost of ~£10–12k, expected to begin in November 2019 with results by December–January.

## Key Topics
- Project status update and team context (two people, tight budget)
- Plans for independent third-party verification via the National Physics Laboratory (UK)
- Selection of carbon paper as the electrode form factor, chosen for manufacturing compatibility
- Reference to Tesla's acquisition of Maxwell Technologies for its dry battery electrode process
- Cell assembly: carbon paper electrodes in conductive plastic, heat-sealed and vacuum-sealed
- Demonstration of a small cell powering a motor
- Series and parallel cell stacking for voltage and current scaling
- Energy density figures for both supercapacitor and battery modes
- Development of a bright plating solution for conductive coating on plastic casings
- Development of a conductive acrylic adhesive for bonding electrode paper to current collectors
- Plans to sell both materials via their online shop (workingink.co.uk) to raise revenue
- Target application: lead-acid battery replacement and home/grid storage

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Carbon** — explored in multiple forms (cloth, felt, powder); settled on carbon paper made via a paper-making process
- **Dimethylformamide (DMF)** — traditional electrode slurry solvent, cited as expensive and hazardous (used as a contrast to their dry approach)
- **NMP (N-methyl-2-pyrrolidone)** — another traditional slurry solvent, similarly cited as problematic
- **Conductive plastic** — used for the cell casing, heat-sealed and vacuum-sealed around the electrode stack
- **Bright plating solution** — described as a "neutral salt" solution applied via wand plating to deposit a metallic coating on the plastic case
- **Conductive acrylic adhesive** — solvent-free, pressure-sensitive acrylic adhesive developed in-house to bond carbon paper to the current collector
- **Aqueous electrolyte** — implied by the statement that the adhesive must not dissolve in water

## Techniques and Methods
- **Paper-making process** — carbon powder incorporated into a paper-making workflow to produce sheets of active carbon electrode material
- **Heat sealing and vacuum sealing** — used to package cells in conductive plastic casing
- **Wand plating (bright plating)** — a neutral-salt solution rubbed onto plastic surfaces to deposit a bright metallic conductive coating without a large tank setup
- **Pressure-sensitive conductive adhesive application** — adhesive wiped on, allowed to dry to a permanent tack, then carbon paper pressed onto current collector
- **Series and parallel cell stacking** — cells folded and stacked with plastic separators between layers to increase voltage (series) or current (parallel)
- **Dry battery electrode manufacturing** — referenced via Maxwell/Tesla as cost-reduction inspiration; Robert later confirms in comments they developed their own version of this

- **Target application:** "We aren't looking at lithium-ion — these batteries are meant to replace lead acid." Also confirms: "We are looking at the lead acid grid and home storage mate."
