## Overview
A short practical tip on how to make a perfectly fitting blanking plate without careful measuring. Robert demonstrates using super glue to temporarily attach a steel plate to the existing fitting, drilling through it to transfer the hole positions exactly, then removing the plate with heat. The result is a blanking plate with holes in exactly the right positions.

## Key Topics
- Making a blanking plate without precise measurement
- Using an existing fitting as a drilling template
- Temporary adhesion with super glue for metalwork

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Steel (used as the blanking plate material)
- Super glue (cyanoacrylate, used as temporary adhesive)

## Techniques and Methods
- Using an existing part as a drill template by placing it directly over the new workpiece
- Temporarily bonding the template to the workpiece with super glue, allowing it to set, then drilling through both
- Applying gentle heat to release the super-glued template after drilling
