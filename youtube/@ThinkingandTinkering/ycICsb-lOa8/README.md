## Overview

Robert demonstrates a graphene-in-water nanofluid and tests its heat absorption efficiency against plain tap water using two methods: light exposure and direct hot-plate heating. The graphene solution absorbs heat significantly faster in both tests — roughly 7x better at absorbing solar/light energy and about 2x better at direct thermal transfer — making it a promising, environmentally benign fluid for solar thermal and cooling applications.
