## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a functional electric bicycle frame almost entirely from rolled newspaper tubes, demonstrating that paper can serve as a viable structural material for a bike frame. He assembles the frame using butt joints wrapped with carbon fiber and resin, then adds an unusual belt-drive system using a timing belt glued to an oversized rim. The finished paper e-bike successfully carries him around a car park under its own power using a 120W motor.

## Key Topics
- Rolling newspapers into structural tubes using a homemade rolling machine (inspired by the commercial "Stixx" machine)
- Designing a bicycle frame as three triangles and replicating that in paper
- Jointing techniques: butt joints, filler adhesives, and wrapping for structural strength
- Belt drive system as an alternative to chain drive
- Building an oversized rear drive cog from two bicycle rims bolted together
- Attaching a timing belt as the drive belt on the large cog
- Testing the completed paper e-bike with a 120W motor

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Recycled newspaper** — primary structural material, rolled into tubes
- **Epoxy resin / PVA mixed with sawdust / wood filler** — suggested as joint fillers (alternatives)
- **Styrene resin** — used for the carbon fiber wrapping; chosen for flexibility over brittleness
- **Polyurethane resin** — mentioned as an alternative, noted as somewhat brittle
- **Carbon fiber** — used to wrap and strengthen joints
- **Cyanoacrylate (super glue / crazy glue)** — used to bond the timing belt to the drive cog rim
- **Rubber cement / acrylic glue** — mentioned as alternatives to cyanoacrylate
- **Timing belt** — used as the drive belt for the belt-drive system

## Techniques and Methods
- Rolling newspaper sheets into tight tubes (DIY rolling machine made from pipe and rubber)
- Butt-joining paper tubes to length and filling joints with adhesive filler for registration
- Wrapping joints with resin-wetted carbon fiber for structural strength
- Sanding wrapped joints smooth
- Super-gluing two bicycle rims together, drilling through both for alignment, then bolting them to create a large composite drive cog
- Filling the bolt-head side of the cog with resin to create a flat surface for the belt
- Gluing a timing belt around the large cog rim to complete the belt-drive system
- Testing the assembled e-bike under load (rider weight + motor torque)
