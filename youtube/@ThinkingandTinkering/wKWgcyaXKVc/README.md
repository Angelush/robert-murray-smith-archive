## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith salvages the motor and gearbox from a shredded paper shredder and repurposes them into a hand-cranked generator by swapping the induction motor for a cheap DC motor from eBay. The build achieves approximately 40 watts of output (19V at 2A), matching the power output of an athlete, at a total cost of around £5 — a fraction of the £40 commercial equivalents. The video demonstrates that capable off-grid generators can be built almost entirely from salvaged and low-cost components.
