## Overview
Luke builds a steampunk-themed budget PC for under £100, walking through component selection and the full assembly process. The build targets light gaming and basic video editing, using a mix of secondhand and budget parts. The completed machine successfully posts and boots, demonstrating that capable PC builds are achievable on a tight budget.

## Key Topics
- Budget PC component sourcing (secondhand, eBay, friend deals)
- Step-by-step PC assembly inside a custom steampunk case
- Cost breakdown of each component
- Basic POST and boot verification
- Cable management in a custom case

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Thermal grease / thermal paste — applied between CPU and CPU cooler to ensure proper heat transfer

## Techniques and Methods
- CPU installation: aligning indicator marks and dropping in without pressure
- RAM installation: aligning the notch to the DIMM slot
- Thermal paste application: pea-sized dot in the centre, spread by cooler pressure
- Motherboard mounting: using standoffs in the case
- Front panel connector wiring: recommended looking up motherboard pin layout online
- PSU discharge procedure: unplugging and holding power button 10–15 seconds before adding components
- Incremental boot test: testing POST before installing the GPU
- HDD and SSD installation via SATA cable and power connector
- Basic cable management: routing cables through the front panel gap
