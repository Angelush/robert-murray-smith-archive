## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith designs and builds a 3D-printed micro-generator intended to fit inside a standard UK rainwater downpipe, harvesting energy from falling rainwater. Extending a previous water-based generator experiment, he assembles a six-part printed turbine with wound coils and neodymium magnets, demonstrating it generating 1.6V when blown on and 5V during a water test. He makes the design files freely available on Tinkercad and outlines clear paths for improvement.
