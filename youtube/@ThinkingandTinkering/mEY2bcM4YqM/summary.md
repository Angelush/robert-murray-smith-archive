## Overview
Luke demonstrates an emergency temporary repair for a burst radiator hose pipe using a bicycle tire puncture repair kit. The repair was prompted by a real breakdown on the way home from a fireworks display. The video follows the repair from initial inspection through a 3–4 day real-world follow-up, confirming the fix held well enough for short daily commutes until a proper garage appointment.

## Key Topics
- Emergency field repair of a burst radiator/coolant hose
- Repurposing a bicycle tire puncture repair kit for automotive use
- Importance of coolant for engine health
- Pressure testing a patch before reinstallation
- Multi-day real-world follow-up on repair durability

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Coolant fluid** — drained from the burst pipe; topped up after repair and monitored over follow-up days
- **Bicycle tire puncture repair kit** — rubber solution (vulcanising cement) and rubber patches; core repair material
- **Sandpaper** — used to key/roughen the pipe surface before patching
- **Waterproof Gorilla tape** — wrapped over the patch for additional security
- **Plaster and bandage tape** — used as a makeshift roadside fix before the proper repair (from a first aid kit)
- **Water** — used in a bucket to pressure-test the patched tube for leaks/bubbles

## Techniques and Methods
- Removing the upper radiator hose for off-vehicle repair
- Sanding the damaged area to improve adhesion
- Applying rubber vulcanising solution and allowing it to tack
- Pressing the largest available rubber patch over the slit, ensuring full coverage
- Reinforcing with wrapped waterproof tape
- Submerged pressure test: hand-pressurising the tube in a bucket of water and checking for air bubbles
- Reinstalling the hose, topping up coolant, and running the engine to check for leaks
