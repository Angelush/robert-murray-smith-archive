## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a functional electrostatic earbud speaker by drawing a graphite pattern on paper with a 6B pencil, driven by an inexpensive high-voltage step-up module. The video expands into a broader discussion of asymmetric capacitors, the Biefeld-Brown thrust effect, and the internal circuit of the high-voltage modules. The central takeaway is that a working speaker element can be made from almost nothing — a piece of paper and a pencil — replacing the magnet and voice coil of a conventional earbud.
