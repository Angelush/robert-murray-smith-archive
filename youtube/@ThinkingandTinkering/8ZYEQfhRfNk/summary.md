## Overview
A short, playful experiment in which Robert tests whether a small computer fan repurposed as a generator can produce electricity from household water flow. He first tries a low-flow toilet flush, which proves too weak to spin the fan, then moves to a laundry sink tap, which succeeds in lighting an LED.

## Key Topics
- Micro-hydro power generation from household water sources
- Low-flow toilets and their limited water pressure
- Repurposing a computer fan as a small water-driven generator
- Comparing toilet flush vs. sink tap as power sources

- None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Attaching a small computer fan to a water stream to act as a turbine/generator
- Partial enclosure of the fan in plastic for water protection
- Visual observation of fan spin and LED illumination as output indicators
