## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith discovers a discarded Reebok ZL8 elliptical cross trainer left by a skip with a "free to good home" note, and begins disassembling it to repurpose it as a wind turbine. The video introduces Part 1 of a series exploring how exercise machines — which he has frequently converted into generation equipment — can be adapted into wind generators, with Robert settling on a plan to mount either a vertical or horizontal axis wind turbine on the stripped frame.

## Key Topics
- Finding discarded exercise equipment (Reebok ZL8 elliptical cross trainer) for free
- Disassembly and inspection of the cross trainer's internals
- Overview of previous exercise machine projects (wind generator, hand crank generator, coil/magnet test bed)
- Planning a vertical axis wind turbine (VAWT) or horizontal axis wind turbine (HAWT) conversion
- Sourcing cheap exercise machines via Gumtree or Craigslist

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Cast iron flywheel (part of the cross trainer)
- Magnetic resistance shoe (braking mechanism)
- Small electronics/motor controlling resistance distance
- Belt/pulley drive system (large driving pulley and idler pulley)
- Cutlass pin (fixing point on the frame)

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly by unbolting the cross trainer frame
- Stripping the machine down to its core mechanical components
- Sawing off unwanted frame sections to prepare a mounting base
- Planning drum attachment for a vertical axis wind turbine configuration
