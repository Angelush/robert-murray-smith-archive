## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith documents his very first print using the Elegoo Mars 2 Pro UV MSLA resin 3D printer, walking through the complete process from setup to finished part. He explains the key mechanical principles at work — particularly the "tug of war" between the build plate and FEP sheet — and demonstrates the preparation steps that led to a successful first print on the first attempt. The printed object is later revealed to be a part for a mini wind turbine.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the Elegoo Mars 2 Pro UV MSLA resin printer (part of an ongoing series)
- The FEP sheet vs. build plate adhesion dynamic ("tug of war")
- Preparing the FEP sheet with PTFE spray to reduce sticking
- Coating the build plate with resin to eliminate air gaps
- Choosing between ABS-like resin and standard polymer resin
- Slicing software (ChiTuBox) and the CTB file format requirement
- Post-processing: IPA wash, support removal, and UV curing
- Future plans to make printed resin electrically conductive

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **ABS-like resin** — harder and slightly flexible; preferred for mechanical/machine parts
- **Standard polymer resin** — harder and more brittle; suited for models
- **PTFE spray** — applied to the FEP sheet to reduce adhesion and ease layer separation
- **Isopropanol alcohol (IPA)** — used to wash uncured resin off the finished print and skin

## Techniques and Methods
- Applying PTFE spray to the FEP sheet via a cloth (not spraying directly into the reservoir)
- Pre-coating the build plate with resin to fill surface micro-valleys and prevent air entrapment
- Slicing STL files to CTB format in ChiTuBox, with the correct printer profile selected
- IPA wash to remove uncured resin from the finished print
- Manual support removal while the part is still slightly flexible (tacky after IPA wash)
- UV curing (sunlight or UV station) to fully harden the post-washed print
