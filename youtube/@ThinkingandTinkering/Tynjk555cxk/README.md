## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores an unconventional variation of the Tesla turbine using stacked hyperbolic funnels (the same shape found in arcade wishing wells) instead of traditional flat discs. A rough 3D-printed prototype spun successfully under nothing more than a hair dryer, demonstrating that the hyperbolic funnel geometry naturally encourages the spiral fluid flow a Tesla turbine requires — making it more tolerant of imprecise construction and low-pressure sources. He redesigned the funnel with a built-in 5mm spacer and released the Tinkercad/STL file publicly for others to experiment with.
