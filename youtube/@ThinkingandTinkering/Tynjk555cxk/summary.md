## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores an unconventional variation of the Tesla turbine using stacked hyperbolic funnels (the same shape found in arcade wishing wells) instead of traditional flat discs. A rough 3D-printed prototype spun successfully under nothing more than a hair dryer, demonstrating that the hyperbolic funnel geometry naturally encourages the spiral fluid flow a Tesla turbine requires — making it more tolerant of imprecise construction and low-pressure sources. He redesigned the funnel with a built-in 5mm spacer and released the Tinkercad/STL file publicly for others to experiment with.

## Key Topics
- Limitations of the traditional flat-disc Tesla turbine (angle sensitivity, high-pressure dependency)
- Prior work on a nested-cone Tesla turbine variant (video 1888)
- Why hyperbolic funnels are geometrically suited to Tesla turbine operation
- Connection to the ongoing Erasmus Darwin wind turbine project
- 3D-printed prototype design with integral spacers (5mm gap between funnels)
- Public release of Tinkercad file for community experimentation
- Tesla's historical mention of spiral grooves on turbine discs

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **3D-printed PLA/filament** — used for the hyperbolic funnel turbine prototype (fast/rough print)
- **CDs** — mentioned as a common DIY flat-disc Tesla turbine material (not used here)
- **Super glue / electrician's tape** — mentioned humorously for finger injuries

## Techniques and Methods
- **3D printing (FDM, fast/rough print)** — rapid prototyping of hyperbolic funnel discs
- **Stacked nested-funnel assembly** — funnels printed individually with integral 5mm spacers, then stacked on a shaft
- **Bearing-mounted axle rig** — 8mm axle through a simple angle bracket with two bearings for low-friction testing
- **Hair dryer airflow test** — low-pressure qualitative spin test to validate the concept
- **Tinkercad CAD design** — geometry designed and shared as a public STL file

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Housing is needed:** Robert acknowledges the turbine hanging in open air won't perform well and confirms he is working on a casing/case for it.
- **Thingiverse upload:** Plans to upload the file to Thingiverse and will do a dedicated video when the transition is complete.
- **This is a side project:** Explicitly clarifies this turbine work is a complete aside while waiting for Darwin wind turbine prints — not a deviation from the main project.
- **Making funnels by hand:** Confirms he has made a separate video on how to fabricate the hyperbolic funnels by hand (not just 3D printed), and also references a video on making them from old pillowcases and car body repair kit.
- **Scaling up:** Suggests two routes for more power — make the funnels bigger, or make more of them and join them together.
- **Fluid drag force:** Clarifies for a commenter that the working principle (fluid effect on the disc surface) is friction/drag force.
- **Spiral grooves:** Does not dismiss the idea; encourages community members to try adding spiral grooves as Tesla described.
- **Patent note:** Mentions awareness of a relevant patent application with a priority claim from US54646204P.
