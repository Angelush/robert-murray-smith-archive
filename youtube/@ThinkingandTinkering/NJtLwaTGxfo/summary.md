## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explains his approach to the review/sponsorship products he receives, and what happens to them once he's done. After a costly incident where a competition prize went missing en route to Australia (costing him £400 compensation), he stopped running giveaway competitions. He now simply gives reviewed equipment away — primarily to local schools, colleges, and his local church — and films this video from the churchyard where his wife Patti is buried.

## Key Topics
- His criteria for accepting product review offers (must provide genuine value to viewers)
- His dislike of mid-roll (embedded) advertising in YouTube videos
- Why he stopped running viewer competitions (£400 loss from a lost prize sent to Australia)
- His current policy: giving equipment away to schools, colleges, and local institutions
- The volume of sponsorship requests he receives (~300–400 emails per day)
- His 3D printer and laser cutter review rotation process
- Plans to donate future equipment to his local church to fund disabled access improvements
- The personal significance of the filming location (his wife Patti's resting place)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Filament (for FDM/filament 3D printers, mentioned in context of his printer collection)

## Techniques and Methods
- FDM (filament) 3D printing — reviewing and rotating machines
- Laser cutting/engraving — reviewing machines and donating to colleges
- Equipment rotation: use until a replacement arrives, then donate the older unit

## Robert's Own Replies
Several comments reveal meaningful additional context:

- **Refuses payment outright:** "Oh I get offered payment mate - I just refuse it - if i take payment I would feel like i was bought." He draws a clear line between receiving products for review and accepting cash.

- **Maker spaces:** He noted he has donated to maker spaces before, but they are not widespread in the UK. He called them "the best places."
- **His wife:** He mentioned she was a teacher, and that he is "a great supporter of teaching and education." One reply also indicates he gave up an unspecified activity when she passed: "I couldn't see any point in it anymore."
- **On the £400 incident:** "it stung if i am honest" — but treated it as a learning experience.
