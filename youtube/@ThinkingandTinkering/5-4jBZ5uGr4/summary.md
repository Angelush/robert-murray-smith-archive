## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explains how the Ratio Zero CVT (continuously variable transmission), patented in 2020 by Edoardo Pavilles, works as a gear-only CVT — avoiding the efficiency losses of friction-based designs. He walks through its operating principle using a 3D-printed model, demonstrating how a planetary gear system combined with a lever mechanism can vary the gear ratio continuously from 0 to 600, while also functioning as a mechanical clutch.

## Key Topics
- Why CVTs matter: keeping engines in their optimal RPM band
- Limitations of friction-based CVTs (~70–75% efficiency) vs. gear-based gearboxes (95–97%)
- Overview of the Ratio Zero CVT patent (2020, Edoardo Pavilles)
- How a planetary gear system forms the basis of the design
- The lever mechanism: moving a pivot point to change gear ratio
- Use of rack-and-pinion instead of the patent's four-bar linkage
- How the selector ring allows lever adjustment while the unit is spinning
- The ratchet-like output using small planet gears
- Gear ratio range of 0–600, including acting as a mechanical clutch
- Files to be posted on Thingiverse for anyone to print and build

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- None mentioned. (The model is 3D-printed from plastic; no specific filament or material is named.)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing (print-in-place herringbone planetary gears)
- Rack-and-pinion mechanism to convert vertical motion to radial pin movement
- Planetary gear system adapted with slotted planets for lever insertion
- Selector ring / selector fork assembly for adjusting lever position during rotation
- Fluorescent dot marking to visually demonstrate gear ratio change

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Torque transfer mechanism:** Clarifies that torque is transferred by the "pull" of the lever, not by the spinning of the gears themselves — addressing questions about one-way clutches.
- **One-way clutches not required:** States that neither the patent nor demo models require one-way clutches; the system works bidirectionally and does not need them for operation.
- **Planetary gear input flexibility:** Notes that in a planetary gear system, either the ring or sun can serve as input, as the planet carrier is stationary relative to the system's frame of reference.
- **Efficiency figures:** Directs viewers to search "Ratio Zero" online for published efficiency figures; notes a production version exists and efficiency loss is reportedly less than expected.
- **Original design intent:** Believes the original Ratio Zero was designed for a bicycle application.
- **Building from blueprints:** Encourages a commenter to build a physical model rather than just working from drawings, noting that hands-on construction reveals design improvements that blueprints cannot.
- **Improvements to his model:** Acknowledges he has learned things building this model and would make changes to the 3D model (not the underlying mechanism).
- **Automatic ratio control:** Suggests a centrifugal governor could be used to automate ratio adjustment.
- **Related videos:** Points viewers to video 2310 (perpetual wedge) for high gear reductions, and mentions a prior e-CVT build from ~2 years ago.
