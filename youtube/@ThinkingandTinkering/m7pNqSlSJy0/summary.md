## Overview
This video demonstrates how to connect a resistive-paint heating panel (from a previous video) to an AC mains supply rather than DC. Robert walks through the components needed for a safe, temperature-controlled installation, then shows a simple test rig using a dimmer switch and a light bulb as a current limiter, achieving ~48°C at ~40W within seconds of powering up.

## Key Topics
- Following up on a previous video about resistive-paint panels on DC supply
- Safety considerations when working with AC mains (RCDs/residual current devices)
- Components required for a proper installation: temperature controller, K-type thermocouple, silicon controlled rectifier (SCR)
- Analogy to commercially available underfloor heating mat kits
- Test rig using a light dimmer switch for manual voltage control
- Using a 60W incandescent bulb as a combined current limiter and fuse
- Live demonstration of the panel heating up on AC

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Resistive paint / conductive paint panel (from prior video)
- K-type thermocouple
- Silicon controlled rectifier (SCR / thyristor)
- 60W incandescent light bulb (used as current limiter)
- Underfloor heating mats (commercial equivalent, referenced for comparison)

## Techniques and Methods
- Connecting a resistive-paint panel to AC mains via a light dimmer switch for variable voltage control
- Using a light bulb in series as a passive current limiter and overcurrent protection device
- Monitoring output voltage with a voltmeter during live testing
- Gradual power increase (dimmer turned up slowly) to avoid sparking or sudden current surges
- Temperature controller + thermocouple + SCR circuit for a proper closed-loop installation
- Referencing underfloor heating mat wiring guides as a practical how-to resource
