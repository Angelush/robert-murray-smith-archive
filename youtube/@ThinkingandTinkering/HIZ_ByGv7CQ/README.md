## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the physics of candle-based plant pot heaters, calculates that a single candle outputs approximately 74 watts of heat, and identifies the core danger of traditional designs: terracotta plant pots crack under thermal stress. He then builds an improved, safer version using a stainless steel toilet brush holder on a broad-based metal frame with a homemade paraffin candle, demonstrating that the concept works well in small spaces when constructed from better materials.
