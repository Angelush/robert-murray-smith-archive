## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith attempts to recreate the ABENICS cross spherical gear — a mechanism developed by Yamagata University that mimics human joint motion with a wide range of articulation. Using Tinkercad, he designs and 3D prints a three-part spherical gear assembly along with a custom dipole drive gear (as a substitute for the university's monopole design he could not replicate), ultimately assembling a working demonstration unit.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the ABENICS (cross spherical gear) mechanism from Yamagata University
- Step-by-step CAD recreation in Tinkercad using rotated metric gears
- Construction of the spherical gear body by rotating a flat gear profile in two axes
- Design of a dipole drive gear as a workaround for the unachievable monopole gear
- Assembly of the full mechanism including cradle, foot, arms, and bearings
- Demonstration of the finished working model

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Skate bearings — 22 mm × 7 mm × 8 mm, pressed into the dipole gear arms
- Nylon — mentioned in author comments as a possible print material
- Glue — used to permanently join printed components

## Techniques and Methods
- CAD design in Tinkercad using the built-in metric gear shape primitive
- Rotational duplication technique: copying a gear profile and rotating it 1° per step, repeated 360 times to form a spherical gear
- Boolean subtraction (hole/merge) to cut the sphere in half and create the cylindrical gear profile
- Cookie-cutter method: using a cylinder as a hole to hollow the spherical gear
- 3D printing all components
- Press-fit bearing insertion
- Gluing sub-assemblies together with careful prong alignment
