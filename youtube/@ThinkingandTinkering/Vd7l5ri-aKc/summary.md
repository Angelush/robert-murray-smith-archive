## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith visits Reclamet, described as the largest scrapyard in Kent and one of the largest in Europe, to source parts for upcoming projects including a steam engine build and a generator conversion. The video is a short tour/vlog showcasing the value of scrapyards as a resource for DIY inventors and tinkerers, highlighting specific finds like alternators and a catalytic converter that will feed into future lab projects.

## Key Topics
- Tour of Reclamet scrapyard in Kent, UK
- Sourcing cheap parts for ongoing DIY/science projects
- Converting car alternators into generators (following on from a previous fan-to-generator project)
- Repurposing a catalytic converter to build a flameless heater
- General advocacy for scrapyards as a resource for makers and inventors

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Catalytic converter** — obtained free from Stuart at Reclamet; catalyst material inside to be extracted for making a flameless heater
- **Alternators** — picked up by weight (~£10–20 each); to be converted into generators using the same principle as a previous fan-based alternator project
- **Air compressor unit** — previously purchased for £100 to be repurposed as a steam engine motor (noting a cheaper equivalent was spotted at the yard)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Alternator-to-generator conversion** — repurposing salvaged car alternators using a principle demonstrated in a prior fan-to-generator video
- **Catalytic converter disassembly** — sawing open the unit to extract the internal catalyst substrate for use in a flameless heater build
- **Scrapyard sourcing by weight** — buying bulk components (e.g., alternators) priced by weight rather than as individual parts
