## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains how his "wind wall" device — a thick plank of wood with drilled holes — functions as a cheap alternative to expensive Venturi shrouds for wind turbines. By applying the industrial concept of the orifice plate, he demonstrates that a simple drilled board creates multiple ducted wind turbine effects simultaneously, boosting efficiency at a fraction of the cost of traditional shroud designs. The core takeaway is that a small loss in theoretical efficiency is acceptable if it dramatically reduces manufacturing cost and thus lowers the levelized cost of energy.
