## Overview
This video, presented by Luke on what appears to be Robert Murray-Smith's channel, demonstrates how to create a realistic wood-grain painting effect on surfaces. Using layered paint and wood stain varnish combined with texturing tools, the technique produces a convincing burr walnut appearance. The method was originally used on a steampunk-style PC case build (video 973) and is shown here in a dedicated tutorial.

## Key Topics
- Replicating a wood-grain ("graining") effect on flat surfaces
- Building up paint layers to create depth and texture
- Applying wood stain varnish over a painted base to simulate natural wood figuring
- Using improvised tools to add knots and grain lines

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Jude Luxe Vanilla Sundae** — base coat paint, used as the light-coloured foundation layer
- **Rich mahogany wood stain varnish** — applied over the dried paint layers to simulate wood grain colouring
- **Clear acrylic sheet** — used as a sample/test piece (allows viewing of layers from the reverse)

## Techniques and Methods
- Applying multiple coats of base paint, allowing each to dry fully before adding the next
- Texturing wet paint with a **stainless steel scourer** to create grain lines and knots
- Rolling an **M8 threaded bolt** over wet paint to produce evenly spaced grain lines
- Applying wood stain varnish sparingly with a dry brush using irregular wrist movements to mimic natural wood figuring
- Building up 2–3 layers of wood stain varnish, darkening progressively, to add depth and make underlying texture "pop"
- Using masking tape to protect edges during painting
