## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how a 12V car winch (rated for 2-ton pulls) can be repurposed as an electrical generator, since DC motors and generators are functionally identical devices. He disassembles the winch to examine its planetary gearbox, then reverses the gearbox orientation to turn the previously driven motor shaft into the output drive shaft. The experiment produces a small but measurable voltage and current (~0.7V, ~50mA), with the main limitation being an impractical gear ratio inherited from the winch's original purpose.

## Key Topics
- DC motors as reversible generators
- Disassembly and analysis of a 12V car winch (Sealey brand, ~£60)
- Planetary (epicyclic) gearbox theory: sun, planet, and ring gears
- Gear ratio calculation for the planetary arrangement (11:1)
- Reversing the gearbox to convert the winch from motor to generator
- Bearing modification using pipe offcuts from an angle grinder head
- Practical output measurement (voltage and current)
- Gear ratio tradeoffs for real-world use (water wheel, wind turbine)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly of a commercial winch to expose the gearbox
- Planetary gear tooth counting to determine gear ratio (sun: 6 teeth, ring: 66 teeth, planets: 72 teeth; ratio = 66/6 = 11:1)
- Gearbox reversal: drilling a hole through the gearbox end cap to allow the drive shaft to pass through in the opposite direction
- Improvised bearing housing fabricated from nested pipe sections taken from a broken angle grinder
- Bolting modified bearing housing with machine screws into original mounting holes
- Electrical output testing with a voltmeter (voltage mode, then current/milliamp mode)
