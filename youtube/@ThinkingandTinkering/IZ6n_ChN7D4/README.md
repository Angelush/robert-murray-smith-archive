## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a kinetic water turbine experiment using pressurized water fed through a 1.5mm nozzle onto a PC fan impeller blade. Building on his previous video (1487) which tested volume-based flow, this experiment achieves approximately 400 milliwatts (~half a watt) from just one liter of water every two minutes — his best result to date, outperforming Pelton and Turgo turbine designs. He concludes by weighing the trade-offs between pressure-based (kinetic) and gravity-fed (potential energy) approaches.
