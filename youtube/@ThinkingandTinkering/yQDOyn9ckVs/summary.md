## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents the official rules for a competition tied to his channel, where the challenge is to create something that can light an LED using any method the entrant chooses. The rules are intentionally minimal and broad, with a multi-stage format: submit a video, Robert creates a shortlist, then a public vote determines the winner. He also offers informal guidance — beyond the rules — on what makes a strong entry and a winning one.

## Key Topics
- Official competition rules: create something that lights an LED (by any means)
- Entry format: submit a video of your creation
- Shortlisting process: Robert curates a shortlist at his sole discretion
- Public voting: most likes / fewest dislikes wins the 3D printer
- Tiebreaker: Robert makes the final call if voting is a draw
- Eligibility: open to individuals or groups, any location, multiple entries allowed
- Guidance (non-binding) on what Robert looks for when shortlisting
- Guidance on video length and format to appeal to public voters
- Competition timeline: entries close 31 October, videos go up shortly after, voting runs for 14 days

## Techniques and Methods
- Chemical, mechanical, solar, and thermal energy sources all cited as valid approaches for powering the LED
- Building your own battery highlighted as an example of the kind of creativity Robert values
- Explicitly discouraged: purchasing a pre-made generator or air battery and simply attaching an LED
