## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith critically examines the AISEG (AI Smart Energy Generator), a device unveiled at COP 28 by the Korean company Semm Group, which claims to generate electricity at 1,769% efficiency with no fuel. He traces its conceptual origins to Tom Bearden's Motionless Electrical Generator (MEG) from 2001 and concludes it is most likely a lossy transformer exploiting back-EMF, dressed up in marketing buzzwords. His core takeaway is that the device's demonstration test contains a fundamental measurement error — applying a power correction factor on the DC input side but not on the AC output side — which can produce apparent output readings that are off by a factor of 100 to 1,000.
