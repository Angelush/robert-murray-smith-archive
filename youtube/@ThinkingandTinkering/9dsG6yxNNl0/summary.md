## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith walks through how to approach choosing free CAD software, emphasising that most advice is personal rather than universal. He frames the decision around skill level, project requirements, and personal goals, offering a tiered set of recommendations from beginner to advanced. His main takeaway is to start simply, build confidence, and progress naturally rather than forcing yourself through an unnecessarily steep learning curve.

## Key Topics
- Why people's CAD advice reflects their own experience, not yours
- Questions to ask yourself before choosing: why do you want CAD, what are your goals?
- Skill-level tiers: beginner, intermediate, and advanced
- Five key selection criteria: functionality, ease of use, project requirements, community support, and scalability
- Specific software recommendations per skill level
- Robert's own CAD journey: starting with TinkerCAD, considering a move to OnShape
