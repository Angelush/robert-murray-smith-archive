## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explains the legal landscape surrounding home heating devices in the UK, focusing on the Ecodesign 2022 regulations and building regulations and how they affect DIY heater projects. He clarifies the key distinctions — between manufacturing vs. making, and installed vs. portable — that determine whether a homemade heater is legal to use. His main takeaway is that a portable, self-made heater burning compliant fuel is entirely lawful, while permanently installed or non-compliant devices are not.

## Key Topics
- What Ecodesign 2022 is and how it became UK law on 1 January 2022
- How Ecodesign 2022 covers both appliances and the fuels burned in them
- The legal distinction between "manufacturing" (intent to sell) and "making" for personal use
- Building regulations and when they apply (installed/permanent vs. portable devices)
- Compliant vs. non-compliant fuels (e.g., seasoned wood, bioethanol vs. waste motor oil, wet wood, coal)
- Why rocket mass heaters are particularly problematic under UK law
- Regional variation in regulations (UK, Europe, US, Canada)
- Practical safety common sense for indoor portable heater use

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Seasoned/dry wood** — compliant fuel if moisture content is below 20%
- **Bioethanol / ethanol / methanol** — listed as Ecodesign-compliant fuels
- **Waste motor oil / used cooking oil** — explicitly non-compliant and illegal to burn since January 2022
- **Coal** — outlawed in the UK in 2022
- **Smokeless solid fuels** (sold at garages) — compliant replacements for coal
- **Paraffin** — mentioned in context of portable paraffin heaters (compliant/portable)
- **Calor Gas (LPG)** — mentioned as an example of a portable, regulation-exempt heater fuel

## Techniques and Methods
- Building a rocket stove for outdoor cooking or portable indoor use
- Using portable heaters (on wheels/with handles) to avoid building regulation requirements
- Drying wood to below 20% moisture content to achieve fuel compliance
- Placing heaters on a non-combustible base (stone or tile) as a safety measure
- Ensuring adequate ventilation when using combustion heaters indoors
