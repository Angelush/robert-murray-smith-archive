## Overview
This video is a progress review of Robert's ongoing "Solar Cell From A Leaf" (or "Lisening") project, summarising experiments to date and explaining where the work is heading. After three weeks of development he has improved output from microwatts to 1.5 watts per square metre, and argues that despite being 20–100× less efficient than commercial silicon cells, the dramatically lower cost already makes it competitive on a cost-per-kilowatt basis.

## Key Topics
- Progress recap: going from microwatts to 1.5 W/m² in three weeks
- Performance comparison against commercial solar cells (30–150 W/m²)
- Cost advantage of a "paint-on" solar cell (~£100 vs £4,000–8,000 for a standard rooftop install)
- Payback period: estimated months rather than the typical 10 years for commercial panels
- Current device structure and planned improvements
- Next development steps and need for product testing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Carbon** — electrode layer in the cell stack
- **Electrolyte** — soaked into fiberglass substrate to keep it in place
- **Fiberglass** — substrate/separator material
- **Plastic sheet** — top cover to protect the cell
- **Polyvinyl alcohol (PVA)** — proposed as a gel matrix for the electrolyte to improve stability
- **Copper and aluminium** — asymmetric electrode pair currently in use (noted to introduce a small faradaic reaction alongside the solar effect)
- **Gold and platinum** — mentioned as preferred electrodes that would eliminate the faradaic contribution

## Techniques and Methods
- Fabrication of a layered thin-film cell (carbon / electrolyte-soaked fiberglass / plastic cover)
- Testing under both fluorescent indoor light and direct sunlight (3× performance improvement outdoors)
- Asymmetric electrode configuration (copper + aluminium) to exploit both solar and minor electrochemical effects
- Proposed gel electrolyte formation using polyvinyl alcohol
- Performance measurement in watts per square metre
- Cost-per-kilowatt comparison against commercial photovoltaic systems

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Electrolyte suggestions:** Recommends trying a **natural deep eutectic solvent** (mostly sugars and urea) as an alternative electrolyte; also suggests tissue paper as a separator material and spectroscopic analysis for characterisation.
- **Electrode clarification:** Confirms he uses **asymmetric copper and aluminium electrodes**; acknowledges a small faradaic reaction is present but says it is overwhelmed by the photovoltaic effect. Gold and platinum would be cleaner but the copper/aluminium contribution is "pretty insignificant."
- **Cost context:** Was told the figure is **$1 per watt** for commercial cells but maintains the leaf cell approach is still compelling regardless.
- **Project continuity:** Notes he is heading to Australia for a couple of months and is shifting focus to the **SWAG** project, encouraging community members to pick up the leaf-cell work independently.
- **Motivation:** States his primary goal is to **inspire others** and that he publishes detailed descriptions specifically so people can replicate the experiments themselves.
