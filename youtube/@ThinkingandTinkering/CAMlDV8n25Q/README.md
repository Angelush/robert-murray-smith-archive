## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith presents what he believes may be a newly invented type of Stirling engine, built from extremely simple and cheap materials — a tin can, wire wool, a balloon, and small magnets. The engine uses a balloon diaphragm coupled to a wire-wool displacer via a magnet rather than mechanical linkages, making it remarkably simple to construct. He invites the Stirling engine community, particularly expert "Blade Attila," to verify whether it is truly novel.
