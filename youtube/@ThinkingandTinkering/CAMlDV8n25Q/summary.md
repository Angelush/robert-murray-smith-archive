## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents what he believes may be a newly invented type of Stirling engine, built from extremely simple and cheap materials — a tin can, wire wool, a balloon, and small magnets. The engine uses a balloon diaphragm coupled to a wire-wool displacer via a magnet rather than mechanical linkages, making it remarkably simple to construct. He invites the Stirling engine community, particularly expert "Blade Attila," to verify whether it is truly novel.

## Key Topics
- Possible invention of a new Stirling engine configuration
- How the engine operates (diaphragm + displacer + magnetic coupling)
- Component breakdown and construction details
- Distinction from thermoacoustic resonance engines (e.g., Blade Attila's designs)
- Open-source ethos — Robert gave the design away freely

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Balloon** — used as the diaphragm (expandable membrane)
- **Wire wool** — wound around templates to form the displacer
- **Tin can** — forms the engine body
- **MDF (Medium-Density Fibreboard)** — used as a retaining ring inside the engine
- **Small magnets (N52 neodymium implied)** — couple the balloon/bolt to the displacer; Curie point noted as ~400°C
- **Copper wire** — used to hold the displacer assembly together
- **Rubber band / button of rubber** — part of the top assembly
- **Super glue** — mentioned in comments as the adhesive used

## Techniques and Methods
- **Magnetic coupling** — a small magnet on a bolt through the balloon picks up and releases the displacer without any mechanical linkage
- **Diaphragm actuation** — balloon expands/contracts with heating/cooling to drive displacer motion
- **Gravity-return displacer** — displacer falls back by gravity when the magnet releases it at the retaining ring
- **Flywheel ballast** — metal weights on top to smooth rotation

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Not a valve, not steam:** What appears to be steam in the demo was actually part of his glove burning on the hotplate — there is no valve in the design.
- **Not thermoacoustic resonance:** He firmly clarified this multiple times. Blade Attila's engine exploits thermoacoustic resonance; this engine does not operate on that principle.
- **Different from NASA Stirling:** He previously built and documented a NASA-style Stirling (which used gas-damped springs); this design is fundamentally different.
- **Balloon is just convenient:** Any diaphragm would work — the balloon is simply the easiest material to use.
- **Curie point not a concern:** For N52 magnets, the Curie point is ~400°C, well above operating temperatures.
- **Freely given away:** "Ideas belong to everyone mate" — he made no attempt to patent or commercialise it.
- **Only one diaphragm:** The displacer is a solid bundle of wire wool, not a second membrane.
