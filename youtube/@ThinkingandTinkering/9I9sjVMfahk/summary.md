## Overview
This short video is a follow-up correction to Robert's previous wind energy harvester video, where he mistakenly said "farads" instead of "microfarads" for the capacitors used. After correcting the error, he works through the power calculations and discovers that the collected power (~0.013 W) closely matches the theoretically available wind power at those low wind speeds — suggesting the device operates at remarkably high efficiency for a low-wind energy harvester.

## Key Topics
- Correction of a capacitor unit error from the previous video (farads vs. microfarads)
- Power output calculation: ~0.013 W from the harvester when fully populated
- Available wind power calculation using an online VAWT calculator (Omni Calculator)
- Near-100% apparent efficiency comparison between harvested and available power
- Comparison with a conventional rotor-blade wind turbine, which yields zero output at the same low wind speeds
- The device's purpose as a low-wind energy harvester and its effectiveness in that niche
