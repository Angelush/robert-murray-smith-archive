## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith gives a first-look review of the Elegoo Neptune 3 Pro FDM (filament) 3D printer, sent to him by Elegoo after he had a positive experience with their resin printer. Having previously dismissed filament printing as frustrating and unreliable, he finds this machine impresses him from the first print, praising its build quality, auto-leveling bed, and ease of assembly.

## Key Topics
- Robert's history of skepticism toward FDM/filament 3D printing and prior bad experiences
- His pivot after a successful experience with an Elegoo resin printer
- Unboxing and assembly of the Elegoo Neptune 3 Pro (~20 minutes)
- Notable hardware features: auto-leveling print bed, magnetic flexible bed, sturdy frame construction
- Software setup using a Cura-based slicer bundled on a USB drive
- File transfer options: Wi-Fi, USB, and SD/print card
- Touch-screen control interface
- First print: Tom Stanton's air engine (printed from Thingiverse), a 6-hour job
- Quiet operation compared to earlier FDM machines

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- FDM filament (plastic, unspecified type) — used as print material
- Resin — referenced from prior Elegoo resin printer experience

## Techniques and Methods
- FDM (Fused Deposition Modelling) / filament 3D printing
- Auto-leveling / auto-sensing print bed calibration
- G-code generation via Cura-based slicer (loading STL files, setting printer/material profiles)
- Flexible magnetic print bed for print removal (bend to release prints)
- File transfer to printer via Wi-Fi, USB, or SD card

## Robert's Own Replies
- He mentions **Darwin turbine blades** as a planned use for the printer — connecting it to his wider energy and water accumulator project interests.
- Confirms the **magnetic print bed** is a standout feature: *"the mag print bed is just plain awesome."*
- Notes the Neptune 3 Pro (the updated version) was **due out around Halloween** at time of posting, clarifying this video covers an earlier unit.
