## Overview
Luke restores a vintage tape measure that belonged to his late grandfather, a jack-of-all-trades who worked as a plumber, DJ, and builder. The video walks through full disassembly, cleaning with several solutions suited to different components, and reassembly. The result is a nearly fully restored tool that Luke intends to keep and use in memory of his grandfather.

## Key Topics
- Personal sentimental backstory behind the restoration
- Disassembly of a single-screw vintage tape measure
- Cleaning strategy differentiated by component sensitivity
- Use of a "miracle cleaning solution" (cross-referenced from video 1037) for rust removal
- Careful reassembly of small fiddly components (guide roller, steel band)
- Acceptance of remaining cosmetic wear (powder coat chips) as character

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **WD-40** — used to loosen a seized internal component before disassembly
- **IPA (isopropyl alcohol) wipes** — initial wipe-down to remove bulk dirt and grease
- **"Miracle cleaning solution"** (from video 1037) — applied to remove rust from the metal rim and outer components
- **Very mild cleaning solution** — overnight soak for the textured outer case
- **Diluted IPA/water solution (~30%)** — gentle cleaning of the tape itself to avoid erasing the printed markings

## Techniques and Methods
- Single-screw disassembly of a mechanical tape measure
- Soak-and-wait technique with WD-40 to free seized parts
- Staged cleaning: coarse IPA wipe first, then targeted rust-removal solution
- Overnight soak for deeply textured surfaces
- Diluted solvent cleaning to preserve delicate printed graduations
- Manual reassembly with sequential placement of guide roller and steel retention band
