## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith traces the history of continuously variable transmissions (CVTs) from simple animal-powered linkages to modern magnetic designs, explaining how they work and why they matter. He highlights their application in wind turbines, where variable wind input can be converted to a constant RPM output for reliable power generation. The video concludes with a hands-on demonstration of a 3D-printed Evans-style cone CVT prototype, improved with a golf ball as a freely-rotating contact point.
