## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a gasifier/rocket stove in action, showing the dramatic flames produced by the gasification process. The video highlights how various stove types — gasifiers, pyrolysis units, and rocket stoves — all operate on the same underlying principles of converting solid biomass into combustible syngas.

## Key Topics
- Gasification of timber as a combustion method
- Comparison of gasifier, pyrolysis unit, and rocket stove designs
- Syngas (synthesis gas) production and combustion
- The shared operating principles across these stove types

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Timber (as the solid fuel feedstock)
- Syngas (the combustible gas produced via gasification/pyrolysis)

## Techniques and Methods
- Gasification — thermochemical conversion of solid biomass into syngas
- Pyrolysis — thermal decomposition of organic material in the absence of oxygen
- Rocket stove combustion geometry (implied as a related design principle)
