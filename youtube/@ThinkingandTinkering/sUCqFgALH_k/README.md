## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates the conditions required to trigger thermal runaway in lithium-ion batteries, using thermite as an analogy for a heat-initiated chain reaction. After repeated attempts — nail puncture on both discharged and charged cells — he finds that significant abuse (including a blowtorch) is required to get any reaction, concluding that Li-ion batteries are far more robust than commonly feared.
