## Overview
Building on his earlier rainwater turbine project (video 1886), Robert explores the fundamental difference between mass-based and momentum-based water turbines. He compares a Stirling-style turbine against a Kaplan turbine blade on the same generator, demonstrating that matching turbine type to water flow characteristics (high mass/low head vs. low mass/high momentum) produces meaningfully better electrical output. The key takeaway is that there is no universal turbine — the right choice depends entirely on your specific water supply conditions.

## Key Topics
- Recap of the previous downpipe rainwater generator project (video 1886)
- The physics principle: small mass moving fast vs. large mass moving slowly can yield equivalent energy
- Impulse turbines vs. reaction turbines
- The Kaplan turbine as the appropriate choice for high-mass, low-head water flow
- The Pelton wheel as appropriate for low-mass, high-momentum (high-head) flow
- Vortex turbines as a hybrid approach
- Using a bell siphon in gutters to concentrate water mass
- Using output voltage as a proxy for turbine rotational speed

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned

## Techniques and Methods
- Swapping turbine blade types (Stirling-style vs. Kaplan) on the same generator body to isolate the variable
- Using open-circuit voltage as a speed measurement (exploiting the known relationship between generator voltage and RPM, holding wire length, magnetic field strength, and cut angle constant)
- Bell siphon integration into guttering to create a concentrated water release
