## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates that his graphene-based conductive ink ("our ink") can be painted onto paper in a spiral pattern to create a functional coreless transformer. By attaching the painted coil to a joule thief circuit generating an ~80–100 kHz AC signal, he shows that AC voltage is transferred inductively between two isolated painted spirals, while DC voltage does not pass through — confirming true transformer action rather than simple leakage.

## Key Topics
- Graphene conductive ink as a medium for passive electronic components
- Painting a spiral inductor/transformer coil directly onto paper
- Demonstrating inductive coupling between two isolated painted spirals
- Using a joule thief circuit to generate AC input for testing
- Potential applications: wireless charging, painted radios, printed circuits
- Comparison of brush painting versus silk screen printing for the ink

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene-based conductive ink ("our ink" / "A8" ink)** — the carbon-based ink used to paint the coils; resistance approximately 0.8 ohm per square
- **Paper** — substrate onto which the spirals are painted

## Techniques and Methods
- **Brush painting** — hand-painting spiral coil patterns onto paper to form inductors
- **Silk screen printing** — mentioned as a viable method for finer-line, higher-turn-count coils
- **Joule thief circuit** — used to convert DC input into ~80–100 kHz AC signal for testing
- **AC/DC voltage measurement with oscilloscope probes** — used to confirm inductive transfer and rule out resistive leakage
- **One-to-one transformer configuration** — tested as a baseline proof of concept

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Capacitive coupling concern:** Robert acknowledges a valid point that some capacitance is always present in a coil, but considers it unlikely to explain the observed results; agrees it's worth investigating further.
- **Brush vs. printer:** Prefers brush painting for its accessibility and adaptability; acknowledges silk screen printing as a good alternative but notes the ink would clog inkjet/laser jets.
- **Sheet resistance:** Confirms the ink is approximately **0.8 ohm per square** — characterising it as a carbon ink, not a miracle material.
- **Silk screen suggestion:** Endorses the idea of using a printable screen stencil material for finer printed coils.
- **General enthusiasm:** Expresses genuine enjoyment exploring the ink's capabilities and welcomes suggestions for future projects.
