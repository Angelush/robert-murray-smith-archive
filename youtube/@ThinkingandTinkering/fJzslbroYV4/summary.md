## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build the frame of an electric trike using rolled-up newspaper tubes as the primary structural material. He walks through constructing a wooden jig based on previously published plans, assembling the frame pieces with wood glue, and wrapping the joints with carbon fiber or fiberglass to provide structural strength. The video is part of a larger series on building a functional electric trike entirely from paper-based materials.

## Key Topics
- Using rolled newspaper tubes as structural construction material
- Building a jig from scrap timber to ensure accurate frame geometry
- Frame dimensions and angles (20°, 30°, 55° joints; key measurements in centimeters)
- Gluing technique and why glue alone is insufficient for joint strength
- Wrapping joints with carbon fiber (or fiberglass/string) for real structural integrity
- Adding trusses to reinforce the seat section
- Spacing the two frame sides with 10 cm projection spacers at each node
- Finishing with sanding and varnish

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Rolled newspaper** — primary structural tube material
- **Cotton** — inner lining layer inside the newspaper tubes
- **Craft paper / strong brown wrapping paper** — outer layer on the newspaper tubes
- **Gorilla Glue (wood glue)** — used to hold joints in place during assembly; noted for staining skin
- **Carbon fiber** — used to wrap and reinforce joints (Robert's personal choice)
- **Fiberglass** — suggested as an alternative wrapping material
- **String** — mentioned as a lower-cost alternative wrapping material
- **Varnish** — applied over carbon fiber wrapping for finish and to bring out color
- **Plastic sheet offcuts** — used under joints to prevent glue bonding to the jig table

## Techniques and Methods
- Rolling newspaper into structural tubes (referencing video 1165 for full method)
- Building a dimensioned wooden jig from scrap timber to maintain correct angles and spacing
- Registering tube positions in the jig using screws as levers/stops
- Gluing joints with wood glue as a temporary hold for handling
- Wrapping joints with carbon fiber/fiberglass/string to achieve load-bearing strength
- Adding diagonal trusses to reinforce the seat section against bending
- Inserting 10 cm spacer projections at each node to set frame width
- Sanding and varnishing the finished frame
