## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith gives his final review of the ADO A20 folding electric bike after using it for over a month in real-world daily commuting conditions. He evaluates it from the perspective of an older, heavier rider with hip arthritis rather than as a performance cyclist, concluding it's an excellent value for money as a practical transport aid.

## Key Topics
- Overall verdict on the ADO A20 after extended real-world use
- Affordability (~£650, considered budget-friendly for an e-bike)
- Foldability and portability as a key practical feature
- Daily commuting use (8-mile each-way commute, ~16 miles/day)
- Battery life and charging frequency
- Step-through low frame design as an accessibility benefit
- The one notable irritation: the fold does not clip/lock securely
- Comparison to e-bikes that become unused "toys" vs. genuinely useful tools

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirmed the battery range is **around 40 miles**, noting a battery project is still ongoing.
- Defended the ~£650 price point against sceptics, saying it's inexpensive for an e-bike and the fold-clip issue was "not much of an irritation — I soon solved the issue."
- Responded humorously to someone suggesting he carry it in a bag, musing whether there's a market for a "bike in a suitcase."
- When asked about the brand, he admitted uncertainty: *"I don't really know — I wonder if they are just badged?"* (suggesting it may be a rebranded generic design).
- Defended channel monetisation against a commenter questioning ads, expressing genuine frustration at the attitude.
- Acknowledged his target audience with self-deprecating humour: *"When I was researching this I thought none of those guys looked like me."*
