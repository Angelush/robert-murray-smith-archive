## Overview
This video concludes the multi-part project of electrifying a homemade scooter (begun in video 1106). Presenter Luke fabricates and installs a steel battery carrier, corrects the motor drive direction, adds a derailleur for chain tension, and completes a successful outdoor test ride. The final build achieves an estimated top speed of 10–15 mph using a 42:12 tooth gear ratio and nickel metal hydride batteries.

## Key Topics
- Fabricating a custom steel battery carrier from bent 8mm bar stock
- Welding and bolting the carrier to the scooter frame
- Correcting the motor drive direction (wheel was spinning backwards in the previous video)
- Installing a bicycle derailleur to replace the original chain tensioner
- Switching battery type from lead acid to nickel metal hydride (NiMH)
- Final wiring and electronics tidying
- Outdoor test ride and gear ratio evaluation
- Reflections on skills learned throughout the project (sprocket fabrication, derailleur setup, chain installation, motor controller wiring)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **8mm steel bar** — bent and welded to form the battery carrier frame
- **Steel tubing** — welded to steel bar for mounting the carrier to the scooter frame
- **Lead acid batteries** — originally planned but replaced
- **Nickel metal hydride (NiMH) batteries, 12V — from HiQuick** — sponsor-provided; used as the final power source (two packs wired together, previously built by Rob)
- **Bicycle derailleur** — repurposed for chain tensioning on the drive system
- **Chain and sprockets** — 42-tooth rear sprocket, 12-tooth front sprocket (custom-fabricated in earlier episodes)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Bending and welding steel bar** — forming the battery carrier arms and cross-sections
- **Drilling and bolting** — attaching carrier mounts to the scooter frame with mirrored hole placement
- **Tack-gluing for alignment** — temporarily bonding parts with adhesive before final welding to allow minor adjustments
- **Derailleur installation** — adapting a bicycle derailleur to maintain dynamic chain tension on the electric drive
- **Motor drive reversal** — physically repositioning the drive assembly to the correct side to fix wheel rotation direction
- **Battery charging and connection** — charging NiMH packs and connecting to motor controller before the test ride
