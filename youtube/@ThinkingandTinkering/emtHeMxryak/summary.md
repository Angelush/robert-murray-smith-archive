## Overview
This video is part 9 of Robert Murray-Smith's series on building a Van De Graaff generator, focusing on the assembly progress and the construction of a discharge rod. Robert demonstrates the partially assembled generator and explains the purpose and construction of a custom discharge rod used to safely dissipate the high-voltage charge (500–600,000 volts). He also emphasises the critical safety requirement of using a dedicated earth ground rather than the household mains earth.

## Key Topics
- Assembly progress update on the Van De Graaff generator (motor, rollers, pulleys, motor controls)
- Purpose and construction of a discharge rod for safely grounding the dome
- Earthing block setup and grounding strategy
- Remaining assembly steps (attaching combs and fitting the belt)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Chrome door handle (used as the discharge contact on the rod)
- Plastic pipe (~20 mm diameter, ~1 metre long) used as the insulating handle shaft
- Rubber tape (used to form a grip handle)
- Electrical cable (soldered to door handle, routed through pipe, connects to earth block)
- Plumbing snap-on fittings (used to create a cradle/holder for the rod)
- Copper earthing rod (mentioned in comments: 2 m rod pounded into the ground outside)
- Thick-gauge wire (for earthing connections, mentioned in comments)

## Techniques and Methods
- Soldering a chrome door handle to a length of cable
- Threading cable through plastic pipe to create an insulated high-voltage discharge rod
- Wrapping rubber tape to form an insulating handle
- Using plumbing fittings as a snap-on cradle for the discharge rod
- Wiring a dedicated earthing block on the base plate
- Driving a separate outdoor earth rod (independent of household earth) for HV safety

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Earthing detail (important clarification):** The bottom set of brushes must be earthed to allow charge separation. Because the device runs at ~500,000 V, this earth must be entirely separate from the house wiring. His recommended method: attach an earthing block to the base plate, run thick-gauge wire from the bottom brushes to the block, then drive a 2 m copper rod into the ground outside and connect it to the block with another wire. Connect the generator to this block only when running.
- **Project status:** The generator was sitting on a shelf unfinished due to Robert being very busy. At the time of the reply he was visiting Auroville in India (a town dedicated to alternative technology) and expected to return mid-November, with the series resuming around December.
