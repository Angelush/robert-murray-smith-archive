## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simple gluing trick for bonding dissimilar materials — plastic to hardboard — using super glue accelerated with bicarbonate of soda. The combination creates a rapid, rock-hard bond and proves effective enough for structural use in a functional wind turbine model.

## Key Topics
- Bonding dissimilar materials (plastic and hardboard/MDF) with super glue
- Using bicarbonate of soda as a super glue accelerator and filler
- Practical application in building a Savonius-style (Grinsky) wind turbine

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Super glue (cyanoacrylate / "crazy glue")** — the primary adhesive
- **Bicarbonate of soda (sodium bicarbonate)** — used as an accelerator/filler sprinkled onto the joint
- **Plastic** — one of the two substrates being bonded
- **Hardboard / MDF** — the second substrate

## Techniques and Methods
- Apply super glue to the plastic surface
- Press the plastic onto the hardboard and align the joint
- Sprinkle bicarbonate of soda liberally over the glue joint
- The exothermic reaction between cyanoacrylate and sodium bicarbonate rapidly cures the adhesive to a hard, filled bond
