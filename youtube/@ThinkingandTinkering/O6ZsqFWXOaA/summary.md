## Overview
In this short, candid rant, Robert Murray-Smith responds to the frequently asked question of why he doesn't improve his video and audio quality. He explains that his channel exists to inform and build a community of like-minded people sharing knowledge freely, not to entertain — and that "sufficient" quality to convey information is all he aims for. He draws a clear philosophical line between being an informer and being an entertainer, and states he has no intention of crossing it.

## Key Topics
- The history and improvement of his video quality over time
- Why he uses basic equipment (a webcam and Windows Movie Maker)
- The impracticality of HD uploads due to slow upload speeds (~6 hours for 8 minutes)
- His channel's core mission: free knowledge-sharing and community-building
- The distinction between *informing* an audience versus *entertaining* one
- His stance on monetisation (he had ~7,000 subscribers at time of filming, with 10,000 being the YouTube monetisation threshold)
- An open invitation to video producers to collaborate for free
