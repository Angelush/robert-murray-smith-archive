## Overview
This video is a competition entry submitted by a fan/follower of Robert Murray-Smith, inspired by Robert's work on DIY green energy systems. The creator built a small wind turbine using salvaged vinyl LP records as rotor blades, a converted fan motor as a generator, a bridge rectifier, and a capacitor — successfully lighting an LED from wind-generated power. It serves as both a project showcase and a heartfelt tribute to Robert's influence as a science communicator.

## Key Topics
- DIY wind turbine construction from salvaged and repurposed materials
- Converting a fan motor into an AC generator
- AC-to-DC rectification using a full bridge rectifier and smoothing capacitor
- Using vinyl LP records as wind turbine rotor blades
- Entry into a competition on Robert Murray-Smith's channel (prize: a 3D printer)
- Pulley and belt drive systems for coupling rotor to generator

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Vinyl LP records and singles — used (salvaged from a skip) as wind turbine rotor blades
- Full bridge rectifier (4-diode configuration) — converts AC output from generator to DC
- Capacitor — used to smooth the rectified DC signal
- LED — load used to demonstrate successful power generation
- Elastic band/cord — used as a drive belt in the pulley system
- Fan motor — repurposed as an AC generator

## Techniques and Methods
- Motor-to-generator conversion: repurposing a DC fan motor as a small AC generator
- Pulley and belt drive: coupling the rotor shaft to the generator using an elastic belt
- Bridge rectification: wiring a full-wave bridge rectifier to convert AC from the generator
- DC smoothing: adding a capacitor across the DC output to reduce ripple
- Soldering: assembling the rectifier and capacitor circuit by hand
- Blade fabrication: heating/bending vinyl records to form turbine blades (with limited success — vinyl prefers to remain flat)
