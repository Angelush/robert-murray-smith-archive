## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith begins building a small wooden automata inspired by driftwood collected near his coastal home. The sculpture will depict a rocking ship in front of cliffs, animated by a hand-turned crank mechanism. This is part 1 of the build, covering the initial design, cutting, and shaping of the main components.

## Key Topics
- Concept and design of a driftwood automata depicting a rocking ship
- Selecting and preparing driftwood pieces for the sculpture
- Cutting driftwood into three functional components: cliff face, wave panel, and boat
- Shaping the wave panel using drilled holes and a hacksaw
- Colouring wood with oil paint
- Assembling the cliff/backing section using dowels
- Shaping the small boat piece with a chimney and crank hole

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Driftwood (likely from a groyne/coastal structure)
- French ultramarine artists' oil paint (used as a wood stain/colourant)
- 6mm wooden dowels (structural joinery and chimney detail)
- 4mm drill bit / hole (for crank shaft fitment)
- 10mm (1cm) drill bit (for wave cutouts)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting driftwood into sections with a saw
- Planing and sanding timber for finishing
- Drilling and hacksawing to create wave profile cutouts
- Rubbing oil paint into wood grain with a cloth for quick-drying colour
- Clamping sections together and drilling through for dowel joinery
- Drilling a pilot hole before cutting to prevent wood splitting
- Fitting a crank mechanism through a drilled hole in the boat piece
