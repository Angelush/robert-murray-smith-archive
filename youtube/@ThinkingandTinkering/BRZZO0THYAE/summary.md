## Overview
This video is a viewer submission by Ron Story from Kamloops, British Columbia, Canada, entered into what he calls the "ILA Goo competition." Ron demonstrates his squirrel cage fan water turbine, which uses a repurposed HVAC squirrel cage blower driven by creek water flow and coupled via belt drive to an ECM (Electronically Commutated Motor) used as a generator. The system produced a maximum of 1.1 watts during early spring melt conditions, with higher output expected at peak flow.

## Key Topics
- Viewer competition submission for a low-cost DIY micro-hydro turbine
- Design evolution across four versions, drawing inspiration from multiple YouTubers and open-source projects
- Using a squirrel cage blower as a water wheel rotor
- Repurposing an ECM (Electronically Commutated Motor) from HVAC equipment as a three-phase generator
- Belt-drive coupling from the modified fan assembly to the ECM
- Rectifying three-phase AC output to DC for usable power
- Low-cost construction using salvaged and scrap materials (~$20 total)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Squirrel cage fan assembly (sourced from metal scrap yard)
- ECM (Electronically Commutated Motor) with permanent magnets, three-phase wound stator
- Half-inch wooden dowel (used as replacement shaft)
- Pillow block bearings (two, acting as fan axle)
- 12-inch bicycle wheel (used as large drive pulley)
- Plywood (platform and uprights)
- Half-inch polyester V-belt (adjustable length, for belt drive)
- Half-inch variable-width pulley (mounted on ECM motor shaft)

## Techniques and Methods
- Modifying squirrel cage fan: removing every second blade, then cutting the assembly in half with tin snips
- Replacing the original metal shaft with a turned wooden dowel fitted to pillow bearings
- Permanently wiring a bicycle wheel to the fan assembly to serve as a drive pulley
- Using a drill press to create bearing seat impressions in uprights
- Sizing platform to fit snugly in local creek features ("overshot" water wheel effect)
- Removing the ECM control module and soldering directly into the three-phase harness wires
- Rectifying three-phase AC to DC (referencing Mr. Electron's method)
- Testing winding resistance across phases (1-2, 1-3, 2-3) to verify motor health
