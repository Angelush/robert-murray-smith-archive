## Overview
A competition entry for Robert Murray-Smith's "Made From Paper" competition, submitted by a non-native English speaker. The video presents a passive, paper-based mechanical hygroscopic sensor that controls a drip irrigation system — watering plants only when the soil is dry, without any electronics. The design addresses key flaws in existing ceramic cone irrigators (clogging, algae, and over-watering) by using a salted-paper element that swells with humidity to pinch shut an air-supply tube, cutting off water flow when the soil is moist.

## Key Topics
- Problems with existing porous ceramic cone drip irrigators (clogging, algae growth, constant watering)
- Mechanical hygrometer principle applied to soil moisture sensing
- Paper-based hygroscopic sensor (salted paper layers) as the core actuating element
- Air-controlled water flow mechanism using a flexible pinched pipe
- System calibration and sensitivity adjustment
- Potential scaling to larger rainwater catchment containers (IBC tanks)
- Waterproofing paper/cardboard with casein paint and oil (mentioned as future improvement)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Salted paper** — multiple layers forming the hygroscopic sensor element; swells when humid to pinch the pipe
- **Cork** — used as the main tap body housing the sensor
- **Flexible rubber pipe** — used as the pinchable air-supply tube (replaced an earlier plastic version)
- **Thin yellow plastic pipe** — used for air inlet to the bottle, controlling water outlet
- **Super glue and baking soda** — used together to seal pipes into the plastic cork (cyanoacrylate/filler method)
- **Casein paint plus oil** — mentioned as a waterproofing treatment for paper/cardboard (geoplastified paper)
- **Credit card pieces** — used to construct a small jaw/clamp for the sensor mechanism

## Techniques and Methods
- **Mechanical hygrometry** — exploiting the expansion of salted paper in humid conditions to actuate a valve
- **Air-lock flow control** — using a pinched air-inlet pipe to regulate siphon/drip outflow from an inverted bottle
- **Pipe-in-cork sealing** — using super glue mixed with baking soda around pipes threaded through a plastic cork for watertight seals
- **Sensitivity adjustment** — positioning the pipe closer to or further from the end of the credit-card jaw clamp
- **System calibration procedure** — wetting the sensor, checking air bubbles in the yellow pipe, then drying in a hot place over several days with a catch jar to observe drip rhythm
- **Geoplastified paper waterproofing** — casein paint plus oil coating on paper/cardboard for weather resistance (referenced but not demonstrated)
