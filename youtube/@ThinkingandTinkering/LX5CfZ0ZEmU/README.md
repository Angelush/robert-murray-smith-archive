## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith visits Skara Brae, a remarkably preserved Neolithic village in Orkney, Scotland, to explore a theme he finds deeply important: that modern society has lost its connection to the origins of technology. Through hands-on demonstrations with clay and flint, he argues that technology — at its core — has always meant taking natural materials from the local environment and transforming them into something more useful, a process fundamentally unchanged from 250,000 years ago to the present day.
