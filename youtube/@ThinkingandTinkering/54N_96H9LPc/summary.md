## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple water-activated emergency battery using readily available materials, inspired by commercial products (the Eaton "Blackout Buddy") and a 2022 research paper. The battery is a layered sandwich of zinc foil, oasis floral foam, and carbon felt, activated by a vinegar-and-salt electrolyte. The key insight is that by pre-soaking the foam in a dried salt/acid solution, the battery can be stored indefinitely dry and activated with plain water in an emergency.

## Key Topics
- Commercial water-activated batteries: the Eaton "Blackout Buddy" and JW Kickstarter batteries
- 2022 research paper demonstrating a salt-soaked paper battery with zinc and carbon electrodes
- Step-by-step construction of a DIY water-activated battery
- Role of vinegar (acetic acid) in dissolving the zinc passivation layer to improve performance
- Using oxalic acid (Barkeeper's Friend) as a dry solid alternative to vinegar for a true "just add water" battery
- Potential emergency applications: torch, radio, phone charger

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Oasis floral foam** — acts as the water-wicking reservoir/separator layer
- **Zinc foil** — anode; can be sourced from disused alkaline batteries
- **Carbon felt (activated carbon)** — cathode; sourced from air filtration cartridges
- **Graphite foil (Grafoil)** — current collector (stainless steel mesh is a suitable alternative)
- **Vinegar** — 20 ml used as the acid component of the electrolyte; dissolves the zinc passivation layer
- **Table salt** — 1 teaspoon combined with vinegar to form the electrolyte
- **Oxalic acid (Barkeeper's Friend)** — solid acid alternative to vinegar; enables a dry, shelf-stable "just add water" version
- **Salt solution** — used to pre-soak foam/felt for the dry-storage variant

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting oasis foam to size with a kitchen knife (~0.5 cm × 2 cm × 4 cm)
- Assembling a three-layer sandwich: zinc foil / oasis foam / carbon felt, with graphite foil or stainless steel as the outer current collector
- Encasing the assembly in a plastic zip-lock bag and compressing with a bulldog clip or clothes peg to ensure electrode contact
- Activating the battery by adding a vinegar + salt electrolyte solution
- Pre-soaking foam and carbon felt in a salt + oxalic acid solution, then drying, to create a shelf-stable battery activated by plain water
- Testing output against a small DC motor (~200–250 mA draw) and discussing LED load (~2 mA) for runtime estimation

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Phone charging**: Confirmed a voltage regulator is needed between the battery and the phone.
- **Battery chemistry**: Explained the underlying reaction — zinc oxidises to zinc hydroxide, then zinc oxide, then zinc chloride, likely with hypochlorite as an intermediate and hydrogen gas produced on the other side. He noted it "won't be neat" but that is the basic sequence.
