## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains the fundamentals of wind turbine design after encountering an overpriced commercial folding turbine that claimed a 40W output. He walks through the physics of wind energy capture, showing why you must start with the blade/rotor section — not the motor — when designing a turbine. The key takeaway is that power output is determined by blade swept area and the cube of wind speed, and any motor mismatch can be resolved with gearing.
