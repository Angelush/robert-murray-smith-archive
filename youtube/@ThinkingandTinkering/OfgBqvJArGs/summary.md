## Overview
This is a competition entry video by Gary Slade (Australia) for Robert Murray-Smith's channel, demonstrating a Coca-Cola can wind turbine connected to a zinc-bromide battery that stores power to light an LED. The video expands into an in-depth exploration of zinc-bromide battery design, focusing on a novel HDPE/carbon separator technique that doubles the per-cell voltage, and Gary's many experimental attempts (most of which failed) to build a practical gravity battery for home energy storage.

## Key Topics
- Coca-Cola can wind turbine using a salvaged 3-phase motor
- Zinc-bromide battery as energy storage for the turbine
- Novel two-layer zinc-bromide cell design using an HDPE/carbon separator to achieve doubled voltage (3.4V instead of 1.7V)
- Fabrication process for conductive, liquid-blocking HDPE/carbon plates
- Power calculations for scaling zinc-bromide batteries to household energy needs
- Challenges with zinc-bromide battery electrode design and cell pressure/leakage
- Ionic liquids as a potential replacement for water in zinc-bromide cells

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Coca-Cola can** — structural housing for the wind turbine
- **Carbon felt** — electrode material in zinc-bromide cells
- **HDPE plastic (from milk bottles)** — used to fabricate conductive, liquid-blocking separator plates
- **Carbon foil** — heat-impregnated into HDPE to create conductive separators
- **Zinc bromide** — the battery chemistry (1.7V nominal per cell)
- **Zinc** — negative electrode material
- **PVC pipe** — housing for tubular series-cell experiments
- **Oasis foam** — tested as a cell separator (failed due to trapped air bubbles)
- **Fiberglass** — used in one experimental cell construction
- **Ionic liquids** — experimental electrolyte replacement for water, mentioned as a promising recent development to reduce pressure buildup

## Techniques and Methods
- Three-phase AC generation from a salvaged motor, rectified to DC
- Heat-impregnation of carbon foil into HDPE plastic at 250°C for 1.5–2 hours (using an aluminium clamp mould) to create conductive, liquid-impermeable separator plates
- Series-stacking multiple zinc-bromide cells internally within a single PVC tube using HDPE separators to multiply voltage without external wiring
- Vacuum cleaner (blower mode) used to spin the turbine in the absence of wind for battery demonstration
- Iterative electrode and separator design experiments, including oasis foam and pressure-sealed housings (documented failures)
- Substituting ionic liquids for aqueous electrolyte to eliminate gas pressure buildup in sealed cells
