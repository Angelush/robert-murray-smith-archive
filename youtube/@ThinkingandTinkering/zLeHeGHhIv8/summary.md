## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how he upcycled four old office desks salvaged from a lab remodel rather than throwing them away. Three desks were converted into storage cupboards, while the fourth was transformed into a coffee table using the desk pedestals as a base structure and desktop plywood for the top, legs, and shelf components. The video is a practical, no-frills joinery project emphasising resourcefulness and waste reduction.

## Key Topics
- Motivation for upcycling: unwillingness to discard usable materials from a lab remodel
- Converting three disassembled desks into fitted storage cupboards
- Step-by-step construction of a coffee table from the fourth desk
- Calculating and cutting dimensions for table components (top, legs, base panel)
- Assembling the unit with screws and bearing legs
- Finishing options (paint, filler, sanding, screw caps)
- Adding a toughened glass top with adhesive blob fittings

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Shuttering ply** — used for the desktop panels; noted to have one good face, prone to splintering on the reverse when sawn
- **Toughened glass** — repurposed piece used as the table top surface, fitted with adhesive blob fittings
- **Screws** — primary fasteners throughout
- **Glue (blobs)** — used to seat the glass top fittings, spreading into a neat circle on contact
- **Wall cupboards (readymade, from home store)** — the desk pedestals, repurposed as the table body

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly and sorting of old flat-pack office desks into reusable components
- Dimensioning and marking out cuts on shuttering ply (pencil lines, stacking panels for matched cuts)
- Circular/hand sawing with good face up to minimise visible splintering
- Butt-joint assembly held with screws and bearing leg supports
- Adhesive blob method for seating glass fittings neatly
