## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith examines a patent (US #11588421) sent by a viewer (Rodney) that adds a simple switch to the classic Tesla-style atmospheric energy collector circuit (antenna → diode → capacitor → ground). The core claim is that rapidly switching the ground connection disturbs the equipotential field lines, inducing voltage pulses in the antenna and charging a supercapacitor significantly faster than passive collection alone. A quick-and-dirty bench test with a 3-metre antenna appears to confirm the patent has real merit.

## Key Topics
- History of atmospheric/static electricity collection (Tesla, Feynman, corona motors)
- The longstanding problem with atmospheric energy: extremely low power (picoamp-level currents)
- Modern ambient RF environment ("electrosmog") as an additional collection source
- Patent #11588421 — adding a switching element to the basic antenna-diode-capacitor-ground circuit
- How breaking the ground connection disturbs equipotential field lines and generates antenna voltage pulses
- Bench demonstration comparing passive charging vs. switch-assisted charging of a supercapacitor
- Potential integration of the switch into a rotary mechanism for a corona/electrostatic motor

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned

## Techniques and Methods
- Clean Earth grounding (separate copper rod ground, isolated from household mains earth)

- Voltage monitoring of the supercapacitor via a meter during charging
- Qualitative comparison: passive ambient charging vs. manual switch-triggered charging
