## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explains how solid-state (piezoelectric) fans work and demonstrates how to build one from scratch. He draws an illuminating analogy to musical acoustics — specifically the physics of vibrating bars — showing that making a solid-state fan is more a problem of resonance tuning than electronics. He successfully builds a working fan using a homemade electromagnetic actuator ("sound bug") and a trimmed strip of steel, achieving ~1 cm deflection at 50 Hz.

## Key Topics
- What solid-state fans are and their advantages (lower power, longer life, smaller footprint, quieter)
- The acoustic physics behind piezoelectric fans: fundamental frequency, clamped vibration bars
- Parallels to musical instruments (glockenspiel, xylophone, guitar, tubular bells)
- Young's modulus and how it determines the required blade length for a target resonant frequency
- Using mains frequency (50/60 Hz) as the drive frequency
- The "sound bug" electromagnetic actuator as an alternative to piezo elements
- Practical construction and tuning (trimming blade length with scissors)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Aluminium** — common blade material for solid-state fans
- **Phosphor bronze** — preferred blade material; commercial fans achieve ~2 cm deflection with it
- **Brass** — listed as a suitable blade material
- **Steel** — used by Robert for his demonstration blade (~0.1 mm thick strip)
- **Ferrite core** — used inside the inductor coil of the sound bug actuator
- **Copper wire** — wound around the ferrite core to form the inductor
- **Neodymium/permanent magnet** — part of the sound bug assembly
- **Rubber bands / neoprene rubber / latex rubber / foam** — used as damping/mounting material in the sound bug
- **Tin can** — used as a resonating surface to demonstrate the sound bug actuator

## Techniques and Methods
- **Clamped-bar resonance tuning**: clamping one end of a thin metal strip and matching its free length to the target fundamental frequency
- **Blade length calculation**: using Young's modulus, material density, and target frequency (via formula or online calculator) to determine required strip length
- **Trimming/tuning by cutting**: iteratively shortening the blade with scissors until resonance at 50 Hz is achieved
- **Electromagnetic actuation (sound bug)**: driving the blade with a coil-ferrite-magnet assembly rather than piezo elements, powered by AC 6 V at 50 Hz from a bench supply
- **Piezoelectric actuation**: sandwiching piezo discs on either side of the blade as the standard commercial method
