## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains how solid-state (piezoelectric) fans work and demonstrates how to build one from scratch. He draws an illuminating analogy to musical acoustics — specifically the physics of vibrating bars — showing that making a solid-state fan is more a problem of resonance tuning than electronics. He successfully builds a working fan using a homemade electromagnetic actuator ("sound bug") and a trimmed strip of steel, achieving ~1 cm deflection at 50 Hz.
