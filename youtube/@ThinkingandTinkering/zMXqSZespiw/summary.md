## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to safely disassemble a second-hand microwave oven (purchased for £5) to salvage reusable electronic components. He walks through the safety precautions required — particularly around discharging the high-voltage capacitor — then identifies and extracts the valuable parts. His main takeaway is that a single scrapped microwave can yield around £50 worth of usable components.

## Key Topics
- Safety risks of microwave disassembly, especially high-voltage capacitors retaining charge after unplugging
- How to discharge a capacitor using a high-voltage rated screwdriver
- Identifying and removing reusable components
- Estimated resale/replacement value of salvaged parts
- Mention of an upcoming project using the microwave oven transformer (MOT)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- None mentioned (purely electronic/mechanical components, no chemicals or materials in a chemistry sense)

## Techniques and Methods
- Discharging a high-voltage capacitor by bridging terminals with a 10,000V-rated insulated screwdriver
- Removing torx/self-tapping screws to open the U-shaped pressed steel casing
- Systematic disassembly to extract individual components without damage
- Extracting ceramic ring magnets from the magnetron
