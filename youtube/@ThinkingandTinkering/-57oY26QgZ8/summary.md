## Overview
This video is a competition entry submitted by a fan inspired by Robert Murray-Smith's channel, presenting the "Counterstar Wind Generator" — a vertical axis wind turbine built entirely from salvaged household junk. The core concept, drawn from Robert's own teaching, is that maximising the relative speed between stator and rotor increases generator output; the design uses a home-built planetary gear system to spin the stator counter to the rotor at 15× the blade speed. While the full wind-powered prototype did not function reliably due to gearing slip, the counter-rotating stator/rotor principle was validated and the device successfully lit an LED.

## Key Topics
- Robert's principle that stator/rotor relative crossing speed is the key factor in wind generator output
- The "Counterstar" concept: counter-rotating stator and rotor to multiply effective generator speed
- Building a functional prototype entirely from discarded/salvaged items
- A home-built planetary gear set achieving a 15:1 speed ratio
- Using a hoverboard hub motor as a three-phase generator
- Constructing a slip ring system to transfer power from a rotating post
- Building a compact three-phase bridge rectifier fitted inside the central post
- Competition entry framing and the motivation of upcycling waste materials

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Washing machine tub** — repurposed as the vertical axis wind turbine fan blades
- **Hoverboard wheel motor** — used as the three-phase generator
- **Adult bicycle wheel (silver)** — component of the improvised gear system
- **Two children's bicycle wheels** — act as planetary gears
- **Rubber inner tube** — wrapped around gear wheels as a friction drive surface
- **3D printer timing belt** — attempted as a toothed drive element (too small, replaced)
- **Twine** — wrapped around gear wheels as an improvised cog/traction surface
- **Copper strips** — glued to the outside of the central post as slip ring contacts
- **Hand blender carbon brushes** — used as slip ring brushes; happened to match the pipe diameter exactly
- **Plastic tube** — central rotating post housing the wiring and rectifier

## Techniques and Methods
- **Vertical axis wind turbine construction** from salvaged materials
- **Planetary gear set fabrication from scratch** to achieve 15:1 speed multiplication
- **Counter-rotating generator configuration**: outer drum (blades) rotates counterclockwise; central post (stator) driven clockwise
- **Friction drive using rubber inner tube** sections wrapped around gear wheels
- **Three-phase bridge rectifier construction**, miniaturised to fit inside the central post
- **DIY slip ring assembly**: copper strips bonded to rotating post contacted by salvaged hand-blender carbon brushes
- **Live electrical testing** with alligator clips, LED, and multimeter to confirm DC output
