## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith lent his Eskute Polluno e-bike to his brother Dave, an avid cyclist, who rode it for nearly a year covering approximately 3,500 miles. Dave provides a detailed long-term real-world review covering maintenance costs, battery degradation, and overall durability. The conclusion is that the bike is a solid entry-level city e-bike that held up well under heavy use, though it feels sluggish compared to a lighter non-electric bike for daily commuting.

## Key Topics
- One-year, ~3,500-mile real-world durability test of the Eskute Polluno e-bike
- Maintenance required and associated costs after heavy use
- Battery capacity degradation over ~60 charge cycles
- Weight comparison and ride feel versus a lightweight non-electric bike (Halfords Boardman)
- Front fork wear and component quality trade-offs at the £1,200 price point
- Overall recommendation and intended use case (city/town riding)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Chrome plating on front fork stanchions (noted to have worn off, exposing bare steel with surface rust)

## Techniques and Methods
- Long-term field testing over approximately 350–355 riding days
- Full bike service including cable replacement, drivetrain strip-down, and brake pad replacement
- Comparison riding between the e-bike and a lightweight non-electric bike to assess ride feel differences
