## Overview

Robert demonstrates a remarkably simple method for copper-plating steel using a brown/green liquid he describes as a precursor to a metal-organic ink made from copper nanoparticles suspended in water. By simply rubbing the fluid onto steel with tissue paper or swirling the steel in the solution, a tough, durable copper coating forms almost instantly — outperforming the traditional copper sulfate replacement reaction method.
