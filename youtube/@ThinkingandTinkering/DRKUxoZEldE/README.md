## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores design considerations for a homemade wind turbine he has recently built, using calm weather days as an opportunity to reflect on principles and address viewer concerns. His central argument is that build quality — specifically the squareness and tight tolerances of the rotor cage — matters far more than magnet type, magnet arrangement, or coil configuration. He also surveys a wide range of salvaged coil options to demonstrate that cheap, accessible materials can yield comparable results.
