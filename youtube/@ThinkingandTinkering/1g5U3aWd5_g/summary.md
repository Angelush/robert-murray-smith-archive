## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith designs and builds a motorised orrery (solar system model) featuring the Sun, Earth, and Moon as a one-day project, combining laser cutting and 3D printing. The video demonstrates how the Xtool P2 laser cutter dramatically accelerates production of gear components compared to 3D printing alone, cutting 5 mm acrylic in under 4 minutes. The finished model uses a clear acrylic gear train to give the planets an aesthetically pleasing "floating" appearance.

## Key Topics
- History and purpose of orreries (first made in 1712 by John Rowley for the Earl of Orrery)
- Design workflow using TinkerCAD, splitting the model into laser-cut flat parts and 3D-printed curved parts
- Complementary use of laser cutting vs. 3D printing for different geometries
- Step-by-step assembly of the gear train, motor, battery box, and planetary bodies
- File sharing on Thingiverse (STL for full 3D print option; SVG for laser cutting the gears)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **5 mm acrylic** — used for the ring gear and flat gear components, laser cut
- **3 mm basswood** — also cut on the laser cutter as an alternative material test
- **PLA / 3D print filament** (implied) — used for sun, moon, earth bodies, feet, and pillars
- **Superglue** — used throughout assembly to fix gears, feet, and planetary bodies
- **Battery box** — under £1, sourced from Amazon; powers the motor
- **Small plastic gearbox motor** — ~£2, sourced from Amazon
- **2 mm diameter × 40 mm steel bar** — used as the motor axle

## Techniques and Methods
- **CAD design in TinkerCAD** — model split strategically between flat (laser-cuttable) and curved (3D-printable) geometry
- **Laser cutting with Xtool P2** — 5 mm acrylic gear set cut in 3 minutes 48 seconds; material positioned using dual on-board cameras; SVG files imported into XCS software
- **FDM 3D printing** — used for double-curved and decorative parts (planets, feet, pillars)
- **Motor-driven gear assembly** — small plastic gearbox motor driving a pinion gear meshing with a ring gear; gear sequence (ring → large → medium) assembled with specific orientation to prevent snagging
- **Optional hand-drive modification** — bevel gears and a crank handle can replace the motor
- **Soldering / wire-twisting** — connecting motor leads to battery box (soldering recommended but twisting viable)
