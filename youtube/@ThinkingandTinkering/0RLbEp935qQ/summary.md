## Overview
This video demonstrates how to remove and reattach bicycle/scooter chain links without specialist chain tools. The presenter, who has been electrifying a scooter, shares a practical workaround using common household tools to seat chain pins correctly when a dedicated chain tool won't accommodate the pin position.

## Key Topics
- Removing and reattaching chain links by hand
- Why standard chain tools may fail when a pin is pushed too far out
- Using pump pliers to partially seat a chain pin
- Using a nut as a spacer/anvil and a screwdriver as a punch to fully drive the pin home
- Tips for stabilising the chain during the process to prevent slipping

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Bicycle/scooter drive chain (pin-type, not quick-link)
- Quick-link chain (mentioned as an alternative, not demonstrated)

## Techniques and Methods
- Using pump pliers (set to smallest setting) to partially press a chain pin through the link
- Using a large nut as a raised platform/anvil to give the pin clearance when driving it through
- Tapping a Torx screwdriver with a hammer to drive the pin fully home
- Spreading multiple nuts under a chain section to minimise slipping during the operation
