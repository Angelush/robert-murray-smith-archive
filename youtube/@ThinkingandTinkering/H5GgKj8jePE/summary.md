## Overview
Robert investigates the composition of commercial aluminium "welding" rods (specifically Durafix brand), which are more accurately brazing rods. By feel alone he suspects the rods are primarily zinc, and he confirms this by rolling zinc foil into a rod and successfully brazing aluminium pieces together — achieving joints stronger than the base metal. His main takeaway is that cheap zinc wire can substitute for expensive branded rods, at least for cosmetic or non-structural fabrication work.

## Key Topics
- What aluminium brazing rods (e.g., Durafix) are actually made of
- Testing pure zinc as an aluminium brazing filler material
- Joint strength comparison: zinc-brazed aluminium vs. base aluminium
- Whether fluoride flux improves the process
- Cost-saving alternative: using zinc wire instead of branded rods

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Durafix aluminium welding/brazing rods** — the commercial product under investigation
- **Zinc foil / zinc wire** — identified as the likely primary (or sole) constituent of the rods; used as a DIY substitute
- **Aluminium bar and scrap aluminium** — base material being joined
- **Fluoride fluxes** — tried during testing but found to have no noticeable effect; rods are advertised as fluxless
- **Magnesium** — briefly mentioned as a possible minor alloying addition in the commercial rods

## Techniques and Methods
- Visual and tactile assessment of rod composition (feel compared to known zinc)
- Rolling zinc foil into a brazing rod
- Torch brazing of aluminium pieces at an angle joint
- Destructive joint testing (attempting to break the brazed joint by hand to assess strength)
