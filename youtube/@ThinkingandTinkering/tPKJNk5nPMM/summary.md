## Overview
This video is part of Robert Murray-Smith's paper-based making competition, featuring a submission by a community member named Ron who built a Vertical Axis Wind Turbine (VAWT) primarily from foam board and recycled materials. Robert opens with an apology for nearly missing Ron's entry due to email overload, then hands over to Ron who walks through the design, construction, and operation of his turbine. The main takeaway is a functional, low-cost VAWT prototype with an innovative asymmetric blade belly design intended to improve wind capture efficiency.

## Key Topics
- Robert's apology for losing Ron's competition entry and his decision to grant Ron extended viewing time
- Overview of the VAWT design and its component parts
- Use of foam board as the primary structural material
- Recycled materials repurposed for mechanical components
- Ron's novel blade design featuring alternating "belly" profiles to improve airflow
- Demonstration of the turbine spinning under fan-assisted wind
- Future potential to add magnets and coils for power generation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Foam board** — primary structural material for the turbine blades and plates (paper-faced, lightweight)
- **Plywood** — used for the main platform/base to mount the bearing
- **Pipe (unspecified)** — used as the central shaft/axle
- **Recycled filament spool** — repurposed as a structural component on the shaft
- **Rollerblade/roller skate wheel/bearing** — used as the rotation bearing in the centre plate
- **Magnets and coils** — mentioned as a future addition for electricity generation (not yet implemented)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Cutting and slotting foam board** — parts cut to shape and assembled by slotting together (top ring, bottom ring, centre plate, blades)
- **Recycled component repurposing** — empty filament spool and rollerblade wheel reused as mechanical parts
- **Asymmetric blade profiling ("belly" design)** — one side of each blade given a curved belly, alternated in orientation around the rotor to capture wind on the active side while reducing drag on the return side
- **Paper-based design/drafting** — all parts designed on paper before fabrication
- **Fan-assisted indoor testing** — turbine tested indoors using a fan due to excessively windy outdoor conditions
