## Overview
In this short video, Robert responds to recurring comments accusing him of "comparing apples and oranges" — specifically regarding his comparisons between batteries and supercapacitors. He argues that the phrase is meaningless without defining the metric of comparison: when a clear metric such as weight, energy, or power is specified, comparing two very different things is entirely valid and meaningful.

## Key Topics
- The logical fallacy behind dismissing comparisons as "apples and oranges" without qualification
- The importance of defining terms and metrics before making comparisons
- How undefined words (e.g., "happiness") cause endless miscommunication
- Applying this reasoning to energy storage devices: batteries vs. supercapacitors compared on energy and power metrics
- The distinction between the "flavor" of a device (discharge behaviour, application suitability) and its fundamental energy/power characteristics
