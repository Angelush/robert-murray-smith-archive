## Overview
Robert explores the application of tubercles — the bumpy ridges found on humpback whale fins — to water power generation. He 3D-prints a scaled-down tubercle turbine blade, attaches it to a converted PC fan acting as a generator, and tests it with a garden hose jet. The experiment confirms the design works as a turbine, and Robert speculates about its potential for broader water energy applications.

## Key Topics
- Tubercles (bumpy leading-edge ridges derived from whale fins) and their fluid dynamics properties
- Comparison of water vs. air as fluids
- History of water power: waterwheel, Pelton wheel (1870s), impulse vs. reaction turbines
- Converting a PC fan into a simple generator
- 3D-printed tubercle turbine blade test using a garden hose
- Current and potential applications: wind turbines, surfboard fins, wave energy devices (e.g., Wave Dragon)
