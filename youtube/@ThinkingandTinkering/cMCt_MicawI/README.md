## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to repurpose a microwave oven transformer (MOT) into a functioning electromagnetic speaker. He salvages the primary coil and iron core laminate sections, combines them with neodymium magnets and a hacksaw blade reed, and attaches a cellophane diaphragm to produce audible sound. The result is a working—if slightly tinny—speaker built entirely from salvaged and household materials.
