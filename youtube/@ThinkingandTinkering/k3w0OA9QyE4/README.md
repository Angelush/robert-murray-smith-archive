## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores Edison's 1878 "Phonomotor" (vocal engine) — a device that converts sound into mechanical motion — and builds a modern version using a 3D-printed horn, a cellophane membrane, a coil, and neodymium magnets to scavenge electrical energy from sound. The experiment successfully generates enough voltage to light an LED by yelling into the horn, demonstrating sound-to-electricity conversion as a proof of concept for acoustic energy harvesting.
