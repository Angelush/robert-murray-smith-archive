## Overview
Robert demonstrates how to repair a burnt-out solar panel that suffered an overload, damaging the internal contact tabs. Working from the back of the panel, he methodically disassembles it, cleans and reconnects the burnt tabs, and reseals it — confirming the repair works by reading 17 volts in the shade.

## Key Topics
- Diagnosing a burnt-out contact in a solar panel
- Safely disassembling a framed, sealed solar panel
- Removing and replacing butyl rubber sealant
- Cleaning and reconnecting burnt internal metal tabs
- Resealing the repaired panel for weatherproofing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **WD-40** — used to soften and loosen butyl rubber for disassembly
- **Butyl rubber strip** — original sealant material; new strip used to reseal the frame
- **Xylene** — applied to loosen the adhesive holding plastic backing layers, allowing access to the burnt tabs; noted in comments as a paint thinner, not particularly flammable
- **Wire wool** — used to clean the burnt metal tabs before joining
- **Waterproof duct tape** — used as a quick cover over the repaired joint (resin recommended as the better alternative)
- **Resin** — recommended as the ideal waterproof sealant for the repaired area
- **Hot soapy water / Brillo pad / scouring pad** — for scrubbing residual butyl rubber off the backing board

## Techniques and Methods
- Prying off the aluminium frame channel using WD-40 as a rubber softener
- Slicing and digging out butyl rubber with a screwdriver and cutting tool
- Dissolving plastic backing layers with xylene and trimming with a box knife
- Cleaning burnt metal tabs with wire wool
- Reconnecting tabs by **tab welding** (from battery-making equipment), with **soldering** or **crimping** offered as equally valid alternatives
- Resealing the frame with new butyl rubber strip and replacing the aluminium surround

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirmed **the panel worked quite well after the repair**.
- Clarified that **xylene is not particularly dangerous to handle** — it doesn't burn intensely even at 100% concentration, and is widely sold as paint thinner, available online.
- Noted that **the diodes are located on the back connection** of the panel (in response to a question about their placement).
- Acknowledged he could have shown the burnt circuit more clearly before starting the repair, noting he thought it was visible enough — "ah well next time."
- Suggested a broken panel of this type is likely a **"Friday panel"** (i.e., manufactured carelessly at the end of the week) — implying manufacturing defect as a probable cause.
