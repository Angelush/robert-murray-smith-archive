## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith traces the history of 3D printing from its earliest conceptual roots in 1945 science fiction through to modern consumer-grade machines, arguing that the technology has a much longer and richer lineage than is commonly credited. He highlights the shift from filament-based FDM printing back toward stereolithographic (resin/MSLA) printing as prices have collapsed. The video doubles as a plug for an ongoing competition to win an Elegoo Mars Pro 2 MSLA printer.
