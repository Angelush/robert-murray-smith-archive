## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple, low-cost vegetable oil burner/heater using cheap or repurposed materials. The core innovation is a perforated metal strapping used as a wick holder, which draws in air alongside oil to produce a surprisingly smokeless flame. The video is aimed at people seeking affordable, DIY heating solutions, particularly using waste cooking oil.

## Key Topics
- DIY vegetable oil heater construction
- Using secondhand or waste cooking oil as fuel
- Perforated metal strapping as an air-assisted wick holder
- Adding a mantle and gauze to improve the flame
- Smokeless combustion through passive air intake

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Vegetable oil (fresh, used for demonstration; waste/secondhand also suitable)
- Used motor oil (mentioned as an alternative fuel)
- Metal strapping — 40mm wide × 2mm thick, perforated (used for jointing roof timbers/joists)
- Activated carbon felt — Robert's preferred wick material
- Cotton balls, old bandages, toilet paper — alternative wick materials
- Ethanol — used to prime/start the wick with heavy oils
- Sugar storage canister lid — used as the oil reservoir
- Wire gauze — fitted around the mantle ring
- Stainless steel kitchen utensil holder (perforated) — added as an outer shield/chimney

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting and bending metal strapping into a T-shape to form a wick holder
- Sliding wick material through the perforated strapping and trimming to length
- Priming a heavy-oil wick with ethanol to aid ignition
- Bending a second strip of strapping into a ring to support a mantle and gauze
- Fitting a perforated stainless steel tube over the assembly to act as an air chimney
