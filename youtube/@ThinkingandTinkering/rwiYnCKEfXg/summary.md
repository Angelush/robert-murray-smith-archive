## Overview
This short video, presented by Luke from Robert Murray-Smith's channel, demonstrates connecting a dust extractor to a belt sander to reduce airborne dust during woodworking. The experiment shows that attaching the extractor to the sander's exhaust port significantly reduces dust spread, though it does not capture every particle.

## Key Topics
- Reusing a dust extractor (previously connected to a circular saw) on other power tools
- Demonstrating the amount of dust a belt sander produces without extraction
- Comparing sanding results with and without the dust extractor connected
- Practical workshop dust management

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Scrap wood (used as the sanding test piece)
- Wood from an e-scooter project (context for why dust management was needed)

## Techniques and Methods
- Connecting a dust extractor to the exhaust port of a belt sander
- Before/after comparison of dust output with and without extraction
- Securing workpiece in a vise for sanding demonstration
