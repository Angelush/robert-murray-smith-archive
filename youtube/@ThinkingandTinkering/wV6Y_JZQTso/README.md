## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores plant microbial fuel cells — devices that harness the natural redox reactions between plant root exudates and soil bacteria to generate electricity. He builds his own small prototype using street-scraped moss in a petri dish, and demonstrates a significant power improvement (from microwatts to milliwatts) by adding a fertilizer solution with salt to increase ionic conductivity. The video teases a forthcoming live workshop where he will build the device from scratch and reveal the specific fertilizer salt used.
