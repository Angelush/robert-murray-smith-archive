## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to convert the internal components of a broken lead-acid battery into a functional metal oxide solar cell. By cooking the battery's lead plates and sleeves at different kiln temperatures to produce yellow lead oxide (PbO), then mixing it with sodium silicate as a binder and painting the paste onto copper mesh, he creates a working solar cell that outputs approximately 0.2 volts in direct sunlight. He connects this project to the broader hot research area of metal oxide solar cells and teases further potential for homemade Peltier devices.

## Key Topics
- Disassembling a lead-acid battery to extract usable materials
- Thermal conversion of battery components into yellow and red lead oxides
- Yellow lead oxide (PbO) as a photoactive n-type semiconductor
- Fabrication of a simple metal oxide solar cell using battery-derived materials
- Comparison to copper oxide solar cells
- Overview of multi-layer metal oxide solar cell research
- Potential for homemade Peltier devices using n-type and p-type metal oxides

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Lead-acid battery** — source of raw materials (plates and protective sleeves)
- **Yellow lead oxide (PbO)** — produced by low-temperature kiln firing; photoactive n-type semiconductor used as the active solar cell layer
- **Red lead oxide (Pb₃O₄)** — produced at a higher kiln temperature from the same battery components
- **Sodium silicate (water glass)** — used as a binder; prepared as a 40% by weight solution in water (40 g sodium silicate in 60 mL water)
- **Copper mesh** — used as the current collector substrate for the solar cell
- **Sulfuric acid** — present inside the battery (noted briefly when opening it)
- **Water** — used during grinding to form the oxide paste

## Techniques and Methods
- Opening a lead-acid battery and removing internal plates and sleeves
- Kiln/oven firing at two distinct temperatures to produce yellow PbO and red lead oxide respectively
- Wet grinding of lead oxide powders in a kitchen grinder with water to produce a fine paste
- Mixing oxide paste with sodium silicate solution to create a paintable adhesive paste
- Painting the paste onto copper mesh and air-drying to form the solar cell
- Outdoor testing of the cell under direct sunlight (voltage measurement)
