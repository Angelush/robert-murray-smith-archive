## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a low-cost Stirling engine based on a NASA design (later developed by "Thrillerblade"), constructed in roughly one hour from salvaged and inexpensive materials. The video covers a working demonstration, a full step-by-step build guide for a second variant, and a second demonstration showing the engine running. The engine operates on a temperature differential and is highly sensitive to tuning via weight adjustments on the diaphragm.
