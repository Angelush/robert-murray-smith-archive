## Overview

This is the second video in Robert Murray-Smith's DIY air conditioning series, focusing on an upgraded bellows-based air pump and the construction of directional air valves. Robert explains how a simple flat valve system can give the pump directional airflow control — sucking from one source and blowing across a heat exchanger — and discusses a range of cooling options from wet blankets to car radiators, arguing broadly that any system that conditions air for comfort qualifies as "air conditioning."
