## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores a 1978 German patent for an unusual vertical-axis wind turbine rotor — the "C Rotor" — which was directly inspired by the Pelton wheel used in hydropower. He traces the concept from its historical roots in fluid mechanics through to modern research validating its efficiency gains, and concludes by proposing a next step: using 3D printing to fabricate a double-cup version more faithful to the original Pelton wheel design.
