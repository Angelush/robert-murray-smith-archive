## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith investigates and replicates the famous graphite expansion demonstration by Viktor Petrik, a controversial inventor who claimed a $10 million reward for anyone who could discover his secret. Robert successfully recreates the dramatic graphite expansion using intercalated graphite and a solution he identifies as glycerol, concluding that the phenomenon is a self-initiating exothermic reaction — essentially a "magic trick" with no genuine mystery.
