## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores a wind turbine design promoted by Dr. Robert Hunt that allegedly challenges conventional physics understanding of wind energy — specifically arguing that drag, not the Bernoulli principle, is the key force at work. He demonstrates the concept by building a simple version using an LP record, Tyvek house wrap, and a homemade hydrostatic water thrust bearing. The result spins impressively under a hairdryer, validating the basic principle as an accessible DIY experiment.

## Key Topics
- Dr. Robert Hunt's patented drag-based wind turbine (patent WO2000047918682)
- Simon Gripenberg's artistic installations as the design inspiration
- Nashan windmills of Persia — the oldest known windmills
- Hydrostatic (fluid) thrust bearings as low-friction axle supports
- The drag-vs-Bernoulli debate in wind energy physics
- DIY construction of a rotating disc wind turbine from household materials
- Oyster fishermen's sail technique as Hunt's original inspiration for drag-based propulsion

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **LP record** — used as the main rotating disc
- **Tyvek house wrap** — used as the flexible flap/wing material (stiff cloth or sheet plastic suggested as alternatives)
- **Empty 500ml plastic bottle** — buoyancy element in the hydrostatic thrust bearing
- **8mm diameter rod** — axle for the rotating assembly
- **Pipe and plastic cup** — housing for the water bearing
- **Water** — working fluid in the hydrostatic thrust bearing
- **Super glue** — bonding the flap material to the disc
- **Aluminium** — material used by Hunt in his patented version
- **Speaker magnets** — mentioned as an alternative to water for creating upward lift/levitation

## Techniques and Methods
- **Hydrostatic (fluid) thrust bearing construction** — using an empty bottle's buoyancy in water to near-eliminate axial friction
- **Magnetic levitation bearing** — proposed as an alternative: opposing speaker magnets to provide lift
- **Disc cutting** — tracing an LP record and cutting a circle from house wrap with a trimming knife
- **Flap fabrication** — scoring and folding the cut circle to create wing-shaped flaps glued at 90° intervals
- **Hairdryer testing** — using a domestic hairdryer on low to demonstrate rotation
- **3D printing** — suggested as a superior method for reproducing the design with integrated slots

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comments are mostly brief acknowledgements and thank-yous. One notable exchange: he was unfamiliar with something referred to as the "LPD Device" (asked twice), suggesting he does not consider the device synonymous with any named technology he knows. He also noted that the energy capture of such a turbine **depends on the energy in the wind** being captured — a practical qualification on the device's real-world performance.
