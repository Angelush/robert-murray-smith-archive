## Overview
Robert introduces the concept of biomimicry in wind turbine design, surveying how natural forms — bird wings, whale fins, and flexible trees — have inspired engineering improvements. He then builds and demonstrates a small prototype turbine based on designs by Finnish artist Simon Gripenberg, whose YouTube channel he recommends.

## Key Topics
- Biomimicry as a foundational principle in wind turbine engineering
- Bird wing geometry influencing blade shape and reducing aerodynamic disturbance
- Tree flexibility inspiring adaptive turbine designs that withstand strong gusts
- Whale fin hydrodynamics inspiring smoother, curved blade edges
- Four benefits of biomimetic turbines: enhanced efficiency, reduced environmental impact, improved adaptability, and noise reduction
- Challenges of scaling natural designs into cost-effective, validated engineering solutions
- Simon Gripenberg's nature-inspired turbine designs as a discovery on YouTube
- Hands-on prototype build and fan-driven demonstration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Aluminium sheet (bent to form turbine blades)
- Fiberglass rod (used as the swivel axle / cross-beam)
- Wood (circular hub piece and stick/mount)

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling paired through-holes at 90° in a wooden hub to create a swivel axis
- Bending aluminium sheet over a fiberglass rod to form self-pitching blades
- Mounting two blades offset 90° on the same axle to exploit differential drag
- Low-speed fan testing at approximately 1.5 m/s wind speed to verify rotation direction
