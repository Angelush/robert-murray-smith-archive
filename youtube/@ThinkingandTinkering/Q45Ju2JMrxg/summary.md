## Overview
Robert unboxes the Eskute Polluno, a redesigned city-oriented e-bike from Eskute (the same brand behind the Pharaoh and Voyager models he has reviewed previously). The Polluno features a step-through aluminium frame, integrated handlebar display, and a larger battery than its predecessors. Robert finds the pedal-assist mode — which is legally mandated on the EU version — to be genuinely enjoyable, reversing his initial preference for throttle control.

- Comparison to previous Eskute models (Pharaoh and Voyager)
- Design improvements: step-through frame, integrated display, chrome/satin recessed bolt heads
- EU legal requirement for pedal-assist-only above 3.6 mph
- Robert's change of opinion on pedal assist vs. throttle
- Mention of a potential e-bike giveaway competition and the challenge of international postage
- Brief discussion on e-bikes' "last mile" environmental impact vs. cars

## Robert's Own Replies
- **EU pedal-assist law:** Clarifies that under EU law, pedal assist is required once the bike exceeds 3.6 mph — "slightly lower than walking pace," he notes with some amusement.
- **Giveaway competition:** Engages warmly with several fan suggestions; likes the idea of factoring shipping costs into the competition structure, though says he probably won't use a lottery format.
- **Last-mile impact:** Expresses genuine enthusiasm for e-bikes as a sustainable transport solution, agreeing with a commenter's point about their "last mile impact" and suggesting people should consider dumping the car in favour of the bike.
- **Riding experience:** Tells a commenter he will share more detailed riding impressions once he has used it more, noting it rides "pretty much like any normal bike."
