## Overview

Robert introduces the Prony brake as a simple, low-cost method for measuring the torque (and therefore power output) of a rotating shaft — specifically in the context of his wind turbine project. He explains both the classic wooden-block design invented in the 1800s and a band-type variant using a drum, rope or leather strap, and spring balances. The video concludes by teasing the construction of an actual Prony brake in a follow-up.
