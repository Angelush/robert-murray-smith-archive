## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the history and practice of stone tool technology, tracing its origins back 3.3 million years to australopithecines — predating homo sapiens by over 3 million years. He demonstrates the Oldowan and Acheulian toolmaking techniques hands-on, including knapping a flint and using a bow-drill with a stone-tipped dowel to bore a hole in wood. His central takeaway is that we tend to overcomplicate engineering when history shows remarkably effective solutions achievable with the simplest possible materials.
