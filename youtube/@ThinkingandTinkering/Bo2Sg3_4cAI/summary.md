## Overview
A competition entry for the Robert Murray-Smith Lead Light Contest, this video demonstrates how a damaged bicycle with a bent frame was repurposed into a stationary exercise bike and flywheel generator. The builder converts pedaling energy into electrical power using a salvaged motor-as-generator, capacitor bank, and buck converter to light an LED.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing a bent-frame bicycle as a home exercise bike
- Using a bicycle as a human-powered generator
- Energy storage via electrolytic capacitors
- Voltage regulation with a buck converter to power an LED

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Cement (used to fill and weight the rear wheel, acting as a flywheel)
- Electrolytic capacitors — nine 2900 µF, 350V units used as energy storage
- A salvaged motor (originally from an automatic door opener, 80% duty cycle rated) used as a generator
- Fan belt (connecting rear wheel to the motor/generator)
- Rubber bands cut from a bicycle inner tube (used as belt tensioning springs)
- Four leg bolts (part of the generator pivot/tensioning assembly)
- A door hinge (used as the generator pivot point)
- A buck converter (stepping down voltage to 12V output)
- An LED ("lead light" — the contest subject)

## Techniques and Methods
- Removing the front wheel and fitting a stability stand to convert a bicycle to a stationary trainer
- Filling the rear wheel with cement to create a weighted flywheel for energy smoothing
- Mounting a salvaged DC motor as a generator using a fan belt drive
- Using a door hinge as a low-cost pivot/tensioning mechanism for the generator mount
- Cutting inner tube rubber into bands as improvised tension springs
- Charging a capacitor bank to ~40V from pedaling, then regulating output via buck converter
