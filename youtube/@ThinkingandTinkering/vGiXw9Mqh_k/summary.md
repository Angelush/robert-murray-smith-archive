## Overview
Robert builds a battery-free spinning torch (flashlight) inspired by a viewer suggestion to turn his swinging-arm generator (video 1219) into a twirling baton for a light show. The key challenge is getting electrical power to an LED at the tip of a spinning rod without wires wrapping and snapping. He solves this elegantly using the metal bar itself as the return/negative path and a small ball bearing as a slip ring for the positive connection.

## Key Topics
- Battery-free, generator-powered spinning light concept
- Viewer-inspired build (credit to Timothy Gorman)
- Using the metal body/bar as the electrical return path (common in automotive wiring)
- Slip ring construction using a ball bearing to maintain rotating electrical contact
- Heat shrink insulation for isolating the slip ring from the shaft
- LED and LED strip as light sources
- Demonstration in a darkened room

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Metal bar (brass, used as the conductive return/negative path)
- 5 mm roller/ball bearing (used as a slip ring)
- Heat shrink tubing (for insulation on the shaft end)
- Bare copper wire (clamped inside and outside the bearing)
- LED (single bulb, later swapped for an LED strip)

## Techniques and Methods
- Using a conductive metal body as the negative/return electrical path (body-earth technique from automotive practice)
- Fabricating a slip ring from a standard ball bearing pressed over an insulated shaft end
- Clamping bare wire inside and outside a bearing to achieve rotating electrical contact
- Heat-shrink insulation to electrically isolate the slip ring from the rotating shaft
- Swapping from a single LED to an LED strip to improve visual effect
