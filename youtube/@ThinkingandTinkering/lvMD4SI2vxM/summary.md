## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a small wind-powered generator using a bicycle bottle dynamo, a magnetic gearbox he developed in a previous video, and propeller blades cut from plastic drainage pipe. The system converts slow propeller rotation (~60 RPM) into the higher speeds needed to generate useful electricity, suitable for charging phones or powering lights while camping or cycling.

## Key Topics
- How bicycle bottle dynamos work (3–5W output at ~6V, designed for 12 mph cycling)
- Using a magnetic gearbox to step up propeller RPM to dynamo-usable speeds
- Cutting propeller blades from plastic drainage pipe
- Mounting the assembly on a bicycle or using it as a portable wind generator
- Voltage regulation for powering USB devices like phones

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Bicycle bottle dynamo (3–5W, 6V)
- 100mm (4-inch) diameter plastic drainage pipe (~5mm wall thickness, orange/grey colour) — used to cut propeller blades
- Soil pipe (black, 100mm) — mentioned as an alternative to drainage pipe
- Voltage regulator module (~£1.50, 5V output) — for regulated USB-compatible output
- Crocodile clips — for wiring connections
- Plastic disc — hub for mounting the blades
- 8mm axle adapter (3D-printed in Tinkercad)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting a 60cm section of pipe and dividing it into quadrants to produce four 50cm propeller blades
- Using a magnetic gearbox to gear up slow wind turbine rotation (~60 RPM) to ~600–700 RPM for the dynamo
- Mounting the dynamo/gearbox assembly on a bicycle frame
- Testing by holding the turbine out of a car window to simulate high airflow
- Voltage regulation using a pre-set fixed-voltage regulator module (wired via crocodile clips)
