## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of using wind power to drive or charge cars, from historical land yachts to modern experimental designs. He builds and demonstrates a 3D-printed model car powered by a Savonius wind turbine, then discusses practical proposals for real vehicles — including a Dutch inventor's integrated turbine design and portable roof-mounted turbines for charging electric cars. The main takeaway is that wind-powered or wind-charged electric cars are more viable than they might seem, especially given that cars spend roughly 90% of their time parked.
