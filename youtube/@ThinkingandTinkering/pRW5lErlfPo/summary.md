## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of using wind power to drive or charge cars, from historical land yachts to modern experimental designs. He builds and demonstrates a 3D-printed model car powered by a Savonius wind turbine, then discusses practical proposals for real vehicles — including a Dutch inventor's integrated turbine design and portable roof-mounted turbines for charging electric cars. The main takeaway is that wind-powered or wind-charged electric cars are more viable than they might seem, especially given that cars spend roughly 90% of their time parked.

## Key Topics
- History of wind-powered vehicles: land yachts, the Green Bird (2009), and the Blackbird (2010)
- Comparison of wind capture methods: propellers, sails, and Savonius rotors
- 3D-printed model car with a Savonius wind turbine driving the wheels via bevel gears
- Velomobile/propeller-driven car concepts
- Oscar de Kiefer's car-mounted turbine design (exhausts through wheels for low drag)
- Portable, foldable roof-mounted wind turbines for charging electric vehicles
- The statistic that cars are parked ~90% of their life, making parked charging viable

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Plastic (3D-printed parts for the model car)
- 6 mm steel axle bars (100 mm and 120 mm lengths)
- Bearings (12 mm OD, 6 mm ID, 4 mm thick)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing parts from Thingiverse/Tinkercad designs
- Press-fitting bearings into a printed frame
- Bevel gear transmission to couple the Savonius rotor to the drive wheels
- Hair dryer used to demonstrate wind-driven locomotion of the model
- Roof-rack-style foldable turbine deployment concept for EVs

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Wind vs. solar for parked cars:** Robert noted that cars are mostly parked overnight when there's no sun, but wind is often available — particularly coastal wind coming in from the sea at dusk and night, making wind a better fit than solar for this use case.
- **Research depth:** He acknowledged the topic goes well beyond a short video and recommended viewers look at Google Scholar papers on the subject, noting he found ideas that genuinely surprised him and shifted his initial skepticism about drag.
- **Skepticism of fringe claims:** He flagged suspicion about "WITTS" and "quantum energy" concepts brought up by commenters.
- **Kite generators:** He confirmed he has seen kite generator designs and finds them fascinating.
- **Rotor ships:** He clarified that wind-rotor ships are called rotor ships and use a Flettner rotor.
- **General encouragement:** Repeatedly encouraged commenters to try things themselves ("try a model first," "go for it").
