## Overview
Luke repairs a salvaged Unilab Retro PSU variable power supply that was found without a plug and with an audible rattle. The repair turns out to be straightforward — a single loose wire needed resoldering and a new plug attached — and the unit is restored to full working condition. The video demonstrates that valuable lab equipment (worth £100–150) can often be saved with minimal effort.

## Key Topics
- "Trash to Cash" repair series: finding and restoring discarded equipment
- Diagnosing a variable power supply with unknown fault(s)
- Visual inspection of internal soldered connections
- Resoldering a loose wire
- Fitting a correctly-rated mains plug
- Testing the repaired unit across multiple voltage steps

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Solder
- Flux

## Techniques and Methods
- Visual inspection of PCB and internal wiring for loose or damaged connections
- Resoldering a disconnected wire
- Rattle diagnosis by reassembly and physical shake test
- Functional voltage test (2V, 4V, 6V, 9V, 12V) using a small DC motor as load
