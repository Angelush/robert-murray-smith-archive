## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reflects on the patent system while waiting for varnish to dry in his lab, arguing that the current system is broken due to "patent trolls" — entities that mass-produce speculative patents with no intent to invent, solely to extract money through litigation. He concludes that one legitimate reason for an individual inventor to file a patent is as a defensive measure against these trolls.
