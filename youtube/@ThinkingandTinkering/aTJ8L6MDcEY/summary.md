## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates alginate impression molding powder — a dental casting material — to create a detailed mold of his hand. Unlike silicone, alginate handles complex overhangs and undercuts, making it ideal for body casting. He successfully produces a plaster cast from the mold and reveals this is part one of a two-part project to ultimately fabricate latex gloves for a friend.

## Key Topics
- The problem of overhangs and undercuts in traditional silicone molding
- Alginate as a dental impression material repurposed for hand casting
- Mixing and setting properties of alginate powder
- Casting plaster of paris into the alginate mold
- Teaser for a follow-up project: making latex gloves from the plaster hand

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Alginate impression molding powder** — the primary mold material, mixed 1:1 by volume with water at 30°C; originally a dental product, also a 1980s kids' toy
- **Water (at 30°C)** — critical temperature for proper working time (~2 minutes)
- **Fine casting powder / Plaster of Paris** — used to fill the alginate mold and create the final cast
- **Aluminium tube** — used as the container/former to hold the alginate while setting

## Techniques and Methods
- **Alginate impression molding** — mixing powder with water at a controlled temperature (1:1 by volume), pouring into a container, inserting the hand, and withdrawing after ~2 minutes
- **Volume measurement by displacement** — using a marked bowl to ensure equal volumes of powder and water
- **Plaster casting** — pouring plaster of paris into the set alginate mold at a pourable consistency, then demolding once hardened
- **Demolding** — removing the bottom of the aluminium tube and pushing the cast out; peeling away the alginate (which is single-use)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Part two context:** Robert clarifies that the plaster hand is not the final goal — it is used in part two to make a latex glove. The dry plaster absorbs moisture from liquid latex, depositing a latex film on the surface.
- **Disposal of alginate:** When asked what to do with used alginate, Robert says simply "in the bin — it's seaweed."
- **Breadth of uses:** He notes alginate is "an astoundingly useful material" and "an interesting material for a lot of uses," echoing genuine enthusiasm beyond this single project.
- **Face casting suggestion:** When someone suggested casting a face, Robert quipped that straws up the nose would be needed for breathing.
- **Theory videos:** He directs a viewer with deeper questions to watch his theory videos alongside the practical ones.
