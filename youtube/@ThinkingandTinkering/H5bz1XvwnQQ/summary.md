## Overview
In this father-daughter project video, Robert and his daughter convert a ceiling fan motor into a small water turbine generator. They fabricate turbine blades from coat hangers, install magnets around the motor to generate electricity, and test whether spinning the turbine with water can light up LEDs. The project successfully produces blinking lights during the water spray test, demonstrating basic principles of electromagnetic induction in a hands-on, DIY setting.

## Key Topics
- DIY water turbine construction using salvaged household materials
- Using a ceiling fan motor as an electrical generator
- Electromagnetic induction via permanent magnets
- Father-daughter collaborative building and experimentation
- Iterative testing (dry spin test, then water test)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Ceiling fan motor (repurposed as generator)
- Wire coat hangers (used as turbine blades)
- Small round permanent magnets (8, arranged alternating north-south around the rotor edge)
- Brackets salvaged from an old window blinds box (used as spacers/mounts)
- Brown, purple/gray, and red wires (motor wiring)
- LED lights (load being powered)
- Water (working fluid for turbine test)
- Tape (to secure magnets)
- Glue (to attach water-catching scoops)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting coat hangers in half to create 10 uniform turbine blade segments
- Arranging permanent magnets alternating north-south around the motor housing to create a magnetic field for induction
- Reassembling the fan motor housing with salvaged brackets to accommodate the modified internals
- Hand-spinning the rotor to perform a dry test before adding water
- Spray-bottle water test to simulate turbine-driven rotation
