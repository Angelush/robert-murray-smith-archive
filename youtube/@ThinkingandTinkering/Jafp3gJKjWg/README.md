## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith presents a philosophical and historical survey of electricity's discovery, arguing that science is defined by three things: curiosity ("I wonder what would happen if"), communication, and rule-making. He traces a chain of serendipitous experiments from 1675 to the Faraday motor, concluding that dismissing early discoveries as useless is "the dumbest question" — since batteries and electric motors grew directly from apparently trivial sparks.
