## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates an outdoor experiment with a Pelton-wheel-style wind turbine blade featuring a distinctive "tadpole" shape. He discovers that orienting the turbine to point directly into the wind causes it to spin significantly faster, which he attributes to a Hero-engine-like jet effect — wind is deflected around the curved tadpole head and jets over an airfoil surface. He proposes combining this blade design with his existing "Darwin" wind collector as a potentially superior wind energy system.
