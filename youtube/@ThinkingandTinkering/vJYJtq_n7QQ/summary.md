## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates an outdoor experiment with a Pelton-wheel-style wind turbine blade featuring a distinctive "tadpole" shape. He discovers that orienting the turbine to point directly into the wind causes it to spin significantly faster, which he attributes to a Hero-engine-like jet effect — wind is deflected around the curved tadpole head and jets over an airfoil surface. He proposes combining this blade design with his existing "Darwin" wind collector as a potentially superior wind energy system.

## Key Topics
- Pelton-wheel-inspired wind turbine blade with a "tadpole" shape
- The Hero engine principle applied to wind turbines — directional jetting creating rotational force
- The Darwin wind collector — a device that captures wind from all directions and channels it in one direction
- Comparison of airfoil lift via flow versus airfoil lift via jetting
- Integration of wind collection, blade rotation, and gearing as a complete turbine system

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Outdoor smoke/field test on a hillside to observe blade behaviour
- Manual orientation testing (pointing the turbine into vs. away from the wind) to compare spin speed
- Observational comparison of Hero-engine-style jet propulsion versus conventional airflow over an airfoil
- Proposed integration of the Pelton-style blade into the Darwin wind collector funnel
