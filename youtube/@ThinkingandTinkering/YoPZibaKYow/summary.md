## Overview
Robert demonstrates painting a functional speaker directly onto a cellophane membrane using conductive graphene ink. Building on video 1389 (where he made a drum-like enclosure from builders board and cellophane), he draws a spiral coil with the ink, attaches wires, and places it over a ring magnet — and it works. The video shows that a fully functional speaker can be created with little more than conductive ink and a thin film.

## Key Topics
- Painting/drawing a spiral speaker coil using conductive graphene ink
- Using cellophane stretched over a builders board frame as the speaker membrane
- Connecting wires to the ink spiral using double-sided tape
- Testing the painted speaker over a ring magnet
- The commercial potential and scalability of printed/painted speakers

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene conductive ink** — the core material, used to paint the spiral coil; sold in Robert's store; also described as sprayable (airbrush/spray compatible)
- **Cellophane** — used as the thin flexible membrane of the speaker
- **Builders board** — used as the rigid frame (from video 1389)
- **Double-sided tape** — used to fix wires to the membrane before applying ink
- **Ring magnet** — used as the magnetic component to drive the speaker

## Techniques and Methods
- Stretching cellophane over a frame to create a drum-like membrane
- Painting/drawing a spiral coil pattern onto the membrane with conductive ink
- "Splodging" ink over wire ends so the ink flows onto and bonds with the wires, making electrical contact
- Drying the ink before testing
- Holding the finished membrane over a ring magnet to test audio output

## Robert's Own Replies
- **IR reflectivity**: Robert notes that a graphene coating is IR reflective, possibly relevant to thermal behaviour of the membrane.
- **Power limitations**: Clarifies that while the reverse (microphone mode) is possible, using a transformer doesn't increase power — the output power is minuscule, though the potential of the technology is huge.
- **Wire connections**: Explains that there is a connecting wire at each end of the spiral, "glued" in place by the ink itself.
- **Cost and alternatives**: Acknowledges the graphene ink is quite expensive, but notes that aluminium foil tape or copper tape could achieve similar results.
- **Sprayable ink**: Confirms the ink is sprayable and points to other videos where he applies it with an airbrush.
- **Members video**: Hints that a related members-only video pushed the concept further and "blew his tiny mind."
