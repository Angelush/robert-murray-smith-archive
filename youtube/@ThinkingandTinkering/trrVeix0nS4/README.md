## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith introduces brush electroplating — a technique using a sponge-tipped electrode dipped in plating solution rather than a bath — and explains how to formulate a DIY plating electrolyte using household chemicals. He references a US patent (7235165) for its directional guidance on chelating agents and electrolyte ratios, then substitutes household-available chemicals to approximate the patented formula without infringement. This is the first in a series on the topic.
