## Overview
Robert demonstrates a quick and effective trick for removing stubborn pulleys that have been stuck for many years — in this case, about 20 years on a compressor. The method involves heating the aluminium pulley with a blowtorch to cause thermal expansion and burn off old oil and grime, allowing the pulley to be levered off easily with a crowbar. He shares this tip while disassembling a compressor for inspection.

## Key Topics
- The difficulty of removing long-stuck pulleys from shafts
- Thermal expansion as a mechanical advantage for disassembly
- Practical workshop tips for machinery maintenance
- Disassembly of a compressor unit

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Aluminium pulley (the component being removed)
- Steel shaft/motor centre
- Butane/propane blowtorch fuel
- Oil and crud (old lubricant residue built up over ~20 years)

## Techniques and Methods
- Heating the aluminium pulley with a blowtorch (approximately 5 minutes) while avoiding direct heat on the steel centre shaft
- Using thermal expansion of aluminium to break the grip of the pulley and burn off old oil residue
- Levering the pulley off with a large crowbar after heating
