## Overview
Robert demonstrates a quick trick for swapping 3D printer filament mid-print without pausing. By feeding the new filament in just as the old one runs out, the filament runout sensor is kept active throughout, allowing large multi-spool prints to complete uninterrupted.

## Key Topics
- Swapping filament rolls on an Elegoo Neptune 3D printer without pausing a print
- Using the filament runout sensor to seamlessly continue printing
- Context: printing NACA aerofoil parts for an aeromyne (flying wing) project

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- PLA or similar FDM filament (multiple kilogram spools implied)

## Techniques and Methods
- Unloading the near-empty spool by pulling remaining filament off manually
- Threading new filament through the runout sensor behind the tail end of the old filament
- Applying gentle manual pressure to feed new filament until the extruder gear grips it
- Verifying grip by lightly tugging the filament to confirm it has been caught
