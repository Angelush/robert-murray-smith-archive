## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make coffee from dandelion roots, walking through the full process from harvesting to brewing. He grinds, dries, and roasts the roots before brewing them in a cafetière, then taste-tests the result with colleagues at his lab. The verdict is mixed but broadly positive — one tester suggests it might pass as actual coffee.

## Key Topics
- Harvesting and preparing dandelion roots
- Grinding and drying the roots before roasting
- Roasting the ground roots in an oven
- Brewing dandelion root coffee in a cafetière
- Informal taste test with lab colleagues

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Dandelion roots — the primary ingredient, harvested fresh and processed into a coffee substitute

## Techniques and Methods
- Root harvesting and washing
- Mechanical grinding using a cheap countertop grinder
- Oven drying at Gas Mark 1 for approximately 25 minutes to remove moisture before roasting
- Roasting the dried, ground root in the oven
- Brewing via cafetière (French press)
