## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a concept model of a rim-based generator built from recycled and 3D-printed parts, revisiting an idea he first discussed in 2006. The device uses an empty 3D printer filament spool as the spinning element, with neodymium magnets at the rim and a stationary coil to generate electricity — no central axle required. The proof-of-concept successfully generates around 3 volts when driven by a hair dryer, validating the principle though not presented as a finished design.
