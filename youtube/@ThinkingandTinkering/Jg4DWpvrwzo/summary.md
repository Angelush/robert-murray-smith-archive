## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a concept model of a rim-based generator built from recycled and 3D-printed parts, revisiting an idea he first discussed in 2006. The device uses an empty 3D printer filament spool as the spinning element, with neodymium magnets at the rim and a stationary coil to generate electricity — no central axle required. The proof-of-concept successfully generates around 3 volts when driven by a hair dryer, validating the principle though not presented as a finished design.

## Key Topics
- Rim-based generation as a concept (originally discussed in 2006)
- Axle-free generator design philosophy
- 3D-printed cradle and support structure
- Cone needle bearing design for low-friction support
- Recycling/repurposing a 3D printer filament spool as the rotor
- Wind and water as potential input sources
- Proof-of-concept voltage output demonstration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Neodymium magnets (2mm × 10mm) — used at the rim to interact with the pickup coil
- Empty 3D printer filament spool — repurposed as the rotor/wheel element
- 3D-printed parts (cradle, cradle arms, cone bearings, guide ring, quarter-pipe vanes) — structural and functional components

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing of structural components (files freely available on Thingiverse)
- Cone-based needle bearing construction for rotor support
- Gluing quarter-pipe vane segments at an angle onto the spool rim for wind/water capture
- Hand-held coil pickup against rim magnets for electricity generation
- Hair dryer used as a wind source to spin the rotor during testing
- Voltmeter measurement to verify output
