## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith shares a quick follow-up to his electroplating leaf video, demonstrating the results of copper-plating a rose. The flower was coated in a conductive ink, left to dry, and then electroplated in a copper plating solution for a day. He is pleased with the results and briefly notes how to improve the process.

## Key Topics
- Electroplating organic materials (flowers, leaves)
- Using conductive ink as a preparatory coating
- Setting up a simple electroplating bath with copper anodes
- Tips for improving plating quality (aquarium pump for agitation)
- Teasing a future project: copper-plating baby shoes

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Conductive ink — used to coat the rose conformally before plating
- Universal plating solution — the electrolyte bath for copper deposition
- Copper anodes — wrapped around a plastic bowl as the positive electrode
- Glass — used to hang the rose in the plating solution

## Techniques and Methods
- Dip-coating an organic object in conductive ink and allowing it to dry
- Electroplating in a copper sulphate-based universal plating solution
- Using a plastic bowl as the plating vessel with copper wrapped around it
- Suspending the workpiece on glass filament/wire connected to the negative terminal
- (Recommended but not used here) aquarium pump agitation to improve plating uniformity
