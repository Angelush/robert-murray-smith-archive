## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents a 3D-printed demonstration and teaching model of the Archimedes Drive, a friction-based gear reduction mechanism developed by IM Systems. The video walks through disassembling and reassembling the model to explain how the drive works, highlighting its use of the Wolfrom principle to achieve very high gear ratios. He positions this as the next evolutionary step beyond his earlier scroller/wolf drive work, noting that the Archimedes Drive solves the band-failure problem by using cylindrical rollers instead.

## Key Topics
- The Archimedes Drive by IM Systems and its operating principles
- The Wolfrom (Wolf-front) principle as applied in the drive
- Gear ratio mechanics: how the "step" (ledge size) between rollers determines reduction ratio
- Comparison to the earlier scroller/wolf drive (bands vs. cylinders)
- The historical "Connections" framing (James Burke series) linking magic wallets → scroller drive → Archimedes Drive
- STL files and build video availability for makers who want to replicate the model
- Robert's collaboration with IM Systems (unpaid, voluntary)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing (STL files provided for the teaching model)
- Physical disassembly/reassembly of the model to expose internal components
- Visual demonstration of gear reduction using a marked dot on the output wheel
- Roller cage design using cylindrical rollers against ground and output rings (replacing bands used in scroller drive)
