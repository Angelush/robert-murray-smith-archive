## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains the mechanical challenge of cornering on a delta trike (two driven rear wheels) and demonstrates a low-cost, DIY differential built entirely from repurposed bicycle freewheel cassettes. By orienting two freewheels on a shared rigid axle so both engage in the same direction, the assembly behaves like a locked axle going straight but allows the inner wheel to freewheel during cornering — achieving a differential effect without a traditional, expensive differential unit.
