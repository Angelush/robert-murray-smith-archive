## Overview
Robert demonstrates the cold molding technique to construct large wooden wheels for an unnamed energy generation project. Cold molding involves bending thin strips of wood around a shaped former and gluing them so they retain their curved shape — a method used in boatbuilding, furniture, and roof trusses. The video emphasizes how simple and versatile the technique is, requiring minimal equipment.

## Key Topics
- What cold molding is and where it is commonly used (boats, furniture, roof trusses)
- Choosing the right wood thickness relative to the desired curve radius
- Glue selection and application for cold molding
- Mold construction and release agents
- Clamping and securing methods while the glue cures
- Building curved laminated plywood semicircles to form wheels

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **5 mm plywood strips** — cut to 2.4 m length, used as the laminate layers
- **Cascamite (Cascamine)** — powdered urea-formaldehyde resin glue, described as the standard boatbuilder's glue; weatherproof, gap-filling, ~1 hour working time
- **Gorilla Wood Glue** — mentioned as easier to apply but less strong than Cascamite
- **A third unspecified glue** — described as "spectacular but difficult to apply" (likely epoxy or similar)
- **Oil** — applied to the mold surface as a release agent
- **Acetone** — used to clean residual oil off the wood before finishing

## Techniques and Methods
- **Cold molding / cold laminating** — bending thin plywood strips around a shaped former and gluing them in successive layers
- **Mold construction** — scrap timber screwed to a bench in the desired curved shape; no precision required
- **Glue application** — brushing Cascamite on one side of each strip (both sides if using certain other glues)
- **Mold release** — oiling the former to prevent adhesion
- **Clamping methods** — luggage strap wrapped around the laminate, screws driven through layers, or sash/bar clamps; mallet used to level the stack
- **Cure time** — approximately 6 hours before removing from the mold
