## Overview
This video explores how James Watt circumvented an existing patent on the crank mechanism to convert the reciprocating motion of a steam engine into rotary motion. Watt's solution was the Sun and Planet gear system, a form of epicyclic gearing, which Robert demonstrates using a 3D-printed model he designed in Tinkercad. The video connects this historical invention to modern applications of planetary gear systems.

## Key Topics
- Brief history of the steam engine, from ancient origins to Newcomen's atmospheric engine
- James Watt's improvements to the steam engine, particularly the separate condenser (1765)
- Watt's use of patents and his need to work around James Pickard's 1780 crank patent
- The Sun and Planet gear as Watt's patented workaround
- How epicyclic/planetary gearing converts reciprocating motion to rotary motion
- The gear ratio advantage of the Sun and Planet system over a simple crank
- Modern applications of epicyclic gearing (drills, generators)
