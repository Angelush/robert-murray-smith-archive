## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith visits a traditional toy shop in Herne Bay to purchase a humming top, then dissassembles it to reveal the twisted-bar-and-slotted-washer spin mechanism at its core. He uses this mechanism — combined with a 3D-printed frame, a ratchet, thrust bearings, a serpentine coil, and a 2.5 kg barbell flywheel — to build a hand-pumped DIY generator capable of producing up to 6 volts, enough to charge a mobile phone.
