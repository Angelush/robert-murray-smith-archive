## Overview
Robert demonstrates his large vibration cleaner — a shaking/tumbling machine built in a previous video — now scaled up to handle big, dirty lawnmower parts. He loads chunky steel components covered in rust, paint, and oil into a 35-litre container with 10mm gravel and a homemade cleaning solution, and shows the results after 30 minutes: most parts come out looking like bare metal.

## Key Topics
- Scaling up the vibration cleaner from a small to a large version
- Container system design (adjustable to fit 3L or 35L tubs)
- Choosing 10mm concrete gravel as the cleaning media
- Cleaning heavily soiled lawnmower steel parts (rust, paint, oil)
- Results after 10 and 30 minutes of vibration cleaning

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Vinegar** — 400 ml, part of the cleaning solution
- **Salt** — 2 tablespoons, part of the cleaning solution
- **Washing-up liquid (dish soap)** — 20 ml, part of the cleaning solution
- **Isopropanol (IPA)** — 100 ml, part of the cleaning solution
- **10 mm gravel (concrete aggregate)** — used as the abrasive cleaning media; described as effectively sharp quartz
- **Steel lawnmower parts** — the workpieces being cleaned (rusty, painted, oily)
- 12 mm threaded bar and locking nuts — used in the machine's container mounting system

## Techniques and Methods
- Vibration/tumble cleaning using a motor-driven shaking machine
- Wet and dry cleaning capability via interchangeable containers
- Abrasive media tumbling (gravel acting as the abrasive against workpieces)
- Container clamping with a top clamp plate to secure the tub during vibration
- Multi-stage cleaning: checking at 10 minutes, continuing to 30 minutes
