## Overview
In this workshop tour, Steve (a collaborator in Robert Murray-Smith's lab) walks through the tools and techniques they use to overcome fabrication challenges on a limited budget. The central message is that expensive powered machinery can often be replaced or supplemented with affordable hand tools and custom-made solutions. The video concludes with a glimpse of a custom-built hopper for producing graphene filament for 3D printing — an example of making what you can't buy off the shelf.

## Key Topics
- Keeping tools organised in a portable, custom-modified tool carrier
- Using a drill clamp/vice to safely drill small workpieces
- Budget-friendly small drill press (Dremel-compatible) for fine work
- Hobby lathe for precision turning, drilling, and threading
- Hand tools vs. powered machinery — cost and space trade-offs
- Japanese pull saw and the knife wall technique for accurate straight cuts
- Custom fabrication philosophy: building equipment that doesn't exist commercially
- Graphene filament production and 3D printing work in the lab

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene filament** — being developed for 3D printing and production of graphene plastics
- **Steel** — turned on the lathe to precise dimensions for lab components
- **Aluminium** — material of the drill clamp/vice jaws

## Techniques and Methods
- **Drill clamping** — using a small aluminium vice/clamp to hold tiny workpieces safely under a pillar drill
- **Dremel drill press** — mounting a Dremel in a small drill press stand (~£15/$25) for fine, accurate drilling; aligning the drill bit both perpendicular and laterally
- **Lathe turning** — turning steel to accurate dimensions, winding coil wire coils, horizontal drilling, and cutting threads for screw holes
- **Knife wall technique** — scribing a cut line with a hobby knife, then paring a wall with a chisel before sawing, for a clean perpendicular cut (credited to woodworker Paul Sellers)
- **Japanese pull saw cutting** — cutting on the pull stroke for cleaner, more controlled cuts
- **Custom fabrication** — scavenging motors and machining bespoke parts (e.g. hopper for graphene filament extruder) when no commercial option exists
