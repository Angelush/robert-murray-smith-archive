## Overview
This video is the second and final part of Robert's build of an electric scooter using a bamboo frame, salvaging mechanical and electrical components from a defunct kids' scooter. He walks through installing the hub motor, fitting replacement lead-acid motorcycle batteries in a stitched-on battery box, wiring a DC motor controller, and adding controls — then takes the finished scooter for a test ride that impresses everyone in the yard.

## Key Topics
- Completing the bamboo e-scooter frame from Part 1
- Mounting a hub motor using steel brackets fed into bamboo tubing
- Replacing dead original batteries with 12V lead-acid motorcycle batteries wired in series
- Attaching the battery box to the bamboo frame using an upholstery stitching technique
- Fabricating a plywood foot plate
- Wiring the DC motor controller (battery, motor, throttle, and brake connections)
- Test ride and performance assessment of the 120W motor

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Bamboo** — structural frame material throughout
- **Steel** (10mm hole, slotted) — motor mounting brackets
- **Lead-acid 12V motorcycle batteries** (x2, wired in series for 24V) — replacement power source
- **HDPE** — original battery box material; also mentioned as a DIY option via thermal/vacuum forming
- **Acrylic** — mentioned as an alternative battery box material
- **Plywood** — used for the foot plate
- **String/wire** — stitching the battery box to the frame
- **Resin** — suggested optionally to waterproof and toughen the stitching

## Techniques and Methods
- **Hub motor installation** — slotting steel brackets with a 10mm hole into bamboo tubing to secure the axle
- **Upholstery stitching** — drilling 6mm holes at 2cm intervals in the battery box and lacing it to the frame with string using a homemade needle bent from wire
- **Series battery wiring** — two 12V lead-acid batteries connected in series to produce 24V
- **DC motor controller wiring** — connecting battery+/−, motor+/−, throttle potentiometer, and brake disconnect cable to a labeled controller board
- **HDPE thermal/vacuum forming** — referenced as a method for making a custom battery box (via a collaborator's video)

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly brief social responses ("cheers mate", "lol"). Two contain substantive points:
- **On flat batteries:** Lead-acid batteries will drop to around 10V when flat, and the scooter can be pushed by foot at that point — suggesting the design has a graceful low-battery fallback.
- **On battery case safety concerns:** He reassured a worried commenter that at ~5 mph the forces involved are far too low to damage the battery casing, implying the build is safe for its intended use.
