## Overview
This video demonstrates how to replace the upper radiator hose (rad pipe) on a Seat Ibiza 6L. Luke walks through the full removal and installation process, following a temporary emergency repair shown in a previous video. After a 70-mile test over the weekend, the repair holds perfectly and the car runs noticeably better.

## Key Topics
- Removing the old upper radiator hose from a Seat Ibiza 6L
- Installing a new replacement rad pipe
- Draining and topping up coolant during the repair
- Post-repair engine check (idle and rev test)
- Real-world follow-up: 70-mile test drive confirming success

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Engine coolant (used in the cooling system; drained during removal and topped up after installation)

## Techniques and Methods
- Removing a push-fit retaining clip using a flat-head screwdriver
- Wiggling and disconnecting the connector flange from the radiator
- Using pliers (butterfly clips) to compress and reposition hose clamps
- Cleaning the connector before fitting the new pipe
- Running the engine for 5–10 minutes post-repair to check for leaks/weeping
- Giving the engine light revs to circulate coolant through the system
