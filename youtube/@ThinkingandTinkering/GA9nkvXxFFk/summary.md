## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of magnetic levitation as a practical form of "anti-gravity," tracing the history from maglev trains to Earnshaw's theorem. He highlights a 2021 discovery by Turkish engineer Hamdi Unal and subsequent 2023 research from the Technical University of Denmark showing that simply spinning a permanent magnet at ~200 Hz causes another magnet to levitate stably — a counterintuitive result that defies classical magnetostatic laws. The key takeaway is that this effect is surprisingly simple and low-power, potentially bringing real magnetic levitation within practical reach.

## Key Topics
- The concept of anti-gravity in science fiction versus real-world physics
- Maglev technology: electromagnetic suspension vs. electrodynamic suspension
- Earnshaw's theorem — why static magnetic fields cannot achieve stable equilibrium
- Hamdi Unal's 2021 discovery of levitation via a spinning permanent magnet
- Technical University of Denmark's 2023 paper confirming and explaining the physics
- The frequency-locking mechanism between the rotor magnet and the floater magnet
- Potential applications: molecular trapping, touchless robotics
- The broader implication that our understanding of magnetism is incomplete
