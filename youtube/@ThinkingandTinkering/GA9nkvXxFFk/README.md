## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of magnetic levitation as a practical form of "anti-gravity," tracing the history from maglev trains to Earnshaw's theorem. He highlights a 2021 discovery by Turkish engineer Hamdi Unal and subsequent 2023 research from the Technical University of Denmark showing that simply spinning a permanent magnet at ~200 Hz causes another magnet to levitate stably — a counterintuitive result that defies classical magnetostatic laws. The key takeaway is that this effect is surprisingly simple and low-power, potentially bringing real magnetic levitation within practical reach.
