## Overview
A short personal video in which Robert Murray-Smith shares the story of his cat going missing for approximately a week — likely trapped in a neighbour's shed — before returning home thin and exhausted. Relieved by her return, Robert fitted her with a radio tracking device so she can be located if she ever disappears again.

## Key Topics
- The cat's disappearance and safe return after roughly a week
- The likely cause: being accidentally shut in a neighbour's garden shed or garage
- The purchase and demonstration of a radio collar tracker (the "Loc8tor")
- The cat's initial reaction to the beeping tracker

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Radio/RF pet tracking via a collar-mounted device paired with a handheld locator unit
- Pressing the locator button to trigger an audible beep on the collar and receive a location signal

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirms the tracker is called the **Loc8tor** ("rubbish name I know — but it seems to work")
- Notes his cat's name is **Kaali**, and jokes she is "a killer" — leaving mice and birds around; he doesn't intervene, citing the "circle of life"
- Reflects warmly on how much he missed her: *"It's funny the hole they leave when they go"*
- Self-describes as "a bit of a softy" when it comes to his cat
- Mentions he considered trying the tracker on the cat himself but she wouldn't sit still long enough
