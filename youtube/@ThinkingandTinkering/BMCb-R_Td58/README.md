## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith responds to a viewer comment asking whether flint (used in the previous video to make tools) could be used to make a transistor — and the answer is yes. The video traces the history from early crystal diodes used in WWI radio communication through to the Bell Labs transistor, explaining how certain rocks and minerals exhibit semiconductor properties like negative resistance that enable transistor-like behaviour. It highlights real-world examples of researchers who have built working transistors from natural minerals.
