## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith repurposes a salvaged mahogany coffee table to hand-craft a wooden cement mixer truck as a gift for his two-year-old grandson. Using mostly dowels and glue with no screws or nails, he builds a chunky, child-safe toy finished with tung oil. The video is a lighthearted departure from his usual science content, celebrating creativity, resourcefulness, and family.

## Key Topics
- Sourcing and repurposing reclaimed mahogany from a found coffee table
- Brief history and current scarcity of mahogany in the UK
- Safety considerations when working with mahogany (dust, woodworm, rot)
- Step-by-step construction of a wooden toy cement lorry
- Child safety in toy design (finish choice, no metal fasteners)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Mahogany** — primary timber, salvaged from an old coffee table; noted as historically common but now scarce in the UK
- **Broom handle** — used as axle material
- **Dowels** — used throughout as fasteners instead of screws or nails
- **22 mm skater bearing** — inserted into the cement drum to allow it to spin
- **8 mm bolt and olive fitting** — used to mount the drum on its support so it rotates freely
- **Tung oil** — chosen as finish because it is food-safe (used in salad bowls), appropriate for a toddler's toy
- **Light wood** — used for bumpers

## Techniques and Methods
- Salvaging and dimensioning timber from reclaimed furniture
- Hole-saw cutting (100 mm) to produce wheel discs and rings for the cement drum
- Doweling and gluing as the sole fastening method (no screws or nails)
- Sanding axle holes for a loose, rotating fit
- Clamping and doweling multiple rings together to form the drum barrel
- Pressing a skater bearing into the drum end cap for free rotation
- Finishing with tung oil for a child-safe, food-grade surface
