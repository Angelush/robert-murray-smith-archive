## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith presents a biodegradable plastic composite made from casein (milk protein), hemp fibers, and tea — developed as a viable alternative to conventional petroleum-based plastics. The material solves key historical problems with milk-based plastics (water sensitivity, formaldehyde curing) by using tea as a curing agent and graphene as an additive, resulting in a waterproof, thermoplastic, hemp-reinforced material he is actively seeking commercial partners to develop.
