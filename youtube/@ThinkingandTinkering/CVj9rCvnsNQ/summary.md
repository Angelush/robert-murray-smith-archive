## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith examines the KBY DISP KD21C controller fitted to the ESKUTE Voyager e-bike, walking through its multi-level menu system in detail. He demonstrates how to navigate the controller's Nokia-style hierarchical menu to access settings including speed limits, throttle control, and various system parameters. The video serves as a practical guide for understanding and configuring the controller, with a note that modifications to speed limits or throttle are intended for off-road or non-UK-road use only.

## Key Topics
- Overview of the KD21C controller display and button layout
- Navigating the multi-level menu system (top level, general settings, system settings)
- Changing assist levels (1–5) and using push-assist mode (6 km/h walk mode)
- Toggling backlight, clearing trip distance, and switching between mph and km/h
- Speed limit settings (maximum 40 km/h hardware-encoded)
- Enabling/disabling throttle control (HL-Y / HL-N setting)
- Installing a thumb throttle on the handlebars
- Password protection for the power button
- UK legal context for e-bikes (throttle limited to 5 km/h, pedal assist to 25 km/h, motor max 250W)

## Techniques and Methods
- Navigating a hierarchical button-based menu (press-and-hold sequences to descend menu levels)
- Changing unit display between mph and km/h via the U-value setting
- Enabling throttle control via the HND system settings submenu
- Physical throttle installation: removing grip, sliding on throttle unit, fastening nut, replacing grip cover, and plugging into pre-wired connector
- Adjusting wheel diameter, speed limit, over-current protection, and pedal assist sensitivity settings
