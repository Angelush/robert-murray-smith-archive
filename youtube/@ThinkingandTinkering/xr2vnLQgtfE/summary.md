## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make a dip pen and inkwell from an old aluminium drinks can, a chopstick or stick, tape, scissors, and a small piece of wet and dry sandpaper. The video was inspired by a viewer comment claiming the only materials available in their area were old cans and bed springs. Robert presents this as a genuinely novel craft he hadn't seen elsewhere — simple, fun, and capable of producing a surprisingly functional calligraphy pen.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing everyday scrap materials (drinks cans) into functional objects
- Step-by-step construction of a dip pen nib from aluminium sheet
- Using the base of the can as an inkwell
- Potential for gifting — making a boxed calligraphy set as a present
- Nib shape variations and how they affect line thickness

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Aluminium drinks can** (e.g., a Coke can) — primary material for the pen nib and inkwell
- **Masking tape** — used to secure the nib to the stick handle
- **Chopstick (or any stick)** — used as the pen handle
- **Wet and dry sandpaper (P400 grit)** — used to smooth/sharpen the nib edge
- **Ink** — poured into the can-bottom inkwell for dipping

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting the bottom off a drinks can to form an inkwell
- Cutting a rectangular sheet from the can body
- Folding and pinching the metal sheet to form a nib shape
- Wrapping the nib base around the stick and securing with tape
- Cutting the nib to a knife/tapered profile
- Sanding the nib tip with wet and dry paper to refine the writing edge
- Varying the nib angle while writing to produce thick or thin lines (calligraphic effect)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Safety is part of the fun:** Robert repeatedly jokes that "if you haven't got a scar or two, you're not trying" — acknowledging the sharp aluminium edges while keeping a light tone.
- **Cost comparison:** A commenter (Richard) told him that brass nibs of the same style cost around £10 in UK shops — Robert was genuinely surprised and delighted by this.
- **Oak galls mentioned:** He replies that he was "going out to collect some galls in the morning" — suggesting he was also exploring making traditional iron gall ink to pair with the pen.
- **Accessibility was the point:** He notes he "tried for something no one could say they couldn't make" — confirming the deliberate philosophy of extreme accessibility behind the video.
- **Scrapyard video interest:** He expresses interest in doing a video at a scrapyard but needs one willing to allow filming.
- **Bed springs aside:** He confirms he was also mulling over doing something with bed springs (the other material mentioned by the original commenter).
- **Homeschool use:** He agrees with a commenter that this would make a great homeschool project.
- **Philosophy on exploration:** "One thing always leads to another — a door opens on six others — who knows where you go when you explore and it really doesn't matter what you explore."
