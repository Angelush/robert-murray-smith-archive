## Overview
This short update video demonstrates the Ugrinsky turbine design being tested with water, following a community comment from Stephen Evans revealing that the Ugrinsky device was originally designed as a hydroelectric turbine before being adapted for wind. Robert sets up a simple cradle and tests the turbine with both a bucket dump and a water spray, confirming it spins effectively under both conditions. The video raises new questions about deploying the design in river currents and ocean waves.

## Key Topics
- The historical origin of the Ugrinsky turbine as a hydroelectric device, not a wind turbine
- The 1950 Russian book *Free Flow Hydroelectric Power Plants of Low Power*
- A Russian patent (available in English) describing the Ugrinsky turbine for domestic power generation
- Live water tests: bucket pour and spray nozzle
- The flywheel effect from the turbine's end-circle discs
- Potential future applications: rivers, waves, above a rocket stove
- Encouragement to read the comments section for community ideas

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Plastic turbine model (handmade, used for testing)

## Techniques and Methods
- Bucket-pour test: dumping water directly onto the turbine to test mass-flow response
- Spray test: directing a water jet at the turbine to simulate pressurised flow
- Patent searching (Google Patents cited in comments as a useful resource with good citation trails)
- Referencing translated Russian academic and patent literature
