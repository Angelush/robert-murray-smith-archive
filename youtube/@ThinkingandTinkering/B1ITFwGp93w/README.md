## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how his conductive ink responds to magnetic fields, aligning itself along field lines that become permanently set once the ink dries. He explores the implications of this behaviour — particularly directional conductivity — and discusses potential applications in electronics, energy harvesting, and antenna design. The video serves as a general introduction to magnetic manipulation of the ink, separate from his SWAG generator project.
