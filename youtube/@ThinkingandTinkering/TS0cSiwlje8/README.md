## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the "Air Loom" (Heirloom) wind turbine, a flexible and compact wind energy device inspired by sailing and wing sail technology. Drawing a line from ancient sailing through modern land yachts and rigid wing sails, he examines how these aerodynamic principles are being applied to renewable energy. He concludes that while the Heirloom is genuinely exciting, its complex control systems may be unnecessary — rigid wing sailboats are already self-trimming — and he sees clear avenues for DIY experimentation and improvement.
