## Overview
Robert 3D prints Pelton wheel buckets using an Elegoo Mars 2B Pro resin printer and mounts them on a pseudo-levitating magnetic bearing (built in a prior video) to create a miniature hydroelectric generator. Running water through the wheel at partial tap pressure, he generates approximately 6 volts and 100 milliamps from a small coil, demonstrating a fast-spinning, effective water motor. The video is part of his ongoing water motor series.

## Key Topics
- 3D printing Pelton wheel buckets using resin SLA printing
- Assembly of a Pelton wheel using printed buckets and an acrylic disc
- Pseudo-levitating magnetic bearings (cross-reference to video 1450)
- Hydroelectric power generation using a small coil and magnets
- Practical performance results from the completed assembly

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- ABS-like resin — used for printing the Pelton wheel buckets due to impact resistance
- Acrylic disc — used as the base/rotor disc for the buckets
- Ring magnet — attached to the underside of the rotor for the magnetic bearing
- Speaker magnet — core component of the magnetic bearing (from video 1450)
- Small neodymium magnets — attached to the rotor for electromagnetic induction
- Induction coil — positioned near the rotor to generate electricity

## Techniques and Methods
- SLA/resin 3D printing (Elegoo Mars 2B Pro) for complex curved bucket geometry
- Pseudo-magnetic levitation bearing assembly using a speaker magnet
- Electromagnetic induction via rotating magnets past a stationary coil
- Partial tap-pressure water jetting to control rotor speed and avoid over-spinning
