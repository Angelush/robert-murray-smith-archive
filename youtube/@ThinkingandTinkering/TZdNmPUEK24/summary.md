## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith takes his ADO A20 folding e-bike on a day trip around London, traveling from Paddington through the West End, Trafalgar Square, and Piccadilly Circus. He demonstrates how the bike's foldable design makes it practical for city exploration — fitting in train luggage racks and even being brought inside restaurants. His main takeaway is that an e-bike gives a far richer experience of London than either walking or the Underground.

## Key Topics
- Using the ADO A20 folding e-bike for urban exploration in London
- Practicality of foldable e-bike design for travel (trains, restaurants)
- Comparing e-bike travel to walking and taking the tube
- Battery life performance during a full day of city riding
- St. Martin-in-the-Fields crypt café as a lunch stop near Trafalgar Square

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirms the bike is operated on **pedal assist**, as required by UK law.
- His lunch at the St. Martin-in-the-Fields crypt was **soup and bread and butter pudding** — his favourite there.
- The return train ticket cost **£37 off-peak**.
- Notes that **e-bikes are now pretty ubiquitous in London**.
- Expressed that he enjoys visiting London but is clear he prefers it as a visit rather than to live there ("just to visit lol").
