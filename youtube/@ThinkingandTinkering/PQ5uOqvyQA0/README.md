## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith provides a concise history of mechanical timekeeping — from water clocks and escapements to the balance wheel — before explaining the tourbillon: a rotating cage invented by Abraham-Louis Breguet in 1801 to counteract the effect of gravity on a pocket watch's balance wheel and spring. He then walks through his own 3D-printed tourbillon design, assembles it on camera, and connects it to the spring motor he built in video 2381 to demonstrate a complete wind-up clock mechanism. The video is explicitly framed as a teaching aid to help viewers understand the principles, not as a blueprint to copy exactly.
