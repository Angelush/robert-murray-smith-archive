## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple wind turbine generator using a repurposed PC fan motor and a recycled CD as the blade assembly. He cuts the CD into 16 segments, heats the polycarbonate with a heat gun to twist the blades to approximately 35 degrees, and glues the assembly onto a previously converted PC fan motor. The completed turbine successfully lights an LED and produces around 2 volts from an artificial wind source, proving the concept as a viable scrap-materials project.
