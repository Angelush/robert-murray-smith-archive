## Overview
Robert introduces "builders board" — a cellular PVC foam board commonly used in roofline construction (fascias and soffits) — as an exceptionally versatile and easy-to-work model-making material. He covers its physical properties, available sizes, suppliers, and adhesive options, then demonstrates its use by building a functional lab beam balance from scratch.

## Key Topics
- What builders board (cellular foam board) is and where it comes from (roofline/building trade)
- Physical structure: polyurethane foam core with soft white PVC laminate outer face and tougher reverse laminate
- Available sizes: 3mm–15mm thick, 10mm–400mm wide; flat, perforated, L-shaped, and profiled (skirting) variants
- Main suppliers: Eurocell and Flooplast (also available on eBay)
- Quality considerations: voiding and end swelling in cheaper boards
- Cutting methods and ease of working
- Bonding with cyanoacrylate glue (with optional hardener)
- Step-by-step construction of a functional beam balance as a demonstration project
- Health and safety note regarding polyurethane dust
- Cleaning with uPVC cleaner (e.g., Ton10 cleaner)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Builders board / cellular foam board** — the primary subject; cellular PVC foam board used in roofline construction
- **Polyurethane foam** — the core material of the board (note: dust may be hazardous)
- **Soft white PVC laminate** — outer face of the board
- **Cyanoacrylate glue (superglue / crazy glue)** — primary adhesive for bonding pieces
- **Cyanoacrylate hardener / accelerator** — applied to one surface to give an instant bond
- **uPVC cleaner / Ton10 cleaner** — used to clean and restore the board's surface
- **Brass pins** — used in the balance beam pan construction
- **Carpet knife blades** — used as the pivot knife-edge for the beam balance
- **Curtain hooks** — used to hang the balance pans
- **Metal washer** — used to fine-tune the balance

## Techniques and Methods
- Marking out with a square, Sharpie marker, and ruler
- Cutting with a carpet knife (4–5 scoring passes through 9mm board), handsaw, or circular saw
- Bonding with cyanoacrylate glue; optional hardener for instant cure; bond stronger than the base material
- Planning models from a "net" perspective (thinking in flat cut pieces before assembly)
- Gluing uprights and base sections sequentially then adding functional components
- Balancing/trimming a beam balance by sliding a washer along the beam to correct off-centre balance
