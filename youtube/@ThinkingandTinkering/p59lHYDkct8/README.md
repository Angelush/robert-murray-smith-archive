## Overview

This video demonstrates a simple home method for testing whether a car thermostat is functioning correctly before spending money on a replacement. The presenter submerges an old thermostat in near-boiling water and observes whether it opens as designed; the tested unit fails to retract, confirming it is faulty. The key takeaway is that a working thermostat should open at its rated temperature (in this case ~92°C), and failure to do so explains symptoms like engine overheating or coolant leaks.
