## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores hidden and experimental features within the Cura 3D printing slicer, focusing on the "wire printing" mode — a technique that prints only the wireframe skeleton of a model, inspired by early 3D pens. He explains that wire printing was removed from Cura 5.2 but remains accessible in earlier versions such as the Elegoo Cura build (based on Cura 4.8), and offers practical advice on how to get better results from it.

## Key Topics
- History of 3D pens and wireframe model printing (originating ~2012 with Oodle)
- Cornell University's 2014 "wire print" research and its influence
- Wire printing feature in Cura: where to find it, when it was removed (v5.2), and where it still exists (Elegoo Cura v4.8)
- Accessing Expert/Experimental settings in Cura via the settings stack icon
- Other hidden Cura experimental features: fuzzy skin and spiralize/vase mode
- Practical tips for improving wire print quality (larger nozzle, higher layer height, higher temperature)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- PLA Plus filament — mentioned as a modern material better suited to wire printing than older options

## Techniques and Methods
- **Wire printing** — printing only the structural wireframe of a model, extruding once per pass; works best with a larger nozzle (0.8 mm or 1.2 mm) and coarser layer height (0.8 mm)
- **Fuzzy skin** — experimental Cura mode that adds a textured/fluffy surface finish
- **Spiralize (vase mode)** — prints a single continuous spiral loop per layer to create thin-walled objects
- **Accessing Expert settings** — clicking the settings stack icon in Cura and selecting "Expert" to reveal the Experimental section
