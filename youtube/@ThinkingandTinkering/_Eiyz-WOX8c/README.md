## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains how the anisotropic electrical properties of graphene ink can be exploited to build a thermoelectric generator. He describes how layering sheets of unidirectionally-painted plastic or paper at an angle, with electrodes at each end and a heat differential applied across the stack, forces electrons along a differential path and generates current. The video is a conceptual explanation prompted by a viewer question, rather than a hands-on demonstration.
