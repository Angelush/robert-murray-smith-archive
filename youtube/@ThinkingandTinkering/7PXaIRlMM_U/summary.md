## Overview
This video is the second stage of a four-part project to create graphene-reinforced plastic. Jeremy, a civil engineer visiting Robert's open lab, demonstrates how to synthesise graphene oxide from graphite using the standard Hummer's method. Robert notes that while Hummer's is quick (a few hours), he personally prefers the Tour method for its higher yield, despite taking a couple of days.

## Key Topics
- Context within a 4-stage graphene-reinforced plastic project (stage 1 was making plastic from urine)
- Graphene oxide synthesis via Hummer's method performed by a non-chemist first-timer
- Step-by-step reagent addition with temperature control
- Comparison of Hummer's method vs Tour method (yield, time, reagents)
- Post-synthesis processing: sedimentation, washing, and drying
- End goal: reducing graphene oxide back to graphene and mixing with plastic

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphite** — 10 g, the starting material
- **Sulfuric acid** — 230 mL, cooled to 0°C before use; sourced from one-shot drain cleaner (e.g. Home Depot); Robert clarified in comments it is 96% concentration
- **Sodium nitrate** — 5 g; available from garden centres as fertiliser
- **Potassium permanganate** — ~30 g total, added 1 g at a time to avoid exothermic runaway; available from local chemists
- **Deionised water** — added after the 35°C heating stage; available from auto stores
- **Hydrogen peroxide (3%)** — ~360 mL, added at the end; available from pharmacies (also used as contact lens cleaner, wound treatment, hair dye, and dental bleaching)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Hummer's method** — acid-based chemical oxidation of graphite to graphene oxide
- **Temperature control** — mixture kept below 20°C during initial mixing; raised to 35°C for ~30 min stirring; then heated to 98°C for 15 min after deionised water addition
- **Incremental reagent addition** — potassium permanganate added ~1 g at a time while continuously stirring to prevent explosive exothermic reaction
- **Hydrogen peroxide quench** — full 3% H₂O₂ bottle added to lighten the suspension and complete oxidation
- **Sedimentation** — product left to settle; graphene oxide/graphite mix sinks, waste remains in suspension
- **Washing and drying** — final purification step referenced via a separate video on Robert's graphene playlist
