## Overview

This video is a two-month follow-up to video #989, in which conductive ink was applied as a thermal paste on a CPU. Presenter Luke runs three stress tests to assess whether the conductive ink is still performing adequately and finds that temperatures have actually *improved* compared to the previous measurement video (#1006). The conclusion is that the conductive ink appears to get slightly better as a thermal interface material over time, though the reason is not fully understood.
