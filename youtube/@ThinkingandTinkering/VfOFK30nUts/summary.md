## Overview
This video is a two-month follow-up to video #989, in which conductive ink was applied as a thermal paste on a CPU. Presenter Luke runs three stress tests to assess whether the conductive ink is still performing adequately and finds that temperatures have actually *improved* compared to the previous measurement video (#1006). The conclusion is that the conductive ink appears to get slightly better as a thermal interface material over time, though the reason is not fully understood.

## Key Topics
- Follow-up testing of conductive ink used as CPU thermal paste (original test: video #989)
- Comparison of current temperatures against previous results from video #1006
- Observation that thermal performance appears to improve over time
- Plan to run the setup for a full six-month period before changing anything

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Conductive ink** — used as a substitute for conventional thermal paste between CPU and cooler

## Techniques and Methods
- **Prime95 torture test** — maximum CPU stress benchmark to measure peak temperatures
- **3DMark benchmark** — GPU-focused test that also loads the CPU; used for thermal comparison
- **Video rendering (OpenShot)** — real-world CPU workload used to observe sustained temperatures under moderate load
- **Ambient temperature measurement** — room temperature recorded at 14.6 °C before testing to establish a baseline
