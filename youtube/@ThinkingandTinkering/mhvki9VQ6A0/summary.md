## Overview
Robert demonstrates a rainwater-powered micro-hydro experiment using a solar-powered pump, a 3D-printed Turgo impeller, and a recycled motor to try to light an LED. The experiment fails due to insufficient pump pressure from the solar panel, but the underlying concept is validated by spinning the motor with a drill and successfully lighting the LED. Robert frames it as a honest "fail" video, emphasizing the concept is sound even if this implementation fell short.

## Key Topics
- Micro-hydro power generation using a Turgo (turbine) impeller
- Solar-powered water pump driving a closed-loop rainwater system
- Upcycling and recycling components from old printers and 3D printing
- Validating electronics (motor, bridge rectifier, LED) independently of the failed water system
- Lessons learned about pump power requirements for micro-hydro setups

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Rainwater (as the working fluid)
- None (no chemicals or specific materials beyond components listed below)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing a nozzle/impeller for the Turgo turbine
- Closed-loop water recirculation using buckets to avoid water waste
- Bridge rectifier circuit to convert AC motor output to DC for LED
- Using a handheld drill as a substitute power source to isolate and test the electrical system independently
