## Overview
Robert demonstrates his DIY large rotary tumble cleaner in action, showing how it cleans rusty and dirty metal parts salvaged from a lawn mower being converted into an electric vehicle. The cleaner uses a food-grade 30-litre drum filled with gravel, metal parts, and a homemade acidic cleaning solution, tumbling for about an hour to produce clean, bright metal.

## Key Topics
- Rotary tumble cleaner build and operation (referencing a previous build video)
- Cleaning metal parts from a lawn mower EV conversion project
- Homemade "magic cleaning solution" recipe
- Post-cleaning rust bloom and how to prevent it
- Motor power and drive mechanism considerations for scaling the design

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Food-grade 30-litre drum** — the main tumbling vessel, purchased on eBay
- **Gravel** — used as tumbling media alongside the metal parts
- **Vinegar (400 ml)** — acidic component of the cleaning solution
- **Salt (2 tablespoons)** — cleaning solution ingredient
- **IPA / Isopropyl Alcohol (100 ml)** — cleaning solution ingredient
- **Washing up liquid (20 ml)** — cleaning solution ingredient
- **WD-40** — applied post-clean to protect unpainted metal parts
- **Oil** — additional protective coating for bright metal parts
- **Primer and paint** — used immediately after cleaning on parts to be painted, to prevent rust bloom

## Techniques and Methods
- Tumble/rotary cleaning: loading parts with gravel and cleaning solution into a sealed rotating drum
- Friction drive mechanism to rotate the drum (with notes on upgrading to pulley/belt or cog/gear for heavier loads)
- Post-clean wipe-down and rinse to remove acidic residue
- Immediate priming and painting to prevent flash rust on cleaned parts
- Polishing + WD-40 + oil treatment for parts that will remain bare metal
