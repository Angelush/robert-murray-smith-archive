## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents an evolution of his powdered foam soap formula, now compressed into a convenient tablet form. The tablet dissolves in water (hot water speeds up the process) to create a clear foaming handwash solution. He demonstrates its effectiveness by cleaning graphite-stained hands without the scrubbing and soreness associated with concentrated washing-up liquid.

## Key Topics
- Tablet-form compressed soap as a user-friendly alternative to powdered foam soap
- Dissolution process: drop tablet in water, wait 5–10 minutes, stir to speed up
- Practical demonstration using graphite as a heavy-duty dirt/stain
- Comparison with concentrated washing-up liquid as the previous cleaning method
- Skin-softening additive included in the formula

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Compressed soap tablet** — the main product; same formula as his powdered foam soap, pressed into tablet form
- **Graphite** — used as a demonstration contaminant to show cleaning power
- **Concentrated washing-up liquid** — referenced as the previous (harsher) cleaning method for graphite
- **Water** — solvent; tap water to near-boiling all acceptable

## Techniques and Methods
- Tablet compression of a powdered soap formula
- Cold/hot water dissolution (hotter water dissolves faster, ~5–10 minutes)
- Optional stirring or breaking the tablet to accelerate dissolution
- Direct skin application and working the foam into contaminants before rinsing
