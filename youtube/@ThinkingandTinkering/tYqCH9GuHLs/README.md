## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith delivers a motivational talk on the critical importance of building physical models when you have an idea, rather than remaining in the theoretical stage. He contrasts two people he is mentoring — an intern who built a cardboard prototype and is thriving, and another person who only has a paper idea and resists getting started. His core message is that the gap between idea and reality is only bridged by hands-on building, and that the real barrier is fear, not lack of skill or money.
