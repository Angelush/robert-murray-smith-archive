## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith introduces the EcoFoam Hard Surface Cleaner, part of his growing "EcoFoam" product line concept. The core idea is to eliminate the waste and expense of shipping water-heavy liquid cleaners by instead providing concentrated powder or tablet form that the user dissolves in 250ml of water at home. He demonstrates the cleaner's impressive ability to remove graphite and molybdenum grease from surfaces and hands with minimal effort.

## Key Topics
- The EcoFoam product line philosophy: reducing water, chemicals, and landfill waste
- Powder/tablet concentrate format — add water yourself to make the product
- Hard surface cleaning applications: worktops, cupboard doors, PVC, floors, paintwork, window ledges
- Live demonstrations of cleaning graphite, molybdenum grease, and mixed grease/graphite stains
- Comparison to conventional cleaners (implied superiority for stubborn grease)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **EcoFoam Hard Surface Cleaner powder** — the product being demonstrated; 6g per use
- **Graphite** — used as a test stain on a cupboard door surface
- **Molybdenum grease** — a heavy black industrial grease used as a challenging test stain
- **Mixed graphite and molybdenum grease** — combined to simulate a worst-case dirty/greasy stain

## Techniques and Methods
- Dissolving 6g of powder in 250ml water in two stages (add half first to control foaming, stir, then add the rest)
- Applying foam directly to a cloth or surface, then rubbing gently to lift grease and grime
- Rinsing residue with a cloth after foam application
- Demonstrating hand-cleaning by working foam directly into heavily greased hands
