## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a wooden model of a scotch yoke — a mechanism that converts rotary motion to linear motion — and then reveals his own novel modification: a rack-and-pinion selector that allows the throw (stroke length) to be varied continuously from zero to maximum while the mechanism is in motion. He proposes the design as a potential basis for a CVT (continuously variable transmission) and plans to upload the files to Thingiverse.

## Key Topics
- Basic operation of the scotch yoke (rotary-to-linear and linear-to-rotary conversion)
- Common applications of the scotch yoke (jigsaws, hedge trimmers, carving knives, actuators, engines)
- Assembly walkthrough of the 3D-printed adjustable variant
- Rack-and-pinion selector mechanism for adjusting throw
- Relationship to CVT (continuously variable transmission) design, specifically the "Max Zero" eccentric-based CVT concept
- Plans to publish the design on Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Wood (for the demonstration model)
- Glue (used during assembly of the 3D-printed parts)

## Techniques and Methods
- Rack-and-pinion mechanism used to laterally shift a pin, changing the eccentricity of the scotch yoke
- Pin-and-slot assembly for the adjustable throw component
- Cover plate glued over the pin recess to retain components
- Selector fork engagement for adjusting throw while the mechanism rotates
- 3D printing (implied by the precision plastic parts and Thingiverse publication)

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's replies in the comments are mostly brief acknowledgements ("cheers mate", "Glad you enjoyed it"). A few notable substantive points:

- **Backdrivability**: He confirms a normal scotch yoke can be backdriven and sees no reason the adjustable version couldn't be either, expressing approval of that line of thinking.
- **Adjustment while moving**: He confirms the throw can be adjusted while the mechanism is in motion.
- **Related content**: He mentions he has done many videos on connecting wires and generators, apparently in response to a question about power generation.
- **Community designs**: He expresses genuine enthusiasm about seeing what others build with the mechanism, asking people to give him a heads-up if they make something based on it.
