## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith presents the results of a second experiment in which a lump of raw coal was subjected to a low-cost, catalyst-free and metal-free process that transformed it from jet-black, poorly-conductive coal into a metallic silver, highly-conductive material consistent with graphite. Resistance dropped from roughly 3 megaohms down to 6 ohms, and the transformation penetrated all the way to the center of a large lump. He concludes the results are exciting but emphasizes that elemental analysis and crystallinity testing are still needed to fully characterize the product.
