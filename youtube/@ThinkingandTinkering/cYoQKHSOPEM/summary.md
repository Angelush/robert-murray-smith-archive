## Overview
This short tutorial demonstrates how to heat-bend plastic into precise right angles using a simple wooden jig and a heat gun. The presenter (Luke) explains the technique as a superior alternative to bending aluminium, which proved difficult to work with cleanly. The result is a crisp, accurate bend that fits the intended project component far better than the metal attempt.

## Key Topics
- Why plastic was chosen over aluminium for a fabrication project
- Building a simple wooden jig to ensure a consistent right-angle bend
- Heating plastic to malleability with a heat gun
- Timing and technique to avoid over-softening the plastic
- Comparing the fit and quality of the bent plastic vs. a bent aluminium piece

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Plastic sheet (type unspecified) — the primary material being bent
- Aluminium sheet — shown as a failed/rejected alternative due to dents and difficulty achieving clean angles
- Wood (three pieces) — used to construct a simple bending jig

## Techniques and Methods
- Constructing a three-piece wooden jig with square edges and a flat plank to guide the bend
- Using a heat gun to bring the plastic to malleability
- Pressing the heated plastic against the flat edge of the jig to achieve a right-angle bend
- Allowing the plastic to cool and set in position
