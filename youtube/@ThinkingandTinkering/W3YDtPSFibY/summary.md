## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to convert a cheap 12-pound heated car seat cover into a mains-powered heated chair by rewiring it to a 12V 5A power supply. The hack is simple, low-cost, and produces a 36-watt resistive heater for back and seat warming. He treats the heating element as a basic resistor and notes the wiring polarity doesn't matter.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing a 12-pound Amazon heated car seat cover for home use
- Wiring a 12V DC power supply to replace the cigarette lighter plug
- Safe enclosure of bare wire connections using a "chuckbox"
- Basic electrical calculations: 12V ÷ 4Ω = 3A → 36W

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Heated car seat cover (12V, resistive, ~4 Ohm element) — the heat source
- 5A 12V DC power supply — replacement for the cigarette lighter adapter
- Chuckbox (wire enclosure) — used to safely contain the spliced connection

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting and stripping the cigarette lighter plug and power supply cable
- Joining wires by twisting, crimping, or soldering (all mentioned as valid options)
- Enclosing the connection in a chuckbox and screwing it shut for safety
