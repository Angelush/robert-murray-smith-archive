## Overview
This video demonstrates how to build a simple DIY camera stabilizer by attaching a weighted pole to the bottom of a tripod. The presenter constructs the device using a pole with approximately 4 kg of weights and shows a side-by-side comparison proving it significantly reduces camera shake. The physics behind the stabilizer — pendulum effect, inertia, and pivot point placement — are briefly explained.

## Key Topics
- Motivation for building a low-cost camera stabilizer
- Construction of a weighted pole that clips onto a tripod
- Side-by-side video comparison of stabilized vs. unstabilized handheld shots
- Physics principles explaining why the design works: pendulum effect, inertia, and pivot point

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Metal or plastic pole (used as the stabilizer arm)
- Weights (approximately 4 kg total, slid over the pole)
- Tape (used to create a snugger fit between the pole and tripod clip)

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling holes in the top of the pole to interface with the tripod's quick-release button
- Wrapping tape around the connection point for a tighter fit
- Side-by-side video comparison to evaluate stabilization performance
