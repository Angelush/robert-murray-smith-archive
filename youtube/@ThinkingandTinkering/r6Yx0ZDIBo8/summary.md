## Overview
Robert introduces triboelectric nanogenerators (TENGs) as a wind energy harvesting technology, explaining how rubbing or vibrating materials in the wind generates electricity. He argues this approach could complement traditional wind turbines — especially at low wind speeds — and that recent academic research makes DIY, large-scale versions feasible using simple everyday materials.

## Key Topics
- What triboelectricity is and how it works (electron transfer via contact/rubbing)
- Connection to his previous raindrop generator project
- Two main approaches to wind-driven TENGs: fluttering/flapping and rotating discs
- The Canadian FLAG project (Flexible Linear Aerostatic Generator)
- Wind belt devices using vibrating bands
- Rotating disc triboelectric generators
- Translating academic lab results into practical, scalable DIY builds
- Google Scholar as a resource for TENG research
- The broader philosophy: diverse energy solutions for diverse living conditions
- Energy storage via capacitors and battery banks

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene)** — used as a triboelectric material in vibrating band generators
- **Kapton** — paired with PTFE to generate high charge and current
- **Aluminium** — deposited as a conductive layer on plastic substrates
- **Plastic (general)** — used for layered structures in the FLAG project and rotating disc devices
- **Ink / varnish** — applied as functional layers in printed generator construction

## Techniques and Methods
- **Fluttering/flapping TENG** — two or more layers of dissimilar plastic materials strung together and allowed to flutter in wind, generating charge through repeated contact
- **Rotating disc TENG** — two electrode discs with a small gap spin in the wind, with plastic elements rubbing together; no electromagnetic component means very low friction and usable at low wind speeds
- **Rectification circuit** — used to convert AC triboelectric output to usable DC
- **Variable capacitor construction** — three-layer plastic structure acting as the core of the FLAG generator circuit
- **Silk screen / inkjet printing** — proposed as a scalable fabrication route for large-area TENGs
- **Capacitor + battery bank storage** — recommended method for accumulating the harvested energy
