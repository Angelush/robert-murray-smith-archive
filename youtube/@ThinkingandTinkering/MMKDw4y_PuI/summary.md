## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith visits the Deal Time Ball in England as a thematic starting point for exploring mechanical power transmission via chains. He demonstrates a 3D-printed chain of his own design, remixed from Thingiverse, and shows a surprising phenomenon: when spun at speed on a cog, a free-hanging chain appears to levitate and self-align to drive a second cog — a curious emergent mechanical behaviour he calls the "floating or levitating transmission."

## Key Topics
- The Deal Time Ball as a historical example of precision timekeeping and mechanical lifting
- Chains vs. rope drives for power transmission
- The history and versatility of chain drives (excavators, chainsaws, roller blinds, bicycles)
- Robert's philosophy on engineering as "the organisation of matter into life"
- 3D-printed chain design: remixing a Thingiverse file for easier assembly (clip-together links)
- The levitating/floating transmission effect when a chain is spun at high speed on a cog

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- PLA or similar FDM plastic (implied by 3D printing context)

## Techniques and Methods
- FDM 3D printing of interlocking chain links
- Remixing/redesigning an existing Thingiverse model for clip-together assembly
- High-speed rotation of a chain on a cog using a handheld drill to demonstrate the levitation effect
