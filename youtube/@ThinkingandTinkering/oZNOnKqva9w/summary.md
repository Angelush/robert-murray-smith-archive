## Overview
Robert builds a portable, foldable, near-silent DIY air conditioner by combining a USB PC fan motor fitted with a 3D-printed toroidal propeller with an insulated cool bag filled with ice. The key innovation over similar "bucket AC" builds is using a collapsible cool bag (instead of a rigid cool box) and a toroidal propeller to dramatically reduce noise. Testing shows the unit outputs air at 9°C against a 21°C ambient temperature.

## Key Topics
- Limitations of fans for cooling in hot weather (blowing hot air over the body)
- Recap of video 1952: toroidal propeller on a USB PC fan
- DIY "bucket ice AC" concept and its noise problem
- Using a cool bag instead of a rigid cool box for portability and storage
- 3D printing custom mounting parts in Tinkercad
- Extracting the motor from a PC fan by snipping out the blades and centre section
- Assembly: gluing and bolting printed parts to the cool bag, cutting air vents
- Live demonstration and temperature measurement

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Ice (cooling medium inside the bag)
- Cool/insulated bag (collapsible, foldable)
- USB-powered PC fan (motor salvaged from it)
- M4 bolts (×8, used to mount the retaining ring)
- 3D-printed toroidal propeller and mounting parts (PLA/filament implied)
- Calcium chloride — mentioned by Robert in comments as a potential alternative cooling medium to ice

## Techniques and Methods
- Toroidal propeller fabrication via 3D printing (Tinkercad design)
- PC fan disassembly: snipping blades and centre spokes to isolate the motor
- Gluing motor into 3D-printed housing, then bolting assembly to cool bag with M4 hardware
- Cutting cloth vents in the cool bag for airflow (inlet and two side outlets)
- Temperature measurement of output air vs. ambient to verify cooling performance

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Dry ice warning:** Strongly advises against using dry ice — "too high a chance of you killing yourself."
- **Calcium chloride:** Suggests it as an alternative cooling medium when someone asks about non-ice options ("no — but try calcium chloride").
- **Rubber/evaporative cooling devices:** Notes he looked into rubber-based cooling devices some years ago and found them "interesting but not very practical"; open to revisiting given recent developments.
- **Desiccant cooling:** Doesn't think a desiccant would work well but encourages experimentation.
- **TEG (thermoelectric generator):** Notes you "won't get a lot of power from a TEG in this setup."
- **Battery/power:** Clarifies the cooling medium doesn't have to be ice specifically — "just colder than the ambient."
- **Tinkercad resizing:** Advises users to modify the hole size in Tinkercad if the standard print doesn't fit their fan, and links a compatible Amazon fan.
- **Voltage/power:** If the fan doesn't run on USB (5V), suggests using 12V or getting a 5V-compatible unit.
- **Build philosophy:** "I try and make them build… I always tend to be heading somewhere and I think the best way is to introduce concepts through practical examples."
