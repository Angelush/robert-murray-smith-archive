## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds "The Wave," a novel-shaped longboard/skateboard whose deck design was inspired by a recumbent bike seat he made in video 1179. Rather than a standard flat deck, the board features organic curves formed by laminating thin plywood sheets over a curved cradle. The prototype is tested by Luke, a skateboarding-experienced collaborator, with plans to convert it into an electric longboard in a future video.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing a recumbent bike seat design as a longboard deck
- Using a printable PDF template to shape the side rails
- Curved deck construction via plywood lamination
- Assembly with wooden dowels and waterproof Gorilla Glue
- Fitting longboard trucks
- First test ride and iterative adjustments
- Future plans for electric conversion with an onboard battery compartment

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **7-ply, 22mm exterior grade plywood** — used for the two side rail frames (20cm × 84cm each)
- **3.6mm thin plywood** — three sheets laminated together to form the curved ~10–11mm deck
- **18mm wooden dowel** — cut to 16cm lengths to join the two side rails
- **Waterproof Gorilla Glue** — used throughout for all bonding; noted to expand and fill gaps
- **Longboard trucks** — standard hardware fitted to a plywood sub-plate under the deck

## Techniques and Methods
- Printing a multi-page PDF template and gluing it to plywood as a cutting guide
- Jigsaw cutting of shaped side rails (emphasis on using a sharp blade)
- Screwing two rail blanks together before drilling to ensure mirror-image hole alignment
- Dowel joinery to connect the two rails into a cradle structure
- Progressive plywood lamination: screwing each thin sheet to the curved cradle while glue dries, then repeating for subsequent layers — using the cradle itself as the mold
- Planing a 45-degree bevel on the inside edges of the rails to prevent wheel/deck rubbing during steering
