## Overview
A short Advent calendar science demonstration (Day 17) in which Robert adds food colouring to milk and then touches a soap-coated cotton swab to the surface, causing the colours to swirl dramatically. The experiment illustrates how soap disrupts the delicate balance between water, fat, proteins, and mineral salts in milk.

## Key Topics
- Advent calendar science series (Day 17)
- Surface tension and emulsion disruption in milk
- The role of soap as a surfactant

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Milk — the base medium, a mixture of water, fat, proteins, and mineral salts
- Food colouring — added to the milk surface to make the movement visible
- Dish soap — applied to a cotton swab; disrupts the balance of the milk emulsion
- Cotton swab — used to introduce the soap to the milk surface

## Techniques and Methods
- Adding drops of food colouring to a shallow dish of milk
- Dipping a soap-coated cotton swab into the milk to trigger the colour-mixing reaction
