## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith visits a historic chimney in Faversham to introduce the concept of the stack effect, then traces how this principle was reimagined around the year 2000 into a "vortex engine" — a device that uses tangentially-directed airflow to create a powerful rising vortex column. The video culminates in Robert presenting a quarter-scale 3D-printed prototype of a solar vortex engine based on a research paper, which uses focused sunlight to generate the hot zone and could feed a wind turbine to generate electricity with minimal emissions.
