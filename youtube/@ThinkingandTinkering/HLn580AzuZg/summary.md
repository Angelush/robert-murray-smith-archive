## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a second method for making "UltraSand" — graphite/graphene-coated sand for water filtration — using bitumen (asphalt) dissolved in toluene as the carbon source. The method is simpler and significantly cheaper to run than the sugar-based method because it requires only 3 hours at 400°C rather than 7 hours at 750°C. The resulting material is effective at removing organic contaminants, pathogens, pesticides, colorants, and some heavy metals such as arsenic.

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Builder's sand** — base material, grain size 200–500 µm
- **Bitumen / asphaltene** — carbon coating source; sourced from road works excavation, roofing asphalt, or bitumen primer paint
- **Toluene** — solvent used to dissolve bitumen and coat the sand
- **Activated carbon** — used as a covering layer during the kiln step to create a reducing/inert atmosphere
- **Concentrated sulfuric acid** — used to activate the finished coated sand (soak for ~30 minutes)

## Techniques and Methods
- Dissolving bitumen in toluene by shaking in a jar and decanting off the liquid
- Varying toluene/bitumen concentration ratios to experiment with carbon loading
- Slow-cooker drying: coating sand with bitumen-toluene solution, then stirring at ~90–95°C for 5 hours to evaporate solvent
- Kiln/oven heating: placing coated sand in a stainless steel container covered with activated carbon, ramping to 400°C over 1 hour, holding for 3 hours to graphitize the carbon coating
- Acid activation: soaking in concentrated sulfuric acid for 30 minutes, then drying at 120°C in an outdoor/sacrificial oven
- Deep-bed filtration: packing activated UltraSand into a column and allowing water to drip through

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Graphene vs. graphitized carbon conductivity**: The product has some conductivity due to graphitization, but not high because of grain boundaries; activated carbon alone is non-conductive. Raman spectroscopy and SEM are recommended to confirm graphene/graphite character.
- **Hydrothermal route skepticism**: Robert doubts that hydrothermal conditions alone would graphitize a carbon precursor, but considers it worth trying and asks anyone who attempts it to report back.
- **Toluene as solvent**: Confirms toluene is essentially the only practical easy-to-obtain solvent for dissolving asphalt.
- **Graphene oxide alternative**: For someone wanting to use graphene rather than graphitized bitumen, Robert recommends mixing graphene oxide with chitosan and hydrothermally carbonising at 180°C for ~18 hours in a normal pressure cooker.
- **Graphene oxide sourcing**: Updated the video description with a link to his online store where graphene oxide can be purchased.
- **Why UltraSand over plain activated carbon**: It is quicker, uses less material for equivalent filtration, and removes more contaminants from water.
