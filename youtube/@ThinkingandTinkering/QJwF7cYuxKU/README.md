## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores a wind turbine concept invented by Erasmus Darwin (1731–1802), polymath of the Midlands Enlightenment and grandfather of Charles Darwin. Darwin's design uses louvred vents arranged in a tower to channel wind upward through a single column, and Robert builds a working square model using off-the-shelf exhaust vents topped with a PC fan converted to a generator. The demonstration confirms the principle works — producing 2.5–3 volts and a few milliamps — and outperforms a bare fan because the tower captures a much larger sweep of air.
