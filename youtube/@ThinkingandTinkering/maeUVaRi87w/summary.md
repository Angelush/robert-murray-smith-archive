## Overview
This video is part of a series on building a Van de Graaff generator, focusing specifically on wiring the motor to the mains supply. Robert walks through the components required and demonstrates the step-by-step wiring process using UK electrical conventions, connecting a double pole switch, a dimmer switch, and a starting capacitor salvaged from the original donor machine.

## Key Topics
- Components required for the motor wiring assembly
- UK mains wiring colour conventions (brown = live, blue = neutral)
- Function and wiring of a double pole (DPDT) switch
- Function and wiring of a dimmer switch for motor speed control
- Role of the starting capacitor and how to connect it
- Cable gland installation for dust and water sealing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 0.75 mm stranded cable — used for internal wiring connections
- Grey mains cable with plug — used as the incoming power supply lead
- Double pole switch — disconnects both live and neutral for safety
- Dimmer switch (rated 800 W) — controls motor speed; switches live only
- Dual gang surface-mounted (patress) box — houses both switch plates
- Cable glands — seals cable entry points against dust and water
- Starting capacitor (non-polarised) — bridges live and neutral on the power-out side to start the motor; salvaged from the original donor machine

## Techniques and Methods
- Stripping and preparing cable ends: twisting strands together and folding in half to form a loop for more secure terminal connections
- Feeding neutral wire through both halves of the patress box to maintain a continuous neutral path
- Wiring a double pole switch with live-in/neutral-in on one side and live-out/neutral-out on the other
- Wiring a one-way dimmer switch (common = power in, L1 = power out) in series with the live only
- Bridging the starting capacitor across the live-out and neutral-out terminals
- Tug-testing all terminal connections to verify they are secure before closing up
