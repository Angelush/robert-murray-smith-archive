## Overview
This video demonstrates how to transfer an inkjet-printed image onto a piece of wood using varnish as a transfer medium. The presenter (Luke) walks through the full process step by step, including a mid-video correction after encountering air bubble problems on the first attempt. The final result is a rustic Spitfire print mounted in a silver frame, made as a Christmas gift.

## Key Topics
- Image transfer technique using varnish and inkjet prints onto wood
- Importance of printing images in mirror/reverse before transfer
- Fixing air bubble failures and refining the method
- Achieving a rustic, aged aesthetic through controlled paper removal
- Framing and finishing the final piece

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Interior varnish** — used as the transfer medium applied to the wood surface
- **Wood** — the substrate receiving the image
- **Inkjet-printed paper** — the image source (must be mirrored, inkjet-specific)
- **Water** — used to pre-soak the paper to prevent air bubbles
- **Masking tape** — applied to edges to keep varnish contained
- **Hot glue** — used to secure glass and frame to the finished piece

## Techniques and Methods
- Printing the image in mirror/reverse before transfer
- Applying masking tape to define transfer boundaries
- Spreading a thin coat of varnish and using a credit card/plastic scraper to remove air bubbles
- Pre-soaking the printed sheet in water (Pyrex glass of water) before laying it down
- Using a sponge to press out air bubbles and stretch the paper flat
- Removing paper backing with a stiff-bristled toothbrush after drying
- Using a hairdryer to dry, then re-wetting to safely remove stubborn paper layers
- Extra toothbrush rubbing to create a distressed/rustic look
