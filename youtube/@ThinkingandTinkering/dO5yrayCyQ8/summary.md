## Overview
This is the fourth entry in Robert Murray-Smith's "Impossible Gears" series, exploring how same-sized gears can still achieve a gear reduction. In this episode, he replaces the lever arm used in the original spur gear design with a rotating cage mechanism, demonstrating a clean 2:1 reduction using only spur gears. He assembles the 3D-printed mechanism on camera and verifies the ratio by marking dots on the input and output gears.

## Key Topics
- Continuation of the "Impossible Gears" series (episode 4)
- Achieving gear reduction with same-size spur gears (no lever arm)
- Rotating cage as the mechanism for counter-rotation
- Step-by-step assembly of a 3D-printed spur gear cage
- Demonstrating and verifying a 2:1 gear ratio
- Back-drivability of the mechanism
- Availability of the model on Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **3D-printed parts** — cage body (top and bottom), fixed gear, idle gears (×2), output gear, drive gear, supports, axle
- **Glue** — used to secure the axle/fixed gear assembly and optionally the press-fit supports

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing components for the gear assembly
- Press-fitting axles to a precise depth (6 mm protrusion noted)
- Gluing parts to constrain a gear as a fixed (stationary) reference gear
- Counter-rotation via a rotating cage enclosing the gear train
- Dot-marking gears to visually verify and demonstrate gear ratio
