## Overview
A hands-on tutorial video showing how to construct the body armature for a papier mache Madonna figure. Robert builds up the figure using newspaper cones, masking tape, and a central rod, working from a cast doll's head created in Part 1, and ends just as the paper mache covering stage is about to begin.

## Key Topics
- Refining the mould/cast head with sandpaper before use
- Proportioning the figure (8:1 body-to-head ratio for elegance)
- Constructing the body armature from rolled newspaper cones
- Shaping the dress, torso, and chest with newspaper and tissue
- Forming arms from folded and twisted paper
- Using masking tape to hold the armature together
- Classroom context: scaling the technique for groups of 30 students

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Newspaper (for cones, body bulk, and arms)
- Masking tape (primary binding material; noted as compatible with later glue)
- Tissue paper (for subtle chest shaping)
- Plasticine (mentioned in context of the Part 1 mould)
- Fibreglass rod / hanger wire / stick (central armature rod)
- Doll's head (used as the face cast from Part 1)

## Techniques and Methods
- Sanding the cast head to refine facial features and cheekbones
- Building a newspaper cone to form the flowing dress
- Inverting a second cone for the upper torso
- Padding and shaping with tissue for anatomical suggestion
- Wrapping and compressing newspaper forms with masking tape to hold shape
- Twisting and folding paper strips to form arms
- Sizing limbs by eye and trimming with scissors
