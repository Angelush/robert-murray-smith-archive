## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a homemade graphene-based conductive ink he has been developing, available in ceramic (sodium silicate binder), water-based, and waterproof variants. He measures the water-based ink's sheet resistance at approximately 29–30 ohms per square, comparing favourably to commercial products like Vink and Zink without requiring any high-temperature annealing step. His stated target is 20 ohms per square to compete directly with commercial conductive inks.

## Key Topics
- Progress update on ongoing graphene research and ink development
- Three ink formulations: ceramic (sodium silicate binder), water-based, and waterproof
- Sheet resistance measurement using a homemade four-point probe
- Comparison with commercial conductive inks (Vink, Zink) targeting ~20 ohms/square
- Selling the water-based ink on eBay
- Application of ceramic ink for induction heating (painting the bottom of a plate)
- Using the ink with a dip pen for drawing conductive traces

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene** — primary conductive component of the ink
- **Sodium silicate (water glass)** — binder used in the ceramic formulation
- **CMC (carboxymethyl cellulose)** — mentioned in comments as a possible thickening agent
- **Agar-agar** — mentioned in comments as an alternative thickening agent
- **PVA** — mentioned in comments as a valid binder, noted as not very abrasion- or water-resistant
- **IPA (isopropyl alcohol) / acetone** — mentioned in comments for surface cleaning
- **Copper foil** — referenced in comments as a comparison conductor

## Techniques and Methods
- Formulating conductive ink with a binder to prevent component separation and aid substrate adhesion
- Painting ink onto substrates with a brush
- Drawing conductive traces with a dip pen
- Sheet resistance measurement with a homemade probe (ohms per square)
- Induction heating test: painting ceramic ink onto the underside of a plate
- Air-drying without annealing (distinguishing feature vs. commercial inks)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Avoiding dry powder:** Recommends always working with graphene in solution or dispersed form to avoid handling hazards.
- **Binder choice:** Advises that binder selection depends on the intended use; PVA is valid but lacks abrasion and water resistance. CMC or agar-agar can be added as thickeners.
- **Probe clarification:** Confirms the measurement device shown is a homemade sheet resistance probe (not a standard multimeter), with stiff end pieces (he used lightning rod material).
- **Surface prep tip:** Suggests cleaning tape/substrate with IPA or acetone and lightly abrading before applying ink.
- **Resources:** Points viewers to his ink-making playlist and graphene-making videos on the channel; graphene/graphite materials available via his eBay store (rmsgraphite).
- **No adhesive plans:** No plans to produce a conductive adhesive product at the time of posting.
