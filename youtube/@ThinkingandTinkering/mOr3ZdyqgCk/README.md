## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a homemade graphene-based conductive ink he has been developing, available in ceramic (sodium silicate binder), water-based, and waterproof variants. He measures the water-based ink's sheet resistance at approximately 29–30 ohms per square, comparing favourably to commercial products like Vink and Zink without requiring any high-temperature annealing step. His stated target is 20 ohms per square to compete directly with commercial conductive inks.
