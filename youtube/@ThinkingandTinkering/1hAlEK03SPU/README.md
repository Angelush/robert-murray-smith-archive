## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a rebuilt analog brushless motor controller — originally designed in videos 1017 and 1019 — driving a prototype switched reluctance motor at 12 volts. The video was prompted by a viewer comment and shows how rotor geometry (specifically a "leading edge") determines the direction of rotation. The motor is explicitly a proof-of-concept prototype, held together with superglue, intended to validate design ideas before committing to a more robust build.
