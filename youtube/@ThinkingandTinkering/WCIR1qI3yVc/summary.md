## Overview
Robert demonstrates a modified batch of hemp plastic — this time made without graphene or plasticizer — which, while it cannot be injection moulded, can be hot-press moulded into dense, hard sheets and rods. He shows examples of a turned rod (machined on a lathe) and a 3mm plate, both remarkably tough, and flags that the next step is finding a plasticizer blend that enables injection moulding while retaining this hardness.

## Key Topics
- Distinction between "hemp converted into plastic" vs. "hemp added to plastic"
- Comparison of two hemp plastic formulations: with glycerol/Kraft plasticizer + graphene (injection mouldable but rubbery) vs. without (injection moulding fails but hot-press works)
- Hot-press moulding to produce sheet and rod stock
- Machining hemp plastic on a lathe
- Biodegradability and water-resistance properties
- Potential non-critical applications (door handles, light switch plates, knobs, feet, etc.)
- Next steps: finding an intermediate plasticizer level for injection moulding

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Hemp bast fibre** — the base feedstock, converted into the plastic matrix
- **Graphene** — used in the earlier batch as an additive (omitted in this batch)
- **Glycerol Kraft plasticizer** — used in the earlier batch; omitted here to test unplasticized properties
- **Clove oil** — mentioned in comments as a potentially useful additive
- **Bicarbonate of soda (bicarb) and silica** — mentioned in comments as additives worth investigating

## Techniques and Methods
- **Hot-press moulding** — heating material in a block mould and applying pressure to form sheets and rods
- **Injection moulding** — used on the earlier plasticized batch; produced a ring, described as rubbery
- **Lathe turning / machining** — used to machine the hot-pressed rod into a stepped profile
- **Composting / biodegradation testing** — referenced as the disposal/end-of-life method (compost heap, ~1–2 weeks)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Contact:** Provides his email for direct enquiries.
- **Additives:** Suggests clove oil as a surprisingly useful additive to explore.
- **Applications:** Enthusiastically endorses non-critical plastic replacement uses — door handles, light switch cover plates, knobs, and equipment feet (e.g., exercise bike parts) — noting the enormous volume of such items that end up as waste.
- **Durability anecdote:** An earlier trial version was "bulletproof" — they reportedly tested it by firing an AK-47 at it.
- **Caps/closures:** Advises against using this material for bottle caps, as the water-based environment would cause them to rot too quickly.
- **Bicarb and silica:** Acknowledges a commenter's suggestion about bicarbonate of soda and silica; says he hasn't done much testing with those yet and wants to improve the base material first.
- **Clarification on hemp plastics:** Explicitly states he is not dismissing other hemp plastic approaches — he is clarifying that his process is different.
