## Overview
A short festive science demonstration for day 12 of an advent calendar series, in which Robert builds a simple balloon-powered racing car from basic craft materials. The video illustrates Newton's Third Law of Motion — the car is propelled forward by the reaction force of air escaping from the balloon.

## Key Topics
- Newton's Third Law of Motion (every action has an equal and opposite reaction)
- Balloon-powered propulsion
- Simple vehicle construction from craft materials

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Balloon
- Straws
- Wheels (toy/craft wheels)
- Lolly (popsicle) sticks
- Rubber band

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting straws to length (~3 cm) to serve as axle housing
- Threading axles through straw sections and attaching wheels
- Affixing the balloon to the car body using a rubber band
- Inflating the balloon to propel the car
