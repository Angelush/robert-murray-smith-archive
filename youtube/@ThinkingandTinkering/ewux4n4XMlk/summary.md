## Overview
"Video 2000" is a milestone celebration marking Robert Murray-Smith's 2000th YouTube upload, structured as a retrospective montage of clips from past videos. Rather than a single focused experiment, it reflects on the channel's journey, philosophy of reuse and fearless experimentation, and the community built over the years. The central takeaway is Robert's encouragement to try new things without fear of failure, and his gratitude to viewers for sharing in that exploration.

## Key Topics
- Milestone reflection on reaching 2000 videos
- Wicking and capillary action in materials
- Toothbrush bristle ratchet mechanism for a wind generator
- H-bridge motor control and direction reversal
- Victorian-era water motors and forgotten engineering ideas
- High-voltage generation (low-current, ~2.5 kV from ~5 V input)
- Repurposing and salvaging components from a broken microwave
- Emergency cooking/heating solutions amid rising energy costs
- Golf ball CVT (continuously variable transmission) for variable speed output
- 3D printing and CAD design (TinkerCAD recommended)
- Channel philosophy: reuse, replace, repurpose, and don't fear failure

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned

## Techniques and Methods
- Capillary wicking — using material structure to draw fluid along a surface
- Brush-based ratchet — toothbrush bristles engaging jagged teeth to transmit torque in one direction only, silently
- H-bridge switching — using a latching switch to reverse motor direction
- High-voltage step-up — generating approximately 2.5 kV at microamp-range current from a ~5 V source
- Golf ball CVT — using a rolling golf ball between drive plates to achieve variable speed reduction (~12:1 at one setting)
- Salvage and repurposing — extracting reusable parts from discarded appliances (microwave)
- Self-taught welding and 3D printing/CAD as examples of learning by doing
