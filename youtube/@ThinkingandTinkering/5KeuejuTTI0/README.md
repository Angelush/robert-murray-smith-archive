## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a simplified replication of the O-Wind turbine concept using a cuboctahedron shape, inspired by a visit to his brother Anthony who was using the geometry for AI research. The cuboctahedron is constructed from hardboard salvaged from old whiteboards, slotted together into a Cartesian frame and fitted with skate bearings on a steel rod axle. Testing with a hair dryer demonstrates the device spins in response to airflow from any direction, validating the multi-directional wind-capture principle of the O-Wind concept.
