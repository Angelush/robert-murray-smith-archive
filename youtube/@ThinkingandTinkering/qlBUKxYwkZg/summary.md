## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith salvages a discarded cordless vacuum cleaner and repurposes its DC motor and impeller as a water-powered generator (hydro generator). The motor alone produces only ~1.2 volts — insufficient for an LED — so he pairs it with a Joule thief circuit to step up the voltage and successfully light an LED. The video is a quick, practical entry for his competition challenging viewers to build water generators.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging electronics from discarded consumer devices
- Converting a DC motor into a generator by spinning it with water
- Using a Joule thief circuit to boost low generator voltage
- Importance of water jet angle when driving an impeller
- Teaser for attempting the same experiment with a full-size vacuum cleaner

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Cordless vacuum cleaner (salvaged from bin) — donor device
- DC motor with pressed metal impeller — core generator component
- Dual lithium battery pack — original power source inside the vacuum (noted but not used)
- LED — load used to demonstrate power output

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly of a cordless vacuum cleaner to extract motor/impeller
- Directing a water jet onto the impeller to spin the motor as a generator
- Voltage boosting via a Joule thief circuit (referred to as "dual thief") to make the ~1.2 V output usable
- Adjusting water jet angle to optimise rotational speed of the impeller
