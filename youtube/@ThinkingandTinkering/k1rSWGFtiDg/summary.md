## Overview
Part 2 of Robert's budget electric trike build series, covering the installation of a 500W electric motor onto a generic Chinese quad/trike frame. He walks through removing and modifying the rear axle, fabricating a motor mounting plate, and fitting the drive chain, completing the mechanical build for under £300 total.

## Key Topics
- Component sourcing and cost breakdown for the electric drive system
- Removing the rear subframe and axle from the quad trike
- Installing the drive sprocket onto the rear axle (by welding or using a pre-flanged cog)
- Fabricating and fitting a motor mounting plate from 1mm steel
- Reassembling the drivetrain with the motor in place
- UK road legality for electric pedal-assist bicycles/trikes
- Real-world test ride to a local supermarket (Asda)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 500W electric motor (sourced from eBay, £20)
- Fun Bikes "96 mini electric quad drive set" — includes front sprocket (drive cog on motor), rear chain sprocket, and chain (£21.60)
- Throttle (eBay, ~£11)
- 1mm steel plate — used to fabricate the motor mounting bracket
- Steel angle iron — part of motor mount fabrication
- Flanged sprocket/cog (alternative to welding; available from Simply Bearings, ~£26)
- Generic Chinese quad/trike frame (eBay, £220)
- Lead acid batteries (Robert's personal preference, mentioned in comments)

## Techniques and Methods
- Subframe removal — unbolting basket, mudguards, wheels, and six subframe bolts; loosening brake and derailleur cables
- Rear axle extraction — undoing retaining nuts and tapping out with a rubber mallet
- Drive sprocket installation — sliding flanged cog onto axle between gear cluster and brake disc; welded in place (or bolted via pre-flanged cog as alternative)
- Slot cutting for motor mount — drilling two holes with an 8mm drill, joining with a round file, then finishing with a flat/square file to create adjustment slots
- Welding the motor mounting plate to the frame (bolt-on alternative also described)
- Chain drive assembly — connecting motor sprocket to rear axle sprocket via chain
- Electronics integration — described as "plug and play" once motor is mounted

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Battery choice:** Robert says he personally always uses lead acid batteries, but notes battery choice is ultimately up to the builder.
- **Motor braking:** In response to a question about a flywheel, Robert suggests that simply breaking the live circuit to the motor (with a switch) should be sufficient — no circuit means no work — and that a flywheel would add workload rather than simplify stopping.
- **Braking options:** Suggests rim brakes (like on a push bike) or disc brakes on the actual wheel as alternatives for braking.
- **Pedal assist electronics:** Clarifies these consist of a cadence sensor, torque sensor, bike speed sensor, and a control board.
- **Differential:** Notes they made a video where a differential was built from bike hubs.
