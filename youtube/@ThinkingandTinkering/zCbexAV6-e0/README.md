## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith revisits the zinc-chlorine battery concept first developed in the late 1970s by Gulf Western (GF Weston), which promised up to six times the energy density of lithium. He traces the technology's history from its early failure — due to achieving only 35% of expected charge capacity — through modern research advances at Stanford and the emergence of the Nano Flow Cell company's "Quantino" vehicle. The video's takeaway is that chlorine-based flow batteries are far from dead and may be on the verge of commercial viability.
