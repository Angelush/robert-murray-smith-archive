## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith addresses the perennial question of which wind turbine design is "best," arguing that efficiency — while easy to calculate — is too narrow a metric. He contends that "best" is a broader, context-dependent concept shaped by personal values, living situation, budget, and priorities. The key takeaway is that the best turbine is the best one *for you*, and doing something is better than waiting for a perfect solution.
