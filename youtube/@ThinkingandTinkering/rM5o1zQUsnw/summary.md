## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith provides an update on his graphene-infused concrete project, taking viewers to Southern Testing lab where independent compressive strength tests are performed on concrete cubes. The results show their graphene concrete is 58% stronger than standard concrete at just 7 days of curing — outperforming all five common commercial concrete types even at their full 28-day cure, suggesting it may be among the strongest concrete ever produced.

## Key Topics
- Progress update on the graphene concrete project
- Live compressive strength testing at Southern Testing laboratory
- Comparison of graphene concrete vs. five standard commercial cement types (7-day vs. 28-day cure)
- Solving the two core challenges: dispersing graphene effectively and reducing cost enough for practical use
- Anticipation of upcoming 28-day test results

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene** — added at approximately 0.5% by weight or less; dispersed directly into the cement mix
- **Standard concrete / CEM A cement** — base mix, sourced from a local builders merchant, made to BS206 specification
- **Ordinary Portland cement** — referenced in comments for price context (~US$50–100/ton at bulk)
- **Magnesium oxide** — referenced in comments as an alternative cement material (~US$300–500/ton)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Compressive strength (cube) testing** — blocks loaded under constant increasing pressure until failure; performed independently at Southern Testing
- **Graphene dispersion** — graphene mixed directly into the wet cement mix by stirring; no special additives or engineered treatment
- **7-day and 28-day curing cycles** — standard concrete curing periods used for strength benchmarking
- **Tensile / pull-force testing** — mentioned as in progress at time of filming (more involved and expensive than compressive testing)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Graphene quantity**: ~0.5% by weight or less is sufficient
- **Cost**: the entire graphene addition process costs only **US$1.64 per ton** of concrete — very close to free at industrial scale
- **Mix method**: simply "chuck it in the mix and stir until dispersed" — no exotic process required
- **Base mix**: standard BS206 CEM A from a local builders merchant; no other additives or special treatment
- **Recipe is proprietary** and not publicly shared
- **Tensile testing** (pull forces) is actively underway; he notes it is harder and more expensive to commission than compressive testing
- **Piezoelectricity**: graphene in concrete is piezoelectric — flagged as an interesting property worth noting
- **Outlier result**: one test achieved **145% stronger** than normal concrete, though the reason is unknown
- **Practical implication**: twice the strength means half the thickness of concrete needed
- **Anomalous result**: he suspects some formulations still have room for further optimisation
