## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith tests whether coating a terracotta plant pot with graphite improves its performance as a room heater when used with a candle. The experiment, inspired by a suggestion from his friend Michael Chapelle, is based on graphite's known infrared-emitting properties. Results show the graphite-coated pot reaches 36–37°C compared to 27°C for an uncoated pot, with an ambient temperature of 17°C, confirming a meaningful improvement.
