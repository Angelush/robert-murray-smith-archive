## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comments address practical questions from new members about how the YouTube membership system works:
- **Auto-renewal**: He clarifies the membership does not auto-renew if not renewed, and directs questions to YouTube's own help pages for backend details.
- **Finding members-only posts**: He advises using the Community tab and filtering posts by "Members" in the dropdown, and recommends searching YouTube's own help documentation.
- **Joining button availability**: He notes the "Join" button was a new YouTube feature at the time and was only visible on desktop/laptop browsers, not yet on mobile.
- **Content commitment**: He confirms he is guaranteeing at least 8 videos per month (averaging two per week) and acknowledges he can be prolific.
- **Philosophy on membership/paywalling**: He thoughtfully addresses a critical comment, explaining that content creation has real costs historically borne by him alone, and frames the membership as a way to share that load fairly rather than rely on goodwill — while acknowledging it is a genuinely complex issue.
- **Why YouTube over Patreon**: He chose YouTube's native membership over Patreon simply because it was already there and easy to set up.
- **Framing the membership experience**: He agrees with a commenter's analysis that members are essentially "buying the experience" of being present as things are created — a form of vicarious participation in his creative process.
