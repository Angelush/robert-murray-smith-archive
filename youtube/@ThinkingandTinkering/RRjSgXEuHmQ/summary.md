## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith reviews the Vevor 12V 8kW diesel heater, priced at around £102.99, by fully disassembling it to examine its construction and engineering. He explains how it uses a convection-based design to isolate the combustion chamber from the living space, then reassembles it and runs it successfully. The video concludes that it represents good value and also serves as a study in how to construct similar heaters yourself.

## Key Topics
- Unboxing and overview of the Vevor 12V 8kW diesel heater
- Explanation of the convection heating principle (combustion air kept separate from room air)
- Full teardown and component walkthrough (burner chamber, fuel pump, glow plug, turbo fan, heat exchanger fins, temperature sensor)
- Reassembly and live test run
- Economics of running on diesel vs. biodiesel vs. mains electricity
- Use cases: caravans, RVs, boats, cabins, truck cabs, workshops
- Space heating vs. whole-house heating strategy
- Reference to "The Outsider" YouTube channel's candle-based convection heater as design inspiration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Diesel** — primary fuel, used in the live test
- **Biodiesel** — discussed as a lower-cost alternative fuel (made from waste cooking oil)
- **Waste vegetable oil** — mentioned in comments as a possible fuel source (Robert notes it may be too thick and prone to gumming)
- **Aluminum** — burner/heat exchanger block material
- **Graphite** — used as high-temperature seals within the unit

## Techniques and Methods
- **Teardown/disassembly** — unit opened with 8 screws; Robert walks through each sub-assembly
- **Convection heating** — combustion gases heat an aluminum block; a separate fan circulates room air over the block's fins
- **Forced-air combustion** — a small turbo/impeller forces outside air into the burn chamber across the glow plug and fuel nozzle
- **Exhaust routing** — external exhaust pipe with included muffler attachment
- **Biodiesel production from waste cooking oil** — referenced as a way to source fuel at ~5–10 cents per litre for the base oil
