## Overview
Robert demonstrates a quick and efficient method for desoldering multi-pin components by bridging all pins with solder simultaneously, rather than removing solder from each pin individually. The technique allows the entire component to be pulled free in one motion once all pins share a molten solder bridge. He shows it working on connectors and transistors across multiple boards.

## Key Topics
- Efficient desoldering of multi-pin components
- Solder bridging as a time-saving technique
- Comparison with traditional methods (solder sucker, individual pin desoldering)
- Application to through-hole transistors (NPN and PNP)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Solder (leaded/unspecified, used both for bridging and already present on boards)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Solder bridging for desoldering**: Adding solder to all pins of a component to create a continuous molten bridge across them, then pulling the component free while heat is applied
- **Speed of application**: Emphasised working quickly to minimise heat transfer to delicate parts
- Demonstrated on 2-pin connectors and 3-pin through-hole transistors
