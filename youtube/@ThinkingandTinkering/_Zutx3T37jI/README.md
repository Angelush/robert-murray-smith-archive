## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates the principle of flux switching in generators, explaining how changing magnetic flux paths — rather than moving magnets or coils — can generate electricity. He builds a simple hand-cranked flux switch generator from salvaged parts (speaker magnets, a bolt, a printer pulley) to illustrate the concept, producing 0.6V and 40mA by hand. The video highlights that this technology, dating to at least 1904, is used in missile power systems, torpedoes, and modern Dyson appliances due to its mechanical simplicity and ability to operate at extremely high RPMs.
