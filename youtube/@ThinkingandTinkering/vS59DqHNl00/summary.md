## Overview
This video demonstrates how to hand-wind toroidal inductors (toroids) with copper wire onto iron powder core rings. The presenter, Luke, shares a practical method for calculating the required wire length and preventing the wire from crimping or breaking during the tedious winding process. The toroids being wound (225 + 25 turns) are destined for a future mag amp build.

## Key Topics
- Why toroid winding is tedious and error-prone (broken/crimped wire)
- Calculating the exact length of copper wire needed based on turns
- Managing wire on a waste pipe spool to prevent kinking
- Winding technique: maintaining tension using foot pressure
- Building two identical toroids for a magnetic amplifier (mag amp)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Copper wire (specific gauge, approximately 8 meters per winding)
- Iron powder core ring (toroid core)
- Waste pipe (used as a temporary spool to keep wire orderly and kink-free)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Wire length calculation**: Feed wire through one turn, measure the length, multiply by the number of turns required (e.g., 3.5 cm × 225 = 787 cm, rounded up to 8 m)
- **Pre-spooling wire on waste pipe**: Winding the measured copper wire onto a piece of waste pipe before winding the toroid prevents crimping
- **Foot-tension technique**: Placing a foot on the wire during winding to maintain tension and avoid kinks
- **Sequential winding**: Working through the wire length in segments (4 m + 4 m), feeding through the core ring turn by turn
