## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a DIY bicycle-powered dual-purpose machine that simultaneously pumps water and charges a USB device at 5V. Using a salvaged pump, a three-phase motor, a bridge rectifier, and a buck converter, he builds a simple system driven by a bicycle on an exercise stand with minimal parts.

## Key Topics
- Converting a bicycle into a generator and water pump using a bike exercise stand
- Repurposing a salvaged (dead) pump by attaching a metal cog via spot welding
- Using a three-phase motor as a generator driven by the bike's rear wheel flywheel
- Rectifying and regulating AC output to 5V DC for USB device charging
- Dual-mode operation: electricity generation only, or combined pumping and charging

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Salvaged water pump (dead/stripped of electrics)
- Metal cog (used as drive coupling on the pump shaft)
- Three-phase motor (from a previous project, thick windings for high amps/low volts)
- Three-phase bridge rectifier
- Buck converter (~£1–2)
- Female USB connector
- Power bank (used as test load)
- Exercise bike stand (with heavy flywheel)
- Pipe fittings / push-fit pipework and union connector
- Wood (used as mounting board)

## Techniques and Methods
- Spot/MIG welding a metal cog onto the pump shaft
- Direct-drive coupling of the three-phase motor to the exercise stand's flywheel shaft
- Three-phase AC rectification using a bridge rectifier
- DC voltage regulation/step-down using a buck converter to output stable 5.3V
- Push-fit pipework assembly for the pump inlet/outlet
- Modular mount design allowing the pump to be lifted (electricity only) or lowered (pumping + electricity)
