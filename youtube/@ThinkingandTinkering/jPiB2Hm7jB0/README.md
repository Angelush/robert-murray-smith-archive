## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simple technique for bending copper pipe into a coil suitable for an induction heater, without resorting to the traditional sand- or salt-filling method. By using a steel pipe as a former and applying slow, incremental bending, he produces a clean 5-turn, 70 mm diameter coil in minutes. The hollow tube ends are intentionally left open to allow water cooling if the coil overheats during operation.
