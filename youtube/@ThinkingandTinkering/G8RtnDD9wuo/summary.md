## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make a pyrotechnic fuse using an imitation Sugru mixture. He combines potassium permanganate, sulfur, and silicone sealant (with cornstarch added to accelerate curing) to create a flexible, slow-burning rubber cord. The fuse is then used to successfully ignite a thermite reaction, confirming its practical effectiveness.

## Key Topics
- Making imitation Sugru from silicone sealant and cornstarch
- Formulating a pyrotechnic fuse from common chemicals
- The role of cornstarch in accelerating silicone curing
- Using the fuse to initiate a thermite reaction
- Confirming the thermite product is metallic iron via magnet test

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Sulfur** — oxidiser/fuel component of the fuse mixture
- **Potassium permanganate** — primary oxidiser in the fuse (5 parts by weight)
- **Silicone sealant** — binder/matrix for the fuse (1 part by weight)
- **Cornstarch** — added to accelerate curing of the silicone (half a tablespoon)
- **Acetone (argan/saline referenced as solvent)** — mentioned as a thinner if the mixture becomes too thick
- **Iron oxide (rust)** — fuel component of the thermite
- **Aluminium powder** — reducing agent in the thermite
- **Molten iron** — product of the thermite reaction, confirmed magnetic

## Techniques and Methods
- Hand-kneading the fuse mixture (described as similar to a breadcrumb mix)
- Using cornstarch moisture to accelerate room-temperature silicone vulcanisation
- Shaping and flattening the mixture, then allowing it to cure (~30 minutes with cornstarch vs. 24 hours without)
- Cutting a section of the cured fuse cord for use
- Building a small thermite pyramid on ceramic tiles for heat protection
- Magnet test on cooled thermite product to confirm iron reduction
