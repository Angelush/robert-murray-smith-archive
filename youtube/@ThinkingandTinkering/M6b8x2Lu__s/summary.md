## Overview
This is a progress update (Part 3) on Robert's budget wind turbine build, where he tests the electrical output of his homemade vertical-axis turbine for the first time. Using a microwave oven transformer coil and ceramic ring magnets, he demonstrates the generator produces up to 15V and around 60mA (roughly 600–800mW) when spun by hand, and can even illuminate an LED panel, showing the design has real promise before being mounted on his roof.

## Key Topics
- Current build status and structural issues (wobbly cross brace, need for aluminium honeycomb circular brace)
- First voltage and current output tests using a multimeter
- Magnet and coil arrangement (north-south ceramic ring magnet configuration, ~1mm gap to coil)
- Collapsible design to allow rooftop installation
- Cost tracking — only £33 spent so far
- Plans for next steps: full outdoor test and roof mounting

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Ceramic magnets** — arranged in a north-south-north-south ring configuration; 4.5cm × 1cm × 2.5cm, ~3.6kg pull each
- **Microwave oven transformer (MOT) coil** — repurposed as the generator stator coil; resistance "a few ohms"
- **Aluminium honeycomb board** — used for structural panels; planned for new circular cross brace
- **Wooden pallet** — donated by neighbours, used as the turbine base
- **Wood** — used for temporary cross brace (to be replaced)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Hand-spin voltage/current testing** — spinning the rotor by hand to measure open-circuit voltage and short-circuit current with a Rigol DM3058 bench multimeter
- **Separate voltage and amperage measurement** — voltage and current read in separate passes rather than simultaneously
- **Cogging assessment** — observing magnetic cogging effect between rotor magnets and stator coil at close (~1mm) gap
- **LED panel load test** — connecting a light panel to verify the generator can produce usable light output under manual spin
- **Collapsible assembly design** — turbine built in sections for rooftop transport and reassembly

## Robert's Own Replies
- **On the budget philosophy**: Emphasises repeatedly that this is a low-cost exploration. Ceramic magnets and large wheel diameter are deliberate choices to explore low-torque generation cheaply, not oversights.
- **On commercial wind turbine claims**: Pushes back firmly against an industry commenter, noting that a commercial "1kW" turbine typically produces only 70–100W at average wind speeds (~10–12mph), and only hits rated 1kW at ~25mph winds which are rarely sustained.
- **On coil quantity**: Currently testing with just one MOT coil but plans to add more (likely 16–20 total).
- **On the magnet spec**: Ceramic magnets are 4.5cm × 1cm × 2.5cm with ~3.6kg pull; he has four but is sourcing more.
- **On the meter**: Rigol DM3058 bench multimeter.
- **On planning files**: There are no separate plan documents — the plans exist only in the videos.
- **On roof regulations**: Notes his stepped roofline means the turbine (~1 cubic metre) is not visible from the street, easing planning concerns.
- **On structural advice**: Good-humouredly acknowledges the turbine was originally horizontal (Part 1) and he was told to go vertical — reflects that there are always competing suggestions and you have to go with what works for your objectives.
- **On a governor idea**: Finds the centrifugal governor suggestion interesting and is mulling it over.
