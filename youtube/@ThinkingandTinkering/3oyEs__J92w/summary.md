## Overview
This video is a community competition entry submitted to Robert Murray-Smith by a viewer named Fosse, who attempted to build a water-driven turbine housed inside a 4-inch PVC pipe (repurposing a plant pot concept) as part of Robert's "generate electricity with water by means of motion" competition. Fosse's goal was to integrate the turbine into a hydroponics system, using the existing water pump to spin the turbine for aeration, visual status indication via an LED, and personal mental stimulation. While individual components worked, the full combined system was not fully operational by the submission deadline.

## Key Topics
- Community competition entry for generating electricity from water motion
- Building a micro water turbine from everyday/salvaged components
- Integration of a turbine into a hydroponic growing system
- Three motivations: water aeration, circulation monitoring via LED, and mental stimulation
- The value of maker projects for learning and personal wellbeing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PC fan** — repurposed as the turbine rotor
- **4-inch PVC pipe** — used as the turbine housing
- **Small water pump** — drives water through a nozzle to spin the turbine
- **Small DC motor (generator)** — purchased with an LED pre-attached, used as the generator
- **Thin aluminium rod** — used as the turbine shaft to reduce weight
- **Nozzle/jet fitting** — directs water flow onto the turbine blades
- **Copper and nickel electrodes** — mentioned briefly as a sea water battery concept that was considered and rejected

## Techniques and Methods
- Fitting a PC fan blade assembly onto a shaft inside a PVC pipe housing to form an in-line turbine
- Directing a water jet via a side-mounted nozzle to spin the turbine
- Using lock nuts and spacers for shaft assembly, later replaced with aluminium rod for weight reduction
- Coupling the turbine shaft to a small DC motor to generate current and light an LED
- Mounting the assembly over a bucket to contain water spray during testing
- Testing with a shower head as a water source (indoors, due to rain outside)
