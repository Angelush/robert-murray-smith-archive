## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith inspects, jet-washes, and strips down a lawn mower chassis in preparation for converting it into a miniature electric vehicle styled after "Bessie," the Third Doctor's iconic yellow Edwardian tourer from Doctor Who. The video focuses on cleaning and disassembly, revealing the surprisingly simple mechanical components — steering linkage, gearbox, drivetrain, and rear axle — that form the foundation for the build. His main takeaway is that a large part of any restoration or upcycling project is simply pulling things apart and cleaning them properly.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the lawn mower chassis and its potential as a vehicle base
- Jet-washing the chassis before further disassembly
- Walkthrough of the front steering mechanism (stub axle, steering arm, L-bracket, linkage)
- Explanation of the rear transmission and gearbox mounting
- Drive pulley, idler/tensioning pulley, and belt drive system
- Wheel attachment method and bushed (bearing-free) wheel hubs
- Full disassembly into major sub-assemblies for cleaning
- Inspiration: building a mini "Bessie" replica (Edwardian tourer / Model T front end aesthetic)
- Philosophy of upcycling: cleaning and disassembly as core restoration work

## Techniques and Methods
- Jet-washing for pre-disassembly cleaning
