## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith replicates and explains the Harmony Wind Turbine, a design by Christopher Moore inspired by the classic Savonius (Cavonius) turbine concept from 1922. The turbine's key innovation is self-furling scoop blades that open and close to adapt to wind speed, allowing operation across a wider range of conditions without the risk of over-spinning. Robert designed and 3D printed a working model to demonstrate the mechanism.

## Key Topics
- History of the Savonius turbine (Finnish, 1922) and its operating principle (differential drag)
- Christopher Moore's Harmony turbine as a modern evolution of the Savonius design
- The furling blade mechanism: blades open in low wind, close (furl) in high wind
- How furling extends the operational wind speed range compared to conventional turbines
- Robert's TinkerCAD design and 3D-printed replication of the Harmony turbine
- The gear/cog mechanism and small control motor used to actuate the furling
- Stacking multiple units into a helical configuration (as in Moore's commercial design)
- Patent status and Penn State University involvement in research
