## Overview
This short video, presented by Luke on Robert Murray-Smith's channel, demonstrates how to build a simple hand roller from a salvaged printer/laminating machine roller and basic hardware. The build is quick and low-cost, and the resulting tool is intended for rolling paper sticks but is versatile enough for screen printing, ink printing, laminating, and many other applications.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging a rubber roller from an old printer or laminating machine
- Designing and assembling a handle using square steel tubing and M8 bolts
- Versatility of the finished roller across multiple DIY and craft applications
- Brief mention of a previous paper-stick rolling machine (to be covered in a future video)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Salvaged rubber roller (from a printer or laminating machine)
- M8 bolts (threaded into the roller axle holes)
- Square steel tubing (for the handle)
- 10 mm steel sleeve (spacer to hold handle at correct height)
- Paint (applied as a finish coat)

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling out square tubing to 8 mm to accept the M8 bolts
- Threading/tapping roller axle holes to M8
- Cutting a 10 mm sleeve as a spacer
- Bolting the assembly together
- Painting the finished tool
