## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores thermocouples and thermopiles as a means of generating electricity from heat differentials, then demonstrates a surprisingly simple version made with just two grades of pencil (HB and 6B) drawn on paper. The core excitement is that all-carbon, printable thermopiles could enable cheap, scalable ambient energy harvesting — potentially even woven into clothing — using nothing more exotic than pencil graphite or commercially available carbon inks.
