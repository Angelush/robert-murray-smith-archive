## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith designs and builds a new variant of rocket stove he names the "I-type" — a straight-through vertical pipe design that differs from the existing L, J, K, and V-type configurations. Fabricated from a single 1.2-metre section of 100mm square steel tube, it burns cleanly and smokeless with no complex bends. Robert notes the design is intentionally modelled on the dimensions of a Rijke tube, giving it a physical basis beyond improvisation.

## Key Topics
- Survey of existing rocket stove types (L, J, K, V) and the motivation for a straight "I-type"
- Fabrication of the stove from a single square steel tube
- Creating an integral grate using a tongue-cutting and bending technique
- Live combustion test demonstrating clean, smokeless burn
- Discussion of the stove's role as an early prototype for others to develop
- The design's basis in Rijke tube acoustics/thermodynamics

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 100mm × 100mm (4-inch) mild steel square tube, approximately 1.2 metres long — primary structural material

## Techniques and Methods
- Marking and cutting square tube to length with compound cuts
- Bending integral legs from the tube wall
- Tongue-cutting: alternating slots cut into the tube wall, every other tongue bent outward and inward to form a self-contained grate
- Cutting and welding a 45-degree angle piece
- MIG/stick welding to secure the grate and angle pieces
- Open-air burn test to verify draw and combustion quality

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Rijke tube basis**: Robert repeatedly clarifies that the stove is not a random design — its dimensions are deliberately based on the Rijke tube, a physics device that sustains thermoacoustic oscillations in a vertical tube. He says "yes it is — I designed this around the Reijke tube dimensions."
- **Early prototype framing**: He stresses this is an intentional early prototype, meant as a starting point for others to develop, not a finished product.
- **Planned improvements**: He intends to insulate the stove and is considering adding a fine mesh over the grate to prevent unburnt small pieces falling through before combustion completes.
- **Complete combustion as the goal**: In a detailed reply he clarifies: "the point isn't to get more heat from the fuel — the point is to get as complete combustion as possible within real world constraints."
- **Christmas gift**: The stove was built as a present for his granddaughter and is designed to fold flat into the bottom box for storage.
- **Copyright/novelty**: He jokes (several times) that publishing the design gives him automatic UK copyright, and good-naturedly deflects suggestions that the design already exists elsewhere.
- **Follow-up video planned**: He acknowledges there are misconceptions he didn't address and wants to produce a more technical follow-up.
- **Catalytic converter reference**: He mentions he has already done a separate video using a car exhaust catalytic converter as a combustion enhancer.
