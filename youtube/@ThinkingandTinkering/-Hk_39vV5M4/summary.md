## Overview
In this follow-up to his graphene lab setup video, Robert Murray-Smith tours the specific equipment he purchased using funds raised through an Indiegogo campaign. He walks through each machine's purpose, explaining how each piece fits into his planned work on graphene production, solar cells, conductive inks, and related materials science. The video serves as both an accountability update to his supporters and a practical introduction to the lab's capabilities.

## Key Topics
- Equipment purchased with Indiegogo crowdfunding money
- UV/visible spectrometry for thin film characterisation
- High-voltage conditioning of piezoelectric inks
- Graphene production using a pulverizer/ball mill
- High-temperature synthesis of graphitic and conductive carbons
- Ink and paste mixing using a pen mixer and muller grinders
- Plans for an open, community-accessible lab

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene** — primary research target; the KM1 pulverizer is specifically cited in a paper for making it
- **Piezoelectric materials** — require high-voltage conditioning before use as inks
- **Metal nanoparticles** — dispersed down to 4–5 nm range using the pulverizer
- **Carbon compounds** — reduced at high temperature using metal catalysts to form graphitic/conductive carbons
- **Nickel and iron catalysts** — used in furnace reduction of carbon compounds
- **Carbon foams** — produced in the kiln/furnace
- **Conductive inks and pastes** — mixed using the pen mixer and muller grinders
- **Soap** — to be tableted using the tablet press

## Techniques and Methods
- **UV/visible spectrophotometry** — measuring transmittance of thin films for solar cell characterisation
- **Four-probe method / square resistor measurement** — measuring sheet resistance/conductivity of films
- **High-voltage poling/conditioning** — conditioning piezoelectric materials for ink formulation
- **High-speed pen mixing (shear mixing)** — dispersing materials uniformly through a carrier in ~100 mL–1 L batches
- **Pulverizing / mechanochemical milling (KM1)** — producing graphene and dispersing metal nanoparticles
- **Wet mull grinding** — low-energy shear mixing of pastes and inks over extended run times
- **High-temperature furnace processing (up to 1200 °C)** — catalytic reduction to graphitic/conductive carbon, carbon foam production
- **Tablet pressing** — compacting materials (and soap) into tablet form
