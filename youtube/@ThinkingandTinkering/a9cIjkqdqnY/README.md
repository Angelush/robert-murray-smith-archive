## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a functional water clock by adapting the water motor he created in video 2170. The device uses the motor's regular oscillating motion (one rock every 4 seconds) to drive a ratchet-and-gear mechanism, ultimately turning clock hands at the correct minute and hour rates. The result is a working, if imprecise, 3D-printed water clock requiring only a steady drip of water to run.
