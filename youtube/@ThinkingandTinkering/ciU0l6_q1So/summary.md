## Overview
In this short build episode, Robert disassembles a belt sander to salvage its internal components for use in a Van de Graaff generator. He extracts the rollers, motor, bushes, stator, and rotor, then prepares the bottom roller assembly for use as the Van de Graaff's belt-drive roller, concluding with a nearly finished bottom roller ready to mount on the generator base.

## Key Topics
- Disassembly of a belt sander to harvest parts
- Identification and extraction of usable rollers (top and bottom)
- Extraction of motor components: rotor, stator, brushes/bushes, commutator
- Repurposing the sander's motor housing for the Van de Graaff build
- Preparation of the bottom roller assembly (removing rubber coating, retaining drive gear and pulley)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Aluminium (bottom roller material beneath rubber coating)
- Rubber (glued coating on the aluminium roller, removed by chisel while chucked in a drill)
- Teflon (mentioned as a possible future coating for a roller)
- Brass (bush/brush mountings)
- Plastic (roller housing, shoe plate)
- Steel (shoe plate)

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembly of a belt sander (unscrewing motor head, handle, forward grip, carriage)
- Chucking the roller in a drill and using a chisel to strip glued-on rubber
- Facing/flattening the roller surface after rubber removal
- Extracting brushes/bushes from their housings
- Reusing a production-made roller assembly in its housing for squareness and rigidity
- Worm-drive and pulley identification for motor-to-roller power transmission
