## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates mechanical plating (also called peen plating or impact plating) as an easy, cheap, and safe method for copper-coating PLA 3D prints. He uses a rock tumbler filled with copper powder, steel ball bearings, and a self-printed bust to achieve real copper adhesion. The key discovery is that pre-treating the PLA surface with acetone makes it tacky, which is essential for the copper to adhere properly.

## Key Topics
- Comparison of metal-coating methods for 3D prints: metallic paint, electroplating, and mechanical plating
- Mechanical/peen plating process using a rock tumbler
- Importance of surface preparation (acetone treatment) for PLA adhesion
- Process variables that affect coating quality
- Post-coating protection with lacquer

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Copper powder** — the plating metal, applied via tumbling
- **4 mm steel ball bearings** (~400 used) — impact media to cold-weld the copper
- **Ceramic filler tumbling rods** (~1 cm × 4 mm) — additional tumbling media included with the tumbler
- **Acetone** — used to briefly dip the PLA model, creating a sticky surface for better copper adhesion
- **Lacquer** — recommended as a topcoat to prevent copper oxidation
- **PLA** — the 3D print substrate being coated
- **Bronze metal powder** — mentioned as a future experiment
- **Graphite** — mentioned as another potential coating material
- **Zirconium balls and glass balls** — noted as alternative impact media options

## Techniques and Methods
- **Mechanical/peen/impact plating** — tumbling a workpiece with fine metal powder and impact media so powder cold-welds to the surface
- **Acetone surface treatment** — briefly dipping PLA in acetone to create a tacky surface for improved adhesion
- **Rock tumbler operation** — sealing workpiece, media, and powder in a barrel and running for ~1 hour
- **Process tuning** — adjusting tumbling time, barrel speed, powder particle size, and media size/weight for different surfaces
- **Lacquer topcoating** — sealing the finished copper surface to prevent oxidation
