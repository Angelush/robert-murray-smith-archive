## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith 3D prints a prototype "wind leaf" device based on a concept submitted by viewer Elizabeth McKinney. The leaf uses an integrally-hinged flap that deflects closed when wind hits it, channeling airflow down a pipe. A smoke test confirms the mechanism works, and Robert enthuses about the potential to scale this into a full "wind tree" structure made from many such leaves.

## Key Topics
- Fan-submitted invention concept from viewer Elizabeth McKinney
- Designing the wind leaf in TinkerCAD
- 3D printing a two-part prototype with an integral hinge
- Smoke-flow testing to verify the flap mechanism
- The "wind tree" concept: assembling many leaves into a larger energy-harvesting structure
- Balancing simplicity of manufacture against efficiency in DIY projects
- Possible energy generation methods (piezo, electrostatics)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Smoke (used as a flow visualisation medium during testing)

## Techniques and Methods
- CAD design in TinkerCAD
- 3D printing with raft support on an FDM printer (approx. 1.5-hour print)
- Integral single-print hinge design (cone-in-cone geometry)
- Slicing in Cura
- Smoke-flow testing to verify airflow direction through the flap
