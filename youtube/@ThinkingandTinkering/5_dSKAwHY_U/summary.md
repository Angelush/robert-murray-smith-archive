## Overview
In this video, Luke demonstrates how to repurpose a discarded washing machine into a functional vertical axis wind turbine (VAWT) using only the components found inside the machine itself. He disassembles the washing machine, cuts and bends fins from the stainless steel drum, and connects the salvaged motor to serve as a generator. The turbine successfully spins in the wind by the end of the video, with electrical output testing deferred to a follow-up video.

## Key Topics
- Brief comparison of vertical axis vs. horizontal axis wind turbine designs
- Rationale for choosing a vertical axis configuration
- Disassembly of a domestic washing machine to extract the drum and motor
- Modifying the drum by cutting and bending fins to capture wind
- Reattaching the washing machine motor as a generator
- Outdoor spin test of the completed turbine

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Washing machine (stainless steel drum used as turbine rotor; plastic top/bottom casing)
- Washing machine motor (repurposed as the electrical generator)

## Techniques and Methods
- Full disassembly of a washing machine to separate drum, motor, and casing
- Cutting along the plastic separation line to remove the bulk of the casing while retaining the base section for levelling
- Marking, cutting, and bending fins directly from the drum body
- Mechanical coupling of the drum rotor to the salvaged motor
