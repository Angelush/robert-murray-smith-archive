## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith documents the accidental discovery of a novel type of solar cell based on zinc sulfide nanoparticles acting as the insulating layer in a MIM (Metal-Insulator-Metal) diode. While attempting to purify bis-thiourea zinc formate for electroluminescent ink research, he mistakenly added a lacy plating solution instead of water, inadvertently producing photoactive zinc sulfide nanoparticles. The resulting device — copper and aluminium electrodes sandwiching the zinc sulfide layer — generated measurable current for hours, suggesting a promising new approach to solar energy harvesting via electromagnetic wave rectification rather than the conventional photon-knock-out mechanism.
