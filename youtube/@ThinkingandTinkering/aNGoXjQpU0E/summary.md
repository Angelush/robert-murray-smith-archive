## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith documents the accidental discovery of a novel type of solar cell based on zinc sulfide nanoparticles acting as the insulating layer in a MIM (Metal-Insulator-Metal) diode. While attempting to purify bis-thiourea zinc formate for electroluminescent ink research, he mistakenly added a lacy plating solution instead of water, inadvertently producing photoactive zinc sulfide nanoparticles. The resulting device — copper and aluminium electrodes sandwiching the zinc sulfide layer — generated measurable current for hours, suggesting a promising new approach to solar energy harvesting via electromagnetic wave rectification rather than the conventional photon-knock-out mechanism.

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Formic acid (85%)** — starting material for zinc formate synthesis
- **Zinc oxide** — reacted with formic acid to produce zinc formate
- **Zinc formate** — intermediate compound, purified by recrystallisation
- **Thiourea** — combined with zinc formate in a 2:1 molar ratio; used in photography
- **Bis-thiourea zinc formate** — the key precursor; not commercially available, must be synthesised; decomposes to zinc sulfide at ~400°C
- **Zinc sulfide (ZnS) nanoparticles** — the photoactive insulating layer; glow-in-the-dark material; insoluble, formed by accident when lacy plating solution was added
- **Lacy plating solution** (oxalic acid, ammonium sulfate, tri-sodium phosphate) — accidentally introduced, triggering ZnS nanoparticle formation
- **Copper** — electrode (higher work function metal)
- **Aluminium** — electrode (lower work function metal)
- **Titanium dioxide (TiO2)** — added experimentally; boosted output to ~25–26 µA
- **Deionised water** — solvent and electrolyte
- **Graphite (mechanical graphing sheet)** — used as counter electrode in one test cell

## Techniques and Methods
- **Zinc formate synthesis** — dissolving zinc oxide in formic acid until fizzing stops, heating to evaporate water, cooling to recrystallise
- **Bis-thiourea zinc formate synthesis** — dissolving zinc formate and thiourea separately in water (2:1 molar ratio), combining, heating, and recrystallising
- **Recrystallisation** — used at multiple stages to purify compounds
- **Accidental nanoparticle synthesis** — mixing bis-thiourea zinc formate solution with lacy plating solution produced colloidal ZnS nanoparticles
- **Tyndall test** — shining a green laser through the solution to confirm the presence of nanoparticles (visible scattering beam)
- **MIM diode cell assembly** — thin aluminium strip between copper electrodes, ZnS nanoparticle slurry applied between them, water used as electrolyte
- **Interdigitated electrode design** — interlocking finger electrodes to maximise junction area without a transparent conductive oxide top coat
- **Current measurement** — multimeter in microamp mode used to monitor output over time

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert was active in the comments and added several useful clarifications:

- **Ingredient confirmation**: explicitly lists the three key materials as "Zinc Oxide, Formic Acid and Thiourea" in response to a commenter's question.
- **TiO2 role**: clarifies that TiO2 is "catalytic for the reverse reaction but not really usable by itself — like in a Grätzel cell." He also notes that commercial TiO2 powder would not be suitable; nanoparticles are needed, and in-situ synthesis would be preferable to purchasing them.
- **Crystal growth**: notes that slow recrystallisation is likely important for performance, though he hadn't confirmed this experimentally at the time.
- **Project status**: mentions he shelved the solar cell work temporarily to finish the EESD project, intending to return to it.
- **Silver electrodes**: rules out silver as too expensive for a general solar cell, suggesting ionic fluids instead.
- **Video philosophy**: states his videos "aren't end products in themselves — just interesting things that might serve as jumping off points," explaining why he doesn't provide precise recipes.
- **Self-deprecating honesty**: admits "I am very often full of shit mate — half the things I just plain make up to cover my ignorance on the subject," characteristic of his informal, exploratory style.
- **Voltage vs. current analogy**: explains to a commenter that having voltage without current is like a hill with no water — the slope (voltage) remains but produces no power without flow (amps).
