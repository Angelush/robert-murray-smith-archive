## Overview
In this video, the creator pursues several DIY projects centred around repurposing bicycle wheels and salvaged materials. The main project is converting a bicycle into a functional lawnmower, involving basic metalworking for the first time. Two side projects — a parabolic mirror water heater made from collected aluminium cans and a bicycle-wheel fan — are shown in various stages of progress.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing discarded aluminium cans found in the environment
- Building a parabolic mirror from a bicycle wheel to heat water
- Converting a bicycle wheel into a cooling fan using tape and cloth
- Turning a bicycle into a lawnmower as the primary project
- First-time metal deformation using heat and brute force

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Aluminium cans (collected from littering in the environment)
- Tape (used to blade-ify the bicycle wheel into a fan)
- Cloth (wound around the wheel for the fan)
- Plasticine / modelling clay ("plastiline" in Austrian German)
- Metal pipes (flattened and drilled to connect the lawnmower to the bicycle)

## Techniques and Methods
- Forming a parabolic reflector from a bicycle wheel using aluminium cans
- Fabricating a fan blade assembly by attaching tape and cloth to a bicycle wheel
- Metal deformation using heat and brute force to flatten pipes
- Drilling holes in flattened metal pipes for mechanical connection
- Mechanical joining of lawnmower to bicycle frame
