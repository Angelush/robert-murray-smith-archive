## Overview
Robert demonstrates using an inverted bicycle as a low-cost, versatile test bed for evaluating wind turbine generator configurations. Rather than building a full turbine and mounting it aloft only to discover problems, this setup allows rapid iteration on gear ratios and electronic controls. He shows live voltage and current measurements using a car alternator driven by hand-crank, validating that output voltage exceeds the excitation voltage fed into the rotor.

## Key Topics
- The rationale for test beds before committing to full turbine builds
- Using an upside-down bicycle as a multi-purpose mechanical test platform
- Gear ratio experimentation via interchangeable sprockets and wheels
- Car alternator operation: exciting the rotor, taking output from the stator
- Live demonstration of voltage gain (1V in → 3V out) under load
- The engineering philosophy of always testing, even when calculations exist

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Car alternator (previously disassembled in video 1502)
- LED load/light-up test load
- Voltmeter / power meter

## Techniques and Methods
- Inverting a bicycle to create a hand-cranked mechanical test rig
- Using the large bicycle wheel driving a small pulley wheel to create a step-up gear ratio
- Feeding DC excitation voltage into the alternator rotor to control torque/output
- Taking AC (or rectified) generation from the stator
- Measuring output voltage and current under load with a meter
