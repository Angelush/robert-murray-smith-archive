## Overview
In this third installment of his rotating ventilator wind power series, Robert Murray-Smith constructs the stator — the fixed coil-bearing ring — for a small homemade generator. He details how to form a circular band from uPVC builders board, mount hand-wound coils inside it, add structural rings, and build a central guide block, producing a lightweight yet rigid stator ready to receive the rotor in the next video.

## Key Topics
- Disassembly and analysis of the ventilator's internal drum structure
- Design rationale for the generator ring (stator vs. rotor separation)
- Using uPVC/Sintra builders board as the primary structural material
- Building a bending jig from MDF and nails to form circular rings
- Gluing coils onto the inside of the stator ring
- Adding reinforcing rings to prevent the band from springing out over time
- Constructing a central guide block to keep the rotor true
- Wiring the coils: feeding wires through, soldering to brass pins

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Ferrite magnets** — ~100 units purchased for ~£10, used in the generator
- **uPVC builders board / Sintra board** — 47 mm wide strip used to form the stator ring; equivalent to roofline trim found in British homes
- **MDF (medium-density fibreboard)** — used to make the bending jig
- **Four-inch nails** — used as jig pegs to hold the uPVC curve
- **Cyanoacrylate (super glue / crazy glue)** — primary adhesive; noted to bond uPVC stronger than the base material
- **Brass pins** — used to anchor and solder the coil wires
- **8 mm rod** — used as a centering pin in the guide block jig
- **Stainless steel (non-magnetic)** — noted as the ventilator's existing material, relevant because magnets cannot attach to it directly

## Techniques and Methods
- **Jig-based circular forming** — MDF board with nails used to bend uPVC strip into a ring of the correct diameter
- **Mitre-joining flat board** — four mitred pieces of builders board glued to form a square frame, then cut into rings with a homemade circle-cutting tool
- **Coil mounting** — individual hand-wound coils glued one by one around the inside of the stator ring with cyanoacrylate and clamped to cure
- **Wire routing** — coil lead wires fed through a drilled channel/ring at the top of the stator
- **Soldering to brass pins** — core wires wound around brass pins and soldered for electrical connections
- **Layered structural reinforcement** — a second ring added to the base to counteract the outward spring tendency of the uPVC band

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Crazy glue and cuts**: Acknowledged cutting himself on the sharp ventilator edge and confirmed he applied cyanoacrylate to the wound — noting the glue was originally developed by the USA during the Vietnam War as a field wound sealant.
- **Coil count**: Confirmed that 29 coils is the correct number that would fit.
- **Power output plan**: Intends to charge a battery or capacitor with the generator output and draw the load from that, rather than direct connection.
- **Wiring arrangement**: Likely to use a standard NSN (north-south-north) pole arrangement.
- **Stator mounting**: Noted the stator will be mounted inside the steel cowl of the ventilator, which itself mounts on a chimney.
- **uPVC material**: Joked he should write to Eurocell (a UK uPVC manufacturer) given how useful the material has proved.
- **White paint**: Mentioned the white colour helps the components stand out visually (likely for the video).
