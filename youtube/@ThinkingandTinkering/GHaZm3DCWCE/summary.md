## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explains the problem of gear backlash — the small gap between gear teeth that causes imprecision — and surveys several approaches to eliminate or reduce it. The video culminates in a 3D-printed model of a spring-loaded zero-backlash worm drive that Robert designed and assembled on camera. The key takeaway is that backlash is a solvable engineering problem with a variety of mechanical and software solutions available.

## Key Topics
- What backlash is and why it occurs in gear systems
- Why backlash matters most in precision applications (CNC machines, 3D printers)
- Friction drives as a way to sidestep backlash entirely (NIF mirror alignment, Archimedes drive, CVTs)
- Gear design approaches to minimise backlash (herringbone gears)
- Software compensation for known backlash distances
- Spring-loaded split-gear mechanical solutions for spur and worm gear arrangements
- Design and assembly of a 3D-printed zero-backlash worm drive model
- The Oldham coupling as a mechanism to accommodate motor wear in a zero-backlash drive

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 3D-printed parts (PLA or similar, implied by printing context)
- Compression springs (24×8 mm, two smaller and one larger)
- White rod (axle/shaft component)
- Glue (used to fix housing sections)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D modelling in Tinkercad (design of worm drive components)
- FDM 3D printing of gear and housing parts
- Spring-loading a split worm wheel to pre-load tooth mesh and eliminate backlash
- Oldham coupling integration to allow the gear to float radially under motor load
- Manual assembly with press-fit and glued joints
