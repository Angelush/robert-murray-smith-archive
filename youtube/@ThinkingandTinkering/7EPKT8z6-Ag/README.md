## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith introduces flash graphene — a method of producing graphene using a capacitor bank or, more accessibly, a low-power laser. He explains that while traditional graphene synthesis is expensive and uses hazardous chemicals, laser scribing onto materials like Kapton tape offers a safe, affordable route that almost anyone can replicate at home. His main takeaway is that laser-scribed graphene is the practical choice for home experimentation, with compelling applications in supercapacitors, concrete reinforcement, speakers, and heating pads.
