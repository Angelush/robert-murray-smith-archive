## Overview
A short festive science demonstration showing how to make a classic Cartesian diver toy, themed as dancing seahorses. Using drinking straws and paper clips, Robert constructs small figures that sink and rise inside a sealed water bottle when pressure is applied, as part of his Advent Science Magic series.

## Key Topics
- Advent Science Magic calendar series (Day 10)
- Cartesian diver principle
- Pressure and buoyancy demonstration
- Simple festive craft for children

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Drinking straws (cut to ~2 cm segments)
- Paper clips (6 total, used as ballast/structure)
- Googly eyes (4, for decoration)
- Water (in a plastic bottle, partially emptied)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting straws to short segments (~2 cm)
- Inserting and linking paper clips to form a seahorse figure
- Gluing googly eyes onto the straw-and-clip assembly
- Dropping the figures into a partially filled water bottle
- Squeezing the sealed bottle to demonstrate sinking and rising
