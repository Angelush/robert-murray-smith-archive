## Overview
A presenter named Luke builds a custom-sized wooden box lined with aluminium foil and aluminium tape to create a DIY Faraday cage capable of blocking all mobile phone signals. The key insight demonstrated is that the conductive lining must be electrically continuous — including bridging strips connecting the lid to the base — for the shielding to work. The completed box is tested live and shown to fully block incoming calls when closed.

## Key Topics
- What a Faraday cage/box is and its practical use (blocking phone signals at social gatherings)
- Two approaches: buying a ready-made enclosure vs. building a custom one
- Importance of electrical continuity in the conductive lining
- Live before/after demonstration of signal blocking

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Aluminium roll** — used to line the outer/base interior surfaces
- **Aluminium tape** — used to line the inner walls with overlapping strips
- **Double-sided sticky tape** — used to fix the aluminium roll to the box surfaces
- **Conductive ink** — briefly mentioned as an alternative but dismissed due to uncertainty about effectiveness
- **Painted wooden box** (blue) — the structural enclosure, custom-built to fit multiple phones

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting and layering aluminium roll with double-sided tape on interior surfaces
- Applying aluminium tape strips along all interior wall edges to ensure overlap and coverage
- Adding bridging strips of aluminium tape from the lid down into the base to establish electrical continuity across the lid/box joint
- Functional testing by calling the phone from a landline with the box open, then closed
