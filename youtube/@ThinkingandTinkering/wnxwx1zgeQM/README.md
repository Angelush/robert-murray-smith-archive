## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the current state and near-future trajectory of direct brain-machine interfaces, framing them through the lens of electronics history — from mechanical switches and vacuum tubes to transistors. The central insight is that liquid transistors, which use ion concentration in an electrolyte to switch or amplify signals, offer a uniquely biocompatible bridge between neurons and silicon. He concludes that direct thought control of machinery is not a distant prospect but an active area of research, driven significantly by military applications.
