## Overview
A short "Factoid of the Day" episode in which Robert shares the popular historical origin of the American phrase "cup of joe." He traces the story from the Boston Tea Party of 1773 — which made tea drinking unfashionable in the US — through to Josephus Daniels' 1914 General Order 99, which banned alcohol on Navy ships and left sailors with only coffee to drink, coining the phrase.

## Key Topics
- The Boston Tea Party (1773) as the catalyst for America's shift from tea to coffee
- The statistic that ~60% of Americans drink coffee in the morning
- The popular etymology of "cup of joe" traced to Josephus Daniels
- General Order 99 (1914) banning alcohol from US Navy ships
- The spread of the phrase from the Navy to the entire US armed forces
