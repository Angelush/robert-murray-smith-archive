## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith begins a multi-part series building a rideable plastic trike entirely from uPVC builders board (roofline plastic), demonstrating the material's remarkable strength and versatility. He constructs the main chassis using H-shaped girders made from flat uPVC sheet, attaches a heavy-duty swivel castor wheel for the rear pivot, and rigs a rear-wheel steering mechanism using bending plates and push rods. The video serves as both a practical build log and an enthusiastic showcase of what this widely available, easy-to-work plastic can achieve.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to uPVC builders board (roofline plastic) and its properties
- Cutting and bonding uPVC sheet into H-section girders for structural strength
- Load-bearing test of the plastic girder construction
- Selection and mounting of a heavy-duty swivel castor wheel as a rear pivot
- Design and assembly of a rear-wheel steering system using bending plates and push rods
- Preview of upcoming build steps (wheels, seat, possible electrification)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **uPVC builders board / roofline plastic** — primary structural material; also known as "Cintra board" in the US; available in widths up to ~250 mm
- **Super glue (CA / cyanoacrylate)** — adhesive used to bond uPVC; bond noted to be stronger than the base plastic; confirmed in comments as the standard adhesive used in the UK building industry for doors, windows, and roofline with a 20-year guarantee
- **M8 bolts** — fasteners used to secure the steering bracket assembly
- **9 mm foam-core uPVC board** — mentioned in comments; uPVC outer skin with what appears to be a polyurethane foam core

## Techniques and Methods
- **Scoring and snapping / box-knife cutting** — uPVC sheet cut with hand tools or power tools
- **H-girder fabrication** — strips glued together at the midpoint to form a hedge-girder (H-section) for greatly increased bending stiffness
- **Packing out with laminated plastic** — building up thickness at a joint by gluing multiple layers of sheet before bolting hardware
- **Swivel castor integration** — repurposing a heavy-duty trolley wheel (ball-bearing type) as a steerable rear pivot
- **Rear-wheel steering linkage** — using L-brackets / bending plates and push rods with handles to steer via the rear wheel

## Robert's Own Replies
- **CA glue is the right choice:** Confirms super glue (CA) is standard in the UK building industry for uPVC doors, windows, and roofline applications, with a 20-year guarantee and tolerance for vibration — justifying its use here.
- **Foam-core board clarification:** The 9 mm board has a uPVC outer skin over what he believes is a polyurethane foam core.
- **US equivalent confirmed:** Reiterates "Sintra board" as the American equivalent to search for.
- **Electrification and unfinished projects:** Acknowledges some projects stall due to cost or finding the right parts (e.g., a suitable motor), but doesn't consider that negative — some projects (like his VAWTs) he regards as complete.
- **Traction concern acknowledged:** Agrees the trike will likely slide around without grip — tyre choice presumably to be addressed in a later episode.
- **Planning videos:** Acknowledges he never films the planning phase and concedes he probably should.
- **Build is fun and family-friendly:** Notes it's a great project to do with kids.
- **Weight comment:** Jokes about not being the lightest person, attributing it to "too much cake."
