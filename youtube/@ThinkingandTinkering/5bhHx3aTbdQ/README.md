## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains the concept of sheet resistance, a property used to characterize thin conductive films such as painted conductive inks. He derives the sheet resistance formula from bulk resistivity, showing why ohms per square is the appropriate unit for thin-film measurements. The key takeaway is that sheet resistance is geometry-independent for square regions, making it the practical standard quoted by conductive ink manufacturers.
