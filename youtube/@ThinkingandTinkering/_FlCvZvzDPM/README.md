## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simple, low-cost home method for producing graphene by blending graphite powder with water, soap (surfactant), and isopropanol in a standard kitchen blender. The method is based on a 2014 Nature Materials paper from Trinity College Dublin on shear exfoliation of graphite in liquid. The key takeaway is that this "green and safe" approach can yield usable few-layer graphene suitable for inks, conductive plastics, and transparent films.
