## Overview
A ~3-hour sponsored live stream in which Robert Murray-Smith and his colleague Luke unbox, assemble, and review the new Elegoo Neptune 4 (filament) and Saturn Ultra (resin) 3D printers, walk through software installation, and host a 33-prize giveaway. The main takeaway is Robert's enthusiastic endorsement of Elegoo machines as reliable, affordable tools after eight months of near-continuous use, with particular excitement about the Neptune 4's dramatic speed improvements and the Saturn Ultra's 0.01mm resolution.

## Key Topics
- Robert's conversion from 3D printer skeptic to enthusiast after Elegoo sent him a printer
- Elegoo Neptune 4 and Neptune 4 Pro unboxing and assembly (4-screw upright attachment)
- Print speed comparison: Neptune 4 rated at 250–500 mm/s vs. ~60–120 mm/s on older machines
- Elegoo Saturn Ultra resin printer: 0.01mm resolution capability
- Pricing overview: Neptune 4 ~£259, Neptune 4 Pro ~£299
- Connectivity improvements: USB-A port replacing awkward Micro SD slot in Neptune 3
- Software walkthrough: Elegoo Cura slicer (filament) and ChituBox / Tango slicer (resin)
- CAD workflow: TinkerCAD for beginners, Fusion 360 as a next step
- Filament vs. resin printer material properties and fracture behaviour
- Water-washable resin as an IPA alternative
- Graphene-infused resin: ongoing experimental work, challenges with UV curing black materials
- Recycling PLA by melting and re-extruding through a filament maker
- 33-prize giveaway (UK only), including a Saturn Ultra and Malibu resin
- Encouragement to beginners: fear/procrastination is the main barrier, not complexity

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA filament** — primary material used for engineering models (engines, turbines, water wheels)
- **ABS filament** — mentioned as having different properties to PLA (higher temperature tolerance)
- **Elegoo Fast PLA** — new Elegoo filament type sent with the Neptune 4, not yet tested
- **Clear filament** — noted as a future experiment for printing optical elements/lenses
- **UV-curable photo resin** — thermosetting; used in the Saturn resin printer
- **Water-washable resin** — preferred over standard resin to avoid IPA costs
- **IPA (isopropyl alcohol)** — standard resin wash solvent; noted as expensive and supply-constrained post-COVID
- **Graphene** — being experimentally stirred into UV resin; curing issues with black pigments are a barrier
- **Cobalt salt** — mentioned as a curing agent to speed up UV cure of black materials (adds handling hazards)
- **PTFE** — material of the feed tube (Bowden tube) in the hot end; brass replacement available
- **Brass** — nozzle material
- **Aluminum** — heating block material

## Techniques and Methods
- Unboxing and physical assembly of Neptune 4 (attaching upright with four screws)
- Bed levelling procedure (noted as worth a dedicated future video)
- Transferring print files via USB stick (preferred over direct USB cable or network connection)
- Slicing STL files in Elegoo Cura (Neptune 4) and ChituBox / Tango (Saturn)
- Sourcing STL models from Thingiverse and Printables
- Overnight printing to maximise convenience and off-peak electricity rates
- UV curing residual resin in wash water by leaving it in sunlight (resin sinks, water clears)
- Stirring graphene into UV resin as a conductivity/property modifier (experimental, incomplete)
- Recycling failed PLA prints by chopping, melting, and re-extruding through a barrel extruder (limited re-use cycles)
