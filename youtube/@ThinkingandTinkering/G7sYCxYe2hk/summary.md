## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a practical technique for salvaging large electrolytic capacitors from old UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) circuit boards. The challenge is that high-current PCBs use thick copper planes that act as heat sinks, making conventional desoldering impossible. His solution is to use a saw to cut the PCB around each capacitor pin, reducing the copper thermal mass to a manageable size before desoldering.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging components from discarded UPS power supplies
- The problem of thick copper PCBs acting as heat sinks during desoldering
- Using a saw to section the PCB as a desoldering aid
- Cost and practical benefits of component reuse and recycling

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Large electrolytic capacitors (250V, 40µF) salvaged from UPS power supplies
- Solder (used to wet/flux the pads before lifting pins)
- Flux (mentioned as insufficient on its own for thick copper boards)

## Techniques and Methods
- Sawing the PCB between and around capacitor legs to isolate each pin on its own small section of copper, minimising the heat sink effect
- Adding fresh solder to wet the pad and improve heat transfer ("tinning" to aid reflow)
- Lifting desoldered pins with a soldering iron once solder has flowed through the full pad thickness
