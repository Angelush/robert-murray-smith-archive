## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith uses this video to demonstrate his process for developing DIY batteries, walking through the history from the Daniell cell (1836) to a modern rechargeable alkaline zinc/copper oxide cell. Using a 2021 research paper as a springboard, he builds an improved solid-state variant by incorporating Bird's historical plaster of paris separator technique, resulting in a rechargeable 1.1V cell that outperforms the original Daniell cell.
