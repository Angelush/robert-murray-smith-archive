## Overview
This is Part 2 of a bike roller build project, presented by Luke, in which he responds to viewer feedback by fabricating sturdy upright angle supports to hold the bike steady during use. The uprights are built using a composite layering technique and are demonstrated successfully, allowing hands-free cycling on the roller. A further part is teased at the end.

## Key Topics
- Responding to community suggestions from Part 1
- Design and construction of upright stabilising supports for a bike roller trainer
- Composite fabrication using layered materials
- Live demonstration of the completed roller setup with the new uprights

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Stool bars** — used as the structural base/frame for the uprights
- **Epoxy resin** — used to fill and bond the top of the uprights
- **Carbon fibre** — wrapped around the uprights for strength
- **Expanding foam** — injected into the middle to add mass and bulk
- **Glass fibre** — applied as an outer coating over the carbon fibre and foam
- **Paint** — finish coat applied after sanding

## Techniques and Methods
- Joining two stool bars and flattening the top
- Filling gaps with epoxy resin
- Wrapping the assembly in carbon fibre
- Injecting expanding foam to fill internal voids and add mass
- Overcoating with glass fibre
- Sanding down to a smooth finish
- Painting for final aesthetics
