## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith and colleague Gary Monahan (of FWG) demonstrate the graphitization of various biological waste materials — including seaweed, coffee grounds, banana peels, and wood — into graphite and potentially graphene. The video's central message is that the graphitization *process* is what matters, not any single "best" material; makers should use whatever abundant local waste stream is available to them. Potential applications include supercapacitors, batteries, and graphene-enhanced cement.
