## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents the third iteration of his cuboctahedron-based wind generator (the "O Wind" concept), in which he replaces the flat faces of the structure with venetian blind slats that act as airfoils. Inspired by viewer comments pointing out that flat faces would stall in wind, he redesigned the panels to generate lift-based thrust, causing the device to spin correctly. The device is proposed as an open-source, urban-friendly alternative to conventional propeller turbines, capable of handling variable wind directions.

## Key Topics
- Critique of the previous flat-faced design (wind stall problem) prompted by viewer comments
- Airfoil principle applied to venetian blind slats to generate perpendicular thrust
- Correct rotational direction confirmed in outdoor testing
- Urban wind energy use case: compact, omni-directional, planning-permission-friendly
- Comparison to propeller turbines (less efficient, but suited to different environments)
- Open-source declaration of the cuboctahedron wind turbine design
- Next steps: connecting the device to a motor/generator under load

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Plastic (used for the structural frame of the airfoil panels)
- Venetian blind slats (pre-shaped airfoil cross-section, used as wind vanes)

## Techniques and Methods
- Fabrication of plastic frames to mount venetian blind slats at an attack angle
- Replacement of flat cuboctahedron faces with airfoil-shaped panels
- Outdoor wind testing with trees as a visual wind-speed reference
- Observation of rotational direction to verify thrust mechanism

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comments are mostly brief, conversational responses to viewer suggestions rather than deep technical clarifications. A few notable points:
- **Motor placement**: He was thinking of mounting the motor/generator in the centre of the structure.
- **Expected output**: Acknowledges the device will likely only generate "a few watts" given the design scale.
- **Bearings**: Confirms he did incorporate bearings into the build but realised he hadn't discussed them on camera.
- **Rotational speed**: Notes the device "was turning quite quickly" without load and doesn't expect very high speed under load.
- **Structural role of the frame**: States the plastic frame "makes the build easier and adds structural strength."
- **Efficiency acknowledgement**: Concedes a circular fan would be more efficient but "wouldn't look as cool."
- **Attitude**: Repeatedly emphasises he is learning as he goes and "just having fun," welcoming tips from commenters with expertise.
