## Overview

Robert demonstrates a self-made conductive ink pen constructed from a fine-tipped HDPE needle dropper bottle filled with his conductive ink. He shows how the pen allows users to draw circuits directly onto paper and attach tiny surface-mount LEDs to light up custom designs — a significant usability improvement over brush application. He closes by envisioning a range of educational kits (light-up cards, school electronics demos, paper pianos) that could be built around this approach.
