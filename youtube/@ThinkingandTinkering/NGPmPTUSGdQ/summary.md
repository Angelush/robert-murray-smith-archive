## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a diesel punk lamp using a disassembled car gearbox donated by Canterbury Automotive, a candle lantern, and an LED tube that mimics the look of a nixie tube. Inspired by Ross from Intelligent Tinkering, the build is intentionally simple and customisable — the key steps are arranging the gears attractively, drilling a hole for a lamp holder, and welding on an M10 nut to secure it. The result is a striking piece of industrial art that Robert ultimately gifted back to Canterbury Automotive as a thank-you.

## Key Topics
- Diesel punk aesthetic and its appeal as a design style
- Repurposing automotive parts (gearboxes) as art/lamp bases
- Simple lamp-making: fitting a lamp holder into a found object
- Credit to Ross from Intelligent Tinkering as the creative inspiration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Gearbox** — donated by Canterbury Automotive; disassembled for gears, rings, bell housing, and clutch plate area used as structural elements
- **Candle lantern / candle holder with glass** — repurposed as the lamp shade/body
- **M10 nut** — welded onto the lamp body to accept the lamp holder thread
- **Lamp holder** — standard electrical fitting screwed into the M10 nut
- **LED tubing** — described as resembling a nixie tube; used as the light source

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling a car gearbox to extract and arrange gears and rings
- Drilling a hole in a found object (candle holder) to accept a lamp holder
- MIG/stick welding a nut onto metal to create a threaded fixing point
- Composing and arranging mechanical parts for aesthetic effect
