## Overview
This short tutorial demonstrates a simple cardboard transfer technique for accurately marking hole positions before drilling, solving the common problem of misaligned holes when attaching hardware. The presenter (Luke) shows how pressing a hardware component through cardboard with a hammer creates a precise template that can then be used to transfer exact hole locations to any workpiece. The method ensures a perfect fit every time with minimal tools.

## Key Topics
- Problem of inaccurate hole marking when attaching drawer mechanisms
- Creating a cardboard transfer template using a hammer
- Using punches to cleanly cut holes in the cardboard template
- Transferring hole positions from template to workpiece
- Drilling from the transferred marks for a perfect fit

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Cardboard (cut from a cardboard box) — used as transfer template material
- Plastic workpiece — the surface being drilled

## Techniques and Methods
- **Hammer impression transfer**: placing cardboard over hardware and striking with a hammer/mallet to indent screw hole positions
- **Punch cutting**: using hole punches to cleanly remove the indented card material at the marked positions
- **Template alignment**: lining up the finished cardboard template on the target workpiece and marking through the holes before drilling
