## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith repurposes a cheap children's skipping toy purchased at Asda — whose curved hemisphere shape reminds him of an Icewind turbine — into a simple, low-friction air motor rotor. By mounting the toy halves on a bottle-based float and setting it in a water-bearing (a technique covered in previous videos), he demonstrates a nearly frictionless spinning rotor that runs on a brief puff of air, and notes it could easily become a generator or motor with the addition of magnets and a coil.
