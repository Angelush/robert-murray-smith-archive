## Overview

In this short video, Robert Murray-Smith completes a prototype axial flux reluctance motor built from six "magnetically assisted solenoids" first developed in video 104.2. He wires them in a three-phase Y configuration to form a six-pole stator, adds a simple four-pole steel rotor made from builder's board and mild steel bar, and demonstrates the motor spinning as a proof of concept.
