## Overview
In this short video, Robert Murray-Smith completes a prototype axial flux reluctance motor built from six "magnetically assisted solenoids" first developed in video 104.2. He wires them in a three-phase Y configuration to form a six-pole stator, adds a simple four-pole steel rotor made from builder's board and mild steel bar, and demonstrates the motor spinning as a proof of concept.

## Key Topics
- Recap of the magnetically assisted solenoid from video 104.2
- Arranging six solenoids in a circular Y-configuration stator
- Three-phase wiring (six-pole stator, opposing pairs per phase)
- Axial flux motor design principles
- Four-pole reluctance rotor construction
- Proof-of-concept demonstration without a dedicated motor controller

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Builder's board / Sintra board** — used for the rotor body/arms
- **Mild steel bar (1-inch / 25 mm, cut to 2-inch / 50 mm lengths)** — glued onto rotor arms as the reluctance poles
- **Magnetically assisted solenoids (×6)** — stator elements from previous video

## Techniques and Methods
- Wiring solenoids in a three-phase Y configuration with opposing pairs per phase
- DC polarity switching to control magnetic field direction (on/off/reverse)
- Rotating magnetic field via phase sequencing
- Rotor fabrication using only a drill press and hacksaw (no lathe)
- Manual switch-flipping to sequence phases as a substitute motor controller
