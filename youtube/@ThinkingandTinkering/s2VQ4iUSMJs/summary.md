## Overview
This video demonstrates how to make a simple smoke flare (smoke bomb) using a three-ingredient pyrotechnic mixture packed into a short PVC pipe casing with a homemade fuse. The presenter walks through mixing, packing, and igniting the device, achieving a large smoke output. The main takeaway is that packing more active material into the casing produces more smoke.

## Key Topics
- Smoke bomb/flare construction for hobbyists
- Ingredient ratios for a potassium nitrate–sugar–baking soda smoke mixture
- Making a homemade fuse by soaking string in a diluted mixture
- Packing a PVC pipe casing and inserting the fuse
- Scaling output by adjusting container size or quantity of material
- Teaser for a follow-up video on colored flames and colored smoke

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Potassium nitrate (KNO₃)** — oxidizer, primary ingredient (3 tablespoons)
- **Sugar** — fuel component (2 tablespoons)
- **Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate)** — smoke-moderating additive (1 tablespoon)
- **Water** — used to dilute a small portion of the mixture for fuse-soaking
- **String** — soaked in diluted mixture to form a fuse
- **PVC pipe** — ~2 inches long, used as the flare casing

## Techniques and Methods
- **Mortar and pestle grinding** — dry ingredients are ground together into a fine powder (target consistency: baby powder)
- **Fuse making** — string soaked in a water-diluted portion of the mixture, then dried and cut to length
- **Casing packing** — mixture spooned into PVC pipe and compressed in quarter-full increments using a pen or finger, leaving a gap at the top
- **Fuse insertion** — a hole bored down the center of the packed casing using an allen key; fuse threaded through
