## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a DIY method for creating a graphene-coated parabolic mirror intended for solar heating and cooking applications. By mechanically exfoliating graphite onto a polypropylene bowl using wool felt, he produces a reflective graphene surface and provides a proof-of-concept demonstration showing the mirror focusing sunlight and raising temperature from 25°C to 38°C.

## Key Topics
- Mechanical exfoliation of graphite to deposit graphene onto curved surfaces
- Graphene's infrared reflectivity (~99.9%) and its potential for solar applications
- Using a polypropylene breakfast bowl as a concave substrate
- Proof-of-concept solar focusing test
- Future directions: applying graphene flat to a sheet, then stretching it into a parabolic shape

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphite (5 micron)** — the source material rubbed onto the bowl surface to deposit graphene
- **Polypropylene bowl** — chosen as the substrate due to ease of coating compared to acrylic
- **Wool felt** — used as the rubbing medium to apply graphite via mechanical exfoliation
- **Mylar sheet** — referenced as an alternative reflector material used by another creator (Nighthawk in Lights)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Mechanical exfoliation** — rubbing graphite hard against a plastic surface with wool felt to shear and deposit graphene layers (based on Japanese research papers)
- **Solar focus testing** — positioning a thermometer at the estimated focal point of the bowl to measure temperature increase as proof of reflectivity
