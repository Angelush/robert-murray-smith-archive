## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores moisture-enabled generation (MEG), a method of generating electricity from damp paper using the natural properties of cellulose fibers. When paper is moistened unevenly, hydrogen ions (H⁺) dissociate from the cellulose's carboxyl and hydroxyl groups and migrate along a moisture gradient, creating a measurable electric current. He demonstrates this with a simple experiment using aluminium foil and paper, generating ~150 millivolts just by breathing on it.
