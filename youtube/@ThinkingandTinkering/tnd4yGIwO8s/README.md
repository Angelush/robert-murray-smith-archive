## Overview

Robert travels 186 miles to Skegness on the east coast of England to visit a Ventom Dynamics wind turbine installation on the pier, tracing its design lineage back to a 1790s wind capture device conceived by Erasmus Darwin (Charles Darwin's grandfather). He explains how the Ventom turbine's nested hyperboloid funnel structure accelerates airflow through a shrouded Venturi system without needing yaw control, and argues that for distributed energy generation the principle of "nearer is better" outweighs the conventional wisdom that "bigger is better."
