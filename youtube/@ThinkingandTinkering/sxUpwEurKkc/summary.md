## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith unboxes and performs a first-look setup of the Elegoo Neptune 4 Max, a large-format FDM 3D printer with a 420×420×480mm build volume. He walks through the full setup process — assembly, bed leveling, Wi-Fi connection, and filament loading — and shares his enthusiasm for the machine based on nearly a year of positive experience with earlier Elegoo models. The video concludes with a preview of upcoming speed comparison tests against the Neptune 3 Max.

## Key Topics
- Background on Elegoo as a company (founded 2011, started with Arduino kits)
- Evolution of the Neptune printer line (Neptune 1 through 4 series)
- Robert's philosophy on 3D printers as practical tools, not objects of worship
- Unboxing and assembly of the Neptune 4 Max
- Print speed improvements in the Neptune 4 series (up to 500 mm/s top speed, 250 mm/s set speed vs. ~60 mm/s on older machines)
- Build volume: 420×420×480mm — described as "half a meter cubed"
- Hardware features: 60W all-brass hot end, ceramic heater, dual gear feed, filament runout sensor, power loss recovery
- Bed leveling process (manual knob adjustment + 121-point automatic leveling)
- Z-offset calibration
- Wi-Fi setup via built-in wireless card
- Filament loading procedure
- Slicing software: Elegoo-modified Cura
- Mention of the Elegoo Orange Storm Giga (800×800×1000mm industrial model)
- Real-world impact of FDM printing (Robert Sanson's $75,000 switched reluctance motor prize; University of Sheffield rethinking electric motors; printed jet engines)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA Plus** — used as the reference filament for speed benchmarking (250 mm/s set speed)

## Techniques and Methods
- Manual bed leveling using paper-slip method at 6 points (knob adjustment)
- Automatic 121-point bed leveling via onboard firmware
- Z-offset calibration using paper-slip resistance test
- Filament loading via dual-gear direct drive extruder (lever-press and manual feed)
- Wi-Fi network configuration via capacitive touchscreen menu
- Slicing with Elegoo Cura (modified Ultimaker Cura)
