## Overview
A short Advent Science Magic video (Day 9) in which Robert demonstrates the "glitter finger" trick: placing glitter on water and then touching it with a soapy finger causes the glitter to rapidly flee to the edges. He uses this simple visual to explain the molecular structure of soap and why it is effective at removing dirt and oil from skin.

## Key Topics
- The "glitter finger" surface tension demonstration
- How soap molecules are structured (amphiphilic — one oil-loving end, one water-loving end)
- Why soap is effective at lifting dirt (which bonds to skin oils) into water
- Soap as an everyday example of "science magic"

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Glitter (used as a surface tension visualiser on water)
- Water
- Soap (discussed in terms of its dual molecular structure)
- Skin oil (referenced as the agent that binds dirt to hands)

## Techniques and Methods
- Floating glitter on the surface of water to visualise surface tension
- Touching the water surface with a soapy finger to break surface tension and displace glitter
