## Overview
Robert demonstrates and explains the key differences between thermoelectric coolers (TECs) and thermoelectric generators (TEGs), using a stove-top fan as a practical entry point. He clarifies why cheap TEC modules (commonly used in DIY projects) underperform or fail when used for power generation, and outlines the four construction differences that make dedicated TEGs superior. The main takeaway is: if you want meaningful electrical output, spend a little more on a proper TEG rather than repurposing a TEC.

## Key Topics
- How stove-top fans use thermoelectric generators to power themselves
- The Peltier effect (thermoelectric cooling) vs. the Seebeck effect (thermoelectric generation)
- Why TECs and TEGs are physically interchangeable in principle but not in practice
- Four construction differences between TECs and TEGs: lapping, element size, solder type, and module construction
- Why cheap TEC1-12706 modules are popular but limited for generation use
- The SP1848 as an affordable dedicated TEG option
- Using graphite foil vs. thermal paste for better thermal contact

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Ceramic plates (white)** — used as the outer faces of TEC/TEG modules
- **N-type and P-type semiconductors** — the active elements forming thermoelectric couples
- **Tin-antimony solder** — used in TEGs, rated to ~240°C
- **Low-melting-point solder (~138°C)** — used in TECs, fails around 100–110°C
- **Aluminium (flame-sprayed)** — used for hot-side contacts in TEG construction
- **Teflon** — used for wire leads in TEGs
- **Graphite foil/sheet** — used for thermal spreading and as a compliant thermal interface material
- **PVC insulation** — used on TEC wire leads (lower temperature rating)
- **Thermal paste** — mentioned as a usable but inferior alternative to graphite foil

## Techniques and Methods
- **Lapping** — precision thickness-matching of module faces to ensure uniform heat distribution across multiple modules in series
- **Flame spraying aluminium** — for forming hot-side electrical contacts in TEG modules
- **Series connection of multiple modules** — for higher voltage output; performance limited by the weakest module
- **Graphite foil as thermal interface** — compresses into a gasket-like seal for superior thermal conductivity vs. paste
- **Swapping TEC for TEG** — direct drop-in replacement in DIY projects for improved power output
