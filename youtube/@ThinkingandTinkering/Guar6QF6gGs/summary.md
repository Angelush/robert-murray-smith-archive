## Overview
Robert unboxes and assembles the VigoTech VGL7X (L3X), a 20W diode laser cutter he purchased for £145 from Banggood, comparing it to his existing K-40 CO2 laser. He walks through the assembly process, installs the VegoWorks 3 software, and runs a brief initial test on paper. The video concludes that the machine looks promising as a simpler, more accessible alternative to CO2 lasers, with cutting tests planned as a follow-up.

## Key Topics
- Comparison between diode lasers and CO2 (K-40-type) lasers
- Unboxing and assembly of the VigoTech VGL7X 20W diode laser
- Software installation (VegoWorks 3)
- Initial test cut on paper
- Safety considerations for open-frame Class IV lasers
- Stability improvements using a tempered glass base

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned

## Techniques and Methods
- Assembly of an open-frame diode laser CNC machine (attaching drive arms, carriage head, belt tensioning, laser head mounting with M3/M5 screws)
- Belt tensioning with fold-over nut slot system
- Laser cable routing through frame slots before mounting the laser head
- Adding rubber feet bonded to a tempered glass sheet for stability
- Software-controlled outline/raster cutting via VegoWorks 3 (speed and laser strength settings)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Fumes/ventilation:** There is little you can do about fumes when cutting certain materials — use a fan for extraction or avoid problematic materials entirely.
- **Electrical safety:** The machine has no earth/ground connection and doesn't need one. The power brick is a Class 2 electrically isolated device; the DC side runs at low enough voltage to be safe. He warns that *adding* an earth would actually make it more dangerous.
- **Voltage caution:** Don't touch the 60V rail, but anything below 50V is effectively harmless under normal conditions.
- **Class IV laser hazards:** Repeatedly directs commenters to read HSE and UK Government guidance on Class IV lasers. He notes the real hazards are fumes and fire risk, not the beam itself. Enclosing the machine in a box would address the open-beam concern.
- **Enclosure:** Confirms it is possible to build an enclosure for the machine for those who want one.
- **Follow-ups:** Confirmed multiple times he would be posting cutting tests within a day or two.
- **Glass base:** Acknowledges the tempered glass was just the first thing to hand as a weighting solution.
- **Prior CNC experience:** Mentions he built a CNC machine in a similar DIY fashion previously.
