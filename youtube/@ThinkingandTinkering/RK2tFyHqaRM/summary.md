## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores forgotten and historically significant battery chemistries from late 19th and early 20th century issues of *The Model Engineer and Amateur Electrician* (1890s–1910s). He demonstrates four distinct battery types — the Bennett, Lalande, Chromate, and Daniel cell variants — hands-on, arguing that lost historical knowledge is worth preserving and revisiting. His central thesis is that the past is "jam-packed full of really good ideas" that were shelved, forgotten, or unfashionable rather than disproven.

## Key Topics
- The philosophy of recovering lost technical knowledge from historical publications
- The Bennett Battery (zinc-iron in KOH) and its lineage toward nickel-cadmium batteries
- The Lalande Battery using a copper oxide depolarizer paste on iron
- The Chromate/Dichromate cell: a high-energy-density but hazardous battery type
- The Daniel cell and its gravity-battery variant using activated carbon to stabilise boundary layers
- The historical progression from the voltaic pile → Daniel cell → gravity battery → nickel-iron → NiCd

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Zinc** — anode material; ideally amalgamated or alloyed to suppress hydrogen evolution
- **Mercury** — historical amalgamation agent for zinc; noted as hazardous and no longer used
- **Iron / steel wool** — cathode material in the Bennett Battery
- **Carbon rod / carbon felt** — electrode material used across multiple battery types
- **Potassium hydroxide (KOH)** — electrolyte (225 g in 500 mL water) for Bennett and Lalande cells
- **Black copper oxide (CuO)** — depolarizer in the Lalande Battery paste; sourced as pottery glaze
- **Magnesium oxide (MgO)** — component of Lalande paste; acts as a geopolymer binder
- **Magnesium chloride (MgCl₂)** — component of Lalande paste; geopolymer activator
- **Potassium dichromate** — electrolyte base for the chromate cell; REACH-regulated since 2017, carcinogenic
- **Sodium dichromate / chromic acid** — alternative chromate cell electrolytes mentioned
- **Sulfuric acid (98%)** — used in chromate electrolyte (8.5 mL) and historically for zinc amalgamation
- **Potassium nitrate** — 16 g added to chromate electrolyte "secret sauce" to increase activity
- **Copper sulfate** — cathodic electrolyte in Daniel cell variant
- **Zinc sulfate (1 molar)** — anodic electrolyte in Daniel cell variant
- **Activated granulated carbon** — used in Daniel cell to adsorb and stabilise the copper sulfate boundary layer; sourced as pond filter media
- **Nickel** — briefly trialled as electrode, observed to form nickel hydroxide surface layer
- **Cadmium** — mentioned as the substitution that produces a NiCd battery

## Techniques and Methods
- **Zinc amalgamation** — rubbing zinc with sulfuric acid then mercury to suppress hydrogen evolution (described historically; not performed due to hazards)
- **Electrode fabrication** — wrapping carbon felt or steel wool around a carbon rod, secured with J-cloth separator and string
- **Lalande paste preparation** — mixing CuO, MgO, and MgCl₂ (25:1:1 ratio) with water into a self-setting geopolymer paste; applied to steel wool and allowed to cure
- **Chromate electrolyte preparation** — dissolving potassium dichromate in a solution of dilute sulfuric acid and potassium nitrate
- **Gravity battery layering** — using density differences between copper sulfate and zinc sulfate solutions to maintain a stable boundary layer, assisted by activated carbon adsorption
- **Zinc activation mechanism** — historical slide/lever mechanism to lower zinc into chromate acid only when needed, preventing continuous corrosion
- **Hybrid supercapacitor configuration** — noted as an emergent property of carbon-vs-metal electrode arrangement in KOH
