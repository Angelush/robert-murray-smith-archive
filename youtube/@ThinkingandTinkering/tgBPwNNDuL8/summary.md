## Overview
Robert explores using a powdered iron toroid core as an alternative to laminate steel for winding motor coils. He builds a simple electromagnet on the toroid using a custom plastic former, then demonstrates the concept of "magnetic assist" using ceramic ring magnets to increase holding strength without increasing current draw. He concludes that this coil design is notably easier to fabricate than laminate steel equivalents and warrants further investigation for reluctance motor applications.

## Key Topics
- Powdered iron toroid cores as an alternative to laminate steel for coil winding
- Designing and 3D-printing a plastic former to hold the toroid during winding
- Constructing a toroid-core electromagnet
- Magnetic assist: boosting electromagnet strength using permanent magnets
- Potential application of this coil design in reluctance motors

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Powdered iron toroid core — used as the coil core in place of laminate steel
- Ceramic ring magnets — used to provide magnetic assist to the electromagnet
- Laminate steel — mentioned as the conventional alternative, not used here
- Plastic — used to fabricate the coil-winding former

## Techniques and Methods
- Fabricating a custom plastic former to mount the toroid for coil winding
- Hand-winding a coil around a powdered iron toroid core
- Testing electromagnet performance by measuring current draw (amps) at 12V
- Applying magnetic assist by adding ceramic ring magnets to an existing electromagnet
