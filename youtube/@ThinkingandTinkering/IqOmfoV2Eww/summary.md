## Overview
This short tutorial demonstrates how to use dowel joinery as an alternative to wood screws when joining pieces of raw wood, preserving the natural appearance of the material. The presenter (Luke) walks through the full process of cutting, gluing, and installing dowels, then sanding them flush. The result is a strong, visually clean joint that is nearly invisible once sanded.

## Key Topics
- Dowel joinery as an alternative to wood screws
- Maintaining the aesthetic of raw/natural wood
- Drilling consistent holes to the correct depth for a controlled protrusion
- Using wood glue to secure dowels
- Sanding dowels flush with the wood surface

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Raw wood (frame construction)
- Wooden dowels (cut to 6.2 cm)
- Wood glue
- Sandpaper / sander

## Techniques and Methods
- Drilling holes to a precise depth (6 cm) slightly shallower than the dowel length (6.2 cm) to ensure a small protrusion
- Applying wood glue to the dowel before insertion
- Tapping the dowel flush using a mallet
- Sanding the protruding dowel flush with the grain
