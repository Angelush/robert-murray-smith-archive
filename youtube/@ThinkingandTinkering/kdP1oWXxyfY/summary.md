## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith traces the history of "water fuel car" claims from Jules Verne's 1874 prediction through a string of fraudulent inventors up to the present day, clarifying what a water car actually is versus hydrogen/HHO systems. He concludes that the real reason we don't have a true water-fuel car isn't government conspiracy but rather that nobody has seriously pursued the correct approach: using water itself as a fuel via cavitation and high-voltage arc phenomena, rather than converting it to hydrogen first.

## Key Topics
- Historical timeline of water-as-fuel concepts, from Jules Verne (1874) to the 1970s oil crisis and beyond
- Clarification of what a "water car" is *not*: water injection, steam engines, hydrogen cars, HHO fuel enhancement
- Survey of notable water car claims and inventors: Garrett (1935), Stan Meyer, Dennis Klein/Aquagen, Genesis World Energy, Genopax, Agha Waqar (Pakistan 2012), Daniel Dingle (Philippines), Charla Primal (Sri Lanka)
- Pattern of fraud and pseudoscience running through most water fuel car claims
- The conceptual limitation: conflating "fuel" with "something that burns," leading everyone toward hydrogen/HHO instead of true water fuel
- The Graneau (Peter and Neil) cavitation/high-voltage arc research as a genuinely promising alternative direction
- Cavitation physics — mechanical and electrical triggering — as a legitimate mechanism for extracting energy from water itself

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Water (H₂O)** — the proposed fuel throughout
- **Hydrogen (H₂)** — produced by electrolysis; the actual energy carrier in most "water car" devices
- **Oxygen (O₂)** — co-product of water electrolysis
- **HHO / Brown's Gas** — partial electrolysis product, mixture of hydrogen and oxygen, burnt in cylinder
- **Methane** — noted as the dominant source (via cracking) for ~96% of commercial hydrogen production
- **Metal hydride** — mentioned in the context of Genopax's electrolyte membrane device
- **Oxyacetylene** — referenced as the application Aquagen pivoted to after abandoning water-car claims

## Techniques and Methods
- Electrolysis of water (splitting water into hydrogen and oxygen using electrical current)
- Water injection into carburettor/piston (distinguished from true water-fuel use)
- Hydrogen/HHO injection as a fuel economy enhancer
- High-voltage arc discharge across water (Graneau experiments)
- Cavitation — mechanically or electrically induced collapse of bubbles in water — as an energy-release mechanism
- Steam expansion as a working fluid (steam engines; noted as distinct from fuel use)
- Resonance-based water splitting (Stan Meyer's claimed spark-plug replacement)
