## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith unboxes and sets up the Aufero Laser 2 (model LU2-4-LF), a diode laser cutter/engraver sent to him by Arturo for review. He provides a brief history of laser cutting technology from 1960 to the present, then walks through the machine's assembly, laser head options, focusing procedure, and key design features. The main takeaway is that modern diode laser cutters like this one are dramatically simpler, cheaper, and more user-friendly than earlier CO2 systems.

## Key Topics
- Brief history of laser cutting: CO2 lasers (1967 Boeing, 1975 desktop), diode lasers (1962 Robert Hall, 1970s CW operation)
- Comparison of CO2 vs. diode laser cutters (cooling, ventilation, complexity, cost)
- Overview of Aufero Laser 2 laser head options (LU2, LU2-4-SF, LU2-4-LF) and optical power ratings
- Machine assembly process and included tools
- Laser focusing procedure for the LF head
- Machine design features: no fixed home position, workspace dimensions, modular design
- Air assist system (mentioned but not used)
- Preview of upcoming software/control video

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Acrylic sheet (A4, 3–5 mm thick, referenced as an example cutting material, noted as costing ~£7–10 per sheet)

## Techniques and Methods
- Diode laser cutting and engraving
- Laser focusing using a physical gauge bar and thumbscrew (LF head method)
- Air assist (compressor-fed, mentioned as an option but not demonstrated)
- Setting laser origin/home by positioning the head manually on the material
