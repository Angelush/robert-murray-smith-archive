## Overview
Robert attempts to build a small water-powered generator that lights an LED, experimenting with different ways to drive a USB fan used as a generator. After two failed approaches — gravity feed (too low pressure at 2.2 psi) and adding dumbbell weights to increase pressure (the "dumb idea") — he settles on a manual water balloon pump as the power source. The video ends with a live demonstration of the working setup, though the structure appears to collapse at the end to comedic effect.

## Key Topics
- Building a water-powered electricity generator from salvaged/everyday parts
- Troubleshooting low water pressure in a gravity-fed system
- Using a USB fan in reverse as a small generator
- Recycling water in a closed loop for continuous pumping
- Using a jewel thief boost converter circuit to drive an LED

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Water (working fluid)
- Plastic bottle (water reservoir/header tank)
- Rubber tubing (connecting components)
- Manual water pump (originally designed for water balloons)
- USB fan (repurposed as a generator)
- LED (output indicator)
- Jewel thief circuit (boost converter to step up voltage to drive the LED)
- 2× 10 lb dumbbells (used as a pressure weight — failed approach)

## Techniques and Methods
- Gravity-fed water flow to drive a turbine/fan generator
- Mechanical pressurisation using dead weights to increase water pressure
- Manual pumping to drive water through the generator
- Closed-loop water recycling (water returned to pump after passing through the wheel/fan)
- USB fan operated inverted to prevent water ingress into the motor
- Jewel thief circuit for low-voltage LED driving
