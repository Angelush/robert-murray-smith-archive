## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a pedal-powered wooden generator frame entirely from MDF and salvaged materials, following up on a previous wooden gears video. The build centres on a large 72-tooth hand-cut cog meshing with a 24-tooth cog to achieve a 3:1 mechanical advantage, driving a pulley that can be connected to any motor or generator. The project demonstrates that a functional human-powered mechanical transmission can be made with basic woodworking tools and scrap materials, though the electrical output ultimately depends on whatever generator is attached.
