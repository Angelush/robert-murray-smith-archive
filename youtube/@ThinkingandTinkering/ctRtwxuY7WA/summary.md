## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how a complete beginner can get started with 3D printing by combining freely available files from repositories like Thingiverse with simple modifications in Tinkercad. Using himself as the subject, he creates a Doctor Who-themed bobblehead by merging a 3D scan of his own head with a downloaded Doctor Who figurine body. The key takeaway is that you don't need to master 3D modelling from scratch — remixing existing open-source designs is a legitimate and community-approved way to create personalised, satisfying objects.

## Key Topics
- Accessibility of 3D printing for beginners vs. mastery of the craft
- Free online repositories for downloadable STL files (Thingiverse, Printables, Cults3D)
- Remixing open-source 3D designs with attribution
- Using photogrammetry or a 3D scanner to capture a head scan
- Designing a bobblehead in Tinkercad: modifying a figurine body and integrating a personal head scan
- Slicing a model in UltiMaker Cura and printing on an Elegoo Neptune 3 Pro
- Assembling the physical bobblehead with a spring and adhesive

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA or similar filament** — implied by FDM printing on the Neptune 3 Pro (33g used)
- **Cyanoacrylate (crazy glue / super glue)** — used to fix the spring into the head and onto the neck post
- **Mitoché kit activator (cyanoacrylate accelerator)** — sprayed to cure the glue quickly and hold the spring solidly in place
- **Metal spring** — 5 mm diameter × 25 mm long, the bobble mechanism connecting head to body

## Techniques and Methods
- **3D scanning** — using a Revo Point scanner (previously filmed); photogrammetry alternatives mentioned (3DF Zephyr, Colmap)
- **STL file downloading and remixing** — sourcing a Doctor Who figurine body from Thingiverse and adapting it
- **Tinkercad modelling** — importing STL files, scaling, adding a base, using boolean hole operations to decapitate the figurine, designing a cylindrical neck post with spring socket, hollowing the head underside with a two-diameter hole for spring fitment, merging/grouping objects
- **Slicing in UltiMaker Cura** — configuring raft and support (touching build plate), generating G-code
- **FDM printing** — on an Elegoo Neptune 3 Pro
- **Cyanoacrylate bonding with accelerator** — for permanent spring attachment

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are almost entirely brief, good-natured banter with his audience ("cheers mate", "lol", "it was the coat that swung it!") and contain no substantive technical clarifications. One reply is mildly informative: when a commenter apparently provided background history on bobbleheads, Robert acknowledged he hadn't known it — consistent with his on-screen admission that the history he cited came from research. He also confirms in reply to one commenter that difficulty depends entirely on the goal: *"if you want to do some super clever super difficult stuff — sure why not — if you want to chop it up for a bobble head — piece of cake,"* reinforcing the video's central message.
