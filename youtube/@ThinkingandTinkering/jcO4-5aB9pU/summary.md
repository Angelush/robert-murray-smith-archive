## Overview
In this personal video, Robert uses his wedding anniversary — and the Lego Star Wars Stormtrooper Helmet his wife gifted him — as a springboard to reflect on his philosophy of life: that excess is the real problem, not consumerism itself, and that meaningful relationships are one of the three foundations of a happy life. He then builds the 647-piece Lego set in stop motion for viewers.

## Key Topics
- Clarifying his attitude toward consumerism: it's *over*-consumerism he dislikes, not buying things for fun
- His "three pillars of a happy life": something to worship, a job you love, and someone you can love
- His wedding anniversary and heartfelt tribute to his wife
- Lego as a legitimate adult hobby, noting Lego has begun advertising to adults
- Stop-motion build of the Lego Star Wars Stormtrooper Helmet (set #75276, 647 pieces)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Lego bricks (ABS plastic) — 647 pieces comprising the Stormtrooper Helmet set

## Techniques and Methods
- Stop-motion video recording of a Lego build assembly
