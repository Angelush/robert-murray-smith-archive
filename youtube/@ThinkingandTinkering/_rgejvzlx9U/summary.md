## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to reduce graphene oxide into graphene using green tea extract — a safe, non-toxic process aligned with the principles of green chemistry. He prepares a green tea extract by steeping tea bags in water, mixes it with a dilute graphene oxide solution, and heats it at 90°C for 10 hours with stirring. The result is a jet-black graphene suspension produced without any harsh chemicals.

## Key Topics
- Green chemistry and its application to graphene production
- Reduction of graphene oxide using plant-based reducing agents
- Preparation of green tea extract as a reducing agent
- Visual evidence of reduction progress (colour change from brown to black)
- Concentration and dilution of graphene oxide solutions

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Green tea bags** — used to prepare the reducing extract
- **Graphene oxide** — the starting material, made via the Tour method from 45-micron flake graphite, ~5 g/75 mL concentrated stock
- **Distilled water** — used to top up the solution during prolonged heating
- **Green tea extract** — 200 mL prepared by stirring tea bags for 30 minutes; acts as the reducing agent
- Other plant-based reducing agents mentioned in passing: acacia leaves, orange peel

## Techniques and Methods
- Steeping green tea bags in 200 mL water for 30 minutes to produce the extract
- Diluting graphene oxide stock (3 mL added to 200 mL extract) to achieve ~0.5 mg/mL concentration
- Heating the mixture at 90°C for 10 hours with continuous stirring
- Periodic top-up with hot distilled water to compensate for evaporation
- Visual monitoring of reduction via colour change (brown → black)
