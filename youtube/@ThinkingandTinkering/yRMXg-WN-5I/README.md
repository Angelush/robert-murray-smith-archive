## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores whether it's possible to build a DIY flameless catalytic heater as a cheaper alternative to expensive commercial platinum-based units. He references a 1965 US Defense Department document by Victor Shikov outlining a multi-oxide catalyst formulation using potassium dichromate, manganese sulfide, and cobalt nitrate. He demonstrates the process on carbon felt, concluding it works but recommends mineral wool as a better substrate.
