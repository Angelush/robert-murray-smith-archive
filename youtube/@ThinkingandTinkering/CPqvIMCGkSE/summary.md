## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents the Dynapod Mark III, the third iteration of his pedal-powered machine design, incorporating two key improvements requested by viewers: a flywheel and a chain drive system. The Mark III is built primarily from salvaged exercise bike and bicycle parts, welded together to create a robust, gear-changeable human-powered mechanical output device. It represents a significant upgrade over previous versions in terms of power transmission quality and mechanical robustness.

## Key Topics
- Evolution of the Dynapod series (Mark I, II, and III) and design rationale
- Adding a heavy flywheel (salvaged from an exercise bike) for energy smoothing
- Dual chain drive system: one input chain (pedalling) and one output chain (power takeoff)
- Using a bicycle gear cluster and Shimano derailleur for variable gear changing
- Welding a bicycle axle onto the flywheel to accept the gear cassette
- Building a cradle for the flywheel from exercise bike parts
- Output shaft construction using a Shimano free hub and chopped bicycle forks
- DIY flywheel alternative: filling a bicycle wheel tyre with concrete

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Exercise bike flywheel** — heavy cast flywheel repurposed as the main energy storage component
- **Bicycle gear cluster (cassette)** — used for variable gear input
- **Shimano free hub** — used as the output shaft hub
- **Shimano gear changer (derailleur)** — for in-machine gear selection
- **Bicycle axle** — cut and welded onto the flywheel to mount the cassette
- **Concrete** — suggested as a fill for a bicycle tyre to create a DIY flywheel alternative
- **Exercise bike frame sections** — repurposed as structural cradle and support arms

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting (sawing) a bicycle axle and welding it onto the flywheel hub
- Fabricating a mounting cradle from exercise bike frame sections
- Welding the cradle to the main Dynapod frame
- Chain alignment and tensioning for dual chain drive
- Cassette removal from Shimano free hub for output shaft configuration
- Welding chopped bicycle fork stays as output shaft support arms
