## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to convert a standard off-the-shelf blender into a human-powered smoothie maker by removing its electric motor and coupling the blade spindle to his "Dinopod" — a modular pedal-power platform built in earlier videos. The attachment uses a cheap 90-degree drill adapter to redirect the pedal drive into the correct rotation for the blender blades. The build succeeds on the first try, producing a smooth banana-and-strawberry smoothie powered entirely by pedalling.

## Key Topics
- The "Dinopod" modular pedal-power system (introduced in videos 1097 and 1099)
- Designing and building a smoothie-maker attachment for the Dinopod
- Disassembling a commercial blender to extract and repurpose its drive mechanism
- Using a low-cost right-angle drill adapter to change rotational direction
- Live demonstration making a banana and strawberry smoothie via pedal power
- Discussion of the concept for eco-friendly ("green") commercial smoothie bars

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Banana, strawberries, and milk (smoothie ingredients)
- Sugar (mentioned but omitted from the smoothie)

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling a commercial blender (unscrewing and identifying motor, drive, and micro-switch components)
- Drifting out the motor body from the motor shaft using a hammer and holding fixture
- Fitting a 90-degree right-angle drill attachment (purchased for £10) to convert vertical pedal rotation to horizontal blade rotation
- Sawing down the motor shaft to fit the drill chuck adapter
- Attaching a flexible coupling with grub screws to connect the Dinopod spindle to the blender shaft
- Notching the blender casing to accommodate the side-exit drive shaft

## Robert's Own Replies
The author replies in the comments are brief, informal, and largely reactive to fan suggestions rather than technical clarifications. Key points that emerge:
- He confirms the smoothie tasted good even without added sugar ("it did mate — even without added sugar lol"), echoing his on-screen scepticism about fruit's natural sweetness.
- He acknowledges a viewer suggestion about using an old drill chuck, noting he already has one lying around.
- He entertained various fan ideas for other Dinopod attachments (e.g., ice cream making, an air-raid siren), commenting "believe it or not I was thinking of an air raid siren lol."
- He affirmed the belief that the setup could be scaled into a real commercial product for a green smoothie bar.
- His preferred way to eat fruit: tinned in syrup — confirmed with "the only way to eat fruit mate!"
