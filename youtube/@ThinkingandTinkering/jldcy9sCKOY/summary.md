## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith reviews the Ortur Laser Master 3, a flagship diode laser cutter/engraver, taking a deliberately unconventional approach by focusing on build quality, ease of use, and ecosystem support rather than repeating the cutting-performance benchmarks already saturating YouTube. His main takeaway is that the laser cutter market has matured to the point where software, support, and thoughtful design are just as important — arguably more so — than raw machine specs.

## Key Topics
- The evolution of the diode laser cutter/engraver market (from cobbled aluminium extrusion frames to polished, purpose-built machines)
- Ortur Laser Master 3 physical design and build quality overview
- Notable hardware features: fold-down focus leg, key lock, emergency stop button, tilt-stop safety, rotary attachment switch
- Companion smartphone app as a low-barrier entry point vs. steeper-learning-curve software like LightBurn
- The shift in how these machines should be reviewed — emphasising support infrastructure, tutorials, and ecosystem over unboxing and speed tests
- Ortur's official YouTube channel as a superior resource for setup and usage guidance
- Extended-frame version and accessory ecosystem, including rotary attachment for cups/wine glasses
- Patent on the laser head air-cooling system as evidence of genuine R&D investment

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Extruded aluminium profile (legacy frame material, used for contrast with newer designs)

## Techniques and Methods
- Fold-down focus leg for quick, tool-free focal distance setting
- Rotary attachment engraving (cups, wine glasses) via dedicated Y/R cable and rear switch
- Smartphone app control as an alternative to desktop laser software (LightBurn)
- Frame extension with longer gantry bars to increase working area (comparable to Glowforge bed size)
