## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a wood tongue drum using salvaged oak floorboards and a minimal toolset (chop saw and drill). The video focuses on simplicity and accessibility, showing how individual wooden keys can be mounted separately on a basic box frame — making the instrument easier to build, tune, and repair than traditional single-block tongue drums. The finished instrument is gifted to his brother.

## Key Topics
- Philosophy of making instruments from scavenged or repurposed materials
- Why tongue drums are a good craft project (fun, sellable, educational about frequency/vibration)
- Construction of a resonance box from salvaged floorboards
- Individual key (tongue) design vs. the common single-block approach
- Tuning flexibility of the separate-key method
- Commercial potential: targeting accessible, affordable instruments over high-end ones

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Oak floorboards** — primary structural material for both box and keys, sourced as salvaged/discounted offcuts
- **MDF** — mentioned as an alternative material suitable for tongue drums
- **8mm bolts** — used to mount and fasten the keys to the frame
- **Tone woods** — referenced as the ideal material for higher-end instruments, contrasted with everyday woods

## Techniques and Methods
- **Chop saw cutting** — used to cut floorboards to length for box sides and keys
- **Drilling** — used to create mounting holes in the oak block/bridge piece
- **Router rebate cutting** — a channel cut along the back of each key (referenced from a previous video) to affect vibration and tone
- **Progressive length scaling** — keys cut in increments (e.g., 1 cm steps) to produce a scale of notes
- **Individual key mounting** — bolting each key separately rather than cutting a pattern into a single block, allowing independent tuning and replacement

## Robert's Own Replies
- Confirms he aims to keep videos under 10 minutes, focusing on absolute essentials — and notes how surprisingly difficult that is.
- Mentions he has **one more follow-up video** planned to simplify the design even further, and will address tuning options in that video.
- Explains he didn't use ball mallets only because his local pound store was out of stock — open to using them in future.
- On the question of holes in the drum body: says he's seen both with and without, found it made little difference, and preferred skipping the extra process — referencing Elon Musk's principle: "the best process is no process."
- On frequency and material choice: notes that frequency seems to depend on **mass, material type, and geometry**, so there likely isn't one single characteristic frequency for a given wood.
