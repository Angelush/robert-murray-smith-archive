## Overview
Luke demonstrates how to restore foggy and scratched car headlights using T-Cut, a paint scratch remover. He compares common DIY methods (toothpaste and WD-40) and explains why they are only temporary fixes, before showing that T-Cut produces a dramatic, long-lasting result with minimal effort.

## Key Topics
- Why foggy headlights are an MOT failure risk in the UK
- Limitations of toothpaste as a headlight cleaner (requires reapplication every 3–4 weeks)
- Limitations of WD-40 (fills scratches temporarily but evaporates)
- T-Cut as a more durable alternative to common DIY methods
- Step-by-step headlight restoration process
- Where to purchase T-Cut in the UK

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **T-Cut Original Restorer** — primary polishing/scratch-removing compound applied to headlights
- **WD-40** — discussed as a common but temporary fix (fills scratches, not a true repair)
- **Toothpaste** — discussed as a common but temporary abrasive polish
- **Masking tape** — used to protect surrounding paintwork during polishing
- **Polishing cloth** — used to apply T-Cut
- **Microfiber cloth** — used to buff and finish after polishing

## Techniques and Methods
- Masking off paintwork around the headlight with tape before starting
- Applying T-Cut to a polishing cloth in small dabs
- Working T-Cut into the headlight surface using small circular motions with moderate pressure
- Repeating passes until clarity improves visibly
- Finishing with a microfiber cloth wipe-down for shine
