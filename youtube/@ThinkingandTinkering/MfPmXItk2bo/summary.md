## Overview
This is a planning video in which Luke and Robert Murray-Smith discuss how they will convert a scrap ride-on lawnmower into an electric vehicle inspired by "Bessie," the yellow car associated with Doctor Who's Third Doctor. The two collaborators divide responsibilities — Robert handling design and visualisation while Luke brings mechanical expertise — and sketch out a practical plan for adapting the existing wheels, adding a larger tyre, and fitting a dual-disc brake system per wheel.

## Key Topics
- Introduction of the collaborative project between Robert and Luke
- Division of roles: Robert on design/visualisation, Luke on mechanics
- Adaptation of existing lawnmower wheels to accept larger tyres
- Braking system design: choosing disc brakes over drum brakes or regenerative motor braking
- Use of a friend's plasma cutter (Mike) to fabricate custom metal plates and discs
- Wheel balancing considerations after welding

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Existing lawnmower wheel rims and bushings
- Inner tubes and tyres (to be removed and replaced with larger tyres)
- Steel plate (for adapter discs to mount new tyres and brake discs)
- Brake discs and brake callipers (already on hand)
- Scrap metal (available at Mike's workshop)

## Techniques and Methods
- Removing existing tyres and inner tubes to work with bare rims
- Designing and fabricating a bolt-pattern adapter plate to mate a larger tyre to the existing smaller rim
- Plasma cutting circular steel plates for adapters and brake disc mounts
- Welding discs/plates onto wheel rims
- Fitting two discs per wheel: one to mount the new tyre, one for the brake disc
- Wheel balancing to correct for weld irregularities

## Robert's Own Replies
In the comments Robert added several notable clarifications:

- **Speed expectations are modest:** He acknowledges the vehicle will likely only reach 10–15 mph, framing it explicitly as "a first effort" — but hints the next build will be more ambitious.
- **Brake choice was pragmatic, not optimal:** He notes they already had brake callipers on hand, so disc brakes were chosen for convenience, and candidly admits the vehicle probably doesn't strictly need brakes — but the exercise is valuable for learning how to design and think through system components.
- **Cycle car inspiration:** He mentions he had originally been thinking about building a cycle car (which he loves), but the project was driven by being given a scrap lawnmower.
- **Unscripted collaboration:** He confirms the video was not scripted and was the first time he and Luke had filmed together.
- **Humorous philosophy on welding:** "If it doesn't move weld it — if it does move weld it until it doesn't."
