## Overview
This short update video shows Robert testing an iteration of his chlorophyll-based solar cell outdoors in direct sunlight for the first time, after previous tests had only been conducted under fluorescent lighting. The key finding is that the cell produces roughly three times more current in sunlight (~300 mA equivalent reading) compared to indoor fluorescent light (~100 mA), though absolute output remains modest at approximately 11 mW per square meter.

## Key Topics
- Chlorophyll-based DIY solar cell construction and assembly
- Comparison of solar cell output under fluorescent light vs. direct sunlight
- Current (milliamp) and voltage readings from the cell
- Discussion of power density and practical limitations
- Plans for future optimisation of cell structure and electrolyte concentration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Conductive ink** — applied to a magnet to create striped conductive lines on the cell
- **Fiberglass** — used as a substrate, soaked in the electrolyte/chlorophyll solution
- **Electrolyte solution with chlorophyll** — the active light-harvesting layer
- **Plastic sheet** — used to hold the assembly together

## Techniques and Methods
- Casting/printing conductive ink onto a magnetic substrate to form striped electrodes
- Soaking fiberglass in chlorophyll–electrolyte solution as the active layer
- Sandwiching layers under a plastic cover to form the cell
- Measuring output current (mA) and voltage under both indoor fluorescent and outdoor sunlight conditions
