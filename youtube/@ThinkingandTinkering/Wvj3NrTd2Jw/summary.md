## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates Tesla's thermal magnetic generator (invented ~1929), a reciprocating engine that exploits the Curie effect — nickel loses its magnetism when heated, then regains it upon cooling, causing oscillating or rotary motion. He builds both the original oscillating version and a novel rotary variant, then scales it up using nichrome wire wound around a pulley wheel, showing the principle works at larger scale with a blowtorch as the heat source.

## Key Topics
- History of Tesla's thermal magnetic generator (c. 1929)
- The Curie effect: nickel losing and regaining magnetism at the Curie temperature
- Original Tesla reciprocating design vs. Robert's rotary adaptation
- A novel low-friction magnetic needle bearing
- Scaling the device up with nichrome wire and a blowtorch
- DIY sourcing: nichrome wire from toasters and hairdryers, magnets from old speakers
- Thermodynamic limitations (Carnot/heat engine efficiency constraints)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Nickel wire** — the magnetic/thermally sensitive element in the original Tesla device
- **Copper wire** — structural winding on the Tesla device
- **Nichrome wire** (~80% nickel, 20% chromium) — used for the larger rotary pulley version; noted as the same material in toaster and hairdryer heating elements
- **Brass rod** — structural component of the perspex frame
- **Perspex (acrylic)** — housing for the original demonstration device
- **Nickel disc** — used as the rotor in the rotary version
- **Steel needle** — pivot element in the magnetic bearing
- **Permanent magnets** — drive the attraction; sourced from speakers or similar
- **Heusler alloy** — mentioned in comments as a material Robert was considering for future experiments (higher Curie temperature variants)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Reciprocating thermal magnetic actuation** — heating nickel past its Curie point to break magnetic attraction, then cooling to restore it, producing back-and-forth motion
- **Rotary thermal magnetic engine** — needle-through-nickel-disc geometry converting the oscillation into rotation
- **Magnetic needle bearing** — a needle piercing a nickel disc, held in place by two unequal magnet stacks; the weaker rear stack aligns the needle without contact, yielding very low friction
- **Nichrome wire winding on a pulley** — scaling up the thermally active mass by wrapping nichrome around a larger rotor
- **Blowtorch heating** — replacing a candle with a blowtorch to drive the larger-scale version
- **DIY component salvage** — extracting nichrome wire from toasters/hairdryers and magnets from old speakers

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Thermodynamic limitations confirmed:** Robert acknowledges the device is a thermodynamic heat engine and therefore subject to the same Carnot efficiency constraints as all heat engines.
- **Curie effect attribution:** Explicitly confirms the operating principle is the Curie effect, which Tesla used as the basis for this motor design.
- **Heusler alloy interest:** Mentions he was thinking of using Heusler alloy (a class of magnetic alloys with tunable Curie temperatures) as a more capable alternative material.
