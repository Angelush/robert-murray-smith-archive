## Overview
This video, presented by Luke (a contributor on Robert Murray-Smith's channel), demonstrates how to make a ring from a coin using basic workshop tools. The process involves hammering the coin's edge to fold and compress it into a band, then drilling and filing out the center to create a wearable ring. The result is a piece of unique handmade jewelry suitable as a last-minute gift.

## Key Topics
- Improvising an anvil from workshop materials
- The coin-hammering technique for forming a ring band
- Marking rotation progress to ensure even hammering
- Drilling out the coin's center
- Using a Dremel vs. a file to remove interior material
- Final cleanup and polishing with a wire brush attachment

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Metal coin (used as the ring blank)

## Techniques and Methods
- Edge-hammering on an improvised anvil to fold and compress the coin rim into a band
- Rotational marking to track even hammer passes around the coin
- Pillar drill / drill press with a drill vise to bore out the center hole
- Filing to remove remaining interior material (~10–20 minutes by hand)
- Dremel with a cutting/grinding bit to speed up interior removal (~3–4 minutes)
- Wire brush drill attachment for final surface cleanup and polishing
