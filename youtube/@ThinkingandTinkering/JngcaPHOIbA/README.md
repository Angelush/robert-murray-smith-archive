## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of a swash plate-based continuously variable transmission (CVT) that operates without gears, belts, or friction drives. He designs, 3D prints, and demonstrates a working prototype inspired by a mechanism found in a public-domain engineering book, showing how the swash plate's angle can be varied to achieve a continuously variable gear ratio. The video concludes that while the prototype is a proof of concept with room for refinement, it demonstrates a legitimate non-friction CVT principle.
