## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith argues that the axle — inherited from ancient wooden rollers — has been an unnecessary constraint on electric motor and generator design. With brushless DC motors, electronic switching divorces timing from physical rotation, making the axle redundant. This opens the door to hubless (axle-less) motors and generators, particularly "generation at the rim," which Robert presents as one of the most exciting near-future developments in power-dense electrical machines.
