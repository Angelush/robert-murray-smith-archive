## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a small electromagnetic generator powered by the rubber band motor from his previous video (2297), capable of lighting a light bulb from the stored energy of a single twisted rubber band. The generator is constructed using a serpentine coil cassette and a dual-disc neodymium magnet rotor, producing approximately 12 volts open-circuit. Robert concludes that while the prototype is basic, it proves the concept and suggests practical improvements such as using a supercapacitor to smooth and extend the power output.
