## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a 3D-printed vane motor (a simple pneumatic rotary motor) that he designed in Tinkercad and published on Thingiverse. He attaches a serpentine coil generator — wound with copper wire and fitted with neodymium magnets — to create a compact air-powered generator. The proof-of-concept successfully produces 3 volts simply by blowing into it.

## Key Topics
- Vane motor design, history, and working principle
- Advantages of pneumatic motors: power density, simplicity, light weight
- Applications: power tools, pneumatic/air-driven cars
- 3D-printed vane motor assembly (4 components)
- Integration of a serpentine coil generator onto the vane motor
- Proof-of-concept test generating electricity from breath/air

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Skater bearing — 22mm × 8mm × 7mm, used for the rotor
- 8mm steel bar — rotor shaft
- 0.2mm copper wire — 250 turns wound into the serpentine coil
- 24 neodymium magnets — 1cm × 2mm, embedded in a rotating disc
- Acrylic (Sonar) glue with activator — used to bond the connection disc
- N,N-dimethyl-p-toluidine and heptane — components of the glue activator (clarified in comments)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D modelling in Tinkercad; files shared on Thingiverse
- Vane motor assembly: rotor, housing, cap, and interlocking sliding vanes
- Serpentine coil winding (250 turns of 0.2mm copper wire on a printed former)
- Neodymium magnet disc fabrication and insertion into coil former
- Bonding components with acrylic glue + activator for fast cure
- Proof-of-concept electrical generation test (blowing by mouth)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Glue activator composition:** Confirmed the activator contains N,N-dimethyl-p-toluidine and heptane.
- **Voltage and speed:** Noted that voltage is proportional to speed, and if combined with vehicle speed, the output voltage would increase by the sum of both speeds.
- **Air supply improvements:** Agreed that bellows (including a double concertina design) would be a good, less-leaky alternative air supply.
- **Adhesion tip:** Suggested that foil washed in acetone adheres well; alternatively, lightly sanding the surface may help.
- **Steam version:** Agreed that a steam-powered version of the vane motor would be interesting and feasible.
- **Stirling-engine analogy:** Noted that a thermally-driven variant would be similar in concept to a Stirling engine.
- **General tone:** Acknowledged this was a knocked-together prototype and agreed that viewer-suggested improvements would yield better results.
