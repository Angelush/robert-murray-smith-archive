## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the Banki Mitchell crossflow water turbine, tracing its history from its 1891 invention through to its modern use in microgeneration. He repurposes the rotor from a salvaged air conditioning unit to simulate the turbine and demonstrates it working with a bucket of water, highlighting its suitability for low-maintenance, variable-load small-scale hydro power.
