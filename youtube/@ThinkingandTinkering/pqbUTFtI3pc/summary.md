## Overview
In this short Advent Science Magic video (Day 14), Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make a DIY lava lamp using common household ingredients. The experiment shows how a chemical reaction between vinegar and sodium bicarbonate produces carbon dioxide gas, creating rising and falling bubbles in oil that mimic the visual effect of a lava lamp.

## Key Topics
- Advent Science Magic series (Day 14)
- DIY lava lamp simulation using a chemical reaction
- How gas production creates movement in immiscible liquids

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Sodium bicarbonate (baking soda)** — reacts with vinegar to produce CO₂
- **Cooking oil** — the primary liquid medium in the glass; immiscible with vinegar
- **Vinegar** — reacts with sodium bicarbonate; coloured with food dye
- **Food colouring (3 drops)** — added to the vinegar to make the bubbles visually distinct

## Techniques and Methods
- Dissolving food colouring into vinegar and stirring to combine
- Pouring coloured vinegar into oil to create a density-separated two-phase system
- Acid–base reaction (vinegar + sodium bicarbonate) to generate CO₂ in situ, driving bubble formation and lava-lamp movement
