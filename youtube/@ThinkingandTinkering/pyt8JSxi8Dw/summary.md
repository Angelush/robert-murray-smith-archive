## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a home-built implementation of a flux-switching transformer based on a 1968 patent (US3368141) by Sunto Gar, which uses a permanent magnet to assist switching action in a transformer core. The experiment uses a mechanically oscillating relay to drive the transformer and compares output voltage with and without neodymium magnets in place. The magnets nearly double the output — from ~300 mV to ~500 mV — confirming the patent's claim that the permanent magnet enhances flux switching.

## Key Topics
- Flux switching as an area of ongoing interest for Robert
- Overview of the Gar 1968 patent (US3368141): a transformer with permanent magnets and a three-coil arrangement around a central iron core
- Construction of a physical implementation of the Gar transformer design
- Use of a self-oscillating relay as a mechanical switching circuit
- Comparative voltage measurements with and without magnets in the core
- Notes on saturation as a limiting factor, favouring small voltages and small magnets

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Iron core/framework** — U-shaped structure forming the transformer's magnetic circuit with an air gap
- **Neodymium (neo) magnets** — two 1 cm neo magnets, one pointing north and one south, placed between the outer frame legs
- **D cell battery (1.5 V)** — isolated supply powering the transformer primary
- **Household relay (12 V coil, rated 240 V)** — repurposed as a mechanical oscillator
- **Two 6 V batteries in series (12 V total)** — powering the relay coil

## Techniques and Methods
- **Flux switching** — using a drive coil on the central arm to switch magnetic flux through the core, assisted by a permanent magnet bias
- **Mechanical oscillation via relay** — a self-oscillating relay used as a low-frequency AC switch to drive the transformer from a DC cell
- **Isolated supply arrangement** — relay drive circuit and transformer supply kept electrically separate
- **Voltmeter measurement** — output voltage measured across pickup coils to compare performance with and without magnets
