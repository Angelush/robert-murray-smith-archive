## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith converts a salvaged rowing machine (previously used as a human-powered generator in video 107.3) and a found Achilles bicycle into a rideable "rowing cycle" — a human-powered vehicle propelled by a full rowing motion. The build reuses fabrication techniques from his earlier quad bike project, resulting in a three-wheeled contraption steered via push rods controlled by the feet while the arms and legs row to drive it forward.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing a donated rowing machine as a mobility vehicle
- Fabricating a custom rear drive axle from scrap motor parts
- Designing a central drive cog to prevent the pulling-to-one-side issue encountered on the quad bike
- Adapting the push rod steering system from video 1066
- Integrating the rowing machine frame with a salvaged bicycle frame

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Rowing machine frame (salvaged from neighbours)
- Achilles bicycle frame (found by the road)
- Motor axles (pulled from a dismantled motor, used to fabricate the drive axle)
- Large connecting bolt (used as central axle component)
- 14mm bar stock (center piece of drive axle)
- Cog/sprocket (from the Achilles bike)
- Cotter pin
- Plastic spacers (from another bike)
- Scrap steel tube/pipe (general fabrication)

## Techniques and Methods
- Welding (joining axle components and attaching the rowing machine frame to the bike rear end)
- Metal cutting (chopping frame legs square, trimming axle stock)
- Custom axle fabrication from salvaged motor shafts and hardware
- Push rod steering assembly (same design as video 1066, operated via heel/ball-of-foot pressure)
- Cog-and-chain drive system with central alignment

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Both hands and feet drive the vehicle** — Robert clarified that it is fundamentally a rowing machine on wheels, so all four limbs are engaged for propulsion, not just the feet.
- **Push rod steering inspired by the Sinclair C5** — he noted he liked a commenter's C5 comparison and confirmed that is actually where he got the push rod steering idea from.
- **Freewheel added back in** — after a comment flagged an issue, he confirmed he re-added the freewheel mechanism.
- **No differential expected to be a problem** — compared it to pedal trikes, which also typically lack differentials, suggesting it may not matter much in practice.
- **Only started welding ~6 months prior** — mentioned in response to someone excited about a welding find, noting how much the skill opens up.
- **Does not sell builds** — confirmed to one commenter he does not sell projects like this.
