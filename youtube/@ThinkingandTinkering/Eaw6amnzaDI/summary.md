## Overview
Robert demonstrates how to build a seat for his "paper trike" recumbent bike project, breaking the build into three components: a structural cradle, a thin plywood seat backing, and foam/leatherette upholstery. The process uses a free downloadable template and simple woodworking techniques, and Robert notes the finished seat shape is attractive enough to work as a standalone contemporary chair design.

## Key Topics
- Overview of the three-part seat assembly: cradle, seat board, and upholstery
- Sourcing and printing a cradle template (PDF, 4 sheets) from the internet
- Cutting matching cradle sides from plywood accurately using a screwed-together registration trick
- Installing dowels to set cradle width and allow mounting on the bike frame
- Bending thin ply in situ using the cradle as its own press (no separate jig needed)
- Upholstering with foam exercise mat layers and leatherette covering
- Observation that the foam mat seat (without leather) looks good enough to sell as a decorative chair

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **22mm plywood (7-ply)** — used for the two cradle side pieces
- **3.6mm / 3.2mm thin plywood** — used as the flexible seat backing; bent in situ
- **Wooden dowels** — glued into drilled holes to set cradle width and interface with the bike frame
- **Wood glue** — for fixing dowels and laminating the seat ply to the cradle
- **Screws** — used for alignment and clamping during glue-up (hidden by upholstery)
- **Filler** — mentioned for filling screw holes if needed
- **High-density foam exercise mat (12mm thick)** — two layers used as seat padding (~25mm total)
- **Spray adhesive** — used to bond foam layers to the seat board
- **Leatherette / leather** — outer upholstery covering

## Techniques and Methods
- Printing and assembling a multi-sheet PDF template, cutting out cradle shapes with a jigsaw
- Screwing two cradle pieces together before final shaping to ensure identical registration
- Using the assembled cradle as an in-situ bending press for thin ply (applying glue, weight, and screws to hold curve while drying)
- Laminating multiple foam exercise mat layers with spray adhesive
- Basic upholstery (wrapping leatherette over foam) — Robert defers to dedicated YouTube tutorials for detail
