## Overview
Robert introduces the Printrbot Metal Simple 3D printer, walking through the full first-time setup process from bed preparation to printing a test object. The video is motivated by his work on a custom nanobubbler head — a part too precise to fabricate with hand tools alone — and concludes with a successful (if slightly imperfect) first print, demonstrating that the setup is straightforward if the calibration coordinates are followed carefully.

## Key Topics
- Why Robert purchased a 3D printer (custom nanobubbler head fabrication)
- Unboxing and overview of the Printrbot Metal Simple
- Preparing the print bed with painters/frog tape
- Hardware setup: leveling, USB connection, and filament loading
- Using Cura 15.04 slicing software to configure and send a print job
- Z-axis calibration using G-code commands (M212, M500, M501)
- First test print (a pre-loaded robot model) and evaluating the result

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA filament** — the plastic material used for printing; referenced when the head is too close to the bed and causes "balling up"
- **Painters tape / frog tape** — applied to the print bed to provide adhesion and allow clean part removal
- **Kapton tape** — mentioned as an alternative bed surface used by some people

## Techniques and Methods
- Covering the print bed with painters tape to prevent permanent adhesion and improve surface quality
- Manual X/Y axis positioning with the printer powered off
- Camera quick-release plate with spirit levels used to roughly level the print bed
- Filament loading by compressing the gear/pulley mechanism and feeding filament into the heat head
- Z-axis offset calibration via G-code commands entered in Cura's command box (`M212 Z-0.2`, `M500`, `M501`)
- Print temperature set to 210 °C
- Printing a pre-sliced test model (robot) bundled with Cura software
