## Overview
This video, presented by Luke on Robert Murray-Smith's channel, demonstrates how to salvage 18650 cylindrical and flat lithium-ion cells from donated laptop battery packs and rebuild them into functional 12V DIY battery packs. Out of roughly 83 extracted cells, about half were found to be still viable. The main takeaway is a practical recycling workflow: test salvaged cells, discard dead ones responsibly, and assemble working cells into reusable packs using nickel strip connections and a thermal cutout for basic safety.

## Key Topics
- Disassembling donated laptop battery packs to recover cells
- Testing salvaged cells to identify dead vs. working units
- Building a 12V pack from 18650 cylindrical cells
- Building a 12V pack from flat lithium-ion pouch cells connected in parallel
- Basic safety considerations (thermal cutout as a BMS substitute)
- Responsible disposal of dead cells at local recycling points

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **18650 lithium-ion cells** — extracted from cylindrical laptop battery packs; ~50 recovered, 24 viable
- **Flat lithium-ion pouch cells** — extracted from slim laptop battery packs; ~33 recovered, 16 viable
- **Nickel strip** — also salvaged/recycled, used to interconnect the 18650 cells
- **Thermal cutout switch** — used as a safety device, cuts power at ~40°C in lieu of a full BMS

## Techniques and Methods
- Mechanical disassembly of laptop battery packs (no specific technique — described as brute-force)
- Voltage testing of individual cells to sort live from dead
- Spot welding / connecting cells with nickel strip to form a battery pack
- Connecting flat pouch cells in parallel to increase amp-hour capacity
- Functional testing of completed packs using a 12V DC motor
