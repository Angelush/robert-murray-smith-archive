## Overview
Robert tests four different adhesives for bonding polyurethane (PU) foam boards (Kingspan, Celotex, Recticel) as part of a larger project to build a tribute replica of the Sinclair C5 electric vehicle. After leaving overnight bonds and performing break tests, he ranks the glues by bond strength and recommends gap-filling foam or gorilla glue as the best options depending on surface conditions.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the Sinclair C5 replica project and motivation
- Overview of PU foam insulation boards (Kingspan, Celotex, Recticel) as a modelling material
- Comparative adhesive testing across four glue types
- Bond strength evaluation via manual break tests
- Recommended glues for different surface conditions (flat vs. gapped)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Polyurethane (PU) foam board** — insulation boards sold under brand names Kingspan, Celotex, Recticel, and Silatex; used as the primary modelling material
- **Aluminium foil facing** — removed from the foam boards before use
- **No More Nails / instant grab builder's adhesive** (Unicorn brand shown) — Kingspan's own recommendation for wall installation; tested as a foam-to-foam adhesive
- **Gap-filling expanding foam** — polyurethane aerosol foam; tested as a bonding agent
- **Gorilla Glue** — expanding polyurethane adhesive; tested for foam bonding
- **Cyanoacrylate (superglue / crazy glue)** — fast-grab adhesive; tested on flat foam surfaces
- **Bamboo skewers** — mentioned as a reinforcement method (not tested in this video)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting PU foam boards into blocks and peeling off aluminium facing
- Applying four different adhesives to foam block faces and leaving overnight to cure
- Manual break/shear testing to assess bond strength and penetration depth
- Visual inspection of adhesive penetration into foam structure
- Ranking adhesives by bond performance for practical selection
