## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith presents a dry powder concentrate he developed as an eco-friendly alternative to conventional liquid hand washes. The core innovation is removing water from the product entirely, drastically reducing shipping weight and chemical usage — one teaspoon (~5.5–6g) of powder reconstitutes into a full bottle of foaming hand wash. He outlines plans to commercialise the product and potentially expand the concept to shampoos, body washes, and mechanics' foams.

## Key Topics
- Environmental and energy costs of shipping water-based cosmetics and cleaning products
- Development of a dry powder hand wash concentrate
- Reconstitution process (adding hot or cold water to the powder)
- The resulting product as a clear foaming hand wash dispensed via an airless/foam pump
- UK/EU regulatory requirements for selling soap (safety testing and product register)
- Plans to start a small business selling the product and expand the line

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Dry powder hand wash concentrate (formulation unspecified, described as proprietary)
- Water (hot tap water at ~40–50°C used for reconstitution)
- Graphite (mentioned as a benchmark soil for testing cleaning performance)

## Techniques and Methods
- Water removal from conventional liquid formulations to produce a dry powder concentrate
- Reconstitution by adding hot water (~40–50°C) and stirring to prevent clumping; dissolves in ~2–3 minutes
- Dispensing via a foaming/airless pump dispenser
- UK/EU cosmetic safety testing and registration process prior to sale
