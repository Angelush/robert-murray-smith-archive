## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a magnetostrictive "sound bug" — a device that turns any surface (such as a glass panel or window) into a speaker — using nothing more than a common inductor and a magnet. He provides background on the commercial history of sound bugs and the rare material Terfenol-D, then shows that the same magnetostrictive principle can be replicated cheaply with off-the-shelf components.
