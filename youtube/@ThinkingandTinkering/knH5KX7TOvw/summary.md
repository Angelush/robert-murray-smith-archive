## Overview
A short demonstration video showing a homemade two-can stove being run on neat vegetable oil instead of its usual fuel. Robert shows that with a small modification to the wick position and an ethanol primer, the stove can burn vegetable oil as a multi-fuel emergency option, though it produces a dirtier flame than conventional fuels.

## Key Topics
- Homemade two-can stove design
- Running a DIY stove on vegetable oil
- Using ethanol as a primer/starter fuel
- Wick adjustment for alternative fuels
- Multi-fuel capability of the stove design

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Vegetable oil — used as the primary fuel
- Ethanol — used as a primer, added around the lip to start combustion
- Two aluminium cans — the stove body (one slotted inside the other)

## Techniques and Methods
- Priming the stove by adding ethanol around the lip before ignition
- Adjusting the wick upward by ~half a centimetre to improve vegetable oil combustion
- Filling the stove no more than half full
- Adding a chimney to improve burn performance
