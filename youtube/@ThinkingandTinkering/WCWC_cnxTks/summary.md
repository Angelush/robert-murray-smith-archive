## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple electrostatic motor using his floating pen kit as the core component. The motor uses a high-voltage generator to create a corona discharge that spins a rotor made from aluminium foil strips on a paper sleeve. The build is intentionally minimal and accessible, requiring very few materials and tools.

## Key Topics
- The floating pen kit as a point bearing for low-friction rotation
- Construction of an aluminium foil rotor
- Using a high-voltage generator to drive an electrostatic (corona) motor
- Demonstrating the completed motor spinning under power

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Floating pen kit (magnetic point bearing)
- Sponge (dish-wash sponge, cut into a circular plug)
- Paper strip (approx. 7 cm × 2.4 cm)
- Aluminium (onion) tape — strips ~1 cm × 2.4 cm, four total
- High-voltage generator (eBay, ~40 kV output, 4–6 V input)
- 6 V battery
- Solder (to fix one electrode wire)

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting a sponge plug to act as a rotor hub on the pen bearing
- Wrapping and sizing a paper strip to match the sponge diameter
- Applying evenly spaced aluminium tape strips (~1 cm gap between each) to form rotor vanes
- Bending high-voltage electrode wires to point in opposing directions for corona discharge
- Powering the motor via a low-voltage battery stepped up through the HV generator
