## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith unboxes and reviews the Liberator rocket stove, a commercially made J-type rocket stove shipped to him from the US company Liberator. He explains the thermodynamic principles behind rocket stove combustion — particularly the distinction between the temperature at which gases ignite (~900°C) versus the temperature they can reach (1200°C+) — and praises the Liberator's ceramic-lined combustion pipe and targeted insulation strategy. He closes by soliciting community suggestions for potential improvements he might propose back to the Liberator team.
