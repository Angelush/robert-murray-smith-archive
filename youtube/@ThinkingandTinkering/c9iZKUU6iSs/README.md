## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple Tesla turbine in roughly ten minutes using CDs, small magnets, and hot glue. He constructs a disc stack by alternating CDs and magnets (aligned north-south) and fits them into a basic plastic case with a tangential water inlet pipe. The experiment culminates in a bonus test: holding a coil near the spinning magnet assembly generates approximately half a volt, proving even this rough build can act as a small generator.
