## Overview
Robert tests a cable drum wind turbine built from scrap wood, following a suggestion from viewer John Wynn. The turbine uses 62 ceramic magnets mounted around the rim of a cable drum, with a salvaged microwave oven transformer acting as the pickup coil. Despite being crudely constructed, it successfully generates enough power to light a 36-watt LED panel in approximately 10–12 mph wind, demonstrating that rim-mounted low-torque generation is a viable approach.

## Key Topics
- Viewer-inspired design: John Wynn's suggestion to mount magnets around the wheel rim
- Rim-mounted magnets as a strategy to increase effective RPM without high axle torque
- Debunking the "you need high torque" assumption for small wind turbines
- Microwave oven transformer repurposed as a generator stator coil
- Effect of a warped cable drum causing output flicker (varying magnet-to-coil distance)
- Philosophy: if something works when built badly, it will work better when built well
- Comparison of horizontal-axis vs. vertical-axis turbine designs
- Wind direction as a key constraint for horizontal turbines
- Plans to rebuild as a true vertical-axis wind turbine with proper thrust bearings

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Ceramic magnets** — 62 mounted around the rim of the cable drum; ~£10 for 50
- **Microwave oven transformer** — repurposed as a pickup coil/stator
- **Scrap timber / cable drum** — structural frame, warped from rain exposure
- **Super glue** — used in assembly alongside screws
- **36-watt LED lighting panel** — used as the load/output test device

## Techniques and Methods
- Mounting permanent magnets around the outer rim of a rotating wheel to maximise linear velocity and thus generator output
- Using a salvaged microwave oven transformer core as a stationary coil to capture flux from passing magnets
- Outdoor wind testing at night, using LED panel illumination as a qualitative power indicator
- Bolt-to-true-machine method proposed to correct drum wobble (attaching the warped drum to a truer rotating frame)
- Proposed simple charge controller: a diode and resistor (mentioned in comments)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Flickering cause confirmed:** "it's the changing distance between the magnets and the coil because the drum is warped" — reinforcing the in-video explanation
- **Simple power conditioning:** suggested "stick a diode and resistor in there as a simple charge controller and bingo"; also floated a supercapacitor bank as an alternative
- **On ceramic vs. neodymium magnets:** "we all rush to neodymium I have no idea why" — implying ceramics are underrated for this application
- **On low torque limitations:** acknowledged the turbine likely cannot drive a pump or motor at the axle directly; "it is low torque"
- **Goal stated:** "I am going towards a kWh genie and I want it cheap"
- **Maker philosophy (notable):** "if you hold back until everything is just right what really happens is you do nothing — basically — pick something and make a start it's a journey and who knows where you will end up"
- **On the experimental nature of his channel:** "I am unashamed about the vids not always being practical — quite often things just don't work — but I am ok with that — what I think is important is that you try"
