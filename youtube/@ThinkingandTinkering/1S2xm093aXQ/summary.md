## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build functional DIY bellows from Venetian blind slats and waterproof tape (specifically Gorilla Tape). The video walks through the full construction process step by step, resulting in a robust, flexible bellows capable of moving a surprising amount of air — useful for fire-lighting, water pumping, vacuum cleaning, or even low-energy air conditioning.

## Key Topics
- Selecting appropriate materials (Venetian blind slats and plastic-coated tape)
- Cutting and preparing slats at 45-degree angles
- Flattening curled PVC slats using an iron
- Assembling slats into a pleated bellows structure using tape
- Sealing the ends with rigid board and adding a nozzle
- Practical applications of DIY bellows

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Venetian blind slats** (plastic/PVC) — the structural ribs of the bellows
- **Gorilla Tape** (waterproof plastic cloth/gaffer tape) — primary bonding and sealing material
- **PVC electrician's tape** — used to bundle slats together initially
- **Masking tape** — used to hold slats in alignment during assembly
- **Greaseproof paper** — used when ironing slats flat
- **Plastic board / plywood / MDF** — options for sealing the ends
- **Steel bar** — used as a weight to hold slats in line during assembly

## Techniques and Methods
- Bundling and cutting slats at 45 degrees to create fold lines
- Ironing PVC slats flat using greaseproof paper to remove curl
- Taping slats in a row and applying Gorilla Tape strips to form a flexible panel
- Rolling/joining the panel into a tube and folding along the 45-degree cuts to create accordion pleats
- Sealing end caps with tape and cutting a hole for a nozzle
