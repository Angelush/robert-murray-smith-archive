## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith argues that the classical six simple machines can be reduced conceptually to just two — the lever and the inclined plane — and that understanding levers unlocks understanding of all machines. He walks through mechanical advantage, work conservation, and then applies these principles to sketch out a design for a lever-based continuously variable transmission (CVT) using a movable fulcrum, a Scotch yoke, and a ratchet mechanism.
