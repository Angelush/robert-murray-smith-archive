## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explains how permanent magnets can be switched on and off without losing power, covering the physics of magnetic reluctance that makes this possible. He demonstrates three types of switchable magnet systems — mechanical sliding chucks, electropermanent magnets, and a simple DIY flux switch — and builds a working example from neodymium magnets, an M12 bolt, and 3D-printed holders. The key takeaway is that by controlling the path of least magnetic reluctance, you can direct flux inward (off) or outward (on) with a simple 60° twist.

## Key Topics
- Magnetic chucks and their industrial uses (milling, grinding, tool holding)
- The anisotropic force property of magnets: high pull-apart force vs. low slide-apart force
- Magnetic reluctance as the magnetic analogue of electrical resistance
- Three types of switchable magnets: mechanical sliding chucks, electropermanent magnets, and DIY flux switches
- Hard vs. soft magnets and the role of Alnico in electropermanent designs
- Why electromagnet-based chucks are unsafe (lose grip on power failure)
- Safety advantage of permanent and electropermanent designs
- DIY construction of a flux switch using household/maker materials
- Potential applications including climbing robots

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Neodymium magnets (N35, 1 cm × 0.5 cm)** — used as the permanent magnet source in the DIY switch
- **Alnico (AlNiCo alloy)** — cited as an example of a soft magnet whose polarity can be reversed with a current pulse, used in electropermanent magnet designs
- **Steel** — low magnetic reluctance material; used as flux path conductor
- **M12 hex bolt and M12 nuts** — used as the structural and flux-path elements in the DIY build
- **8 mm steel bar** — used as the central axle/alignment pin in the assembly
- **3D-printed holder plate (designed in Tinkercad)** — holds magnets and nuts in the correct hexagonal arrangement

## Techniques and Methods
- **Flux path switching via sliding** — mechanically sliding a plate or magnet array to redirect flux in or out of a workpiece bed
- **Electropermanent switching** — pulsing a coil to flip the polarity of a soft magnet (Alnico), toggling the flux path without moving parts
- **Keeper arrangement** — pairing magnets pole-to-pole to contain flux internally and prevent external attraction
- **Reluctance path engineering** — arranging alternating north/south magnets around a hex bolt so a 60° rotation aligns or misaligns internal flux paths
- **3D printing (Tinkercad)** — designing a custom holder plate with magnet pockets and nut recesses
- **Pattern shared on Thingiverse** — bare/minimal design published for community adaptation
