## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explains how permanent magnets can be switched on and off without losing power, covering the physics of magnetic reluctance that makes this possible. He demonstrates three types of switchable magnet systems — mechanical sliding chucks, electropermanent magnets, and a simple DIY flux switch — and builds a working example from neodymium magnets, an M12 bolt, and 3D-printed holders. The key takeaway is that by controlling the path of least magnetic reluctance, you can direct flux inward (off) or outward (on) with a simple 60° twist.
