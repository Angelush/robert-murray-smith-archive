## Overview
In this video, Robert Murray-Smith continues his bamboo electric quad bike build by tackling the front steering columns and wheels. He experiments with using jute string coated in resin as a joining material — rather than carbon fibre or fibreglass — to attach metal steering columns to the bamboo frame, acknowledging it as an untested but worthwhile experiment. By the end of the video the wheels are fitted, the steering is linked, and only the motor installation remains before a test run.

## Key Topics
- Attaching metal steering columns to a bamboo frame
- Comparing joining materials: carbon fibre, woven fibreglass, jute string, and natural hemp fibre
- Using jute string with a resin coat as an experimental structural joint
- Salvaging components (rear axle, steering columns, handlebars) from previous bike builds
- Linking dual front wheels with a rod for synchronised steering
- Battery placement options: lead-acid on the rear carrier vs. lithium under the seat
- Fitting the rear axle with U-bolts

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Bamboo** — main structural frame material
- **Carbon fibre** — used on previous builds for structural joints; considered here
- **Woven fibreglass** (glass fibre) — suggested by community as an alternative; noted as stronger than chopped strand mat
- **Chopped strand mat** — mentioned as insufficient strength for structural joints
- **Jute string / jute lashing** — used experimentally to bind steering columns and seat to frame
- **Natural hemp fibre** — suggested as a future alternative to jute for resin-impregnated joints
- **Resin (epoxy)** — used to impregnate/coat jute joints for strength; confirmed as epoxy in comments
- **Lead-acid batteries** — planned for the rear carrier
- **Lithium battery pack** — mentioned as a lighter alternative that could sit under the seat
- **U-bolts** — used to secure base plate, rear carrier, and rear axle

## Techniques and Methods
- **Jute-and-resin wrapping** — binding metal steering columns to bamboo tubes with jute string then coating with epoxy resin as a structural joint
- **Salvaging / repurposing** — stripping steering columns, rear axle, and handlebars from scrapped children's bikes
- **Handlebar modification** — cutting and joining handlebars with a linking rod to create synchronised two-wheel steering
- **U-bolt clamping** — securing flat plates and axles to bamboo tubes without drilling or welding
- **Seat mounting** — legs cut from a dining chair; seat lashed to frame with jute
