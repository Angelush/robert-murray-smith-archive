## Overview
Robert reviews the Elegoo Neptune 3 Plus, a large-format FDM filament printer sent to him as a follow-up to his earlier Neptune 3 Pro coverage. He assembles it in about 20 minutes, praises its ease of use and Cura-based software, and uses it to print a massive integrated Archimedes screw-in-pipe component — a third of a meter tall — as the first section of a planned two-meter-tall water-lifting machine.

## Key Topics
- Review and unboxing of the Elegoo Neptune 3 Plus 3D printer
- Comparison with the Neptune 3 Pro (smaller sibling)
- Assembly process and bed leveling procedure
- Use of Elegoo's customized Cura slicer software
- Printing a large integrated Archimedes screw component from Thingiverse
- Plans for a multi-section, ~2-meter Archimedes screw machine
- Brief mention of upcoming Neptune 3 Max model

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **PLA filament** — supplied by Elegoo, used in black and white for all prints shown

## Techniques and Methods
- **FDM filament 3D printing** — using the Elegoo Neptune 3 Plus
- **Manual bed leveling** — loosening six knobs, using a paper-slip method at each point before running auto-leveling
- **Slicing with Cura** — Elegoo's ported version; importing STL, scaling, rotating, and slicing
- **High-speed printing** — Robert printed the Archimedes screw at 120 mm/s (vs. the standard 50–60 mm/s) to halve print time, accepting a slight quality trade-off on a functional part
- **Scaling STL files** — the Archimedes screw was scaled up to produce sections ~1/3 meter tall each

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Print speed:** Confirmed the large Archimedes screw was printed at **120 mm/s** (double the standard 50–60 mm/s setting), which halved print time; he accepted a small quality reduction given the functional, non-aesthetic use.
- **Print duration:** The full print took **5 days**.
- **Nozzle size:** Confirmed as **0.4 mm**.
- **Software:** Clarified the slicer is standard free Cura — "open it, grab the image, scale and slice — done."
- **Filament changing:** Mentioned he has a trick for changing filament mid-print that he plans to show in a future video.
- **Stability stays:** Clarified the bracing struts on the frame are to keep the machine rigid, not a structural failure point.
- **CAD interest:** Expressed interest in learning CAD and printing an aeromine wing, asking a commenter if they could share an STL file.
- **Rubber feet tip:** Suggested placing the printer on rubber feet to reduce vibration/noise, responding to a commenter's question.
- **Crazy glue on prints:** Recommended running cyanoacrylate (crazy glue) over printed parts to make them last longer.
