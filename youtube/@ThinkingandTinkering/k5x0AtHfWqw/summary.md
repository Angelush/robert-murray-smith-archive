## Overview
Robert discovers his Vigotec VG-17X 20W diode laser cutter has lost nearly all power output — unable to burn through tissue paper. Rather than replacing the expensive laser module (~£70), he identifies the culprit as a dirty and cracked focusing/collector lens, which costs just £6–8 to replace. The video serves as a practical repair tip for other laser cutter owners facing the same issue.

## Key Topics
- Diagnosing sudden power loss in a diode laser cutter
- Identifying the focusing/collector lens as the failure point
- Cleaning vs. replacing a dirty or cracked lens
- Cost-saving repair advice vs. replacing the full laser module
- Brief comparison to CO₂ laser cutters and why he switched

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Acetone** — mentioned as a cleaning agent for smoke residue on the lens
- **Focusing/collector lens** — plastic or glass screw-in lens, universal fit, rated for 5–20W lasers, costs £6–8

## Techniques and Methods
- Visual inspection of the collector lens by unscrewing and peering through it
- Cleaning the lens with a cotton bud and acetone (as a maintenance option)
- Replacing the focusing lens as a unit (repair method used in this video)
