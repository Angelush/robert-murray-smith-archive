## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the potential of using the shape of a samara (winged seed, specifically from a sycamore tree) as the basis for a new wind turbine blade design. He 3D scans a real sycamore samara, models it in TinkerCAD, prints three copies, and assembles them into a prototype rotor hub which he then tests outdoors in the wind. The video concludes that the samara-shaped blades do spin effectively, validating the concept as worth pursuing further.
