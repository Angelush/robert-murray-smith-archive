## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith examines LPD (Luna Pulse Drum) Clean Energy, a tidal/wave power concept promoted by James Gin Van Groy, placing it in the broader context of marine renewable energy. He explains how LPD works — capturing tidal pressure changes to drive a turbine — but argues it is more of a rebranding of existing oscillating column technology than a genuinely new idea. The video serves as a concise primer on the main categories of wave and tidal power generation.

## Techniques and Methods
- **Oscillating Water Column (OWC):** Open-bottomed coastal structure; incoming waves compress air over a turbine (commonly a Wells turbine)
- **Wells Turbine:** Blade geometry allows rotation in the same direction regardless of airflow direction, acting as a fluid rectifier
- **Oscillating Body generators:** Floating platforms or articulated structures (e.g., the Pelamis / "sea snake") that pump hydraulics or air to drive a turbine
- **Overtopping devices:** Reflector structures direct waves into an elevated reservoir; water drains through a turbine (e.g., Wave Dragon)
- **Tidal stream turbines:** Two- or three-bladed rotors anchored to the seabed, turning at 12–18 RPM; also Savonius-style vertical-axis variants
- **Tidal barrages:** Dam-like structures across tidal estuaries; gates close at high tide, water released through turbine arrays on ebb
- **Lagoon/lido capture:** Nearshore enclosure capturing high-tide water and releasing it through turbines; compatible with Archimedes screw designs
- **LPD pressure capture:** Uses tidal rise to pressurise a sealed vessel; releases through a turbine on both flood and ebb cycles
