## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith uses a visit to a historic church in Reculver, Kent (visible from a nearby offshore wind farm) to explore the question of scale in energy systems: is it better to build one large wind turbine or many small ones? Drawing on analogies from solar panels and electric motors, he argues that networks of small, modular components are often more robust, efficient, and cheaper than single large structures — and that the control electronics required are far simpler than most people assume.
