## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith introduces three common 3D printing filaments — PLA/PLA+, ABS, and TPU — drawing on roughly 10 months of hands-on printing experience. He explains the practical strengths and weaknesses of each material and shares his first successful attempt at printing flexible bellows using TPU. His main takeaway is that TPU is far easier to work with than its reputation suggests, and starting with generic slicer settings is usually enough to get good results.
