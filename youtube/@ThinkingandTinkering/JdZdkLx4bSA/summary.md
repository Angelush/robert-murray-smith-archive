## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates the third iteration of his DIY mobility scooter build, upgrading the motorized wheelchair (previously constructed from a car seat and shopping trolley) by integrating the rear axle assembly from a Chinese electric tricycle. The key engineering challenge solved in this video is implementing Ackermann steering geometry to prevent wheel slip during turns. The result is a fully functional, low-cost electric mobility chair that drives and steers well.

## Key Topics
- Recap of prior builds in the series (videos 1198, 1200, 1201, 1203)
- Fabricating custom mounting brackets to attach the tricycle rear axle to the chair frame
- Ackermann steering geometry: history, theory, and practical low-speed implementation
- Graphical floor-based method for calculating correct steering arm angles
- Assembly of the steering linkage (pivot arms, connecting rod, steering rod)
- Battery selection for the vehicle
- Test drive and handling assessment

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Box section steel (sourced from industrial shelving) — used to fabricate mounting brackets
- Lead-acid batteries — chosen as the power source, described as appropriate for a golf-cart-style application
- Lithium batteries — mentioned as a costlier alternative
- Nickel metal hydride (NiMH) batteries — mentioned as a third battery option

## Techniques and Methods
- Welding — used to attach bracing bars, new mounting brackets, steering pivot arms, and battery rack remnants
- Drilling and channel chasing — used to finish the box-section mounting brackets
- Geometric floor-marking — rear axle centre and front wheel pivot points marked on the floor to derive the correct Ackermann steering arm angle graphically
- Bolted assembly — tricycle subframe slid into fabricated brackets and secured
- Steering linkage fabrication — 90-degree levers welded to wheel hubs, connected by a tie rod to implement Ackermann geometry

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Reverse capability:** Confirmed the vehicle can be given reverse — it just needs a reverse switch added.
- **Battery choice:** Pushed back on upgrading to more expensive batteries, stating it makes no real difference to performance while adding cost.
- **Tiller vs. steering wheel:** Acknowledged the tiller arm was awkward to use but pointed out it folds down to allow the rider to get in and out — an important practical consideration.
- **Car seat vs. office chair:** Discussed the trade-off with a commenter: the car seat is more comfortable for extended use due to its contouring, but an office chair could be a good basis for a *folding* version of the build.
- **Next video tease:** Hinted that the next video would be amusing/surprising ("you are going to laugh when you see the next vid").
