## Overview
Robert tests the Elegoo Orangestorm Giga large-format 3D printer by printing a large wind turbine blade — a part he plans to use in a massive wind turbine build. The print took 14 hours (vs the slicer's estimate of ~12 hours), used 800g of filament, and produced an acceptable surface finish with minor jitter and ghosting only at the very top of the tall print. His conclusion is that the Orangestorm is a purpose-specific machine best suited to large-part fabrication, and it performed well straight out of the box.

- Step-by-step filament loading procedure
- Print quality assessment: surface finish, jitter, and ghosting on a tall print

- Plans to build a large wind turbine using three printed blades
- Post-processing plans to strengthen printed parts

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Elegoo Rapid PLA+** (3 kg spool) — main filament used; ~£14–15/kg; came bundled with the printer
- **Cyanoacrylic resin** — planned as a skin coat to strengthen and stiffen the printed blade (referenced extensively in comments; see video 2012 for technique)
- **Carbon fibre** — mentioned in comments as an alternative reinforcement coating
- **Superglue** — mentioned as an initial strengthening option to test before more involved coatings

## Techniques and Methods
- FDM 3D printing on the Elegoo Orangestorm Giga large-format printer
- Slicing STL files in **Cura** and exporting G-code to USB
- USB sneaker-net file transfer (preferred by Robert over Wi-Fi)
- Raft bed adhesion (noted in comments as his preference over brim to avoid print failures)
- Coating with cyanoacrylic resin as a post-print structural skin (details in video 2012)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Strengthening method:** Plans to coat the blade with a layer of **cyanoacrylic resin** as a skin coat; also considering **carbon fibre**. Repeatedly directs viewers to **video 2012** for the full technique and results ("the result is amazing").
- **Filament usage:** Confirms the blade used **800 grams** of filament.
- **Nozzle sizes:** Forgot to mention in the video — the printer ships with **0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8, and 1 mm** nozzles. He used the **0.6 mm** nozzle and was considering the 1 mm for faster future prints.
- **Bed adhesion:** Uses a **raft** rather than a brim, which he finds more reliable for avoiding failures.
- **Wind turbine power goal:** Hopes to achieve **kilowatt-scale** output ("I am an optimistic guy").
- **Printer noise:** Confirms it is noisy when you are in the same room.
- **Printer price/value:** At ~£2,500, acknowledges some wish-list features are absent but considers it reasonable for the price.
- **Pellet extruder:** Agrees the machine would be ideal for a pellet-based extruder adaptation.
- **Multiple print heads:** Notes adding more heads could significantly reduce print time.
- **Slicer preference:** Tried alternatives but returned to **Cura** ("horses for courses").
- **Other printers owned:** Has a **Bambu X1** (rarely used) and a **Qidi**.
