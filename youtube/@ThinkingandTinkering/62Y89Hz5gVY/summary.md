## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith unboxes and assembles the ESKUTE Voyager 27.5" electric mountain bike, a 250W rear-motor e-bike aimed at the EU/international market. He gives initial impressions on build quality, packaging, and components, then does a brief test ride in a car park with help from a younger companion named Luke. The video sets up a series of follow-up videos exploring the bike's controller and off-road performance.

## Key Topics
- Unboxing and inspection of shipping damage/packaging
- Assembly process (front wheel, handlebars, fenders, derailleur)
- Build quality and included tools/manual assessment
- Battery specifications and charging advice
- UK e-bike legal requirements (motor labeling, throttle restrictions)
- Initial test ride impressions
- Plans for future controller deep-dive and off-road testing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 36V 12.5Ah / 450Wh lithium battery (removable, charges in or out of bike)

## Techniques and Methods
- Unboxing and visual damage inspection
- Basic bicycle assembly (wheel fitting with quick-release and retaining bolt, handlebar fitting, fender attachment)
- Battery removal and external charging
- Suspension lock/unlock adjustment
- Throttle installation (demonstrated for educational purposes only in a private car park)
- Derailleur adjustment (mentioned as forthcoming, described as minor screw adjustment)
- Controller parameter exploration (KD21C controller — planned for a separate video)
