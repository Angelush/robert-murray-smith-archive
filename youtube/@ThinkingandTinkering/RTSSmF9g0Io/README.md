## Overview

Robert demonstrates a simple, accessible method for reducing graphene oxide (GO) back to graphene using nascent hydrogen — a "green" approach requiring only aluminium kitchen foil and dilute hydrochloric acid. The reaction takes about 30–60 minutes at mild heat (~35–40°C) and produces a black graphene suspension that can be cleaned up with water washes. He briefly showcases a downstream application: formulating the resulting graphene into a conductive ink currently achieving ~50 ohms/cm.
