## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith unboxes the Elegoo Mars Pro 2 Mono MSLA UV resin 3D printer, sent to him for review at a price of £280 (£260 with discount). He walks through all included components and accessories, explains how MSLA printing works, and expresses genuine enthusiasm for the technology — particularly its superior resolution compared to filament printers — while outlining his plans for setup, software configuration, and future experiments including adding graphene to resin.

## Key Topics
- Unboxing and contents overview of the Elegoo Mars Pro 2 Mono MSLA printer
- How MSLA/LCD UV resin printing works (UV light + LCD mask + fluoropolymer film)
- Comparison of MSLA resin printing vs. filament (FDM) printing
- Resin types included: standard resin and ABS-like flexible resin
- Plans for future experiments, including conductive graphene-infused resin
- Wash and cure station discussion (not included; Robert chose not to request one)
- Toolkit contents walkthrough
- Next steps: setup, levelling the print foot, software installation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Standard UV resin** — brittle, good for models; 500ml bottle (~£20)
- **ABS-like resin** — flexible/springy, better suited for engineering parts; also available in red
- **Graphene** — mentioned as a planned additive to create conductive resin prints
- **Isopropyl alcohol (IPA)** — used for washing uncured resin off prints
- **Fluoropolymer film (FEP film)** — non-stick layer at the bottom of the resin vat; spare sheets included

## Techniques and Methods
- **MSLA (Masked Stereolithography Apparatus) / LCD UV resin printing** — UV light projected through an LCD mask to cure resin layer by layer
- **Layer-by-layer resin curing** — print plate lifts through the resin pool, curing each slice
- **Post-print washing** — immersing prints in IPA to remove uncured resin
- **Post-print UV curing** — additional UV exposure after washing to fully cure prints (wash-and-cure station or sunlight/UV lamp alternatives)
- **Resin filtering** — using gauze filters included in the toolkit to strain resin
- **Foot levelling** — aligning the build plate to the vat as part of initial setup
- **File transfer via USB** — sliced print files loaded onto the printer via USB stick
