## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith walks through a complete step-by-step build of a replica Antikythera mechanism, constructed using a laser cutter and 3D printer rather than traditional brass and hand tools. The build is split into four parts covering the gear base unit, the inner planets (Sun, Mercury, Venus), the outer planets (Mars, Jupiter, Saturn), and the drive mechanism with decorative elements. Files are available on Thingiverse, and the project took approximately three days total — two days of 3D printing and one day of laser cutting.

## Key Topics
- Overview and inspiration for the build (referencing Clickspring's brass replica)
- Multi-part build methodology to avoid confusion with large numbers of gears
- Part 1: Base gear unit assembly
- Part 2: Inner planets epicycle mechanism (Sun, Mercury, Venus)
- Part 3: Outer planets epicycle mechanism (Mars, Jupiter, Saturn)
- Part 4: Frame, base plate, dial indicators, decorative columns, and feet
- Moon gear mechanism (inertia-driven)
- Hand-crank bevel gear drive mechanism
- Use of xTool P2 laser engraver for engraving planetary symbols on dial indicators
- Files available on Thingiverse; calibration covered in a separate linked video

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **5 mm acrylic sheet** — primary material for most gears (cream/white color-coded in TinkerCAD)
- **10 mm acrylic sheet** — for specific larger gears (orange color-coded)
- **2 mm acrylic sheet** — for spacers and washers (light green and orange color-coded)
- **8 mm acrylic bar/rod** — cut to lengths of 10 mm, 12 mm, 15 mm, 20 mm, 22 mm, 25 mm, 27 mm, 28 mm for pegs and axles
- **6 mm acrylic bar/rod** — cut to various lengths (25 mm, 50 mm) for axles
- **4 mm acrylic bar/rod** — cut to 20 mm and 22 mm lengths
- **10 mm clear acrylic sheet (300 × 300 mm)** — for the base plate
- **Fluorescent acrylic (5 mm)** — used for dial indicators, chosen for aesthetics
- **Super glue (cyanoacrylate)** — general adhesive; noted to leave a whitish film on clear acrylic
- **Acrylic solvent weld** — preferred adhesive for clear acrylic; dissolves and bonds acrylic without white residue
- **3D printed parts** — light blue color-coded in TinkerCAD diagrams; used for structural and mechanical components

## Techniques and Methods
- **Laser cutting** — cutting acrylic gears, spacers, and structural plates on an xTool P2
- **FDM 3D printing** — printing structural components, arms, and housings
- **Solvent welding** — applying acrylic solvent around joints to bond clear acrylic cleanly
- **Super glue bonding** — used for non-clear parts and structural joints
- **Press fitting** — gears and pegs assembled as tight press fits where rotation is not required
- **Keyed shaft assembly** — flanged/keyed gears slotted onto shaped rods to prevent free spinning
- **Laser engraving** — engraving planetary names and traditional astronomical symbols onto dial indicators using the xTool P2
- **Bevel gear drive** — 90° motion transmission from hand crank to main ring gear
- **Epicycle gear train construction** — stacking and meshing gear layers for each planetary period
- **TinkerCAD color-coded design** — using color to differentiate material thickness and type before fabrication
