## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith reviews the EcoFlow Delta 2 portable power station, framed around the UK government's warnings of potential rolling blackouts in early 2023. He unboxes, tests, and fully disassembles the unit to examine its internals, concluding it is a practical, easy-to-use solution for backup power that goes well beyond a simple battery by supporting multiple charging sources.

## Key Topics
- UK rolling blackout context and rationale for backup power preparation
- EcoFlow Delta 2 product overview: capacity, pricing (£1,600), and expandability
- Solar panel design, folding mechanism, and carry case as stand
- Multiple charging input options: solar, mains, car, generator, and potentially wind turbine
- Output options: AC sockets, car DC socket, USB ports
- Wi-Fi/app control feature (and Robert's mild skepticism about its utility)
- Full internal teardown and assessment of repairability/modularity
- Comparison to Fairphone's modular design philosophy
- LFP battery chemistry advantages over conventional lithium-ion

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP / LiFePO4)** — the battery chemistry used; 16 prismatic cells at 3.2V, 20Ah each
- **Lithium-ion (conventional)** — mentioned as a contrast/industry standard with lower cycle life
- **Cobalt** — explicitly noted as absent from LFP cells; discussed as a conflict mineral concern
- **Nickel** — also noted as absent from LFP cells

## Techniques and Methods
- Unboxing and physical inspection of the unit and foldable solar panel
- Internal disassembly: removal of top clip, six screws, front/rear panels, fans, electronics board, and battery pack
- Identification of BMS (Battery Management System) placement and cell configuration
- Solar panel orientation technique: angled in morning/evening, flat at midday for optimal collection
- Charging the unit from the mains post-reassembly to verify functionality
