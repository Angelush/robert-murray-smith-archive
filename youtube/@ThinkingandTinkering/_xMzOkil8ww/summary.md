## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a multi-stage water-powered animatronic device that harvests energy from flowing water at two stages to animate a small rocking figure and flash an LED. The project was originally conceived as a "three times the power" system but the third stage (a vibrating blade) was abandoned. It showcases creative use of salvaged and household materials to generate, rectify, and store small amounts of electrical energy.

## Key Topics
- Multi-stage water energy harvesting from a single water flow
- Three-phase AC generation and rectification using salvaged components
- Animatronic motion driven by a tipping-bucket water mechanism
- Capacitor-based energy storage and reed-switch-triggered LED flash
- Upcycling household and skip/dumpster materials for electronics projects

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- CDs (used as spinning rotor elements)
- Drinking straws (spacers between CDs)
- Milk bottle tops (water wheel paddles for second stage)
- Relay coil (repurposed as generator coil)
- Building site channels/guttering (salvaged from a dumpster, used for water routing)
- Capacitor (energy storage)
- Reed switches (trigger for LED)
- LEDs (three and two, used as indicator/flash lights)
- Magnets (paired with coil for AC generation)

## Techniques and Methods
- Three-phase motor/generator construction using CDs and straws
- Three-phase rectification (same topology as a car alternator)
- Tipping-bucket mechanism to convert water flow into oscillating mechanical motion
- AC-to-DC rectification of coil output
- Capacitor charge-and-dump via reed switches to produce a flashing LED effect
