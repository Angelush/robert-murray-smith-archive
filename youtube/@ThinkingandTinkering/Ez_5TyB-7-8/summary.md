## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make homemade nut milk as a simple, additive-free alternative to store-bought plant milks. The process requires only nuts, water, and a blender, and Robert argues the result genuinely surpasses commercial alternatives in both taste and purity. The video is a casual, practical how-to with characteristically dry British humour throughout.

## Key Topics
- Why Robert switched away from cow's milk
- The cost and quality problems with supermarket nut milks (watered down, full of additives)
- Basic recipe and method for making cashew milk at home
- Comparison of different nuts for milk-making: cashews, hemp hearts, peanuts, almonds
- The absence of preservatives, stabilisers, and thickeners in homemade versions
- Brief scepticism about seaweed-derived additives (carrageenan) post-Fukushima

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Cashews** — primary nut used in the demonstration; described as making a great milk
- **Hemp hearts / hemp seeds** — produce a good but earthy milk; recommended for pancakes
- **Peanuts** — make a passable milk, especially good on cereal
- **Sweet almonds** — produce excellent milk with a marzipan-like aftertaste
- **Bitter almonds** — cautioned against; contain cyanide
- **Water** — blended with nuts at roughly 100 g nuts per 1 litre
- **Seaweed-derived additives** (e.g. carrageenan) — mentioned as a commercial additive Robert is suspicious of

## Techniques and Methods
- Soaking not mentioned; nuts blended directly
- Ratio: approximately 100 g nuts per 1 litre of water (adjustable to taste)
- Blend for approximately 1 minute with lid secured
- No straining step shown — milk used as-is from the blender
