## Overview
A Day 5 entry in Robert's Advent Science Magic series, this short demonstrates two optical phenomena using a glass of water. He shows how refraction causes fish to appear to swim in the opposite direction when viewed through water, then reveals a clever trick using different materials — a figure drawn on paper versus an outline on a plastic bag — to illustrate the difference between refraction and total internal reflection.

## Key Topics
- Optical refraction through water
- Total internal reflection (referred to as "total internal refraction" in the video)
- How different surfaces interact with light when submerged
- Practical demonstration of optical magic as a Christmas advent activity

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Water (glass of)
- Paper (pepper/regular paper) — used as the substrate for a drawn figure
- Plastic bag — used as the substrate for an outline drawing
- Drawn fish image and a drawn boy figure (for demonstration purposes)

## Techniques and Methods
- Submerging paper and plastic bag drawings in water to demonstrate differing optical behaviour
- Visual comparison of refraction vs. total internal reflection using simple household materials
