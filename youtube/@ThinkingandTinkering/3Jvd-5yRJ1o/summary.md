## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith accidentally broke a tap off inside a drilled hole while re-tapping a grub screw hole on a motor drive flange, a costly mistake. He discovered that applying extreme heat via spot welding — far hotter than a standard blow torch — softened the tap enough to punch it straight through and out of the hole. The video shares this practical tip as a reliable method for recovering from one of the most frustrating workshop mishaps.

## Key Topics
- Motorizing a track and welding a flange onto a drive cog
- The sequence of mistakes that led to a broken tap in a hole
- Why blow torches are insufficient for this task
- Using a spot welder to generate enough heat to free a broken tap
- General advice on tapping technique to avoid breaking taps

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Steel flange and drive cog (workpiece)
- Grub screw
- Tap (threading tool, broken off in hole)

## Techniques and Methods
- Spot welding a lump of metal onto the workpiece near the broken tap to generate extreme heat
- Heating the workpiece to bright glowing red (~800°C+)
- Using a screwdriver, mallet, and punch to drive the softened tap through and out of the hole
- Correct tapping technique: back-screwing periodically to clear swarf and prevent binding
