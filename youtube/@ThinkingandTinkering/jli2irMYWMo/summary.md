## Overview
Robert demonstrates a low-cost, off-the-shelf home heating solution assembled from two inexpensive items purchased on Amazon for a combined £29. The system pairs a Chinese paraffin/kerosene stove with an "Outdoor Cozy" heat concentrator attachment, and the key insight is that running it on homemade biodiesel (from a previous video) reduces fuel cost to roughly 50p per litre versus £3–4 for commercial kerosene.

## Key Topics
- Off-the-shelf heating options for people without tools or workshops
- The "Outdoor Cozy BS-150" heat concentrator attachment (£12)
- Chinese paraffin/kerosene stove (£17)
- Using homemade biodiesel as a cheap alternative fuel
- Heat capture and infrared conversion (same principle as a plant pot heater)
- Carbon monoxide/safety considerations for indoor combustion

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Paraffin / Kerosene / Lamp oil** — primary fuel for the stove; interchangeable terms
- **Biodiesel (homemade from waste oil)** — demonstrated as a substitute fuel at ~50p/litre; burns cleanly with a blue flame and no noticeable odour
- **Pressed steel** — construction material of both the stove top and the Outdoor Cozy

## Techniques and Methods
- Placing the Outdoor Cozy directly over the paraffin stove burner to concentrate rising heat
- Adjusting the wick on the kerosene stove to achieve a clean blue flame (complete combustion)
- Monitoring surface temperature with a thermometer (reached ~120°C on the top cap within minutes)
- Cross-referencing video #1783 for the biodiesel-making process (waste oil transesterification described as "as easy as making a cup of tea")
