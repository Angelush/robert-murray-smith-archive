## Overview
This video demonstrates a bicycle-powered hammer mill setup used to process homemade biochar. The presenter, a gardener, explains their full biochar production workflow — from burning wood in a converted propane tank, quenching the coals, and then milling the dried charcoal (mixed with leaves) into a fine material used as chicken coop bedding. The activated biochar-manure mix is then added to compost or garden soil as a soil amendment.

## Key Topics
- Biochar production using a converted 250-gallon propane tank
- Processing dried biochar through a hammer mill
- Bicycle-powered drive mechanism for the hammer mill
- Using charcoal-leaf mix as chicken coop bedding to "activate" the biochar
- Applying activated biochar to compost/garden as a soil amendment

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Wood and organic material (feedstock for biochar)
- Charcoal / biochar (end product of the burn)
- Water (used to quench coals)
- Leaves (mixed with charcoal for bedding)
- Chicken manure (used to activate the biochar)

## Techniques and Methods
- Burning wood in a sealed 250-gallon propane tank to produce biochar
- Quenching coals with water and allowing the charcoal to dry
- Hammer milling dried charcoal using a bicycle rim driving a C-channel belt
- Nailing the mill to the ground for stability
- Adding an off-balance weight to create vibration and improve material feed
- Using charcoal bedding in a chicken coop as a biological activation step
