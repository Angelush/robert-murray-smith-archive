## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith attempts to build a small wind turbine using a dried poppy head as the fan blade, aiming to generate enough power to light an LED. After two salvaged motors prove unsuitable — one too weak, one too torque-heavy — he solves the problem by hand-building a lightweight replacement commutator from cardboard, sticky tape, and neodymium magnets extracted from a Samsung phone case. The modified dishwasher motor successfully lights two LEDs from poppy-powered wind.
