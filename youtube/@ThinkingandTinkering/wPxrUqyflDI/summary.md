## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith uses the Creality Ferret Pro 3D scanner to capture the 9-foot bronze Mahatma Gandhi statue in Parliament Square, London. After completing 10 separate scans and stitching them together, he repairs the mesh in 3D Builder and 3D prints the result, demonstrating the scanner's capability to handle large-scale public sculptures. The video serves as both a capability showcase for the Creality Ferret Pro and a reflection on the value of public art.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to Art UK, a digital catalog of over 50,000 publicly owned sculptures in the UK
- History and significance of the Gandhi statue in Parliament Square (sculptor Philip Jackson, unveiled 2015)
- Using the Creality Ferret Pro 3D scanner to scan large outdoor public sculptures
- Workflow: multi-scan stitching → mesh repair in 3D Builder → export as STL → slice in Cura → 3D print
- Sharing the resulting model on Thingiverse for free download
- Copyright considerations around scanning and sharing models of public sculpture

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Bronze (material of the Gandhi statue)

## Techniques and Methods
- Multi-scan photogrammetry/structured-light scanning (10 individual scans stitched together)
- Mesh repair using 3D Builder
- STL export for 3D printing
- Slicing with Cura
- FDM 3D printing

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Copyright on public sculptures:** Robert firmly states there is no copyright on public statues and encourages viewers to look it up. He is relaxed about people using his scan commercially — he gives everything away freely and explicitly welcomes others to use his scan even to sell models if they wish.
- **Scanner relationship with Creality:** The scanner was provided free for review purposes only. He notes it's a good scanner, as is the Miraco, but they serve different use cases ("horses for courses") — the Creality pairs with a PC while the Miraco is more standalone.
- **Paris Métro idea:** He mentions he's seriously considering travelling to Paris specifically to scan the Art Nouveau Métro entrances and would post any such models on Thingiverse.
- **Print size:** He notes he printed the Gandhi model at only 4 inches tall.
