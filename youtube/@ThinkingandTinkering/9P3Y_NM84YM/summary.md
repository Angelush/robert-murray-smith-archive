## Overview
A short practical tip video showing how to remove and reinsert incorrectly wired pins in plug/wire connectors without cutting and re-soldering. Robert demonstrates that the retaining spring on each pin can be compressed to release it from the plastic housing, allowing wires to be moved to the correct positions. The video was prompted by a miswired connector on his electric trike build.

## Key Topics
- Why plug-and-play connectors sometimes have wiring errors (manufacturing fatigue, relaxed QC)
- How the spring-retention mechanism inside wire connector pins works
- Technique for releasing and reinserting pins using common tools
- Avoiding the need to cut and re-solder when fixing a miswired connector

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Wire plug connectors (multi-pin type)
- Metal pins with spring-retention tabs

## Techniques and Methods
- Compressing the retaining spring on a connector pin using needle-nose pliers, a nail, or similar thin tool
- Sliding the released pin out of the plastic housing
- Reinserting the pin in the correct position so the spring snaps back into place
