## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple, adaptable axial flux generator using 3D-printed discs, neodymium magnets, and a serpentine copper coil. The design uses only a few parts, is lightweight, tolerant of manufacturing error, and can be coupled to wind turbines, hand cranks, bike gearing, or water wheels. A demonstration shows it producing 60 volts with a planetary gear hand crank.

## Key Topics
- Difference between radial flux and axial flux motor/generator designs
- Construction of a 3D-printed axial flux generator (TinkerCAD / Thingiverse files provided)
- Serpentine coil winding and placement between magnet discs
- Modular cog system for connecting to wind turbines, planetary gear sets, or other drives
- Magnetic locking between discs and optional contra-rotation
- Advantages of axial flux designs: fewer parts, lighter weight, higher torque density, looser tolerances
- Applications: wind turbine, hand crank generator, bike generator, water wheel

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Neodymium magnets** — 32 magnets per disc, 15 cm × 1 cm, arranged north-south alternating in 18 holes per disc
- **Enamel copper wire** — 2 mm diameter, used for the serpentine coil (500 turns)
- **Plastic** — 3D-printed discs (or cut from sheet plastic as an alternative)
- **M8 bolt** — used as the central axle
- **M8 washer** — placed between magnet disc and coil disc to prevent plastic rubbing
- **Broom handle adapter** — used as a handle for hand-held demonstration

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D modelling in TinkerCAD; STL files shared on Thingiverse
- Serpentine coil winding (referenced to a separate dedicated video)
- Magnetic locking of the two discs (no mechanical axle lock by default, allowing slip and contra-rotation)
- Coupling via modular cog system to a planetary gear set for speed multiplication
- Optional steel disc addition to direct magnetic flux path

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert noted that he included the steel disc flux-path tip (mentioned at ~`[05:00]`) because viewers kept suggesting it, though he himself is not fully convinced of its benefit. This implies the steel disc addition should be treated as community-suggested rather than a technique he has personally validated.
