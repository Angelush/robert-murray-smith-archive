## Overview
This is Part 4 of Robert Murray-Smith's project to convert a ride-on lawn mower into an electric vehicle styled after a Siva Edwardian Tourer (inspired by "Bessie," the Doctor Who car). The video focuses specifically on the mechanical adaptations required before further build work can proceed, covering wheel hub modification, brake disc integration, and steering relocation. The overall theme is resourceful repurposing of existing components rather than buying new parts.

## Key Topics
- Progress recap of the lawn mower-to-EV conversion project
- Modifying original pressed-steel wheel hubs to accept larger wheels and brake discs
- Selecting space-saver spare wheels as a practical, readily available option
- Relocating the steering column to enable a side-by-side seating arrangement
- Using an angle plate to reposition the steering block

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Pressed steel wheel hubs** — original hubs from the lawn mower, cut down and repurposed
- **Steel plate** — welded/bolted to the modified hubs to accept wheel bolts
- **Brake discs** — to be added to the hub; paired with brake calipers (the original had no brakes)
- **Space saver wheels** — 58 cm diameter, ~100 mm width; chosen for size and availability
- **Bolts** — welded into the steel plate for wheel attachment
- **Angle plate** — used to mount and reposition the steering block

## Techniques and Methods
- **Cutting with angle grinder** — removing one half of the original two-piece pressed-steel hub
- **Sandblasting** — cleaning up the cut and ground hub components
- **Welding** — attaching the steel plate and bolts to form the new hub
- **Drilling/bolting** — attaching the brake disc side of the hub
- **Steering block relocation** — flipping and remounting the steering arm assembly onto an angle plate to offset the wheel to one side
