## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores the value of repurposing cordless drills, even broken or cheap secondhand ones, as sources of high-quality motors, battery packs, and mechanical components. He demonstrates disassembly and explains how each part can be salvaged and reused in DIY projects ranging from stirrers to e-bikes to boats.

## Key Topics
- Why cordless drills are valuable even when broken or cheap
- Disassembly walkthrough of a cordless drill
- Extracting and reusing the DC motor
- Using the drill case as a motor housing/mount
- The gearbox and chuck as a ready-made drive mechanism
- Understanding the battery pack internals and electronics
- Running the motor from an external DC supply
- Real-world reuse examples (stirrers, e-bikes, boats)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 18V, 1.5 Ah lithium-ion battery pack (five 18650 cells in series)
- DC motor (available standalone for ~£10)
- Battery control/protection electronics (overcharge and over-discharge prevention)
- Torx and Phillips head screws

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling a cordless drill by removing case screws
- Identifying and isolating plus/minus wires from the three-wire battery connector
- Snipping motor leads and connecting directly to an external DC power supply
- Voltage ramping from ~4V up to 18V (rated voltage) to run the motor
- Using the drill case as a fixed motor mount (screwing the housing back together)
- Rebuilding or replacing 18650 cells in the battery pack
