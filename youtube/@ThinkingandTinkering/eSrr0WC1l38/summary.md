## Overview
In this video, Robert Murray-Smith explains why he works on many projects simultaneously rather than focusing on one at a time. He uses the waiting time forced by long 3D print jobs as a jumping-off point to argue that being a "jack of all trades" develops a generalist problem-solving mindset that is essential when facing novel, unseen problems — something a deep specialist ("master") cannot do. His core takeaway is that both mindsets have value, but for invention and exploration, breadth of experience is at least as important as depth.

## Key Topics
- The practical reason for juggling multiple projects: long 3D print times create forced downtime that he fills productively
- The full "jack of all trades" proverb: *"…oftentimes better than master of one"* — the half usually omitted
- Why specialists/masters are limited: they can only solve problems they've seen before and are constrained to their "box"
- Why generalists/tinkerers excel at novel problems: broad experience builds a flexible, cross-domain problem-solving mindset
- The liquid metal battery inventor anecdote: he specifically hired people with *no* battery experience to avoid expert tunnel vision
- The lead-acid battery thought experiment: it would be rejected today by any domain expert
- Cross-pollination of design elements across projects (e.g. rim generation, turbine blades appearing in multiple builds)
- Working on many projects simultaneously builds mental links between ideas and reveals what is common and transferable
- Philosophy: *how* you think matters more than *what* you build

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Lead-acid battery (used as a thought-experiment example — contains lead, sulfuric acid, produces hydrogen gas)

## Techniques and Methods
- FDM 3D printing across multiple machines run in parallel to minimise idle time
- Project scheduling/sequencing: deliberately interleaving long-running print jobs with active work on other projects
- Cross-project design reuse: applying components (e.g. rim generation sections, turbine blades) across different experimental builds
