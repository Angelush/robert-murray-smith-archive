## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the overlooked potential of micro-scale energy generation, framing it around the low power demands of 5G infrastructure and IoT devices. He argues that not all power generation needs to be at mega- or kilowatt scale, and demonstrates a small flutter-based wind energy harvester built from 3D-printed parts that successfully lights an LED in a gentle breeze. The key takeaway is that micro-generation can realistically replace disposable batteries for low-power applications.
