## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith provides an update on his ongoing 1kW wind turbine build, explaining a key design change: removing the baffle plates that were slowing the turbine down and replacing the structural role they played with a lazy Susan-style caster system. The video demonstrates the turbine spinning in a car park and teases the next step of re-coiling the generator using microwave oven coils.

## Key Topics
- Removal of baffle plates that were obstructing airflow and reducing efficiency
- Introduction of a caster-based lazy Susan bearing system to maintain structural stability
- Car park spin test to verify the redesigned turbine's performance
- Upcoming re-coiling of the generator using donated microwave oven coils
- Brief mention of commercial lazy Susan-style industrial bearings as an alternative

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Casters (repurposed/recycled, used to create a lazy Susan rotation system)
- Magnets (part of the generator assembly on the turbine)
- Microwave oven coils (donated by friend Sid Sykes, to be used for re-coiling the generator)
- Industrial lazy Susan-type bearing (~£49, mentioned as a commercial alternative; manufacturers cited: Turner in the UK, Rockwell in the USA)

## Techniques and Methods
- Removal of baffle plates to reduce aerodynamic obstruction
- Repurposing casters to form a lazy Susan bearing, transferring lateral stability while the central post carries the 19 kg axial load
- Wind speed measurement at multiple points around the turbine during the car park test (using an anemometer)
- Re-coiling of the generator stator (planned, using microwave oven transformer coils)

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert acknowledged in the comments that removing the baffle plates was the right call in principle, while admitting the casters may not be the optimal long-term solution. He indicated that if he changes the bearing arrangement, he would most likely switch to a proper lazy Susan-type bearing — consistent with the £49 industrial bearing he mentioned in the video.
