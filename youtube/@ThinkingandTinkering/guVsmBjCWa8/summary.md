## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates the fundamental principles of burning used motor oil, explaining why it is difficult to ignite and how to overcome those challenges. He walks through progressively improved setups — from open-plate burning to a contained chimney structure — before showcasing a simple DIY waste oil burner built from hollow concrete retaining wall blocks. The video concludes that with a drip-feed system and proper airflow, used motor oil can be an effective, cheap, and abundant fuel for space heating.

## Key Topics
- Why used motor oil is difficult to ignite (needs to vaporize before it burns)
- The role of oxygen and airflow in achieving a clean, hot burn
- Progressive demonstration: open plate → chimney → enclosed structure → raised cage
- Using a cotton wick as a starter to localise heat and initiate vaporisation
- Oil drip-feed systems
- Full DIY build using hollow concrete retaining wall blocks (rocket-stove principle)
- Applications: mass heater, indirect air heating, potential water-coil integration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Used motor oil (waste oil)** — primary fuel; free or cheap, high energy density but difficult to burn cleanly
- **Cotton** — used as a starter wick to localise oil and initiate vaporisation
- **Mineral wool** — used in the burn cage as a wick/absorber medium for the oil drip
- **Hollow concrete retaining wall blocks** — structural material for the DIY burner body
- **Ceramic/terracotta tiles** — placed on the floor as a base layer
- **Steel (bean can / tank connector valve)** — used for the oil feed and drip pan assembly

## Techniques and Methods
- **Cotton-wick starting** — using cotton to hold oil in place and provide initial combustion to reach vaporisation temperature
- **Chimney draft** — placing a chimney over the flame to draw air upward and improve combustion
- **Enclosed burn chamber** — surrounding the flame so air is channelled in a single controlled direction
- **Raised cage / elevated burn platform** — lifting the burn point to allow oxygen ingress from underneath
- **Gravity drip-feed** — a bean-can tank with a connector valve dripping oil at a controlled rate onto the mineral wool bed
- **Angle grinder cutting** — cutting the central hole of a concrete block to split it, forming the burner opening
- **Dry-stacking** — assembling concrete blocks without mortar (simple "lego-like" build)
- **Indirect air heating** — conceptual method of passing a tube through the burner and pushing heated air into a building
