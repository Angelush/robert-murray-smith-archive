## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith uses this video as a channel update, explaining organisational changes he has made to manage his large and growing body of work. He introduces two companion channels — TNT Omnibus (topic compilations) and TNT Talk Time (discussion/philosophy videos) — and announces a new membership benefit: new videos will be published to members two weeks before the main channel, ad-free.

## Key Topics
- Approaching 500,000 subscribers milestone
- Introduction of the **TNT Omnibus** channel — compiling related multi-part videos into single coherent videos (e.g. collapsible wind turbine, whirlybird turbine, kiln build)
- Introduction of the **TNT Talk Time** channel — collecting more philosophical and discussion-style content
- YouTube's revenue model: advertising and channel memberships
- Concerns about YouTube over-serving ads and potentially alienating viewers
- New policy: all new videos premiere on the members channel first (ad-free, two-week exclusive window) before moving to the main channel
- How to join as a member via the red "Join" button

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comment replies are mostly brief acknowledgements ("cheers mate"), but several add meaningful clarification:

- **On the members/main channel gate:** "The deal is videos will appear on the main channel after they have been on the member's channel — but it is a one-way gate — not every members video will get moved to the main channel." Members get exclusive content that never goes public, in addition to early access.
- **On the value to members:** "I think it adds benefit tbh — you get the exclusive vids and first view of non-exclusive vids!"
- **On uncertainty about the new approach:** "I honestly don't know mate — part of this is to see how it goes! so I guess we will find out and see if I need to change what I am doing."
- **On YouTube's ad strategy:** "I think they are doing the wrong thing myself" and "I'll bet that is the plan" (agreeing ads are becoming excessive).
- **On his content licensing:** "Everything I do is public domain as far as I am concerned — every one is free to use it as they wish and I make no requirement on anyone."
- **On live streaming:** "I need to do more live streaming."
- Plans to do "something in the new year" in response to a suggestion he was considering.
