## Overview

In this video, Robert Murray-Smith explains the reasoning and exploratory process behind a series of videos on mechanical drives and continuously variable transmissions (CVTs). Rather than presenting a finished project, he walks through the various mechanisms he investigated — including the Scotch yoke, epicyclic drives, Geneva drives, and the idograph — before arriving at his current goal: a mechanical CVT for automatically controlling wind turbine output speed. The central takeaway is that creative problem-solving requires looking broadly at possibilities rather than fixating on a single path.
