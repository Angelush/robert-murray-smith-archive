## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how a magnetic gear assembly — consisting of two magnetic rings and an iron modulator ring — can be directly integrated into a wind turbine design. He shows a 3:1 gear ratio prototype built with 3D-printed parts and M8 bolts, highlighting the key advantage of magnetic gears: they slip under excessive torque, providing a built-in safety limiter. His main conclusion is that magnetic gears are not just a curiosity but a genuinely compelling option for DIY and commercial wind turbines due to their low cost, low wear, and inherent torque protection.

## Key Topics
- Recap of magnetic gear principles from video 1922 (two magnetic rings + iron modulator)
- Design of a bolt-based modulator ring (24 × M8 bolts through a disc)
- 3:1 magnetic gear ratio (12-magnet inner ring, 36-magnet outer ring, 24-segment modulator)
- Integration of the magnetic gear as a wind turbine drivetrain
- Torque-slip behaviour as a built-in overload/runaway protection feature
- Advantages of plastic construction: no wear, low cost, long lifespan

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Permanent magnets (12-pole and 36-pole rings)
- Iron/steel segments (modulator ring)
- M8 bolts × 24 (used as modulator pole pieces)
- Plastic / 3D-printed parts (structural components)

## Techniques and Methods
- Tinkercad CAD design (disc with bolt holes; shared in video description)
- 3D printing for structural rings and housing
- Magnetic gear assembly (coaxial arrangement of inner ring, modulator, outer ring)
- Axial flux generator concept (attaching coils to the fast-spinning ring)
