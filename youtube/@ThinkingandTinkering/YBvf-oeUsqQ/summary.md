## Overview
In this short Christmas advent video, Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simple paper chromatography experiment using kitchen towel and washable markers. As water travels up the paper by capillary action, it separates the ink colours by particle weight, creating a rainbow effect. The video is aimed at families looking for fun, safe science activities to do with children over Christmas.

## Key Topics
- Paper chromatography as a Christmas science activity for kids
- Capillary action in kitchen towel
- Separation of ink colours by particle weight
- Connection to real-world science (DNA separation)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Kitchen towel (paper towel)
- Washable markers (multiple colours — yellow and blue specifically noted)
- Water

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting kitchen towel into strips
- Colouring the edge of the paper with different markers
- Placing the paper in water to initiate capillary action
- Paper chromatography — separating materials by particle weight as water wicks upward
