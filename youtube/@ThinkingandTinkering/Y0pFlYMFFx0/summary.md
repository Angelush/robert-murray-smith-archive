## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores old and new sources of flyback transformers for high voltage experimentation. He disassembles a CRT computer monitor to extract a flyback transformer, demonstrates the internal components, and then reveals that modern inverter microwave ovens now contain flyback transformers as a replacement for the traditional bulky microwave oven transformer — making them a new and accessible source for hobbyists.

## Key Topics
- Historical and current sources for high voltage supplies (car ignition coils, Marx generators, online HV generators)
- Flyback transformers from CRT monitors becoming scarce as CRTs disappear
- Disassembly of a CRT monitor and identification of salvageable components
- Safety hazards of CRT tubes (lead glass, phosphors, residual capacitance)
- Smashing open a CRT to examine internal construction
- Discovery of flyback transformers inside modern inverter microwave ovens
- Comparison of CRT-sourced vs. microwave-sourced flyback transformers
- Recommendation to hoard traditional microwave oven transformers before they disappear

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Lead glass** — used in CRT envelopes; regulated for disposal due to lead content
- **Phosphors** — coating on CRT screen face; hazardous if inhaled
- **Ferrite** — core material visible in the deflection yoke assembly
- **Copper wire** — recovered from the de-gaussing coil (unsresined, reusable)
- **Aluminized plastic** — wrapping around the de-gaussing coil

## Techniques and Methods
- Disassembling a CRT monitor (removing four back screws, unplugging all connectors)
- Desoldering the flyback transformer from the CRT mainboard
- Releasing the suction cup / anode connector from the CRT bell using a screwdriver
- Recovering copper wire from the de-gaussing coil by stripping plastic wrapping
- Smashing a CRT open (safety glasses + hammer) to inspect internal structure
- Disassembling an inverter microwave oven to locate and extract its flyback transformer
- Rewinding transformer secondaries (mentioned as possible modification on the microwave unit)
