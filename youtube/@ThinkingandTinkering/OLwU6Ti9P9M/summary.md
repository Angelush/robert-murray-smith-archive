## Overview
Luke (presenting on Robert Murray-Smith's channel) finds a Coomba 2242-7C cassette/CD recorder/player in a bin and attempts to repair it. He diagnoses and fixes several faults including non-working inputs, a cassette mechanism, and bad speaker solder joints, partially restoring the unit. The CD laser turns out to be dead beyond repair, but the cassette functionality — the main goal — is successfully restored.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging discarded retro electronics ("trash to cash")
- Diagnosing faults on a combo cassette/CD recorder
- Reassembling and testing a consumer audio device
- Cosmetic refurbishment alongside functional repair

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Powdered blue spray paint (used to repaint the cassette portion of the unit)

## Techniques and Methods
- Visual inspection of solder joints on disassembled PCBs
- Reseating/repositioning incorrectly placed components
- Resoldering speaker connections to replace poor-quality original joints
- Cleaning the CD laser assembly (attempted, unsuccessful)
- Functional testing via Gibson guitar plugged into aux input and a pre-recorded blank cassette
