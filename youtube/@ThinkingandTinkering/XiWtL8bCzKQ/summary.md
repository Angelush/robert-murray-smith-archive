## Overview
In this short video, Robert Murray-Smith presents a Spanish-language tutorial on how to synthesise graphite oxide using a simple wet-chemical method. Speaking in broken but earnest Spanish, he walks through the reagents, quantities, and step-by-step procedure, concluding that ultrasound is required to disperse the final product in water. The video is explicitly aimed at Spanish-speaking viewers who may not follow his English-language content.

## Key Topics
- Introduction and apology for imperfect Spanish
- Overview of the seven required reagents for graphite oxide synthesis
- Step-by-step mixing and reaction procedure with quantities
- Post-reaction workup: washing, sedimentation, and purification with hydrochloric acid
- Conversion of graphite oxide to graphene oxide via ultrasound

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄)** — 98%, 320 mL; primary acid medium
- **Phosphoric acid (H₃PO₄)** — 75%, 80 mL; co-acid in the reaction mixture
- **Potassium permanganate (KMnO₄)** — 18 g; oxidising agent added slowly
- **Hydrochloric acid (HCl)** — ~35% concentrate (diluted to 1 M for washing), 1 litre; used in purification step
- **Graphite** — 3.2 g; preferably flakes, though powder is acceptable per his comments
- **Water** — used in washing steps (2 litres hot water + additional washes)
- **Hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂)** — 100 mL; added after 3-day reaction to indicate completion (bright yellow colour = success)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Acid-based oxidation (modified Hummers-style):** Mixing sulfuric acid, phosphoric acid, graphite, and potassium permanganate; leaving at room temperature (~25–30 °C) for 3 days
- **Colour-change endpoint detection:** Addition of hydrogen peroxide to confirm reaction success by producing a bright yellow colour
- **Sedimentation/gravity settling:** Diluting with 2 litres of hot water, allowing product to settle to the bottom over ~1 day, then decanting the supernatant
- **Acid washing:** Treating settled product with 1 M hydrochloric acid for 1 day to remove residual manganese salts
- **Water washing:** Triple wash with water after the HCl step
- **Ultrasound exfoliation:** Sonicating the graphite oxide in water to produce graphene oxide (with the caveat noted in comments that prolonged ultrasound can fragment graphene sheets)

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Graphite powder vs. flakes:** Confirmed that graphite powder works; pencil graphite can also be used, but powder is better.
- **Ultrasound caution:** Noted that prolonged ultrasound exposure fragments graphene sheets, and expressed willingness to read a paper on alternative methods sent by a commenter.
- **Product colour:** Explained that the product appears black in large quantities but is transparent when thin/dilute.
- **Nature of the product:** Clarified to commenters that graphite oxide is not a simple substance — it is graphite reacted with acid and permanganate. Also confirmed that the next step (graphene oxide) is covered in a separate video.
- **Application context:** Mentioned in passing that he uses graphene-derived materials for "powerful batteries and bullet proof plastics."
