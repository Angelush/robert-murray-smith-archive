## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simplified, three-ingredient method for producing graphene oxide at a home-lab scale, using sulfuric acid, potassium permanganate, and microwave-exfoliated expanded graphite (previously intercalated with ferric chloride). The video walks through the full synthesis and washing procedure, noting that the updated washing technique — drying first, then using vacuum filtration with HCl — dramatically reduces processing time from weeks to hours.
