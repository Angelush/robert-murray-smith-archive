## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a self-limiting conductive ink based on Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) behaviour, applied to fabric as a flexible heating element that regulates its own temperature without any control electronics. The material works by expanding when hot — pushing conductive particles apart to increase resistance and halt heating — then contracting and conducting again as it cools. Beyond heating, he also shows its use as a pressure sensor, opening up applications in smart flooring and position detection.
