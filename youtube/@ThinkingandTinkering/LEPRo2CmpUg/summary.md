## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple DIY radiant heater using two tins, a mason jar mesh strainer, and wicking material soaked in alcohol fuel. The device produces a striking sheet of flame that heats by radiation rather than convection, meaning it warms you directly rather than heating the surrounding air. He notes it is easy to make but cautions about fire safety.

## Key Topics
- Construction of a simple radiant heater from household materials
- How radiant heating differs from convective heating (heats you, not the air)
- Suitable alcohol fuels for the device
- The dramatic visual effect of the flame envelope when lit

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Two tins** (one small, one large) — form the body of the heater
- **Mason jar mesh strainer** (stainless steel mesh, sold on Amazon as a tea/coffee filter for mason jars) — acts as a structural or containment component
- **Carbon felt** — preferred wicking material; alternatives include bandage, sock, cotton, or paper
- **Methanol** — the fuel used in the demonstration; methanol, ethanol, or isopropanol are all compatible

## Techniques and Methods
- Lining the small tin with wicking material and nesting it inside the larger tin
- Soaking the wick with alcohol fuel
- Punching holes in the tin to allow gas/vapour flow
- Igniting the fuel to produce a radiant flame sheet
