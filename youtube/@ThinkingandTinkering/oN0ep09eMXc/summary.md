## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith covers a newly published paper on using a whey protein aerogel to extract gold from industrial mining leachates, contextualizing it against the more attention-grabbing headline of "gold from milk." He walks through both the aerogel fabrication process and a practical home method for gold reclamation from circuit boards, before arguing that the foam's real value lies in removing heavy metal toxins and radioactive materials from water — a more impactful application than gold recovery.

## Key Topics
- The whey protein aerogel described in the paper and how it was made
- How proteins bind heavy atoms, making them effective for gold capture
- Industrial gold recovery from mining tailings vs. home use feasibility
- Home circuit board gold reclamation using vinegar, hydrogen peroxide, and bleach
- Water remediation as the more significant application of the foam
- Comparison with activated charcoal as the current industrial standard

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Whey protein / whey powder** — dairy byproduct used to form the aerogel
- **Casein** — the fat/protein removed during cheese-making, leaving whey behind
- **Hydrochloric acid (HCl)** — used to unfold whey proteins into long strings
- **Carboxylic acid (cross-linker)** — used to join protein strands into a stable network
- **Charcoal (activated carbon)** — current industrial standard for gold recovery, energy-intensive
- **Vinegar (acetic acid)** — used in home circuit board gold extraction
- **Hydrogen peroxide** — used alongside vinegar and salt to dissolve copper/tin/solder
- **Table salt (sodium chloride)** — part of the home extraction solution
- **Household bleach (sodium hypochlorite)** — used to dissolve gold flakes into solution
- **Sodium bicarbonate** — used to buffer the bleach solution
- **Sulfuric acid** — added to remove lead from the gold solution
- **Vitamin C (ascorbic acid)** — used to precipitate gold out of solution
- **Aqua regia** — mentioned as the industrial solvent wash for gold dissolution
- **Mercury and Lead** — heavy metal toxins the foam can remove from water

## Techniques and Methods
- **Aerogel/aerofoam fabrication**: acidifying whey protein to denature it into strings, cross-linking with a carboxylic acid, then freeze-drying
- **Gold extraction via foam**: flowing a gold-containing solution through the protein foam; gold adsorbs to the protein matrix; gold is recovered by burning away the protein
- **Home circuit board gold reclamation**: chopping boards, dissolving copper/tin/solder in vinegar + hydrogen peroxide + salt, collecting floating gold flakes, dissolving gold in buffered bleach solution, precipitating gold with vitamin C, filtering and smelting
- **Water remediation**: flowing contaminated water through the protein foam to adsorb heavy metals and radioactive materials (90% toxin removal reported on first pass)
