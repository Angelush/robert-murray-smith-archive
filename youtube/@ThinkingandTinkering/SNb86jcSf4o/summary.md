## Overview
A short Tinkercad tutorial demonstrating how to design eccentric cams using basic cylinder shapes and alignment tools. Robert explains that eccentric cams are a fundamental mechanical component used in crankshafts and steam/air engine slide valves, and shows step-by-step that the construction method scales to any size, thickness, or throw. The key takeaway is that the same alignment technique applies equally to gears, since gears are simply circles with indentations.

## Key Topics
- What eccentric cams are and why they matter (crankshafts, slide valves)
- Step-by-step construction of an eccentric cam in Tinkercad
- Using a reference circle to define the cam's path of travel
- Aligning objects to center vs. edge to achieve eccentricity
- Scaling and adapting the technique to different sizes, thicknesses, and throws
- Applying the same method to gears

None mentioned.

## Techniques and Methods
- Creating a reference/path circle (100×100×2 mm) as a guide for the follower trace
- Creating the cam disc (80×80×5 mm) as a separate cylinder
- Adding an axle hole (10×10 mm) centered to the reference circle
- Aligning the cam to the center of the reference circle on one axis and to its edge on the other axis to produce the eccentricity
- Removing the reference circle and grouping/joining the cam and hole to complete the part
- Noted as directly transferable to gear shapes
