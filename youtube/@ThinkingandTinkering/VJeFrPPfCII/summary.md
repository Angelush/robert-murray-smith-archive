## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith completes and tests his "rowing cycle" — a human-powered vehicle built by combining rowing machine mechanics with a children's bike drivetrain. The proof-of-concept succeeds in that the vehicle moves, though it currently travels slowly due to over-gearing, and Robert discusses plans to tune the gear ratios or add proper gears for more speed.

## Key Topics
- Completion and first test ride of the rowing cycle build
- Pulley and gear ratio adjustments needed to make the vehicle movable
- Repurposing parts from a children's bike (cog, pedal, drive chain)
- Welding as a newly learned skill enabling the project
- Future improvements: adding gears for higher speed

## Techniques and Methods
- Welding (used to attach the children's bike pedal and drivetrain components)
- Pulley ratio adjustment to reduce required torque
- Chain drive transmission from rowing mechanism to rear wheels
- Gear ratio tuning (identified as needed for future speed improvement)
