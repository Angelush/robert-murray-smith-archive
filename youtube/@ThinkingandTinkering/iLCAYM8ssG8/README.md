## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a "liquid diode" — a simple rectifier built from kitchen ingredients — after first explaining how diodes work using the analogy of a heart valve and covering the basics of AC rectification. He shows that a saturated sodium bicarbonate solution with one aluminum electrode and one lead electrode acts as a half-wave rectifier, capable of spinning a DC motor from an AC supply.
