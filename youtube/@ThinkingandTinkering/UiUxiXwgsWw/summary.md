## Overview
Robert demonstrates the safe procedure for discharging and disassembling a CO₂ fire extinguisher before scrapping it. The key safety point is that these extinguishers can retain dangerous pressure even when seemingly empty, so the valve must be safely released before unscrewing the head. The video is a brief practical guide aimed at scrappers and recyclers handling this type of equipment.

## Key Topics
- Safety hazards of pressurised CO₂ fire extinguishers
- How to safely discharge residual pressure before disassembly
- Materials recovered from CO₂ extinguishers for scrap

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Carbon dioxide (CO₂)** — the pressurised gas inside the extinguisher, which can be retained even in "empty" units
- **Aluminium** — thick-walled body of the CO₂ extinguisher, a valuable scrap material
- **Brass** — the chunky valve/head assembly at the top, also scrap-valuable
- **Steel** — mentioned as the predominant body material in other types of fire extinguishers (not this specific unit)

## Techniques and Methods
- Identifying residual pressure in a CO₂ extinguisher before disassembly
- Using the internal diaphragm and small hole in the valve to safely vent residual gas by partially releasing the valve
- Disassembling the extinguisher head once pressure is fully relieved
