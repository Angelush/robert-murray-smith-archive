## Overview
Luke continues building his DIY scooter by addressing a structural flaw (the front end sitting too low) and begins the electrification process. He modifies the frame through welding, redesigns the footrest to clear space for a motor, and fabricates a custom drive sprocket system using a salvaged bicycle cassette cog. Part 1 concludes with a successful test of the motor spinning the rear wheel via a motor controller.

## Key Topics
- Fixing the scooter's front-end height by trimming and re-welding the stem
- Redesigning the footrest as a flat plate to make room for the motor and batteries
- Welding the bottom protective panel and headpiece onto the frame
- Planning and fabricating the rear drive sprocket (42-tooth) and its mount
- Repurposing a 12-tooth bicycle cassette cog as the motor drive sprocket
- Gear ratio selection (12T motor sprocket to 42T rear sprocket)
- Chain tensioning and motor alignment challenges
- First successful motor spin test using a motor controller

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Steel plate/disc (used as sprocket adapter and bottom panel)
- Rubber strip (reused from original footrest)
- Washers (used as centering aids during welding)
- Bicycle cassette (salvaged; 12-tooth cog extracted for motor drive sprocket)
- Chain (bicycle-style drive chain)

## Techniques and Methods
- Angle grinding / metal cutting (trimming stem, cutting new foot plate)
- MIG/stick welding (frame modifications, sprocket fabrication, panel attachment)
- Cardboard/paper template marking (to find sprocket center before welding)
- Freehand centering using washers as alignment tools
- Motor controller wiring and throttle testing
