## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith introduces the concept of switched flux (variable reluctance) generators — devices that generate electricity by redirecting a magnetic field through a steel rotor rather than spinning magnets or coils. He demonstrates the principle hands-on with a stationary coil, magnet, and a moving piece of steel, showing that a changing magnetic field can be induced by exploiting magnetic reluctance. The key takeaway is that this elegant and mechanically robust approach powers real-world applications from guitar pickups to missile power supplies.
