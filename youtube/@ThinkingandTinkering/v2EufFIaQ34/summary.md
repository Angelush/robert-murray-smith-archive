## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith unboxes the ADO A20, a folding electric city bike sent to him by ADO, and gives a first impressions walkthrough with his friend Luke. He covers the bike's key specs, included accessories, build quality, and takes it for a brief test ride, concluding it is a well-thought-out package at a good price that suits his personal mobility needs.

## Key Topics
- Unboxing and initial inspection of the ADO A20 folding e-bike
- Build quality assessment (weld quality, finish, mudguard design)
- Included accessories in the box
- Key specifications (weight, range, battery, motor, gears)
- Brief test ride (non-electric, battery flat on arrival)
- Folding mechanism and portability as a city/last-mile travel bike
- Motor controller modes and speed settings
- Plans for follow-up videos (controller hack, London city trip)
- Personal context: Robert's arthritis and step-through frame accessibility

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Lithium-ion battery — 36V, 10.4Ah, 374.4Wh, used to power the motor
- Steel frame — pressed steel spanner included in toolkit; frame welds noted

## Techniques and Methods
- Visual weld quality inspection as a build quality indicator ("stack of pennies" weld pattern)
- Battery removal and in-situ charging (two charging options demonstrated)
- Controller mode configuration (modes 0–3 explained; speed limit hack to 35 km/h mentioned, to be covered in a separate video)

- Noted the bike's UK price is approximately £899.
