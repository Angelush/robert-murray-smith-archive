## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a hand-cranked axial flux generator from a 3D-printed design sourced from Thingiverse, working without any provided instructions. The video focuses on the mechanical assembly process — fitting magnets into the rotor discs, assembling the gear train, mounting the stator with custom-wound coils, and connecting the coils in series. He concludes with the generator spinning and notes the designer claimed an output of 15 watts, with testing to follow in a separate video.
