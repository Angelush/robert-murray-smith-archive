## Overview

This video is the fourth and final installment in Robert Murray-Smith's series on building a cuboctahedron-shaped wind generator. He demonstrates the completed device spinning using only a small household fan, measures voltage and amp output from its stepper motor generator, and walks through the construction details. The key takeaway is that a functional, weather-tight wind turbine can be built cheaply from scrap aluminium, kitchen bowls, and old venetian blind slats.
