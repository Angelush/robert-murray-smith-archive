## Overview
A short DIY tutorial showing how to build a simple USB-powered pocket warmer using a strip of carbon fiber ribbon as a resistive heating element. Robert walks through identifying the power wires in a USB cable, cutting the carbon fiber to the correct resistance, and assembling the heater, demonstrating it reaching 32°C powered by a small battery bank.

## Key Topics
- Identifying power wires in a USB cable using a multimeter
- Designing a resistive heater using Ohm's law (V = IR)
- Building a carbon fiber ribbon heater sized for USB power
- Assembling and finishing the heater with Kapton tape
- Runtime and temperature trade-offs based on resistance and battery size

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- USB cable (USB-C or standard, with one end cut off)
- Carbon fiber ribbon — used as the resistive heating element
- Ribbon crimps (25 mm) — used to terminate the carbon fiber strip
- Kapton tape (or sellotape) — used to enclose and stiffen the assembly
- USB battery bank — 5V, 2–2.5A output, powers the heater

## Techniques and Methods
- Cutting and stripping a USB cable to expose the four internal wires
- Using a multimeter on the ohms setting to identify the two power wires by continuity with the outer USB prongs
- Cutting carbon fiber ribbon to a target resistance of ~2–2.5 ohms (~100 mm length)
- Crimping ribbon connectors onto the carbon fiber ends
- Connecting wires by twisting, soldering, or crimping through the ribbon crimp loops
- Enclosing the assembly in Kapton tape for rigidity and a cleaner finish
