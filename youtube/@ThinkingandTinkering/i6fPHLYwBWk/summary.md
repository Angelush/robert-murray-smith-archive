## Overview
This short tutorial demonstrates a simple technique for assembling perfectly square wooden mitre/butt joint frames using clamps and a flat reference board. The presenter shares a tip he discovered after ruining a frame on a previous project by failing to keep it flat and square during glue-up. The method produces clean, accurate right-angle corners with minimal effort.

## Key Topics
- Common problem of frames going out of square during glue-up
- Using a flat board as a reference surface for assembly
- Clamping technique to hold frame pieces square while glue dries
- Step-by-step frame assembly sequence

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Wood (frame pieces, flat reference board)
- Plastic sheet (used as a glue barrier to prevent sticking to the board)
- Wood glue

## Techniques and Methods
- Clamping the first piece to a flat reference board to establish a fixed baseline
- Using a square to verify 90° angles at each corner during assembly
- Sequential piece placement (pieces 1–4 added one at a time, each checked with a square)
- Leaving assembly clamped until glue fully dries before removing clamps
