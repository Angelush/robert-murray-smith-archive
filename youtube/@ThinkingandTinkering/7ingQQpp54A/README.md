## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith draws a historical parallel between the Great Stink of 1858 — when London's River Thames became a toxic cesspool due to inadequate sanitation infrastructure failing to keep pace with population growth and new technology (the flushing toilet) — and the modern adoption of electric vehicles. He argues that EVs don't solve the problem of overconsumption and pollution; they merely shift it, and the real lesson is that infrastructure, end-of-life recycling, and above all personal consumption habits must change before any new technology can be genuinely beneficial.
