## Overview
This short tutorial demonstrates how to desolder and remove components from a circuit board using a heat gun. The presenter (Luke) successfully removes toroid components from a board to reuse them in another project, emphasizing that iron-core components like toroids survive the process well, while silicon components are likely to be damaged by the heat required.

## Key Topics
- Desoldering components from a PCB using a heat gun
- Selecting appropriate components to safely remove (iron vs. silicon)
- Using a vise to secure the board during the process
- Using a flathead screwdriver as a pry tool to free stubborn pins
- Safety precautions when working with a heat gun

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Toroid components (iron-core) — the target components being removed for reuse
- Circuit board — donor board being stripped for parts

## Techniques and Methods
- Securing the PCB in a vise for stability
- Marking target pins before applying heat to focus the work area
- Applying heat gun directly to solder joints to reflow solder
- Using a flathead screwdriver as a pry tool to lever out pins once solder is molten
- Working in a well-ventilated area with appropriate PPE
