## Overview

Robert corrects a mistake from video 1748, where he implied that an Ugrinsky wind turbine required 3D printing — that was only true of his twisted helix variant; the original Ugrinsky is a simple square-box design that can be made by hand from common materials. He builds one using 3D-printed parts as templates and stainless steel sheet for the curved infill panels, then tests it outdoors in a horizontal (rooftop) orientation. The video demonstrates that the turbine can spin under its own weight at low wind speeds (~0.8–1.3 m/s) even without a generator attached.
