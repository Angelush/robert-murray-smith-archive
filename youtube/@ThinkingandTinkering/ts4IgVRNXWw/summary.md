## Overview
Robert demonstrates the first experiment with his Flynn Device — a multi-coil electromagnetic apparatus wired according to a prior video. Using a sound card oscilloscope to drive a square wave at 50 Hz with a 25% duty cycle through a stereo power amplifier, he feeds roughly 3–4 mV into the input coil and measures approximately 20 mV out of the output coil — around twice the input. He briefly explores two candidate explanations and outlines the next experimental steps toward self-sustaining operation.

## Key Topics
- Replication of the Flynn Device based on earlier mathematical modelling video
- Coil configuration: input coil, output coil, and tickler coil
- Observed voltage gain (~2×) and two candidate explanations
- Role of permanent magnets in the magnetic circuit
- Plan to feed DC from output back into the device
- Tickler coil as a transistor-switched feedback mechanism for self-resonance

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Two permanent magnets (embedded in the test apparatus to contribute flux)
- Multiple coils: input coil, output coil, tickler coil
- Transistor (planned as an on/off switch driven by the tickler coil)
- Stereo power amplifier
- Sound card oscilloscope (PC-based)

## Techniques and Methods
- Sound card oscilloscope used as a signal generator and measurement tool
- Square wave excitation at 50 Hz with a 25% duty cycle
- Audio power amplifier to boost the drive signal before injection into the coil
- Voltage comparison between input and output coils to assess energy ratios
- Tickler coil feedback topology for self-resonant (self-oscillating) switching
