## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to wind a serpentine coil for a homemade generator, building on a previous episode where he constructed a one-way bearing. He explains the underlying electromagnetic theory (Fleming's right-hand generator rule) before walking through the practical winding process using a simple jig of bolts on a board. The finished coil is tested successfully, lighting an LED.

## Key Topics
- Serpentine coil design and why it suits this generator
- Fleming's right-hand generator rule (movement, current, and magnetic field at 90° to each other)
- How alternating north-south magnets require the coil to redirect current so the net flow is unidirectional
- Practical winding technique using a wooden board and bolt jig
- 3D-printed cover plate to hold the coil on the former
- Successful live test generating enough current to light an LED

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Fine (hair-thin) magnet wire (large spool)
- Wooden board (used as winding base)
- Two bolts with rubber covering (winding jig pegs)
- Tape (to hold wound coil together)
- Small tack/clip pieces (to keep coil in shape during transfer)
- 3D-printed cover plate (PLA or similar, not specified)
- 20 permanent magnets (10 north, 10 south, arranged alternately)
- LED (used as load for testing)

## Techniques and Methods
- Template-guided serpentine coil winding
- Bolt-and-board jig winding (wrapping wire around two pegged bolts to form flat coil sections)
- Measuring coil path length with string (+10% allowance)
- Transferring wound coil sections onto a former in a serpentine pattern
- 3D printing a snap-on cover plate to retain the coil on the former
- Live LED test to verify generator output

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comments are largely brief social replies, but a few contain useful clarifications:
- **How to wind it:** "Wind it around like a snake mate — the rest is to understand why — practically all you need do is wrap it like a snake and it works fine — the why is the up/down north/south bit." This is the clearest distillation of the technique for beginners.
- **3D print files:** The STL files for the cover plate are publicly available on Tinkercad; details are in the video itself.
- **Coil arrangements:** He confirms he made additional videos covering alternate coil arrangements and encourages viewers to check them out.
- **Contact:** He directs several viewers to his email (robertmurraysmith64@gmail.com) for further questions.
- **Wire unspooling:** Acknowledges that the large spool of wire he is using really needs to be unspooled before winding — a practical tip for anyone replicating the build.
