## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith introduces rolling contact joints using the "Magic Wallet" as a fun starting point — a toy invented by French waiters that grips cards via elastic bands. He 3D-prints his own version in TinkerCAD, then extends the concept to curved-surface rolling joints using a design from Brigham Young University (BYU), demonstrating that these joints have very low friction, require no lubrication, and show strong potential for robotics and mechanical design.
