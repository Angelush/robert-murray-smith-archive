## Overview
Robert explains the motivation behind his "Mechanical Alphabet" video series. While planning to build a Wimshurst electrostatic generator to power an electrostatic motor, he needed a contra-rotating disc mechanism and realised that a library of reusable mechanism videos would serve as a practical shorthand reference — eliminating the need to re-explain common mechanisms every time they appear in a project. He invites viewers to contribute their own mechanism videos in the same format.

## Key Topics
- Origin and purpose of the Mechanical Alphabet series
- Wimshurst generator as the immediate motivating project
- Contra-rotating disc mechanism as a specific need
- The standard video format used in the series (explanation → build → demonstration → use cases)
- The Mechanical Alphabet as a reusable reference library
- Open invitation for community contributions to the series
- Desk toys / gifts as a secondary inspiration

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- FWG version two ink (mentioned as the material used in a viewer-suggested electrostatic motor)

## Techniques and Methods
- Building a Wimshurst electrostatic generator (high-voltage charge generation using contra-rotating discs)
- Collecting charge in capacitors to drive an electrostatic motor
- Fabricating mechanical desk models as demonstrations of linkage mechanisms
