## Overview
This is a short video from Robert's "Advent Science Magic" series, posted on December 22nd as Day 22 of his Christmas advent calendar. It demonstrates a simple but striking optical illusion using a hand-drawn ladder: by bending the drawing and adjusting your viewpoint, the ladder appears straight and seems to cast a shadow, making a pencil placed near it look like it's either on top of or beneath the ladder depending on where you position it.

## Key Topics
- Optical illusion using a 2D drawing of a ladder
- How perspective and viewpoint can trick the brain into perceiving depth and shadow
- Demonstration of figure-ground and depth perception effects

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Paper with a hand-drawn ladder
- Pencil (used both to crease the drawing and as a prop in the illusion)

## Techniques and Methods
- Drawing a ladder on paper and folding/bending it at the middle
- Adjusting viewing angle (~90 degrees) until the ladder appears as a straight, 3D object
- Using a pencil as a reference prop to demonstrate the perceived depth reversal
