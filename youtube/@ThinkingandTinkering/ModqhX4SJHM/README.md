## Overview

This video, presented by Luke (a collaborator on the channel), explores London's largely forgotten high-pressure hydraulic water network, which operated from 1883 to 1976. It explains how high-pressure water — rather than electricity — was used to distribute power across London for nearly a century, and reflects on what might have been had the network survived into the energy crises of the late 1970s.
