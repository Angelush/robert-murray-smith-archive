## Overview
In this short-form video, Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates the production of graphene via electrochemical exfoliation of intercalated graphite using a welder (arc/MMA welding). Starting with approximately 5 millilitres of intercalated graphite, the process achieves a remarkable 150× volume expansion into 750 millilitres of exfoliated material.

## Key Topics
- Electrochemical/arc-assisted exfoliation of graphite to produce graphene
- Volume expansion as a measure of exfoliation success
- Low-cost, accessible approach to graphene production using standard welding equipment

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Intercalated graphite** — the starting material subjected to exfoliation
- **Welding rod (6013 standard)** — used as the electrode in the arc/MMA process

## Techniques and Methods
- **Arc welding (MMA — Manual Metal Arc)** — used to drive exfoliation of the intercalated graphite
- **Electrochemical exfoliation** — the underlying mechanism expanding graphite into graphene layers
- Volume measurement (before/after) to quantify expansion (~5 ml → ~750 ml, 150× expansion)
