## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith constructs a small wind turbine using pairs of men's underwear ("tighty-whities") as the sail elements, mounted on a DIY frame. The structure is assembled from a music stand tripod, wooden discs with bearings, timber uprights, and wooden stair sections. The result is a novelty demonstration that the underwear-sail concept can generate rotation in the wind.

## Key Topics
- DIY wind turbine construction on a budget using unconventional materials
- Use of a repurposed music stand as the main structural support
- Demonstration of sail-style wind capture using fabric (underwear)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Music stand (used as tripod base)
- Wooden discs with bearings
- Timber uprights (three)
- Wooden stair sections (six, used as rotating arms)
- Three pairs of men's briefs ("tighty-whities") used as wind sails

## Techniques and Methods
- Mounting wooden discs with bearings onto a tripod to allow free rotation
- Stretching fabric (underwear) between timber uprights to form wind-catching sails
- Staking the structure into the ground for stability
