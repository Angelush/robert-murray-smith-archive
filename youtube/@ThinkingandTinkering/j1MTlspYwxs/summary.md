## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build an ultra-simple gasification stove (or mini rocket stove) using just two supermarket tin cans — one of mushy peas and one of new potatoes — costing 55p total. The build requires only drilling and filing holes in specific patterns before nesting the cans together, and the result burns twigs effectively. He then fits a gas mantle (from a previous video) to transform the flame from a simple fire into a bright, efficient heat and light source.

## Techniques and Methods
- Punching/drilling holes in tin cans using a punch tool (rather than a drill bit) for ease
- Cutting a large hole in the outer can using a snips/knife, then filing to size with a round file (filing inward only, to curl the edge)
- Nested-can assembly: inner can inserted through hole in outer can for a tight fit
- Hole pattern: double ring of small holes at the bottom of the outer can; ring of 4 mm holes at the top; 10 mm holes on the inner can; inner can bottom filled with holes
- Lighting with twigs and observing gas jets burning from the side holes
- Fitting a gas mantle over the flame to dramatically increase light and heat output
