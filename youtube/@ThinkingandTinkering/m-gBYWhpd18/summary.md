## Overview
Robert unboxes and reviews the Windgoo B9 electric scooter, a seated e-scooter with 12-inch wheels and a shopping basket, which was sent to him for review. Beyond the product itself, the video focuses heavily on the legal status of privately-owned e-scooters in the UK as of mid-2022, explaining why they are technically illegal on public roads and pavements, while noting that legislation to regularise them under an electric bicycle framework was expected by late 2022. Robert concludes that the B9 is likely to sit at the top end of what will be legally permissible once that legislation passes.

## Robert's Own Replies
- Robert notes he has **arthritis** and personally appreciates the seated design and comfort of the B9, finding it especially suitable for his condition.
- He mentions the B9 has **cruise control**, which he acknowledges he forgot to cover in the video itself — a useful clarification.
- He disagrees with commenters who are negative about small scooters, arguing that the boom in scooters and bikes is an opportunity to do something different, and that a small scooter is particularly convenient for short hops.
- He agrees the legal situation is "getting a little ridiculous" but expresses hope it will be resolved soon.
