## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to build a simple DIY flywheel "mechanical battery" using a standard gym weight plate, 3D-printed adapter parts, bearings, and neodymium magnets. The video shows the assembled flywheel spinning freely and generating electricity via a coil and LEDs, proving the concept works with minimal cost and effort. His main takeaway is that flywheel energy storage can be made accessible to home builders using cheap, readily available materials.
