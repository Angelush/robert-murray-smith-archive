## Overview
Robert provides a brief constructional walkthrough of two prototype Tesla (bladeless/disc) turbine generators he has built, comparing their designs side by side. He explains how magnet placement, disc size, and the geometry of the air-inlet lip directly affect efficiency, and notes that a third prototype is already in development with further improvements planned.

## Key Topics
- Comparison of a small and a larger disc turbine generator prototype
- Role of disc size and magnet proximity to the centre in reducing magnetic cogging and improving torque
- Importance of the air-inlet lip angle and dimensions for efficient operation
- Coil winding configuration (Y/star configuration, three north–south pairs)
- Core material choice and its impact on eddy-current losses
- Evaluation of an evolute (snail-shell/volute) casing design versus the simpler lip design
- Mention of an ongoing Prototype 3 build by collaborator Steve

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Mild steel** — used as the magnetic core in the larger prototype; noted as lossy due to eddy currents
- **Ferrite** — planned replacement for the mild steel core in the next version to reduce eddy-current losses

## Techniques and Methods
- Coil winding in a **Y (star) configuration** with three north–south pairs
- **Brushless DC motor** repurposed as the generator stator/rotor base
- Comparison of **evolute (volute/snail-shell) casing** vs. **lip-inlet casing** for airflow efficiency
- Hand-spinning and hair-dryer-driven testing to evaluate machine momentum and performance

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Not a free-energy device**: Robert explicitly pushes back on "free energy" framing, directing commenters to his efficiency video and stressing the project is about experimentation and inspiration, not overunity claims.
- **Practical mindset**: He encourages thinking about *how* these devices could be used rather than cataloguing reasons they can't work, contrasting the "average person" with the "inventor" mindset.
- **Cost-per-kilowatt angle**: He finds the device interesting from a constructional/economic standpoint — how its build cost compares to traditional generators — rather than as an energy-production breakthrough.
- **Airflow vs. spinning discs**: Clarifies to a commenter that reversing the machine (spinning discs to move air rather than air turning discs) would not work the same way, though he's open to trying.
- **Pancake coils**: Acknowledges a suggestion to try pancake coils, and mentions familiarity with Viktor Schauberger's work.
- **Evolute airflow**: Explains that the lip design works because the airstream enters tangentially, whereas the evolute does not achieve this as effectively.
- **Location matters for energy strategy**: Notes that any advice about energy generation depends heavily on local resources (geothermal, solar, wind, etc.).
