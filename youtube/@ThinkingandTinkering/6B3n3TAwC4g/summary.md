## Robert's Own Replies
- **Temperature limit:** The practical upper limit for this ink in heating applications is around **65°C**; for underfloor heating, standard operating temp is ~45°C with a max of 65°C, and PET is normally used as the substrate.
- **High-temperature variant:** He previously coated a large porcelain floor tile with the ink and achieved up to **400°C**.
- **Thickness clarification:** "1 mil" is a US unit of measurement equating to **25 micrometers**.
- **Safety note:** This egg-frying demo is "just a bit of fun" — not a recommended real-world cooking method.
- **Pattern matters for uniformity:** For practical heating applications, he suggests varying the ink pattern away from a solid block to ensure more **even heat distribution**.
- **Didn't eat the egg:** He confirms he did not actually eat it.
- **Glazing idea:** Responded positively to a suggestion of mixing the ink with frit for an overglaze application.
