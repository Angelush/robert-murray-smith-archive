## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates that his waterproof graphene ink can generate a small voltage when salt water is flowed across its surface, inspired by a Chinese research paper on the phenomenon. He measures approximately 7–8 millivolts during the experiment and dubs the concept the "SWAG" (Salt Water And Graphene) generator. The video is a brief proof-of-concept showing that the waterproof property of his ink makes it uniquely suited for this application, as the graphene coating remains intact when wetted.
