## Overview
This is Part 7 of Robert's series on building a 1kW vertical axis wind turbine (VAWT) for under £100, capturing its first real outdoor wind test in a car park at night. The turbine successfully generated power at very low wind speeds, lighting up 30V LED loads, demonstrating the potential of rim-mounted magnet VAWTs. Robert argues this design is better suited to urban environments than conventional horizontal axis wind turbines (HAWTs).

## Key Topics
- First real-world wind test of the DIY VAWT prototype
- Cut-in and stall wind speeds of the turbine
- Rim-mounted magnet generator design concept and its advantages for VAWTs
- Comparison of VAWTs vs HAWTs for urban vs offshore applications
- Reference to Honeywell's failed rim-magnet HAWT product
- Plans for next steps: adding meters, measuring output properly, and roof mounting

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- LED lights rated at 30V / 700mA (used as load/indicator during the test)
- Permanent magnets mounted at the rim of the turbine rotor
- Coils (only 4 installed at time of filming; more being collected)

## Techniques and Methods
- Anemometer (handheld wind speed meter) used to measure wind speed during the test
- Visual output assessment via LED brightness as a proxy for power output
- Ground-level testing on a trolley jack in a car park before planned roof installation

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Cost so far:** Confirms he has spent only about £45 on the build to date.
- **Stall speed clarification:** Reiterates the stall speed is 0.9 m/s and considers this not a problem.
- **VAWTs are self-limiting:** States he believes VAWTs are inherently self-limiting in high winds (no separate brake needed), which is a positive side effect of the design.
- **No materials list provided:** Declines to publish a formal materials list, noting the design is so simple and variable that a list would be unhelpful — though he mentions considering a "standard" design, especially given his laser-stacking work.
- **Bedini coil reference:** Points a commenter to "Bedini coil" or "SSG motor" on Google for related coil design info.
- **Fan testing not ideal:** Notes that using a fan for testing would give misleading results as fan airflow is concentrated at the centre and negligible at the edges.
- **Roof mounting:** Plans to mount it on the roof but prefers to keep working on it at ground level for now.
- **Self-deprecating on expertise:** Emphasises he is not an expert and is openly learning through the process — mistakes are part of the journey.
