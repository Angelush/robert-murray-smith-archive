## Overview
Robert demonstrates a hand-made Hall effect sensor constructed from paper painted with graphene ink, showing a live voltage change when a magnet is brought near. He explains the physics behind the Hall effect — how a magnetic field deflects electron flow in a conductor, creating a measurable voltage difference perpendicular to the current. He concludes that graphene ink can serve as a viable material for painting functional Hall effect sensors, opening up possibilities for DIY sensor fabrication.

## Key Topics
- What the Hall effect is and how it works physically
- Live demonstration of the Hall effect using graphene-painted paper
- Graphene as a suitable material for Hall effect sensors
- Potential applications of Hall effect sensors (position, current, and timing sensing)

## Techniques and Methods
- Painting a conductive graphene ink strip onto paper to form a Hall effect sensor
- Attaching crocodile clips to opposite edges of the strip to supply current and measure transverse voltage
- Applying a magnet to the painted strip to observe the Hall voltage deflection
- Using a multimeter/display to read the voltage change in real time
