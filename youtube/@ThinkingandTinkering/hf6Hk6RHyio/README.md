## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith makes the case that 3D printing is no longer the exclusive domain of technical experts and hobbyists, drawing an analogy to how most car owners simply want transportation, not engine knowledge. After three months of personal use, he concludes that modern 3D printers are accessible, easy-to-use tools suitable for everyday makers who just want to create things, not master the machinery.
