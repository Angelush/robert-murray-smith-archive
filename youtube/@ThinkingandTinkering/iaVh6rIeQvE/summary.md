## Overview
Part 3 of Robert's electric kick scooter build series focuses on fabricating and fitting the metal panels — specifically floor pans, mudguards, and a front shield. He repurposes galvanized steel kitchen table tops, plans to sand off the galvanization and copper-plate the bare steel for a copper aesthetic with steel strength, then bends the panels using a vise and angle iron. By the end of the video, the scooter's bodywork is largely complete, with only the electrics and front control panel remaining.

## Key Topics
- Designing and cutting floor pans to house the battery and electronics
- Material selection: galvanized steel over copper for cost and strength reasons
- Copper-plating steel panels for appearance
- Metal bending without a steel brake (improvised vise method)
- Walk-around of the scooter's current state: front light, indicators, extended handlebars, rear light cluster, copper mudguard, and copper splash plate

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Galvanized steel** — sourced from kitchen table tops; used for the floor pans and panels
- **Copper** — considered initially but rejected as too expensive and soft; planned as a plated finish over bare steel
- **Lacquer** — to be applied over the copper plating as a protective topcoat

## Techniques and Methods
- **Angle grinding** — cutting panel shapes from the steel sheet
- **Steel bending in a vise** — clamping steel between angle iron pieces and forcing/hammering a bend, as an improvised substitute for a steel brake
- **Sanding/grinding off galvanization** — preparing bare steel surface prior to copper plating
- **Electroplating (copper over steel)** — to achieve copper appearance with steel structural properties
