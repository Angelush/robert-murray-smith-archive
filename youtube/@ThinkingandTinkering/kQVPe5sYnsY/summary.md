## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith combines two previously built components — a push-button mechanism and a modular generator — into an integrated "Magic Button Generator." By adding compression springs and reprinting adapter plates to fit a bearing, he completes the assembly and demonstrates it generating 4–6 volts from repeated button presses. The key takeaway is that any source of vibrational or mechanical energy (footsteps, sitting, wind) can drive this button-rotation-generation chain to harvest useful electricity.

## Key Topics
- Integration of a push-button mechanism with a modular electromagnetic generator
- Spring-loading the push button using compression springs
- Adapting the coil former and plates to accept a bearing for smooth rotation
- Voltage output measurement (4–6 V demonstrated)
- Miniaturisation possibilities and practical energy-harvesting applications
- Modular design philosophy for reuse in future projects
- Potential use as a wind generator

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Compression springs (25 mm long × ~18.5 mm diameter, used in pairs)
- Ball bearing (42 mm × 20 mm × 12 mm, sourced from Amazon, ~£3 for two)
- 3D-printed parts: spring housing, cover plate, coil former, adapter plates, cradle (designed in TinkerCAD)
- Permanent magnets (loaded into the magnet backing disc/flywheel)
- Coil (wound on a coil former)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing (TinkerCAD design, FDM fabrication of all structural parts)
- Spring-loaded push-button assembly (pairing two compression springs to achieve required length)
- Press-fit bearing installation into reprinted coil-former plate (20 mm circumference gap matched to bearing OD)
- Modular assembly/disassembly for component reuse
- Voltage measurement with a multimeter during button-press demonstration
