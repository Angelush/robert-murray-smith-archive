## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith visits Locomotion, the National Railway Museum's satellite site in Shildon, County Durham — the birthplace of the railways. He tours the collection of early steam locomotives, including a pre-Locomotion No.1 Stephenson engine, a model of Locomotion, the Sans Pareil (1829), and part of the Royal Train exhibit. The video celebrates the ingenuity of early steam engineering and the surprising power generated by simple piston mechanisms.

## Key Topics
- History of Shildon Works and its connection to the Stockton & Darlington Railway
- George Stephenson's early locomotive-building career
- Evolution of steam boiler design (fire tube vs. water tube vs. combined boilers)
- Comparison of early "simple" engines vs. more developed steam engines
- Various oddities in the collection: snowplow attachments, a Wickham trolley for track repair, a velocipede/boneshaker

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Water (as working fluid in steam boilers)

## Techniques and Methods
- Fire tube boiler design: hot exhaust gases pass through tubes surrounded by water
- Water tube boiler design: water flows through tubes surrounded by fire/hot gases
- Combined fire tube and water tube boiler construction for improved efficiency
