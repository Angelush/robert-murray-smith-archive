## Overview
Robert introduces complete beginners to the world of CNC machining, using his new Makera Carver Air as the context. Rather than a software walkthrough, the video makes the conceptual case that the real skill in CAD is developing 3D spatial thinking — not memorising any particular program. He argues that all CAD tools share the same underlying logic (primitives → parts → assemblies) and recommends focusing on what you are trying to build rather than letting the software overwhelm you.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to the Makera Carver Air CNC machine for beginners
- Difference between additive manufacturing (3D printing) and subtractive manufacturing (CNC)
- Why 3D spatial thinking is the core skill, not software proficiency
- Tinkercad as a beginner-friendly "Lego block" CAD tool
- FreeCAD as Robert's chosen open-source CAD/CAM program
- The concept of primitives (cylinders, cubes, arcs) as building blocks
- The workflow: sketch → primitive → part → assembly
- CAD workbenches and how to navigate them without feeling overwhelmed
- The philosophy of "job dictates the tool" rather than the reverse
- A reciprocating clock used as a real-world assembly example

## Robert's Own Replies
- **File formats clarified:** SVG files are well-suited for laser cutters; STL files are common in 3D printing as they describe surface meshes; STP/STEP files contain richer design data but cannot be 3D printed as they do not describe the surface in the same way.
- **FreeCAD experience:** Robert found it took about an hour to get comfortable with FreeCAD. He relates it to technical drawing lessons at school and found the "sheet of paper" analogy natural, noting that this prior experience may have given him an advantage.
- **3D/2D translation difficulty:** He acknowledges this is where many people struggle and suggests kinaesthetic activities — he mentions clay sculpture specifically — as a way to build the link between visualising and feeling a 3D form.
- **Context for the video:** He confirms the video is connected to a product launch by Makera and that he has wanted to get into CNC for a while; he deliberately avoided the standard unboxing/tutorial format to offer something different.
- **Production values:** He explicitly states he will not be changing his production style, as clarity of content matters more to him than high production values.
