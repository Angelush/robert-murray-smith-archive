## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reflects on the wide gap between theoretical predictions and practical reality for graphene, arguing that most of the hype surrounding the material is rooted in theoretical constructs rather than achievable fabrication. He contends that graphene is genuinely impressive, but that a practical, problem-solving mindset—rather than theoretical dreaming—is what will unlock its real-world value.
