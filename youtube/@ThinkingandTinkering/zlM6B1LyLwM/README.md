## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a simple mathematical formula for calculating the correct tongue lengths needed to produce specific musical notes on a tongue drum (or any percussion instrument). Using salvaged wooden flooring as his starting material, he derives a material-specific constant `k` from a single measurement, then applies it to compute tongue lengths for a full octave. The key insight is that by keeping material thickness and width constant, frequency depends only on length.
