## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a 3D-printed flywheel mechanism inspired by Edison's phonomotor, which used a vibrating membrane to spin a ratcheted flywheel via mechanical energy conversion. He combines Edison's ratchet flywheel concept with a "SEO magic winding mechanism" — a bidirectional-input, unidirectional-output hook-fork ratchet — to create a button-driven spinning device. The key takeaway is that attaching a generator to this assembly would yield a compact button-powered electrical generator.

## Key Topics
- Edison's phonomotor and its mechanical energy conversion principle
- Flywheel design with peripheral weighting using steel ball bearings
- Ratchet mechanisms: traditional ratchet-and-pawl vs. the SEO magic winding mechanism
- 3D-printed parts designed in TinkerCAD
- Concept of converting button-press motion into rotational energy for electricity generation

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Steel ball bearings (10 mm, press-fitted into 11 mm holes for flywheel weighting)
- Skate bearings (22 mm × 8 mm × 7 mm) for the axle cradle
- Glue (used to fix the guide plate and cap)
- Springs (mentioned as a planned addition to automate button return)

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing parts in TinkerCAD (flywheel, cradle, hooked fork, guide sleeve, cap)
- Press-fitting steel ball bearings into the flywheel rim for added rotational inertia
- Hammer-fitting bearings into tight holes
- Assembling a ratchet flywheel using a hooked-fork "magic winding" mechanism instead of a conventional pawl
- Spring-loading a push-button to automate the reciprocating input motion
