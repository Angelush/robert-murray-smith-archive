## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to make graphitic carbon nitride (g-C₃N₄) using a home kiln, starting from cheap urea pellets. The video highlights that this remarkable photocatalytic material — capable of splitting water into hydrogen using sunlight — can be synthesized at home for pennies per gram despite costing ~$100/gram commercially. The process is simple enough to serve as a first experiment for anyone with a kiln.

## Key Topics
- Introduction to graphitic carbon nitride as a high-value wonder material
- Photocatalytic properties: hydrogen production from water splitting using sunlight
- Home kiln setup and usage
- Step-by-step synthesis procedure from urea
- Safety considerations (ammonia off-gassing)
- Cost comparison: DIY vs. commercial pricing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Urea** — the sole precursor; ~150g used, costs ~£2–3/kg
- **Graphitic carbon nitride (g-C₃N₄)** — the product; lemon yellow when pure, pinkish-gray if contaminated
- **Ammonia** — produced as a by-product during heating; ventilation required

## Techniques and Methods
- Thermal pyrolysis of urea in a ceramic cup with an inverted saucer as a loose lid
- Controlled ramp-up heating: 100°C increments with 15-minute holds at each step up to 550°C
- Hold at 550°C for 3 hours, then allow furnace to cool naturally before opening
- Use of a 3kW home kiln (bought, not built)
- Contamination control to achieve correct lemon-yellow product color
