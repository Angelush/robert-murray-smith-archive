## Overview
This video, presented by Luke (not Robert), demonstrates how to recycle old bicycle inner tubes into a functional slingshot. The build combines metalwork, woodworking, and rubber cutting, using almost entirely reclaimed or low-cost materials. The finished slingshot proves to be quite powerful in outdoor testing, with the potential to be made even stronger by layering the rubber.

## Key Topics
- Repurposing waste bicycle inner tubes as slingshot elastic
- Bending and welding steel bar to form the slingshot frame
- Carving a custom oak pistol-grip handle
- Making a duct tape projectile pouch as a leather substitute
- Attaching elastic to frame and pouch using inner tube strips
- Outdoor performance testing with pebbles

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Bicycle inner tubes — used as elastic bands and binding strips
- 8mm steel bar — bent and welded to form the Y-frame
- Oak wood — carved into the pistol-grip handle
- Resin/glue — used to bond the oak handle halves around the frame
- Duct tape — layered to create the projectile pouch

## Techniques and Methods
- Bar bending to form the slingshot fork
- MIG/spot welding the two frame halves together
- Grinding welds flush for a clean handle fit
- Chiselling oak blocks to seat the metal frame
- Clamping and curing the glued handle assembly for 24 hours
- Freehand carving the pistol grip with hacksaw and chisels
- Cutting inner tube strips for elastic bands
- Hole-punching the duct tape pouch
- Wrapping and tucking inner tube strips (using a screwdriver as leverage) to secure elastic to frame and pouch
