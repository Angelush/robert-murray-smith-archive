## Overview
This video traces the long, cyclical history of solar energy from antiquity to the mid-20th century, arguing that solar adoption has never been driven by foresight or planning, but only by fuel crises when easier alternatives become scarce. Robert uses the post-WWII energy shortage (1947–1956) as the central case study, showing how solar thermal flourished briefly before being displaced by nuclear power and renewed oil production.

## Key Topics
- Solar energy use in ancient civilisations (burning mirrors, sun rooms, cliff-dwelling passive solar)
- The Great Clearance deforestation in England (1534–1696) as an early driver of solar interest
- 18th–19th century solar thermal technology: solar ovens, steamboats, and Augustine Mouchot's 1878 solar cooling engine
- Frank Schuman's 1910 solar steam engine using parabolic reflectors
- The transition from wood to coal and its role in suppressing solar development
- The post-WWII fuel crisis (1947–1956) and the solar thermal revival it triggered
- The 1947 book *Your Solar House* by Liberty Owens and Ford Glass Company
- The 1956 World Symposium on the Practical Use of Solar Energy (Phoenix, Arizona)
- Maria Telkes and solar distillation / phase-change thermal storage
- Early history of solar photovoltaics (1916 photovoltaic effect, 1932 CdSe cells, 1954 silicon cells, 1964 NASA space mission)
- The 1976 oil crisis and the shift toward solar PV over solar thermal
- The recurring pattern: solar only revives during energy crises, then is abandoned when easier sources return
- The underutilisation of solar thermal in the current renewable energy transition

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Wood/timber** — the dominant fuel and construction material prior to coal; its scarcity drove early solar interest
- **Coal** — replaced wood from the 17th century onward, suppressing solar development
- **Oil** — dominated post-WWII energy, again displacing solar after the mid-1950s
- **Cadmium selenide (CdSe)** — demonstrated the photovoltaic effect in 1932; used in early experimental solar cells
- **Silicon** — used in the first practical solar cells built in 1954 (4% efficiency)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Burning mirrors / magnifying materials** — ancient use of concentrated sunlight to ignite fires and light torches
- **Passive solar architecture (sun rooms)** — Roman bathhouses and Anasazi cliff dwellings using south-facing orientation to capture heat
- **Solar ovens and cookers** — demonstrated for long ocean voyages in the 1700s–1800s
- **Solar-powered steam engines** — Schuman's 1910 installation using parabolic reflectors to generate steam
- **Solar cooling / refrigeration** — Mouchot's 1878 Paris Exhibition demonstration (solar collector + cooling engine to make ice cream)
- **Solar distillation** — Maria Telkes's WWII-era device for emergency seawater desalination for downed pilots
- **Phase-change material thermal storage** — credited to Maria Telkes as the first solar thermal system using a PCM
- **Solar photovoltaics** — early silicon cells (1954), NASA space applications (1964), scaled up during the 1976 energy crisis
