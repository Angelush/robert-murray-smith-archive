## Overview

Robert takes the "Perpetual Wedge" mechanism from video 2310 — an external gear system achieving a 100:1 gear ratio from just three parts — and adapts it into an internal ring-gear configuration suitable for clockmaking. By converting the external spur-gear design into a pair of internal ring gears (60 and 61 teeth), he eliminates the elliptical motion problem and produces a compact, concentric 60:1 reduction mechanism. The video illustrates a general principle: understanding what a mechanism *does* allows you to adapt it into the specific form your application requires.
