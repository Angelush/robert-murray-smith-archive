## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith explores the concept of the "sand battery" — a thermal energy storage system developed commercially by Finnish engineers that heats sand to 500–600°C using resistive electric heaters to store renewable energy as heat for months at a time. He contextualizes the technology historically (noting NASA researched it in 1979), discusses its feasibility at the home scale, and argues that necessity, not desire, is what drives society to adopt such energy solutions.
