## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith explores Leonardo da Vinci's iconic catapult design, discussing Da Vinci's legacy as a Renaissance polymath before walking through his own 3D-printed interpretation of the machine. He demonstrates assembly of the printed model and then shows how he designed it in Tinkercad using simple primitive shapes, encouraging viewers to try drawing it themselves.

## Key Topics
- Leonardo da Vinci's life, journals, and inventive philosophy
- Da Vinci's catapult as a historical and educational artifact
- 3D printing a personal interpretation of the catapult design
- Assembly of the printed model (frame, throwing arm, ratchet, trigger mechanism)
- Step-by-step CAD design walkthrough in Tinkercad
- Availability of STL files on Thingiverse

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- PLA (polylactic acid) — used for 3D printing the catapult parts; noted as not very springy and subject to wear, particularly in the spring arms

## Techniques and Methods
- 3D printing with PLA using a personal CAD design
- Tinkercad modelling using primitive shapes (cubes, cylinders) with boolean hole operations for slots and recesses
- Peg-and-slot joinery for tool-free, press-fit assembly
- Ratchet mechanism design (referenced separately in video 2078)
- String trigger mechanism for releasing the throwing arm
