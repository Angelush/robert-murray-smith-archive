## Overview
Robert demonstrates several wind turbine blade designs mounted on a homemade DVD-drive-based generator, testing each with a hair dryer and measuring milliamp output. The video builds on a previous project (converting a DVD drive motor into a generator) and explores both conventional and unconventional rotor configurations. The standout finding is a flat disc impeller design inspired by inventor Mike Waters, which Robert argues could be cheaply deployed in large numbers on flat building surfaces.

## Key Topics
- Recap of the DVD drive motor-to-generator conversion from a prior video
- Flywheel construction using stacked CDs with a lead sheet insert
- Comparison testing of multiple rotor/blade designs
- Savonius (S-curve) rotor design
- Squirrel cage rotor design
- Circular-blade squirrel cage variant
- Flat disc impeller design inspired by Mike Waters, with central cone to direct airflow
- Concept of tiling many such generators across flat building facades as distributed micro-generators
- Relationship to Tesla turbine design principles

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **DVD/CD drive motor** — stripped from a CD/DVD drive and converted to a generator
- **CDs** — stacked (2–3) to form the flywheel
- **Lead sheet** — sandwiched between CDs to add flywheel mass
- **Builders board (white board)** — used as the mounting base
- **Butyl rubber** — used as a quick adhesive to attach blade prototypes for testing
- **Microwave oven fan** — repurposed as the conventional fan blade test
- **Vacuum cleaner impeller** — two metal plates with internal curved blades; used as the flat disc turbine
- **Metal cone** — placed at the centre of the flat disc turbine to direct incoming airflow outward through the blades

## Techniques and Methods
- Converting a brushless DC DVD drive spindle motor into a generator
- Constructing a weighted flywheel from laminated CDs and lead sheet
- Using a hair dryer as a controlled wind source for bench testing
- Attaching interchangeable rotor prototypes with butyl rubber for rapid swapping
- Measuring electrical output in milliamps from each rotor configuration
- Adding a central cone to an axial-inflow impeller to improve airflow guidance and output
