## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith builds a simple piezoelectric capacitor using graphene oxide sandwiched between two Kapton dielectric layers and aluminium electrodes, inspired by research on the piezoelectric response of graphene oxide. While investigating its piezoelectric behaviour, he discovers it acts as a surprisingly effective EMF antenna — picking up his body's proximity, generating measurable voltage and current, and ultimately functioning as a working TV aerial.
