## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith builds a simple piezoelectric capacitor using graphene oxide sandwiched between two Kapton dielectric layers and aluminium electrodes, inspired by research on the piezoelectric response of graphene oxide. While investigating its piezoelectric behaviour, he discovers it acts as a surprisingly effective EMF antenna — picking up his body's proximity, generating measurable voltage and current, and ultimately functioning as a working TV aerial.

## Key Topics
- Piezoelectric properties of graphene oxide (GO)
- Construction of a graphene oxide capacitor cell
- Unexpected antenna/EMF detection behaviour of the device
- Voltage and current measurements from the GO cell
- Using the GO cell as a television aerial

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Graphene oxide (GO)** — active piezoelectric/antenna layer, noted as applied too thickly; spin coating recommended for future attempts
- **Kapton** — used as the dielectric layers on either side of the GO
- **Aluminium** — used as the electrode/capacitor plates
- **Coaxial cable** — used to connect the device to the TV tuner input

## Techniques and Methods
- Painting graphene oxide between two dielectric (Kapton) sheets with metal plate electrodes to form a capacitor structure
- Multimeter measurement of voltage (volts), current (milliamps), and frequency output
- Earthing the device to a water pipe to measure current draw
- Connecting the GO cell to a TV coaxial input via paper clips and wires to test as an aerial
- Proximity detection via body EMF disturbance

## Robert's Own Replies
- The graphene oxide layer was **too thick** in this experiment; he recommends **spin coating** the GO for anyone trying to reproduce it.
- He tried connecting the structure directly to the balun (without the wires) and it still worked as an antenna; he plans to look at signal strength.
- He suspects the body/earth connection is part of a **dipole antenna** system.
- He acknowledges not having an oscilloscope, limiting his ability to show detailed signal data.
- He notes the inner GO layer is not connected to anything and considers connecting it in a follow-up.
- He attributes some signal degradation to **impedance mismatching**.
- He did explore similar structures in later videos with a slightly different design.
- His general stance: the knowledge belongs to everyone, encourages others to experiment and make videos of their results.
