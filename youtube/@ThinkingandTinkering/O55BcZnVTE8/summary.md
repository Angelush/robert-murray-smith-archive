## Overview
Robert unboxes and does an initial assembly review of the AlgoLaser Alpha MK2, a 20W diode laser sent to him for review. He compares it favourably against more expensive CO2 lasers (like the xTool X2 P2) as a more affordable entry point into laser cutting at around £700. After assembling the machine and connecting it via Wi-Fi, he runs a quick test cut on 3mm basswood using the AlgoLaser phone app, concluding it offers good value out of the box with a few minor assembly quirks.

## Key Topics
- Comparison between diode lasers and CO2 lasers (cost, power, footprint)
- Unboxing contents: what's included in the basic kit vs. pro kit vs. safe kit
- Assembly process and minor oddities encountered
- Wi-Fi connectivity and phone app control (AlgoLaser app)
- Open-frame design and fume management considerations
- Software options: LightBurn vs. LaserGRBL
- Smart camera (pro kit add-on) for workpiece positioning
- Test cut demonstration on basswood

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **3mm Basswood** — used as the test cut material during the initial run
- **Acrylic** — referenced as a benchmark material; the 10W-per-1mm rule of thumb cited for cutting

## Techniques and Methods
- Manual focus using a top-hat focus tool on the workpiece
- Wi-Fi-based remote operation via the AlgoLaser phone app
- Air assist (pump included in the box) for cutting
- Test grid power/speed calibration for diode lasers (mentioned as best practice for finding correct settings)
- Optical power stacking of diode modules to achieve higher wattage (explained as background context)
