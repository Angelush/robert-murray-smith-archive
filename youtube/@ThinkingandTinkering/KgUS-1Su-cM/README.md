## Overview

Robert demonstrates a DIY method for measuring the torque output of a 3D-printed wind turbine rotor using a worm gear mechanism. Rather than measuring volts or amps (which reflect the generator, not the rotor), he shows how torque — the effectiveness of a force in causing rotation — can be directly measured by hanging calibrated weights from a pulley driven through the worm gear. The key takeaway is that this low-tech approach gives a true measure of how well a rotor converts wind force into useful rotation.
