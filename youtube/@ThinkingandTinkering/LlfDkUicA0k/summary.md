## Overview
This video is part 6 of Robert Murray-Smith's series on building a Van de Graaff generator, focusing specifically on constructing the charge-collecting combs (brushes). Robert demonstrates multiple methods for making brushes, then shows his preferred approach using brass strip and pins soldered together, and explains the correct positioning of the combs relative to the belt and rollers.

## Key Topics
- Alternative materials for making VdGG brushes/combs
- Robert's preferred method: brass strip with soldered pins
- Wire-wrapping technique to hold pins in place for sweating (soldering)
- Correct positioning of combs at the top and bottom rollers
- Grounding requirements for the bottom comb
- Routing the top comb's wire to the interior of the sphere

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Stranded wire (cut and fanned for brush use)
- Copper sheet (A4 size, from arts and crafts shops such as Hobbycraft, used for embossing kits)
- Brass strip (base for the comb assembly)
- Ordinary dressmaking/sewing pins (for the comb teeth)
- Thin binding wire (for temporarily holding pins in place)
- Solder
- 6mm earth cable (for grounding the bottom comb)
- Insulated copper wire/cable (for the top comb connection to the sphere)

## Techniques and Methods
- Stripping and fanning stranded wire to form a simple brush
- Cutting copper sheet with pinking shears to create serrated brush edges
- Wire-wrapping pins onto a brass strip to hold them in alignment before soldering
- Sweating (soldering dissimilar or multiple metal components simultaneously)
- Positioning combs at the precise point where the belt joins (top roller) or leaves (bottom roller) the roller
- Running the top comb's tail wire up to approximately the centre of the sphere interior

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert added two important safety and grounding clarifications in the comments:
- **Against using house mains earth:** He explicitly warns that grounding a VdGG of this power to the household electrical earth is dangerous — it can discharge through the house wiring and damage plugged-in equipment.
- **Recommended grounding method:** He strongly advises sinking a dedicated copper pipe into the ground outside and running a separate earth cable from it directly to the generator. He acknowledges that alternatives can work, but insists a dedicated external earth is definitively better.
