## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates a hand-built "blow torch" generator — a device powered by blowing air into a rotor that spins a magnet disc inside a serpentine coil to generate electricity. The video walks through the full system from energy input to light output, illustrating the universal principles of electrical generation. His key takeaway is that all generators share the same basic subsystems: an energy input, a magnet-and-coil arrangement, a rectifier, a voltage regulator, and an energy storage/output stage.

## Key Topics
- The two fundamental approaches to generation: torque vs. speed
- Design of flat disc generators with edge-mounted magnets and serpentine coils
- 3D-printed generator housing, rotor, and handle components
- Assembly of a breath-powered "blow torch" generator
- Using the generator disc as a flywheel for energy smoothing
- Harvesting AC output via a rectifier and storing in a small battery
- Repurposing a cheap solar garden light as a self-contained charge/LED circuit
- Cost breakdown of the entire build (~£10 total)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Circular neodymium magnets (arranged N-S-N-S at disc edge) — £3.60
- 8mm × 100mm steel bar (axle through rotor)
- 22mm × 7mm × 8mm bearings (standard size used across all his generator builds)
- 8mm washers (to reduce bearing friction)
- 8mm olive compression fitting (axle retention)
- 3D-printed PLA parts (generator housing, disc, coil former, cap, handle)
- Solar garden light (£1.50 or 75p) containing: lithium battery, charge protection circuit, LED, on/off switch
- Bridge rectifier module
- Copper wire (serpentine coil)

## Techniques and Methods
- Serpentine coil winding (favoured for simplicity of construction)
- 3D printing of generator components (STL files provided; designed in Tinkercad)
- Magnet disc assembly with alternating N-S pole arrangement
- Press-fit bearing installation into printed housing
- Washer shimming to prevent axle/bearing friction
- Rectification of AC generator output using a bridge rectifier
- Harvesting energy into a lithium battery via an existing solar charge protection circuit (solar panel disconnected and replaced with generator output)
