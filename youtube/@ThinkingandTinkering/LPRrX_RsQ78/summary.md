## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith begins building an e-scooter frame from bamboo, using a salvaged drivetrain (motor and belt drive) from a discarded kids' e-scooter. He demonstrates his signature "stitch and glue" bamboo construction technique — drilling, slotting, binding with twine, and applying resin — to create a lightweight, low-cost frame. This is Part 1, ending with a finished structural frame ready for motor, battery box, and handlebars in the next video.

## Key Topics
- Salvaging a 120W motor and belt drivetrain from a discarded children's e-scooter
- Sourcing and selecting bamboo cane sizes (20mm and 45mm diameter) from UK Bamboo
- Applying the "stitch and glue" bamboo joining technique (also used in videos 1152 and 1156)
- Constructing a steering head using ball bearing cups from the donor scooter
- Anti-split drilling technique: drilling a hole at the end of a cut slot to arrest crack propagation
- Clamping fork joints with hose clips
- Environmental argument for bamboo as an alternative to plastic in consumer products

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Bamboo cane (20mm diameter)** — 8-foot lengths used for the main frame; sourced from UK Bamboo (~£6 for a bundle of 20)
- **Bamboo cane (45mm diameter)** — used for the steering column; sourced from UK Bamboo (~£20 for a bundle of 10)
- **Twine / string** — natural fiber used to bind and reinforce bamboo joints
- **Resin** — applied over twine bindings to consolidate joints
- **Carbon fiber or fiberglass** — mentioned as alternative reinforcement options to twine for a more permanent build
- **Doped fabric** — mentioned in author replies as a lightweight, strong alternative reinforcement material

## Techniques and Methods
- **Stitch and glue** — drilling holes, inserting bamboo, stitching with twine, and applying resin to joints
- **Hole saw cutting at 15° angle** — 45mm hole saw used to cut angled seat for tube-to-tube joints
- **Anti-split drill stop** — drilling a hole at the terminus of a saw slot to prevent crack propagation along the bamboo grain
- **Hose clip clamping** — slotted bamboo clamped onto fork legs with hose clips
- **Ball bearing cup press-fit** — steering head cups from donor bike inserted directly into 45mm bamboo bore
