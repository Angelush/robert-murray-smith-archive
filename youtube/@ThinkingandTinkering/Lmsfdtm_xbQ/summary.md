## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to modify an LCD monitor so that its screen appears blank (white) to the naked eye, but can be viewed normally through polarized glasses. The trick exploits how LCD screens use polarization filters to control light output — by removing the front polarization film, the screen looks blank until you look through a matching polarized filter, effectively creating a private display only the wearer can see.

## Key Topics
- How LCD screens use polarization filters to produce visible images
- The effect of removing the front polarization layer from an LCD
- Creating "privacy glasses" from polarization film to view the otherwise blank-looking screen

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Polarization film (removed from the LCD front panel)
- Anti-glare layer (noted as sometimes present on LCD screens)
- Protective plastic coating on LCD screens

## Techniques and Methods
- Scoring around the edge of the LCD's polarization film with a knife blade (lightly, to avoid cracking the glass)
- Using a palette knife to lever and peel off the front polarization film
- Repurposing the removed polarization film to fashion wearable glasses for private viewing
