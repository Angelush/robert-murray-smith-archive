## Overview
Robert acquires a second-hand hoverboard for £20 and disassembles it to extract its hub motors for repurposing as wind turbine generators. The video walks through the full teardown process and concludes with a quick hand-spin test demonstrating the motor's generator potential (6–7V, ~5mA from a hand spin), setting up a multi-part project series.

## Key Topics
- Consumer waste critique: hoverboards as short-lived fad products
- Hoverboard internals overview (battery, control board, accelerometer/gyro, hub motors)
- Step-by-step disassembly of the hoverboard chassis
- Hub motor construction: neodymium magnets, rotor, bearings, thrust washer
- Tire removal and motor disassembly technique
- Initial generator output test using the motor as a hand-spun alternator

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Neodymium (rare earth) magnets — arranged in a ring inside the hub motor rotor
- Cast metal frame — the main structural chassis of the hoverboard
- Bearing and thrust washer — internal motor components behind the rotor plate

## Techniques and Methods
- Full hoverboard disassembly by removing all visible screws
- Vise-gripping the axle to overcome neodymium magnet attraction and pull the motor hub off
- Tire removal using a lever between the case lip and the tyre bead
- Connecting two motor phase wires to produce single-phase AC output
- Using a soldering iron as a resistive load for the generator test
- Measuring open-circuit voltage and short-circuit current with a multimeter during hand-spin test
