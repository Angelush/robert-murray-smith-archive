## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith introduces carbon felt as a revolutionary wick material capable of wicking viscous oils like vegetable oil that standard cotton wicks cannot handle. He demonstrates it burning vegetable oil in a simple container and then shows it as the basis for an improved "plant pot heater," arguing its near-indestructibility makes it a practically permanent wick compared to cotton.
