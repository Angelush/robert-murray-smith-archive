## Overview
Robert unboxes and sets up the Elegoo Saturn 4 Ultra 16k resin 3D printer, sent to him by Elegoo for review. After initially resisting the idea (mirroring his earlier resistance to filament printers), he comes around to giving resin printing a fair try, motivated by its exceptional resolution potential for precision engineering parts. The video concludes that the machine is impressively easy to set up out of the box, with a follow-up video planned to cover the wash station and final print quality.

## Key Topics
- Robert's reflection on habit formation and resistance to change (the "2190 rule")
- His prior skepticism about resin printers vs. filament printers
- Physical unboxing and hardware overview of the Elegoo Saturn 4 Ultra 16k
- Notable design features and build quality
- Initial software setup using Chitubox slicer
- Post-processing requirements for resin prints (washing and curing)
- Preview of an air engine with rotary valve as the first test print

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Resin** — the primary print medium for this machine
- **IPA (isopropanol alcohol)** — used to wash printed resin parts; Robert's prior low-tech method was swishing parts in a jar of IPA
- **Sunlight** — mentioned as a low-cost curing method (leaving prints on a windowsill)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Resin MSLA/LCD 3D printing** — printing layers by exposing a resin vat through an LCD screen
- **Tilt-peel mechanism** — the build plate tilts rather than pulling straight up, reducing force on the FEP film and extending component life
- **Auto-levelling build platform** — spring-loaded plate that compensates for any twist during levelling
- **Slicing with Chitubox** — importing STL models, selecting machine profile (Saturn 4 Ultra 16k), and exporting print files
- **Washing with Mercury wash station** — Elegoo's dedicated wash unit (to be covered in a follow-up video)
- **UV curing** — required post-wash step (Mercury station also handles this)

## Robert's Own Replies
Robert's comments are mostly brief acknowledgements, but a few add useful context:
- He clarified that when he mentioned printers costing ~£125, he meant **FDM printers in general**, not the Bambu X1C specifically.
- He confirmed the **Elegoo Saturn 4 Ultra 16k had literally just launched** on the day of publication — it wasn't previously available.
- He accidentally **released the video early**, took it down almost immediately, and re-released it on the correct date.
- On sonicators and adjusting exposure times for resin, he acknowledged the point but said he **hadn't tried it yet** and had no recommendation.
- He teased that **the print quality reveal is in Part 3**, and Part 2 will cover the wash station.
- He mentioned he's **thinking about** black resin and thanked someone for tips on printing in black.
