## Overview
A short advent calendar video (Day 1) in which Robert performs a simple "magic trick" — pouring water into a cup and making it appear to vanish. The reveal is a science lesson: the cup contains super absorbent polymer, which soaks up the water invisibly. The takeaway is that everyday chemistry underlies many apparent tricks.

## Key Topics
- Advent calendar video series tradition
- Super absorbent polymers (SAP) as the science behind the trick
- How SAPs are used in everyday products (nappies/diapers)
- The absorptive capacity of SAPs (~500× their own weight in water)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Water** — the liquid being "disappeared"
- **Super absorbent polymer (SAP)** — the material hidden in the cup that absorbs the water; described as capable of absorbing ~500 times its own weight in water

## Techniques and Methods
- Simple demonstration: pouring water into a cup pre-loaded with SAP to show instant absorption
- No complex lab techniques; intended as an accessible, visual science communication piece
