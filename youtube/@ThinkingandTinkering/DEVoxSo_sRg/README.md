## Overview

Robert converts a broken vacuum pump's induction motor into a Frenette heater — a device that uses the shearing force of oil between rotating surfaces to generate heat. He disassembles the pump, modifies the motor stator with oil circulation channels, and demonstrates that the improvised heater raises oil temperature from ~8°C to ~40°C in roughly five minutes. The main conclusion is that a salvaged motor can be adapted into a working Frenette heater with minimal effort, validating the concept before further refinement.
