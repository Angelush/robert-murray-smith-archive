## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith reviews the Bambu Lab X1 Carbon 3D printer, framing it within a broader historical narrative of technologies — mobile phones, cars, computers — that evolved from niche curiosities into everyday tools. He tests the printer's claimed ease of use firsthand, finding that it delivers seamlessly within its "walled garden" ecosystem but requires a learning curve the moment you step outside it. His main takeaway is that 3D printing is reaching an inflection point, transitioning from a hobbyist/engineering niche into a mainstream tool.
