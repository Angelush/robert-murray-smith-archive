## Overview

Robert demonstrates a breakthrough iteration of the Darwin wind turbine — a wind catcher that redirects airflow down a pipe to a ground-level turbine with no moving parts. His key insight was replacing external mechanical flaps with internal "cheesy wedges" arranged in an octagonal formation, combined with a research-backed optimal louver angle of 35°. A smoke-flow test and a live turbine-and-light demo confirm the design works effectively.
