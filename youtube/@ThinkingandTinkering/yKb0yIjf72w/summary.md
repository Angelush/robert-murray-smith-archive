## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith gives a concise history of solar panel inverter technology, tracing the evolution from simple string inverters to modern microinverters. He explains the electrical and safety problems that drove this evolution, and concludes that microinverters represent a beneficial shift toward plug-and-play solar installations, though they come with their own trade-offs.

## Key Topics
- How solar panels produce DC voltage and why it must be converted to AC
- How an H-bridge inverter works
- Early string-wiring systems and their limitations (the "fairy light effect")
- Blocking diodes and bypass diodes as basic panel-level optimisation
- Safety risks of high-voltage DC strings (fire hazard, electrocution risk)
- Legislative requirements for fast shutdown driving adoption of panel-level electronics
- Power optimisers as a precursor to microinverters
- How microinverters work and their advantages (distributed resilience, scalability, plug-and-play)
- Pros and cons of single central inverters vs microinverters
- Concerns about longevity, weather exposure, and manufacturer compatibility for microinverters
- Possible future trend: routing DC wire into the loft/attic for more accessible microinverter placement

## Techniques and Methods
- H-bridge switching to invert DC to AC
- Series ("string") wiring of solar panels
- Use of blocking diodes to prevent reverse current flow between panels
- Use of bypass diodes to route current around failed cells
- Panel-level power optimisation/fast-shutdown electronics
- Distributed microinverter architecture (one inverter per panel)
