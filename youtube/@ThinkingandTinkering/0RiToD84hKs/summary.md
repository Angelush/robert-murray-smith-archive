## Overview
This video demonstrates how to successfully drill through ceramic without breaking it, a technique the presenter learned after breaking four pieces. The core lesson is that using the right drill bit, applying water for cooling, and letting the drill do the work (no downward pressure) are the keys to clean results. The method is shown step-by-step using progressively larger masonry bits to achieve a final 8mm hole.

## Key Topics
- Choosing the correct drill bit (masonry bit) for ceramic
- The three critical steps for drilling ceramic without cracking it
- Using a drill vice for safety and accuracy
- Progressive hole sizing: pilot hole → intermediate size → final 8mm
- Applicability to ceramic tiles

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Ceramic piece (the workpiece being drilled)
- Water (used as a coolant/lubricant during drilling)

## Techniques and Methods
- **Pilot hole first**: Start with a small drill bit to establish a centered, controlled entry point
- **Water cooling**: Apply water via pipette at regular intervals to cool the drill bit and reduce friction
- **Zero downward pressure**: Allow the drill's own weight and speed to do the cutting; forcing it causes cracking
- **Progressive bit sizing**: Step up bit diameter incrementally to reach the final target size
- **Drill vice use**: Secures the workpiece for straight, safe drilling without hand exposure
