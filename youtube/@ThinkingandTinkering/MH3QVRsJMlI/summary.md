## Overview
This video demonstrates how to fabricate a custom motor mount for an e-scooter using repurposed aluminium shelving brackets. The presenter cuts, bends, and bolts the bracket material into a clamp that hugs the motor securely, then adds feet for mounting to the scooter frame. The result is a lightweight, low-cost motor mount finished with a copper coat and draft sealant to dampen vibrations.

## Key Topics
- Designing a motor mount from recycled shelving brackets
- Fitting and clamping a motor using bent aluminium channel
- Fabricating mounting feet for attachment to an e-scooter
- Vibration damping and surface finishing

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Aluminium shelving bracket (U-profile)** — primary structural material for the mount
- **Steel base plate** — secondary bracket used for the mounting feet
- **Draft sealant** — foam/rubber strip applied inside the clamp to reduce motor vibrations
- **Copper spray paint** — cosmetic finish applied to the completed mount

## Techniques and Methods
- Measuring the motor circumference to determine cut dimensions (35 cm)
- Cutting angle notches at bracket corners to allow bending around the motor
- Cold bending aluminium channel by hand to conform to the motor body
- Drilling holes for a clamping bolt and for the mounting feet bolts
- Bolting the aluminium clamp to a steel base plate with wire pass-through hole
