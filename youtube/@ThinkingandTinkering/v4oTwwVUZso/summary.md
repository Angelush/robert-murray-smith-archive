## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith takes viewers on a brief walking tour of Canterbury, his hometown in England. He highlights the city's deep historical roots — from Neolithic settlements to its medieval pilgrimage heritage — while also pointing out forward-thinking modern initiatives. The video serves as a warm, personal introduction to a city that balances ancient history with contemporary urban planning.

## Key Topics
- Etymology of the word "canter" (derived from "Canterbury pace," the riding gait pilgrims used approaching the city)
- Canterbury's size and demographics: ~55,000 permanent residents, ~40,000 students annually, 7.8 million tourists in 2017
- The River Stour and its role as a scenic walking route
- The Abbott Mill Project: Canterbury City Council's plan to install a water wheel generating ~20 kWh peak at the site of an 800-year-old mill
- The Marlowe Theatre and Christopher Marlowe memorial (playwright born 1564, rumoured spy)
- Bird electric scooters introduced for last-mile transport in the pedestrianised city centre
- Canterbury's continuous settlement history: Neolithic, Iron Age (the Cantaci hill fort), Roman, Saxon, and medieval periods
- Westgate Tower, one of seven remaining original city gates
- The Buttermarket and Mercery Lane as the main pilgrim route to the cathedral
- King's School, one of the oldest continuously running schools (est. ~597–598 AD)
- Canterbury's pedestrianised city centre (ring road built during WWII, centre progressively car-free since)
