## Overview
Luke repairs a friend's Beyerdynamic DT 990 Pro studio headphones, which had lost audio in one ear. He diagnoses two soldering faults — a detached wire and a bridged solder joint — and resolves both using a wiring diagram for reference. The headphones are tested successfully before reassembly.

## Key Topics
- Disassembly of Beyerdynamic DT 990 Pro headphones
- Diagnosing common audio failure modes (loose/unsoldered wires)
- Identifying and correcting a bridged solder joint
- Using a wiring diagram to verify correct connections
- Testing before reassembly (community-suggested best practice)
- Reassembly and final result

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Solder (implied throughout the repair process)

## Techniques and Methods
- Prying off the outer ear cup ring with a flat-head screwdriver
- Removing internal screws with a Torx T6 screwdriver
- Inspecting solder joints visually for faults
- De-bridging (separating) incorrectly joined solder points
- Re-soldering a detached wire to its correct pad using a wiring diagram
- Functional audio test connected to a computer before final reassembly
