## Overview
A tribute video narrated by Robert Murray-Smith's son, documenting his father's attempt to build a water-powered over-balance wheel for a competition. The project explores why perpetual motion over-balance wheels fail (they reach equilibrium), then pivots to using water as the unbalancing element, ultimately powering an LED via a repurposed shake torch mechanism.

## Key Topics
- History and mechanics of over-balance wheels and perpetual motion
- Why over-balance wheels fail (reaching equilibrium)
- Using water as an unbalancing force for a wheel
- Converting kinetic energy from a water wheel into electrical energy
- Competition entry: powering an LED from a water-driven wheel

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Wine bottle screw caps (used as water vessels, ~12g per capful)
- Pan lid handles and a shaft (improvised pulley)
- Two small motors mounted in grommets
- Elastic band (drive belt)
- Double-sided sticky tape
- Compact fluorescent lamp (salvaged for its ferrite ring and coiled wire)
- Ferrite ring and coiled wire (to build a "dual thief" voltage booster)
- LED
- Flat battery (~1V, used for testing)
- Shake torch (containing a magnet-and-coil generator)

## Techniques and Methods
- Constructing and testing an over-balance wheel
- Weight testing with wire pieces (12g) to verify unbalancing threshold
- Improvised pulley system for energy transfer
- Building a "dual thief" boost converter from salvaged CFL components to amplify low voltage
- Replacing the pulley/motor assembly with a shake torch to use electromagnetic induction (magnet through copper coils) for power generation
- Testing voltage retention over 24+ hours with an LED load
