## Overview
Robert tests a vertical axis wind turbine (VAWT) rotor he built from a scrap tumble dryer drum, demonstrating that it spins in real outdoor conditions. After an initial indoor test with a hairdryer drew skepticism, he shows the rotor turning in a very light natural breeze (0.9–1.2 m/s), validating the concept. The video is a brief proof-of-concept update confirming the drag rotor design works in a real-world environment.

## Key Topics
- Outdoor wind testing of a homemade VAWT rotor
- Drag rotor vs. lift rotor design distinction
- Repurposing scrap materials (tumble dryer drum) for wind energy projects
- Responding to skepticism about DIY wind turbine viability
- VAWT self-limiting behaviour at high wind speeds
- Comparison to a previous "chair legs" VAWT design

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Scrap tumble dryer drum — used as the rotor body
- Bent fins — cut/bent from the drum to catch wind (drag-based)
- Glycerine and water — mentioned in comments as a DIY smoke-stick mixture for flow visualisation

## Techniques and Methods
- Bending fins outward from the drum to create a drag rotor
- Initial indoor spin testing using a hairdryer
- Outdoor field testing with an anemometer to measure wind speed
- Using pre-balanced drum geometry to simplify rotor construction

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Drag rotor behaviour:** Confirms it is a cylindrical drag rotor, suspects the wind flows around it rather than through it, but acknowledges he is guessing — his main goal is simply that it turns in a breeze.
- **VAWT self-regulation:** Has heard VAWTs are self-limiting at high wind and plans to test whether brakes are needed rather than assuming they are, pushing back on commenters who jumped straight to problem-solving without evidence.
- **Previous design:** Acknowledges the tumble dryer version is a significant improvement over an earlier "chair legs" version; lessons from that build carry forward.
- **Generator potential:** Speculates that old washing machine drums might not even need added magnets — residual magnetism could be sufficient, similar to how an alternator works.
- **Smoke visualisation:** Plans to make smoke sticks from glycerine and water to visualise airflow around the rotor.
- **Philosophy on building vs. buying:** Strongly advocates building over buying to remove the "black box" mystery, even if it takes longer; refuses to pay for parts and waits to find scrap instead.
- **VAWT relevance:** Defends VAWTs against dismissal by pointing to real-world installations (VAWTs on lamp posts in Kent, UK), arguing everything has its place.
