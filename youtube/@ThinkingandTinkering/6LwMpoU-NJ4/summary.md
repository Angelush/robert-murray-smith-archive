## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates that a loudspeaker, being a linear motor, can be reversed to act as a linear generator. Using a Manchester dynamo, a shaker-torch-style linear generator, and finally a speaker cone driven by a spinning magnet, he shows each producing enough power to light an LED. He also debunks several viral YouTube videos claiming exotic speaker modifications produce significant power.

## Key Topics
- Electromagnetic generation principles: wire length, magnetic field strength, and speed determine output
- Rotary generators vs. linear generators
- The Manchester dynamo as a classic rotary generator example
- Linear generators (shaker torch concept)
- Speakers as linear motors — and therefore as linear generators
- Using a spinning permanent magnet to drive a speaker cone at high frequency
- Debunking clickbait videos about speaker "free energy" modifications (static coils wrapped around a speaker do nothing useful)
- How to genuinely improve speaker-as-generator output: more wire turns, stronger magnet, or higher vibration frequency

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Manchester dynamo** — early rotary dynamo used as demonstration of magnetic generation
- **Permanent magnet** (neodymium-style, north/south polarized) — used in dynamo and as spinning driver above speaker
- **LED** — used as load to demonstrate generation in all three setups
- **Loudspeaker** — repurposed as a linear generator
- **Copper wire / voice coil** — the conductor whose motion through the magnetic field generates current
- **Capacitor / supercapacitor** — mentioned in context of shaker torch energy storage

## Techniques and Methods
- Spinning a permanent magnet inside a two-coil armature (rotary generation demo)
- Shaking a magnet through a hollow coil-wrapped tube (linear generation / shaker torch demo)
- Tapping a speaker cone connected directly to an LED (speaker-as-generator demo)
- Spinning a magnet on a drill above a speaker's internal magnet to induce high-frequency cone vibration (non-contact rotary-to-linear drive)
- Evaluating proposed methods for improving output: increasing voice coil turns, using stronger magnets, increasing vibration frequency
