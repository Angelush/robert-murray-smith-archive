## Overview
Robert disassembles, cleans, and refurbishes the rusty single-phase induction motor from a potter's wheel, demonstrating that such motors can be restored to working condition with minimal effort. He successfully brings the motor back to life after a basic clean-up, oiling, and reassembly, concluding that motor refurbishment is a practical and worthwhile repair strategy.

## Key Topics
- Disassembly and inspection of a single-phase induction motor
- How single-phase induction motors work (no brushes, no commutator — minimal wear parts)
- Checking field coils for continuity
- The role of start capacitors in single-phase motors
- Bushing vs. bearing lubrication (felt oil reservoirs)
- General philosophy of motor refurbishment vs. replacement
- Analogy to automotive alternator and starter motor refurbishment

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Oil (for lubricating the felt bushings)
- Paint (coat applied to cleaned motor body)
- Rust (present on original motor; removed during refurbishment)

## Techniques and Methods
- Motor disassembly and component inspection
- Rust removal by brushing down
- Continuity check on field coils
- Oiling felt bushings/oil reservoirs
- Reassembly and functional test (powered-up spin test)
