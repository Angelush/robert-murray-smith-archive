## Overview
This video is an announcement by Jackie Marie Smith (Rob's wife) and Robert Murray-Smith for a live interactive YouTube event titled "Make A Battery With Rob," scheduled for 27 October 2018. Participants would work along in real time using a materials kit to fabricate a small battery at home. The event was conceived as a solution to the problem of people being unable to attend FWG's in-person workshops, making the guided experimentation experience globally accessible.

## Key Topics
- Announcement and format of the live YouTube battery-making workshop
- Structure of the three-hour event (theory seminar, guided experimentation, Q&A)
- Overview of the battery chemistry and the kit contents
- Equipment participants need to source independently
- How to test the finished battery using a small electric motor
- Kit availability and ordering details

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Zinc** — used as a current collector (anode side)
- **Copper foil** — used as the other current collector (cathode side)
- **Titanate particles** (likely sodium or lithium titanate) — active cathode material, synthesised during the workshop
- **Carbon black** — conductive additive mixed into the cathode material
- **CMC binder** (carboxymethylcellulose) — used to bind the cathode slurry
- **Filter paper** — used in the synthesis/filtration step
- **Water** (DI water preferred; tap water noted as workable per author comments)
- **Hemp (graphitised)** — mentioned in comments as a potentially conductive material worth experimenting with

## Techniques and Methods
- Hydrothermal or precipitation synthesis of titanate particles (cathode material made during the workshop)
- Slurry preparation: mixing titanate particles, carbon black, and CMC binder
- Coating the cathode current collector with the slurry
- Improvised filtration using a tea strainer folded into a funnel (in place of a filter funnel)
- Electrochemical testing using a digital multimeter
- Functional testing by driving a small electric motor as an inductive/resistive load

## Robert's Own Replies
- **Event is free to view** — no charge to watch the live stream; monetisation is through kit sales, but the information itself remains free.
- **Water quality** — DI water is best, but tap water will still produce a working result.
- **Hemp as electrode material** — once graphitised, hemp is conductive; exact extent untested, but he encourages people to try it.
- **Recording/archive** — details still being worked out at the time, likely via a private archive.
