## Overview
A short build-update video, presented by Luke on Robert's channel, focused on the choice and replacement of axles for a DIY bamboo electric trike (the "bamboo cord"). The video argues that a simple rear single-wheel axle is the right trade-off for low-budget, low-material electric vehicles, drawing on historical examples to justify the tricycle layout despite its known stability limitations.

## Key Topics
- Why axle design matters for low-budget, post-war-style vehicle construction
- Trade-offs of tricycle vs. quadricycle stability (four contact points vs. three)
- Historical precedent: BMW Isetta (1955) as a frugal-design tricycle example
- Modern parallel: Polaris Slingshot (2015) as a tricycle-format vehicle
- Simplicity advantages of a single rear-wheel axle — easier drivetrain, bearings, and frame attachment
- Replacing the rear axle on the in-progress bamboo cord electric vehicle

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Bamboo** — used as the primary structural material for the "bamboo cord" vehicle frame

## Techniques and Methods
- Fitting a drive cog and bearings onto an axle shaft
- Attaching wheels and connecting the axle to a vehicle frame
- Swapping a rear axle from a two-wheel to a single-wheel configuration on an existing build
