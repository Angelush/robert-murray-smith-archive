## Overview

Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how to synthesize zinc acetate via a three-stage process: first dissolving zinc metal in hydrochloric acid to form zinc chloride, then precipitating zinc carbonate using sodium carbonate, and finally reacting the zinc carbonate with acetic acid to yield zinc acetate. Direct reaction of zinc with acetic acid is far too slow, making the intermediate steps essential. The video emphasizes practical sourcing of materials (zinc from old batteries, HCl from hardware stores) and important safety and handling notes throughout.
