## Overview
Robert shares a short reflective monologue sparked by news of a large Bitcoin theft, using it as a springboard to explore the difference between opinion and fact. He argues that most heated arguments—whether about Bitcoin, politics, or anything else—are ultimately "big end vs little end" disputes: coherent-sounding but fundamentally trivial. His core takeaway is that being comfortable with being wrong, and giving others space for differing views, is healthier than defending opinions as if they were facts.

## Key Topics
- Bitcoin news (a large theft) used as a personal reality check on his own views
- The distinction between opinion and fact, and how opinion masquerades as fact
- A Chinese proverb: if you can change something, act; if you can't, accept it
- The "big end / little end" metaphor (from *Gulliver's Travels*) for trivial arguments
- Embracing being wrong as a natural part of learning
