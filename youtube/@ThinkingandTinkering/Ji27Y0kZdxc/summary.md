## Overview
Part 6 of the "Building Bessie" series covers the fitting of body panels to a 40-year-old riding lawn mower being converted into an electric vehicle. Robert uses two fabrication approaches — repurposed industrial steel shelving and fiberglass panels made in earlier episodes — to assemble the seat, footplates, running boards, mudguards, bulkhead, and radiator grille. By the end of the episode the vehicle has taken on a recognisable, cohesive form, with a clear cabin and engine/battery compartment.

## Key Topics
- Fitting body panels as the stage where the build "takes shape"
- Two panel fabrication approaches: fiberglass and repurposed steel shelving
- Seat selection and installation (single leatherette office chair)
- Installing footplates, running boards, and front and rear mudguards
- Fitting the fiberglass bulkhead (between engine compartment and cabin) and radiator grille
- Walk-around inspection of the nearly complete bodywork
- Remaining items still to fit: steering column, brake pedal, accelerator pedal, engine/battery compartment flaps
- Philosophy of repurposing old equipment ("don't throw anything away")

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Industrial sheet steel shelving** — repurposed as cabin panel, footplates, running boards, and mudguards
- **Fiberglass** — used for the main bulkhead and radiator-area panels, fabricated in earlier episodes
- **Leatherette office chair** — adopted as the single driver's seat
- **Yellow paint** — applied to the steel cabin panel

## Techniques and Methods
- Measuring, cutting, and bending steel shelving to form body components
- Drilling and bolting panels to the frame and outriggers
- Fiberglass panel fabrication without traditional molds (described in prior episodes)
- Sequenced assembly: steel panels first, then fiberglass/plastic panels on top
