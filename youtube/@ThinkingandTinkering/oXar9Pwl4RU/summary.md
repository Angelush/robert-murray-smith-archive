## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith makes a chess set as a Christmas gift for his older brother — a former chess master — using acrylic salvaged from broken LCD screens. He laser-cuts the pieces from acrylic of varying thicknesses, constructs the board from the reflective backing material found inside LCDs, and sandwiches it between recovered tempered glass. The video emphasises that no laser cutter is required, as he offers the free cut-file plans to anyone who emails him.

## Key Topics
- Upcycling broken LCD screens into a functional chess set
- Laser cutting acrylic chess pieces from free downloadable plans
- Constructing a chessboard from LCD reflective backing and recycled tempered glass
- Surface finishing acrylic to remove the crisscross/diamond pattern left by the screen
- Making rubber feet for the finished board

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Acrylic (from LCD screens)** — ~1 cm thick for white pieces; two ~5 mm sheets laminated together for black pieces
- **Black enamel paint** — applied to one face of the 5 mm acrylic to create black pieces
- **LCD reflective/shiny backing sheet** — cut into squares for the chessboard pattern
- **Tempered glass (recycled from a shelving unit)** — used as top and bottom layers of the board
- **Spray adhesive / clear spray glue** — to adhere chessboard squares to the glass
- **T-Cut (cutting compound)** — used to polish acrylic and remove the diamond/crisscross surface pattern
- **Very fine grit sandpaper** — initial surface prep on acrylic pieces
- **Double-sided tape** — for attaching rubber feet
- **Sheet rubber** — for fabricating custom feet
- **Clear protective plastic sheet** — used to protect the underside of the board

## Techniques and Methods
- **Laser cutting** — cutting chess piece profiles from acrylic using JPEG plans loaded into the laser cutter
- **Laminating acrylic sheets** — gluing two 5 mm pieces together to achieve 1 cm thickness for black pieces
- **Wet sanding on glass** — placing fine sandpaper flat on glass and working the acrylic in circular motions to flatten and clean the surface
- **T-Cut polishing** — using cutting compound on a glass surface in circular motions to restore clarity
- **Spray-booth improvisation** — using a slit-open cardboard box to contain overspray when applying adhesive
- **Punch cutting rubber** — using a punch tool on sheet rubber to make custom circular/oval feet
