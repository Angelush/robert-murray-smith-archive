## Overview
This is Part 1 of a two-part series on building a DIY tabletop arcade cabinet from scratch. The video covers the full woodworking and structural construction phase — from prototyping in cardboard through cutting panels, assembling the frame, fitting the monitor, and applying primer — concluding with a fully primed cabinet shell ready for electronics in Part 2.

## Key Topics
- Prototyping the cabinet layout using a cardboard mockup before committing to wood
- Cutting and assembling the main wooden panels (side panels, bottom panel, back panel)
- Building an internal frame for structural strength and stability
- Constructing the control board with holes for buttons and joysticks
- Building a monitor mount using a back brace and a pine bottom brace
- Fitting an acrylic sheet as the screen cover/"glass"
- Filling gaps with resin and sanding flush before priming

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- **Wood (sheet/plywood)** — used for all main cabinet panels and framing
- **Pine** — used for the bottom monitor brace and angle-matching trim piece
- **Acrylic sheet** — used as the transparent screen cover ("glass")
- **Resin** — used to fill gaps at high-contact areas (preferred over poly filler for durability underfoot)
- **Poly filler** — mentioned as an alternative to resin for gap filling
- **Primer (spray/paint)** — applied to the control board and full cabinet shell

## Techniques and Methods
- Cardboard prototyping to verify panel dimensions and fit before cutting wood
- Tracing cardboard templates onto wood panels
- Panel cutting (circular saw or jigsaw implied)
- Drilling button and joystick holes using paper templates as guides
- Screw assembly of panels and control board
- Internal framing for rigidity
- Monitor mounting via back brace and bottom brace support
- Acrylic cutting and flush-fitting around curved edges
- Gap filling with resin followed by sanding flush
- Primer coating of all wooden surfaces
