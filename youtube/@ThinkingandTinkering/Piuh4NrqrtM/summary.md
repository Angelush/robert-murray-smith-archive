## Overview
Robert Murray-Smith shares a quick, elegant tip for drawing large circles without a traditional compass. The method uses a nail (or screw) as a pivot point and a lockable tape measure as the radius arm, with a pen inserted at the desired distance. He considers it one of the simplest and most practical workshop tricks he's encountered.

## Key Topics
- Drawing large circles without a compass
- Using a tape measure as a improvised large-radius drawing tool
- Brief mention of previous methods he has demonstrated (big DIY compass, drilled wood)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Nail (or screw) — used as a fixed pivot point
- Standard lockable tape measure — used as the radius arm
- Pen — used as the drawing instrument

## Techniques and Methods
- Fix a nail or screw into the surface at the desired circle centre
- Pull the tape measure out to the required radius and lock it
- Loop the tape measure end around the nail
- Insert a pen at the locked measurement point with slight tension
- Sweep the pen around the nail to draw the circle
