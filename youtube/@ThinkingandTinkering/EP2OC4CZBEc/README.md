## Overview

This video traces the largely forgotten history of solar thermal energy, from early pioneers in the 19th century through the post-WWII housing boom in America. Robert argues that solar thermal was a viable, widely-adopted technology in the 1950s — with roughly 80% of Florida homes using it — until electrical utility companies (GE, Westinghouse) actively suppressed it through advertising campaigns and backroom deals with mass homebuilder William Levitt. The core takeaway is that today's renewed interest in solar is a rediscovery of something that was deliberately killed for commercial reasons.
