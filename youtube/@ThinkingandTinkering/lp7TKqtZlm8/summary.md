## Overview
A short demonstration of "Components on a Card," a product concept in development that makes simple electronics accessible by mounting battery, switch, and LED components on adhesive card-format modules connected with conductive ink — no soldering required. The video showcases a light-up greeting card as a proof of concept and highlights scrapbooking as a key use case.

## Key Topics
- The complexity barrier of traditional electronics (soldering, component knowledge)
- The "Components on a Card" system: a simplified electronics kit for crafts
- Current component lineup: battery, switch, LED
- Future components in development: motor, sound module, 555 timer
- Using conductive ink to connect components and complete circuits
- Application ideas: greeting cards, scrapbooking
- Availability through an online store (WWE K)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- Conductive ink — used to draw circuit connections between components on card

## Techniques and Methods
- Peeling double-sided sticky tape backing and placing components on card surface
- Drawing connections between components with conductive ink to complete a circuit
- Folding a card-mounted switch to make contact and trigger the LED
