## Overview
In this short supplementary video, Robert Murray-Smith demonstrates how he fabricated a custom steel grate for his DIY gasification unit, rather than using the colander recommended in the FEMA gasifier book. He walks through welding a grid of 6mm rod, attaching chain hangers, and fabricating hooks from bar stock to suspend the grate inside the gasifier body. The takeaway is a sturdier, more industrial-looking solution than the off-the-shelf alternative.

## Key Topics
- Reason for abandoning the FEMA-book colander recommendation in favour of a custom grate
- Construction of a welded steel grid grate from bar stock and rod
- Chain-based suspension system for the grate inside the gasifier pipe
- Fabricating bent hooks using a pipe as a forming tool, welded directly to the plate (avoiding drilled holes that could leak)

## Materials and Chemicals Mentioned
- 1.5 cm flat bar metal (frame of the grate)
- 6 mm steel rod (grid pattern of the grate)
- Heavy-gauge chain (donated, used to suspend the grate)
- Aluweld rod (mentioned in comments as the brazing/joining product used)
- Galvanised steel (implied — Robert notes he ground the zinc off before welding)

## Techniques and Methods
- MIG/stick welding of rod grid and hooks
- Crimping and spot-welding chain links
- Bending hooks from bar offcuts using a length of pipe as a former
- Grinding zinc coating off galvanised material prior to welding
- Brazing with Aluweld rod (clarified in comments)
