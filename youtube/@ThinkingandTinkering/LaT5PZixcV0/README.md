## Overview

Robert demonstrates how to build a simple urea electrolysis cell for hydrogen production, adapted from a design by "Nighthawk Light." The key insight is that urea can be electrolyzed at just 0.37 volts — roughly 70% less than the 1.23 volts required for water electrolysis — making it a far more energy-efficient route to hydrogen. The video covers both the construction of the cell and the electrolyte recipe, and frames urea electrolysis as a way to extract value from a biological waste resource.
